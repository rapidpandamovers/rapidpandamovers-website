Pinecrest is one of those Miami-Dade communities where the best parts reveal themselves slowly. Drive through on US-1 and you see strip malls and traffic. Live here for a few months and you start finding the canopy-covered trails, the Sunday market that locals plan their weekends around, and the parks that feel nothing like South Florida.

Here are 10 spots that Pinecrest residents know and newcomers should discover.

## 1. Pinecrest Gardens Sunday Farmers Market

The weekly farmers market at Pinecrest Gardens (11000 Red Road) runs every Sunday from 9:00 AM to 2:00 PM, year-round, rain or shine. About 60 vendors set up during peak season with organic produce, artisan bread, fresh flowers, local honey, handmade soaps, and prepared foods from small-batch producers.

![Aerial Shot Of An Empty Swimming Pool In 3](/images/blog/2029/09/10-secret-spots-in-pinecrest-worth-discovering/aerial-shot-of-an-empty-swimming-pool-in-3.webp)


What makes this market different from others in Miami-Dade is the setting. You are shopping inside a 12.5-acre botanical garden that was originally built in 1936 as the home of Parrot Jungle. The combination of live oak canopy, tropical plants, and live music makes it feel like a weekend ritual rather than a grocery run.

## 2. Red Road Linear Park

This 2.5-mile lighted walking and bicycle path runs along the Snapper Creek Canal on Red Road between Kendall Drive and Killian Drive. Three rest areas built with coral rock walls and shaded by landscaping break up the route. It is flat, paved, and quiet enough that you can hear birds over traffic.

Most newcomers do not know this trail exists because it does not look like a traditional park from the road. Look for the path entrances at the major cross streets.

## 3. The Pinecrest Gardens Petting Zoo and Splash Pad

Pinecrest Gardens is listed on the National Register of Historic Places and houses over 1,000 varieties of tropical plants. But families with young children should head straight to the back of the park. The petting zoo has goats, tortoises, and parrots, and the Splash 'N Play water feature keeps kids cool during the summer months. Entry to the gardens costs $5 for adults and $3 for children, which is remarkably cheap for what you get.

## 4. Suniland Park's Dog Park (Pawcrest Park)

Suniland Park on Pinecrest Parkway underwent a major renovation in recent years. The old space-themed playground equipment is gone, replaced by modern structures under a new canopy cover. But the real draw for dog owners is Pawcrest Park, the village's only off-leash dog park, tucked into the southwest corner of the 10-acre complex. It is where you meet your neighbors.

## 5. The Desert Garden Inside Pinecrest Gardens

Most visitors stick to the main walking path through the tropical plantings, but Pinecrest Gardens contains one of the most diverse desert gardens in Florida. Cacti, succulents, and arid-climate plants from around the world are grouped together in a section that feels completely out of place in humid South Florida. It is easy to miss if you do not know it is there.

## 6. Evelyn Greer Park's Walking Track

Evelyn Greer Park on SW 97th Avenue is an active 10-acre park with baseball and soccer fields, batting cages, and a playground. The walking and jogging track that circles the park is popular with morning runners, and exercise stations along the route let you mix in bodyweight workouts. The shaded playground draws a regular crowd of parents with young children.

## 7. Coral Pine Park

Coral Pine Park sits on SW 104th Street and offers a more low-key alternative to the larger parks. It has a community center, playground, and open green space. Local families use it for birthday parties and casual weekend hangouts without the crowds of Pinecrest Gardens.

## 8. The Pinecrest Gardens Amphitheater

A 500-seat amphitheater inside Pinecrest Gardens hosts over 50 performances per year, from jazz concerts and dance recitals to children's theater and holiday events. The schedule is posted on the Pinecrest Gardens website. Because the venue is intimate and outdoors, even back-row seats feel close to the stage.

## 9. Suniland Shops on US-1

This strip of small businesses on US-1 near Suniland Park does not look remarkable from the road, but it houses a post office, a tag agency, a long-running hardware store, several locally owned restaurants, and a grocery store. It is the kind of commercial node where regulars greet each other by name and the staff at the hardware store can tell you which plumber to call.

## 10. Flagler Grove Park

Flagler Grove Park on SW 87th Avenue is one of Pinecrest's smaller neighborhood parks with athletic fields and open lawn space. It is less crowded than Suniland or Evelyn Greer, which makes it a good option for quiet afternoons, casual soccer games, or letting kids run without competing for space.

## Getting to Know Your New Neighborhood

The common thread across these spots is that Pinecrest rewards exploration. The village is residential and quiet by design, so the best finds are not advertised on billboards. Walk the Red Road trail, show up at the Sunday market a few times, and chat with the regulars at Suniland Shops. Within a month, Pinecrest starts feeling like home.
