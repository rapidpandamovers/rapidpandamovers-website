Welcome to your January guide for moving to Miami Springs! Whether you're relocating from within South Florida or making a bigger move this winter, understanding your new community is key to a successful move.

## Why Choose Miami Springs?

Miami Springs stands out as one of Miami-Dade County's most appealing locations. Founded in 1926 by aviation pioneer Glenn Curtiss, the community offers a historic and charming atmosphere. The city is famous for its distinctive Pueblo Revival architecture, tree-lined streets, and small-town feel just minutes from Miami International Airport.

![A Beautiful Two Story House With Stone F 4](/images/blog/2026/01/miami-springs-living-essential-relocation-tips/a-beautiful-two-story-house-with-stone-f-4.webp)


The area attracts families, professionals, and retirees alike, thanks to its quality of life, convenient access to major employment centers, and excellent amenities.

### Location and Accessibility

One of Miami Springs's biggest advantages is its strategic location within Miami-Dade County. The city sits along the Miami Canal and offers quick access to the Palmetto Expressway (SR 826) and the Airport Expressway (SR 112). Downtown Miami is about 15 minutes away, and Miami Beach is a 25-minute drive. For frequent travelers, living near MIA means easy domestic and international flights.

### Community and Lifestyle

Miami Springs residents enjoy a strong sense of community. The city maintains several parks including Miami Springs Recreation Center and the Miami Springs Golf Course, an 18-hole municipal course dating back to 1953. Community events at the Curtiss Mansion and the annual "Circle" gatherings on Westward Drive bring neighbors together throughout the year. January weather in the 60s and 70s makes outdoor activities especially enjoyable.

## Neighborhoods to Consider

When planning your move to Miami Springs, areas around "The Circle" on Westward Drive and the Country Club section are particularly popular with newcomers. The historic district near Curtiss Parkway features original 1920s Pueblo Revival homes. Each neighborhood has its own character. Some offer walkable dining and shopping on Canal Street, while others provide quiet, tree-lined streets with mature oaks and banyans.

### Choosing Your Perfect Spot

Consider these factors:

- **Proximity to work and schools**: Factor in your daily commute
- **Local amenities**: Parks, shopping, dining, and entertainment options
- **Property types**: Single-family homes, condos, townhouses, or apartments
- **Community vibe**: Family-oriented, young professional, or mixed demographics

## Moving to Miami Springs in January

January is an excellent time to consider your move. The winter weather in South Florida provides mild temperatures and lower humidity, ideal for the moving process.

### Timing Your Move

When planning your relocation, consider:

- **Best moving days**: Weekdays often offer better availability and rates
- **Weather considerations**: Winter in Miami means mild temperatures and lower humidity, ideal for moving
- **Local events**: Check for any street closures or community events that might affect your move
