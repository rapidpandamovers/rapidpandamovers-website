Kendall stretches across a wide swath of southwestern Miami-Dade County, covering everything from the upscale shops of Dadeland to the family-oriented subdivisions of The Crossings and the newer developments of West Kendall. It is not technically a city; Kendall is an unincorporated census-designated place, which means it falls under Miami-Dade County governance rather than having its own municipal government.

What it lacks in formal city status, Kendall makes up for in substance. The area consistently attracts families and professionals who want suburban space, strong schools, and reasonable proximity to Downtown Miami without paying Coral Gables or Key Biscayne prices.

## Neighborhoods to Know

### Dadeland and Downtown Kendall

![A Black Suv Parked In Front Of A Suburba 4](/images/blog/2029/09/kendall-for-new-residents-a-complete-resource/a-black-suv-parked-in-front-of-a-suburba-4.webp)


The northeastern corner of Kendall, centered around Dadeland Mall and the Dadeland South Metrorail station, is the area's commercial and transit hub. Dadeland Mall is one of the highest-grossing shopping centers in the country, and the surrounding blocks include office towers, restaurants, and high-rise condos. If you want Metrorail access for commuting to Downtown Miami or Brickell, this is where to look.

### The Crossings

Located about 16 miles southwest of Downtown Miami, The Crossings is one of Kendall's most popular family neighborhoods. Tree-lined streets run through subdivisions of ranch-style homes, Spanish Revivals, and midcentury moderns, many with pools or canal views. The Crossings Country Club at the center of the community includes a clubhouse, pool, and tennis courts managed by the homeowners association. Public schools in The Crossings are highly rated, and crime rates run about 20 percent lower than the Miami-Dade average.

### West Kendall

West Kendall is the fastest-growing part of the area, with newer subdivisions, townhome communities, and retail centers extending toward the Everglades. Housing prices here are generally lower than eastern Kendall, and you get more square footage for your money. The tradeoff is a longer commute: rush-hour drives on Kendall Drive (SW 88th Street) or the Don Shula Expressway can stretch a 20-minute trip into an hour.

### Pinecrest-Adjacent Kendall

The area north of Killian Drive and east of the Turnpike borders the Village of Pinecrest and shares some of its character: larger lots, mature tree canopy, and quiet residential streets. Homes here are generally more expensive than The Crossings or West Kendall, but the proximity to Pinecrest's schools and parks adds value.

## Schools

Kendall's school system is a major draw for families. Miami-Dade County Public Schools serves the area, and several Kendall-area schools are A-rated:

- **Elementary**: Kendale Elementary, Kendale Lakes Elementary, and Sunset Elementary are among the higher-performing options
- **Middle**: Kendale Lakes Middle School and Hammocks Middle School serve much of the area
- **High**: Miami Killian Senior High and G. Holmes Braddock Senior High are the main public high schools

Charter and magnet programs are also available throughout the district. School zone boundaries should factor into your neighborhood choice if you have children.

## Getting Around

### Driving

Kendall's major east-west artery is Kendall Drive (SW 88th Street), which connects the area to US-1, the Palmetto Expressway, and the Florida Turnpike. The Don Shula Expressway (SR 874) and the Snapper Creek Expressway (SR 878) provide north-south connections. Rush-hour traffic is heavy on all of these routes, especially eastbound in the morning and westbound in the evening.

### Public Transit

The Dadeland North and Dadeland South Metrorail stations at the northeastern edge of Kendall connect to Downtown Miami, Brickell, the Health District, and Miami International Airport. From Dadeland South, the ride to Downtown Miami takes about 25 minutes. Metrobus routes serve other parts of Kendall, though coverage is limited in the western neighborhoods.

### Commute Times

- **Downtown Miami**: 25 to 50 minutes depending on route and traffic
- **Brickell**: 20 to 40 minutes
- **MIA (Miami International Airport)**: 20 to 35 minutes
- **Miami Beach**: 35 to 60 minutes

## Cost of Living

Kendall offers a middle ground in the Miami-Dade housing market. Expect to pay $2,000 to $3,000 per month for a two-bedroom apartment, or $450,000 to $750,000 for a single-family home depending on the neighborhood. The Crossings and West Kendall tend toward the lower end of that range, while Dadeland-adjacent and Pinecrest-border areas sit higher.

![Aerial View Of A Suburban Neighborhood S 3](/images/blog/2029/09/kendall-for-new-residents-a-complete-resource/aerial-view-of-a-suburban-neighborhood-s-3.webp)


Groceries, dining, and utilities are in line with the broader Miami-Dade average. Property taxes are set by the county rather than a municipal government, which can be slightly lower than incorporated cities with their own millage rates.

## Parks and Recreation

Kendall has several parks worth knowing:

- **Kendall Indian Hammocks Park**: A large park with a skate park, baseball diamonds, a disc golf course, trails, and fitness zones
- **Kendall Soccer Park (KSP)**: A 42-acre complex with five artificial turf fields and a mile-long walking path
- **A.D. Barnes Park**: Features a nature trail, playground, and one of Miami-Dade's public pool facilities

## Healthcare

Baptist Health Kendall and West Kendall Baptist Hospital are the two major medical facilities in the area, offering emergency services, maternity care, and specialty treatment. Nicklaus Children's Hospital (formerly Miami Children's Hospital) is located just north of Kendall on SW 62nd Avenue for pediatric care.

## September Moving Tips for Kendall

### Weather and Timing

![Gray Toyota Tacoma Parked In A Suburban  1](/images/blog/2029/09/kendall-for-new-residents-a-complete-resource/gray-toyota-tacoma-parked-in-a-suburban--1.webp)


September in Kendall brings the same conditions as the rest of South Florida: temperatures in the low 90s, high humidity, and afternoon thunderstorms between 2:00 and 5:00 PM. Start your move early in the morning. Weekday moves offer better availability and rates than weekends.

### HOA and Community Rules

Many Kendall subdivisions, especially in The Crossings and West Kendall, have HOA rules governing move-in procedures. Common requirements include 48 to 72 hours advance notice, a refundable deposit ($250 to $500), designated moving hours, and floor protection in condos with shared hallways.

### Traffic Planning

Avoid scheduling your move during morning or evening rush hours on Kendall Drive. If you are moving between Kendall neighborhoods, the crew will know which side streets avoid the worst congestion.

## FAQ

**Is Kendall a city?**
No. Kendall is an unincorporated census-designated place within Miami-Dade County. It does not have its own mayor or city council. Services like police and fire are provided by Miami-Dade County.

**How far is Kendall from Miami Beach?**
About 20 miles, or 35 to 60 minutes depending on traffic. The Palmetto Expressway to I-195 is the most common route.

**What is the safest part of Kendall?**
The Crossings and the Pinecrest-adjacent areas consistently report lower crime rates. Check current statistics for your specific neighborhood before deciding.

**Does Kendall have a Metrorail station?**
Yes, at the Dadeland North and Dadeland South stations in the northeastern part of Kendall. Most of the area west of the Turnpike is not served by rail.
