Your November moving day is coming up, and it's going to be hectic. The last thing you need during a fall move in Miami is to realize something critical is buried in a box on the truck. These items should stay with you, accessible and ready when you need them.

## 1. Important Documents

Passports, IDs, birth certificates, and lease/closing papers should stay on your person or in your personal vehicle, never in a moving box.

![A Pile Of Empty Cardboard Boxes Ready Fo 2](/images/blog/2024/11/6-moving-day-essentials-you-cant-forget/a-pile-of-empty-cardboard-boxes-ready-fo-2.webp)


## 2. Medications and First Aid

Current prescriptions, over-the-counter essentials, and any medical devices you might need in the next 24 hours stay with you. In Miami's heat, also pack sunscreen and insect repellent, especially if you're moving during summer months or near areas like Coral Gables or Coconut Grove with lots of greenery.

## 3. Phone Chargers and Power Banks

Dead phones on moving day mean missed calls from movers, utility companies, and anyone trying to help coordinate.

![Person Packing Glassware Into Boxes With 1](/images/blog/2024/11/6-moving-day-essentials-you-cant-forget/person-packing-glassware-into-boxes-with-1.webp)


## 4. Basic Toolkit

A screwdriver set, pliers, box cutter, and hammer cover last-minute needs at both locations. You'll use these more than you expect.

## 5. Cleaning Supplies

Paper towels, all-purpose cleaner, and trash bags help with quick clean-ups during the transition at both old and new locations. Miami's humidity can leave dust and grime that builds up quickly, so a quick wipe-down before unloading furniture is always a good idea.

![Woman Sealing Boxes With Tape For Reloca 4](/images/blog/2024/11/6-moving-day-essentials-you-cant-forget/woman-sealing-boxes-with-tape-for-reloca-4.webp)


## 6. First-Night Box

Toiletries, towels, bedding, change of clothes, and anything you'll need to sleep comfortably night one without unpacking everything. If you're moving in summer, include a portable fan since Miami apartments can take time to cool down, especially in high-rises in Brickell or Downtown.
