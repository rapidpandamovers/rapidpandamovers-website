Key Biscayne feels like a small town that happens to be 15 minutes from downtown Miami. The island is compact, the streets are bikeable, and the ratio of parks to people is hard to beat anywhere in South Florida. If you just moved here with kids, or you are planning to, these are five activities worth putting on your calendar right away.

## 1. Ride the Carousel at Crandon Park Amusement Center

The Crandon Park Family Amusement Center has a restored 1949 carousel that operates on weekends and holidays. Kids can also roller skate at the old-fashioned outdoor rink, splash around in the spray fountain, and climb the marine animal sculptures in the playground area.

![A Charming Brick House Near The Cape Flo 3](/images/blog/2029/06/5-family-friendly-activities-in-key-biscayne/a-charming-brick-house-near-the-cape-flo-3.webp)


The amusement center sits right on the beach, so you can pair a carousel ride with a swim. Crandon Beach itself has calm, shallow water that works well for younger kids, and the beach is backed by palm trees and picnic shelters with grills. Lifeguards are on duty during peak hours.

If your kids are a little older, rent a kayak and paddle through the mangroves on the north end of the park.

## 2. Climb the Lighthouse at Bill Baggs Cape Florida State Park

Bill Baggs State Park covers the southern tip of Key Biscayne and centers around the Cape Florida Lighthouse, built in 1825. Free guided tours run at 10 AM and 1 PM, Thursday through Monday. The climb is 109 spiral steps, and the views from the top stretch across the Atlantic, Biscayne Bay, and the Stiltsville houses standing on stilts in the water.

Beyond the lighthouse, the park has over a mile of sandy beach, hiking and biking trails, a playground, and a youth camping area. Two restaurants on-site, Lighthouse Cafe and Boaters Grill, serve Cuban food, sandwiches, and cold drinks so you do not have to pack lunch.

The park is open from 8 AM until sunset every day. Entry is $8 per vehicle.

## 3. Explore the Biscayne Nature Center

The Marjory Stoneman Douglas Biscayne Nature Center sits at the north end of Crandon Park. The solar-powered building has nature exhibits, a lab classroom, and an audiovisual room. Staff lead guided programs where kids can wade into the seagrass beds and handle sea stars, horseshoe crabs, and other marine animals.

The center also runs Eco-Adventure snorkeling tours on the fossilized reef just offshore. These work best for kids who are comfortable in the water and at least 8-10 years old.

For younger children, the nature trails around the center wind through coastal hammock and mangrove habitat. You will see herons, iguanas, and if you are lucky, a manatee in the shallows.

## 4. Spend an Afternoon at Village Green Park

Village Green Park is the community gathering spot on Crandon Boulevard. The park has a large playground, a dog park split by size, and a field with AstroTurf used for soccer, flag football, field hockey, and lacrosse.

On weekends, the park fills up with youth sports leagues and families having picnics under the shade trees. There is a century-old African baobab tree on the grounds that is worth finding.

The Key Biscayne Community Center is right next door, with a swimming pool, activity rooms, and a full schedule of programs for kids of all ages. If you are new to the island, signing up for a community center program is one of the fastest ways to meet other families.

## 5. Bike the Island

Key Biscayne is flat, compact, and has dedicated bike paths that make it safe for family rides. You can rent bikes near Crandon Park or the Village and ride the full length of the island, from the Rickenbacker Causeway entrance down to Bill Baggs State Park, in about 30 minutes at a relaxed pace.

![Stunning View Of A Lighthouse Amidst A C 2](/images/blog/2029/06/5-family-friendly-activities-in-key-biscayne/stunning-view-of-a-lighthouse-amidst-a-c-2.webp)


The Rickenbacker Causeway itself is a popular cycling route with views of Biscayne Bay and the Miami skyline. On weekends, the causeway is packed with cyclists. The ride from the mainland to Key Biscayne is roughly 4 miles each way, with a bridge climb that gives older kids a workout and a great view.

For a shorter loop, ride through the residential streets in the Village. The sidewalks are wide, the traffic is slow, and you will pass several small parks along the way.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Key Biscayne good for families with young children?

Yes. The island has low traffic, walkable streets, excellent parks, and top-rated schools. The beach access alone makes it one of the best family neighborhoods in the Miami area.

### How do I get to Key Biscayne?

The only road access is via the Rickenbacker Causeway from the mainland. There is a toll of $2.25 each way for vehicles. Cyclists and pedestrians cross free of charge.

### Are the beaches on Key Biscayne safe for kids?

Crandon Beach has calm, shallow water and lifeguards during peak hours, making it one of the safest family beaches in South Florida. Bill Baggs State Park beach does not have lifeguards, so keep a closer eye on younger swimmers there.
