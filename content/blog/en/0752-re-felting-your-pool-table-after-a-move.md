# Re-Felting Your Pool Table After a Move

A move is one of the most common reasons pool table owners end up re-felting their table. Even when the felt is removed carefully during disassembly, the process of pulling staples, rolling the cloth, transporting it, and stretching it back into place takes a toll. If your felt was already a few years old before the move, re-felting during reassembly is often the smartest investment you can make.

## When Re-Felting Makes Sense

Not every move requires new cloth. Here's how to decide:

![A Man Setting Up Billiard Balls On A Poo 2](/images/blog/2027/04/re-felting-your-pool-table-after-a-move/a-man-setting-up-billiard-balls-on-a-poo-2.webp)


**Re-felt if:**
- Your current felt is more than 3-4 years old with regular use
- There are visible worn spots, thin areas, or pilling
- The cloth has tears, even small ones (they'll grow)
- You notice stains, moisture damage, or any musty smell from Miami's humidity
- The felt stretched during removal and won't pull tight again
- You want to upgrade from woolen to worsted cloth

**Keep your existing felt if:**
- It's less than 2 years old and shows no wear
- The removal went cleanly with no tears or stretching
- The cloth is premium worsted (Simonis, Championship Tour Edition) in good condition
- You're planning to re-felt on a set schedule anyway and have time left

## Choosing Your New Felt

If you decide to re-felt, you'll pick between two main categories:

### Woolen Felt

Woolen cloth is the standard for home game rooms. It's thicker, more durable for casual play, and costs $150-$300 for a full table. The downside is that woolen felt pills over time (those little fuzz balls that collect on the surface), and balls roll slower. Popular brands include Championship Invitational and Teflon-treated options that resist spills.

### Worsted Cloth

Worsted cloth is what you'll find on tournament tables and in serious players' homes. Simonis 860 is the industry standard. Worsted cloth is thinner, faster, and doesn't pill. Balls roll truer and more predictably. The cost is higher ($300-$600 for the cloth), and installation requires more skill because the thinner material shows every imperfection in the slate underneath.

### Color Options

Green is traditional, but Miami game rooms often go with tournament blue, burgundy, or camel. We've installed every color in the book. Keep in mind that lighter colors show chalk marks and stains more easily, which matters in a humid climate where you might be playing with the patio doors open.

## The Re-Felting Process

Re-felting happens during reassembly, after the slate is installed and leveled. Here's what our crew does:

1. **Prepare the slate**: The surface is cleaned thoroughly. Any old adhesive, wax residue, or debris is removed. The slate seams are filled with fresh beeswax and scraped perfectly smooth.

2. **Cut the cloth**: The new felt is cut to size with extra material on all sides for wrapping under the slate edges. Proper cutting is critical. Too little overhang means the felt can't be pulled tight. Too much creates bulk under the rails.

3. **Apply the cloth**: For woolen felt, the cloth is stretched across the slate and stapled to the underside. The key is even tension across the entire surface. Pulls happen from the center outward to avoid wrinkles and bunching. For worsted cloth, many installers use a spray adhesive on the slate before laying the cloth, which provides a smoother surface.

4. **Pocket cuts**: The cloth is carefully cut at each pocket opening, folded under, and secured. Sloppy pocket cuts lead to fraying and balls catching on fabric edges.

5. **Rail cloth**: Each rail cushion gets its own cloth covering, stretched tight and stapled. Rails are reinstalled with properly torqued bolts.

6. **Final inspection**: We roll balls across the entire surface, checking for dead spots, wrinkles, or uneven areas. You should see consistent ball speed and straight tracking in every direction.

## Felt Care Tips for Miami

Once your new felt is installed, these habits will extend its life in South Florida's humid climate:

![A Row Of Empty Billiard Tables In A Diml 1](/images/blog/2027/04/re-felting-your-pool-table-after-a-move/a-row-of-empty-billiard-tables-in-a-diml-1.webp)


- Keep the room air-conditioned consistently. Temperature swings cause the cloth to expand and contract.
- Brush the table regularly with a proper billiard brush (always in one direction, toward the foot end).
- Cover the table when not in use. A fitted cover keeps dust, humidity, and pet hair off the surface.
- Keep drinks on a separate table. One spilled beer on worsted cloth means a permanent stain.
