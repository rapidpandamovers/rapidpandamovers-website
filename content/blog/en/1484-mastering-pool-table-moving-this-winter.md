A pool table is not a piece of furniture you can tip on its side and slide into a truck. A standard 8-foot table weighs between 700 and 1,000 pounds, with three-piece slate tops that can crack from a single bump. Moving one correctly requires full disassembly, careful transport, and precision reassembly. Winter in South Florida is actually a strong window for scheduling this kind of job, and here is what the process looks like from start to finish.

## Why Pool Tables Cannot Be Moved in One Piece

The slate bed is the heart of a pool table, and it is also the most fragile component. A standard three-piece slate set weighs 450-600 pounds total, and each piece must be transported flat and individually wrapped. Trying to move a fully assembled table risks:

- Cracking or chipping the slate
- Warping the frame from uneven pressure
- Damaging the felt beyond reuse
- Stripping the bolt holes that hold the rails

Professional movers disassemble the table into its components: rails, pockets, felt, slate pieces, legs, and frame. This is the only way to guarantee the table arrives at your new home in playing condition.

## The Disassembly Process Step by Step

### 1. Remove the pockets
Staple-mounted pockets come off with a staple remover. Bolt-mounted pockets require a socket wrench. Bag each set of hardware and label it.

### 2. Detach the rails
The rails bolt to the slate from underneath. Use a socket wrench to remove the bolts, and keep them organized. Rails are heavy but not fragile, so they can be wrapped in moving blankets and stacked.

### 3. Handle the felt carefully
If the felt is in good condition and you want to reuse it, remove the staples slowly and roll the felt rather than folding it. Creases in felt are permanent. If the felt is worn, plan to have your table recovered after the move. This is a good time to upgrade.

### 4. Remove the slate
This is the step that requires extra hands. Each slate piece weighs 150-200 pounds and must be lifted straight up off the frame, then carried flat. We wrap each piece individually in thick moving blankets with corner protectors and transport them standing on edge in the truck, braced so they cannot shift.

### 5. Disassemble the frame and legs
The frame comes apart with basic tools. Legs typically bolt on. Label everything.

## Why Winter Works Well for Pool Table Moves

December through February in South Florida offers practical advantages for this type of specialty move:

- **Lower humidity.** Miami's winter humidity drops into the 60-70% range compared to 80-90% in summer. Slate and felt both respond better to lower moisture levels during transport.
- **Comfortable working conditions.** Carrying 200-pound slate pieces in 95-degree heat adds risk for both the crew and the equipment. Mid-70s temperatures make the physical work safer.
- **Better scheduling availability.** While general moving stays busy in winter, specialty services like pool table moving tend to have more open slots in December and January.

## Reassembly and Leveling

Getting the table back together is where precision matters most. The frame must be level before the slate goes on. Each slate piece gets shimmed individually using a machinist's level, then the seams between pieces are filled with beeswax to create a smooth, continuous playing surface.

After the slate is set, the felt is stretched and stapled, the rails are reattached, and the pockets are mounted. A proper reassembly takes 1-2 hours. Rushing this step shows up every time a ball rolls toward an unintended pocket.

## FAQ

**How long does it take to move a pool table?**
Full disassembly takes about 1-1.5 hours, transport time varies by distance, and reassembly with leveling takes another 1-2 hours. Plan for a half-day process.

**Can you move a pool table without taking it apart?**
Not safely. The slate will crack, the frame will warp, and the felt will be ruined. Full disassembly is the standard for a reason.

**Should I replace the felt during a move?**
If the felt has visible wear, pilling, or stains, a move is the ideal time to re-felt. The felt comes off during disassembly anyway, so there is no extra labor for removal.
