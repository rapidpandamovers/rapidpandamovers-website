February packing in Miami benefits from dry season conditions, but proper technique still matters. Cardboard can still weaken in direct sun, tape adhesive holds better in moderate temperatures, and items left in a parked truck can still heat up on sunny afternoons.

## February Packing Strategies That Work

### Start Non-Essentials Early

![Close Up Of Hands Packing A Box Labeled  1](/images/blog/2028/02/packing-services-strategies-for-february-moves/close-up-of-hands-packing-a-box-labeled--1.webp)


Begin packing 4-6 weeks before moving day. Guest room items, seasonal decorations, books, and rarely-used kitchen items can all be boxed early. By moving day, you're only packing daily essentials.

### Pack in a Climate-Controlled Space

Even in February's milder weather, a closed garage or lanai can heat up in direct sun. Pack inside with AC running to keep cardboard sturdy and tape adhesive effective. This is especially important for delicate items.

### Protect Against Moisture

Wrap electronics in plastic before boxing to protect against dust and potential condensation from temperature changes between air-conditioned spaces and the outdoors. Use sealed plastic bins for items you want extra protection on.

### Time Your Full-Service Packing

If hiring professional packers, schedule them to finish the day before your move. This keeps boxes sealed and ready for an efficient morning start with the moving crew.

## February Packing Checklist

**4-6 weeks before:**
- [ ] Declutter and donate items you won't move
- [ ] Order packing supplies or schedule packing services
- [ ] Start packing non-essential rooms

**2 weeks before:**
- [ ] Pack most of the house except daily essentials
- [ ] Confirm packing service schedule if using professionals
- [ ] Photograph valuable items for insurance records

**Day before:**
- [ ] Pack remaining items except overnight bag
- [ ] Ensure boxes are sealed tightly
- [ ] Keep essential boxes separate and labeled
