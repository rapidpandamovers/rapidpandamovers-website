West Miami is one of the smallest incorporated cities in Miami-Dade County, covering roughly 0.6 square miles between Coral Way and the Tamiami Trail (SW 8th Street). It has no waterfront, no skyline, and no tourist attractions. What it does have is affordable housing relative to neighboring Coral Gables, a predominantly residential layout, and a community that has maintained its character since incorporation in 1947. If you are looking for a quiet pocket close to everything, here is the full picture.

## Why West Miami Works

### Location Between Two Major Corridors

![Captivating View Of Miami S Iconic Skyli 2](/images/blog/2029/09/your-ultimate-guide-to-moving-to-west-miami/captivating-view-of-miami-s-iconic-skyli-2.webp)


West Miami sits between Coral Way (SW 24th Street) to the south and the Tamiami Trail (US-41/SW 8th Street) to the north. Both roads provide direct east-west routes into Downtown Miami, Brickell, and Coral Gables. The Palmetto Expressway (SR 826) is accessible within minutes, connecting north to Hialeah and the airport, and south to Kendall and Homestead.

Commute times from West Miami:
- **Coral Gables:** 5 to 10 minutes
- **Downtown Miami/Brickell:** 15 to 20 minutes via Coral Way
- **Miami International Airport:** 15 minutes via SR 836
- **Florida International University (main campus):** 10 minutes west on the Tamiami Trail
- **Kendall:** 15 to 20 minutes south on the Palmetto

### Affordability Compared to Neighbors

West Miami offers lower housing costs than Coral Gables to the south and east. One-bedroom apartments range from $1,800 to $2,200 per month. Single-family homes, mostly 2 to 3 bedrooms built in the 1950s and 1960s, sell in the $400K to $650K range. For buyers priced out of Coral Gables but who want the same school access and commute, West Miami is the practical alternative.

### A Community with Roots

The city was incorporated in 1947 by four local businessmen who each invested $400 in bonds to launch the municipality. Post-World War II veterans took advantage of federal land incentives and built homes here, giving the city its residential foundation. Over the decades, the community has shifted to a predominantly Hispanic population (roughly 85 percent as of the 2020 Census), with a mix of Cuban, Colombian, and Nicaraguan families.

The population of about 7,200 residents in less than a square mile creates a density that keeps the local businesses alive. Everyone walks to the same grocery store, eats at the same restaurants, and sees the same faces at city events.

## Housing and Neighborhoods

West Miami does not have named neighborhoods in the way larger cities do. The entire city spans about 15 blocks north-to-south and 8 blocks east-to-west. What varies is proximity to the two main corridors:

### Near Coral Way (South Side)

Closer to Coral Gables, with slightly higher home values. Streets here are quieter, and some homes back up to Coral Gables residential areas. Walking distance to restaurants and shops along Coral Way.

### Near Tamiami Trail (North Side)

More commercial activity along SW 8th Street, including restaurants, auto shops, and small businesses. Housing is marginally more affordable. Closer to FIU and the cultural activity along the Tamiami Trail corridor.

### Central Residential Core

The blocks between SW 12th Street and SW 20th Street form the quiet center of the city. Mostly single-family homes with small yards, mature trees, and sidewalks. This is where the "small-town inside a metro" feeling is strongest.

## Schools and Services

**Schools:** West Miami is served by Miami-Dade County Public Schools. West Miami Middle School sits within city limits. For elementary and high school, students attend nearby schools in the Coral Gables or Flagami feeder patterns. Private options include several schools within a short drive in Coral Gables.

**Groceries:** A Publix and several Latin markets serve the area. Coral Gables adds Whole Foods and Trader Joe's within a 10-minute drive.

**Healthcare:** South Miami Hospital and Coral Gables Hospital are both within 10 to 15 minutes. Baptist Health and University of Miami Health System facilities are accessible via Coral Way.

**Parks:** West Miami Park on SW 62nd Avenue provides sports fields, a playground, and community event space. For larger green spaces, Coral Gables' parks and the Tropical Park complex in Kendall are close by.

## Moving to West Miami in September

September in South Florida means afternoon thunderstorms, high humidity, and the tail end of hurricane season. But it also means lower moving demand compared to summer, better scheduling flexibility, and potentially lower rates. If you time your move for a Tuesday or Wednesday morning in early September, you get the best of both worlds.

### What to Know About Moving Here

- **Street width:** West Miami's residential streets are standard width, but parked cars can narrow access. Reserve curb space on both ends of your move if possible.
- **Single-family layout:** Most homes have driveways and carports rather than garages, which simplifies truck access compared to gated condo buildings.
- **Compact city:** Moves within West Miami itself are short-distance, often under 2 miles. For moves into West Miami from other parts of Miami-Dade, the Palmetto Expressway and Coral Way provide direct routes.

### Is West Miami a good area for families?

Yes. The low crime rate, residential layout, proximity to Coral Gables schools, and affordable housing relative to neighboring cities make it a solid choice for families. The small size of the city means kids grow up knowing their neighbors.

### How does West Miami compare to Coral Gables?

West Miami offers similar location advantages at lower housing costs. Coral Gables has more upscale dining, larger homes, and its own school system. West Miami trades those amenities for a more affordable, no-frills residential experience.
