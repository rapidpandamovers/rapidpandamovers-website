Miami Shores has been called "The Village Beautiful" since it was established in 1932, and the name still fits. With about 11,500 residents, tree-canopied streets, and a Biscayne Bay waterfront, it is one of Miami-Dade County's most sought-after residential communities. Here is what you should know before your move.

## What Makes Miami Shores Stand Out

Miami Shores occupies roughly 2.8 square miles between North Miami and the Upper Eastside, with Biscayne Bay forming its eastern border. The village has its own government, police department, parks system, and distinct identity that separates it from the surrounding City of Miami.

![A Tall Modern Skyscraper Reflecting The  2](/images/blog/2029/11/your-ultimate-guide-to-moving-to-miami-shores/a-tall-modern-skyscraper-reflecting-the--2.webp)


What draws people here is straightforward: mature neighborhoods with well-maintained homes, good schools, walkable commercial areas, and bay access, all within 20 minutes of downtown Miami.

### Location and Commute

- **Downtown Miami**: About 20 minutes south via I-95 or Biscayne Boulevard
- **Miami Beach**: Roughly 20 minutes east via the Julia Tuttle Causeway
- **Fort Lauderdale**: About 35 minutes north on I-95
- **Miami International Airport**: 25 minutes west

Biscayne Boulevard (US-1) runs through the village, and I-95 is a few minutes west. For commuters, the proximity to major corridors makes Miami Shores practical despite being a quiet residential area.

## Neighborhoods and Housing

Miami Shores does not have named subdivisions in the traditional sense, but different areas have distinct characters:

### The Bayfront

The eastern section along Biscayne Bay features larger homes, many with direct water access. Bayfront Park sits along N. Bayshore Drive between NE 94th and 96th Streets, offering a playground, waterfront benches, and bay views. This is the premium area of the village.

### The Village Center (NE 2nd Avenue)

The commercial heart of Miami Shores stretches along NE 2nd Avenue from about 94th Street to 101st Street. Known as Village Place, this strip has locally owned restaurants, coffee shops, boutiques, and service businesses. Homes within walking distance of this corridor are popular with buyers who want everyday errands on foot.

### The Interior

The blocks between the bayfront and the western edge are the classic Miami Shores: single-family homes on generous lots with mature oak and banyan canopies. Architectural styles range from 1920s Mediterranean Revival to mid-century modern ranch homes.

### Housing Snapshot

Most of Miami Shores is single-family homes, with some condos and townhomes mixed in. The village enforces strict zoning and property maintenance standards, which keeps the streetscapes consistent and well-kept.

## Schools

Miami Shores offers strong school options, one of the main reasons families move here:

- **Miami Shores Elementary**: The village's public elementary school, consistently well-rated
- **Doctors Charter School of Miami Shores**: A public charter school serving K-8
- **St. Rose of Lima School**: A Catholic private school with a long local history
- **Miami Shores Montessori School**: Early childhood and elementary
- **Barry University**: A Catholic international university with a campus right in the village, named one of "America's Best Colleges" by Forbes

## Parks and Recreation

Miami Shores maintains 11 parks across the village:

- **Bayfront Park**: Waterfront green space with a playground, benches, and bay views
- **Miami Shores Aquatic Center**: Public pool complex open year-round
- **Brockway Memorial Library Park**: Green space adjacent to the public library
- **Miami Shores Country Club**: An 18-hole golf course with tennis courts, soccer fields, and baseball diamonds

The village runs recreation programs for all ages, from youth sports leagues to adult fitness classes and senior activities.

## Moving to Miami Shores: Practical Tips

### Village Move-In Requirements

Miami Shores has specific regulations for moving activities. Check with the village about:
- **Parking permits** for moving trucks on residential streets
- **Time restrictions** on moving activities (many villages restrict weekend and evening moves)
- **Tree protection** around the truck's path

### Property Maintenance Standards

Miami Shores is known for enforcing its property codes. Your new home should be brought up to standards quickly after move-in: lawn maintenance, exterior paint, and any code violations from the previous owner.

### HOA Considerations

Some sections of the village have homeowner associations with their own move-in procedures, including security deposits, elevator reservations, and required insurance certificates from your moving company.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Miami Shores a good place to raise a family?**
Yes. The village is known for its family-friendly atmosphere, strong public and private school options, safe neighborhoods, and active recreation programs.

**How far is Miami Shores from the beach?**
Miami Beach is about 20 minutes east via the Julia Tuttle Causeway. The village itself sits on Biscayne Bay, so waterfront access is available without crossing to the beach side.

**What is the cost of living in Miami Shores?**
Miami Shores is a mid-to-upper range community within Miami-Dade County. Home prices are higher than many inland areas but lower than waterfront communities like Coral Gables or Key Biscayne.

**Does Miami Shores have its own schools?**
The village is served by Miami-Dade County Public Schools, with Miami Shores Elementary as the primary public school. Several charter and private schools also operate within the village boundaries.
