Thinking about moving to Miami Shores this November? You're considering one of Miami-Dade County's most desirable communities. This fall guide will help you understand what makes Miami Shores special and how to plan your relocation.

## Why Choose Miami Shores?

Miami Shores stands out as one of Miami-Dade County's most appealing locations. Founded in 1932, this charming village sits along Biscayne Bay between NE 87th Street and NE 113th Street. The community offers a historic and bayfront atmosphere and is particularly known for beautiful Mediterranean Revival homes and community events at the Miami Shores Country Club.

![A Picturesque View Of Miami Skyline With 3](/images/blog/2025/11/making-miami-shores-your-new-home-a-practical-guide/a-picturesque-view-of-miami-skyline-with-3.webp)


The area attracts families, professionals, and retirees alike, thanks to its quality of life, convenient access to major employment centers, and excellent amenities.

### Location and Accessibility

One of Miami Shores's biggest advantages is its strategic location within Miami-Dade County. Residents enjoy easy access to I-95, US-1 (Biscayne Boulevard), and the Tri-Rail station at NE 79th Street. The village is just 10 minutes from Wynwood, 15 minutes from Downtown Miami, and 20 minutes from Miami Beach. Whether you're commuting to Brickell, heading to Surfside, or traveling for work, Miami Shores provides convenient connections.

### Community and Lifestyle

Miami Shores residents enjoy a strong sense of community. Barry University anchors the area's academic scene, while Bayshore Park and Miami Shores Aquatic Center provide recreation for families. The weekly farmers market at Village Hall (NE 2nd Avenue and 95th Street) brings neighbors together every Friday morning. The fall season is particularly vibrant, with the annual Tree Lighting Ceremony and holiday events at the Miami Shores Community Center.

## Neighborhoods to Consider

When planning your move to Miami Shores, several areas stand out for newcomers. The Grand Concourse area along NE 96th Street features walkable dining and shopping at spots like Mojo Donuts and Cafe Prima Pasta. The streets west of Biscayne Boulevard offer quiet, tree-lined blocks with classic 1930s and 1940s homes. The bayfront section east of NE 4th Avenue provides water views and larger properties.

### Choosing Your Perfect Spot

Consider these factors:

- **Proximity to work and schools**: Factor in your daily commute
- **Local amenities**: Parks, shopping, dining, and entertainment options
- **Property types**: Single-family homes, condos, townhouses, or apartments
- **Community vibe**: Family-oriented, young professional, or mixed demographics
