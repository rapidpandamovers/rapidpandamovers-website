Miami attracts the rich and famous, and their homes are as impressive as you'd expect. From Star Island mansions to South Beach penthouses, here are some of the most notable celebrity properties in the area.

## 1. Star Island Estates

This exclusive island, connected to Miami Beach by a single causeway off MacArthur Causeway, has housed Madonna, Diddy, and Gloria Estefan. The private security and limited access mean any moving crew needs advance clearance, background checks, and specialized equipment that won't damage the pristine landscaping. Most moves here happen during early morning hours to maintain privacy.

## 2. Versace Mansion (Casa Casuarina)

Gianni Versace's famous South Beach residence at 1116 Ocean Drive is now a boutique hotel, but its history draws visitors from around the world. The Mediterranean Revival mansion features 10 suites, the famous Thousand Mosaic Pool with 24-karat gold tiles, and original Versace furnishings. When the property changed hands, moving crews faced the challenge of transporting priceless artwork and antiques through South Beach's narrow streets.

## 3. Fisher Island Properties

Accessible only by ferry from the terminal at Government Cut, Fisher Island consistently ranks among America's wealthiest zip codes (33109). Moving to this exclusive community requires ferry reservations for trucks, often multiple trips, and coordination with the island's strict HOA rules. Summer moves need extra planning since the ferry schedule adjusts for afternoon thunderstorms common in Miami from May through October.

## 4. Coral Gables Mansions

The historic Mediterranean-style estates along streets like Coral Way and Alhambra Circle have housed celebrities including Sylvester Stallone and Madonna. Coral Gables' strict zoning requires moving trucks to avoid certain historic streets, and the city's tree canopy ordinances mean careful maneuvering around protected banyans and oaks. Many homes here feature intricate tilework and wrought iron that require white-glove handling during any relocation.

## 5. Miami Beach Penthouses

Luxury high-rises like the Faena House, Four Seasons Surf Club, and 321 Ocean attract A-list celebrities seeking panoramic Biscayne Bay and Atlantic Ocean views. Moving into these buildings requires 48-72 hour notice, elevator reservations (often at premium rates), certificates of insurance, and careful scheduling around other residents. The loading docks at some Collins Avenue towers can only accommodate standard-size trucks, requiring multiple trips for larger moves.

## 6. Indian Creek Island

Known as "Billionaire Bunker," this 300-acre private island in Biscayne Bay has just 41 homes and its own police force. Residents have included Tom Brady, Ivanka Trump, Jeff Bezos, and hedge fund billionaires. The single bridge entrance requires security clearance for all vehicles, and moving crews must pass background checks weeks in advance. The island's manicured golf course and waterfront docks mean logistics for yacht delivery of oversized items.

## 7. Key Biscayne Estates

This barrier island, accessible via the Rickenbacker Causeway, offers village-like privacy just 15 minutes from downtown Miami. Tennis legends like Andre Agassi and business executives have owned homes along Harbor Drive and Mashta Drive. Moving here means timing around the causeway's occasional closures for storms and scheduling deliveries outside of Crandon Park's heavy traffic on weekends.

## 8. Hibiscus Island Mansions

Connected to the Venetian Causeway between Miami and Miami Beach, Hibiscus Island offers waterfront living with quick access to South Beach nightlife. The guard-gated community requires advance moving permits, and the narrow streets mean careful planning for large truck access. Shakira and other celebrities have called this island home, drawn by the Biscayne Bay views and proximity to Design District restaurants.

## 9. La Gorce Island Properties

This private island off Miami Beach, near the La Gorce Country Club, has attracted celebrities including Cher, Billy Joel, and Ricky Martin. The island's winding streets and mature tropical landscaping create a resort-like atmosphere. Moving logistics here include coordinating with the 24-hour security gatehouse and working around the island's single-lane roads, which require careful scheduling to avoid blocking residents.

## 10. Bal Harbour Oceanfront Estates

These beachfront mansions north of Miami Beach offer direct Atlantic Ocean access with some of South Florida's widest private beaches. International celebrities and business moguls are drawn to the village's upscale shopping at Bal Harbour Shops and its quiet, residential character. Moving here requires working within strict village ordinances on truck sizes and timing, with most buildings requiring moves to complete before 5pm.
