Most people picture furniture and boxes when they think about hiring movers. But professional moving crews see far stranger cargo on a regular basis. Here are 10 items that actually end up on moving trucks more often than you would think.

## 1. Fully Stocked Aquariums

Fish tanks weighing hundreds of pounds need to be drained, have their fish temporarily housed in bags or buckets, and then be carefully transported without cracking the glass. The gravel, filters, and live coral all travel separately. One wrong bump can mean a shattered tank and a very expensive mess. In Miami, saltwater reef tanks are especially common and can be worth tens of thousands of dollars in livestock alone.

## 2. Backyard Chicken Coops

Urban farming has grown popular across South Florida, and that means coops need to move with their owners. The coop itself requires disassembly, but the real challenge is coordinating the timing so the chickens have shelter set up at the new address before they arrive.

## 3. Cremation Urns

This one catches new movers off guard, but it is extremely common. Families trust professional movers to transport urns containing the ashes of loved ones. These items receive the most careful handling of any item on the truck, often riding in the cab rather than the cargo area.

## 4. Tanning Beds

Home tanning beds are bulky, heavy, and loaded with fragile acrylic panels and UV bulbs. Disconnecting the electrical components, protecting the bed surface, and maneuvering a 300-pound unit through doorways requires a team that knows what they are doing.

## 5. Batting Cages and Sports Nets

Families with serious athletes sometimes install full batting cages, basketball hoops with concrete bases, or tennis ball machines in their backyards. Disassembling and transporting these setups takes time, but replacing them from scratch costs significantly more.

![Two Men Carrying A Green Sofa On A City  2](/images/blog/2029/07/10-surprising-things-people-pay-movers-to-haul/two-men-carrying-a-green-sofa-on-a-city--2.webp)


## 6. Safes and Vaults

A residential gun safe or fireproof document vault can weigh anywhere from 500 to 2,000 pounds. Moving one requires a heavy-duty dolly, sometimes a stair-climbing machine, and a crew that understands weight distribution. In South Florida, where hurricane preparedness drives safe ownership, these moves are a regular part of the job.

## 7. Home Saunas and Steam Rooms

Portable home saunas and custom-built steam rooms have gained popularity in recent years. The wooden panels, heating elements, and electrical components all need careful disassembly. Getting a cedar sauna down a narrow hallway without scratching the walls or the wood takes practice.

## 8. Antique Jukeboxes and Slot Machines

Collectors of vintage entertainment machines pay movers to handle items that are both heavy and mechanically delicate. A 1950s Wurlitzer jukebox can weigh over 300 pounds and contains a turntable, amplifier, and fragile glass panels that all need individual protection.

## 9. Koi Ponds (the Fish, Not the Pond)

Premium koi fish can be worth thousands of dollars each. When owners move, they hire movers to carefully net the fish, transport them in oxygenated bags or tanks, and help set up temporary holding at the new home while the pond is rebuilt. Miami's year-round warm weather makes koi keeping popular, and owners treat these fish like family.

![Two Movers In Uniform Carrying Boxes In  4](/images/blog/2029/07/10-surprising-things-people-pay-movers-to-haul/two-movers-in-uniform-carrying-boxes-in--4.webp)


## 10. Entire Home Bars

Custom-built home bars with granite countertops, built-in refrigeration, tap systems, and bottle displays require careful disassembly and transport. The countertop alone might weigh several hundred pounds, and the glassware collection that goes with it can fill a dozen boxes.

## Why Professional Handling Matters

Every item on this list shares something in common: moving it without experience risks damage, injury, or both. Professional movers have the equipment, training, and insurance to handle the unexpected.
