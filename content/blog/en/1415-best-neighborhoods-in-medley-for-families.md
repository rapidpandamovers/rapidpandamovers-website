Medley is unlike any other town in Miami-Dade County. With about 1,100 residents spread across 6 square miles, it is primarily an industrial hub that hosts over 1,800 businesses and draws a weekday daytime population of around 60,000 workers. If you are considering a family move here, the residential options are limited but distinct. Here is what you need to know.

## Understanding Medley's Layout

Medley was incorporated in 1949 and sits in the northwestern corner of Miami-Dade County, bordered by Hialeah Gardens to the east and Doral to the south. The town is dominated by warehouses, distribution centers, and manufacturing facilities. Companies like Sysco Foods Services of South Florida occupy massive campuses here, and Lincoln Property Company manages over 670,000 square feet of industrial warehousing across 36 acres.

![A Rustic Seaside Warehouse With Vintage  3](/images/blog/2029/09/best-neighborhoods-in-medley-for-families/a-rustic-seaside-warehouse-with-vintage--3.webp)


The residential area is concentrated in the southeastern corner of the town. This is a tight-knit pocket where neighbors live close to one another, creating a community feel that contrasts with the industrial surroundings.

## The Southeastern Residential Area

Nearly all of Medley's housing sits in this single residential zone. The housing stock is primarily a mix of:

- **Small to medium apartments**: Studio through two-bedroom units in low-rise complexes
- **Single-family homes**: Three and four-bedroom homes on modest lots
- **Multi-family housing**: A mix of owner-occupied and rental properties

The median age in Medley is 58.2 years, meaning the community skews older. About 87% of residents over age five primarily speak Spanish at home, reflecting the town's strong South American and Cuban heritage. For families moving in, this means your kids will grow up in a bilingual environment with strong cultural roots.

### What Families Find Here

The residential area offers a few practical advantages:

- **Affordable housing**: Medley's prices sit below the Miami-Dade County average
- **Low traffic at home**: The industrial traffic stays on the main corridors, keeping residential streets quiet after business hours
- **Proximity to jobs**: If you work in logistics, distribution, or manufacturing, your commute could be minutes rather than hours
- **Community feel**: With so few residents, neighbors actually know each other

### Honest Limitations

Families should understand the trade-offs:

- **No public schools within town limits**: Students are zoned for Miami-Dade County Public Schools in nearby Hialeah and Doral
- **Limited retail and dining**: You will drive to Doral, Hialeah, or Miami Lakes for groceries and restaurants
- **Industrial surroundings**: Truck traffic on NW 74th Street and Okeechobee Road is heavy during business hours
- **Small housing inventory**: When a home comes on the market, options are few

## Nearby Communities Worth Considering

Many families who work in Medley choose to live in adjacent towns that offer more residential amenities while keeping the commute short.

### Doral

Just south of Medley, Doral has exploded with family-friendly development. CityPlace Doral and Downtown Doral offer walkable shopping and dining. The schools are highly rated, and housing ranges from starter condos to large single-family homes.

### Hialeah Gardens

East of Medley, Hialeah Gardens provides a suburban feel with larger lots and more housing inventory. It is one of the more affordable areas in western Miami-Dade.

### Miami Lakes

North of Medley, Miami Lakes is a planned community with tree-lined streets, excellent parks, and a strong town center with shopping and dining along Main Street. Families consistently rate Miami Lakes schools among the area's best.

## Getting Around from Medley

Medley's location along the Palmetto Expressway (SR 826) and close to the Florida Turnpike makes commuting straightforward:

- **Downtown Miami**: About 25 minutes via the Palmetto to I-95
- **Miami International Airport**: 15 minutes south on NW 74th Street
- **Miami Beach**: 35 to 40 minutes depending on causeway traffic
- **Fort Lauderdale**: 30 minutes north via I-75 or the Turnpike

The town has no Metrorail station, so a car is essential.

## Moving to Medley in September

September falls within hurricane season, which runs through November. When planning your move:

- **Watch the forecast**: Tropical weather systems can develop quickly. Have a backup moving date.
- **Schedule for mornings**: Temperatures in the low 90s and afternoon thunderstorms make early starts practical
- **Plan around truck traffic**: Moving trucks on NW 74th Street mix with semi-trailers during business hours. Weekends and early mornings avoid the worst congestion.
- **Confirm building access**: If moving into an apartment complex in the residential area, confirm parking for the moving truck in advance

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Medley a good place for families?**
Medley works well for families who want affordable housing close to industrial employment centers. The trade-off is limited in-town amenities and schools in neighboring cities.

**Are there parks in Medley?**
The town has limited park space given its industrial focus. Families typically use parks in Doral, Hialeah Gardens, and Miami Lakes for recreation.

**What schools serve Medley residents?**
Medley children are zoned for Miami-Dade County Public Schools. The closest elementary, middle, and high schools are in Hialeah and Doral.

**How far is Medley from the beach?**
Miami Beach is about 35 to 40 minutes east, depending on traffic and which causeway you take.
