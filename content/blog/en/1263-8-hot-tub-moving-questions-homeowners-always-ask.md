# 8 Hot Tub Moving Questions Homeowners Always Ask

When homeowners in Miami find out they need to move a hot tub, the same questions come up every time. After years of handling [**Hot Tub Moving**](/hot-tub-moving) jobs across South Florida, here are the eight we hear most often, along with straight answers.

## 1. How Much Does It Cost to Move a Hot Tub?

For a local move within Miami-Dade County with ground-level access, expect $300 to $600. The price goes up if crane service is needed, if fence panels need removal, or if the tub is exceptionally large. Long-distance moves (Miami to Orlando, for example) can run $1,000 to $2,500. Always get a detailed quote based on your specific property layout rather than relying on a generic phone estimate.

## 2. Can I Move a Hot Tub Myself?

Technically, you can try. Realistically, you shouldn't. An empty acrylic hot tub weighs 400 to 900 pounds and is awkward to grip, lift, and maneuver. Without proper dollies, straps, and training, the risk of back injuries, dropped tubs, and property damage is high. Inflatable spas are the exception, as those you can handle on your own.

## 3. How Long Does the Move Take?

A straightforward local move with clear access takes about 2 to 3 hours from start to finish. That includes wrapping the tub, moving it through the yard, loading, driving, unloading, and placing it at the new location. Add time for difficult access, fence removal, or crane work. The prep (draining, electrical disconnection) happens before the movers arrive and is on your schedule.

## 4. Do I Need to Drain the Tub Before Moving?

Absolutely. A full hot tub can weigh 3,000 to 6,000 pounds. Even moving a partially filled tub is dangerous. Drain it completely, vacuum out residual water from the jets and footwell, and let it dry. Your tub should be bone dry when the crew shows up.

## 5. Will My Warranty Still Be Valid After Moving?

It depends on the manufacturer. Some warranty terms require that the tub be moved by a certified dealer or authorized mover. Others simply require that the move be done by a licensed, insured moving company. Check your warranty documentation before the move. If you can't find it, call the manufacturer with your model and serial number.

## 6. What If My Gate Is Too Narrow?

This is one of the most common issues in Miami backyards. If the gate is too narrow for the tub, there are three options: remove a fence panel temporarily, crane the tub over the fence, or (if the fence is yours to modify) widen the gate permanently. Most aluminum and vinyl fence panels can be removed and reinstalled within 30 minutes.

## 7. How Far in Advance Should I Book?

Two to three weeks is a good target for most of the year. During Miami's peak moving season (May through September), three to four weeks of lead time gives you better scheduling options. If you need a crane, book even earlier since crane operators have their own schedules to coordinate.

## 8. What Happens If the Tub Gets Damaged During the Move?

A reputable moving company carries liability insurance and cargo coverage that protects your spa during transport. Before hiring anyone, ask for proof of insurance and find out what their claims process looks like. Document the condition of your tub with photos before the move so you have a baseline if you need to file a claim.

## The One Question People Should Ask But Don't

"Can you do a site visit before quoting?" The answer from a good mover should be yes, or at minimum they should ask for detailed photos and measurements. The companies that quote a flat rate over the phone without seeing the property are the ones most likely to hit you with surprise charges on moving day.
