## Why May Requires Extra Planning for Labor-Only Moves

As Miami's wet season begins in May, afternoon thunderstorms become more common and humidity climbs. Temperatures reach the upper 80s and low 90s. These conditions matter for labor-only moves where crews are working outdoors at your truck. Timing your move for the morning hours helps avoid the worst heat and the typical 3-5 PM storm window.

## Key Tips for Labor Only Moving in May

### Book Early for the Busy Season

May kicks off the busy summer moving season as leases turn over and families relocate before the new school year. Book your labor-only crew at least 2 weeks ahead, especially for weekend dates.

### Have everything packed and the truck ready when movers arrive

This single step can save hours of stress and potential problems on moving day. Our experienced crews have seen what happens when this step is skipped.

### Consider the Weather

May brings the start of Miami's wet season with daily afternoon thunderstorms, temperatures in the upper 80s to low 90s, and high humidity. Schedule your move for morning hours (start by 8 AM) to finish before the typical afternoon storms roll in. Our crews bring tarps and waterproof materials to protect your items during loading.

### Prepare Your Belongings

Take time to inventory your items before the move. This is especially important for labor only moving, as proper documentation helps ensure everything arrives safely at your new location.

### How much does labor only moving cost in Miami?
Labor only moving in Miami typically costs $80-$120 per hour for a 2-person crew. Most apartment loads take 2-3 hours and house loads take 3-5 hours. You only pay for the time the crew is working.

### What should I have ready when the labor only crew arrives?
Have everything packed in boxes, furniture disassembled if possible, and your rental truck parked as close to the entrance as possible. The more prepared you are, the faster (and cheaper) the move goes.
