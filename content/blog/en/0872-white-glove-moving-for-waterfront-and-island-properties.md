# White Glove Moving for Waterfront and Island Properties

Miami's waterfront and island properties sit in some of the most desirable real estate in the country, and also some of the most logistically challenging to move into or out of. Whether it's a Star Island mansion, a Fisher Island estate, or a waterfront home on the Venetian Islands, these moves come with obstacles that mainland relocations simply don't have.

## Access Challenges Unique to Island Properties

Fisher Island is only accessible by ferry. Star Island has a guarded gatehouse and a single bridge with weight restrictions. The Venetian Islands connect to the mainland via causeways with limited truck parking. Each of these access points creates logistical constraints that must be planned around, not discovered on moving day. Our white glove team coordinates with island security, ferry operators, and bridge authorities well in advance. For Fisher Island, we schedule truck space on the ferry and plan the entire move around departure times.

## Waterfront Loading and Unloading

Many waterfront homes have long driveways, narrow approaches, or seawall-adjacent entrances that limit how close a truck can get to the front door. Some properties can only be accessed from the water side for large items. In these cases, we coordinate with marine transport services to bring furniture and crated items by barge or flat-bottom boat. The crew stages items on the dock and carries them into the home using hand-carry methods designed for uneven surfaces.

## Salt Air and Humidity Protection

Saltwater proximity accelerates corrosion on metal finishes, hardware, and electronics. The salt content in the air along Biscayne Bay and the Atlantic coast is significantly higher than inland areas like Pinecrest or Palmetto Bay. During a waterfront move, we use additional moisture barriers on metal components, wrap electronics in anti-corrosion packaging, and minimize the time items spend exposed to outside air during loading and unloading.

## Protecting the Property

Waterfront homes often feature imported stone driveways, custom landscaping, dock structures, and outdoor living areas that sit in the moving path. Our crew protects these surfaces with plywood sheeting over driveways, padded runners across decking, and careful routing that avoids irrigation systems and landscape features. Damage to a custom coral stone driveway or a teak dock can cost thousands to repair.

## Elevated Structures and Flood-Zone Construction

Many waterfront properties in Miami are built on raised foundations or stilts to comply with flood zone requirements. This means stairs, ramps, and sometimes exterior elevators are the only way to get furniture inside. Moving a 600-pound armoire up an exterior staircase requires planning, equipment, and a crew trained in elevated carry techniques. We survey these structures during the pre-move walk-through and bring the specific equipment each access point demands.

## Security and Privacy

Island and waterfront homeowners in Miami often prioritize privacy. Gated entrances, security cameras, and private security teams are standard. Our white glove team works within existing security protocols, provides crew identification in advance, and uses unmarked vehicles when requested. We coordinate arrival and departure times with on-site security to avoid any disruption to the homeowner's routine or their neighbors.

## Coordination With Property Staff

Luxury waterfront properties frequently have house managers, groundskeepers, and maintenance staff. Our move manager works directly with these teams to ensure the move integrates smoothly with the property's daily operations. This includes coordinating around scheduled deliveries, pool maintenance, landscaping work, and any other activities that might conflict with the moving timeline.
