Kendall stretches across a large swath of southwestern Miami-Dade County, and one of its biggest draws for new residents is the amount of green space. From 175-acre nature preserves to neighborhood pocket parks, there is something for every type of outdoor activity. Here are 8 parks and outdoor spaces worth visiting after your move.

## 1. Kendall Indian Hammocks Park

**Address**: 11395 SW 79th Street, Miami, FL 33173
**Size**: 175 acres

![Captivating Night View Of Miami Skyline  5](/images/blog/2029/09/8-best-parks-and-outdoor-spaces-in-kendall/captivating-night-view-of-miami-skyline--5.webp)


This is Kendall's crown jewel. The park sits on land that contains one of urban Miami's last stands of tropical hardwood hammock, the **Snapper Creek Glade**. A 32-acre nature preserve within the park protects this habitat, and boardwalk trails wind through it.

Beyond the nature side, the park has **36 holes of disc golf**, three lighted softball fields, a soccer area, and a 14,000-square-foot skate park with ramps, rails, and obstacles for all skill levels. A universally accessible playground opened in recent years, connecting kids of all abilities to the natural surroundings. Plan to spend a full morning here.

## 2. A.D. "Doug" Barnes Park

**Address**: 3401 SW 72nd Avenue, Miami, FL 33155
**Size**: 65 acres

A.D. Barnes Park is a pocket of old Florida tucked between subdivisions. The park preserves 15 acres of native tropical hardwood hammock and pine rockland habitats. A fishing lake stocked with largemouth bass sits at the center, surrounded by jogging trails and picnic shelters.

The park also has a heated swimming pool, a large playground, and covered pavilions available for reservation. Weekday mornings are the quietest time to visit, especially if you want the jogging trails to yourself.

## 3. Tropical Park

**Address**: 7900 SW 40th Street, Miami, FL 33155
**Size**: Nearly 300 acres

Tropical Park is one of the largest parks in Miami-Dade County. Built on the site of a former horse track that closed in the 1970s, it was converted to a county park in 1979. The park hosts youth football leagues, an equestrian show ring, a boxing program, and a community center.

For casual visitors, there are paved walking and jogging paths, open fields, a large playground, and multiple picnic areas. Tropical Park is also the home of the annual **Santa's Enchanted Forest** holiday carnival during November and December.

## 4. West Kendall District Park

**Address**: 15500 SW 128th Street, Miami, FL 33196
**Size**: 164 acres

![Explore A Tranquil Boardwalk Winding Thr 2](/images/blog/2029/09/8-best-parks-and-outdoor-spaces-in-kendall/explore-a-tranquil-boardwalk-winding-thr-2.webp)


West Kendall District Park anchors the western end of the Kendall area. The **6.5-acre dog park** is divided into separate zones for large dogs (over 35 lbs) and small dogs, with play equipment, drinking fountains, and water spray features.

Beyond the dog park, you will find soccer fields, basketball courts, a large playground, and a walking trail that loops around the entire property. The park is newer than many Kendall options and tends to be less crowded on weekday mornings.

## 5. The Falls Shopping Center Greenway

**Address**: 8888 SW 136th Street, Miami, FL 33176

Not a traditional park, but The Falls is an open-air shopping and dining complex built around natural waterfalls and tropical landscaping. The walking paths through the center feel more like a botanical garden than a mall. If you are looking for a shaded stroll followed by lunch, this is the spot.

The greenway is pet-friendly during non-peak hours, and benches along the water features provide a quiet break from shopping. It is a good option when you want to be outdoors without committing to a full park visit.

## 6. Kendall Green Park

**Address**: 14975 SW 80th Street, Miami, FL 33183

Kendall Green is a neighborhood park nestled between several lakes. It features a butterfly garden along the lakeside, a playground with swings and slides, and a small outdoor fitness station. The dog park section has separate areas for larger and smaller dogs, each with water stations.

This park flies under the radar because of its residential location, but the lake views and butterfly garden make it worth a visit. Bring a book and a blanket for a quiet afternoon.

## 7. Larry and Penny Thompson Memorial Park

**Address**: 12451 SW 184th Street, Miami, FL 33177
**Size**: 270 acres

![Photograph Of An Empty Swing Set In A Qu 3](/images/blog/2029/09/8-best-parks-and-outdoor-spaces-in-kendall/photograph-of-an-empty-swing-set-in-a-qu-3.webp)


Located on Kendall's southern edge, this park is one of the few in Miami-Dade County with a **campground** (240 RV sites and tent camping). The freshwater swimming lake, complete with a sandy beach, is open year-round and is one of the few non-ocean swimming spots in the area.

There is also a half-mile nature trail, picnic shelters, and a large playground. The park borders the Zoo Miami property, so you may spot peacocks wandering through the grounds. September is a good time to camp here because the summer crowds have thinned.

## 8. Ron Ehmann Park

**Address**: 11255 SW 84th Street, Miami, FL 33173

Ron Ehmann is a community-focused park with lighted tennis courts, basketball courts, baseball fields, and a playground. The park also has a small community pool and hosts seasonal youth sports leagues.

It is one of the more centrally located parks in Kendall and works well for quick weekday visits. The lighted courts mean you can play tennis or basketball after sunset, which is a plus during the hotter months when midday outdoor activity is brutal.

## Tips for Visiting Kendall Parks in September

- **Arrive before 9 AM or after 5 PM** to avoid the worst of the heat. September temperatures still reach the high 80s to low 90s by midday.
- **Bring mosquito repellent**, especially to parks with water features or natural hammocks. September's afternoon rains keep standing water around.
- **Check the Miami-Dade Parks Department website** for event schedules. Free yoga, boot camp, and tai chi classes rotate through many of these parks.
- **Weekday mornings** are the least crowded. Weekend mornings see sports leagues and large family gatherings.
