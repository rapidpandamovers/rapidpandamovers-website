Miami Springs sits five minutes east of Miami International Airport and feels nothing like the rest of Miami-Dade County. There are no high-rises, no bottle-service clubs, and no tourist crowds. Instead, you get Pueblo Revival homes from the 1920s, a canopy of mature oaks, and neighbors who organize golf cart parades during the holidays. If that sounds like your speed, here is what to know before moving in.

## Why People Choose Miami Springs

Aviation pioneer Glenn Curtiss founded this city in 1926 as a planned community called Country Club Estates. That original vision still shapes the place today: tree-lined residential streets, a public golf course in the center of town, and strict architectural standards that keep the historic character intact. Roughly 800 properties fall within the designated historic district.

![Classic Vintage Car Parked Outside Park  2](/images/blog/2029/09/moving-to-miami-springs-what-you-need-to-know/classic-vintage-car-parked-outside-park--2.webp)


The city covers about 3 square miles and sits between the Palmetto Expressway (SR 826) to the west and Hialeah to the north. The Dolphin Expressway (SR 836) provides direct access to Downtown Miami and Brickell in 20 to 25 minutes. Coral Gables is 15 minutes south. Miami Beach runs 30 to 35 minutes across the causeways.

### Airport Proximity: Benefit and Trade-Off

The biggest draw for many residents is the short commute to MIA. Airline crews, airport managers, and travel industry professionals fill many Miami Springs homes. The flip side is flight noise, especially north of Curtiss Parkway. If you are noise-sensitive, spend time at a property during peak flight hours before signing anything.

### Community Events and the Curtiss Legacy

Miami Springs celebrated its 100th anniversary in 2026, and the city leans into that history. The Glenn Curtiss Vintage Motorcycle and Car Show draws crowds to The Circle on Curtiss Parkway each November. Seasonal events at the golf course, block parties, and a well-attended Halloween trick-or-treating route along Curtiss Parkway keep the small-town calendar full year-round.

## Neighborhoods and Housing Options

### Historic District (Central Miami Springs)

This is the core of the city along Westward Drive, Hunting Lodge Drive, and Curtiss Parkway. Homes here are 1920s to 1940s builds in Spanish Revival and Pueblo styles with original tile work, barrel-tile roofs, and mature landscaping. Expect 2 to 3 bedrooms and prices ranging from $450K to $750K depending on condition and lot size. Historic designation limits exterior modifications but keeps the streetscape cohesive.

### Country Club Section (East Side)

Bordering the Miami Springs Golf and Country Club, this area offers larger lots, mid-century ranch homes, and easier street parking. Homes run $500K to $850K. The golf course is public and has hosted events since the Curtiss era, so you do not need a membership to play.

### North Miami Springs

Closer to Hialeah with smaller lots and more affordable options in the $350K to $500K range. Less historic architecture but still walkable to Westward Drive shops and restaurants.

### Rental Market

Apartment buildings are scarce here. Most rentals are single-family homes or duplexes, running $2,200 to $3,500 per month for a 3-bedroom house.

## Schools, Groceries, and Healthcare

**Schools:** Miami Springs Senior High has a strong aviation magnet program, fitting for an airport town. Miami Springs Elementary and Springview Elementary serve younger students. Blessed Trinity Catholic School is a popular private option.

![Explore This Modern Miami Residence With 4](/images/blog/2029/09/moving-to-miami-springs-what-you-need-to-know/explore-this-modern-miami-residence-with-4.webp)


**Groceries:** The Publix on Curtiss Parkway handles everyday shopping. For Whole Foods or Trader Joe's, head 15 minutes to Coral Gables. Sedano's in Hialeah covers Latin grocery needs.

**Healthcare:** No hospital sits within city limits. Hialeah Hospital is 10 minutes north. Jackson Memorial and the University of Miami Health System are 20 minutes south on US-1.

**Dining:** Westward Drive anchors the local food scene with spots like Sergio's for Cuban food and Sushi Maki. For more options, most residents drive to Coral Gables or Doral.

## Moving to Miami Springs in September

September sits at the tail end of hurricane season but also marks the start of lower moving demand in South Florida. That means better scheduling flexibility and potentially lower rates. Temperatures still hit the upper 80s with afternoon thunderstorms, so morning moves work best.

### Logistics to Plan For

- **Street parking:** Many historic homes lack garages or have narrow driveways. Confirm where the moving truck can park before moving day.
- **Tree coverage:** The mature oak and banyan canopy can make large truck access difficult on some streets. Our crews scout routes ahead of time.
- **HOA and historic district rules:** Some sections have architectural guidelines that affect temporary dumpster placement and signage during a move.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does it cost to move to Miami Springs?

A local move within Miami-Dade typically runs $400 to $1,200 depending on home size and services needed. Interstate moves for airline employees relocating to Miami vary more widely. [Request a free quote](/quote) for your specific situation.

### Does Miami Springs require a moving permit?

The city does not require moving permits, but some homes have HOA rules about truck parking duration and dumpster placement. Check with your HOA or the property seller before scheduling.

### Is airport noise a problem?

It depends on the block. Homes north of Curtiss Parkway get more flight noise. Homes in the Country Club section on the east side are generally quieter. Visit the property at different times of day to judge for yourself.
