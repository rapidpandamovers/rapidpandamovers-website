Every move has a moment where you need something that is already on the truck, sealed in a box, and buried under furniture. These five items should stay with you at all times on moving day. Pack them in a bag you carry personally or keep them in your car.

## 1. Important Documents

Your IDs, passports, birth certificates, lease or closing papers, insurance documents, and vehicle registration should never go in a moving box. If the truck is delayed, rerouted, or anything goes wrong, you need these on hand.

![A Person Handling A Moving Box With A Ca 4](/images/blog/2029/07/5-must-have-items-for-a-successful-moving-day/a-person-handling-a-moving-box-with-a-ca-4.webp)


Pack them in a waterproof folder or zip-lock bag. If you are moving in South Florida during summer, rain can hit without warning, and a soaked lease agreement is not something you want to deal with on day one.

**Also include:**
- Copies of your moving contract and inventory list
- Contact numbers for your movers, landlord, and utility companies
- Cash for tips, tolls, or unexpected expenses (not every vendor takes cards on short notice)

## 2. Medications and First Aid

Current prescriptions, over-the-counter pain relievers, allergy medication, band-aids, and any medical devices you might need in the next 24 to 48 hours stay with you. This goes double for medications that need specific storage temperatures. In the July heat of Miami, a prescription left on a moving truck for hours can be ruined.

**Pack a small first aid kit with:**
- Pain relievers (ibuprofen, acetaminophen)
- Band-aids and antiseptic wipes
- Antihistamines for dust and allergens (you will encounter both during a move)
- Sunscreen and insect repellent if your move involves outdoor loading

## 3. Phone Chargers and Power Banks

A dead phone on moving day means missed calls from your movers about arrival time, missed utility activation confirmations, and no access to GPS directions to your new place. Carry a charger in your bag and keep a fully charged power bank with you.

**Why this matters more than you think:**
- Movers may call to confirm the address or ask about parking
- Utility companies often send activation codes via text
- You may need to share your location with friends or family helping with the move
- Building managers sometimes call about elevator reservations or loading dock availability

## 4. Basic Toolkit

A screwdriver set, pliers, box cutter, tape, and a hammer cover about 90% of last-minute needs at both locations. You will use these more than you expect.

**Common uses on moving day:**
- Removing doors from hinges to fit furniture through tight hallways
- Cutting zip ties and tape on boxes that need immediate access
- Tightening loose hardware on furniture that shifted during transport
- Reattaching curtain rods, outlet covers, or switch plates at the new place
- Reassembling bed frames and desks

Keep the toolkit in your car, not in a box. When you need a screwdriver at 8 PM after the movers have left, you do not want to dig through 40 boxes to find one.

## 5. Cleaning Supplies

Paper towels, all-purpose cleaner, trash bags, and a small broom handle the mess that every move generates. You will need them at both the old place (final walkthrough cleaning) and the new place (dust and debris from moving boxes and furniture).

![From Above Of Utility Knife On Heap Of C 1](/images/blog/2029/07/5-must-have-items-for-a-successful-moving-day/from-above-of-utility-knife-on-heap-of-c-1.webp)


**Pack a small cleaning kit with:**
- Roll of paper towels
- All-purpose spray cleaner
- Trash bags (you will generate more garbage than you expect from packing materials)
- Disinfecting wipes for counters, doorknobs, and light switches at the new place
- A hand towel and bar of soap (the bathroom may not be set up yet)

If you are in Miami, add extra water bottles to your car. Moving in summer heat with no running water set up at the new place is miserable.

## Bonus: The "Open First" Box

Beyond these five personal items, label one box "OPEN FIRST" and load it last on the truck (so it comes off first). Fill it with:

- Toilet paper
- Hand soap
- Paper plates and cups
- Basic snacks and bottled water
- One change of clothes per person
- Sheets and pillows for the first night
- A nightlight or flashlight

This box turns a chaotic first night into a manageable one.

## FAQ

**What should I never put on the moving truck?**
Keep documents, medications, jewelry, cash, car keys, laptop, and phone chargers with you. Anything irreplaceable or needed within 24 hours stays in your personal vehicle or bag.

**How much cash should I have on moving day?**
Bring $100 to $200 in small bills. Movers typically receive tips of $20 to $50 per crew member for a standard local move. You may also need cash for food, tolls, or parking meters.

**Should I pack a bag for each family member?**
Yes. Each person should have a small bag with a change of clothes, toiletries, medications, phone charger, and any personal items they will need before boxes are unpacked.
