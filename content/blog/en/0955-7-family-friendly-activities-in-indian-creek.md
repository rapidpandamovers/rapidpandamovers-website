Moved to Indian Creek with kids? This exclusive island community sits at the center of Miami-Dade's best family attractions. While Indian Creek itself is a private residential haven, you're minutes from world-class beaches, museums, and parks. Here are seven spots your family will love.

## 1. Surfside Community Center Pool Complex

**9301 Collins Avenue, Surfside** (5 minutes from Indian Creek)

![A Mother And Daughter Label A Cardboard  4](/images/blog/2028/05/7-family-friendly-activities-in-indian-creek/a-mother-and-daughter-label-a-cardboard--4.webp)


This oceanfront facility offers a recreational pool, dedicated children's pool with water slide, jacuzzi, and snack bar. Surfside residents get priority access, but day passes are available. The shallow children's pool makes it perfect for toddlers, while older kids love the water slide.

## 2. Oleta River State Park

**3400 NE 163rd Street, North Miami Beach** (15 minutes from Indian Creek)

Florida's largest urban park spans 1,000 acres along Biscayne Bay. Rent kayaks or paddleboards for calm-water family adventures. The park features miles of paved biking trails, sandy beaches for picnics, and nature centers with wildlife exhibits. Pack a picnic and spend the whole day exploring.

## 3. Phillip and Patricia Frost Museum of Science

**1101 Biscayne Boulevard, Downtown Miami** (20 minutes from Indian Creek)

![Father And Children Packing Luggage Toge 2](/images/blog/2028/05/7-family-friendly-activities-in-indian-creek/father-and-children-packing-luggage-toge-2.webp)


Kids get hands-on with science through interactive exhibits. The three-level aquarium showcases sharks and sea creatures, while the 250-seat planetarium runs shows about Earth and space. Annual family memberships pay for themselves after two visits and include guest passes.

## 4. 96th Street Park

**9580 Bay Drive, Surfside** (5 minutes from Indian Creek)

Surfside's main family park includes a playground, multipurpose sports field, and kayak launch on the bay. The playground equipment suits kids of various ages, and the open green space works great for soccer, frisbee, or just running around.

## 5. Miami Children's Museum

**980 MacArthur Causeway, Watson Island** (15 minutes from Indian Creek)

Almost entirely hands-on, this museum lets kids run a grocery store, explore a makers space, and interact with marine tanks featuring South Florida wildlife. The piggy bank exhibit teaches basic money skills while keeping kids entertained for hours.

## 6. Haulover Beach Park and Skate Park

**10800 Collins Avenue, Bal Harbour** (10 minutes from Indian Creek)

![Young Child Sitting On Bed Packing A Sui 3](/images/blog/2028/05/7-family-friendly-activities-in-indian-creek/young-child-sitting-on-bed-packing-a-sui-3.webp)


The beach section offers calm waters and lifeguards, while the newer skate park and pump track keep older kids active. This facility is a collaboration between five municipalities including Bal Harbour and Surfside, so it's well-maintained and popular with local families.

## 7. Zoo Miami

**12400 SW 152nd Street, Miami** (35 minutes from Indian Creek)

Florida's largest zoo houses more than 3,000 animals in spacious, cageless exhibits grouped by geographic region. The splash playground cools kids off between animal exhibits, and the Zoofari Monorail gives tired legs a break while covering the 750-acre grounds.
