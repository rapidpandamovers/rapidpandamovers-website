The Roads is one of those Miami neighborhoods that locals love but visitors rarely hear about. Tucked between Brickell and Coconut Grove, this quiet residential pocket has kept its charm while the towers and traffic grew all around it.

## Why Choose The Roads?

The Roads was designed by Mary Brickell in the early 1920s as a pedestrian-friendly neighborhood with wide, tree-canopied streets, roundabouts planted with native tropical plants, and walkable blocks. A century later, that original design still defines the area.

![Tree-lined residential street with palm trees in a Miami neighborhood](/images/blog/2029/07/why-the-roads-is-one-of-miamis-best-kept-secrets/scenic-view-of-a-suburban-street-lined-w-3.webp)


The neighborhood is bounded roughly by SW 11th Street to the north, South Miami Avenue to the east, and SW 15th Road to the west. That puts it within walking distance of Brickell's restaurants, shops, and Metrorail station, but with a completely different feel. Where Brickell is glass towers and traffic, The Roads is shaded sidewalks and single-family homes.

### Location and Accessibility

The Roads sits in one of Miami's most convenient spots:

- **Brickell** is a 3-minute drive or a 10-minute walk north
- **Downtown Miami** and the courthouse district are just beyond Brickell
- **Coconut Grove** borders the neighborhood to the south
- **I-95** and **US-1** are both within a few minutes, connecting you to the rest of Miami-Dade County
- **Vizcaya Metrorail station** provides direct access to the rail system

### Community and Lifestyle

The Roads has a tight-knit community that feels more like a small town than part of a major city. Neighbors walk their dogs along the shaded streets, kids ride bikes through the roundabouts, and block parties pop up throughout the year. The Five Points intersection at Coral Way and SW 22nd Street is a local gathering point with longtime restaurants and shops.

## What Makes The Roads Special

### The Homes

The Roads is known for its residential architecture:

- **Mediterranean Revival homes** from the 1920s and 1930s with barrel-tile roofs and arched doorways
- **Spanish-style ranch houses** that make up much of the housing stock
- **Newer construction** including modern single-family homes and a few small condo buildings
- **Historic bungalows** on larger lots with mature tropical landscaping

Unlike many Miami neighborhoods that have gone all-in on high-rise development, The Roads has largely preserved its single-family character.

### Dining and Local Spots

The Roads has its own food scene, separate from the Brickell restaurant strip:

- **Mykonos** at Five Points has served Greek dishes for over 20 years
- **Moshi Moshi Sushi** is a neighborhood favorite for Japanese food
- **Perricone's Marketplace and Cafe** recently relocated to The Roads after decades in Brickell
- **Budare Bistro** offers traditional Venezuelan fare

### Cost of Living

The Roads is not cheap, but it offers better value than waterfront Brickell or Key Biscayne. Single-family homes here hold their value well thanks to the neighborhood's character, walkability, and location. A local realtor who knows the block-by-block differences within The Roads can help you find the right fit.

## Moving to The Roads in Summer

Summer is a solid time to move. Expect hot, humid days with afternoon thunderstorms, so aim for a morning move-in.

### Timing Your Move

- **Parking**: Street parking in The Roads is mostly residential. Coordinate loading zone needs with your moving company in advance
- **Tree canopy**: The large trees that shade the streets can also limit access for tall moving trucks on some blocks. Your movers should scout the route beforehand
- **Weather**: Schedule your move for early morning to beat the afternoon heat and storms
- **Condo rules**: If moving into one of the few condo buildings, reserve the elevator and check HOA move-in requirements

## Local Resources for New Residents

- **Schools**: Southside Elementary (public), several private options including St. Kieran Catholic School
- **Parks**: Simpson Park (a rare tropical hardwood hammock), Alice Wainwright Park near the bay
- **Healthcare**: Baptist Health South Florida, Mercy Hospital, and clinics along Coral Way
- **Shopping**: Brickell City Centre and Merrick Park in Coral Gables are both a short drive away

## FAQ

### Is The Roads a good neighborhood to live in?
Yes. The Roads is one of Miami's most desirable residential neighborhoods, known for tree-lined streets, walkability to Brickell, and a strong sense of community. It is especially popular with families and professionals who want a quieter alternative to high-rise living.

### How far is The Roads from Brickell?
The Roads borders Brickell directly. Most locations within The Roads are a 3-minute drive or 10-minute walk from Brickell's main corridor along Brickell Avenue.

### What type of homes are in The Roads?
The Roads is primarily single-family homes, mostly Mediterranean Revival and Spanish-style ranch houses from the 1920s and 1930s. There are also some newer builds and a small number of condo buildings.
