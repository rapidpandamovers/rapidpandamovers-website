Weekends in Key Biscayne offer endless possibilities, especially in April. Whether you prefer relaxing brunches, outdoor adventures, or cultural experiences, here's how to make the most of your time off in your new neighborhood.

## 1. Find Your Farmers Market Routine

The Key Biscayne Community Center hosts a farmers market on Saturday mornings where locals gather for fresh produce, artisan goods, and that island-community vibe. Beyond the shopping, these markets are where you'll meet neighbors and discover what your new community cares about. The mild April weather makes it perfect for strolling through the stalls.

![Brightly Colored Clown Game Machines At  1](/images/blog/2025/05/5-weekend-activities-to-enjoy-in-key-biscayne/brightly-colored-clown-game-machines-at--1.webp)


## 2. Leave Earlier Than You Think for Beach Days

Crandon Park and Bill Baggs Cape Florida State Park are right on the island, but parking fills up fast on weekends. If you're heading to these pristine beaches, arrive by 8am or prepare for a parking headache. The upside? April mornings in Key Biscayne are warm enough for swimming but not yet at peak summer heat.

## 3. Make Brunch Reservations Thursday Night

Popular spots like Lightkeepers and the Rusty Pelican book up fast on weekends. If you're planning to explore the Key Biscayne brunch scene, reserve by Thursday or prepare for long waits. The Ritz-Carlton's brunch is worth the splurge for special occasions.

## 4. Rent Before You Buy Gear

Paddleboards, kayaks, bikes - rent them first while you explore Key Biscayne. Crandon Park Marina and Virginia Key Outdoor Center offer rentals for water sports, while bike rentals let you ride the scenic paths around the island. You'll learn which activities you actually enjoy before investing in your own gear.

## 5. Check Do305.com and TimeOut Miami

These sites aggregate weekend events across Miami, including Key Biscayne happenings and nearby Brickell and Coconut Grove activities just across the Rickenbacker Causeway. Subscribe to their newsletters and keep an eye on the Key Biscayne Community Foundation calendar for island-specific events like the annual Fourth of July parade and community festivals.
