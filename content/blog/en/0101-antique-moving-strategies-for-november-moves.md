## Key Tips for Antique Moving in November

### Plan Ahead for Peak Times

November can be a busy time for moving companies in Miami. With Art Basel visitors in Miami Beach, holiday season activities, and snowbirds arriving from up north, scheduling your antique moving early ensures you get your preferred date and time. Many families in neighborhoods like Pinecrest and Coral Gables also use the school break to relocate.

### Get appraisals and photograph items before the move for insurance purposes

This single step can save hours of stress and potential problems on moving day. Our experienced crews have seen what happens when this step is skipped.

### Consider Miami's November Weather

South Florida's November weather brings lower humidity than summer months, which is actually ideal for moving delicate antiques. However, temperatures can still reach the 80s, and sudden rain showers are common. Professional movers know how to protect your belongings from humidity, rain, and heat exposure during transport. If you're moving antique wooden furniture, the cooler November temperatures help prevent warping and cracking that can occur during our hot summer months.

### Prepare Your Belongings

Take time to inventory your items before the move. This is especially important for antique moving, as proper documentation helps ensure everything arrives safely at your new location.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does antique moving cost in Miami?
Antique moving typically costs $200-$800 per piece depending on size, fragility, and distance. Custom crating adds $75-$300 per piece. A full household with antiques can range from $1,500-$5,000+ for local moves.

### How do you protect antiques during a Miami move?
We use custom crating, acid-free tissue paper, moving blankets, and climate-controlled trucks. Each piece gets photographed and inventoried before loading. For especially fragile items, we build wooden crates to exact dimensions.

### Is November a good time to move antiques in Miami?
November is one of the best months. Lower humidity (compared to summer) reduces the risk of moisture damage to wood finishes and delicate fabrics. Temperatures in the 70s mean less thermal stress on glues and veneers.
