# 5 Access Challenges When Moving a Hot Tub

Every hot tub move has its own set of obstacles, and in Miami, those obstacles tend to involve tight spaces, fenced yards, and creative property layouts. Here are the five access challenges we run into most often on [**Hot Tub Moving**](/hot-tub-moving) jobs around South Florida, along with how we deal with each one.

## 1. Narrow Side Yards

Miami homes, especially in older neighborhoods like Little Havana, Shenandoah, and parts of Hialeah, often have side yards that are barely three feet wide. The tub needs to travel from the backyard, down the side of the house, to the driveway where the truck is parked. When the side yard is too narrow for the tub to pass through flat, we tip the spa on its side and use furniture dollies to roll it along the narrow path. This requires careful padding to protect the shell and at least four crew members to control the tub's angle and speed.

## 2. Pool Cages and Screen Enclosures

Screened-in pool areas are standard in South Florida. These aluminum-framed enclosures have small entry doors and rigid structural frames. If the hot tub sits inside the pool cage, getting it out means either removing screen panels, unbolting a section of the frame, or craning the tub over the entire structure. We assess the enclosure during our pre-move walkthrough and coordinate with screen contractors when frame removal is needed.

![A Woman Relaxes In A Steamy Outdoor Jacu 1](/images/blog/2027/10/5-access-challenges-when-moving-a-hot-tub/a-woman-relaxes-in-a-steamy-outdoor-jacu-1.webp)


## 3. Privacy Fences with No Gate (or a Too-Small Gate)

Many Miami backyards have 6-foot privacy fences made of wood, vinyl, or aluminum. The gate, if there is one, is almost always too narrow for a hot tub. The tub was either installed before the fence went up or was craned into place. Our team can remove and reinstall fence panels on most fence types in under 30 minutes. For concrete block walls or situations where fence removal isn't an option, we arrange crane service.

## 4. Elevated Decks and Patios

Some hot tubs sit on elevated wood decks, second-floor balconies, or raised concrete patios. Getting a 600-pound tub from a raised surface to ground level requires controlled lowering with straps and sometimes ramp systems. The deck itself also needs to be evaluated. Older wood decks in Miami's salt air and humidity may not support the concentrated weight of a tub being slid across the surface. We check the structural integrity before putting any load on a deck.

![Friends Having Fun And Relaxing Together 5](/images/blog/2027/10/5-access-challenges-when-moving-a-hot-tub/friends-having-fun-and-relaxing-together-5.webp)


## 5. Landscaping and Hardscaping Obstacles

Between the tub and the truck, there's often a gauntlet of mature palm trees, coral rock landscaping, paver patios, and decorative planters. Rolling a heavy tub over uneven pavers can crack them. Dragging it across a lawn tears up the turf. Tree roots create bumps that can tip a dolly. We protect landscaping with plywood sheets laid over the path, and we use wide-wheel dollies that distribute weight more evenly across paver surfaces.

## Planning Around Access Issues

The key to handling any of these challenges is knowing about them before moving day. That's why we always recommend a site visit or at least detailed photos and measurements from both the pickup and delivery locations. When we know what we're dealing with in advance, we bring the right equipment and the right size crew. Surprises on moving day slow everything down and can lead to property damage.
