You signed the papers, got the keys, and started unpacking. But those keys in your hand? Previous owners have copies. So do their family members, past contractors, cleaning crews, the real estate agent, and the staging company. Before you settle in for the night, security needs to be handled. Here are 8 features to get in place during your first week.

## 1. Rekey or Replace Every Lock

This is the single most important security step and it should happen before you even start unpacking boxes. You have no way of knowing how many copies of your house keys are floating around.

![A Hand Using Fingerprint Access On A Dig 3](/images/blog/2029/11/8-must-have-security-features-post-move/a-hand-using-fingerprint-access-on-a-dig-3.webp)


**Rekeying vs. replacing**: Rekeying changes the internal pins of existing locks so old keys no longer work. It costs $15 to $25 per lock through a locksmith and keeps your existing hardware. Replacing with new deadbolts or smart locks costs $100 to $300 per door but gives you upgraded hardware.

Smart locks with changeable codes are worth considering, especially in South Florida where you might give temporary access to cleaners, pet sitters, or contractors. Models with auto-lock features ensure the door is never accidentally left unlocked.

Do not forget the garage entry door, any side gates with locks, and the interior door between the garage and house.

## 2. Test Every Window Latch and Sliding Door

Walk through every room and physically test each window. Push, pull, and try to force them. Older Miami homes, especially those built before the 1990s, often have jalousie windows or single-pane designs with worn latches.

Sliding glass doors deserve special attention. They are everywhere in South Florida homes and they can be lifted off their tracks if the rollers are worn or the frame is loose. Add a security bar or a Charlie bar in the track. Install a secondary lock at the top of the door frame where it cannot be reached from outside.

If any window or door lock is broken, replace it immediately. Do not add it to your renovation list for later.

## 3. Set Up a Security Camera System

Modern security cameras have dropped dramatically in price. A basic four-camera wireless system costs $150 to $400 and installs in under an hour. Focus your cameras on:

- **Front door**: This is where most package thefts and break-in attempts start
- **Back door or patio**: The second most common entry point
- **Driveway or carport**: Monitors vehicle access and visitors
- **Side yard or gate**: Covers blind spots that face-mounted cameras miss

Cloud storage plans run $3 to $10 per month per camera. Local storage via SD card or NVR avoids monthly fees but requires manual review. Many systems now offer AI-powered alerts that distinguish between people, vehicles, and animals, which cuts down on false notifications.

If you live in a condo or HOA community, check the rules before mounting exterior cameras. Some associations restrict placement and camera types.

## 4. Install a Video Doorbell

A video doorbell combines a camera, motion sensor, microphone, and speaker at your front door. You see who is there whether you are home or not, and you can speak to delivery drivers, visitors, or strangers through your phone.

The motion detection zone is adjustable on most models, which matters if your front door faces a busy sidewalk. Set the sensitivity to avoid constant alerts from pedestrians and passing cars.

Video doorbells run on batteries or hardwired power. Battery models install in minutes with no electrical work. Hardwired models connect to your existing doorbell wiring and never need charging.

## 5. Add Motion-Activated Exterior Lighting

Dark corners around your home are an invitation. Motion-sensor lights eliminate hiding spots and draw attention to anyone approaching.

![A Set Of Smart Home Devices Including A  1](/images/blog/2029/11/8-must-have-security-features-post-move/a-set-of-smart-home-devices-including-a--1.webp)


Priority placement:

- Front entry and walkway
- Garage door area
- Back patio and rear entry
- Side yards between your home and the fence line

Solar-powered options work well in South Florida, where you get ample sunlight year-round. They install without wiring and cost $20 to $60 per unit. Place them high enough that they cannot be easily unscrewed or knocked down.

LED flood lights with built-in motion sensors offer the brightest output for driveways and backyards. A 3000-lumen unit covers a typical residential backyard.

## 6. Replace Smoke Detectors and Add CO Monitors

Move-in day is the right time to address fire safety. Replace batteries in every existing detector, and if any unit is more than 10 years old, replace it entirely. Florida building code requires smoke detectors in every bedroom, outside each sleeping area, and on every floor.

Carbon monoxide detectors are critical if your home has:

- A gas stove or oven
- A gas water heater
- An attached garage (car exhaust can seep in)
- A gas fireplace

Combination smoke/CO detectors simplify installation and reduce the number of devices on your ceiling. Smart models send alerts to your phone, which is useful when you are away from home.

## 7. Reinforce Entry Door Strike Plates

Most homes come with builder-grade strike plates held in with 3/4-inch screws that only reach the door frame trim. A solid kick can pop these out. Upgrading to reinforced strike plates with 3-inch screws that sink into the wall studs costs under $15 per door and takes 10 minutes to install.

This is one of the highest-impact, lowest-cost security upgrades you can make. It turns a door that can be kicked open into one that holds firm.

For Miami homeowners, reinforced strike plates with longer screws also help during hurricane season. The same structural reinforcement that resists a break-in attempt helps the door hold up against wind pressure and debris impact.

## 8. Trim Vegetation Near Windows and Entry Points

South Florida's tropical climate means plants grow fast and thick. Dense hedges, overgrown palms, and untrimmed bushes near windows and doors create concealment for anyone trying to access your home.

Within your first week, trim back:

- Any bushes taller than window sill height
- Palm fronds that block sight lines from the street
- Hedges that create enclosed spaces near doors

Keep plants at least 3 feet from windows and entry points. If you want natural security, thorny plants like bougainvillea beneath windows make an effective deterrent while keeping the landscaping attractive.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much should I budget for post-move security?

A basic setup covering locks, a video doorbell, exterior lights, and smoke detectors runs $500 to $1,000 for a typical single-family home. Adding a full camera system and smart locks pushes the total to $1,000 to $2,000. This is a one-time investment that pays for itself through better protection and potential insurance discounts.

### Should I get professional monitoring or a DIY system?

Professional monitoring (ADT, Brinks) costs $30 to $60 per month and provides 24/7 response. DIY systems (Ring, SimpliSafe) rely on self-monitoring through your phone. Many Miami homeowners use a hybrid approach: a DIY camera system for daily monitoring and a professionally monitored alarm for break-in detection.

### Do security systems lower homeowners insurance?

Most Florida insurers offer discounts of 5% to 20% for monitored security systems, smoke detectors, and deadbolts. Ask your insurance provider what qualifies before purchasing. The discount can offset the monthly monitoring cost.
