# 6 Heavy Home Items and the Equipment Needed to Move Them

Some household items cross the threshold from "heavy" into "you need specialized equipment or someone gets hurt." These are the six items Miami homeowners most often underestimate, along with the exact tools we bring to handle each one safely.

## 1. Gun Safes (400-2,000 lbs)

A mid-range Liberty or Browning safe runs 600-800 pounds. Commercial-grade vaults from AMSEC or Fort Knox can exceed a ton. **Equipment needed:** Heavy-duty safe dolly rated for 1,800 lbs with pneumatic tires, stair-climbing attachment for multi-level homes, 4-point ratchet straps, plywood floor runners, and a hydraulic lift gate on the truck. For safes over 1,500 lbs, we sometimes bring a gantry crane or coordinate with a rigging company.

## 2. Cast Iron Wood Stoves (400-700 lbs)

Cast iron stoves are dense, compact, and have no good grip points. They're common in older Florida homes and outdoor living areas. **Equipment needed:** Heavy-duty furniture dolly with rubberized deck, lifting straps (the kind that loop under the stove body and distribute weight across the carriers' shoulders), floor sliders for hardwood and tile protection, and reinforced ramps for truck loading.

## 3. Slate Pool Tables (700-1,000 lbs)

A standard 8-foot pool table with three-piece slate weighs 800-900 pounds assembled. The slate alone accounts for 450+ pounds. **Equipment needed:** Pool tables always get disassembled. The frame breaks down with standard tools. The slate sections (150 lbs each) ride on a flat dolly. We use a slate jack for precision lifting during disassembly and reassembly, plus a machinist's level for ensuring the playing surface is perfectly flat at the destination.

## 4. Grand Pianos (500-1,200 lbs)

A baby grand runs 500-600 pounds. A full concert grand pushes 1,200. **Equipment needed:** Piano board (a reinforced plank with casters and heavy-duty straps), piano skid shoes for the legs, padded blankets and stretch wrap for body protection, and a ramp with at least a 1,000-lb rating. In Brickell and Downtown condos, we also need building-required floor protection runners and elevator padding kits.

## 5. Hot Tubs (600-900 lbs empty)

A standard 6-person hot tub weighs 700-800 pounds completely drained. Even empty, that weight combined with the awkward shape makes it a two-equipment job. **Equipment needed:** Furniture dollies with pneumatic tires for backyard terrain, a 4-person lifting strap system, and often a crane for lifting over fences, screen enclosures, or landscaping. For Miami homes with pool screen enclosures, we coordinate with a screen company to temporarily remove a panel for access.

## 6. Marble and Granite Countertop Remnants (200-600 lbs per slab)

Homeowners moving from a kitchen renovation often want to keep their leftover stone slabs for the new home's outdoor kitchen or bathroom project. A 5-foot granite remnant weighs 250+ pounds. **Equipment needed:** A-frame stone dolly for upright transport, suction cup lifters rated for 200+ lbs per cup, edge protectors to prevent chipping, and vibration-dampening padding inside the truck. Stone rides upright, never flat, to prevent breakage from its own weight.

## Why Equipment Matters More Than Muscle

Additional crew members can't substitute for the right tools. Six strong people carrying a 1,000-pound safe have no safe way to set it down if someone loses their grip. A motorized stair climber handles the same weight with two trained operators and maintains complete control at every step. The equipment isn't just about efficiency. It's about preventing injuries and property damage that turn a $500 moving job into a $5,000 insurance claim.
