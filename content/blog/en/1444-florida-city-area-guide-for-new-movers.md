Florida City sits at the southernmost point of mainland Miami-Dade County, about 30 miles south of Downtown Miami. With a population around 13,000, it offers something increasingly hard to find in South Florida: affordable single-family homes with actual yard space, a small-town pace, and direct access to both Everglades National Park and Biscayne National Park. The trade-off is a longer commute if you work in central Miami, but for families and retirees who prioritize space and outdoor access, the math works out.

## Why People Move to Florida City

### Affordability

![Close Up Of An American Crocodile Restin 3](/images/blog/2029/10/florida-city-area-guide-for-new-movers/close-up-of-an-american-crocodile-restin-3.webp)


Florida City is one of the most affordable places to buy a home in Miami-Dade County. Median home prices run significantly lower than the county average, and property taxes reflect the lower valuations. For families priced out of Kendall, Homestead, or Cutler Bay, Florida City offers a path to homeownership that doesn't require a dual six-figure income.

### Gateway to the Outdoors

The city earned its "Gateway to the Everglades" nickname honestly. The Ernest F. Coe Visitor Center at Everglades National Park is about 10 miles southwest. Biscayne National Park's Dante Fascell Visitor Center is roughly 10 miles east in Homestead. Residents who kayak, fish, hike, or birdwatch have two national parks within a short drive. That proximity is the primary draw for many new residents.

### Agricultural Heritage

Florida City has deep agricultural roots. The surrounding farmland produces tomatoes, tropical fruits, and nursery plants that supply much of South Florida. Local U-pick farms and roadside fruit stands are part of daily life here. Robert Is Here, the famous fruit stand on SW 344th Street, draws visitors from across the county but sits right in Florida City's backyard.

## Where to Live in Florida City

### Palm Drive Corridor

Palm Drive (SW 344th Street) runs east-west through the center of town and serves as the main commercial strip. Living near Palm Drive puts you close to restaurants, grocery stores, and retail. The trade-off is more traffic noise, particularly during tourist season when Everglades visitors pass through.

### Residential Streets South of Palm Drive

The quieter residential neighborhoods sit south of Palm Drive. Single-family homes on larger lots line these streets, many with mature tropical landscaping. Families with children tend to settle here for the calmer atmosphere and proximity to local schools.

### Northern Florida City Near Homestead

The northern edge of Florida City blends into Homestead, and the boundary between the two cities is not always obvious. This area offers newer construction and slightly higher price points. Homestead Town Center and the Florida Keys Outlet Marketplace are both within a few minutes, giving northern Florida City residents quick access to major retail.

### Areas Near the Speedway

Homestead-Miami Speedway sits just north of Florida City. Properties near the speedway may experience noise during race weekends (NASCAR and other events draw large crowds several times per year). Some residents enjoy the proximity; others prefer distance from the event traffic.

## What New Residents Need to Know

### The Commute

If you work in Downtown Miami, Brickell, or Doral, plan for a 45-75 minute commute depending on traffic. The Florida Turnpike and US-1 are the two main routes north. Some residents use the South Dade TransitWay (Busway) that connects to the Dadeland South Metrorail station for a car-free commute option, though the ride takes about an hour.

### Hurricane Preparedness

Florida City took a direct hit from Hurricane Andrew in 1992, which devastated much of the area. The community rebuilt with stricter building codes. Newer homes are constructed to post-Andrew standards, but older properties may need upgrades. Flood insurance requirements vary by lot, so check FEMA flood maps before purchasing.

### Local Schools

Florida City is served by Miami-Dade County Public Schools:

- **Florida City Elementary**: The local K-5 public school
- **Homestead Middle School**: Serves grades 6-8
- **Homestead Senior High**: The zoned high school
- **Miami Dade College Homestead Campus**: Offers associate degrees and workforce training for adults

Private and charter school options exist in nearby Homestead and Cutler Bay.

## Essential Services

### Healthcare

Homestead Hospital on NW 8th Street in Homestead is the nearest major medical facility, about 10 minutes from most of Florida City. Baptist Health South Florida operates urgent care clinics in the area. For specialized care, you will travel to hospitals in Kendall or Miami proper.

### Groceries and Shopping

- **Publix**: Multiple locations along US-1 in Homestead
- **Walmart Supercenter**: On Campbell Drive in Homestead
- **Florida Keys Outlet Marketplace**: Major retail center just north on Florida Turnpike
- **Robert Is Here**: Iconic fruit stand for local produce, smoothies, and tropical specialties
- **Homestead Town Center**: Target, Best Buy, and other national retailers

### Recreation

- **Everglades National Park**: Hiking, kayaking, airboat tours, and wildlife viewing
- **Biscayne National Park**: Snorkeling, kayaking, and boat tours to the barrier islands
- **Homestead-Miami Speedway**: NASCAR, sports car racing, and driving experiences
- **Coral Castle**: A historic landmark on US-1 built single-handedly by Edward Leedskalnin
- **Fruit and Spice Park**: A 37-acre botanical garden with over 500 varieties of fruits, vegetables, spices, and herbs

## Moving to Florida City This Fall

October is a solid time for a Florida City move. The summer heat has started to ease, daily thunderstorms become less frequent, and the dry season is approaching. Temperatures sit in the low to mid-80s with lower humidity than the June through September stretch.

### Timing Tips

- **Avoid race weekends**: Check the Homestead-Miami Speedway calendar and plan around major events that bring traffic to the area
- **Morning starts**: Even in October, starting early beats the afternoon heat
- **Weekday moves**: Less traffic on US-1 and the Turnpike, and better availability from movers
- **Hurricane awareness**: Season runs through November 30. Monitor forecasts in the weeks before your move

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Florida City a good place to raise a family?

Florida City appeals to families who want affordable housing, yard space, and outdoor access. The proximity to two national parks and the agricultural character give kids experiences that urban Miami cannot match. The trade-off is a longer commute if parents work in central Miami and fewer entertainment options compared to denser areas.

### How far is Florida City from Miami Beach?

About 45 miles, or roughly an hour by car depending on traffic. Most residents use the Florida Turnpike to I-95 or take US-1 north.

### What is the cost of living in Florida City?

Housing costs in Florida City run well below the Miami-Dade County average. Groceries, utilities, and everyday expenses are comparable to the rest of the county. The biggest savings come from lower home prices and property taxes.
