Whether you're moving from Brickell to Coral Gables, relocating to a new condo in Aventura, or heading across town from Kendall to Doral, certain items should never go on the moving truck. Here's our list of 10 belongings you should always keep with you during your Miami move.

## 1. Hazardous Materials

Gasoline, propane, paint thinner, aerosols, and household chemicals are fire hazards and illegal to transport on moving trucks. This includes pool chemicals common in Miami homes, cleaning supplies with bleach or ammonia, and automotive fluids from your Homestead garage. Dispose of these properly at Miami-Dade's HazMat collection sites in Pinecrest or North Dade before moving day.

## 2. Explosives and Ammunition

Fireworks, ammunition, and anything explosive must be disposed of or transported personally with proper safety measures. If you are a gun owner in Coral Gables or Pinecrest, transport ammunition in your personal vehicle in a locked container. Florida law allows this, but moving companies cannot take the liability.

## 3. Perishable Foods

Refrigerated and frozen items will not survive the Miami heat sitting in a truck. Finish off freezer contents the week before your move, donate unopened pantry items to Feeding South Florida, and plan to restock after you arrive at your new place in Aventura or wherever you are headed.

## 4. Plants (for Long-Distance Moves)

Agricultural regulations prohibit transporting plants across state lines due to pest concerns. For local Miami-Dade moves, plants can go on the truck, but they are fragile. Consider transporting orchids, palms, and your favorite Monstera in your air-conditioned car instead.

## 5. Pets and Living Creatures

Fish, pets, and any living animals cannot travel on moving trucks. The interior of a truck in Miami can exceed 120 degrees by mid-morning. Arrange separate transportation for your pets, board them during the move, or have a friend take them while the movers work.

## 6. Irreplaceable Documents

Passports, birth certificates, Social Security cards, property deeds, financial records, and legal documents should stay with you personally. Keep these in a fireproof folder in your personal vehicle. If the moving truck gets delayed or damaged, you will still have everything you need.

## 7. Jewelry and Cash

High-value, easily stolen items belong in your personal vehicle, not on a truck full of boxes. This includes watches, engagement rings, gold chains, and any cash you keep at home. Moving trucks get loaded and unloaded by multiple people in public view. Keep valuables close.

## 8. Medications

Keep prescriptions accessible and in your possession throughout the move. Pack a 7-day supply in your personal bag along with copies of prescriptions. If your moving truck gets delayed overnight, you will still have what you need. This is especially important for temperature-sensitive medications that cannot sit in a hot truck.

## 9. Pressurized Containers

Fire extinguishers, scuba tanks, aerosol hairsprays, and compressed air cans can explode under pressure or temperature changes during transport. Either empty and dispose of them, or transport them in your climate-controlled vehicle. Many Miami residents with boats forget about scuba gear until moving day.

## 10. Anything Irreplaceable

Family photos, heirlooms, your grandmother's wedding ring, and items of extreme sentimental value should travel with you in your personal vehicle. Moving companies provide liability coverage, but no amount of money replaces a one-of-a-kind family treasure. When in doubt, keep it with you.

## Benefits of Knowing What to Keep

Understanding which items to transport yourself offers several advantages:

- **Safety**: Hazardous materials stay off the truck, protecting movers and your belongings
- **Peace of mind**: Your valuables and irreplaceable items remain in your control
- **Legal compliance**: You avoid liability issues with prohibited items
- **Faster claims**: If something does happen, your most important items are already safe

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What happens if I accidentally pack prohibited items?

Movers may refuse to load the truck if they discover hazardous materials during loading. Be upfront about what you have so we can advise you on proper disposal or personal transport options.

### Can movers transport wine or alcohol?

Yes, sealed bottles of wine and liquor can go on the moving truck for local Miami moves. For interstate moves, regulations vary by state. We recommend keeping valuable wine collections in your personal vehicle with climate control.

### Should I empty dresser drawers before moving?

You can leave lightweight clothing in drawers, but remove anything heavy or valuable. Jewelry, cash, and important documents should always come out and travel with you.
