Coconut Grove is the oldest continuously inhabited neighborhood in Miami, and it has held onto its bohemian, village-like feel even as the rest of the city has built up and out. Think banyan-canopied streets, waterfront parks, sidewalk cafes, and a sailing marina five minutes from your front door. If you are planning a move here, this guide covers the real details you need, from schools and dining to parking logistics and housing costs.

## The Neighborhood at a Glance

Coconut Grove sits along Biscayne Bay in south-central Miami, bordered by Coral Gables to the west and Brickell to the north. The neighborhood covers roughly three square miles and is known for its lush tropical canopy, walkable village center, and waterfront lifestyle.

![A Serene Landscape Of A Coconut Grove Wi 4](/images/blog/2029/05/coconut-grove-living-what-new-residents-should-know/a-serene-landscape-of-a-coconut-grove-wi-4.webp)


The area draws a mix of young professionals, established families, and creative types. It is one of the few Miami neighborhoods where you can walk from your apartment to a restaurant, a park, a marina, and a bookshop without touching your car.

### Getting Around

Coconut Grove connects to the rest of Miami-Dade via US-1 (South Dixie Highway) and Bayshore Drive. The Coconut Grove Metrorail station provides a direct line to Downtown, Brickell, and points south toward Dadeland. The free Coconut Grove Trolley runs a loop through the village center, making car-free errands possible for residents near the core.

## Where to Eat and Drink

The dining scene in Coconut Grove punches above its weight for a neighborhood this size.

**Glass and Vine** in Peacock Park serves farm-to-table dishes in a glass-walled space that opens onto the park. It earned "Best Place to Dine with Dogs" from Miami New Times and draws a crowd for weekend brunch.

**Ariete** brings Cuban-inspired flavors with a modern twist. Chef Michael Beltran's dishes lean on local ingredients and old family recipes, and the restaurant has become a staple of the neighborhood's food identity.

**Le Bouchon du Grove** is a French bistro tucked along a side street that feels like it belongs in a Paris arrondissement. Escargots, duck confit, and creme brulee are the highlights, paired with a focused French wine list.

**Greenstreet Cafe** has been a Coconut Grove landmark for decades. The open-air patio on Main Highway is the default spot for a late breakfast or a glass of wine on a weekday evening. It is as close to a town square as the Grove has.

**Monty's Raw Bar** at the Coconut Grove waterfront serves fresh seafood with live music and views of the marina. It is casual, loud on weekends, and a rite of passage for new residents.

For quick bites, **Ava in the Grove** serves Mediterranean-Aegean dishes like tuna ceviche and lobster pasta, and the CocoWalk complex has a rotating lineup of casual restaurants and coffee spots.

## Parks and Outdoor Life

Coconut Grove's outdoor spaces are a major part of daily life here.

**Peacock Park** sits right in the village center and hosts live music events, food festivals, and the annual Coconut Grove Arts Festival, one of the top outdoor art shows in the country, held every Presidents' Day weekend.

**The Barnacle Historic State Park** is a five-acre waterfront preserve built around the 1891 home of Commodore Ralph Munroe, one of the oldest houses in Miami-Dade County. The park offers kayak launches, guided tours, and moonlight concerts on the lawn.

**David T. Kennedy Park** runs along the bayfront south of Mercy Hospital, with walking and cycling paths, a playground, and open green space with water views.

**Dinner Key Marina**, one of the largest marinas in Miami, is the hub for sailing, kayaking, and paddleboarding. Several sailing schools operate from here, and the marina hosts regattas throughout the year.

## Schools

Schools are a major draw for families considering Coconut Grove.

![A Stunning View Of Miami S Illuminated S 1](/images/blog/2029/05/coconut-grove-living-what-new-residents-should-know/a-stunning-view-of-miami-s-illuminated-s-1.webp)


**Ransom Everglades School** is a private college-prep day school (grades 6 through 12) named one of the Top 10 Private Schools in North America and one of the Top 100 Schools in the World by Spear's School Index in both 2024 and 2025. It is the only school in Florida and the entire Southeast to appear on these rankings.

**Carrollton School of the Sacred Heart** is an all-girls private school (pre-K through 12) with a campus right on Biscayne Bay. It has a strong academic reputation and a focus on community service.

**Coconut Grove Elementary** is the neighborhood's zoned public school and has an active parent community. For public middle and high school options, students typically feed into George Washington Carver Middle and Coral Gables Senior High.

## Housing and Cost of Living

Coconut Grove's housing market has climbed significantly. The median single-family home price reached approximately $2.35 million by late 2025, more than double the median from five years earlier. Demand is driven by the neighborhood's schools, walkability, and waterfront access, plus a wave of high-profile buyers moving into the area.

Condos offer a more accessible entry point. Units at developments like Grove at Grand Bay, Park Grove, and the newer projects along South Bayshore Drive range from the mid-$500,000s for smaller units to several million for penthouses with bay views.

Rentals in the Grove average higher than the Miami-Dade median, with one-bedroom apartments typically running $2,200 to $3,500 per month depending on proximity to the village center and water.

### Sub-Areas to Know

- **Center Grove** is the walkable village core around CocoWalk, Main Highway, and Grand Avenue. Restaurants, shops, and the Metrorail station are all within a few blocks.
- **South Grove** features larger single-family homes on tree-lined streets with a quieter, more residential character. This is where many families settle.
- **North Grove** borders Brickell and has seen the most new condo development. It offers easier access to Downtown but less of the village atmosphere.

## Moving to Coconut Grove: Logistics

### Parking and Building Access

Street parking in the village center is competitive, especially on weekends. Most condos have garage parking, but on moving day you will need to coordinate truck access carefully. Reserve freight elevators and loading docks at least a week in advance. Buildings like Grove at Grand Bay and Park Grove have specific move-in windows and require a Certificate of Insurance from your moving company.

For single-family homes, narrow streets and mature tree canopies can limit large truck access. A crew familiar with the neighborhood will know which streets to avoid and where to stage the truck.

### Best Time to Schedule Your Move

Morning moves (starting by 8 a.m.) avoid the worst of the heat and afternoon rain. If you are moving on a weekend, book early because the Grove's restaurant and event traffic picks up after noon. Weekday moves between Tuesday and Thursday offer the easiest parking and building access.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Coconut Grove walkable?

Yes. The village center around CocoWalk is one of the most walkable areas in Miami. You can reach restaurants, shops, parks, and the Metrorail station on foot. Areas farther from the center are more car-dependent.

### How does Coconut Grove compare to Coral Gables?

Both are established, tree-lined neighborhoods. Coral Gables has more of a planned, suburban feel with uniform Mediterranean architecture. Coconut Grove is more eclectic and bohemian, with a stronger waterfront and nightlife scene. Coconut Grove also tends to be slightly more expensive per square foot for single-family homes.

### What is the commute from Coconut Grove to Downtown Miami?

About 10 to 15 minutes by car outside of rush hour, or roughly 12 minutes on the Metrorail from Coconut Grove station to Government Center. During rush hour, driving can take 25 to 35 minutes depending on the route.
