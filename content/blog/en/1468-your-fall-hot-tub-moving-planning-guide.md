Fall is the best season to move a hot tub in South Florida. Temperatures drop into the mid-70s, humidity eases up, and you are past the worst of hurricane season. That combination makes the physical labor safer and reduces the risk of weather delays.

But hot tub moves are not simple. A standard acrylic spa weighs 500-800 pounds empty and upward of 3,000-4,000 pounds full. The job requires draining, disconnecting electrical and plumbing, working through tight access points, and transporting a bulky item that cannot be tipped, dropped, or scraped without causing serious damage.

Here is how to plan your fall hot tub move from start to finish.

## Why Fall Works Better for Hot Tub Moves

Summer hot tub moves in Miami mean working in 90-degree heat with high humidity, which creates real safety risks for the crew handling 600+ pounds of fiberglass and acrylic. Fall brings three advantages:

![A Tranquil Hot Tub Retreat In An Outdoor 4](/images/blog/2029/11/your-fall-hot-tub-moving-planning-guide/a-tranquil-hot-tub-retreat-in-an-outdoor-4.webp)


- **Lower temperatures** reduce heat exhaustion risk during the 2-4 hours a hot tub move typically takes
- **Less rain** means fewer weather delays and a drier path for the dolly and equipment
- **Scheduling availability** opens up as the summer moving rush winds down

The one thing to watch in fall is holiday scheduling. November and December book up fast for moving companies, so lock in your date early.

## The Draining Timeline

The single most important prep step is draining your hot tub, and timing matters.

**48 hours before the move:**
1. Turn off the heater but leave the pump running
2. Add a line flush product to clean the plumbing (available at any pool supply store)
3. Run the jets for 20-30 minutes to cycle the flush through the lines

**24 hours before the move:**
1. Power off the hot tub completely at the breaker
2. Open the drain spigot (most tubs have one near the base) and connect a garden hose to direct water away from the foundation
3. Alternatively, use a submersible pump to drain faster (a 1,200 GPH pump drains a 400-gallon tub in about 20 minutes)

**Morning of the move:**
1. Use a wet/dry vacuum to blow remaining water out of the jets and plumbing lines
2. Wipe down the interior shell to prevent mildew during transport
3. Remove, clean, and bag the filter cartridges separately

Skipping the drain step or leaving residual water in the lines creates two problems: extra weight that makes transport harder, and standing water that breeds mold in a sealed tub.

## Disconnecting Electrical and Plumbing

Most hot tubs run on a 240V dedicated circuit. Disconnecting this is not a DIY job. Hire a licensed electrician to:

- Disconnect the tub from the GFCI breaker panel
- Cap and secure exposed wiring
- Confirm the circuit is dead before the moving crew arrives

If your tub has a permanent plumbing connection (rather than a garden hose fill), a plumber should disconnect and cap those lines as well.

**Cost note:** Budget $100-$200 for an electrician disconnect and $75-$150 for plumbing work. These are one-time costs that protect both your tub and your home.

## Access Planning

Hot tubs rarely fit through standard doors or gates. Before your move, measure:

![A Woman Enjoying A Peaceful Moment In An 2](/images/blog/2029/11/your-fall-hot-tub-moving-planning-guide/a-woman-enjoying-a-peaceful-moment-in-an-2.webp)


- **The hot tub dimensions**: Length, width, and height (most are 7-8 feet long, 6-7 feet wide, and 32-36 inches tall)
- **Every access point**: Gates, fence openings, side yards, and the final destination at the new home
- **The path surface**: Grass, gravel, concrete, or pavers all affect dolly traction
- **Overhead clearance**: Low-hanging branches, power lines, and patio covers

If the tub will not fit through existing access points, the crew may need to remove a fence section (we reinstall it afterward) or use a crane for elevated decks.

## What Professional Movers Bring

[**Hot tub moving**](/hot-tub-moving) requires specialized equipment that general movers often do not carry:

- **Hot tub dollies**: Heavy-duty 4-wheel carts rated for 1,000+ pounds, with pneumatic tires for uneven ground
- **Moving straps and harnesses**: Load-rated straps that distribute weight evenly across 2-4 crew members
- **Furniture blankets and corner protectors**: Wrap the shell to prevent scratching and cracking
- **Plywood sheets**: Laid over grass or gravel to create a stable rolling path

A typical crew size for a hot tub move is 3-4 people. The move itself usually takes 2-4 hours depending on access complexity.

## Protecting the Hot Tub During Transport

Once the tub is loaded onto the truck, securing it properly is just as important as getting it there:

- The tub travels upright (never on its side) to prevent shell stress cracks
- Straps anchor it to the truck walls at multiple points to prevent shifting
- Moving blankets cover the entire exterior surface
- The cover is transported separately (covers crack if weight is placed on them)

## At the New Location

Before the crew arrives at your new home, confirm:

- **The pad or deck** is ready and level (a concrete pad should be cured at least 7 days)
- **Electrical is pre-wired** with a dedicated 240V GFCI circuit within 5 feet of the tub location
- **Water access** is available nearby for refilling
- **Access path** is cleared and measured

After placement, a licensed electrician reconnects the power. Fill the tub, prime the pump, check for leaks, and run a full heat cycle before use.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How much does it cost to move a hot tub?**
Local hot tub moves in Miami-Dade typically run $300-$600 depending on access complexity, distance, and whether fence removal or crane service is needed.

**Can I move a hot tub myself?**
We strongly advise against it. Hot tubs weigh 500-800 pounds empty. Without proper dollies, straps, and trained crew members, the risk of injury and property damage is high.

**Do I need to drain my hot tub before moving?**
Yes. A full hot tub can weigh 3,000-4,000 pounds. It must be completely drained, and residual water blown out of the jets and plumbing, before the move.
