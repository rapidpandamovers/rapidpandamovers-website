Golden Beach sits on a narrow barrier island between the Atlantic Ocean and the Intracoastal Waterway, roughly one mile long and four blocks wide. With a population of about 1,000 residents, it is one of the smallest and most private municipalities in Miami-Dade County. If you are planning a move here, this guide covers the practical details you need to settle in.

## What Makes Golden Beach Different

Golden Beach is not a typical South Florida town. There are no commercial businesses, no high-rises, and no through-traffic. The entire community is single-family homes on large oceanfront or Intracoastal lots. The town has maintained strict zoning since its incorporation in 1929, and those rules have kept it exclusively residential.

![A Tranquil View Of Ormond Beach Fl At Su 4](/images/blog/2029/10/the-newcomers-guide-to-golden-beach-living/a-tranquil-view-of-ormond-beach-fl-at-su-4.webp)


The only road into Golden Beach is The Strand, which is monitored by a police guardhouse 24 hours a day. Almost all access points from A1A were closed in the early 1980s to restrict non-resident traffic. That level of controlled access, combined with a dedicated police force, is the main reason the town attracts high-profile residents, including international business figures and entertainment professionals who value privacy.

Home values in Golden Beach consistently rank among the highest in Miami-Dade County. Most properties sell well above the million-dollar mark, with oceanfront estates reaching significantly higher.

## The Neighborhood Layout

Because Golden Beach spans just four blocks from the ocean to the Intracoastal, the layout is straightforward. Ocean Boulevard runs along the Atlantic side, and properties on this stretch have direct beach access. The Intracoastal side offers waterfront living with boat dock access. The interior streets provide slightly more modest lots but still maintain the quiet, tree-lined character of the town.

### Choosing Your Property

Consider these factors when looking at Golden Beach homes:

- **Oceanfront vs. Intracoastal**: Oceanfront lots offer beach access and unobstructed views. Intracoastal lots are better for boaters who want dock space
- **Lot size and setbacks**: The town enforces strict setback requirements, so verify what you can build or renovate before purchasing
- **Flood zones**: Eastern lots sit in FEMA flood zones. Confirm flood insurance requirements and elevation certificates before closing
- **Renovation rules**: Golden Beach has its own building department with design review standards. Any exterior changes require town approval

## Key Locations and Amenities

### Loggia Beach Park and Pavilion

The Loggia Beach Park and Pavilion at 399 Ocean Boulevard is the social center of Golden Beach. The Spanish-cloistered pavilion sits directly on the beach and includes fire pits, barbecue areas, and covered seating. Residents use it for gatherings, events, and everyday beach access. It is the one shared public space in a town that is otherwise entirely private property.

### Nearby Shopping and Dining

Golden Beach has zero commercial development within its borders, so you will do all shopping and dining in neighboring communities:

- **Aventura Mall**: About five minutes west on Biscayne Boulevard, one of the largest malls in the United States with over 300 stores and restaurants
- **Bal Harbour Shops**: About 10 minutes south on Collins Avenue, high-end retail including Chanel, Gucci, and Prada
- **Sunny Isles Beach**: Collins Avenue in Sunny Isles has grocery stores, pharmacies, and casual dining within a five-minute drive
- **Hallandale Beach**: Just north of Golden Beach with additional grocery options, restaurants, and the Gulfstream Park racing and entertainment complex

### Groceries

The nearest Publix locations are in Sunny Isles Beach on Collins Avenue and in Aventura. Whole Foods is at Aventura Mall. For specialty items, Fresh Market and Trader Joe's are within a 15-minute drive in neighboring communities.

## Schools and Healthcare

Golden Beach children attend Miami-Dade County Public Schools. The zoned schools vary by grade level, so confirm your specific address assignments through the MDCPS school boundary tool. Private school options in the area include Scheck Hillel Community School in North Miami Beach and several preparatory academies in Aventura and Hallandale.

For healthcare, Aventura Hospital and Medical Center is the closest major facility, about 10 minutes away. Memorial Regional Hospital in Hollywood and Mount Sinai Medical Center on Miami Beach are also within reasonable driving distance.

## Getting Around

Golden Beach is car-dependent. There is no commercial district to walk to, and public transit options are limited to county bus routes along A1A and Collins Avenue in neighboring towns.

Key driving connections:

- **I-95**: Accessible via Ives Dairy Road or Hallandale Beach Boulevard, about five minutes west
- **Downtown Miami**: 25 to 40 minutes south via I-95, depending on traffic
- **Fort Lauderdale**: 20 to 30 minutes north via I-95
- **Miami Beach**: 20 to 30 minutes south via Collins Avenue or I-95 to the Julia Tuttle Causeway

## Moving to Golden Beach

Moving into Golden Beach requires coordination with the town's police department and building department. Because access is controlled through The Strand guardhouse, your moving company needs to be cleared for entry in advance.

Additional considerations:

- **HOA and town rules**: Some properties have additional homeowner association requirements on top of the town's building codes
- **Truck access**: The narrow residential streets mean moving trucks need to plan routes carefully, especially for larger vehicles
- **Time restrictions**: The town may have restrictions on moving hours to minimize disruption to neighbors. Confirm these with the building department before scheduling

## FAQ

**Is Golden Beach gated?**
Golden Beach is not technically gated in the traditional sense, but access is highly controlled. The Strand is the only road into town, and it is monitored by a police guardhouse around the clock. Almost all other entry points from A1A were permanently closed in the 1980s.

![People Enjoying A Sunny Day At A Crowded 1](/images/blog/2029/10/the-newcomers-guide-to-golden-beach-living/people-enjoying-a-sunny-day-at-a-crowded-1.webp)


**Can you walk on Golden Beach's beach if you are not a resident?**
The beach itself is public below the mean high-water line, as is the case throughout Florida. However, there is no public parking or public access point within the town. Most non-residents access the beach from neighboring Sunny Isles Beach or Hallandale Beach.

**Are there any restaurants or stores in Golden Beach?**
No. The town is zoned exclusively residential with no commercial development. Residents rely on Sunny Isles Beach, Aventura, Bal Harbour, and Hallandale Beach for all shopping and dining.

**What is the commute from Golden Beach to Downtown Miami?**
Expect 25 to 40 minutes via I-95 during normal conditions. During peak morning rush, it can stretch to 50 minutes or more.
