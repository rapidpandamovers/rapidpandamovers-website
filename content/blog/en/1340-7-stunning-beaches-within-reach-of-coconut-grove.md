Living in Coconut Grove puts you within a short drive of some of South Florida's best shoreline. Whether you want calm water for the kids, space for a paddleboard, or a quiet stretch of sand away from the tourist crowds, these seven beaches deliver.

## 1. Matheson Hammock Park Beach

**Location:** 9610 Old Cutler Rd, Coral Gables (about 10 minutes south of Coconut Grove)

![Colorful Lifeguard Tower At Pompano Beac 3](/images/blog/2029/05/7-stunning-beaches-within-reach-of-coconut-grove/colorful-lifeguard-tower-at-pompano-beac-3.webp)


Matheson Hammock is the closest proper beach to Coconut Grove, and it is a local favorite for good reason. The man-made atoll pool, flushed naturally by Biscayne Bay tides, creates a calm, shallow wading area that is ideal for young children and anyone who prefers gentler water. Mangrove-lined walkways surround the beach, and the on-site NOMA Beach at Redfish serves Italian-inspired seafood with a waterfront view. Parking fills up by mid-morning on weekends, so arrive early.

## 2. Hobie Island Beach Park

**Location:** Rickenbacker Causeway, Virginia Key (about 10 minutes east)

Hobie Island sits right along the Rickenbacker Causeway on your way to Key Biscayne. This is the go-to spot for windsurfers, kiteboarders, and paddleboarders in the Coconut Grove area. Rentals are available on-site for kayaks and SUP boards. The beach itself is a narrow strip of sand with shallow water and steady breezes. It is not a lounging beach, but it is perfect for an active afternoon on the water. Free parking along the causeway.

## 3. Historic Virginia Key Beach Park

**Location:** 4020 Virginia Beach Dr, Virginia Key (about 12 minutes east)

Virginia Key Beach played a significant role in Miami's civil rights history as one of the only beaches open to Black residents during segregation. Today, it is a spacious, uncrowded park with a long stretch of sand, a restored carousel, picnic pavilions, and nature trails. The water is calm and the beach is wide, making it a strong choice for families who want space without the South Beach scene. Parking costs $8 per car on weekdays.

## 4. Crandon Park Beach

**Location:** 6747 Crandon Blvd, Key Biscayne (about 20 minutes east)

Crandon Park regularly lands on "best beaches in America" lists, and the two-mile stretch of sand backs up the reputation. An offshore sandbar keeps the surf gentle, lifeguards are on duty, and the park offers walking trails, picnic shelters, and a nature center. Concession stands sell food and rent beach chairs and umbrellas. Weekend parking fills quickly, especially between November and April, so plan to arrive before 9 a.m.

## 5. Bill Baggs Cape Florida State Park

**Location:** 1200 S Crandon Blvd, Key Biscayne (about 25 minutes east)

At the southern tip of Key Biscayne, Bill Baggs is one of the most scenic beaches in all of Florida. The Cape Florida Lighthouse, built in 1825, anchors the park. The sand is soft, the water is clear, and the beach feels more remote than its distance from downtown Miami would suggest. Two casual restaurants, the Lighthouse Cafe and Boater's Grill, sit right on the beach. Entry fee is $8 per vehicle.

## 6. South Pointe Park Beach

**Location:** 1 Washington Ave, Miami Beach (about 20 minutes northeast)

![Cyclists Ride Along A Scenic Path In Mia 1](/images/blog/2029/05/7-stunning-beaches-within-reach-of-coconut-grove/cyclists-ride-along-a-scenic-path-in-mia-1.webp)


South Pointe sits at the very southern tip of Miami Beach, where Government Cut channels large cruise ships in and out of the Port of Miami. The beach is cleaner and less crowded than the main South Beach strip, and the adjacent park has walking paths, a playground, and green space. Watching cruise ships pass within a few hundred feet of the shore is a unique experience you will not get anywhere else in Miami. Metered street parking and nearby garages are available.

## 7. Lummus Park Beach

**Location:** Ocean Drive between 5th and 15th Streets, Miami Beach (about 25 minutes northeast)

This is the classic South Beach experience, with white sand, turquoise water, and the iconic Art Deco buildings of Ocean Drive as your backdrop. Lummus Park Beach is wide, lively, and packed with energy. It is the most crowded option on this list, but sometimes that is exactly what you want. Volleyball courts, lifeguard towers, and beachside bars line the shore. From Coconut Grove, take I-95 north to the MacArthur Causeway for the most direct route.

## Tips for Beach Days From Coconut Grove

- **Leave early.** Beach parking anywhere in Miami is a different experience at 8:30 a.m. versus 10:30 a.m.
- **Bring a beach wagon.** Miami sand is soft and deep, and hauling coolers and chairs from the parking lot is much easier with wheels.
- **Know the flag system.** Green means safe to swim, yellow means caution, red means stay out of the water, and purple signals dangerous marine life nearby.
- **Pack extra water.** Miami heat plus salt air leads to fast dehydration. Bring at least twice what you think you need.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Does Coconut Grove have its own beach?

Coconut Grove does not have a traditional sandy beach, but it sits directly on Biscayne Bay with waterfront parks and marina access. The closest proper beach is Matheson Hammock Park, about 10 minutes south.

### Which beach near Coconut Grove is best for kids?

Matheson Hammock Park Beach is the top pick for families with young children. The man-made atoll pool creates a calm, shallow wading area protected from open water waves.

### Are these beaches free to visit?

Hobie Beach and Lummus Park Beach are free. Others charge parking or entry fees ranging from $5 to $8 per vehicle. South Pointe has metered street parking.
