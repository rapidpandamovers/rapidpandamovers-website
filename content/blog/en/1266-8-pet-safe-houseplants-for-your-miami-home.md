Moving with pets means rethinking your houseplant collection. Many popular plants are toxic to cats and dogs, and the stress of relocation often leads pets to explore and chew things they normally ignore. These eight plants thrive in Miami's humid climate and are safe for your four-legged family members.

## Why Pet-Safe Plants Matter After a Move

Pets in new environments behave unpredictably. A cat that never touched your pothos for years might suddenly nibble leaves in an unfamiliar apartment. Dogs exploring a new backyard may dig up planters. Choosing non-toxic plants eliminates one major worry during an already stressful transition.

![A Vibrant Collection Of Tropical Indoor  1](/images/blog/2029/02/8-pet-safe-houseplants-for-your-miami-home/a-vibrant-collection-of-tropical-indoor--1.webp)


## 1. Spider Plant (Chlorophytum comosum)

One of the most forgiving houseplants and completely safe for pets. Spider plants thrive in Miami's humidity and tolerate low light conditions in north-facing Brickell condos. They produce baby plants you can propagate, making them ideal for filling multiple rooms in your new home.

**Care:** Bright indirect light, water when soil dries. Trim brown tips caused by fluoridated water.

## 2. Boston Fern (Nephrolepis exaltata)

This lush fern loves Miami's natural humidity. Hang one in your Coconut Grove bathroom or Coral Gables porch for maximum impact. Boston ferns are completely non-toxic to cats and dogs, even if they decide the fronds make good toys.

**Care:** Indirect light, consistent moisture, high humidity. Mist regularly in air-conditioned spaces.

## 3. Areca Palm (Dypsis lutescens)

Adds instant tropical appeal to any Miami home. Areca palms grow tall enough to fill empty corners in Kendall living rooms or Aventura sunrooms. Safe for both cats and dogs, these palms also improve indoor air quality.

**Care:** Bright indirect light, water when top inch of soil dries. Wipe leaves to prevent dust buildup.

## 4. Calathea (Calathea spp.)

Known for stunning patterned leaves, calatheas are non-toxic and love humidity. Varieties like Calathea medallion and Calathea ornata add visual interest to any Doral or Hialeah home. Their leaves fold up at night, earning them the nickname "prayer plants."

**Care:** Indirect light, consistent moisture, high humidity. Sensitive to chemicals in tap water.

## 5. Peperomia (Peperomia spp.)

Compact and pet-safe, peperomias come in dozens of varieties with different leaf shapes and colors. Perfect for small Miami apartments or adding greenery to home office desks. Watermelon peperomia is especially popular for its striped leaves.

**Care:** Bright indirect light, allow soil to dry between waterings. Avoid overwatering.

## 6. Parlor Palm (Chamaedorea elegans)

A smaller palm option that thrives in lower light conditions. Parlor palms are completely safe for cats and dogs and add elegance to entryways and bedrooms in any Miami neighborhood. They grow slowly, requiring less maintenance than larger palms.

![Vibrant Tropical Foliage With Large Leav 2](/images/blog/2029/02/8-pet-safe-houseplants-for-your-miami-home/vibrant-tropical-foliage-with-large-leav-2.webp)


**Care:** Low to medium indirect light, water when soil is slightly dry. Tolerates air conditioning well.

## 7. Polka Dot Plant (Hypoestes phyllostachya)

Adds vibrant pink, red, or white spots to your plant collection. Polka dot plants are non-toxic and thrive in Miami's humidity. They work well in mixed planters or as standalone accent plants in Miami Beach apartments.

**Care:** Bright indirect light for best color, consistent moisture. Pinch back leggy growth.

## 8. African Violet (Saintpaulia)

One of the few flowering houseplants that blooms reliably indoors and is safe for pets. African violets produce purple, pink, or white flowers year-round in Miami's climate. Perfect for kitchen windowsills or bathroom shelves with good light.

**Care:** Bright indirect light, water from below to avoid leaf spots. Feed monthly during growing season.

## Plants to Avoid With Pets

If you're moving, consider rehoming or giving away these common but toxic plants:
- **Pothos** - Causes mouth irritation and vomiting
- **Peace Lily** - Toxic to cats and dogs
- **Monstera** - Causes oral irritation
- **Snake Plant** - Mildly toxic, causes nausea
- **Fiddle Leaf Fig** - Causes oral irritation

## Where to Buy Pet-Safe Plants in Miami

Stock your new home with pet-friendly greenery:

- **Fairchild Tropical Botanic Garden** (Coral Gables) hosts regular plant sales with knowledgeable staff
- **Pinecrest Gardens** sells plants from their botanical collection
- **Local nurseries** in Little Haiti and Homestead offer unique tropical specimens
- Most **Home Depot** and **Lowe's** locations label pet-safe plants

## Moving Your Plants Safely

Plants should travel in your personal vehicle, not the moving truck. Pack them last, transport upright, and unpack first at your new home. Keep pets separated from plants during unpacking until you've confirmed placement.
