May brings the start of peak moving season to South Florida, and full-service moving becomes the go-to choice for families who want professionals to handle everything. With rising temperatures, afternoon thunderstorms beginning, and end-of-school relocations surging, handing the entire process to experienced movers removes weeks of stress.

## Why Full-Service Moving Makes Sense in May

Full-service means your moving company handles packing, loading, transport, unloading, and unpacking. For May moves specifically, this approach offers key advantages.

![Family Enjoys A Joyful Moment While Unpa 3](/images/blog/2028/05/full-service-moving-strategies-for-may-moves/family-enjoys-a-joyful-moment-while-unpa-3.webp)


Miami's transition from dry to wet season creates weather challenges that professional crews know how to manage. Afternoon thunderstorms start becoming regular in May, humidity rises, and temperatures climb into the upper 80s. [**Full-service moving**](/full-service-moving) professionals plan around these conditions, scheduling loading for early morning and protecting your belongings from moisture during transport.

## Key Strategies for May Full-Service Moves

### Book Two to Three Weeks Early

May demand climbs as school years end and leases turn over. Popular dates, especially weekends and month-end, fill fast. Booking early secures your preferred date and gives the moving company time to properly assess your belongings for accurate pricing.

### Create a Room Priority List for Unpacking

Tell your crew which rooms matter most. Kitchens and bedrooms first means your family can eat and sleep comfortably on night one, even if the living room is still stacked with boxes.

### Plan Around Afternoon Weather

Schedule your move for an early start. Our crews typically begin at 7 or 8 AM, completing the heaviest outdoor work before afternoon thunderstorms arrive. Full-service crews also bring weatherproof covers and protective wrapping.

### Inventory Special Items in Advance

Mention pianos, gun safes, antique armoires, or oversized TVs during the estimate. These items require specialized padding and sometimes additional crew members.

## Popular Miami Neighborhoods for May Moves

May is a busy time for relocations across Miami's diverse neighborhoods:

- **Brickell**: High-rise condos require elevator reservations and loading dock scheduling
- **Coral Gables**: Historic homes often have narrow driveways and landscaping to work around
- **Coconut Grove**: Tree-lined streets may need permits for moving trucks
- **Kendall**: Suburban moves often involve more square footage and garage storage
- **Wynwood**: Loft apartments and converted warehouses need specialized handling
- **Doral**: Growing families moving to this area appreciate a full-service approach
- **Aventura**: Condo associations often require insurance certificates and advance notice

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does full-service moving cost in Miami?

Full-service local moves in Miami-Dade typically range from $1,500 to $4,000 for a 2 to 3 bedroom home. The price includes packing materials, labor, transport, and basic unpacking. Factors like floor level, distance, and specialty items affect the final cost. May pricing may be slightly higher than winter months as demand increases.

### How long does a full-service move take?

Packing usually takes one full day. Loading, transport, and unloading typically take another day for local moves. A 3-bedroom home generally takes 2 days total from start to finish.
