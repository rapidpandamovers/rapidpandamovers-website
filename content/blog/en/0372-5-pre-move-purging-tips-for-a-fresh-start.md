Whether you're moving from Brickell to Coral Gables or relocating to Miami from out of state, purging before your move saves time, money, and stress. Here are five practical tips to help you declutter before moving day.

## 1. The Room-by-Room Approach

Tackle one room at a time, completely. Jumping between rooms creates chaos and makes progress feel impossible. Start with the room you use least, like a guest bedroom or home office, and work toward high-traffic areas like the kitchen.

![A Cardboard Box Labeled With Winter Clot 4](/images/blog/2025/11/5-pre-move-purging-tips-for-a-fresh-start/a-cardboard-box-labeled-with-winter-clot-4.webp)


## 2. The Destination Test

For each item, ask: "Will this have a place in my new home?" If the answer is uncertain, reconsider keeping it.

## 3. Set a Donation Deadline

Schedule donation pickup for one week before moving. This deadline prevents last-minute hoarding and second-guessing. In Miami, Goodwill and The Salvation Army offer free pickup for larger donations. Habitat for Humanity ReStore in Doral accepts furniture and home goods too.

![A Collection Of Labeled Cardboard Boxes  3](/images/blog/2025/11/5-pre-move-purging-tips-for-a-fresh-start/a-collection-of-labeled-cardboard-boxes--3.webp)


## 4. Sentimental Item Limit

Allow yourself one box for sentimental items with no practical use. Everything else must have a function in your new space.

## 5. Digital Declutter Too

Scan important papers and photos. Digital copies take no moving space and are easier to organize. Miami's humidity can damage paper documents over time, so going digital protects your important records while reducing what you need to pack.
