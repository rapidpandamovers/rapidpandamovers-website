A December move was probably not in your original plans. Maybe a lease ended early, a job transfer came through, or a home sale closed faster than expected. Whatever the reason, moving during the holidays adds layers of complexity that other months simply do not have. Here is how to handle a last-minute December move without derailing your family's holiday season.

## Why December Moves Are Different

December is actually one of the least busy months for the moving industry nationally. However, in South Florida the picture is different. Snowbird season brings a wave of seasonal residents, and end-of-year lease cycles create a mini surge in relocations. Add holiday parties, school breaks, and travel plans to the mix, and scheduling gets tight quickly.

![A Collection Of Labeled Cardboard Boxes  4](/images/blog/2029/12/december-last-minute-moving-advice-for-families/a-collection-of-labeled-cardboard-boxes--4.webp)


The good news: movers who are available in December tend to have more flexible schedules on weekdays. The challenge is that the weeks around Christmas and New Year's fill up fast, and many companies take holidays off entirely.

## Step-by-Step Plan for a Last-Minute December Move

### Week One: Lock In the Essentials

**Book your movers immediately.** Do not wait to get multiple quotes if you are within two weeks of your move date. Call your top choice, explain your timeline, and confirm availability. With [**last-minute moving**](/last-minute-moving) services, experienced companies can mobilize crews on short notice, but December weekends get claimed quickly.

**Create a priority packing list.** Instead of packing room by room (the standard approach), sort by necessity:
- Items you need daily until moving day (kitchen basics, toiletries, work equipment)
- Items your kids need for school and bedtime routines
- Holiday decorations you want at the new place
- Everything else

**Contact utilities.** Set up electricity, water, internet, and gas at your new address. In Miami-Dade, FPL transfers can be done online and typically take one business day.

### Week Two: Execute the Move

**Pack holiday items last.** If you are mid-holiday-decorating, leave decorations up until a day or two before the move. This keeps normalcy for the kids and saves you from packing and unpacking them twice.

**Coordinate with your children's school.** If your move crosses school district boundaries, get records transferred before winter break starts. Most school offices close for the holidays, so handle this early.

**Declutter with purpose.** December offers a unique advantage here. Charities are more active during the holiday season than any other time of year. Goodwill, Salvation Army, and local organizations often provide free pickup for furniture and household goods. Donating before your move reduces volume, lowers moving costs, and gives you a tax deduction before year-end.

## Saving Money on a December Move

Several cost-saving strategies work well for December:

- **Move midweek.** Tuesday through Thursday rates are typically lower, and availability is better.
- **Take advantage of holiday sales.** Black Friday and Cyber Monday deals on packing supplies, storage bins, and organizing products can save 30-50% over regular prices.
- **Ship heavy items separately.** Books, tools, and other dense items sometimes cost less to ship via UPS or FedEx than to include in a full moving truck.
- **Use what you have.** Suitcases, laundry baskets, and even trash bags work for soft items like linens and clothing.

## Keeping Kids Grounded During a Holiday Move

A December move hits kids differently than a summer move. They are already excited about the holidays, and adding moving stress can be overwhelming.

![Labeled Cardboard Boxes For Summer Cloth 1](/images/blog/2029/12/december-last-minute-moving-advice-for-families/labeled-cardboard-boxes-for-summer-cloth-1.webp)


**Maintain traditions.** If your family always bakes cookies, watches certain movies, or drives through a neighborhood for lights, fit those in around the move. Even if it is a scaled-down version, keeping some traditions provides stability.

**Give them ownership.** Let each child pack a "moving day bag" with their favorite toys, a blanket, snacks, and a tablet or book. Knowing they control their essentials reduces anxiety.

**Set up their rooms first.** On move-in day, prioritize getting kids' bedrooms functional. A familiar bedspread and a few favorite items on a shelf go a long way toward making a new space feel like home.

## Holiday Season Preparation Checklist

Before your December move, make sure to:

- [ ] Book movers at least one week in advance (two weeks preferred)
- [ ] Forward mail through USPS (can be done online)
- [ ] Notify banks, insurance, and subscription services of address change
- [ ] Transfer or set up utilities at the new address
- [ ] Get school records transferred before winter break
- [ ] Separate holiday gifts and decorations for easy access
- [ ] Arrange pet care or child care for moving day

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is December more expensive for moving?**
Not typically. December falls in the off-season for most of the country. In South Florida, rates are moderate compared to summer peak season, though availability can tighten around the holidays.

![Packed Cardboard Box Labeled Bedroom Ind 3](/images/blog/2029/12/december-last-minute-moving-advice-for-families/packed-cardboard-box-labeled-bedroom-ind-3.webp)


**How far in advance should I book a last-minute December move?**
One to two weeks is ideal. If you need to move within a few days, call directly rather than filling out online forms. Same-day and next-day service is sometimes available depending on crew schedules.

**Should I move before or after Christmas?**
The week after Christmas (December 26-31) often offers the best combination of availability and pricing. Most companies resume normal operations by December 26.

**Can I move on Christmas Eve or Christmas Day?**
Most professional moving companies do not operate on December 25. Some offer limited service on December 24. Plan around these dates if possible.
