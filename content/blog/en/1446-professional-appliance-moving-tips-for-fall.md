Major appliances are some of the heaviest and most damage-prone items in any move. A refrigerator weighs 200 to 400 pounds, a washer and dryer set adds another 250 to 350 pounds combined, and all of them have components that can break if handled wrong. Fall in South Florida adds humidity and heat into the equation, which affects defrost timelines and condensation risk. Here is how to prepare each appliance properly.

## Refrigerator and Freezer

The refrigerator requires the most lead time of any appliance. Start prep at least 48 hours before your move.

![Cardboard Moving Boxes In A Modern Kitch 5](/images/blog/2029/10/professional-appliance-moving-tips-for-fall/cardboard-moving-boxes-in-a-modern-kitch-5.webp)


### Two Days Before

- **Empty all food**: Anything perishable needs to go. Use coolers with ice packs for items you want to keep, and plan meals to use up what you can in the days leading up to the move
- **Turn off the ice maker**: If your fridge has a water line for ice and water dispensing, shut off the water supply valve behind or beneath the unit. Run the ice maker one final time to clear residual water from the line

### One Day Before

- **Unplug and defrost**: Turn off the refrigerator and leave both doors open. Place towels on the floor to catch meltwater. In Miami's October humidity, the defrost will produce more water than you might expect, so check the towels every few hours
- **Clean the interior**: Wipe down shelves and drawers with a baking soda solution. Remove all drawers, shelves, and bins and pack them separately in moving blankets or bubble wrap. Loose glass shelves are the most common refrigerator casualty during a move

### Moving Day

- **Tape the doors shut**: Use painter's tape or appliance straps to keep doors from swinging open during transport
- **Keep it upright**: Refrigerators should stay upright during the move whenever possible. Tilting past 45 degrees can allow compressor oil to flow into the cooling lines, which may require a 24-hour settling period before plugging back in
- **Protect the floor**: Use furniture sliders or a refrigerator dolly to move the unit without scratching hardwood or tile

### At the New Home

After delivery, let the refrigerator stand upright for at least two hours before plugging it in. If the unit was tilted significantly during transport, wait a full 24 hours. Check your owner's manual for the manufacturer's specific recommendation.

## Washer

Front-load and top-load washers have different prep requirements, but both need attention before moving day.

### Drain the Water

Run a spin or drain cycle with nothing inside the drum. This clears residual water from the pump and internal hoses. After the cycle completes, disconnect the hot and cold water supply hoses and let them drain into a bucket. Label the hoses so reconnection is straightforward.

### Install Transit Bolts

This is the step people miss most often, and it is the most expensive mistake. Front-load washers have a drum that floats on suspension springs and shock absorbers. During a move, the drum can swing freely and damage the internal components. Transit bolts lock the drum in place and prevent this.

Most washers ship with transit bolts, and you should have received them with your original purchase. If you have lost them, order replacements from the manufacturer. They are specific to your model and cost a fraction of what a drum repair or replacement would run.

Top-load washers generally do not require transit bolts, but check your manual to confirm.

### Disconnect and Secure

Unplug the power cord, disconnect the drain hose, and tape all hoses and cords to the back of the unit so they do not drag during transport.

## Dryer

Dryers are simpler to prepare than washers, but gas dryers add a critical safety step.

![Close Up Of Male Hands Operating A Blend 4](/images/blog/2029/10/professional-appliance-moving-tips-for-fall/close-up-of-male-hands-operating-a-blend-4.webp)


### Electric Dryers

- Clean the lint trap and the exhaust vent area
- Unplug the power cord
- Disconnect the exhaust vent hose from the wall
- Tape the cord and hose to the back of the unit

### Gas Dryers

- **Hire a licensed technician** to disconnect the gas line. Do not attempt this yourself. A faulty disconnection can cause gas leaks
- After the gas line is capped, clean the lint trap and disconnect the exhaust vent hose
- At your new home, have a licensed technician reconnect the gas line and test for leaks before operating the dryer

### Stacked Units

If your washer and dryer are stacked, they need to be separated before moving. The stacking kit that holds them together is designed for stationary use and will not keep the dryer secure during transport. Separate the units, prep each one individually, and restack at the new location.

## Dishwasher

Dishwashers are often built into cabinetry, which adds complexity to the removal process.

- Run an empty cycle with a cup of white vinegar to clean the interior and drain residual water
- Turn off the water supply and disconnect the supply line
- Disconnect the drain hose from the garbage disposal or sink drain
- Remove the mounting brackets that secure the dishwasher to the underside of the countertop
- Slide the unit out carefully to avoid scratching the floor

## Fall-Specific Considerations in Miami

October in South Florida still brings afternoon humidity in the 70 to 80 percent range and temperatures in the mid-80s. That affects appliance moves in a few ways:

![Crop Anonymous Female Chef Beating Eggs  3](/images/blog/2029/10/professional-appliance-moving-tips-for-fall/crop-anonymous-female-chef-beating-eggs--3.webp)


- **Condensation**: Moving a cold appliance into humid outdoor air produces heavy condensation on metal surfaces. Wipe down appliances before loading them onto the truck to prevent water from dripping onto other items
- **Mold risk**: Any residual moisture inside a washer drum or refrigerator interior can develop mold quickly in Miami's humidity. Leaving doors and lids open during the defrost and drain period is not optional here
- **Heat exposure**: Appliances sitting in a truck or on a driveway in direct sun can reach surface temperatures that warp plastic components. Minimize the time appliances spend outside the air-conditioned environment

## FAQ

**Do I need to defrost a frost-free refrigerator before moving?**
Yes. Frost-free models still have moisture in the system and need to be unplugged and drained. The defrost cycle prevents ice buildup during normal use, but the unit still contains water that will leak during transport if not addressed.

**Can movers disconnect and reconnect my appliances?**
Our crew handles electrical disconnection and reconnection for standard appliances. Gas line work requires a licensed technician, which we can help you coordinate.

**What happens if I forget to install the transit bolts on my washer?**
The drum can swing freely during transport, damaging the suspension springs, shock absorbers, and potentially the drum itself. Repair costs for this type of damage often exceed the cost of a new washer.

**Should I move old appliances or buy new ones at the new home?**
If your appliances are more than 10 years old, calculate the cost of moving them against the cost of replacement. Newer models are more energy-efficient, and the moving cost for heavy appliances can be significant, especially for long-distance moves.
