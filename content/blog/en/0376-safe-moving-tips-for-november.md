Moving a safe during November presents unique challenges that require careful planning and professional expertise. Whether you own a gun safe, fireproof document vault, or commercial security safe, these practical tips will help ensure your valuable security equipment arrives safely at your new Miami home.

## Understanding Safe Weight Classifications

Before scheduling your November move, determining your safe's exact weight is important. Safes fall into distinct weight categories that dictate the equipment and crew size needed:

![A Cheerful Man Carries A Large Couch Acr 3](/images/blog/2025/11/safe-moving-tips-for-november/a-cheerful-man-carries-a-large-couch-acr-3.webp)


**Lightweight safes (under 200 lbs)**: These portable security boxes typically include small fireproof document safes and compact gun safes. Two experienced movers with a standard dolly can usually handle these.

**Medium safes (200-500 lbs)**: This category covers most residential gun safes and medium-sized commercial safes. Requires a heavy-duty appliance dolly and a three-person crew.

**Heavy safes (500-1,000 lbs)**: Large gun safes and commercial vaults fall here. Specialized stair-climbing equipment and a four-person crew are essential.

**Extra-heavy safes (1,000+ lbs)**: Bank-grade vaults and oversized commercial safes require rigging equipment, hydraulic lifts, and a specialized crew.

## Pre-Move Preparation Steps

Proper preparation prevents damage and delays on moving day:

### 1. Empty the Safe Completely

Remove all contents before the move. This includes:
- Firearms (transport separately per Florida law)
- Ammunition (must be transported according to regulations)
- Important documents
- Jewelry and valuables
- Cash and precious metals

### 2. Secure Moving Parts

Lock the safe door and tape the handle to prevent accidental opening during transport. If your safe has interior shelving, remove and wrap these separately.

### 3. Document Current Condition

Photograph your safe from all angles before the move. Note any existing scratches or marks for your records.

### 4. Measure Doorways and Pathways

Verify your safe will fit through all doorways, hallways, and stairwells at both locations. Account for the equipment space needed alongside the safe.

**How do I find my safe's weight?**
Check the manufacturer's documentation, look for a plate inside the door, or search the model number online. If unknown, we can estimate during an in-person assessment.

**Should I move the safe empty or full?**
Always empty your safe before moving. This reduces weight, prevents damage to contents, and is required for firearm transport compliance.

**Will moving damage my safe's locking mechanism?**
Properly executed moves don't damage safe mechanisms. However, we recommend testing your lock before and after the move to document functionality.
