## Why Same Building Moves Are Trickier Than You Think

Most people assume moving within your building is simple. No truck, short distance, easy day. But Miami high-rises have elevator schedules, loading dock rules, and management offices that close at 5pm sharp. Skip the planning and you'll spend your Saturday hauling boxes through the lobby while residents give you the stink-eye.

## Key Tips for Same Building Moving in June

### Book Early for Summer Season

June kicks off Miami's summer moving rush. Families relocate before school starts, snowbirds head north, and every elevator in Aventura gets reserved three weeks out. Contact your building management and moving company at least two weeks ahead.

### Work Your Building's System

Every Miami high-rise has different rules. Wynwood lofts might let you use the freight elevator anytime. The Four Seasons in Brickell? You're looking at specific four-hour windows and security escorts. Call your property manager first, get the requirements in writing, and share them with your movers.

### Beat the Afternoon Storms

June through October means daily 3pm thunderstorms in Miami. Schedule your move to start at 8am and wrap up by early afternoon. Our crews plan loading around the weather because nobody wants to haul a sectional through a lightning storm in the Edgewater breeze.

### Take Inventory Room by Room

Even though you're staying in the same building, document everything. Photograph furniture, note existing scratches, and keep a checklist. If something gets dinged in the hallway, you'll know exactly when it happened.
