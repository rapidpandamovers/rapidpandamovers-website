# Pool Table Moving: Disassembly, Transport, and Setup

Moving a pool table isn't like moving a sofa or a bookshelf. A standard slate pool table weighs between 700 and 1,000 pounds, and its playing surface is precision-engineered to be perfectly flat. One wrong move during transport can crack the slate, tear the felt, or warp the frame. Here in Miami, where many homes and condos have game rooms as a selling point, we handle pool table relocations every week.

## Why Pool Tables Can't Be Moved in One Piece

Some homeowners assume a pool table can be tipped on its side, loaded onto a truck, and carried to the next location. That approach almost always ends badly. The slate bed is typically a three-piece set made of Italian or Brazilian stone, with each piece weighing 150 to 250 pounds. Moving the table assembled puts enormous stress on the joints, legs, and frame. Even a short trip across a parking lot can shift the slate out of alignment, and the vibration during transit can crack it along seam lines.

## The Disassembly Process

Professional [**Pool Table Moving**](/pool-table-moving) starts with a careful teardown. Our team follows a specific sequence to protect every component:

- **Remove the pockets and rails**: The rail bolts are loosened in a specific pattern to avoid warping. We bag and label every bolt, nut, and washer so nothing gets lost.
- **Remove the felt**: If the felt is in good condition and the owner wants to reuse it, we carefully pull the staples and roll the cloth without folding it. Folding creates creases that affect ball roll. Miami's humidity can make older felt more fragile, so we assess its condition before deciding on reuse.
- **Detach the slate pieces**: Each slate section is lifted off the frame using specialized slate jacks. We wrap every piece in thick moving blankets and secure them with straps. Slate must always be transported flat and upright, never stacked.
- **Break down the frame and legs**: The frame is disassembled and wrapped separately. Legs are removed, labeled, and padded.

## Transporting Everything Safely

Once the table is fully disassembled, loading matters just as much. Slate pieces go into the truck first, standing vertically in custom-built slots or secured against the truck wall with heavy-duty straps. They should never lay flat on the truck bed where road vibrations can cause hairline fractures. The frame, rails, and legs get their own blanket wrapping and are positioned so nothing shifts during the drive.

For Miami condo moves, we also plan for elevator dimensions and hallway clearances before moving day. Some high-rise buildings in Brickell and Downtown require freight elevator reservations, which we coordinate ahead of time.

## Setup and Reassembly at Your New Location

Reassembly is where experience really shows. Our crew:

- **Rebuilds the frame** and checks it for level on your specific floor. South Florida's tile and concrete slab floors can have subtle slopes that affect play.
- **Positions and aligns the slate** using machinist-level precision tools. The seams between slate pieces are filled with beeswax to create a perfectly smooth playing surface.
- **Re-levels everything** with micro-adjustable shims. We use a precision level at multiple points across the surface, not just the center.
- **Stretches and attaches the felt**, making sure it is taut and wrinkle-free.
- **Reinstalls the rails and pockets**, then tests ball roll in every direction to confirm the table plays true.
