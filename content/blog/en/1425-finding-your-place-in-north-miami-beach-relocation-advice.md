North Miami Beach sits in the northeast corner of Miami-Dade County, offering a mix of waterfront living, cultural diversity, and affordability that draws families and professionals from across the country. If you are considering a September move here, this guide covers the neighborhoods, amenities, and logistics you need to know.

## What Makes North Miami Beach Stand Out

Despite its name, North Miami Beach is mostly an inland city, though the **Eastern Shores** neighborhood does border the Intracoastal Waterway. The city is home to about 43,000 residents and sits between Aventura to the north and North Miami to the south. A diverse population, including a large Haitian-American community and growing Caribbean and Latin American communities, gives the city its distinctive cultural identity.

![Palm Trees Sway Against A Backdrop Of Wh 1](/images/blog/2029/09/finding-your-place-in-north-miami-beach-relocation-advice/palm-trees-sway-against-a-backdrop-of-wh-1.webp)


The **Ancient Spanish Monastery** on West Dixie Highway, an 800-year-old structure shipped from Spain by William Randolph Hearst and rebuilt stone by stone, ranks among the most unusual landmarks in all of South Florida. You will not find anything else like it in Miami-Dade County.

### Location and Accessibility

**I-95** runs just west of the city, and **Biscayne Boulevard (US-1)** cuts through it, providing direct access to Downtown Miami (about 15 miles south) and Fort Lauderdale (about 15 miles north). **163rd Street** serves as the main commercial corridor, with grocery stores including **Publix**, **Aldi**, and international markets. Miami International Airport is about 25 minutes away.

### Community and Lifestyle

**Oleta River State Park**, at 1,043 acres the largest urban park in Florida, sits right on the city's eastern border. It offers mountain biking trails, kayaking, a swimming beach, and shaded picnic pavilions. **Greynolds Park** (265 acres) on the northwest side provides camping, kayaking, and hiking through mangrove forests. These two parks alone give North Miami Beach more green space access than most Miami-Dade cities.

The city runs free community events at **Allen Park** and hosts youth programs through its recreation centers. September brings slightly cooler mornings, making outdoor activities more comfortable than the summer months.

## Neighborhoods to Consider

**Eastern Shores** is a gated waterfront community with single-family homes on canals, private docks, and direct Intracoastal access. It is the most expensive part of North Miami Beach but appeals to boaters and anyone wanting water views.

**Skylake** surrounds the **Skylake Mall** area near 183rd Street and features mid-century single-family homes and condos at more accessible price points. Families appreciate the proximity to **Ojus Elementary** and the commercial options along NE 6th Avenue.

**North Miami Beach City Center**, near the intersection of 163rd Street and West Dixie Highway, offers condo living and easy walking access to shops and restaurants. The **Mall at 163rd Street** anchors the area.

### Choosing Your Perfect Spot

Consider these factors when selecting a neighborhood:

- **Proximity to work and schools**: The commute to Downtown Miami or Aventura varies significantly by neighborhood
- **Property types**: Eastern Shores skews toward single-family homes; the central and western areas have more condos and apartments
- **Budget**: Eastern Shores waterfront homes start around $500K+, while condos near 163rd Street can be found for under $250K
- **Lifestyle preferences**: Boaters gravitate to Eastern Shores; families often prefer Skylake for its school access

## Moving to North Miami Beach in September

September falls within hurricane season, so check forecasts when scheduling your move. That said, late September brings lower humidity and temperatures averaging 84-88°F, which is more manageable than peak summer. Moving companies also tend to have better availability since the summer rush has subsided.

### Timing Your Move

- **Best moving days**: Weekdays, especially Tuesday through Thursday, typically mean lower rates and less traffic on I-95
- **Weather window**: Schedule morning moves to beat afternoon thunderstorms, which remain common through September
- **Traffic**: Avoid the 163rd Street corridor during rush hours (7:30-9 AM and 4:30-6:30 PM)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is North Miami Beach actually on the beach?**
Not exactly. The city is mostly inland, though the Eastern Shores neighborhood borders the Intracoastal Waterway. Actual ocean beaches at Sunny Isles Beach and Haulover Beach are a short drive east.

**What is the cost of living like in North Miami Beach?**
North Miami Beach is more affordable than neighboring Aventura and Sunny Isles Beach. Median home prices hover around $350K-$400K citywide, with apartments renting from about $1,800/month for a one-bedroom.

**How long does it take to commute to Downtown Miami?**
About 25-35 minutes via I-95, depending on traffic. The commute to Brickell or Downtown is straightforward since I-95 runs right along the western edge of the city.
