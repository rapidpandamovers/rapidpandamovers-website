November puts families in a tight spot when it comes to moving. School is in session, Thanksgiving takes up the last week of the month, and everyone has holiday commitments stacking up. But November also offers some real advantages: cooler weather in South Florida, lower demand compared to summer, and the motivation to settle in before the December holidays begin.

Here is how to make a full-service move work for your family this month.

## Why November Works for Family Moves

### Better Weather, Lower Stress

![A Cheerful Family Takes A Selfie While S 3](/images/blog/2029/11/november-full-service-moving-advice-for-families/a-cheerful-family-takes-a-selfie-while-s-3.webp)


Miami-Dade's November temperatures average in the mid-70s to low 80s, a significant drop from the summer months when heat and occasional showers make moving physically draining. Humidity falls too, which means less sweat for your crew and less moisture risk for upholstered furniture and electronics.

### More Availability, Better Rates

Summer is peak moving season nationwide. By November, demand drops and professional moving companies have more open dates. This translates to:

- **Easier scheduling**: You are more likely to get your first-choice date
- **Competitive pricing**: Some companies offer off-peak rates from October through February
- **Dedicated attention**: Smaller crews can take more time per job during slower months

### The Thanksgiving Window

The week of Thanksgiving offers a strategic opportunity. Many families take time off work and school, creating a natural window for moving without burning separate vacation days. If your employer gives Wednesday through Friday off, you can use those days to move, unpack essentials, and still have Thanksgiving dinner in your new home.

## Planning Your Full-Service Move

### What Full-Service Actually Covers

When you book [full-service moving](/full-service-moving), the company handles every stage of the relocation:

1. **Pre-move survey**: A walk-through of your home to assess volume, special items, and access challenges
2. **Packing**: Professional packers wrap, box, and label every item in your home
3. **Loading**: Trained crews load the truck using protective blankets, straps, and strategic weight distribution
4. **Transport**: Your belongings travel in a dedicated or shared truck to the new address
5. **Unloading**: Furniture and boxes are placed in the correct rooms at your new home
6. **Unpacking** (optional): Crews unpack boxes and remove packing materials

For families, the packing and unpacking components save the most time. Instead of spending two weekends filling boxes while managing kids, you hand that work to professionals and focus on the logistics that only you can handle.

### Create a Room Priority List

Before the movers arrive at your new home, decide which rooms get set up first:

- **Kids' bedrooms**: Familiar bedding and a few favorite toys help children adjust on night one
- **Kitchen**: Being able to prepare meals avoids expensive takeout during the first week
- **Master bedroom**: Adults need rest to manage the rest of the unpacking
- **Bathrooms**: Towels, toiletries, and shower curtains are easy wins that make the house feel livable

Give this list to your moving crew so they can prioritize unloading those rooms first.

### Handle School Transitions Early

If your move crosses school district lines, start the enrollment process before moving day:

- **Request records** from your current school, including transcripts, immunization records, and IEP or 504 plans if applicable
- **Contact the new school** to confirm enrollment requirements and start dates
- **Tour the new school** with your child before the move if possible
- **Connect with the guidance office** to discuss placement and any support services

Miami-Dade County Public Schools allows mid-year transfers, but processing takes time. Starting in October for a November move gives the system time to handle paperwork.

### Coordinate Around Thanksgiving

If you are moving the week of Thanksgiving:

- **Monday or Tuesday**: Schedule the move for early in the week to leave buffer days
- **Wednesday**: Use this day for unpacking essentials and a grocery run
- **Thursday**: Have Thanksgiving in your new home, even if it is simple
- **Friday through Sunday**: Continue unpacking at a relaxed pace

Avoid scheduling your move for the Wednesday before Thanksgiving. Traffic across South Florida spikes that day as people travel for holiday plans, and highway congestion can delay your moving truck.

## Packing Tips for Families with Children

### Involve Kids in the Process

![Family Enjoying Selfie Time While Surrou 1](/images/blog/2029/11/november-full-service-moving-advice-for-families/family-enjoying-selfie-time-while-surrou-1.webp)


Children handle moves better when they feel included rather than sidelined:

- Let younger kids decorate their moving boxes with stickers or markers
- Give older children the responsibility of packing their own rooms with guidance
- Have each child pack a personal backpack with items they want to keep close during the move

### Pack a "First Night" Box

Label one box per family member as the first-night box. Include:

- **For kids**: Pajamas, a favorite stuffed animal, toothbrush, a book, and a nightlight
- **For adults**: Phone chargers, medications, a change of clothes, basic toiletries, and important documents
- **For the household**: Paper towels, toilet paper, a few garbage bags, snacks, and bottled water

Load these boxes last so they come off the truck first.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How far in advance should I book a full-service move in November?**
Two to three weeks is usually sufficient for November since it is off-peak season. However, if you need a specific date during Thanksgiving week, book as early as possible since those dates are popular.

**Will movers work on Thanksgiving Day?**
Most professional moving companies are closed on Thanksgiving Day. Some offer limited availability on Black Friday. Plan your move for earlier in the week.

**How long does a full-service move take for a 3-bedroom home?**
Packing typically takes one full day. Loading, transport, and unloading usually take another day for local moves within Miami-Dade. Total door-to-door time for a full-service local move averages 2 days.

**Should I tip the movers?**
Tipping is not required but is appreciated for good service. A common guideline is $20 to $40 per mover for a full-day job, or 15% to 20% of the total move cost split among the crew.
