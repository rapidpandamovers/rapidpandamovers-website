After years of October moves across Miami-Dade County, we've seen it all. From waterfront mansions in Coral Gables to high-rise condos in Brickell, some items present unique challenges that require specialized knowledge and equipment. Here are the strangest things professional movers have been asked to transport.

## 1. Grand Pianos

We once moved a 1920s Steinway from a historic Coconut Grove estate to a new home in Pinecrest. These massive instruments require specialized equipment, multiple crew members, and expertise to avoid damage to both the piano and property. Miami's humidity makes this even trickier since wood expands and contracts with moisture changes.

![A Ghostly Figure Wearing Sunglasses Capt 3](/images/blog/2029/10/6-weird-things-people-have-asked-movers-to-move/a-ghostly-figure-wearing-sunglasses-capt-3.webp)


## 2. Wine Collections

A Coral Gables collector asked us to move 2,000 bottles to their new wine cellar in Aventura. Temperature sensitivity, fragile bottles, and proper positioning require climate-controlled handling and custom crating. In South Florida's heat, we schedule these moves during early morning hours and use refrigerated trucks.

## 3. Hot Tubs and Spas

Draining, disconnecting, and moving these heavy items requires professionals familiar with the process. We've hauled spa units from Doral backyards to Miami Beach penthouses. The real challenge? Getting them through tight spaces and sometimes up onto rooftops.

## 4. Large Aquariums

A Kendall family had us relocate their 500-gallon saltwater reef tank to their new home in Homestead. Fish need separate transport in temperature-controlled containers. The tank itself must be drained, carefully padded, and moved level to prevent cracking. We coordinated with their aquarium maintenance company to ensure nothing went wrong.

![Creepy Portrait Of A Young Man Biting In 2](/images/blog/2029/10/6-weird-things-people-have-asked-movers-to-move/creepy-portrait-of-a-young-man-biting-in-2.webp)


## 5. Pool Tables

Slate tops require disassembly by specialists. We moved a custom-built table from a Wynwood loft to a game room in Miami Lakes. Improperly moved pool tables never play the same, so we always bring in certified billiard technicians for disassembly and reassembly.

## 6. Fine Art and Sculptures

Miami's thriving art scene means we regularly handle valuable pieces. From Wynwood gallery installations to private collections in Fisher Island, custom crating, climate control, and white-glove handling protect artwork during transit. One memorable move involved a 12-foot bronze sculpture that barely fit through the service elevator.
