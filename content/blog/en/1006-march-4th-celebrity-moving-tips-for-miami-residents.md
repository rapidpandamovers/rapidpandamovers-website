Miami's social season peaks in March. Art Basel may be over, but yacht shows, charity galas, and private events keep the spotlight on South Florida's elite neighborhoods. ## Why March Works for Celebrity Moves in Miami

March sits at the tail end of Miami's dry season. Temperatures stay in the comfortable low 80s, rain is rare, and the shorter tourist crowds compared to December-February mean fewer prying eyes. For clients moving between Star Island, Fisher Island, and Coconut Grove estates, the timing is strategic.

## Key Tips for Celebrity Moving in March

### Plan Ahead for Peak Times

March is busy for luxury moves as seasonal residents make decisions about their South Florida properties. Book your vetted crew at least 3-4 weeks ahead to ensure the same trusted team handles both locations.

### Request the Same Background-Checked Crew

Consistency reduces risk. When the same NDA-signed team handles your origin and destination, fewer people learn your address, schedule, and inventory. Our celebrity crews undergo background checks and have years of experience with high-profile Miami clients.

### Schedule During Low-Traffic Hours

Early morning or late evening moves attract less attention. March's comfortable temperatures make pre-dawn loading feasible without the safety concerns of summer heat. Our crews bring portable lighting for early starts and late finishes.

### Document Everything with Photography

High-value artwork, wine collections, and designer furniture need thorough documentation. Our team photographs and catalogs every piece before wrapping, providing insurance-grade records that protect your investment during transit.
