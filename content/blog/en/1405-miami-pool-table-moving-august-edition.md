August is one of the busiest months for moving in Miami, and if your household includes a pool table, the move gets more complicated. A standard slate pool table weighs between 700 and 1,000 pounds, and the combination of South Florida's summer heat, afternoon thunderstorms, and high demand for movers means you need a solid plan.

Here is what to know about moving a pool table in Miami during August.

## Why August Pool Table Moves Need Extra Planning

Three factors make August pool table moves in Miami more demanding than other times of year:

![Close Up Of A Billiard Table With Chalk  1](/images/blog/2029/08/miami-pool-table-moving-august-edition/close-up-of-a-billiard-table-with-chalk--1.webp)


**Heat and humidity.** Slate itself handles heat well, but the felt (cloth) and wood components are sensitive to moisture. Humidity above 70 percent, which is typical for August in Miami, can cause the wood frame to swell and the felt to absorb moisture. Both issues affect how the table plays after reassembly.

**Afternoon storms.** Miami's summer weather pattern brings daily thunderstorms, usually between 2:00 and 5:00 PM. A pool table cannot be exposed to rain during loading or unloading. Professional crews time the outdoor portions of the move around these storms.

**Peak-season demand.** August is prime moving season thanks to back-to-school timelines and lease turnovers. Specialty movers book up fast, so scheduling two to three weeks ahead is a minimum.

## The Disassembly Process

A pool table should never be moved in one piece. Proper disassembly includes:

1. **Remove the pockets and rails.** The rails are bolted to the slate and frame. Each bolt is labeled for reassembly.
2. **Remove the felt.** If the felt is in good condition, it can be carefully removed and reused. Stapled felt is harder to save than glued felt.
3. **Separate the slate sections.** Most tables have a three-piece slate top. Each piece weighs 150 to 250 pounds and must be carried on edge, not flat.
4. **Disassemble the frame and legs.** The frame comes apart into manageable sections.

Every piece gets wrapped and padded for transport. Slate pieces in particular need custom padding to prevent chipping at the edges.

## Protecting the Felt in Miami's Humidity

The felt (or cloth) is the most vulnerable part of a pool table during a summer move. Here is how to protect it:

- **Remove it before moving day if possible.** Felt that sits in a hot, humid truck for hours can stretch or absorb moisture that affects playability.
- **Store felt in a climate-controlled space.** Do not leave it in the truck or garage overnight.
- **Consider replacement.** If your felt is already worn, moving day is a good time to schedule new cloth installation during reassembly.

Many pool table owners in Miami choose to replace felt after a move regardless, since the cost is relatively modest compared to the value of a properly playing table.

## Timing Your August Move

For a pool table move in August, schedule the start time as early as your building allows. A 7:00 or 8:00 AM start gives the crew the best window to complete loading before afternoon storms roll in.

**Weekday moves** are easier to schedule and often cost less. If you can avoid Friday and the weekends, you will have more flexibility.

**Coordinate with your general move.** Pool table disassembly and reassembly take time on both ends. If you are moving the rest of your household the same day, the pool table crew may work alongside or separately from your general movers.

## What to Look for in a Pool Table Mover

Not every moving company handles pool tables. The specialty requires specific skills and equipment:

![Vibrant Billiard Balls Neatly Arranged O 2](/images/blog/2029/08/miami-pool-table-moving-august-edition/vibrant-billiard-balls-neatly-arranged-o-2.webp)


- **Disassembly and reassembly experience.** Ask how many tables they move per month.
- **Leveling equipment.** After reassembly, the table must be leveled precisely. Professional crews use machinist levels, not phone apps.
- **Slate handling tools.** Proper dollies and edge protectors prevent damage.
- **Insurance coverage.** Make sure the mover's policy covers pool tables specifically. A three-piece slate table can cost $3,000 to $10,000+ to replace.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Moving the table assembled.** This damages the frame, slate alignment, and felt. Always disassemble.
- **Using general movers without pool table experience.** The weight distribution and fragility of slate require specific know-how.
- **Skipping leveling after reassembly.** A table that is off by even a fraction of an inch will affect ball roll and game quality.
- **Ignoring the new room's floor.** Confirm the floor in your new space can handle the weight. Carpet, tile, and hardwood each require different leveling approaches.

## FAQ

**How much does it cost to move a pool table in Miami?**
Typical costs range from $300 to $600 for a local move, depending on table size, distance, and whether stairs or tight spaces are involved. Felt replacement adds $200 to $400.

**Can I move a pool table myself?**
It is technically possible, but not recommended. The slate alone weighs 450 to 750 pounds total, and improper handling can crack the slate, damage the frame, or injure someone.

**How long does pool table disassembly and reassembly take?**
Plan for 1 to 2 hours for disassembly and 2 to 3 hours for reassembly including leveling.

**Does moving a pool table affect its level?**
Yes. Every pool table must be re-leveled after a move, even if it is going into a similar space. Floor conditions, leg placement, and shim requirements will differ.
