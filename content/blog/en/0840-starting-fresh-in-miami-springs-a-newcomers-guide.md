You are tired of the traffic on I-95, the endless apartment hunting in overpriced Brickell, and the feeling that Miami has become too crowded. Then you discover Miami Springs, a hidden gem where banyan trees shade quiet streets and neighbors actually wave when you drive by. Six months from now, you could be grilling in your backyard, walking to the golf course, and wondering why you did not make this move sooner.

## Why Choose Miami Springs?

Miami Springs offers something rare in Miami-Dade County: a genuine small-town feel just 10 minutes from Miami International Airport. Founded in 1926 by aviation pioneer Glenn Curtiss, this community of 14,000 residents maintains its historic charm while offering modern conveniences.

![Loc A Miami Monorail Travels Past Modern Hig 1](/images/blog/2027/07/starting-fresh-in-miami-springs-a-newcomers-guide/loc-a-miami-monorail-travels-past-modern-hig-1.webp)


The city draws families seeking top-rated schools like Miami Springs Senior High, professionals who want a short commute to Doral or Downtown Miami, and retirees looking for a quiet community with established character. Home prices run 20-30% below comparable properties in Coral Gables, making Miami Springs one of the best values in the county.

### Location and Accessibility

Miami Springs sits at the intersection of convenience and calm. The Palmetto Expressway (SR 826) runs along the eastern edge, putting you 15 minutes from Aventura or 20 minutes from Kendall. Miami International Airport is literally next door, which frequent travelers appreciate.

The Metrorail station in Hialeah connects you to Downtown Miami, Brickell, and Dadeland without fighting traffic. For daily errands, Westland Mall and the shops along Okeechobee Road are just minutes away, while Doral's dining and retail scene sits right across the Palmetto.

### Community and Lifestyle

Miami Springs runs on tradition. The annual Holiday Parade down Westward Drive has happened every December since 1960. The Miami Springs Golf and Country Club hosts weekend tournaments where three generations play together. At the Circle pool complex, kids learn to swim in the same lanes their parents did.

The city operates five parks, including the popular Miami Springs Recreation Center with its tennis courts, basketball courts, and after-school programs. Local restaurants like the Sandalwood Cafe and Bangkok Bangkok have been serving the same families for decades. This is not a transient community; people move here and stay.

## Neighborhoods to Consider

Miami Springs packs distinct neighborhoods into just 2.9 square miles. Each area has its own personality, and the one you choose shapes your daily life.

### The Country Club Section

The area around Miami Springs Golf and Country Club features the largest lots in the city, many exceeding 10,000 square feet. You will find mid-century modern homes alongside Spanish-style villas, most built in the 1950s and 60s. Golf course frontage commands premium prices, but side streets offer more reasonable options with the same established trees and quiet atmosphere.

### Downtown Miami Springs

The blocks surrounding Westward Drive and Canal Street offer walkable living. Grab coffee at a local cafe, walk to the library, and pick up groceries without starting your car. Homes here tend to be smaller 1940s-era cottages, but many have been renovated with modern interiors. Young families and first-time buyers favor this area for its convenience and relative affordability.

### The Eastern Section

East of the Curtiss Parkway, closer to Hialeah, you will find more diverse housing stock including duplexes and smaller single-family homes. Prices run lower here, and the neighborhoods feel more urban. Access to the Palmetto Expressway makes commuting easier if you work in North Miami or Aventura.

### Choosing Your Perfect Spot

Consider these factors when selecting your Miami Springs neighborhood:

- **Commute direction**: The Country Club section works for Doral commuters, while the eastern areas suit those heading north
- **School zones**: Miami Springs Elementary and Miami Springs Middle School serve the entire city, so all neighborhoods have equal access
- **Property age**: Older homes have character but may need updates; factor renovation costs into your budget
- **Lot size**: Larger lots mean more yard work but more space for kids, pets, and entertaining

April marks the end of dry season in South Florida, making it ideal for moving. Temperatures average 75-85 degrees, afternoon thunderstorms have not yet started their daily pattern, and humidity remains manageable.

### Timing Your Move

When planning your relocation, keep these local factors in mind:

- **Weekday moves**: Miami Springs residential streets handle moving trucks easily, but parking can get tight near the downtown area during school pickup hours between 2-3pm
- **Golf course traffic**: Saturday mornings see increased traffic around the Country Club section due to tee times; schedule accordingly
- **City permits**: Miami Springs requires parking permits for moving trucks that will block streets; your moving company should handle this, but confirm before moving day

## Services to Locate

As a new Miami Springs resident, you will need to establish relationships with local service providers:

- **Healthcare**: Palmetto General Hospital in Hialeah sits 5 minutes away, and Baptist Health has an urgent care facility on Okeechobee Road
- **Schools**: Register at Miami-Dade County Public Schools; bring proof of residency, immunization records, and previous school transcripts
- **Utilities**: Miami-Dade Water and Sewer handles water; FPL provides electricity; set up accounts before your move date
- **Recreation**: The Miami Springs Recreation Center offers resident rates for pool access, tennis courts, and youth programs

## Benefits of Living in Miami Springs

Miami Springs delivers advantages that appeal to families, professionals, and retirees alike.

**Affordability without compromise.** Home prices run 20-30% below neighboring Coral Gables while offering similar lot sizes and architectural charm. Your housing dollar stretches further here.

**Genuine community.** Unlike transient apartment complexes or sprawling suburbs, Miami Springs residents know their neighbors. Block parties, holiday traditions, and local businesses create lasting connections.

**Central location.** The Palmetto Expressway and proximity to Miami International Airport mean you can reach jobs throughout the metro area without excessive commute times.

**Established character.** Mature landscaping, historic architecture, and thoughtful city planning give Miami Springs a sense of place that new developments cannot replicate.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does it cost to move to Miami Springs?

Local moves within Miami-Dade typically run $400-$1,200 depending on home size and distance. A 2-bedroom move from Brickell to Miami Springs averages $600-$800 with professional movers.

### What are Miami Springs property taxes like?

Miami Springs has its own city millage rate in addition to Miami-Dade County taxes. Total rates run around 2% of assessed value, slightly lower than Coral Gables but higher than unincorporated areas.

### Are there any deed restrictions in Miami Springs?

Yes, the historic sections have architectural guidelines that govern exterior changes. The city building department reviews permits for compliance with community standards.
