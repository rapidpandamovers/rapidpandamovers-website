Moving day chaos unsettles pets. Strangers enter your home, furniture disappears, and routines vanish. Dogs pace and bark. Cats hide in boxes about to be loaded onto trucks. Without preparation, your pet's stress becomes your stress on an already demanding day.

Here's what to pack in a dedicated pet survival kit that stays with you throughout the move.

## The Carrier or Crate

Your pet's carrier should be easily accessible from the moment movers arrive until you're settled in your new home. This is their safe space when activity peaks.

![A Blue Volvo Truck Parked Outside A Ware 1](/images/blog/2028/04/the-pet-owners-moving-day-survival-kit/a-blue-volvo-truck-parked-outside-a-ware-1.webp)


For dogs too large for carriers, designate a specific room at both locations where they can stay during active loading and unloading. In Miami high-rises, this might be a bathroom or bedroom cleared early or last. For houses, the backyard can work if it's securely fenced and you're checking on them regularly.

## Current Medications and Vet Records

Pack all pet medications in your personal bag, not in moving boxes. Include:

- Current prescriptions
- Flea and tick preventatives
- Any calming medications your vet recommended for the move
- Recent vaccination records (required if you're boarding or need emergency vet care)
- Your vet's contact information and your new area's emergency vet clinic

In Miami-Dade, the nearest 24-hour emergency vet to your new location is worth knowing before moving day.

## Food, Water, and Bowls

Pack three days' worth of your pet's regular food in sealed containers. Moving often means delayed grocery runs, and sudden food changes upset digestive systems.

![A Truck Loaded With Sacks Of Potatoes Hi 2](/images/blog/2028/04/the-pet-owners-moving-day-survival-kit/a-truck-loaded-with-sacks-of-potatoes-hi-2.webp)


Include:
- Travel water bowl or collapsible bowl
- A gallon of water from your old home (familiar water helps some pets adjust)
- Treats for rewards and comfort
- Any special dietary items

## Familiar Items

Pets rely on scent for comfort. Pack items that smell like home:

- Their regular bed or blanket (unwashed)
- A favorite toy
- A worn T-shirt of yours for their carrier

Resist the urge to wash these items before the move. The familiar scent is the point.

## Cleanup Supplies

Stress causes accidents. Pack in an accessible spot:

- Paper towels
- Enzyme cleaner (crucial for neutralizing urine odors)
- Waste bags
- Disposable litter pan and litter for cats

You'll likely need these at both locations before you unpack your regular supplies.

## Identification and Leashes

Update your pet's tags with your new address before moving day if possible. Keep on hand:

![Vintage Truck Carrying Heavy Cargo Bags  3](/images/blog/2028/04/the-pet-owners-moving-day-survival-kit/vintage-truck-carrying-heavy-cargo-bags--3.webp)


- Collar with current tags
- Backup collar or harness
- Leash (even if your dog doesn't usually wear one in the house)
- Recent photos of your pet on your phone (in case they escape during the chaos)

Microchip information should be updated with your new address as soon as you know it.

## A Plan for Moving Day

Beyond the physical kit, have a plan:

**Morning**: Before movers arrive, secure your pet in their designated safe space with their carrier, water, and a few treats. Hang a sign on the door: "Do Not Open - Pet Inside."

**During loading**: Keep your pet contained. Even well-behaved animals bolt when stressed and doors stand open.

**Transport**: Never put pets in the moving truck. They travel in your vehicle with air conditioning. In Miami's heat, even fall months can be too warm for pets in parked cars.

**Arrival**: Before releasing your pet, do a safety check. Are all doors and windows closed? Are there any gaps where a scared cat could hide and get stuck? Is the yard fenced properly?

**First hours**: Set up one room with their bed, food, water, and litter box. Let them adjust to this space before exploring the whole house.
