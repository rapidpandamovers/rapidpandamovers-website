Welcome to your September guide for moving to Kendall! Whether you're relocating from within South Florida or making a bigger move this fall, understanding your new community is important for a successful move.

## Why Choose Kendall?

Kendall stands out as one of Miami-Dade County's most appealing locations. The community offers a suburban and diverse atmosphere and is particularly known for affordable housing and family amenities.

![A Family Drawing Together On Cardboard E 4](/images/blog/2027/04/making-kendall-your-new-home-a-practical-guide/a-family-drawing-together-on-cardboard-e-4.webp)


The area attracts families, professionals, and retirees alike, thanks to its quality of life, convenient access to major employment centers, and excellent amenities.

### Location and Accessibility

One of Kendall's biggest advantages is its strategic location within Miami-Dade County. Residents enjoy easy access to major highways, public transportation, and nearby communities. Whether you're commuting to Downtown Miami or Brickell, heading to Coral Gables for shopping, or traveling to Coconut Grove for dining, Kendall provides convenient connections. The Dadeland area sits just to the north, offering quick access to the Metrorail.

### Community and Lifestyle

Kendall residents enjoy a strong sense of community. Local parks, recreation facilities, and community events bring neighbors together throughout the year. The fall season is particularly vibrant, with fall moving season activities bringing residents together.

## Neighborhoods to Consider

When planning your move to Kendall, The Crossings area is particularly popular with newcomers. Other notable areas include Kendall Lakes, Three Lakes, and the neighborhoods near Dadeland Mall. Each area has its own character. Some offer walkable dining and shopping, while others provide quiet, tree-lined streets perfect for families.

### Choosing Your Perfect Spot

Consider these factors:

- **Proximity to work and schools**: Factor in your daily commute
- **Local amenities**: Parks, shopping, dining, and entertainment options
- **Property types**: Single-family homes, condos, townhouses, or apartments
- **Community vibe**: Family-oriented, young professional, or mixed demographics

## Moving to Kendall in September

September is an excellent time to consider your move. The fall weather in South Florida provides gradually cooling temperatures and decreasing humidity for the moving process.

### Timing Your Move

When planning your relocation, consider:

- **Best moving days**: Weekdays often offer better availability and rates
- **Weather considerations**: Fall in Miami means gradually cooling temperatures and decreasing humidity
- **Local events**: Check for any street closures or community events that might affect your move

## What to Expect in Kendall

New Kendall residents can expect a car-dependent lifestyle with excellent access to shopping and dining. The area is more suburban than urban Miami, which means larger lots and more space between neighbors. Traffic on US-1 and Kendall Drive (SW 88th Street) gets congested during rush hour. You will find a diverse mix of cultures, with strong Hispanic and Caribbean influences reflected in local restaurants and businesses. Summers are hot and humid with daily afternoon thunderstorms.

## Essential Services to Locate

As a new Kendall resident, you'll want to find:

- **Healthcare facilities**: Baptist Health South Florida, Kendall Regional Medical Center, and numerous urgent care clinics
- **Schools**: Highly rated options including G. Holmes Braddock Senior High, Coral Reef Senior High, and several charter schools
- **Shopping**: Dadeland Mall, The Falls, and numerous shopping plazas along Kendall Drive
- **Recreation**: A.D. Barnes Park, Tropical Park, and Zoo Miami nearby
