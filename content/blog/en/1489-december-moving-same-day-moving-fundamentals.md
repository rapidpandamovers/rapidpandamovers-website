December is one of the slowest months for the moving industry, and that works in your favor. Whether a lease is ending, a job transfer came through, or a closing date shifted unexpectedly, [same-day moving](/same-day-moving) during the holiday season is more available and often more affordable than peak-season moves.

Here is how to make a same-day December move work smoothly.

## Why December Works for Same-Day Moves

Most people avoid moving during the holidays, which means moving companies have more open slots. That translates into several advantages:

![A Cheerful Man Carries A Large Couch Acr 3](/images/blog/2029/12/december-moving-same-day-moving-fundamentals/a-cheerful-man-carries-a-large-couch-acr-3.webp)


- **Better availability** -- Crews that are fully booked during summer often have same-day openings in December.
- **Lower rates** -- Off-peak pricing can save 10 to 20 percent compared to summer moves.
- **Flexible scheduling** -- You are more likely to get your preferred morning start time.
- **Mild weather** -- South Florida winters average highs in the mid-70s with low humidity, which is far more comfortable for moving than the summer heat and rain.

The main exception is the week between Christmas and New Year's, when some companies reduce their schedules. Book at least a few days in advance if your move falls during that window.

## How Same-Day Moving Works

Same-day moving means you call a moving company and the crew arrives the same day, typically within two to four hours. Here is the general process:

1. **Call early** -- Contact us in the morning for the best same-day availability. Afternoon calls still work, but morning bookings give the crew more time.
2. **Describe your move** -- Share the number of rooms, any heavy or bulky items, and the distance between locations. This helps us assign the right crew size and truck.
3. **Confirm the estimate** -- We provide an hourly rate quote based on what you describe. No hidden fees.
4. **Crew arrives** -- The team shows up with the truck, blankets, dollies, and straps. You point, they pack and load.
5. **Delivery** -- Everything gets transported to your new place and unloaded where you want it.

## What to Prepare Before the Crew Arrives

Even with a same-day timeline, a little preparation goes a long way:

![Moving Company Team With Van Parked Outs 1](/images/blog/2029/12/december-moving-same-day-moving-fundamentals/moving-company-team-with-van-parked-outs-1.webp)


- **Pack what you can** -- Box up smaller items, clothes, and kitchen supplies. If you do not have boxes, garbage bags and suitcases work in a pinch.
- **Separate valuables** -- Keep documents, jewelry, medication, and electronics with you rather than on the truck.
- **Clear pathways** -- Move shoes, rugs, and small furniture away from doorways and hallways so the crew can work efficiently.
- **Confirm building access** -- If you are moving into or out of a condo, call the building management to check elevator reservation requirements and loading dock availability.
- **Arrange parking** -- Make sure there is a spot for the moving truck at both locations. In Miami, double-check for any holiday event street closures.

## December-Specific Considerations

### Holiday Event Conflicts

Miami hosts several large events in December, including Art Basel (early December), the Bayfront Park New Year's Eve celebration, and various neighborhood parades. Check for road closures near your origin and destination addresses.

### Building Schedules

Some condo buildings limit move-ins during the holiday weeks or require extra advance notice. Call your building manager before booking your move.

### Utility Transfers

Utility companies may have reduced hours during the holidays. Set up your FPL (Florida Power & Light) and water service transfers online a day or two before your move to avoid delays.
