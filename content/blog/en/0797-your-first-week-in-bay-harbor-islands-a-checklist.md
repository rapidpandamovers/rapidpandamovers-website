The boxes are in. The movers are gone. Now what? Your first week in Bay Harbor Islands sets the tone for your new life on these exclusive islands. Here is exactly what to do, day by day.

## Day 1: The Essentials

### Grocery Run
Head to **Publix on 96th Street** in Surfside (5 minutes away). Stock up on basics, but do not overdo it. You will discover preferred spots as you settle in.

![Loc Aerial View Of Miami Harbor With Boats A 2](/images/blog/2027/06/your-first-week-in-bay-harbor-islands-a-checklist/loc-aerial-view-of-miami-harbor-with-boats-a-2.webp)


### Find Your Coffee Spot
**Crema Gourmet** on Kane Concourse opens early and makes excellent espresso. Grab a cortado and watch the morning foot traffic. This is where you start recognizing faces.

### Secure Your Parking
If you are in a condo, confirm your parking space assignment with building management today. Street parking on the islands is limited, especially for West Island single-family homes.

## Day 2-3: Administrative Tasks

### Update Your Address
- Florida driver's license (visit DMV in Aventura)
- Vehicle registration
- Voter registration
- Bank and credit card accounts
- Insurance policies

### Utilities and Services
- Set up FPL electric account
- Register for Miami-Dade water service
- Choose internet provider (Xfinity and AT&T serve the area)
- Arrange trash and recycling pickup

### Meet Building Management
For condo residents, introduce yourself to the property manager. Learn the building rules for guests, pool hours, and package delivery. Ask about upcoming building events.

## Day 4: Explore Your Island

### Walk Kane Concourse
This is East Island's main street. Walk the strip and note:
- **Il Mulino** for Italian fine dining
- **Cosmica** for upscale Mexican
- **The Deli Den** for quick lunches
- Local banks and dry cleaners

![Svc A U Haul Moving Truck Parked On Gravel W 1](/images/blog/2027/06/your-first-week-in-bay-harbor-islands-a-checklist/svc-a-u-haul-moving-truck-parked-on-gravel-w-1.webp)


### Cross to West Island
Walk across the bridge to see the residential character. Notice the boat docks behind homes. Wave to anyone you see gardening. Small-town friendliness is real here.

### Find the Beach Access
Surfside beach is your closest option, about 10 minutes walking from East Island. Haulover Beach is 5 minutes by car and offers more space.

## Day 5: Set Up Your Routine

### Morning Exercise Options
- **Walking the loop**: Both islands combined make a pleasant 2-mile walk
- **Bay Harbor Community Center**: Classes and gym access
- **Haulover Park**: Running trails and outdoor fitness areas

### Evening Relaxation
Sunset views from the Broad Causeway bridge are spectacular. Bring a camera. Walk to Bal Harbour Shops for window shopping and people watching.

## Day 6-7: Connect with the Community

### Introduce Yourself to Neighbors
Ring doorbells or wave from across the yard. Bay Harbor Islands residents tend to be welcoming. Ask for restaurant recommendations. Everyone has favorites.

![Svc High Angle View Of An Asda Delivery Truc 2](/images/blog/2027/06/your-first-week-in-bay-harbor-islands-a-checklist/svc-high-angle-view-of-an-asda-delivery-truc-2.webp)


### Join Local Groups
- Bay Harbor Islands Community Facebook Group
- Ruth K. Broad School PTA (if you have school-age children)
- Town Hall meetings (open to residents)

### Explore Beyond the Islands
Now that essentials are handled, venture out:
- **Aventura Mall** (15 minutes north) for major shopping
- **Bal Harbour Shops** (walking distance) for luxury retail
- **Haulover Marina** for fishing charters and boat rentals

## Quick Reference: Essential Numbers

- **Bay Harbor Islands Police**: (305) 866-6241
- **Town Hall**: (305) 866-6241
- **Fire/Emergency**: 911
- **Miami-Dade Water**: (305) 665-7477
- **FPL Outage Line**: 1-800-4-OUTAGE

## Welcome to Island Life

Bay Harbor Islands moves at its own pace. The first week is about finding your rhythm. By week two, you will have favorite walking routes, know the barista by name, and understand why people stay here for decades.
