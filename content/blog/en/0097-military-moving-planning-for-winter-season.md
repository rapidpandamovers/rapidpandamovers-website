As we enter December, many Miami residents find themselves preparing for a move. Whether you're taking advantage of the winter season or responding to life changes, military moving becomes an important factor to plan for.

## Why Military Moving Matters During Winter

The winter season presents specific considerations for anyone planning a move. From weather conditions to scheduling availability, understanding these factors can make the difference between an easy move and a stressful experience.

![A Person Handling A Moving Box With A Ca 4](/images/blog/2024/12/military-moving-planning-for-winter-season/a-person-handling-a-moving-box-with-a-ca-4.webp)


Miami's winter weather requires careful planning. [**Military Moving**](/military-moving) professionals understand these local conditions and can help you handle potential challenges. Whether you're dealing with afternoon thunderstorms or the intense Florida sunshine, having experienced movers makes all the difference.

Service members stationed at Homestead Air Reserve Base or moving to the Key West Naval Air Station will find Miami's winter months (December through February) offer the most comfortable moving conditions. The humidity drops, temperatures stay in the low 70s, and you avoid the summer hurricane season entirely.

## Key Tips for Military Moving in December

### Plan Ahead for Peak Times

December can be a busy time for moving companies. With winter activities and seasonal transitions, scheduling your military moving early ensures you get your preferred date and time. Many military families relocating to South Florida prefer neighborhoods near bases, including Kendall, Cutler Bay, and Florida City for Homestead ARB, or areas like Coral Gables and Coconut Grove for those working at SOUTHCOM in Doral.

### Keep all documentation organized for reimbursement purposes

This single step can save hours of stress and potential problems on moving day. Our experienced crews have seen what happens when this step is skipped.

### Consider the Weather

South Florida's winter weather is predictable in some ways and surprising in others. Professional movers know how to protect your belongings from humidity, rain, and heat exposure during transport. Even in December, Miami can see occasional rain showers, though nothing like the afternoon thunderstorms of summer. The lower humidity means less risk of moisture damage to electronics and wooden furniture during your PCS move.

### Prepare Your Belongings

Take time to inventory your items before the move. This is especially important for military moving, as proper documentation helps ensure everything arrives safely at your new location.
