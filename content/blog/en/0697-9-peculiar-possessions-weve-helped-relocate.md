After thousands of moves across Miami-Dade, our crews have handled just about everything. From Coral Gables mansions to Brickell high-rises, these are the most unusual items we have successfully relocated, and what it takes to move them safely.

## 1. Grand Pianos

A Steinway grand piano can weigh over 1,000 pounds and has thousands of delicate moving parts. We have moved concert grands from Coconut Grove estates and baby grands into 40th-floor Brickell condos. Each piano requires a specialized piano board, padded blankets, and a crew trained specifically in piano moving. The humidity in South Florida also means extra care to protect the wood finish and internal mechanisms during transport.

## 2. Wine Collections

Miami collectors take their wine seriously, and so do we. One Pinecrest client had a 2,000-bottle collection worth over $200,000. Wine must stay at consistent temperatures during transport, so we use climate-controlled trucks and move collections during cooler morning hours. Bottles travel horizontally in custom wine boxes to keep corks moist. We have relocated wine cellars from Key Biscayne to Coral Gables without a single broken bottle.

## 3. Hot Tubs and Spas

Hot tubs are popular in Miami backyards, but they are a challenge to move. A standard 6-person hot tub weighs 500-800 pounds empty. The process involves draining, disconnecting electrical and plumbing, then using specialized equipment to lift and transport without damaging the shell. We have moved hot tubs from Doral pool decks to Aventura rooftop terraces, working with electricians and plumbers to ensure safe reinstallation.

## 4. Large Aquariums

Miami's tropical fish hobby is huge, and we have moved aquariums ranging from 100 to 500 gallons. The fish go into temporary containers with battery-powered aerators. The tank itself gets drained, with gravel and decorations packed separately. Large acrylic tanks must stay perfectly level during transport to prevent stress cracks. One Kendall client had a custom 400-gallon saltwater reef tank with live coral. That move took 8 hours of careful planning and execution.

## 5. Pool Tables

A professional pool table has a slate bed weighing 450-800 pounds, and it must be moved in sections. We disassemble the rails, pockets, and legs first, then remove the slate pieces with a crew of four. The felt usually needs replacing after a move, so we coordinate with local billiard shops in Miami for professional re-felting. One wrong bump during transport can crack the slate, so this is never a rush job.

## 6. Fine Art and Sculptures

Miami's art scene means we regularly move pieces worth more than most cars. Wynwood gallery owners and Coral Gables collectors trust us with original paintings, bronze sculptures, and installations. Each piece gets custom crating built to its exact dimensions. We use climate-controlled trucks to prevent damage from Miami's heat and humidity. One recent move included a 12-foot steel sculpture that required a crane and a police escort through Brickell streets.

## 7. Full Gym Equipment

Home gyms exploded in popularity, and now we move commercial-grade equipment from Miami homes regularly. A Peloton weighs 135 pounds. A fully loaded squat rack with plates can exceed 1,000 pounds. We have moved complete home gyms from Palmetto Bay to Miami Lakes, including disassembling cable machines and reassembling them at the new location. The key is having crews who know how these machines come apart and go back together.

## 8. Classic Cars

Miami's car culture means we have moved everything from vintage Porsches to restored muscle cars. A 1965 Mustang or a classic Corvette needs enclosed transport with soft tie-downs that will not scratch the paint. We coordinate with specialty auto transporters for long-distance moves, but for local relocations from one garage to another, our crews handle the loading and securing. One Homestead collector had us move three classic Chevys to his new warehouse in one day.

## 9. Medical Equipment

Hospital beds, oxygen concentrators, and mobility devices require careful handling and often need to be set up immediately at the destination. We have helped elderly clients move from Miami Beach condos to assisted living facilities in Kendall, making sure their medical equipment is unpacked and operational before we leave. Stairlifts and wheelchair ramps sometimes need to be disassembled and reinstalled, which we coordinate with medical equipment technicians.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much extra does it cost to move unusual items?

Pricing depends on the item's size, weight, and special requirements. Grand pianos typically add $300-500 to a move. Wine collections depend on the number of bottles and distance. We provide free quotes for all specialty items.

### Do you insure high-value items like art and wine collections?

Yes. Our standard liability coverage applies to all items, and we offer additional valuation protection for high-value pieces. We recommend documenting valuable items with photos before the move.

### How far in advance should I schedule a specialty move?

For items requiring special equipment like cranes or climate-controlled trucks, book at least two weeks ahead. During peak moving season (May through August), three weeks is safer.
