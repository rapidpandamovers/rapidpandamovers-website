Thinking about moving to Brickell? Miami's financial district has transformed into one of the city's most desirable residential neighborhoods, packed with high-rise condos, rooftop pools, and more restaurants per block than almost anywhere else in South Florida.

## Why Choose Brickell?

Brickell is Miami's densest neighborhood, home to roughly 40,000 residents who live in gleaming towers along Brickell Avenue and the surrounding streets. The area is often called the "Manhattan of the South" for good reason. You can walk to work, grab dinner, hit the gym, and pick up groceries without ever getting in your car.

![A Vibrant View Of Miami S Skyscrapers By 2](/images/blog/2029/06/moving-to-brickell-insider-tips-and-local-insights/a-vibrant-view-of-miami-s-skyscrapers-by-2.webp)


The neighborhood draws young professionals, international buyers, and families who want an urban lifestyle with a tropical backdrop. If you like being in the middle of the action, Brickell delivers.

### Location and Getting Around

Brickell sits just south of Downtown Miami and the Miami River, with Coconut Grove to the south. The Metrorail stops at Brickell Station, and the free Metromover loops through the neighborhood with stops at Fifth Street, Eighth Street, and Financial District. I-95 is a few blocks west, and the Rickenbacker Causeway connects you to Key Biscayne.

For day-to-day errands, most Brickell residents walk or use rideshare. The neighborhood scores well for walkability thanks to its grid layout and density of shops and restaurants at street level.

### Community and Lifestyle

Brickell has a social energy that sets it apart from quieter Miami neighborhoods. Happy hours spill onto sidewalks along South Miami Avenue. Weekend brunches pack the restaurants at Mary Brickell Village and Brickell City Centre. Fitness culture runs strong here, with boutique studios and rooftop yoga scattered throughout the area.

The neighborhood also hosts regular community events, from art walks to outdoor markets, especially during the cooler months from November through April.

## What Makes Brickell Special

### Mary Brickell Village

This open-air lifestyle center on South Miami Avenue is the social hub of the neighborhood. With restaurants like Batch Gastropub and Novecento, a Publix for groceries, and rotating pop-up events in its central plaza, Mary Brickell Village is where residents spend their evenings and weekends.

### Brickell City Centre

Opened in 2016, this mixed-use development brought luxury retail, a movie theater, and a climate-controlled outdoor mall to the heart of Brickell. It also includes Saks Fifth Avenue and a food hall that draws crowds from across Miami.

### Brickell Key

This small island just off the mainland offers a quieter alternative to the bustle of Brickell Avenue. Connected by a short bridge, Brickell Key has its own restaurants, a waterfront jogging path, and some of the best skyline views in Miami.

### Living Options

Brickell is overwhelmingly a condo and apartment market. Expect to choose from:

- **Luxury high-rises**: Buildings like SLS Brickell, Brickell Heights, and Reach at Brickell City Centre with resort-style amenities
- **Mid-range condos**: Older towers along Brickell Avenue that offer lower price points
- **Rental apartments**: Newer buildings with flexible lease terms for those not ready to buy
- **Townhouses**: A small but growing inventory in newer mixed-use developments

### Cost of Living

Brickell is one of Miami's pricier neighborhoods, though not as expensive as waterfront areas in Miami Beach or Key Biscayne. One-bedroom condos typically rent from $2,500 to $4,000 per month, with purchase prices varying widely by building and floor. HOA fees in newer buildings can be significant, so factor those into your budget.

## Tips for Moving to Brickell

Summer is peak moving season in Miami, and Brickell's high-rise environment adds some specific wrinkles to plan for.

### Timing Your Move

- **Elevator reservations**: Most Brickell condo buildings require you to reserve the freight elevator days or weeks in advance. Miss this step and you could be rescheduling your entire move
- **Loading zones**: Street parking is limited and metered. Your moving company will need to use building loading docks, which also require advance booking
- **Building rules**: Many buildings restrict move-in times to weekday business hours and require proof of insurance from your moving company
- **Weather**: Plan for morning moves to beat the afternoon thunderstorms that roll through almost daily from June through September
- **Traffic**: Avoid scheduling your move during rush hour, when Brickell Avenue and South Miami Avenue slow to a crawl

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Brickell a good neighborhood for families?**
Brickell works well for families who want an urban lifestyle. There are parks like Simpson Park and the Brickell Hammock, plus charter and private school options nearby. That said, it is more geared toward young professionals and couples than some of Miami's suburban neighborhoods.

**How walkable is Brickell?**
Very. Brickell consistently ranks as one of Miami's most walkable neighborhoods. You can reach restaurants, groceries, gyms, and the Metrorail station on foot from most buildings.

**What is traffic like in Brickell?**
Morning and evening rush hours are busy on Brickell Avenue. However, if you live and work within the neighborhood, you may not need a car at all. The Metromover is free and connects Brickell to Downtown and the Omni area.
