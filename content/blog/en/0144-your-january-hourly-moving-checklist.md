## Key Tips for Hourly Moving in January

### Plan Ahead for Peak Times

January can be a busy time for moving companies. With winter break activities and seasonal transitions, scheduling your hourly moving early ensures you get your preferred date and time. Keep in mind that snowbird season means heavier traffic on I-95 and the Palmetto, so plan your route accordingly.

### Have everything packed and ready before the crew arrives to maximize efficiency

This single step can save hours of stress and potential problems on moving day. Our experienced crews have seen what happens when this step is skipped.

### Consider the Weather

South Florida's winter weather is predictable in some ways and surprising in others. January temperatures typically range from 65-78°F with low humidity, making it one of the best months for moving. Rain is rare during the dry season, but professional movers still protect your belongings from any unexpected moisture. Schedule your move for morning hours to take advantage of the pleasant winter weather.

### Prepare Your Belongings

Take time to inventory your items before the move. This is especially important for hourly moving, as proper documentation helps ensure everything arrives safely at your new location.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does hourly moving cost in Miami?
Hourly moving typically costs $100-$180 per hour for a 2-person crew with a truck. A 3-person crew runs $140-$220 per hour. Most local moves take 2-6 hours depending on the size of your home.

### What is the minimum booking for hourly moving?
Most companies require a 2-hour minimum. For small moves (studio or 1-bedroom), 2-3 hours is usually sufficient if everything is packed and ready before the crew arrives.

### How can I keep hourly moving costs down?
Pack everything before the crew arrives, disassemble furniture in advance, and have a clear path from rooms to the truck. Every minute of preparation saves money on the clock.
