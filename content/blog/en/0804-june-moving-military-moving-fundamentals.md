## Key Tips for June PCS Moves in Miami

### Book 4-6 Weeks in Advance

June is peak season, period. Service members from Homestead Air Reserve Base, USCG Sector Miami, and SOUTHCOM headquarters in Doral all compete for the same moving dates. Contact your Transportation Office as soon as orders drop, and book your movers the same week if going with a PPM or partial DITY move.

### Keep Your Documentation Tight

TMO reimbursement paperwork has no patience for disorganization. Before moving day:

- Photograph every item of value, including serial numbers on electronics
- Keep weight tickets, receipts, and the Bill of Lading in a folder you carry personally
- Document any pre-existing damage so you don't get blamed for it later
- Save fuel receipts if doing any portion of a DITY move

Our crews photograph and inventory as we load, giving you a paper trail that holds up when you file your travel voucher.

### Work Around Miami Weather Patterns

South Florida's summer weather follows a predictable pattern: clear mornings, building clouds by noon, thunderstorms between 3-6pm, then clearing again by evening. Schedule your load-out for 7am start times. By the time storms hit, your belongings should be wrapped, loaded, and on the road.

For service members moving into Miami from drier climates, know that cardboard boxes left in a non-air-conditioned garage soften overnight from humidity. Use plastic bins for anything valuable, or keep boxes inside until load time.

### Prepare Heat-Sensitive Items

Take time to inventory items that can't handle a 120-degree truck interior if left in the sun:

- Vinyl records, CDs, and old photographs
- Candles, crayons, and anything wax-based
- Medications (especially insulin and other temperature-sensitive prescriptions)
- Electronics and batteries
- Musical instruments with wood components

These items should travel in your POV with AC running, or specify climate-controlled transport if shipping long distance.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How far in advance should I book summer PCS movers?

For June moves in South Florida, book 4-6 weeks ahead minimum. If your orders drop in June for a June report date, call us immediately. We can sometimes accommodate short-notice moves, but availability tightens fast during peak season.

### Will my items be safe in the summer heat?

Standard moving trucks can reach 120-140 degrees inside when parked. We schedule early morning load times and keep trucks moving rather than parked. For long-distance moves, we use climate-controlled options for heat-sensitive items. Items like medications, vinyl records, and electronics should travel in your personal vehicle with AC.

### Do you handle both PPM/DITY and full TMO moves?

Yes. We work with service members doing partial DITY moves (you keep the weight difference payment) as well as coordinating with TMO-contracted carriers. We provide certified weight tickets and documentation either way.
