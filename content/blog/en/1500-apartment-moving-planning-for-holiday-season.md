Moving into or out of an apartment during the holiday season adds layers that do not exist at other times of year. Buildings have restricted hours, elevators are booked for deliveries and party setups, and scheduling a moving crew gets harder as December fills up. Here is how to plan an apartment move that works around all of it.

## Why Holiday Season Moves Are Different

### Building Restrictions Tighten

![Contemporary Apartment Blocks With Balco 2](/images/blog/2029/12/apartment-moving-planning-for-holiday-season/contemporary-apartment-blocks-with-balco-2.webp)


Many Miami condo and apartment buildings restrict moving activity during the holidays. Some ban moves entirely on Christmas Eve, Christmas Day, New Year's Eve, and New Year's Day. Others limit moving to specific morning windows when lobby traffic is lighter. If you do not confirm the building's holiday moving policy in writing before booking your crew, you risk showing up to a locked freight elevator.

### Scheduling Gets Competitive

December is not peak moving season in most of the country, but South Florida is the exception. Seasonal residents returning for winter, new tenants taking advantage of year-end lease deals, and families wanting to settle before school resumes in January all drive demand. Book your moving date at least three to four weeks ahead.

### Weather Is Unpredictable

Miami's winter weather is generally mild, but cold fronts can drop temperatures into the 50s, and December rain is not uncommon. Professional movers plan for both, bringing floor runners to protect lobby areas from wet shoes and wrapping furniture to prevent moisture damage during loading.

## Your Holiday Apartment Moving Timeline

### 4-6 Weeks Before Move Day

- Confirm your move-in or move-out date with building management
- Request the freight elevator reservation in writing
- Ask about required deposits, insurance certificates, and floor protection rules
- **[Get your free quote](/quote)** and lock in your moving date

### 2-3 Weeks Before Move Day

- Begin packing non-essential items: off-season clothes, books, decor
- Notify utility companies of your address change
- Forward your mail through USPS
- Confirm parking arrangements for the moving truck at both locations

### 1 Week Before Move Day

- Pack remaining items, labeling every box by room
- Set aside an essentials box: medications, phone charger, toiletries, one change of clothes, basic kitchen supplies
- Confirm your freight elevator time and truck parking with both buildings
- Verify your moving crew's arrival time

### Moving Day

- Have building access credentials ready for your moving crew
- Meet the team at the truck or lobby to walk them through the building's rules
- Keep hallways and elevator lobbies clear of personal items
- Do a final walkthrough of your old unit before handing over keys

## Building Coordination Tips

### Freight Elevator Scheduling

This is the single most common point of failure for apartment moves. In high-rise buildings, freight elevators are shared among all residents, maintenance teams, and delivery services. During the holidays, they are also used for building decorations and event setups.

Reserve your time slot as early as your building allows. Ask for a backup window in case of delays. Most buildings give you a 3-4 hour block; make sure your movers know the exact window so they can work efficiently within it.

### Insurance and Deposits

Many buildings require a Certificate of Insurance (COI) from your moving company before allowing a move. They may also require a refundable deposit to cover potential damage to common areas. Professional movers carry the insurance and can provide COIs quickly, but do not leave this for the last minute. Request it a week before your move.

### Parking and Loading Zones

Some buildings have dedicated loading docks. Others require you to reserve street parking or arrange for a temporary no-parking zone with the city. In Downtown Miami, Brickell, and Miami Beach, street parking for a 26-foot moving truck is not guaranteed without advance planning.

## Holiday-Specific Considerations

**Package deliveries compete for elevator time.** December brings a flood of online shopping deliveries. Your building's freight elevator may be busier than usual during afternoon hours. Morning moves often go more smoothly.

**Staff availability changes.** Building management offices may close early or operate with reduced staff during the holiday week. Get all approvals and paperwork handled before December 23rd.

**Traffic patterns shift.** Miami's holiday traffic is different from regular commute patterns. Tourist traffic increases in beach areas, while residential neighborhoods may be quieter. Your movers should plan routes based on the actual day of your move, not typical weekday patterns.

## FAQ

**Can I move on Christmas Day?**
Most buildings prohibit moving on major holidays. Even if your building allows it, finding an available moving crew on December 25th is difficult and typically costs a premium. Schedule around the holiday.

![Smiling Mover Giving Thumbs Up From A Mo 3](/images/blog/2029/12/apartment-moving-planning-for-holiday-season/smiling-mover-giving-thumbs-up-from-a-mo-3.webp)


**How far in advance should I book holiday season movers?**
At least 3-4 weeks for December moves. Popular dates like the weekends before and after Christmas fill up fast.

**What if my freight elevator slot is too short?**
Professional movers experienced with apartment moves work within tight windows. For a one-bedroom, 3-4 hours is usually sufficient. For larger units, discuss timing with both your movers and building management to arrange an extended slot if needed.
