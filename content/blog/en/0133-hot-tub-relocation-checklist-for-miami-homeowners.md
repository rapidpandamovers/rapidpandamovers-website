# Hot Tub Relocation Checklist for Miami Homeowners

Relocating a hot tub in Miami means dealing with fenced yards, screened-in patios, HOA regulations, and the ever-present heat and humidity. Forgetting a single step can turn a smooth move into an expensive headache. Use this checklist to make sure everything is handled before, during, and after your spa relocation.

## Two Weeks Before the Move

- **Schedule your movers.** Book a professional [**Hot Tub Moving**](/miami-hot-tub-moving) team at least two weeks in advance. During Miami's peak moving season (May through September), you may need even more lead time.
- **Measure access paths at both locations.** Walk the route from the tub's current position to where the truck will park. Then do the same at your new property. Measure gate widths, side yard clearances, and any overhead obstructions like pergolas or lanai frames.
- **Check HOA rules.** Many communities in Kendall, Doral, and Weston have specific rules about when movers can operate, where trucks can park, and whether hot tubs are even allowed. Get written approval if needed.
- **Schedule an electrician.** You'll need a licensed electrician to disconnect the 240V circuit at your current home and reconnect it at your new one. Book them for the day before and the day after the move.
- **Contact your spa manufacturer.** Some warranties have specific requirements for relocation. A quick call to the manufacturer can save you from voiding your coverage.

## One Week Before the Move

- **Stop adding chemicals.** Give the water a few days to off-gas before draining. This makes disposal safer and reduces chemical exposure during the draining process.
- **Order any needed supplies.** Pick up moving blankets, stretch wrap, or a submersible pump if you plan to handle draining yourself.
- **Confirm access at both properties.** If you need a fence panel removed or a gate widened, arrange that now. Some Miami homeowners have to temporarily remove a section of their vinyl or aluminum fence to create enough clearance.
- **Notify your HOA.** If your community requires advance notice for moving trucks or exterior work, submit that request now.

## The Day Before Moving Day

- **Drain the hot tub.** Use a submersible pump or gravity drain through a garden hose. In Miami's heat, a drained tub dries faster, but give it several hours.
- **Vacuum out remaining water.** Use a wet/dry vac to get water from jets, footwells, and low spots.
- **Have the electrician disconnect wiring.** The dedicated 240V spa circuit needs to be safely disconnected and capped.
- **Disconnect plumbing.** If your tub has a dedicated water feed or hard-plumbed drain, disconnect those lines.
- **Remove accessories.** Pull off the cover, steps, headrests, filter cartridges, and any loose parts. Pack them separately.
- **Clean the shell.** Wipe everything down and let it dry. South Florida humidity means mold can start forming within hours on a damp surface.

## Moving Day

- **Clear the path.** Move patio furniture, potted plants, and anything else out of the access route.
- **Be present or have someone available.** The crew may have questions about the route, parking, or placement at the new location.
- **Verify padding and strapping.** Your movers should wrap the shell in blankets and secure it with heavy-duty straps for transport.

## After Arrival

- **Position the tub on a level, reinforced pad.** Concrete pads are ideal. Pavers work if they're properly set. Bare grass won't cut it.
- **Have the electrician wire the new circuit.** In Miami-Dade County, this work typically requires a permit and inspection.
- **Reconnect plumbing and fill the tub.** Check all connections for leaks before filling completely.
- **Balance the water chemistry.** Add chemicals, run the jets, and test the water before your first soak.
- **Inspect the shell for damage.** Document any scratches or cracks from transit while you can still file a claim.
