Moving companies cannot transport certain items on their trucks. This is not a preference. Federal regulations under FMCSA (Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration) and PHMSA (Pipeline and Hazardous Materials Safety Administration) prohibit mixing hazardous materials with household goods. Violations can result in fines up to $75,000 per item for the moving company.

Before your next move, go through this list and make separate arrangements for anything that applies.

## 1. Gasoline, Propane, and Fuel

Gasoline cans, propane tanks (even ones that appear empty), kerosene, and lighter fluid are fire hazards that cannot ride on a moving truck. Propane tanks retain flammable gas residue even after the gauge reads zero, which means they can still explode under heat or pressure.

![A Collection Of Labeled Cardboard Boxes  2](/images/blog/2029/10/10-prohibited-items-for-moving-day/a-collection-of-labeled-cardboard-boxes--2.webp)


**What to do**: Use up fuel in grills and equipment before your move. Transport small quantities in your personal vehicle in a well-ventilated area. For propane tank disposal, many Home Depot and Lowe's locations in Miami-Dade accept old tanks when you purchase a new one.

## 2. Ammunition and Explosives

Fireworks, ammunition, blasting caps, and flares fall under federal explosive regulations. Professional movers will not touch them.

**What to do**: Transport ammunition in your personal vehicle using a locked container. For fireworks disposal, contact the Miami-Dade Police Department non-emergency line. Several gun stores in Kendall and Hialeah also accept ammunition for safe disposal.

## 3. Aerosol Cans and Pressurized Containers

Spray paint, hairspray, cooking spray, WD-40, fire extinguishers, and scuba tanks can explode if they overheat inside a truck during transport. The pressure inside these containers increases as temperature rises.

**What to do**: Use up partially full cans before your move. Pack remaining aerosols in your personal vehicle with climate control running. Fire extinguishers should also travel with you.

## 4. Paints, Solvents, and Chemicals

Paint, paint thinner, nail polish remover, turpentine, pool chemicals, pesticides, and cleaning solvents are classified as hazardous materials. Even a single can of paint thinner hidden in a box can trigger fines and forced unloading if discovered.

**What to do**: Miami-Dade County hosts household hazardous waste collection days at drop-off locations in Doral, Homestead, and other areas. Check the county's Solid Waste Management website for schedules.

## 5. Perishable Foods

Refrigerated and frozen foods will not survive a move in good condition. In South Florida's heat, food spoils fast inside a truck, and leaking liquids can damage other belongings.

![A Warning Sign On A Bridge Railing Again 4](/images/blog/2029/10/10-prohibited-items-for-moving-day/a-warning-sign-on-a-bridge-railing-again-4.webp)


**What to do**: Plan meals around your remaining food in the week before your move. Donate unopened, non-expired items to Feeding South Florida or a local food bank. Host a "clean out the fridge" dinner for neighbors.

## 6. Plants (for Long-Distance Moves)

Federal agricultural regulations restrict transporting plants across state lines to prevent pest spread. Even for local moves, large potted plants can leak water and soil onto other items.

**What to do**: For interstate moves, give plants to friends or neighbors. For local moves, transport plants in your personal vehicle to prevent tipping and soil spills. The Fairchild Tropical Botanic Garden in Coral Gables sometimes accepts healthy plant donations.

## 7. Pets and Living Creatures

Fish, dogs, cats, reptiles, and any living animals cannot travel on moving trucks. The temperature inside a closed truck can reach dangerous levels within minutes.

**What to do**: Transport pets in your personal vehicle with air conditioning. For fish tanks, drain the water, bag the fish separately, and transport them in a temperature-controlled environment. Board pets on moving day if the transition will take several hours.

## 8. Prescription Medications

Medications can be damaged by heat exposure in a truck, and losing access to prescriptions during a move creates a health risk. Movers also cannot take liability for controlled substances.

**What to do**: Keep all medications in a personal bag that stays with you throughout the move. If you take temperature-sensitive medications like insulin, use an insulated bag with ice packs.

## 9. Irreplaceable Documents and Valuables

Passports, birth certificates, social security cards, financial records, legal documents, jewelry, and cash should never go on a moving truck. If a box goes missing or gets damaged, these items cannot be replaced.

![A Warning Sign With A Skull Indicating A 1](/images/blog/2029/10/10-prohibited-items-for-moving-day/a-warning-sign-with-a-skull-indicating-a-1.webp)


**What to do**: Pack a dedicated "essentials" bag or box that rides with you in your personal vehicle. Include important documents, jewelry, cash, spare keys, and anything with high sentimental value.

## 10. Batteries (Loose or Lithium)

Loose batteries can short-circuit if terminals touch metal or each other. Lithium batteries in particular are a fire risk and are subject to transport restrictions.

**What to do**: Tape over battery terminals with electrical tape. Pack batteries in a sealed plastic bag and transport them in your personal vehicle. For old or damaged batteries, use Miami-Dade County's battery recycling drop-off locations.

## FAQ

### What happens if prohibited items are found on a moving truck?

The moving company can face fines up to $75,000 per violation under FMCSA regulations. The entire shipment may be unloaded and the move halted until the items are removed. In serious cases, the company's operating license can be suspended.

### Can movers transport open cleaning supplies?

Most movers will not transport opened or partially used cleaning supplies because they contain chemicals that could leak or combust. Unopened, factory-sealed products in original packaging may be accepted for very short local moves, but it is best to ask your moving company directly.

### Are there items movers can take but prefer not to?

Yes. Items like heavy safes, pianos, and hot tubs are not prohibited, but they require specialized equipment and planning.
