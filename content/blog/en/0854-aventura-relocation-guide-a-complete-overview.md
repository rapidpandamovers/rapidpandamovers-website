## Why Choose Aventura?

Aventura packs a lot into its 3.5 square miles. The city incorporated in 1995 and quickly became one of Miami-Dade's most desirable addresses. With a population of around 40,000, it maintains an intimate feel while offering big-city amenities.

![Loc Scenic Aerial Shot Of A Sprawling Reside 1](/images/blog/2027/08/aventura-relocation-guide-a-complete-overview/loc-scenic-aerial-shot-of-a-sprawling-reside-1.webp)


The Aventura Mall anchors the community with over 300 stores, including Nordstrom, Bloomingdale's, and a three-story Zara. But beyond shopping, residents choose Aventura for its A-rated schools, low crime rates, and proximity to both Sunny Isles Beach and Hallandale Beach.

### Location and Accessibility

Aventura sits at the intersection of Biscayne Boulevard and William Lehman Causeway, putting you 20 minutes from Downtown Miami and 15 minutes from Fort Lauderdale. The Brightline station at Aventura Mall connects you to Miami Central and West Palm Beach without fighting I-95 traffic.

For daily errands, you're surrounded by options. Whole Foods, Publix, and Trader Joe's are all within a 10-minute drive. The Aventura Arts and Cultural Center hosts Broadway touring shows, and Founders Park offers tennis courts, a playground, and waterfront walking trails.

### Community and Lifestyle

Aventura draws a mix of young professionals working in Brickell, families attracted to the schools, and retirees from Coral Gables and Kendall looking for low-maintenance condo living. The city hosts free concerts at Founders Park, a monthly food truck gathering, and seasonal events like the Turkey Trot and Fourth of July fireworks over the Intracoastal.

## Neighborhoods to Consider

Aventura is primarily a condo and high-rise community, but distinct areas offer different experiences.

### Turnberry Isle

The Turnberry area centers around the JW Marriott Turnberry Resort with its two championship golf courses. Residences here tend to be luxury condos and single-family homes with HOA fees that reflect the resort-style amenities. Expect to pay $800,000+ for a 2-bedroom condo and $2 million+ for a single-family home with golf course views.

### Williams Island

Often called the "Florida Riviera," Williams Island is a private island community accessible via Country Club Drive. Seven high-rise towers offer waterfront living with marina access, a spa, tennis courts, and fine dining at the Cafe at Williams Island. Condos range from $500,000 to over $3 million depending on views and square footage.

### Aventura Lakes

The newest development, Aventura Lakes offers mid-rise condos with a more accessible price point. Two and three-bedroom units start around $400,000, making this a popular choice for families priced out of Coral Gables or Coconut Grove who want excellent schools without the Brickell commute.

### Porto Vita

This gated waterfront community features two towers with direct Intracoastal access. Residents have their own marina, beach club, and tennis facilities. Porto Vita attracts buyers looking for Bal Harbour-style exclusivity without crossing into Broward County.

## Moving to Aventura in May

May marks the transition into South Florida's wet season. Temperatures climb into the mid-80s, and afternoon thunderstorms become a daily possibility. Smart scheduling makes a real difference.

### Timing Your Move

Schedule your move for the morning. By 3 PM, there's a good chance of rain, and moving trucks don't operate in lightning. Most buildings require 48-72 hours notice for elevator reservations, so book early.

**May-specific considerations:**

- **Mother's Day weekend**: Restaurants and hotels book up, but moving services often have availability since families are busy celebrating
- **Memorial Day weekend**: Higher demand for movers as people take advantage of the long weekend
- **School schedules**: Miami-Dade schools run through early June, so family moves often cluster in late May to minimize disruption
- **Condo rules**: Many Aventura buildings restrict moves to weekdays only, and some prohibit moves during snowbird season (which ends in April)

## Essential Services to Locate

**Healthcare:** Aventura Hospital and Medical Center on Biscayne Boulevard handles emergencies. For specialists, Mount Sinai Medical Center in Miami Beach is 15 minutes south, and Memorial Regional Hospital in Hollywood is 15 minutes north.

**Schools:** Aventura City of Excellence School (ACES) consistently ranks among Miami-Dade's top public schools. For private options, families look to Hebrew Academy (RASG), David Posnack Jewish Day School in Davie, or the array of private schools in Pinecrest for those willing to commute.

**Groceries:** Beyond the chains, locals shop at Fresh Market at Aventura Mall for specialty items and Milam's Market in nearby North Miami Beach for Cuban and Latin American groceries.

**Fitness:** LA Fitness has two locations nearby, Equinox opened at the mall, and most condo buildings include gym facilities. Outdoor enthusiasts use the trails at Oleta River State Park, a 10-minute drive north.
