Moving day brings plenty of tasks, but one thing many Miami residents overlook is knowing exactly what cannot go on the moving truck. Professional movers follow strict regulations about prohibited items, and violating these rules can result in damaged belongings, safety hazards, or even legal issues.

Here are the 10 items you should always keep off the moving truck and transport yourself or dispose of properly.

## 1. Hazardous Materials

Gasoline, propane, paint thinner, aerosols, and cleaning chemicals pose serious fire risks during transport. Moving trucks can reach temperatures over 120 degrees in Florida's heat, turning these items into potential hazards.

![A Blue Volvo Truck Parked Outside A Ware 4](/images/blog/2029/02/10-things-to-keep-off-the-moving-truck/a-blue-volvo-truck-parked-outside-a-ware-4.webp)


**What to do:** Use up or properly dispose of these items at Miami-Dade's Household Hazardous Waste Collection centers in Doral or South Dade before your move.

## 2. Explosives and Ammunition

Fireworks, ammunition, and anything explosive are strictly prohibited by federal transportation regulations. Even small quantities of ammunition require special handling.

**What to do:** Transport ammunition in your personal vehicle in a locked container, or store it with a licensed dealer until after your move.

## 3. Perishable Foods

Refrigerated and frozen items rarely survive the unpacking and setup time of a typical move. In Miami's humidity, food spoils even faster once removed from refrigeration.

**What to do:** Plan meals around your perishables in the week before moving. Donate unopened non-perishables to Feeding South Florida or a local food bank in Kendall, Coral Gables, or your neighborhood.

## 4. Plants (for Long-Distance Moves)

Florida's agricultural inspection stations check vehicles entering and leaving the state. Many houseplants can harbor pests that threaten Florida's $8 billion agricultural industry.

![A Classic Hong Kong Tram Traverses Moder 3](/images/blog/2029/02/10-things-to-keep-off-the-moving-truck/a-classic-hong-kong-tram-traverses-moder-3.webp)


**What to do:** For local Miami moves, plants can travel in your car. For long-distance moves, give plants to friends or take cuttings to regrow at your new home.

## 5. Pets and Living Creatures

Fish, dogs, cats, and any living animals cannot travel in an enclosed moving truck. The temperature fluctuations and lack of ventilation can be fatal.

**What to do:** Transport pets in your air-conditioned vehicle. For fish, use sealed bags with tank water and battery-powered aerators. Many Miami pet stores offer boarding services during moves.

## 6. Irreplaceable Documents

Passports, birth certificates, financial records, property deeds, and legal documents should never leave your sight during a move.

**What to do:** Create a "moving day bag" that stays with you at all times. Include these documents plus a list of important phone numbers, recent financial statements, and your moving contract.

## 7. Jewelry and Cash

High-value items that are small and easily stolen should never be packed in boxes destined for the moving truck. Even with the most trustworthy crew, boxes get opened and inspected at various points.

**What to do:** Wear valuable jewelry on moving day or keep it in a personal bag. Deposit cash before moving day, as you will have plenty of expenses to cover after the move.

## 8. Medications

Prescription medications need to stay accessible and at controlled temperatures. Leaving them in a hot truck, especially in Miami's summer heat, can degrade their effectiveness.

![Tow Truck Operator Loading White Gmc Pic 2](/images/blog/2029/02/10-things-to-keep-off-the-moving-truck/tow-truck-operator-loading-white-gmc-pic-2.webp)


**What to do:** Keep all medications in your personal vehicle or carry-on bag. This includes over-the-counter medicines, vitamins, and any medical equipment you use daily.

## 9. Pressurized Containers

Fire extinguishers, scuba tanks (common in Miami's diving community), propane tanks for grills, and aerosol cans can explode when exposed to heat or pressure changes during transport.

**What to do:** Transport these items in your air-conditioned vehicle. For scuba tanks, have them emptied at a local dive shop in Brickell or Coconut Grove before moving.

## 10. Anything Irreplaceable

Family photos, heirlooms, artwork with sentimental value, and items that money cannot replace should travel with you personally.

**What to do:** Designate a few boxes as "priceless" items that only you handle. Consider photographing valuable items for insurance purposes before moving day.
