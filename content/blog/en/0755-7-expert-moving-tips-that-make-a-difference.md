After completing over 500 Miami moves this year alone, our crews have seen what works and what causes problems. These seven tips come straight from the field, tested in Brickell high-rises, Coral Gables estates, and everything in between.

## 1. Packing Takes Longer Than You Think

A 3-bedroom house takes 3-5 days to pack properly. Not one frantic night. Start early and pace yourself.

![A Man Packing Items Into Cardboard Boxes 3](/images/blog/2027/06/7-expert-moving-tips-that-make-a-difference/a-man-packing-items-into-cardboard-boxes-3.webp)


In Miami's summer humidity, rushing means sweating through clothes while wrapping dishes at midnight. Start your packing two weeks before moving day. Tackle one room per evening after work. Your back and your belongings will thank you.

## 2. Small Boxes for Heavy Items

Books, dishes, and tools go in small boxes. Large boxes are for light items like bedding and clothes. This is physics, not a suggestion.

We see this mistake constantly in Kendall and Doral homes with extensive book collections or kitchen gadget arsenals. A large box packed with hardcovers can weigh 70+ pounds. That box will break, someone might get hurt, and your books end up scattered on the driveway. Keep heavy items under 30 pounds per box.

## 3. Labeling Saves Everyone Time

"Kitchen" is not enough information. Write "Kitchen - Coffee Station - Fragile" on the box AND the side so stacked boxes stay identifiable.

When we unload into your new Coconut Grove condo at 5pm, you want that coffee maker accessible for the next morning. Color-coded labels by room help even more. Our crews can place boxes exactly where they belong instead of creating a mountain in the living room for you to sort later.

## 4. Fragile Items Need Communication

If something is exceptionally fragile or valuable, tell movers before they touch it. We handle items carefully, but we are not mind readers.

That antique mirror from your grandmother? The art piece you bought in Wynwood? Point it out. Walk us through what needs special attention. Miami homes often contain irreplaceable items, from vintage Cuban art to custom furniture pieces. A 30-second conversation prevents heartbreak.

## 5. Access Matters More Than You Think

Clear pathways, reserved elevators, and available parking make moves faster. Every obstacle adds time and stress.

![A Professional Mover Packs A Fragile Ite 2](/images/blog/2027/06/7-expert-moving-tips-that-make-a-difference/a-professional-mover-packs-a-fragile-ite-2.webp)


Brickell and Aventura high-rises have strict moving rules. Reserve the service elevator 48 hours ahead. Get COI (Certificate of Insurance) from your moving company to management beforehand. Know if there are blackout hours when moving is prohibited. Clear hallways and doorways of clutter. The easier we can get in and out, the faster your move finishes.

## 6. Disassembled Furniture Helps

Beds, tables, and desks move faster when disassembled. Keep hardware in labeled bags taped to the furniture.

Those IKEA bed frames from your first Hialeah apartment? Faster to move in pieces. Your solid wood dining table from a Coral Gables estate sale? Same thing. Take photos before disassembly so reassembly goes smoothly. Sandwich bags with screws taped directly to the furniture frame prevent the "mystery hardware" problem weeks later.

## 7. Have the New Layout Ready

Know where furniture goes before we arrive at the new place. Uncertainty means multiple rearrangements.

Walk through your new Miami Shores home or South Miami townhouse beforehand if possible. Measure rooms. Decide where the couch goes, where the bed faces, where the TV mounts. When our crew asks "where does this go?" you can point and answer immediately instead of debating while everyone waits.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How far in advance should I start packing?

Start two weeks before moving day for a typical 3-bedroom home. Give yourself three weeks if you have a garage, attic, or storage unit full of items to sort.

### Should I tip the movers?

Tips are not required but always appreciated. Most Miami customers tip $20-40 per mover for a standard local move, more for long days or difficult conditions like stairs or tight parking.

### What should I not pack myself?

Leave hazardous materials (paint, propane, cleaning chemicals) behind. Movers cannot transport these items legally. Plants also struggle in enclosed trucks, especially during Miami summers when temperatures inside can exceed 120 degrees.
