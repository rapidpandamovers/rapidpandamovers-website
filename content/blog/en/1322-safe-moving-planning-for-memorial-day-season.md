Memorial Day weekend is one of the most popular times to move in South Florida. The long weekend gives you an extra day, kids are finishing the school year, and summer leases often start June 1. But if your move includes a heavy safe, that piece of the job needs its own plan.

Safes are one of the most difficult household items to relocate. A small fireproof document safe might weigh 200 pounds. A mid-size gun safe runs 600 to 1,000 pounds. Larger vault-style models can exceed 2,000 pounds. Without the right equipment and preparation, you risk damaging your floors, doorframes, and the safe itself.

Here is how to handle safe moving the right way during the busy Memorial Day season.

## Step 1: Know Your Safe's Exact Weight

This is the single most important preparation step, and it is the one most people skip.

![A Mover Carefully Handles Boxes In A Bri 3](/images/blog/2029/05/safe-moving-planning-for-memorial-day-season/a-mover-carefully-handles-boxes-in-a-bri-3.webp)


Check the manufacturer's label, manual, or website for the empty weight of your safe. Then factor in the contents. A gun safe loaded with firearms, ammunition, and accessories can weigh significantly more than its listed empty weight.

Why this matters: your moving crew needs to select the right dolly, straps, and vehicle configuration based on the actual weight. A standard furniture dolly tops out around 300-400 pounds. Safe moving requires a heavy-duty appliance dolly rated for 1,000 pounds or more, and for the largest safes, a hydraulic dolly with a built-in lift system.

## Step 2: Empty the Safe Completely

Before moving day, remove everything from the safe:

- **Firearms**: Transport these separately in locked cases per Florida law
- **Ammunition**: Pack in original boxes or padded containers
- **Documents and valuables**: Place in a portable lockbox you keep with you
- **Shelves and organizers**: Remove any detachable interior components

An empty safe is lighter, and more importantly, the contents will not shift during transport. Shifting weight inside a safe being tilted onto a dolly is how accidents happen.

## Step 3: Protect Your Floors and Doorways

Safes destroy flooring. Even a 400-pound safe dropped an inch can crack tile, gouge hardwood, or punch through vinyl. Before the move begins:

![Man Handling Moving Boxes In A Spacious  2](/images/blog/2029/05/safe-moving-planning-for-memorial-day-season/man-handling-moving-boxes-in-a-spacious--2.webp)


- Lay down plywood sheets along the path from the safe to the truck
- Use furniture sliders or blankets under the safe during repositioning
- Measure every doorway, hallway, and turn in the path. If the safe barely fits, your crew needs to know in advance.
- Remove doors from hinges if they create tight clearances

For stairs, professional movers use stair-climbing dollies or controlled-descent straps. This is not a two-person job with a hand truck.

## Step 4: Use the Right Equipment

Professional safe moving requires specific gear:

- **Appliance dolly**: Heavy-duty model rated for 1,000+ pounds with stair-climbing treads
- **Ratchet straps**: To secure the safe to the dolly during transport. Regular bungee cords are not strong enough.
- **Moving blankets**: Wrap the safe to prevent scratches on both the safe and your walls
- **Plywood runners**: Protect flooring along the entire path
- **Hydraulic dolly**: For safes over 1,000 pounds, a hydraulic lift dolly makes the job safer and faster

Never exceed the weight rating of your equipment. Check the specs on every dolly, strap, and ramp before loading.

## Step 5: Keep the Safe Upright

Safes are designed to stand upright. Laying a safe on its side during transport can damage the locking mechanism, shift the internal fire insulation, and void the manufacturer's warranty. Your moving crew should:

- Strap the safe upright to the truck wall or a secured anchor point
- Use wheel chocks or blocks to prevent rolling
- Avoid sharp stops and turns during the drive

## Memorial Day Weekend Timing Tips

The long weekend adds scheduling pressure across South Florida. Here is how to stay ahead of it:

![Smiling Mover Giving Thumbs Up From A Mo 5](/images/blog/2029/05/safe-moving-planning-for-memorial-day-season/smiling-mover-giving-thumbs-up-from-a-mo-5.webp)


- **Book early**: Moving companies fill Memorial Day weekend slots weeks in advance. If you need [**safe moving**](/safe-moving) service, call at least 3-4 weeks ahead.
- **Avoid Sunday moves**: Many buildings restrict moving on Sundays. Confirm with your HOA or building management.
- **Morning starts**: Miami's May heat builds quickly. Starting your move by 7 or 8 AM keeps your crew working in cooler conditions and avoids afternoon thunderstorms.
- **Traffic awareness**: Memorial Day weekend traffic in Miami is heavy, especially on I-95 and US-1. Factor in extra time for the drive between locations.

## When to Hire Professionals

You should hire professional movers for your safe if any of these apply:

- The safe weighs more than 300 pounds
- The move involves stairs (up or down)
- The safe needs to go through tight doorways or hallways
- You do not own a heavy-duty appliance dolly and proper straps
- The safe is going into or out of a second-floor room

Attempting a heavy safe move without proper equipment risks serious injury. Back injuries, crushed fingers, and dropped safes are common when homeowners try this themselves.
