North Miami sits between Miami Shores to the south, Aventura to the north, and Biscayne Bay to the east. With about 62,000 residents, it is a mid-sized city that blends waterfront neighborhoods, a revitalizing downtown district, and some of Miami-Dade County's more affordable housing. Here is what you need to know as a new resident.

## The Neighborhoods

North Miami is not one thing. The neighborhoods range from gated waterfront enclaves to modest residential blocks, and picking the right one depends on your budget and priorities.

![Captivating View Of Miami S Iconic Skyli 1](/images/blog/2029/09/north-miami-101-what-every-new-resident-should-know/captivating-view-of-miami-s-iconic-skyli-1.webp)


### Keystone Point

The most prestigious address in North Miami. Keystone Point is a guard-gated community east of Biscayne Boulevard with direct waterfront access to Biscayne Bay. Homes here sit on deep-water canals, and residents can sail out to the bay in minutes. The 24/7 gated entrance is at NE 123rd Street. Expect prices well above the North Miami average, but you get a private, quiet setting with large lots and water access that would cost significantly more in Miami Beach or Coral Gables.

### Sans Souci

Just south of Keystone Point, Sans Souci is another waterfront neighborhood between 108th and 123rd Streets, bordered by Biscayne Boulevard to the west and the bay to the east. The area mixes single-family homes, condos, and apartments, mostly in mid-century modern style. Sans Souci Estates is a gated section with luxury bayfront properties, and the Sans Souci Tennis Center has been voted one of Miami's best tennis facilities multiple times.

### The MOCA District (Downtown North Miami)

The downtown area along NE 125th Street is undergoing significant redevelopment. Named after the Museum of Contemporary Art (MOCA), this corridor is adding new mixed-use developments, restaurants, and commercial tenants through public-private partnerships. Housing here is more affordable than the waterfront neighborhoods, with a mix of older apartments and newer condo projects.

### Griffing Park

A quieter residential area in the western part of the city with single-family homes on tree-lined streets. Griffing Park offers a suburban feel at North Miami prices, and the city has plans for development improvements including a Police Athletic League facility in the park.

## Things to Do

### Museum of Contemporary Art (MOCA)

MOCA anchors North Miami's cultural scene with 8 to 10 exhibitions annually plus public educational programs. Hours are Tuesday, Thursday, and Saturday from 11 AM to 5 PM; Wednesday from 1 PM to 9 PM; and Sunday from noon to 5 PM. Admission is $5 for adults and $3 for seniors and students. North Miami residents get in free, so bring a utility bill or ID showing your address.

The last Friday of every month brings Jazz at MOCA, an evening concert series from 7 PM to 10 PM that fills the outdoor space with live music and food vendors. It is the city's best recurring social event.

### Dezerland Park

A 250,000-square-foot indoor entertainment complex with go-kart tracks, roller skating, arcade games, rock climbing, and a trampoline park. This is the go-to option for families with kids who need to burn energy, especially during summer when outdoor activities in the heat are limited.

### Enchanted Forest Elaine Gordon Park

A 22-acre subtropical park with nature trails winding through tree canopies and native wildlife habitat. It is one of North Miami's hidden treasures and a welcome green escape from the surrounding urban landscape.

### Oleta River State Park

Technically in North Miami Beach, but right on the border. Florida's largest urban park offers mountain biking trails, kayaking, paddleboarding, and a secluded beach on Biscayne Bay. If you enjoy outdoor activities, this park alone is worth the move to the area.

## Schools

North Miami is served by Miami-Dade County Public Schools. Key options include:

- **North Miami Elementary**: Serves the central part of the city
- **North Miami Middle School**: Located off NE 12th Avenue
- **North Miami Senior High**: The main public high school for the area
- **Private options**: Several charter schools and private academies operate within the city

Research your specific address on the MDCPS school boundary map before buying or renting. School zones in North Miami can shift by a few blocks.

## Getting Around

- **Biscayne Boulevard (US-1)**: The main north-south corridor, connecting you to Downtown Miami in about 20 minutes heading south and Aventura in 10 minutes heading north
- **I-95**: Accessible from NE 125th Street, providing faster access to Fort Lauderdale (25 minutes) and Miami International Airport (25 minutes)
- **Tri-Rail**: The North Miami Beach station connects you to Fort Lauderdale and West Palm Beach for commuters heading north
- **Bus service**: Miami-Dade Transit runs multiple routes along Biscayne Boulevard and 125th Street

A car is still the practical choice for daily life, but the bus service along Biscayne Boulevard is better than in most Miami-Dade suburbs.

## Cost of Living

North Miami is one of the more affordable waterfront-adjacent cities in Miami-Dade. Some benchmarks:

![Top Down View Of A Miami Home With Solar 2](/images/blog/2029/09/north-miami-101-what-every-new-resident-should-know/top-down-view-of-a-miami-home-with-solar-2.webp)


- **Rent**: One-bedroom apartments average less than in Miami Beach, Brickell, or Coral Gables
- **Home prices**: More accessible than neighboring Miami Shores or Aventura, especially west of Biscayne Boulevard
- **Groceries**: Publix, Presidente Supermarket, and several Latin markets serve the area
- **Dining**: A mix of Caribbean, Latin American, and American restaurants along 125th Street and Biscayne Boulevard, with prices below the Miami Beach and Brickell average

## Moving to North Miami in September

September is still hurricane season, so monitor the weather closely. Beyond that:

- **Street parking**: North Miami's older neighborhoods have narrow streets. Confirm that your moving truck can park close to your front door, especially in Keystone Point and Sans Souci where tree canopy is heavy.
- **Building requirements**: Condos along Biscayne Boulevard often require move-in deposits ($300 to $500), insurance certificates, and elevator reservations
- **School timing**: September means school is in session. Factor school zone speed limits and bus routes into your moving day route.
- **Best moving days**: Weekdays offer better pricing and availability. Avoid Friday afternoons when Biscayne Boulevard traffic peaks.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is North Miami safe?**
Safety varies by neighborhood. Keystone Point, Sans Souci, and areas along the bay are generally considered safe and well-maintained. Downtown areas are improving with redevelopment. Check the city's crime statistics for your specific block before committing.

**What is the difference between North Miami and North Miami Beach?**
They are separate cities with different governments, tax rates, and services. North Miami Beach is north of North Miami, closer to Aventura. The names cause confusion, so always verify which city you are looking at.

**Is North Miami a good place for families?**
Yes, particularly in the residential neighborhoods west of Biscayne Boulevard and in Griffing Park. Proximity to Oleta River State Park and Dezerland Park gives families plenty of activity options.
