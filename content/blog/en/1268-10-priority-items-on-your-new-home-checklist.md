Whether you're a long-time Miami resident or just making your November move to South Florida, there's always something new to discover. Here's our curated list to help you make the most of life in the Sunshine State this fall.

## 1. Change All the Locks

You don't know who has keys from before. Change locks immediately for security and peace of mind.

![A Joyful Family Unpacking Boxes While Mo 3](/images/blog/2029/02/10-priority-items-on-your-new-home-checklist/a-joyful-family-unpacking-boxes-while-mo-3.webp)


## 2. Test All Appliances

Run the dishwasher, check the stove, test the washer/dryer. Identify problems while warranty claims are still fresh.

## 3. Locate Emergency Shutoffs

Find and label water main, electrical panel, and gas shutoff. You'll need to know these eventually, so better to find them now while things are calm. This is especially important in South Florida where hurricane prep means knowing how to shut off your water main quickly.

## 4. Set Up Mail Forwarding

Visit USPS.com to ensure nothing gets lost during the transition period. This should happen before you move.

## 5. Transfer Utilities to Your Name

Electricity, gas, water, internet - confirm all accounts are active and in your name before move-in day. In Miami-Dade, you'll likely deal with FPL for electricity and Miami-Dade Water and Sewer for water. Coral Gables and some other cities have their own utilities, so check your specific area.

![Couple Unpacking Boxes In Their New Apar 2](/images/blog/2029/02/10-priority-items-on-your-new-home-checklist/couple-unpacking-boxes-in-their-new-apar-2.webp)


## 6. Schedule Critical Installations

Internet, cable, security systems - book these early as installation windows fill up quickly.

## 7. Deep Clean Before Unpacking

If you didn't do it before moving in, clean thoroughly now while rooms are still relatively empty.

## 8. Update Your Address

DMV, banks, subscriptions, employers - create a list of everyone who needs your new address and work through it systematically.

## 9. Test Smoke and Carbon Monoxide Detectors

Replace batteries in all detectors and ensure they're functioning properly. This is a safety essential often overlooked.

![Young Couple Moving Boxes Into Their New 1](/images/blog/2029/02/10-priority-items-on-your-new-home-checklist/young-couple-moving-boxes-into-their-new-1.webp)


## 10. Meet Your Neighbors

Introduce yourself within the first week. Knowing the people around you builds community and improves safety. Whether you're in a Brickell high-rise, a Coral Gables single-family home, or a Kendall townhouse, good neighbor relationships make life easier, especially during hurricane season when everyone looks out for each other.

## What to Expect Your First Month

The first 30 days in a new home set the tone for your experience. With these priority tasks complete, you can focus on what matters: making the space your own and exploring your new neighborhood.

Most Miami newcomers find that handling these essentials during week one frees up weekends for discovering local parks, restaurants, and community events. By month two, you will feel settled rather than scrambling.
