Memorial Day weekend is one of the busiest moving periods in South Florida. If you or a family member has mobility challenges or medical equipment needs, extra planning makes all the difference between a smooth transition and a stressful one.

## Why Memorial Day Moves Need Extra Attention

The long weekend seems like the perfect time to relocate. Schools are winding down, and the extra day off gives you breathing room. But for families with special needs, the holiday weekend also brings challenges: moving companies book up fast, Miami's afternoon thunderstorms roll in without warning, and the late-May heat can be brutal for anyone spending hours in transit.

![A Couple Unpacking Books In Their New Ho 2](/images/blog/2029/05/special-needs-moving-essentials-for-memorial-day/a-couple-unpacking-books-in-their-new-ho-2.webp)


Booking your [**special needs moving**](/special-needs-moving) crew at least three to four weeks ahead of Memorial Day gives you the best selection of dates and ensures your movers have time to plan for any accessibility requirements.

## Prepare Medical Equipment First

Start by inventorying every piece of medical equipment, mobility aid, and assistive device in your home. Wheelchairs, hospital beds, CPAP machines, oxygen concentrators, and shower chairs all need careful handling during a move.

For each item, note whether it folds or disassembles. Keep any tools needed for breakdown in a clearly labeled bag, and attach those bags directly to the equipment. Removable parts like wheelchair cushions, armrests, and footrests should be packed separately with padding around delicate components.

Pack a "day of" essentials bag with medications, medical supplies, charging cables for powered equipment, and personal hygiene items. Keep this bag with you throughout the move, not on the truck.

## Check Accessibility at Your New Home

Before moving day, visit your new home and confirm that it meets your accessibility needs:

- **Doorways and hallways** are wide enough for wheelchairs and walkers
- **Grab bars and ramps** are installed, or you have an installation appointment scheduled before move-in
- **Electrical outlets** are accessible for medical equipment that needs constant power
- **Bathroom layout** accommodates any shower chairs, transfer benches, or roll-in access

If modifications are needed, schedule them before your move date. Contractors in Miami stay busy around the holidays, so give yourself a buffer.

## Communicate Your Needs to the Moving Crew

Call your moving company at least a week before moving day to walk through every specific requirement. Share details about:

- Equipment that cannot be tipped, jolted, or exposed to extreme heat
- Items that must be loaded last and unloaded first
- Any mobility limitations that affect how you can supervise the move
- Clear pathways needed through both homes during loading and unloading

## Beat the Memorial Day Heat

South Florida's late-May temperatures regularly hit the low 90s, and humidity makes it feel even worse. For anyone with health sensitivities, heat management is critical:

![Side View Of Crop Anonymous Man Packing  1](/images/blog/2029/05/special-needs-moving-essentials-for-memorial-day/side-view-of-crop-anonymous-man-packing--1.webp)


- Schedule your move for early morning, ideally starting by 7 a.m.
- Keep air conditioning running in both homes as long as possible
- Have cold water, electrolyte drinks, and a cooler accessible throughout the day
- Identify a cool rest area at both locations where anyone who needs a break can sit comfortably

## Memorial Day Moving Checklist

Before your move, take care of these steps:

- Inventory all medical and mobility equipment with photos
- Pack an essentials bag with 48 hours of medications and supplies
- Confirm accessibility features at the new home
- Brief your moving crew on all special requirements
- Arrange for utilities, especially electricity and AC, to be on at the new address
- Notify doctors, pharmacies, and home health aides of your address change
