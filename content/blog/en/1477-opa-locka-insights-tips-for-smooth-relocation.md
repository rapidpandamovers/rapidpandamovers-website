Opa-locka sits in north-central Miami-Dade County and holds a distinction no other city in the Western Hemisphere can claim: the largest collection of Moorish Revival architecture in the Americas. Founded in 1926 by aviation pioneer Glenn Curtiss, the city was designed around themes from One Thousand and One Nights, and that heritage still shapes its streets, buildings, and cultural identity today.

If you are planning a move to Opa-locka, here is what you should know about the community, its neighborhoods, and how to make your transition as smooth as possible.

## What Defines Opa-locka

### The Architecture

![Captivating View Of Spain S Iconic Plaza 3](/images/blog/2029/11/opa-locka-insights-tips-for-smooth-relocation/captivating-view-of-spain-s-iconic-plaza-3.webp)


Architect Bernhardt E. Muller designed over 100 buildings in the Moorish Revival style during the city's founding years between 1926 and 1928. The hallmarks of this style include onion-shaped domes, minarets, crenelated parapets, Saracenic arches, and mosaic tile detailing. While the 1926 Miami hurricane destroyed many original structures, 17 surviving buildings are listed on the National Register of Historic Places as the Opa-locka Thematic Resource Area.

The most recognizable landmark is the Opa-locka City Hall, which features domes, towers, and ornamental details that make it one of the most distinctive municipal buildings in Florida. Street names carry the theme forward: Sharazad Boulevard, Ali Baba Avenue, Sinbad Avenue, and even Sesame Street.

### The Arts Scene

Opa-locka has invested heavily in public art and cultural programming in recent years. The Opa-locka Community Development Corporation hosts the annual Art of Transformation festival, a multi-day event that brings large-scale installations, murals, and exhibitions to the city. The festival has helped establish Opa-locka as a destination for contemporary and Afro-diasporic art.

Several studio spaces and galleries operate in the area, drawing artists who are priced out of Wynwood and the Design District but want to stay within Miami-Dade.

### The Airport

Miami-Opa locka Executive Airport (OPF) serves as a general aviation and private jet facility on the western edge of the city. It handles charter flights, corporate aviation, and flight training. The airport is a significant employer in the area and contributes to the city's connection to the broader aviation industry that Glenn Curtiss helped pioneer.

## Neighborhoods and Areas to Know

Opa-locka covers approximately 4.2 square miles and includes several distinct areas:

### Downtown Opa-locka

The historic core of the city, centered around City Hall and the original Moorish-themed commercial district. This area is the focus of ongoing redevelopment efforts led by the Opa-locka Community Redevelopment Agency, established in 2010 to revitalize the central urban district.

### Residential Areas

Most housing in Opa-locka consists of single-family homes, with a mix of garden-style apartments and smaller multifamily buildings. Property prices are among the most affordable in Miami-Dade County, making the city accessible for first-time buyers and families on a budget.

Key factors when choosing a neighborhood:

- **Proximity to Tri-Rail**: The Opa-locka station on the Tri-Rail commuter line provides direct service to Fort Lauderdale and West Palm Beach
- **Access to major roads**: NW 27th Avenue, NW 135th Street, and the Palmetto Expressway provide connections across the county
- **School zones**: Miami-Dade County Public Schools serves Opa-locka, and zone assignments vary by address

### Zip Codes

Opa-locka primarily covers zip codes 33054 and 33055. When updating your address, confirm which zip code applies to your specific location since service boundaries for utilities and mail delivery differ between the two.

## Planning Your Move to Opa-locka

### Timing and Weather

![Stunning Night View Of Hamburg City Hall 1](/images/blog/2029/11/opa-locka-insights-tips-for-smooth-relocation/stunning-night-view-of-hamburg-city-hall-1.webp)


November through April is the best window for moving in South Florida. Temperatures during this period average between 70 and 82 degrees, with lower humidity and minimal rain. November specifically offers the advantage of off-peak moving rates since summer demand has subsided.

### Parking and Street Access

Several residential streets in Opa-locka are narrow, and some older neighborhoods have limited driveway access. Before moving day:

- **Check street parking restrictions** near your new address
- **Measure driveway and gate clearances** to confirm your moving truck can fit
- **Note any overhead obstructions** like low-hanging power lines or tree branches along the approach

If you are moving into a multifamily building, confirm whether the property requires advance notice or a reserved loading zone.

### Utilities and Services

Set up the following before your move-in date:

- **FPL (Florida Power & Light)**: Electric service, which can be activated online
- **Miami-Dade Water and Sewer Department**: Water service requires contacting the county
- **Internet and cable**: AT&T and Xfinity are the primary providers in Opa-locka
- **Waste collection**: Miami-Dade County handles garbage and recycling pickup

## Essential Resources for New Residents

### Healthcare

- **Jackson North Medical Center** in North Miami Beach is the closest major hospital, roughly 10 minutes east
- **Jessie Trice Community Health System** operates clinics in and around Opa-locka for primary care

### Shopping and Groceries

- **Opa-locka Hialeah Flea Market**: One of the largest open-air markets in South Florida, open on weekends
- **Major grocery chains**: Sedano's, Presidente Supermarket, and Walmart Supercenter locations are within a short drive
- **Westland Mall** in Hialeah is the nearest enclosed shopping center

### Parks and Recreation

- **Segal Park** and **Sherbondy Park** offer playgrounds, athletic fields, and picnic areas
- **Amelia Earhart Park** in nearby Hialeah provides a lake, petting zoo, mountain biking trails, and event spaces

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Opa-locka safe?**
Like many urban areas in Miami-Dade, safety varies by neighborhood. The city has increased police presence and invested in community programs in recent years. Research specific blocks, talk to neighbors, and visit at different times of day before committing to a property.

![The Ca D Zan Mansion Tower Set Against A 4](/images/blog/2029/11/opa-locka-insights-tips-for-smooth-relocation/the-ca-d-zan-mansion-tower-set-against-a-4.webp)


**What is the cost of living in Opa-locka?**
Opa-locka is one of the most affordable cities in Miami-Dade County. Median home prices run significantly below the county average, and rental rates are lower than nearby cities like Miami Gardens, Hialeah, and North Miami. Grocery and utility costs are consistent with the broader metro area.

**How far is Opa-locka from Downtown Miami?**
Approximately 15 miles, or 25 to 35 minutes by car depending on traffic. The Tri-Rail station in Opa-locka also provides an alternative commute option.

**Are the Moorish buildings open to the public?**
Opa-locka City Hall is accessible during business hours. The Heritage Trail, a self-guided walking tour, passes by many of the historic Moorish Revival buildings. Several are occupied by city offices and businesses, so exterior viewing is the primary option for most structures.
