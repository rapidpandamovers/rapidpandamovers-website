Thinking about moving to Virginia Gardens this August? This small village in Miami-Dade County packs a lot of charm into roughly half a square mile. Here is what you should know before your move and how to settle in quickly.

## Why Choose Virginia Gardens?

Virginia Gardens stands out as one of Miami-Dade County's most affordable and convenient locations. With a population of about 2,400, the village offers a tight-knit, mostly residential atmosphere. It was originally founded in 1947 when residents broke away from a neighboring municipality to preserve their equestrian lifestyle, and that independent streak still defines the community today.

![Beautiful Beachfront Home In Florida Cit 4](/images/blog/2029/08/starting-fresh-in-virginia-gardens-a-newcomers-guide/beautiful-beachfront-home-in-florida-cit-4.webp)


The area attracts families and professionals thanks to relatively low property taxes, an urban-suburban mix, and proximity to Miami International Airport.

### Location and Accessibility

Virginia Gardens sits between Miami Springs and MIA, making it one of the best-connected small towns in the county. NW 36th Street and NW 42nd Avenue provide direct access to the Dolphin Expressway (SR 836) and the Palmetto Expressway (SR 826). Downtown Miami is about 10 miles east, and Doral is right next door. If you fly frequently for work, living minutes from MIA is a major advantage.

### Community and Lifestyle

Virginia Gardens residents tend to know their neighbors. The village government is hands-on, with a small police department and active code enforcement that keeps streets clean and quiet. Local parks and the nearby Miami Springs Recreation Center provide gathering spots. Summer in particular brings back-to-school prep and outdoor evening walks once the afternoon rain clears.

## Where to Look for Housing

Virginia Gardens is compact, so there are no sprawling subdivisions to compare. Most homes are single-family ranches built between the 1950s and 1970s, with some renovated properties reaching the $800K-$1M range. Older unrenovated homes offer more affordable entry points.

### Choosing Your Perfect Spot

Consider these factors:

- **Proximity to work and schools**: Factor in your daily commute. Schools in the area fall under Miami-Dade County Public Schools, with several magnet and charter options nearby.
- **Airport noise**: Properties closer to NW 36th Street may experience more aircraft noise. Walk the block at different times before signing a lease or contract.
- **Property types**: Mostly single-family homes, with a few small multifamily buildings.
- **Neighborhood feel**: The predominantly Hispanic community (over 80%) gives the area a warm, family-oriented character with plenty of Latin dining options along nearby corridors.

## Moving to Virginia Gardens in August

August is peak moving season in South Florida. Expect high demand for movers and hot, humid conditions with afternoon thunderstorms nearly every day.

### Timing Your Move

When planning your relocation, consider:

- **Best moving days**: Weekdays typically offer better availability and rates than weekends.
- **Weather window**: Schedule loading and unloading for early morning (before 10 a.m.) to beat the worst heat and afternoon storms.
- **Street parking**: Virginia Gardens streets are narrow. Confirm with the village if you need a temporary parking permit for a moving truck.

## Essential Services to Locate

As a new Virginia Gardens resident, you will want to find:

- **Healthcare**: Hialeah Hospital and Palmetto General Hospital are both within a 10-minute drive. Baptist Health and Jackson Memorial are accessible via the expressways.
- **Schools**: Virginia Gardens Elementary is the local public school. Robert Morgan Educational Center and Doral Academy are nearby options.
- **Shopping**: Publix and Sedano's are the closest grocery stores. Miami International Mall in Doral is about five minutes west for bigger retail trips.
- **Recreation**: Virginia Gardens Park has a small playground, and the larger Miami Springs Golf and Country Club is a short drive away.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Virginia Gardens safe?**
Yes. The village has its own police department and consistently low crime rates for the area. Its small size means officers know the streets well.

![Scenic View Of A Suburban Street Lined W 1](/images/blog/2029/08/starting-fresh-in-virginia-gardens-a-newcomers-guide/scenic-view-of-a-suburban-street-lined-w-1.webp)


**How far is Virginia Gardens from the beach?**
Miami Beach is about 30 minutes east via the 836 and MacArthur Causeway, depending on traffic.

**Are there HOA fees in Virginia Gardens?**
Most single-family homes do not have HOA fees, though some smaller condo or townhome developments may.
