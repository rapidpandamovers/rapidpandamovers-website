May marks the beginning of Miami's wet season, and that means extra precautions when moving antiques. Rising humidity, afternoon thunderstorms, and temperatures climbing into the upper 80s all create risks for wooden furniture, vintage upholstery, and delicate finishes. Here is how to protect your valuable pieces during a May move.

## Why May Antique Moves Need Special Attention

Moving antiques any time of year requires skill. Moving them as Miami transitions into wet season demands specialized knowledge and preparation.

![A Collection Of Vintage Suitcases And An 4](/images/blog/2028/05/antique-moving-guide-for-may/a-collection-of-vintage-suitcases-and-an-4.webp)


**The humidity factor:** May humidity in Miami starts climbing toward 70-80%. Unprotected wood absorbs moisture, swells, and warps. Veneer separates. Brass tarnishes faster. Antique furniture with hide glue joints (common in pre-1950 pieces) becomes vulnerable when moisture levels rise.

**The rain factor:** Afternoon thunderstorms become a regular occurrence in May. Loading and unloading windows need to be planned around weather patterns, typically scheduling for early morning before the storms roll in.

Many Miami homes, especially in historic neighborhoods like Coral Gables and Coconut Grove, contain valuable antiques that require specialized care. Victorian furniture from Coral Gables estates or art deco pieces from Miami Beach condos need expert handling that general movers simply cannot provide. Our [**antique moving**](/antique-moving) team uses climate-controlled trucks and custom crating to protect your pieces.

## Key Tips for Antique Moving in May

### Schedule Early Morning Moves

Book your move for a 7 AM to 10 AM window. Miami's May afternoons bring thunderstorms almost daily, and you do not want a 200-year-old armoire sitting on a loading ramp during a downpour. Morning moves also mean cooler temperatures while items are in transit.

### Get Appraisals and Photograph Items Before the Move

Photograph each antique from multiple angles before moving day. Get appraisals for pieces worth over $5,000. This protects you in two ways: insurance claims require documentation, and you will catch any existing damage before movers arrive.

### Prepare Items for Humidity Exposure

Even with climate-controlled trucks, your antiques will spend time outside during loading and unloading. Wipe down wood with furniture polish before the move to create a temporary moisture barrier. Remove drawers from dressers, wrap them separately, and tape the wrapped drawers shut so hardware does not scratch surfaces.

### Plan Ahead for Building Logistics

May is busy for moving companies across Miami-Dade. Scheduling your antique move early ensures you get your preferred date. This is especially true in high-demand areas like Brickell, Aventura, and Pinecrest where building elevators and loading docks fill up quickly.
