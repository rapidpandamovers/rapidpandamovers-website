July is the busiest month for long distance moves nationwide. If you're planning a cross-state or cross-country relocation from South Florida this summer, solid preparation is what separates a smooth move from a stressful one.

## Why Summer Long Distance Moves Need Extra Planning

The Fourth of July holiday adds a wrinkle to July relocations. Moving companies are booked solid, highways are packed with holiday travelers, and Florida's heat demands extra care for temperature-sensitive belongings.

![Semi truck driving on a rural highway under clear summer skies](/images/blog/2029/07/mastering-long-distance-moving-this-summer/a-blue-semi-truck-driving-on-a-rural-hig-1.webp)


Book your [long distance move](/long-distance) at least 4 to 6 weeks ahead during summer. Waiting until the last minute limits your options and often increases costs.

## Practical Tips for a July Long Distance Move

### Lock In Your Costs with a Binding Estimate

A binding estimate guarantees your final price based on an in-home or virtual survey of your belongings. Without one, your costs can jump on moving day when the truck arrives and the actual weight exceeds the phone estimate.

### Schedule Around the Holiday

Avoid booking your move for July 3rd through 5th. Movers may charge holiday rates, and highway traffic peaks around the Fourth of July weekend. Mid-month weekdays are typically the most affordable and least congested.

### Protect Your Belongings from the Heat

Florida summer temps regularly hit the mid-90s. Items that need temperature protection include:

- **Electronics**: Laptops, TVs, and gaming consoles can overheat in a parked truck
- **Candles and vinyl records**: Will warp or melt in extreme heat
- **Medications**: Many require storage below 77 degrees
- **Photographs and artwork**: Humidity can cause warping and mold

Ask your movers about climate-controlled truck options or plan to transport heat-sensitive items in your air-conditioned car.

### Inventory Everything Before Packing

Create a detailed inventory with photos. For a long distance move, this documentation is important for two reasons: it speeds up the claims process if anything is damaged, and it helps you verify that every box arrives at your destination.

## FAQ

### How far in advance should I book a long distance summer move?
Book at least 4 to 6 weeks ahead during peak summer months. July is the busiest time for long distance movers, and waiting too long limits your date options and can increase costs.

### What items should I not put on a moving truck in summer?
Keep medications, candles, vinyl records, and perishable foods out of the truck. Transport them in your air-conditioned car instead, since truck interiors can exceed 130 degrees when parked.

### Is it cheaper to move on weekdays in July?
Yes. Mid-month weekdays are typically the least expensive and least congested. Avoid July 3rd through 5th, when movers may charge holiday rates and highways are packed.
