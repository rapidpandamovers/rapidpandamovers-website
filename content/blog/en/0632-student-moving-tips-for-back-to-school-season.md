## Key Tips for Student Moving in September

### Plan Ahead for Peak Move-In Times

September is the busiest time for student moving. With UM, FIU, Barry University, and Miami Dade College all starting fall semester around the same time, scheduling your student moving early ensures you get your preferred date and time. Many students moving to areas like Coral Gables, University Park, or the Biscayne Bay campus find that weekday moves offer more flexibility.

### Book Early During Peak Move-In Weekends

Most colleges have designated move-in weekends at the end of August and beginning of September. This single step can save hours of stress and potential problems on moving day. Our experienced crews have seen what happens when this step is skipped.

### Consider the Weather

South Florida's late summer weather brings heat, humidity, and afternoon storms. Professional movers know how to protect your belongings from heat exposure, humidity, and rain during transport.

### Prepare Your Belongings

Take time to inventory your items before the move. This is especially important for student moving, as proper documentation helps ensure everything arrives safely at your dorm or apartment.

## Back-to-School Moving Checklist

Before your move, make sure to:

- Sort through belongings and declutter over the summer
- Gather important documents (ID, housing forms, class schedule) in one accessible place
- Check university move-in policies and parking restrictions
- Coordinate with roommates to avoid duplicate items
- Notify relevant parties of your address change
- Arrange utilities at your new location (if applicable)
