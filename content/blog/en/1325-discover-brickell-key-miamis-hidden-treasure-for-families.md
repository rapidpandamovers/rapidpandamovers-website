Brickell Key is a private island in Biscayne Bay connected to mainland Miami by a single bridge. It sits just south of the Brickell Financial District, within a 10-minute walk of Downtown, yet it feels completely removed from the city's noise. The island has 24-hour gated security, a 1.1-mile bayfront jogging path, landscaped grounds, and a small village of amenities that make daily life surprisingly self-contained.

For families looking for the security and calm of a gated community with the convenience of urban Miami right across the bridge, Brickell Key is hard to beat.

## What Makes Brickell Key Different

Most Miami neighborhoods sit on the mainland grid, sharing streets with traffic, commercial activity, and the general bustle of the city. Brickell Key is an island. There is one way in and one way out, and a guardhouse monitors every vehicle. That simple fact changes the feel of daily life dramatically.

![A Picturesque View Of Miami S Skyline Fr 4](/images/blog/2029/05/discover-brickell-key-miamis-hidden-treasure-for-families/a-picturesque-view-of-miami-s-skyline-fr-4.webp)


Kids ride bikes on the bayfront path. Residents jog the 1.1-mile loop around the island's perimeter with bay views in every direction. The landscaping is lush and maintained. It does not feel like Miami. It feels like a resort where you happen to live.

### Island Amenities

Despite its small size, Brickell Key has everyday necessities right on the island:

- **Marketplace and grocery**: A small gourmet market for daily essentials
- **Restaurants and cafes**: Waterfront dining without leaving the island
- **Dry cleaning**: On-island pickup and delivery
- **Beauty salon**: Resident-focused services
- **Flower shop**: Because a private island should have one

The **Mandarin Oriental Miami** hotel has been a fixture on the island for over two decades. The original hotel closed in 2025 to make way for The Residences at Mandarin Oriental, a new luxury development that will include a reimagined hotel and residential tower.

## Condo Buildings on Brickell Key

Brickell Key has eleven bayfront condo developments, each with its own character and price point:

- **Asia**: One of the island's most prestigious addresses, offering both bay and river views. Known for spacious layouts and high-end finishes.
- **Carbonell**: A 40-story tower with 284 luxury residences, only nine units per floor. Located on the northwest side of the island with expansive water and city views.
- **One Tequesta Point, Two Tequesta Point, Three Tequesta Point**: Three interconnected towers that are among the island's original developments. Well-maintained with strong condo associations.
- **Courts Brickell Key**: A mid-rise option with more accessible pricing and bayfront exposure.
- **Courvoisier Courts**: Modern finishes with a strong amenity package.
- **Isola**: Contemporary design with floor-to-ceiling glass.
- **Brickell Key One and Brickell Key Two**: Established buildings with solid rental and resale markets.
- **St. Louis**: A boutique building with a smaller, more intimate community feel.

A major new development, **The Residences at Mandarin Oriental**, will add roughly 220 units across approximately 80 stories, featuring 2-4 bedroom residences from 1,591 to 7,000 square feet. The project is expected to deliver around 2030, bringing the Mandarin Oriental's signature spa, multiple pools, a hammam, pickleball and basketball courts, an indoor golf simulator, and a signature restaurant to the island.

## Family Life on Brickell Key

The island's layout is genuinely family-friendly in a way most of Miami is not:

- **Safety**: The gated entrance and security presence give parents peace of mind. Kids can play outdoors without the risks of a typical urban neighborhood.
- **Outdoor space**: The bayfront path is used for walking, running, cycling, and stroller laps. Playgrounds and green spaces are scattered throughout the island.
- **Community feel**: With only about 2,500 residents, neighbors know each other. It is a small-town dynamic in the middle of a major city.
- **School access**: Brickell Key is within the Miami-Dade County Public Schools district. The bridge puts you minutes from schools in Brickell and Downtown, plus private school options throughout the city.

## Moving to Brickell Key

Moving onto an island with gated access and high-rise buildings requires coordination that a standard residential move does not. Here is what to expect:

### Building and Island Logistics

- **Gate access**: Your moving company needs to be pre-authorized with island security. Provide their vehicle information and crew details in advance.
- **Building reservations**: Each condo building has its own move-in process, freight elevator schedule, and insurance requirements. Contact your building management at least 2-3 weeks before your move.
- **Certificate of insurance (COI)**: Most buildings require your mover to carry specific insurance and name the building as an additional insured.
- **Loading dock timing**: Dock space on Brickell Key is limited. Missing your window means rescheduling, potentially by days.

### The Bridge Factor

Everything coming onto or leaving Brickell Key crosses a single two-lane bridge. On a busy moving day, this can create bottlenecks:

- Schedule your move for early morning when bridge traffic is lightest
- Avoid moving on weekends when resident and visitor traffic peaks
- Your moving company should plan for a single trip if possible, using the right truck size to minimize bridge crossings

### Spring Weather

May in Miami brings daily afternoon thunderstorms and high humidity. Morning moves that wrap up by early afternoon avoid weather delays and keep your crew working in better conditions.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Brickell Key a gated community?**
Yes. Brickell Key has a single entry point with a guardhouse that monitors all vehicle traffic 24 hours a day. Visitors and service providers must be pre-authorized.

**How many condo buildings are on Brickell Key?**
There are currently eleven bayfront condo developments on the island, with The Residences at Mandarin Oriental bringing the count up when it completes.

**Do I need a car on Brickell Key?**
It depends on your lifestyle. The island has basic amenities, and Brickell and Downtown are walkable across the bridge. Many residents keep a car for trips to the beach, airport, and suburban errands, but day-to-day life can work without one.

**What is the price range for condos on Brickell Key?**
Prices vary widely by building, floor, and view. Entry-level units in older buildings may start in the $300,000s, while premium units in towers like Asia or Carbonell can exceed $2 million. The upcoming Mandarin Oriental residences will price at the luxury end of the market.
