## About Kendall

Kendall is a large census-designated place (CDP) in Miami-Dade County, located about 10 miles southwest of Downtown Miami. Covering 16.1 square miles with approximately 80,000 residents, Kendall offers diverse neighborhoods, shopping centers, and suburban living within Miami's urban core.

## Neighborhoods

Kendall comprises multiple distinct neighborhoods:

### West Kendall
Newer development with master-planned communities, shopping centers, and family-friendly amenities. Site of the proposed $2 billion City Park development. Families love the proximity to Zoo Miami and the newer construction with modern hurricane-resistant features. The area around SW 137th Avenue and Kendall Drive offers plenty of dining and retail options.

### Kendall Proper
Established neighborhoods with mature landscaping and diverse housing options. You will find a mix of 1970s-80s ranch-style homes with larger lots, many featuring pools and Florida rooms. Streets like SW 104th Street and the areas around Dadeland are popular with professionals who want quick access to the Metrorail.

### The Falls Area
Near The Falls shopping center, offering retail access and established residential areas. This open-air mall features a waterfall landscape and upscale retailers, making it a weekend destination for the whole family. Homes here tend to be larger and more established.

### Country Walk/Three Lakes
Master-planned communities with parks, lakes, and family amenities. These gated neighborhoods offer a true suburban feel with community pools, playgrounds, and walking trails around scenic lakes. Country Walk Elementary is a coveted school choice for families moving to the area.

## Moving Tips for Kendall

### Best Time to Move
- **Fall/Winter (October-March)**: Best weather for moving, with lower humidity and minimal rain. October through December sees the most pleasant temperatures.
- **Weekdays**: Avoid weekend traffic at Dadeland Mall and The Falls. Saturday afternoons on Kendall Drive can add 30 minutes to your commute.
- **Early Morning Start**: Beat the afternoon thunderstorms that roll in May through October. Most experienced Miami movers recommend starting by 7am.

### Local Considerations
- **Traffic**: Kendall Drive (SW 88th Street) is one of Miami's most congested roads. Plan your moving route to use the Palmetto Expressway for east-west travel when possible.
- **Gated Communities**: Many Kendall neighborhoods require 48-hour notice for moving trucks. Get your gate access code and visitor passes arranged at least a week ahead.
- **HOA Rules**: Moving hours are often restricted to 8am-5pm in many communities. Some HOAs require a moving deposit that is refundable after inspection.
- **Flood Zones**: Parts of West Kendall fall into flood zones. Check FEMA maps and factor flood insurance into your housing budget.
- **Hurricane Season**: If moving June through November, have a hurricane plan ready. Know where your shutters are stored and keep important documents accessible.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does it cost to move to Kendall?

Local moves within Miami-Dade typically range from $400 to $1,200 depending on home size and distance. A 2-bedroom apartment moving from Brickell to Kendall averages $500-700, while a 4-bedroom house from Coral Gables might run $800-1,100. Factors like stairs, long carries, and specialty items (pianos, safes) affect the final price.

### Is Kendall a safe neighborhood?

Kendall has lower crime rates than most Miami neighborhoods. The gated communities in West Kendall and Country Walk are particularly family-friendly. Like anywhere in Miami, common-sense precautions apply: lock your car, secure packages, and get to know your neighbors.

### What is the commute from Kendall to Downtown Miami?

Expect 25-35 minutes during off-peak hours, or 45-60 minutes during rush hour (7-9am and 5-7pm). The Dadeland South and Dadeland North Metrorail stations connect to Downtown in about 20 minutes, making them a popular option for commuters who want to skip the traffic.

### Are there good schools in Kendall?

Kendall has several A-rated public schools, including Sunset Elementary and Palmetto Senior High. The area also offers magnet programs and charter school options. Proximity to Florida International University and Miami Dade College makes higher education accessible.
