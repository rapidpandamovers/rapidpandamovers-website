Miami attracts the rich and famous, and their homes are as impressive as you'd expect. This May, take a look at some of the most notable celebrity properties in the area. From Star Island mansions to South Beach penthouses, these homes showcase why Miami remains a top destination for A-list residents.

## 1. Star Island Estates

This exclusive island connected to the MacArthur Causeway has housed Madonna, Diddy, and Gloria Estefan. Moving here requires discrete, high-security services and coordination with island security.

![Intricate Facade Of A Historic Ottoman M 1](/images/blog/2025/07/9-celebrity-homes-in-miami-you-wont-believe/intricate-facade-of-a-historic-ottoman-m-1.webp)


## 2. Versace Mansion (Casa Casuarina)

Gianni Versace's famous South Beach residence on Ocean Drive has seen multiple transformations and celebrity occupants. Now a boutique hotel, it remains one of Miami Beach's most photographed landmarks.

## 3. Fisher Island Properties

Accessible only by ferry from the terminal near South Beach, this ultra-exclusive community requires specialized logistics for any move. Movers must coordinate with the ferry schedule and building management.

## 4. Coral Gables Mansions

The historic Mediterranean-style estates in Coral Gables, near the Biltmore Hotel and University of Miami, have housed numerous celebrities over the decades. The tree-lined streets and strict zoning keep the area private and prestigious.

## 5. Miami Beach Penthouses

Luxury high-rise penthouses along Collins Avenue and the Faena District require coordinated elevator access and building management approval for moves. Many buildings restrict moving to weekday mornings only.

## 6. Indian Creek Island

Known as "Billionaire Bunker," this ultra-private island in the northern part of Biscayne Bay has attracted residents like Ivanka Trump, Tom Brady, and Jeff Bezos. With its own 13-officer police force, security here is tight.

![Vintage Victorian Interior With A Decora 3](/images/blog/2025/07/9-celebrity-homes-in-miami-you-wont-believe/vintage-victorian-interior-with-a-decora-3.webp)


## 7. Key Biscayne Estates

This barrier island paradise accessed via the Rickenbacker Causeway has been home to presidents and celebrities seeking privacy while staying close to Miami. Richard Nixon famously had his "Winter White House" here.

## 8. La Gorce Island

This exclusive gated community in Miami Beach, just north of the Fontainebleau, has housed celebrities like Cher. It remains one of the most prestigious waterfront addresses in Florida.

## 9. Hibiscus Island Properties

This man-made island in Biscayne Bay, connected to Palm Island near the MacArthur Causeway, offers waterfront living and has attracted numerous entertainment industry figures. The island provides easy access to both South Beach and Downtown Miami.
