July falls right in the middle of PCS peak season, which runs from roughly May 15 through August 31. If you have orders bringing you to or from South Florida, you are competing with thousands of other military families for moving dates, carriers, and crew availability. Here is how to handle it.

## Why July Is the Hardest Month for Military Moves

The Department of Defense processes the bulk of its permanent change of station moves during summer. That concentration creates real problems:

![A Diverse Couple Joyfully Unpacking Boxe 3](/images/blog/2029/07/your-guide-to-military-moving-in-july/a-diverse-couple-joyfully-unpacking-boxe-3.webp)


- **Carrier shortages**: Government-contracted movers get booked weeks in advance
- **Date flexibility drops**: Your preferred pack and pickup dates may not be available
- **Delays increase**: More moves in the system means more chances for scheduling conflicts
- **South Florida heat**: Daily afternoon thunderstorms and temperatures above 90 degrees add weather delays on top of everything else

The good news is that planning ahead and understanding your options can make a July move manageable.

## PCS Move Options for Service Members

You have two main paths for a military move, and choosing the right one matters more during peak season:

### Government Move (HHG)

The military arranges and pays for the move through a contracted carrier. In 2025, HomeSafe Alliance manages most household goods moves. When you receive orders:

1. Complete pre-move counseling through your Transportation Office (TO) or Personal Property Shipping Office (PPSO)
2. Finish your pre-move survey within 48 hours of receiving the HomeSafe email
3. Schedule pack and pickup dates as early as possible
4. Document all belongings with photos and video before the packers arrive

### Personally Procured Move (PPM)

Formerly called a DITY move, a PPM lets you arrange your own move and get reimbursed. The DoD reimburses PPM moves at a percentage of the government's estimated cost, which means careful planning can leave you ahead financially. For a July move in South Florida, a PPM gives you more control over your timeline.

## Tips for a Smooth July Military Move

### Book Early, Be Flexible

Contact your TO or PPSO the day you get orders. During peak season, a delay of even a few days can push your move dates by weeks. If you can be flexible on your pack date (even by two or three days), you will have more options.

### Reduce Your Load Before Packing

Less weight means lower costs for a PPM and faster work for an HHG move. Start sorting and donating items you no longer need while you wait for orders. Storage units, base thrift stores, and local donation centers can all help.

### Organize Your Paperwork

Keep these documents together and accessible throughout the move:

- PCS orders and amendments
- Housing office checkout paperwork
- Weight tickets (for PPM moves)
- Inventory sheets with photos/video of high-value items
- Insurance documentation
- Receipts for all moving-related expenses (meals, lodging, fuel)

### Prepare for South Florida Weather

If your move touches Miami-Dade, Broward, or Palm Beach County in July:

- Schedule loading and unloading for early morning (before 11 AM) to beat the worst heat
- Have tarps ready for afternoon rain that can roll in with little warning
- Keep electronics and documents in waterproof containers during transport
- Make sure the moving truck has working AC for items sensitive to heat

## Military Moving Checklist for July

Use this timeline to stay on track:

![Labeled Cardboard Boxes For Summer Cloth 2](/images/blog/2029/07/your-guide-to-military-moving-in-july/labeled-cardboard-boxes-for-summer-cloth-2.webp)


**6-8 weeks before move date:**
- Contact TO/PPSO and schedule pre-move counseling
- Research housing options at your new duty station
- Start decluttering and donating items you will not take

**3-4 weeks before move date:**
- Confirm pack and pickup dates
- Arrange temporary housing if needed for the gap period
- Notify your landlord, utility companies, and update your address with USPS

**1 week before move date:**
- Complete a full photo and video inventory of all belongings
- Pack a personal essentials bag (documents, medications, chargers, change of clothes)
- Confirm building access, parking, and elevator reservations at both locations

**Moving day:**
- Be present during loading to answer questions and verify inventory
- Keep your essentials bag and paperwork with you, not on the truck
- Get signed copies of all inventory and condition reports

## FAQ

**What is PCS peak season?**
PCS peak season runs from roughly mid-May through the end of August. This is when the majority of military permanent change of station moves happen, leading to higher demand for movers and tighter schedules.

**Can I do a PPM move in July?**
Yes. A personally procured move gives you more control over your schedule during peak season. You arrange your own movers and transportation, then submit weight tickets and receipts for reimbursement.

**How far in advance should I book movers for a July PCS?**
Book as soon as you receive orders. During peak season, even a few days of delay can push your available dates by weeks. Six to eight weeks of lead time is ideal.
