August back-to-school moves flood Miami every year. Students heading to the University of Miami, Florida International University, Miami Dade College, and other local schools pack up and relocate on tight timelines. Most of those moves involve standard dorm furniture and boxes, but some students bring family heirlooms, antique desks, vintage dressers, or inherited pieces that cannot be replaced.

If you are moving antique items to a college residence in Miami, the process requires more care than tossing boxes into a U-Haul. Here is what to consider.

## Why College Moves and Antiques Are a Tricky Combination

College moves have constraints that make antique transport harder:

![A Couple Carefully Packing Chairs With B 3](/images/blog/2029/08/college-guide-to-antique-moving-in-miami/a-couple-carefully-packing-chairs-with-b-3.webp)


**Tight windows.** Most universities assign specific move-in dates and time slots. You may have only a few hours to unload, which puts pressure on the handling process.

**Shared spaces.** Dorms and student apartments have narrow hallways, small elevators, and doorways that were not designed for large furniture. An antique armoire that fits fine in a family home may not clear a dorm hallway.

**Limited climate control during transit.** August in Miami means temperatures in the 90s with humidity above 70 percent. Antique wood expands in humidity and can crack if moved between drastically different environments (like an air-conditioned home to a hot truck to an air-conditioned dorm).

**Budget pressure.** Students and families often try to minimize costs, which can lead to shortcuts that damage valuable pieces.

## Before the Move: Preparation Steps

### Get an Appraisal or Valuation

If the antique has significant monetary or sentimental value, get it appraised before the move. This documentation serves two purposes: it establishes the item's condition before transport, and it provides a basis for insurance claims if something goes wrong.

Take detailed photographs from multiple angles, including close-ups of any existing damage, hardware, and joints.

### Measure Everything Twice

Measure the antique piece, then measure every doorway, hallway, elevator, and stairwell it needs to pass through at the destination. College buildings often have dimensions that differ from residential homes. A 42-inch-wide dresser does not fit through a 36-inch door frame, and forcing it risks damage to both the piece and the building.

### Decide What Actually Needs to Go

Be honest about whether the piece belongs in a college living situation. A valuable antique writing desk might be better suited for a college apartment than a shared dorm room where it is more likely to get dinged by roommates or damaged by spills. Consider keeping the most valuable pieces at home and bringing them to a permanent residence later.

## Packing Antiques for a College Move

### Materials You Need

![A Couple In A New Apartment Carefully Wr 1](/images/blog/2029/08/college-guide-to-antique-moving-in-miami/a-couple-in-a-new-apartment-carefully-wr-1.webp)


- **Acid-free tissue paper** for wrapping hardware and small components
- **Moving blankets** (furniture pads) for wrapping large surfaces
- **Bubble wrap** for fragile elements like glass panels, mirrors, or carved details
- **Blue painter's tape** rather than regular tape, which can leave residue on finished wood
- **Custom crating** for extremely valuable or fragile items

### Packing Tips by Item Type

**Wood furniture (desks, dressers, tables):** Remove drawers and shelves. Wrap legs individually. Pad all corners. Do not wrap wood directly in plastic; it traps moisture. Use moving blankets as the outer layer.

**Upholstered antiques (chairs, settees):** Wrap in moving blankets, then cover with stretch wrap to keep blankets in place. The fabric should not contact plastic directly.

**Mirrors and glass elements:** Use mirror boxes or custom crating. Tape an X across the glass with painter's tape to reduce the chance of shattering.

**Hardware (pulls, knobs, hinges):** Remove, bag, and label. Tape the bag to the inside of a drawer so nothing gets lost.

## Moving Day: August-Specific Tips

### Start Early

A 7:00 AM start lets you load and transport before afternoon thunderstorms hit, typically between 2:00 and 5:00 PM in Miami during August. Antiques should never be exposed to rain, even briefly.

### Climate Transition

If the antique has been in an air-conditioned home, try to minimize the time it spends in the heat. Load it last and unload it first, keeping the hot-truck exposure as short as possible.

### Coordinate with the College

Contact the residence hall or apartment management in advance. Ask about:

- Elevator reservations
- Loading dock access and approved unloading times
- Floor protection requirements
- Whether furniture dollies are allowed on the floors

Many Miami-area schools have specific move-in day logistics. FIU and UM both publish move-in guides with approved entry points and time windows.

## Professional vs. DIY for Antiques

For standard college supplies, a DIY move makes sense. For antiques, weigh the cost of professional help against the replacement value:

![Close Up Of A Person Preparing Wicker Fu 4](/images/blog/2029/08/college-guide-to-antique-moving-in-miami/close-up-of-a-person-preparing-wicker-fu-4.webp)


| Factor | DIY | Professional |
|--------|-----|-------------|
| Cost | Lower upfront | $200-$500+ for specialty items |
| Risk | Higher (no equipment, less experience) | Lower (trained crews, proper tools) |
| Insurance | Your renter's or homeowner's policy | Mover's liability coverage |
| Time | You handle everything | Crew handles packing and transport |

If the antique is worth more than $1,000, professional handling usually pays for itself in risk reduction alone.

## FAQ

**Can antique furniture handle Miami's humidity?**
Most antiques can tolerate normal humidity, but rapid changes between air conditioning and outdoor heat cause the most damage. Gradual transitions and keeping pieces out of direct sunlight reduce the risk.

**Should I insure antiques separately for a move?**
If the piece has significant value, yes. Standard moving insurance (released value) covers only $0.60 per pound. Full value protection or a separate rider on your homeowner's policy provides real coverage.

**What if my antique does not fit in the dorm?**
Consider short-term [**storage**](/miami-storage-solutions) in a climate-controlled facility until you move to a larger apartment. This is better than leaving a valuable piece in a space where it could be damaged.

**Is it worth moving antiques for just a school year?**
If you are living off-campus in a year-long lease, it may make sense. For a single semester in a dorm, the cost and risk may outweigh the benefit of having the piece with you.
