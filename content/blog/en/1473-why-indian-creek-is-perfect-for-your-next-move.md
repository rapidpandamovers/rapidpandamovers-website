Indian Creek Village sits on a 250-acre man-made barrier island in Biscayne Bay, connected to the mainland by a single guarded bridge. Often called the "Billionaire Bunker," this private enclave has just 41 residential lots and fewer than 90 full-time residents. If you are considering a move to one of the most exclusive communities in the United States, here is what you need to know.

## What Makes Indian Creek Different

Unlike most Miami-Dade communities, Indian Creek is an incorporated village with its own municipal government and a dedicated police force that patrols both land and water around the clock. The resident-to-officer ratio is roughly 10 to 1, making it one of the most heavily secured residential areas in the country.

![A Picturesque View Of Miami Skyline With 1](/images/blog/2029/11/why-indian-creek-is-perfect-for-your-next-move/a-picturesque-view-of-miami-skyline-with-1.webp)


The island is home to the Indian Creek Country Club, which occupies most of the land and features an 18-hole golf course designed by William S. Flynn. Club membership is by invitation only and comes with access to tennis courts, a fitness center, and waterfront dining.

### Who Lives Here

Indian Creek's current and former residents include names like Jeff Bezos, Tom Brady, Ivanka Trump, and Julio Iglesias. Properties regularly trade for $30 million or more, with several sales exceeding $100 million in recent years. The village has no commercial businesses, no sidewalks, and no through-traffic. Privacy is the product.

### Location and Access

Despite its seclusion, Indian Creek sits minutes from some of Miami's most popular areas:

- **Bal Harbour Shops** and Surfside are directly across the waterway
- **Downtown Miami** is roughly 20 minutes south via I-95
- **Miami Beach** and its dining and nightlife scene is a short drive east
- **Miami International Airport** is about 30 minutes west

The single bridge entrance at Indian Creek Drive connects to Surfside, providing quick access to Collins Avenue and the broader Miami Beach corridor.

## Planning a Move to Indian Creek

Moving to Indian Creek requires more coordination than a typical residential relocation. Every detail, from gate access to delivery windows, involves advance planning.

### Village Regulations

Indian Creek Village maintains strict rules governing construction, landscaping, and moving logistics. Before your move date, you will need to:

- **Coordinate with village administration** for gate access and moving truck approval
- **Confirm delivery windows** since the bridge and island roads have vehicle restrictions
- **Review property-specific requirements** including any covenants or deed restrictions
- **Arrange marine access** if your property includes a dock and you plan to transport items by water

### Timing Considerations

November through April is South Florida's dry season, which means lower humidity and minimal rain disruption. However, this is also peak season for real estate closings and renovations across Miami Beach and the barrier islands. Booking your move early helps secure your preferred date.

Weekday moves tend to run more smoothly at Indian Creek because weekend traffic on Collins Avenue and the surrounding causeways is heavier.

## What to Expect as a New Resident

### The Country Club

For most residents, the Indian Creek Country Club serves as the social center of the community. The club hosts seasonal events, holiday dinners, and member tournaments throughout the year. Joining the club is a separate process from purchasing property and requires sponsorship from existing members.

### Schools and Family Life

Indian Creek does not have its own schools. Families typically enroll children in nearby private institutions such as:

- **Ransom Everglades School** in Coconut Grove
- **Gulliver Preparatory School** in Pinecrest
- **Miami Country Day School** in Miami Shores

These schools are 20 to 40 minutes by car depending on traffic.

### Daily Essentials

Since there are no stores or restaurants on the island, residents rely on nearby communities for daily needs:

- **Grocery shopping**: Bal Harbour, Surfside, and Bay Harbor Islands have multiple options including Publix and specialty markets
- **Dining**: Bal Harbour and Miami Beach offer a wide range of restaurants
- **Healthcare**: Mount Sinai Medical Center in Miami Beach and Aventura Hospital are both within 15 to 20 minutes

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How many homes are on Indian Creek Island?**
The village has 41 platted residential lots. As of recent records, roughly 29 estate homes occupy these lots, with some owners holding multiple adjacent properties.

![Stunning Aerial View Of Miami Beach S Sk 2](/images/blog/2029/11/why-indian-creek-is-perfect-for-your-next-move/stunning-aerial-view-of-miami-beach-s-sk-2.webp)


**Can anyone visit Indian Creek?**
No. The village is a private, gated community with 24/7 security. Visitors must be pre-approved by a resident, and vehicles are subject to inspection at the bridge checkpoint.

**What does a home cost on Indian Creek?**
Properties typically list between $30 million and $150 million. Sale prices vary based on lot size, waterfront footage, and the condition of the existing structure. Vacant lots also trade at a premium.

**Is Indian Creek part of Miami Beach?**
No. Indian Creek is an independent municipality, separate from both Miami Beach and the City of Miami. It has its own mayor, village council, and police department.
