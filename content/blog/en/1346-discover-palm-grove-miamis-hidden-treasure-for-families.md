You want a Miami neighborhood with character, but Brickell feels too corporate and Wynwood is too loud. Palm Grove sits right in that sweet spot: a quiet, historic neighborhood in Miami's Upper Eastside with tree-lined streets, 1930s bungalows, and an emerging arts scene just steps from your front door.

Here is what you need to know before moving to Palm Grove.

## Where Is Palm Grove?

Palm Grove is a subdivision within Miami's Upper Eastside, bordered by NE 54th Street to the south, Biscayne Boulevard to the east, Little River to the north, and the Florida East Coast Railway tracks to the west. The neighborhood is home to about 1,850 residents across a compact, walkable grid.

![A Tranquil Street Scene In Orlando Fl Fe 2](/images/blog/2029/06/discover-palm-grove-miamis-hidden-treasure-for-families/a-tranquil-street-scene-in-orlando-fl-fe-2.webp)


You are 10 minutes from Downtown Miami, 15 minutes from Miami Beach via the Julia Tuttle Causeway, and directly connected to Biscayne Boulevard's MiMo Historic District for shopping and dining.

## The Ironside District: Palm Grove's Creative Heart

The biggest draw for newcomers is Miami Ironside, an urban art and design district that runs along the western edge of Palm Grove. What used to be industrial warehouses has been transformed into over 65 designer showrooms, art galleries, architecture firms, and eateries spread across 100,000 square feet.

Highlights include:

- **Ironside Pizza & Coffee Co.** serves wood-fired pizza, fresh pasta, and cold cuts. It is the anchor restaurant of the district.
- **Over 70 creative tenants** ranging from interior designers to photographers to independent retailers
- **Four permanent pieces of the Berlin Wall** by street artist Thierry Noir, displayed as public art
- **Rainbow eucalyptus trees** lining the outdoor walkways, a favorite for family photos

Ironside hosts year-round public art exhibitions, pop-up markets, and community events. It is the kind of place where you grab coffee, browse a gallery, and run into your neighbors all in one walk.

## The MiMo Historic District

On Palm Grove's eastern edge, Biscayne Boulevard features the MiMo (Miami Modern) Architecture Historic District. These beautifully renovated mid-century buildings from the 1950s and 1960s now house restaurants, boutiques, and cafes. The neon signs, curved facades, and bold colors make this stretch one of Miami's most photogenic corridors.

Popular spots along the MiMo strip include local restaurants, vintage shops, and cocktail bars that keep the neighborhood active into the evening.

## Dining and Nightlife

Palm Grove and the surrounding Upper Eastside offer plenty of options:

- **Ironside Pizza & Coffee Co.** for casual Italian in the Ironside district
- **Uptown 66 Taqueria** for tacos and Mexican street food
- **Raw & Refined** for craft burgers and creative cocktails
- **Biscayne Boulevard restaurants** along the MiMo strip for everything from sushi to Cuban fare

The dining scene here trends casual and neighborhood-focused. You will not find bottle-service clubs, but you will find places where the staff knows your name after a few visits.

## Schools in and Near Palm Grove

Families moving to Palm Grove have solid school options:

- **Morningside K-8 Academy** (6620 NE 5th Ave) is the local public school, housed in a beautiful 1930s Spanish Mission building with courtyards full of fruit trees. It is an International Language Magnet offering Spanish, Haitian Creole, and French. The school holds an A+ rating and serves about 400 students with a 14:1 student-teacher ratio.
- **The Cushman School** is a well-regarded private school nearby serving pre-K through 8th grade.
- **Morningside Montessori School** offers an alternative approach for younger children.
- **MAST Academy** and **Design and Architecture Senior High (DASH)** are top-rated magnet high schools accessible from the neighborhood.

## Parks and Recreation

Palm Grove residents enjoy several nearby green spaces:

- **Legion Park** is a waterfront park along Biscayne Bay with a playground, walking paths, and a popular Saturday morning farmers market
- **Morningside Park** features tennis courts, a pool, playgrounds, and large open fields along the bay
- **Lemon City Park** offers basketball courts and a community recreation center

These parks are within walking or biking distance, which is a big reason families choose Palm Grove over more congested Miami neighborhoods.

## Housing in Palm Grove

The neighborhood offers a range of housing styles:

![A Tranquil Suburban Street With A Canopy 3](/images/blog/2029/06/discover-palm-grove-miamis-hidden-treasure-for-families/a-tranquil-suburban-street-with-a-canopy-3.webp)


- **Historic bungalows**: 1920s-1940s Spanish Eclectic, Mission, Art Deco, and Craftsman homes. Many have been renovated while preserving original architectural details. The Palm Grove Neighborhood Historic District protects many of these properties.
- **New construction**: Modern single-family homes and townhouses are filling in vacant lots
- **Condos**: Some mid-rise options along Biscayne Boulevard
- **Rental apartments**: More affordable than Brickell or Wynwood, with prices reflecting the neighborhood's growing popularity

Home prices in Palm Grove have appreciated steadily, but the neighborhood still offers value compared to waterfront areas like Bay Point and Belle Meade. Work with a realtor who understands the micro-markets within the Upper Eastside to find the right fit.

## Moving to Palm Grove: What to Know

Summer is peak moving season in Miami, so plan ahead. Here are Palm Grove-specific tips:

- **Street parking**: Most homes have driveways, but loading a moving truck on narrower residential streets requires coordination. Confirm with your moving company about truck size and positioning.
- **Tree canopy**: Palm Grove's mature tree canopy provides shade but can limit truck clearance on some streets. Your movers should do a pre-move site check.
- **Historic home considerations**: Older homes may have narrow doorways, tight staircases, or non-standard layouts. Measure furniture and doorways before moving day.
- **Weather timing**: Start early in the morning. Miami's daily afternoon thunderstorms typically hit between 2 and 4 PM from June through September.

## Getting Around from Palm Grove

- **Downtown Miami**: 10 minutes via Biscayne Boulevard or I-95
- **Miami Beach**: 15 minutes via the Julia Tuttle Causeway (I-195)
- **Wynwood and Midtown**: 5-10 minutes south along Biscayne Boulevard
- **Miami International Airport**: 20 minutes via I-195 to SR-112
- **Public transit**: Biscayne Boulevard bus routes connect to the Metrorail and Metromover systems

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Palm Grove a good neighborhood for families?

Yes. Palm Grove offers walkable, tree-lined streets, highly rated schools like Morningside K-8 Academy, and nearby parks including Legion Park and Morningside Park. The neighborhood has a quieter, more residential feel compared to nearby Wynwood or Edgewater.

### How much does it cost to live in Palm Grove, Miami?

Home prices vary widely depending on whether you are looking at a historic bungalow or new construction. The neighborhood is more affordable than waterfront areas like Bay Point but has seen steady appreciation. Rental prices are moderate for the Upper Eastside area.

### What is the Ironside district in Palm Grove?

Miami Ironside is an art and design district on Palm Grove's western edge. It houses over 65 showrooms, galleries, and studios in converted warehouses, along with restaurants and creative businesses. It is open to the public and hosts regular exhibitions and events.
