Spring Garden is one of those Miami neighborhoods that most people drive past without realizing what's there. Tucked between the Dolphin Expressway to the north and the Miami River to the southwest, this small historic district packs more character per block than neighborhoods ten times its size.

If you're thinking about making it home, here's what to expect.

## Why Spring Garden

Spring Garden was designated as a Historic District by the City of Miami in 1997, and for good reason. It's the oldest intact single-family neighborhood still remaining along the Miami River, with homes dating from the mid-1910s through the 1940s.

![Charming Art Deco Building In Miami With 3](/images/blog/2029/06/welcome-to-spring-garden-your-moving-handbook/charming-art-deco-building-in-miami-with-3.webp)


The streets are lined with giant banyan trees and moss-draped live oaks. The homes are a mix of Florida vernacular bungalows, Mediterranean Revival, and frame vernacular styles, most of them one or two stories. It feels like old Florida in a way that very few Miami neighborhoods still do.

### Location

Spring Garden is bounded by the Dolphin Expressway (SR 836) to the north, the Seybold Canal to the east, the Miami River to the southwest, and West 12th Avenue to the west. That puts you:

- About 5 minutes from Downtown Miami
- 10 minutes from Brickell
- 15 minutes from Wynwood and the Design District
- Easy access to I-95 and the Dolphin Expressway for county-wide commuting

### The Community

This is a neighborhood where people know their neighbors. The Spring Garden Civic Association is active and engaged, organizing neighborhood events and advocating for the historic district's preservation. New residents who get involved find it's one of the fastest ways to feel at home.

## What Makes Spring Garden Different

### Historic Architecture

Unlike most Miami neighborhoods that have been rebuilt or developed with modern construction, Spring Garden's building stock is largely original. The historic designation means exterior changes to homes require review, which keeps the neighborhood's character intact.

If you're buying here, be aware that renovations on historic homes may need approval from the city's Historic and Environmental Preservation Board. This can add time to projects but protects the investment for everyone.

### Housing Options

Spring Garden is primarily single-family homes:

- **Historic bungalows**: The signature housing type, with covered porches, wood framing, and generous lot sizes by Miami standards
- **Mediterranean Revival homes**: Stucco and tile-roofed homes from the 1920s and 1930s
- **Renovated historic homes**: Updated interiors with preserved exterior character
- **Some newer infill**: Limited new construction that must conform to historic district guidelines

### The Miami River

Spring Garden's proximity to the Miami River is part of its appeal. The river has seen significant investment in recent years, with new restaurants, breweries, and public spaces opening along its banks. Wharf Miami, a waterfront dining and events venue, is a short drive away.

## Moving to Spring Garden in Summer

Summer moves in this neighborhood have some specific considerations.

### Street and Access Planning

- **Narrow streets**: Many of Spring Garden's residential streets are narrower than modern standards. Make sure your moving company knows to bring appropriately sized trucks
- **Tree canopy**: The beautiful old trees that shade the streets can also create clearance issues for tall trucks. Verify height clearances on your specific block
- **Parking**: Street parking is the norm here. Coordinate with your movers about reserving curb space on moving day
- **Historic homes**: Older doorways and hallways tend to be narrower than modern construction. Measure everything before moving day

### Weather Timing

Like all summer moves in Miami, aim for an early morning start. Spring Garden's tree cover actually provides some shade advantage over more exposed neighborhoods, but afternoon thunderstorms are still a factor. Plan to have everything loaded by early afternoon.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Spring Garden a safe neighborhood?**
Spring Garden is a small, close-knit residential area. The active civic association and engaged residents contribute to the neighborhood's safety. As with any urban Miami neighborhood, standard awareness applies.

![Enchanting Gothic Style Bungalow Nestled 1](/images/blog/2029/06/welcome-to-spring-garden-your-moving-handbook/enchanting-gothic-style-bungalow-nestled-1.webp)


**Are there restrictions on renovating historic homes?**
Yes. Spring Garden is a designated Historic District, so exterior changes require review by the city's Historic and Environmental Preservation Board. Interior renovations typically don't need this approval unless they affect the building's structure or external appearance.

**What's the commute like from Spring Garden?**
Very manageable. Downtown is about 5 minutes away, and highway access is excellent via the Dolphin Expressway and I-95. The location is one of Spring Garden's biggest advantages.

**Are there schools in the neighborhood?**
The neighborhood itself is small and primarily residential, but several public and private schools are within a short drive. Riverside Elementary and Jose de Diego Middle School are among the nearest options.
