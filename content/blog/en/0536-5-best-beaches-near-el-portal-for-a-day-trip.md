Living in El Portal puts you within easy reach of some of Miami-Dade's best beaches. From quiet family-friendly shores to active water sports hubs, here are five beaches worth the short drive from your new home.

## 1. Crandon Park Beach

About 25 minutes from El Portal, Crandon Park on Key Biscayne offers wide sandy shores backed by natural dunes. The calm, shallow waters make it perfect for families with children. Amenities include picnic pavilions, volleyball courts, and a nature center. Parking costs $8 on weekdays, $10 on weekends.

![A Classic Motorcycle Parked By The Ocean 2](/images/blog/2026/08/5-best-beaches-near-el-portal-for-a-day-trip/a-classic-motorcycle-parked-by-the-ocean-2.webp)


## 2. North Beach Oceanside Park

Head 15 minutes east to this quieter stretch of Miami Beach at 80th Street and Collins Avenue. The expansive green space, laid-back atmosphere, and smaller crowds set it apart from South Beach. Locals gather here for the legendary full moon parties. Free street parking available on surrounding blocks if you arrive early.

## 3. Hobie Beach (Windsurfer Beach)

Located on the Rickenbacker Causeway en route to Key Biscayne, Hobie Beach is one of the few dog-friendly beaches in Miami. The shallow waters attract windsurfers, paddleboarders, and kayakers. From El Portal, you can reach it in about 20 minutes. Parking is along the causeway.

![Hand Holding A Straw Hat And Beverage On 1](/images/blog/2026/08/5-best-beaches-near-el-portal-for-a-day-trip/hand-holding-a-straw-hat-and-beverage-on-1.webp)


## 4. Sunny Isles Beach

Drive 20 minutes north to this stretch between the Atlantic Ocean and Intracoastal Waterway. Crystal-clear waters and wide beaches offer a more upscale feel. Walk out on the Newport Fishing Pier or rent paddleboards from local shops. Metered parking available along Collins Avenue.

## 5. Bill Baggs Cape Florida State Park

At the southern tip of Key Biscayne, this state park beach offers natural beauty and history. The 1825 lighthouse provides a great photo opportunity. Gentle waves and clean sand attract families seeking quieter shores. Park admission is $8 per vehicle, and the beach has full amenities including kayak rentals.
