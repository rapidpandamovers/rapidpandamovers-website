Thinking about moving to Homestead? You're considering one of Miami-Dade's most affordable and fastest-growing communities, with easy access to the Everglades, Florida Keys, and a rich agricultural heritage.

## About Homestead

Homestead is a rapidly growing city of approximately 86,000 residents, located at the southern tip of Miami-Dade County. Known for its affordability, agricultural roots, and proximity to natural attractions, Homestead offers a different pace of life than coastal Miami.

![American Alligator Basking In Bonita Spr 3](/images/blog/2025/12/settling-into-homestead-what-new-residents-need-to-know/american-alligator-basking-in-bonita-spr-3.webp)


### Demographics at a Glance

- **Population**: 85,796 (2024)
- **Growth Rate**: Faster than 92% of similarly sized U.S. cities since 2000
- **Median Age**: 32.3 years (younger than Miami-Dade average)
- **Median Household Income**: $61,975
- **Hispanic Population**: 67.7%
- **Foreign-Born Residents**: 40%

### A Young, Growing Community
Homestead's population skews young:
- 33% are adults aged 25-44
- 20.8% are aged 45-64
- Only 7.5% are 65 or older

## Real Estate and Housing

Homestead offers some of the most affordable housing in Miami-Dade County.

### Housing Market
- Significantly lower prices than coastal areas
- Mix of single-family homes and apartments
- New construction developments
- Growing suburban neighborhoods

### Affordable Housing Options
- 6 affordable housing complexes with 409 Section 8 apartments
- 1,496 income-based apartments
- 6,006 rent-subsidized apartments
- Homestead Housing Authority manages 1,990 vouchers

### Rental Market
- Average apartment rent: $1,995/month
- Section 8 vouchers cover approximately $1,000/month
- Budget options available starting around $859/month

## Agricultural Heritage

Homestead sits in the heart of the Redland agricultural district.

### Local Agriculture
- Tropical fruit farms (mangos, avocados, lychees, guava)
- Plant nurseries
- U-pick farms
- Agritourism destinations

### Notable Attractions
- **Fruit and Spice Park**: 37-acre tropical fruit botanical garden
- **Robert Is Here**: Famous fruit stand
- **Schnebly Redland's Winery**: Tropical fruit wines
- **Three Sisters Farm**: Organic produce and agritourism

### Agricultural Employment
10-17% of local workers are employed in agriculture and related industries.

## Transportation

### Highway Access
- US-1 (to Keys and Miami)
- Florida's Turnpike (Homestead Extension)
- Card Sound Road (alternative Keys route)

### Commuting to Miami
- 45-60 minutes to Downtown Miami
- 30 minutes to Kendall
- Bus connections to Metrorail

### Local Transportation
- Busway Express to South Miami
- Local bus routes
- Increasing rideshare availability

## Climate and Hurricane Preparedness

### Weather Considerations
- Hot, humid summers
- Mild winters
- Afternoon thunderstorms June-September
- Hurricane season June-November

![Wild Alligator In A Serene Swamp In Sebr 2](/images/blog/2025/12/settling-into-homestead-what-new-residents-need-to-know/wild-alligator-in-a-serene-swamp-in-sebr-2.webp)


### Hurricane History
Hurricane Andrew devastated Homestead in 1992, leading to rebuilt infrastructure and improved building codes.

### Preparedness Requirements
- Shutters or impact windows
- Emergency supplies
- Evacuation route knowledge
- Flood insurance for some areas

## Moving Tips for Homestead

### Distance from Miami
- Plan for longer travel times from central Miami
- Consider hiring local movers familiar with the area
- Account for toll costs on Turnpike

### Best Moving Times
- **Morning hours** before heat builds
- **Avoid Keys holiday weekends** with heavy US-1 traffic
- **Hurricane season** requires flexibility

### Climate Protection
- Protect belongings from humidity
- Use climate-controlled storage if needed
- Quick unloading to avoid heat exposure
