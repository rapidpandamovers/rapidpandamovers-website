# White Glove vs Standard Moving: Is the Upgrade Worth It

You're planning a move across Miami and two very different price quotes land in your inbox. The standard option looks reasonable. The white glove option costs more. So what exactly are you paying for, and does the upgrade make sense for your situation?

## What Standard Moving Covers

A standard move gets your belongings from Point A to Point B. Movers load furniture and boxes onto a truck, drive to the new location, and unload. They'll wrap large items in moving blankets, but packing is typically your responsibility. Placement tends to be basic: boxes go to the rooms labeled on their sides, and furniture lands in a roughly logical spot. For a small apartment in Wynwood or a starter condo in Doral, standard service handles the job.

## What White Glove Adds to the Picture

White glove service wraps around your entire move. The team packs everything from kitchen drawers to walk-in closets. Fragile items get custom crating. A move manager coordinates the entire timeline and acts as your single point of contact. At the new home, every box gets unpacked, every piece of furniture goes exactly where you want it, and the crew removes all packing debris before they leave.

## The Cost Difference

White glove moving typically costs 30-50% more than standard service. For a three-bedroom home in Coconut Grove, that difference might run $2,000-4,000 depending on the specifics. But consider what you're really comparing. A standard move still requires you to spend days packing and unpacking, buy supplies, and risk damage to items you packed yourself. When you factor in your time and the replacement cost of damaged belongings, the gap narrows.

## When Standard Service Makes Sense

If you're moving a small apartment with mostly everyday furniture, standard moving works fine. Students, young professionals, and anyone without high-value furnishings can save money with the basic option. The same goes for moves where you've already packed everything yourself and just need loading and transport.

## When White Glove Is Worth Every Dollar

The math changes when you own a Steinway piano, original artwork, a curated wine collection, or custom Italian furniture. It also changes when you're moving into or out of a Brickell high-rise where building rules demand floor protection, elevator reservations, and insurance certificates. For Star Island estates with $50,000 dining tables and museum-quality sculptures, skipping white glove service is a gamble with terrible odds.

## The Hidden Value: Your Time

Most people underestimate how long packing and unpacking takes. A four-bedroom home easily requires 40-60 hours of packing work. White glove teams handle that in a fraction of the time with better results. For busy professionals and families, reclaiming those hours has real value.
