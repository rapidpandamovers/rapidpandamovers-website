Thinking about moving to Key Biscayne? You're considering one of Miami's most exclusive island communities, offering a resort-like lifestyle just minutes from downtown. This guide provides essential information to help you plan your relocation.

## About Key Biscayne

Key Biscayne is an affluent barrier island village of approximately 15,100 residents, accessible only via the Rickenbacker Causeway. The island offers a unique combination of beach living, natural beauty, and proximity to urban amenities.

![A Delightful Black And White Image Of A  1](/images/blog/2026/09/discover-key-biscayne-your-complete-movers-guide/a-delightful-black-and-white-image-of-a--1.webp)


### Demographics at a Glance

- **Population**: 15,111 (2024)
- **Median Age**: 43 years
- **Median Household Income**: $172,604
- **Median Home Value**: $1.24 million
- **Hispanic Population**: 72.8%
- **Foreign-Born Residents**: 58.6%

### Island Character
- Only access via toll causeway
- Resort-like atmosphere
- Strong international community
- Quieter pace than mainland Miami

## Real Estate Market

Key Biscayne features some of Miami-Dade's most desirable real estate.

### Property Values
- Median property value: $1.24 million (4x national average)
- Homeownership rate: 69.3%
- Median monthly housing costs: $3,796
- Median gross rent: $3,501

### Property Types
- Single-family homes on village streets
- Oceanfront and bayfront estates
- High-rise condominiums
- Townhouse communities

### Market Characteristics
- Limited inventory due to island boundaries
- Strong demand maintains prices
- International buyer interest
- Premium for waterfront properties

## Location and Access

### Rickenbacker Causeway
The only access to Key Biscayne:
- Toll: Approximately $2.25 each way
- Spectacular bay views
- Hurricane evacuation considerations
- Can be congested during events

### Proximity to Miami
- 15-20 minutes to Downtown Miami/Brickell
- 25 minutes to Miami International Airport
- 10 minutes to Coconut Grove
- Direct causeway to Virginia Key

## Natural Attractions

### Bill Baggs Cape Florida State Park
- Southern tip of the island
- Cape Florida Lighthouse (1825)
- Beautiful beaches
- Bike trails and kayaking

### Crandon Park
- 808 acres of parkland
- Two miles of beach
- Golf course
- Tennis center (former site of Miami Open)

### Virginia Key
- Adjacent island with beaches
- Historic Virginia Key Beach Park
- Miami Seaquarium
- Outdoor recreation

## Community and Lifestyle

### Village Atmosphere
- Walkable downtown area
- Local shops and restaurants
- Community events and festivals
- Tight-knit neighborhood feel

### Recreation
- Tennis: Crandon Park Tennis Center
- Golf: Crandon Golf at Key Biscayne
- Boating: Marina and yacht clubs
- Cycling: Popular biking destination

### International Community
- 70%+ speak Spanish at home
- Large Latin American population
- International schools available
- Bilingual services common

## Schools and Education

### Public Schools
Key Biscayne is served by Miami-Dade County Public Schools:
- Key Biscayne K-8 Center (highly rated)
- MAST Academy on Virginia Key (magnet program)

### Private Options
- St. Agnes Academy (Catholic)
- Several private schools on the mainland
- International school options

## Language Considerations

### Multilingual Community
- 80.4% speak non-English language at home
- 70.9% speak Spanish
- 9.2% speak other Indo-European languages
- Most services available in English and Spanish

## Moving to Key Biscayne

### Causeway Logistics
- Plan moves during off-peak hours
- Large trucks face toll costs
- Single access point can create delays
- Weather affects causeway conditions

![Fashionable Woman In Bikini And Leather  4](/images/blog/2026/09/discover-key-biscayne-your-complete-movers-guide/fashionable-woman-in-bikini-and-leather--4.webp)


### Building Requirements
Many condos have specific rules:
- Moving hours restrictions
- Elevator reservations required
- Insurance certificates needed
- Damage deposits common

### Best Moving Times
- **Weekday mornings** after rush hour
- **Avoid weekend beach traffic**
- **Never during major events** (marathons, festivals)
- **Hurricane season** requires flexibility

### Island-Specific Considerations
- Limited storage facilities on island
- All supplies must come across causeway
- Plan for potential causeway closures
- Schedule buffer time for access

## Cost of Living

Key Biscayne has a higher cost of living:
- Premium grocery prices
- Limited service provider options
- Causeway tolls add up
- Higher insurance due to coastal location
