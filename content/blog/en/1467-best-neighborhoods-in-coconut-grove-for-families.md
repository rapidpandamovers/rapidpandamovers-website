Coconut Grove is Miami's oldest continuously inhabited neighborhood, and it remains one of the city's top picks for families. With tree-canopied streets, walkable dining and shopping, top-ranked schools, and a crime rate lower than 58% of Florida cities, the Grove earns its reputation as a family-friendly enclave. But Coconut Grove is not one single neighborhood. It breaks down into four distinct areas, each with its own character and price point.

Here is what families should know about each one before choosing where to settle.

## Center Grove

Center Grove is the heart of Coconut Grove and the area most people picture when they think of the neighborhood. CocoWalk, the open-air shopping and dining complex, anchors this section. Families here can walk to restaurants, ice cream shops, bookstores, and boutiques without getting in the car.

![Exterior View Of Mr C Coconut Grove A Mo 2](/images/blog/2029/11/best-neighborhoods-in-coconut-grove-for-families/exterior-view-of-mr-c-coconut-grove-a-mo-2.webp)


**Best for:** Families who prioritize walkability and want their kids to grow up in a pedestrian-friendly environment with easy access to entertainment and dining.

**Housing:** Center Grove leans heavily toward townhomes and condominiums. Townhomes here are popular entry-level properties for families with young children who want outdoor space without maintaining a large lot.

**Drawbacks:** Street noise increases near CocoWalk, especially on weekends. Families who want a quiet cul-de-sac will find better options in North or South Grove.

## North Grove

North Grove sits closer to Brickell, Key Biscayne, and Downtown Miami. This section is home to some of Coconut Grove's most exclusive waterfront communities, including Camp Biscayne, Hughes Cove, The Anchorage, The Moorings, and Entrada Estates.

**Best for:** Families with higher budgets who want waterfront living, larger lots, and proximity to Downtown for commuting.

**Housing:** Primarily single-family homes, many with direct bay access. Properties here command premium prices, with homes in the $3M-$5M range averaging around $901 per square foot.

**Schools nearby:** Ransom Everglades, one of the top-ranked private schools in Florida, sits at the edge of North Grove. The school serves grades 6-12 and is known for rigorous academics and competitive athletics.

**Drawbacks:** Limited walkability to dining and shopping compared to Center Grove. Most errands require a car.

## South Grove

South Grove borders Coral Gables and South Miami, giving residents easy access to two additional commercial districts. This area tends to feel quieter and more residential than Center Grove while still offering reasonable proximity to shops and restaurants.

**Best for:** Families who want a quieter residential setting but still want to be close to the action. The border with Coral Gables means access to the Miracle Mile shopping district and Coral Gables dining options.

**Housing:** A mix of single-family homes and newer townhome developments. Lot sizes are generally larger than Center Grove, and many properties have mature tropical landscaping that provides privacy and shade.

**Schools nearby:** Carrollton School of the Sacred Heart (an all-girls college prep school) and Immaculata-La Salle High School are both accessible from South Grove.

## West Grove (Little Bahamas)

West Grove, historically known as Little Bahamas, is the original settlement of Bahamian immigrants who helped found Coconut Grove. You can still find colorful historic clapboard "Conch houses" in this section. The neighborhood has undergone significant development in recent years, with new townhomes and condos rising alongside historic structures.

![Front View Of Miami City Hall With Palm  4](/images/blog/2029/11/best-neighborhoods-in-coconut-grove-for-families/front-view-of-miami-city-hall-with-palm--4.webp)


**Best for:** Families who value cultural history and community character, and who are looking for more affordable entry points into Coconut Grove.

**Housing:** The most diverse housing stock in the Grove. Options range from historic Conch houses to modern townhome developments. Prices tend to be lower than North or Center Grove, making this area attractive for families entering the Coconut Grove market for the first time.

**Drawbacks:** West Grove is still in transition. Some blocks are well-developed while others lag behind in terms of infrastructure and walkability.

## Schools Across Coconut Grove

Education is often the deciding factor for families, and the Grove delivers strong options at every level.

**Public schools:**
- Coconut Grove Elementary (grades K-5)
- George Washington Carver Middle School, a public magnet school with partnerships with French, German, Italian, and Spanish education ministries

**Private schools:**
- Ransom Everglades (grades 6-12), a nationally elite college prep school
- Carrollton School of the Sacred Heart (all-girls, PreK-12)
- St. Stephen's Episcopal Day School (PreK-8)
- Saint Hugh Catholic School (PreK-8)

## Parks and Family Activities

Coconut Grove's parks are a major draw for families with young children. The neighborhood offers several green spaces with playgrounds, picnic areas, weekend soccer fields, and dog parks. Peacock Park and David T. Kennedy Park both sit on the waterfront and provide open space for kids to run.

The annual Coconut Grove Arts Festival, held each Presidents' Day weekend, is a family tradition that draws over 100,000 visitors. The Barnacle Historic State Park offers nature programs and is one of the few places in Miami where kids can explore a preserved hardwood hammock.

## Getting Around

Coconut Grove is one of Miami's most walkable neighborhoods, especially in and around Center Grove. The Metrorail Coconut Grove station connects residents to Downtown Miami, Brickell, and the airport without dealing with traffic. The station is particularly useful for families with teenagers who need independent transportation.

By car, the Grove sits about 20-30 minutes from Miami International Airport and the beaches, depending on traffic.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What is the safest neighborhood in Coconut Grove for families?**
North Grove and South Grove are the quietest residential sections. Coconut Grove overall has a crime rate lower than 58% of Florida cities, with 24/7 police patrols throughout the neighborhood.

**How much does it cost to live in Coconut Grove?**
Housing costs vary significantly by sub-neighborhood. Center Grove townhomes offer more affordable entry points, while North Grove waterfront homes in the $3M-$5M range average about $901 per square foot.

**Are there good public schools in Coconut Grove?**
Yes. Coconut Grove Elementary is well-regarded for grades K-5, and George Washington Carver Middle is a public magnet school with international education partnerships. Many families also choose from the neighborhood's strong private school options.
