Thinking about moving to Homestead? You are looking at one of Miami-Dade County's most affordable and distinctive communities. Situated at the southern end of the county, Homestead sits at the gateway to both Everglades National Park and Biscayne National Park, giving residents access to nature that no other Miami-Dade city can match.

This guide covers the neighborhoods, local attractions, and practical details you need before making the move.

## What Makes Homestead Different

Homestead is not a typical South Florida suburb. The city grew out of an agricultural heritage that still defines the area today. The surrounding Redland district produces tropical fruits, orchids, and nursery plants that ship across the country, and that rural character gives the community a pace of life that feels far removed from the Miami metro even though Downtown Miami is only about 35 miles north on the Turnpike.

![American Alligator Resting Near Water In 1](/images/blog/2029/08/exploring-homestead-tips-for-newcomers/american-alligator-resting-near-water-in-1.webp)


The cost of living here runs lower than most of Miami-Dade, especially housing. Families who want more space for their money often find that Homestead delivers larger lots and newer construction at prices well below Kendall, Cutler Bay, or Palmetto Bay.

## Neighborhoods and Areas to Know

### Downtown Homestead

The historic downtown along Krome Avenue has been revitalized with local restaurants, shops, and murals. It is walkable and has a small-town main street feel that draws both residents and visitors on weekends.

### The Redland

Named for its distinctive red soil, the Redland stretches west and south of the city center. This unincorporated agricultural area is known for large lots, farm stands, and a quiet, rural atmosphere. Robert Is Here, a fruit stand that has operated for over 50 years near the Everglades entrance, is a local landmark. Knaus Berry Farm's cinnamon rolls have been a seasonal tradition since the 1950s.

### Keys Gate and Leisure City

These planned communities south and east of the city center offer newer townhomes and single-family homes. Keys Gate in particular has a golf course community feel with its own commercial areas.

### Homestead Air Reserve Base Area

The eastern side of Homestead sits near the former Homestead Air Force Base, now the Homestead Air Reserve Base. The Homestead-Miami Speedway, which hosts NASCAR and other racing events, is located on the former base property.

## Local Attractions Worth Visiting

Homestead has more to offer than most newcomers expect:

![Detailed Close Up Of An American Crocodi 2](/images/blog/2029/08/exploring-homestead-tips-for-newcomers/detailed-close-up-of-an-american-crocodi-2.webp)


- **Fruit and Spice Park**: A 37-acre county park with over 500 varieties of fruits, vegetables, spices, herbs, and nuts from around the world. It is the only tropical botanical garden of its kind in the United States.
- **Coral Castle**: A collection of massive coral rock sculptures built single-handedly by Latvian immigrant Edward Leedskalnin between 1923 and 1951. It is listed on the National Register of Historic Places.
- **Schnebly Redland's Winery**: A winery producing tropical fruit wines from lychee, mango, guava, and passion fruit grown in the Redland.
- **Everglades National Park**: The main Ernest Coe Visitor Center entrance is just minutes from Homestead, making it the closest Miami-Dade city to the park.
- **Biscayne National Park**: The Dante Fascell Visitor Center at Convoy Point is about 9 miles east, offering access to snorkeling, kayaking, and island camping.

## Practical Tips for New Residents

### Getting Around

Homestead is a car-dependent area. The Florida Turnpike (Homestead Extension) and US-1 are the main routes north toward Miami. The South Dade TransitWay (bus rapid transit) runs from Dadeland South Metrorail station to the Florida City terminal, giving residents a public transit option for commuting north.

Plan on a 45-to-60-minute commute to Downtown Miami during peak hours. Many Homestead residents work locally or in the southern Miami-Dade corridor to avoid the drive.

### Schools

Miami-Dade County Public Schools serves the area. Homestead Senior High and South Dade Senior High are the main public high schools. Several charter schools also operate in the community.

### Shopping and Dining

The Homestead Town Center on US-1 is the main retail hub with big-box stores and chain restaurants. For groceries, you will find Publix, Walmart, ALDI, and several Latin American markets. The downtown Krome Avenue corridor is where you will find locally owned restaurants and cafes.

### Hurricane Preparedness

Homestead was famously devastated by Hurricane Andrew in 1992 and rebuilt from the ground up. As a result, most construction in the area meets strict post-Andrew building codes. Still, hurricane season (June through November) requires preparation: stock supplies, know your evacuation zone, and have a plan.

## Moving to Homestead: What to Know

### August Moving Considerations

August in South Florida means heat indexes above 100 degrees and daily afternoon thunderstorms. If you are scheduling a move, book early morning start times. Professional movers working in Homestead are used to these conditions and plan around the afternoon rain.

Weekday moves typically offer better availability and lower rates than weekends, especially during the busy summer season.

### HOA and Community Rules

Many Homestead neighborhoods, particularly the newer developments, have homeowners associations with specific move-in requirements. Check with your HOA about moving truck restrictions, approved hours, and any deposits required before move-in day.

## FAQ

**What is the cost of living in Homestead compared to Miami?**
Homestead generally runs 15 to 25 percent lower than the Miami-Dade average, with the biggest savings in housing costs. Rent and home prices are noticeably lower than central Miami-Dade communities like Kendall or Coral Gables.

![Tranquil View Of The Everglades Wetlands 3](/images/blog/2029/08/exploring-homestead-tips-for-newcomers/tranquil-view-of-the-everglades-wetlands-3.webp)


**How far is Homestead from Miami Beach?**
About 45 miles, or roughly one hour by car depending on traffic. The fastest route is the Florida Turnpike north to I-95 or I-395.

**Is Homestead safe?**
Like any community, safety varies by neighborhood. The newer planned developments like Keys Gate tend to have lower crime rates. Check current statistics and visit neighborhoods at different times of day before deciding.

**What is the Redland area?**
The Redland is an unincorporated agricultural district surrounding Homestead, known for tropical fruit farms, nurseries, orchid growers, and a rural lifestyle. It is named after the distinctive red soil found in the area.
