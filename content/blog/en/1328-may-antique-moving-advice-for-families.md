That Victorian dresser your grandmother passed down or the hand-carved dining table your family brought from Cuba cannot be replaced. When you are moving this spring, these pieces deserve more care than a standard moving crew typically provides. Here is what Miami families should know about protecting antiques during a May relocation.

## Why Antiques Need Special Attention

Regular furniture can absorb a bump or two. Antiques cannot. Older wood is dryer and more brittle, making it prone to cracking under stress. Veneer lifts easily. Dovetail joints loosen if a piece is tilted the wrong way. Original hardware bends or snaps if not removed first.

![A Charming Vintage Room Featuring Elegan 3](/images/blog/2029/05/may-antique-moving-advice-for-families/a-charming-vintage-room-featuring-elegan-3.webp)


Beyond physical fragility, antiques carry financial and emotional value that makes damage especially painful. A professional appraisal before the move establishes value for insurance purposes and gives movers a clear understanding of what they are handling.

## The Miami Spring Factor

May in South Florida brings heat, humidity, and afternoon storms. Each of these conditions affects antique furniture:

- **Humidity causes wood to swell.** Drawers that fit perfectly in an air-conditioned home may stick or warp after 20 minutes in a hot truck. [**Antique Moving**](/miami-antique-moving) specialists use climate-controlled vehicles to prevent this.
- **Heat softens old finishes.** Shellac and lacquer finishes common on antiques become tacky in temperatures above 85 degrees. Movers place felt or cotton blankets (never plastic) directly against these surfaces.
- **Rain creates water damage risks.** Even brief exposure to rain can stain unfinished wood or warp thin panels. Morning scheduling and waterproof covers are standard practice.

## How to Prepare Your Antiques Before Moving Day

### Document Everything

![A Vintage Lamp And Rotary Telephone On A 1](/images/blog/2029/05/may-antique-moving-advice-for-families/a-vintage-lamp-and-rotary-telephone-on-a-1.webp)


Photograph each piece from multiple angles, including close-ups of existing damage, maker's marks, and hardware. This creates a clear record for insurance claims if anything goes wrong.

### Get Appraisals for Valuable Pieces

If you own pieces worth more than a few thousand dollars, get a written appraisal from a certified appraiser. South Florida has several specialists who focus on estate furniture and decorative arts.

### Remove All Hardware and Drawers

Take out drawer pulls, knobs, keys, and removable shelves. Bag and label each set of hardware by piece. This prevents scratching during transport and makes reassembly straightforward.

### Skip the Wax and Oil

Do not polish or oil antiques before a move. Slippery surfaces make gripping difficult, and oil residue can leave imprints from packing materials on the wood.

### Use the Right Wrapping Materials

Antiques need breathable protection. Use cotton moving blankets or acid-free tissue paper as the first layer. Never wrap bare wood in bubble wrap or plastic shrink wrap, which traps moisture and can damage the finish.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does antique moving cost in Miami?

Antique moving typically costs more than standard furniture moving because of the extra time, materials, and care involved. Expect a premium of 20% to 40% over standard rates. A [**free quote**](/quote) gives you exact pricing based on your collection.

### Should I insure my antiques separately for the move?

Yes. Standard moving insurance covers items by weight, not by value. A 50-pound antique chair worth $5,000 would only be covered for about $30 under basic coverage. Ask about full-value protection when you book.

### Can antiques go into storage during a move?

They can, but only in climate-controlled storage. Heat and humidity in a standard Miami storage unit will damage wood finishes and warp frames within weeks.
