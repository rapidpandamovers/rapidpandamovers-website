You just finished the move. The truck is gone, the boxes are stacked, and you finally have the keys. Before you start hanging pictures and arranging furniture, focus on these 7 tasks that will save you headaches later.

## 1. Change the Locks

You have no idea how many copies of the old keys are floating around. Previous owners, their contractors, dog walkers, and real estate agents may all have had access. Swap out the deadbolts and entry locks on day one. A locksmith can rekey the entire house in under an hour, or you can buy new lock sets and install them yourself.

## 2. Locate Your Emergency Shutoffs

Find and label three things before you do anything else:

- **Water main shutoff** (usually near the front of the house or in the garage)
- **Electrical panel** (garage, utility room, or hallway closet)
- **Gas shutoff valve** (near the meter, typically on an exterior wall)

In South Florida, knowing your water shutoff location is especially important during hurricane season. A burst pipe during a storm can cause thousands in damage if you can't cut the water fast.

## 3. Deep Clean Before You Unpack

Cleaning empty rooms is ten times easier than cleaning around furniture and boxes. Wipe down cabinets and closet shelves, scrub bathrooms, mop hard floors, and vacuum carpets. If the previous owners had pets, consider getting carpets professionally cleaned to remove allergens.

## 4. Test Every Appliance

Run the dishwasher through a full cycle. Turn on each stove burner and the oven. Test the washer and dryer. Flush every toilet. Run water in every sink and check under the cabinets for leaks. Do this within the first few days while warranty claims or seller negotiations are still on the table.

## 5. Update Your Address Everywhere

Beyond USPS mail forwarding, you need to update your address with:

- Driver's license and vehicle registration (Florida gives you 30 days)
- Bank and credit card companies
- Insurance providers (auto, health, homeowners)
- Employer and payroll
- Subscriptions and online shopping accounts
- Your kids' school records
- Voter registration

Missing even one of these can cause problems months later when an important document goes to your old address.

## 6. Document the Condition of Everything

Walk through every room with your phone and take photos and videos. Capture the condition of walls, floors, appliances, countertops, and fixtures. This documentation protects you if you need to file an insurance claim later, and it gives you a baseline for tracking any changes over time.

## 7. Meet at Least One Neighbor

Knock on a door or wave when you see someone outside. Introduce yourself. Ask about trash pickup days, neighborhood quirks, or where to find the best coffee nearby. Having one friendly face on your street makes a big difference, especially when you need to borrow a tool or keep an eye on a package.

## FAQ

### What should I do on the very first day in a new home?
Change the locks, locate your emergency shutoffs, and do a walkthrough to document the home's condition. These three tasks address safety and protection right away.

### How long does it take to fully settle into a new home?
Most families feel settled within 2 to 4 weeks, but the first week matters most. Handling these 7 tasks early prevents small problems from turning into bigger ones.

### Should I clean the house before or after moving in?
Before unpacking, if possible. Empty rooms are far easier to clean, and you will not want to move furniture and boxes twice.
