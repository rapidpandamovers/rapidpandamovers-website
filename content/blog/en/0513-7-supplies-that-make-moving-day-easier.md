Moving day in Miami comes with its own challenges. Between the humidity, the traffic on I-95, and the tight parking in neighborhoods like Brickell and Coral Gables, having the right supplies on hand can make all the difference. Here are seven essential items to keep with you on moving day.

## 1. Important Documents

Passports, IDs, birth certificates, and lease or closing papers should stay with you. Keep them on your person or in your personal vehicle, never in a moving box.

![A Collection Of Labeled Cardboard Boxes  3](/images/blog/2026/05/7-supplies-that-make-moving-day-easier/a-collection-of-labeled-cardboard-boxes--3.webp)


## 2. Medications and First Aid

Current prescriptions, over-the-counter essentials, and any medical devices you might need in the next 24 hours stay with you.

## 3. Phone Chargers and Power Banks

Dead phones on moving day mean missed calls from movers, utility companies, and anyone trying to help coordinate.

![A Collection Of Large Cardboard Boxes La 4](/images/blog/2026/05/7-supplies-that-make-moving-day-easier/a-collection-of-large-cardboard-boxes-la-4.webp)


## 4. Basic Toolkit

A screwdriver set, pliers, box cutter, and hammer cover most last-minute needs at both locations. You'll use these more than you expect.

## 5. Cleaning Supplies

Paper towels, all-purpose cleaner, and trash bags handle quick clean-ups during the transition at both old and new locations.

## 6. First-Night Box

Toiletries, towels, bedding, change of clothes, and anything you'll need to sleep comfortably night one without unpacking everything.

![Labeled Cardboard Boxes For Summer Cloth 1](/images/blog/2026/05/7-supplies-that-make-moving-day-easier/labeled-cardboard-boxes-for-summer-cloth-1.webp)


## 7. Snacks and Water

Moving in Miami heat means staying hydrated. Pack a cooler with bottled water, sports drinks, and easy snacks like granola bars. You can grab extra supplies at Publix or Sedano's on your way. Your movers will appreciate the gesture if you offer them cold drinks too.
