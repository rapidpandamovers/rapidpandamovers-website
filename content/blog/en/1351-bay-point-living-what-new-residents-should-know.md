Bay Point is not the kind of Miami neighborhood you stumble into. The entrance is gated, the streets are private, and the community is designed to feel removed from the city even though downtown is less than 20 minutes away. If you are moving here, you are joining one of Miami's most exclusive residential enclaves.

Here is what you should know before you arrive.

## Where Is Bay Point?

Bay Point sits on the Upper East Side of Miami, along Biscayne Boulevard between NE 41st Street and NE 50th Street. The community is bordered by Biscayne Bay to the east, which means many homes have direct waterfront access with private docks.

![A Picturesque View Of Miami Skyline With 4](/images/blog/2029/06/bay-point-living-what-new-residents-should-know/a-picturesque-view-of-miami-skyline-with-4.webp)


The neighborhood is part of the broader Upper East Side / MiMo Historic District area, but it operates as its own gated, guard-controlled community. You will not find Bay Point on most casual walking tours of the neighborhood. That is by design.

## What Makes Bay Point Different

### Gated Security

Bay Point is guarded 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. All streets within the community are privately owned by residents, and access is restricted to homeowners, their families, and pre-approved guests. This level of security is one of the primary reasons people choose Bay Point over other waterfront neighborhoods in Miami.

### The Homes

There are roughly 250 homes in Bay Point, mostly single-family residences built between the 1940s and 1960s. Many have been renovated or rebuilt in recent years, and the range of architectural styles runs from original mid-century to modern custom construction.

Home sizes range from about 2,400 square feet to over 11,000 square feet. Waterfront properties come with private docks and direct ocean access through Biscayne Bay, making Bay Point popular with boaters.

Listing prices typically range from around $2 million to $18 million or more, depending on the lot size, waterfront footage, and condition of the home.

### Location

Bay Point's position on the Upper East Side puts it within easy reach of most of Miami:

- **Downtown Miami**: About 15 minutes south on Biscayne Boulevard
- **Miami International Airport**: Around 9 minutes west
- **South Beach**: About 20 minutes east
- **Design District**: 5 minutes south along Biscayne Boulevard
- **Aventura Mall**: About 20 minutes north

## Dining Near Bay Point

Bay Point itself does not have restaurants within the gates, but the surrounding Upper East Side and MiMo District have a growing food scene:

- **Blue Collar** on NE 79th Street serves scratch-made comfort food with a rotating menu of braised dishes, ribs, and fresh vegetables. It is a neighborhood favorite.
- **Pinch Kitchen** on Biscayne Boulevard offers a casual atmosphere with a surprisingly wide menu, from brunch to dinner.
- **Mandolin Aegean Bistro** in the nearby Design District serves Greek and Turkish dishes in a converted cottage with a garden patio. It is about a 5-minute drive.
- **Lagniappe** in the MiMo District is known for its backyard setting with live jazz, charcuterie boards, and a wine-and-beer bar under string lights.

The Design District and Wynwood, both within 10 minutes, add dozens more dining options.

## Schools Near Bay Point

Bay Point families have several school options nearby:

- **Morningside K-8 Academy** is about 6 minutes away, serving Pre-K through 8th grade
- **Shadowlawn Elementary School** is a 7-minute drive, covering Pre-K through 5th grade
- **Eneida M. Hartner Elementary School** is roughly 9 minutes west

For private education, **Morningside Montessori School** offers daycare and preschool programs about 5 minutes from Bay Point. Several private schools in Coconut Grove, Coral Gables, and Miami Beach are also within a reasonable commute.

While Bay Point does not have public parks inside the gates, many homes have their own pools, docks, and waterfront yards. For public green space, the surrounding area offers:

## Moving to Bay Point in Summer

A summer move to Bay Point comes with a few specific considerations:

### Gate and Security Coordination

Your moving company needs to be pre-approved for entry. Contact the guardhouse or your HOA representative ahead of time to register the moving truck, crew names, and expected arrival window. If your movers show up without clearance, they will not be let through the gate.

### Street Access

Bay Point's streets are residential and relatively narrow. Large moving trucks can fit, but parking and maneuvering require coordination. Let your movers know the specific address and any turns or tight spots they should be aware of.

### Weather

Miami's summer afternoon storms hit between 2 and 5 PM almost daily. Start your move early in the morning. If you are moving waterfront furniture or anything humidity-sensitive, the morning hours are far better than the afternoon.

### HOA Rules

Bay Point has a homeowners' association with rules about noise, parking, and exterior modifications. Confirm any move-in requirements or quiet hours before scheduling your move. Some communities restrict moving activity to weekday hours only.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much do homes cost in Bay Point?

Listing prices typically range from around $2 million to over $18 million. Waterfront lots with private docks command the highest prices. Work with a local realtor who specializes in the Upper East Side for the most accurate current pricing.

### Is Bay Point safe?

Bay Point is one of the safest neighborhoods in Miami. The gated entrance, 24/7 security guards, and private streets create a controlled environment. Residents own the streets, so all traffic is either homeowners or approved visitors.

### Can you walk to restaurants and shops from Bay Point?

The community is residential with no commercial establishments inside the gates. However, Biscayne Boulevard and the MiMo District are a short drive or bike ride away, with restaurants, cafes, and shops along the corridor.
