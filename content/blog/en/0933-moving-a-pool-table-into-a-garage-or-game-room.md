# Moving a Pool Table Into a Garage or Game Room

Garage game rooms are a South Florida tradition. With mild winters and plenty of outdoor entertaining, converting your garage or a spare room into a billiards space makes a lot of sense. But getting a pool table into that space involves more than just rolling it through the door. The room itself needs to be right for the table, and the table needs to be right for the room.

## Is Your Garage or Game Room Ready?

Before we move a single slate piece, the destination room needs to meet a few requirements:

![A Man Setting Up Billiard Balls On A Poo 2](/images/blog/2028/04/moving-a-pool-table-into-a-garage-or-game-room/a-man-setting-up-billiard-balls-on-a-poo-2.webp)


### Space Requirements

A pool table needs more room than you might expect. The table itself is only part of the equation. Players need room to pull back a cue on all four sides. Here's a quick guide using a standard 58-inch cue:

- **7-foot table**: Minimum room size of 13 x 16 feet
- **8-foot table**: Minimum room size of 13.5 x 17 feet
- **9-foot table**: Minimum room size of 14 x 18 feet

Many Miami garages are 20 x 20 feet or larger, which gives you plenty of room for an 8-foot table with space to spare. But measure before you commit, especially if the garage has storage shelving, a workbench, or HVAC equipment eating into the usable area.

### Flooring

Garages typically have bare concrete, which is actually a good surface for a pool table. Concrete is flat, stable, and strong enough to support the weight. If you're putting the table on tile or laminate in a game room, check that the flooring is solid and level. Floating floors can flex under the weight of a slate table and cause leveling problems.

If you're adding carpet or rubber flooring to the garage, do it before the table goes in. The table legs will compress carpet over time, so we'll need to re-level after a few weeks once the carpet settles.

### Climate Control

This is the big one for Miami garages. A non-air-conditioned garage in South Florida can hit 90 degrees with 85% humidity in the summer. That environment will destroy pool table felt, warp MDF surfaces, and cause the wooden frame to swell. If you're setting up a garage game room, invest in climate control first:

- A mini-split AC unit is the most popular option for garage conversions
- Insulate the garage door or replace it with an insulated model
- Seal any gaps around the door frame, windows, and walls
- A dehumidifier can supplement AC during the wettest months

## Getting the Table Through the Door

Standard garage doors are 7 to 8 feet tall, which is plenty of clearance. But the individual entry door from the house to the garage (or from outside) might be a standard 32-inch or 36-inch interior door. The good news: when a pool table is fully disassembled, every component fits through a standard doorway. Slate pieces are about 26 to 30 inches wide, and frame sections are narrower than that.

For dedicated game rooms inside the house, we check every doorway and hallway between the front door and the room. Older Miami homes sometimes have 28-inch interior doors, which require removing the door and potentially the frame to get the wider components through.

## Setting Up for the Best Playing Experience

Once the table is assembled and leveled, a few setup tips will make your new game room play better:

![Close Up Of A Billiard Table With Chalk  3](/images/blog/2028/04/moving-a-pool-table-into-a-garage-or-game-room/close-up-of-a-billiard-table-with-chalk--3.webp)


- **Lighting**: Hang a proper billiard light centered over the table, 32 to 36 inches above the playing surface. Overhead recessed lights create shadows. A dedicated billiard light eliminates them.
- **Cue rack**: Wall-mount a cue rack near the table so cues aren't leaning against walls where they can warp or fall.
- **Scoring and seating**: Bar stools or pub tables along the wall give spectators a place to sit without crowding the players.
- **Table cover**: Always cover the table when not in use. Even in a climate-controlled space, dust, pet hair, and chalk residue accumulate.
