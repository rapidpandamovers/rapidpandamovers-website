Moving into a Miami high-rise involves more than loading a truck and heading to your new address. Every condo and apartment building has specific requirements that can delay or complicate your move if you're not prepared. Here's what you need to know before scheduling your move-in date.

## The COI Requirement

Most Miami high-rises require a Certificate of Insurance (COI) from your moving company before they allow any move to proceed. This document proves the movers carry liability and workers compensation coverage.

![Loc View Of Miami Skyline From Coral Gables  1](/images/blog/2028/06/miami-high-rise-moving-building-requirements/loc-view-of-miami-skyline-from-coral-gables--1.webp)


What buildings typically require:
- General liability of $1,000,000 minimum
- Workers compensation coverage
- The building listed as certificate holder
- Submission 3-7 days before your move

## Elevator Reservations

Freight elevator access determines everything about your move timeline. Here's how reservations work in different Miami areas:

### Brickell High-Rises

Buildings like Icon Brickell, 500 Brickell, and Brickell House limit moves to specific hours, typically 9 AM to 5 PM on weekdays. Weekend moves require additional fees and earlier booking. Expect to reserve 2-3 weeks in advance during busy periods.

The freight elevator holds priority. If another resident scheduled the same day, you may share time slots or need to reschedule. Confirm your exact window when booking.

### Edgewater and Downtown

Newer buildings like Paraiso, Aria on the Bay, and 1000 Museum have dedicated moving windows. Most allow 4-8 hour blocks. These buildings often require deposits ($300-500) refundable after a successful move with no damage.

### Aventura

Luxury towers including Turnberry Isle, Williams Island, and Porto Vita have the strictest requirements. Expect detailed move-in packages with rules about padding elevator walls, covering floors, and designated parking for trucks. Some buildings only allow moves Monday through Friday.

### Coconut Grove

Buildings here vary widely. Older structures like Grove Isle may have smaller elevators requiring furniture disassembly. Newer developments like Park Grove follow Brickell-style protocols.

## Loading Dock and Parking

Where your moving truck parks matters more than you might think.

**Loading dock access**: Most high-rises have loading docks in parking garages. Truck height limits (usually 12-14 feet) mean smaller moving trucks work better than large semis. Professional movers know which vehicles fit which buildings.

**Street parking permits**: Some buildings lack loading docks entirely. You may need to reserve metered spots or request a temporary parking permit from the city. In Brickell and Downtown, this requires advance coordination.

**Time limits**: Many buildings restrict truck presence to your reserved window. If your move runs long, you may face daily fees or be asked to move the truck mid-move.

## Common Building Rules

### Prohibited Items

Most Miami high-rises ban certain items from freight elevators and common areas:
- Open containers of liquids
- Items without protective wrapping
- Loose small items not in boxes
- Unwrapped mattresses

### Floor and Wall Protection

Many luxury buildings require movers to lay protective material on floors and elevator walls before any items enter the building. Some provide this; others expect your movers to bring their own. Clarify expectations with building management.

### Move-Out Inspections

If you're leaving a building, expect a unit inspection before or during your move. Damage found during move-out may delay your deposit return. Some buildings require a walkthrough with a building representative present.

## What to Expect on Move-In Day

**Morning of move:** Arrive early to check in with building management, confirm elevator availability, and handle any last-minute paperwork. Have your COI confirmation ready.

**During the move:** Building staff may inspect items entering the building. Movers will need to pad the elevator before loading. Expect the process to take longer than a standard move due to elevator wait times and building protocols.

**After unloading:** Return building materials (elevator pads, floor protection) and complete any required inspections before your deposit refund is processed.
