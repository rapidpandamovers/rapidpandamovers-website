Some items look manageable until you actually try to move them. The weight, fragility, or specialized assembly of certain belongings means that one wrong move can result in thousands of dollars in damage. These five items send DIY movers to the phone to call professionals more than almost anything else.

## 1. Grand Pianos

A grand piano weighs between 500 and 1,200 pounds depending on size. Beyond the weight, the internal components, including over 200 strings under roughly 20 tons of combined tension, are sensitive to jolts and tilting. The legs must be removed before transport, and the body needs to be wrapped in specialized padding and secured to a piano board for safe carrying.

![Two Movers In Uniform Carrying Boxes In  3](/images/blog/2029/12/5-difficult-belongings-that-need-pro-movers/two-movers-in-uniform-carrying-boxes-in--3.webp)


Professional piano movers use skid boards, heavy-duty straps, and custom dollies built for the instrument's shape. They also know how to angle a grand piano through doorways and down stairs without scraping walls or stressing the frame. After a move, a piano needs retuning regardless, but improper handling can cause soundboard cracks that are far more expensive than a tuner's visit.

## 2. Wine Collections

Wine reacts to temperature, vibration, and light. A single bottle of aged Bordeaux or Napa Cabernet can be worth hundreds or thousands of dollars, and a full collection multiplies that risk quickly. Bottles stored on their sides need to stay in that orientation during transport to keep corks moist. Excessive shaking can disturb sediment and affect flavor.

Professional movers use climate-controlled vehicles when transporting wine. They pack bottles in custom inserts that prevent contact between glass surfaces and maintain consistent temperature. In South Florida, where outdoor temperatures regularly exceed 90 degrees, even a short stretch in a hot truck can ruin a collection. Movers familiar with wine transport plan routes to minimize heat exposure and never leave bottles sitting in a vehicle.

## 3. Hot Tubs and Spas

A standard hot tub weighs 500 to 800 pounds empty. Fill it with water and that number jumps past 3,000 pounds. Before moving, the tub must be fully drained, all electrical and plumbing connections disconnected, and the shell cleaned and dried to prevent mold during transport.

The challenge goes beyond weight. Hot tubs rarely fit through standard doorways or gates. Many installations require a crane, forklift, or specialized dolly to get the tub from the backyard to the truck. Professional movers assess access routes before moving day, measure every opening, and bring the right equipment. Attempting this without proper planning often leads to damaged fencing, scratched shells, or back injuries.

## 4. Large Aquariums

Fish tanks over 50 gallons present a unique set of problems. Glass aquariums are fragile and cannot handle uneven pressure during transport; even a small twist can crack a seam. The fish need separate transport in insulated bags or containers with battery-powered air pumps. Live coral and plants require their own climate-stable containers.

![Two Movers Smiling From Their Moving Com 2](/images/blog/2029/12/5-difficult-belongings-that-need-pro-movers/two-movers-smiling-from-their-moving-com-2.webp)


The tank itself must be completely emptied, including gravel, rocks, and decorations. Water weight alone makes full transport impossible: a 100-gallon tank holds over 800 pounds of water. Professional movers experienced with aquariums coordinate the breakdown, transport, and reassembly to minimize the time fish spend outside their habitat. Rushing this process can kill livestock that took years to cultivate.

## 5. Pool Tables

A regulation slate pool table weighs between 700 and 1,000 pounds. The playing surface is made from precision-leveled slate, typically in three pieces, that must be disassembled before moving. Attempting to move a pool table without removing the slate risks cracking the stone, warping the frame, or tearing the felt.

Reassembly requires leveling the slate with precision shims and re-stretching or replacing the felt. Professional pool table movers carry the specialty tools for this work and understand the calibration involved. A pool table that is not properly leveled after a move will not play correctly, and the balls will roll toward low spots on the surface.

## When DIY Does Not Make Sense

The common thread with all five items is that the cost of professional moving is a fraction of the replacement cost if something goes wrong. A cracked piano soundboard, a shattered aquarium, or a warped pool table slate costs far more than hiring specialists who handle these items regularly.

Before your next move, identify which belongings fall into the specialty category and plan accordingly.

## FAQ

**How much does it cost to move a grand piano?**
Professional piano moving typically runs between $200 and $600 for local moves, depending on piano size, stairs, and distance. Long-distance moves can cost $1,000 or more. Compare that to $5,000 to $30,000 or more to replace the instrument.

![White Pickup Truck And Bulldozer Parked  1](/images/blog/2029/12/5-difficult-belongings-that-need-pro-movers/white-pickup-truck-and-bulldozer-parked--1.webp)


**Can I drain a hot tub myself before movers arrive?**
Yes, and most movers prefer it. Draining takes 1-2 hours with a submersible pump. Disconnect electrical at the breaker and cap plumbing lines. The moving crew handles the physical relocation.

**How long can fish survive during a move?**
Most freshwater fish tolerate 24-48 hours in properly oxygenated bags. Saltwater fish and coral are more sensitive, typically needing temperature-controlled transport and shorter transit times.
