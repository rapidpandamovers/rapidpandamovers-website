# What to Do With Appliances When Downsizing

Downsizing is one of the most common moves we handle in Miami. Empty nesters trading a four-bedroom house in Pinecrest for a two-bedroom condo in Coral Gables. Retirees moving from a Kendall home to a smaller place on Key Biscayne. Young professionals swapping a rental house for a more manageable apartment in Edgewater. In every case, the appliances become a puzzle: your full-size, side-by-side fridge won't fit in a galley kitchen, and your oversized washer-dryer set has nowhere to go in a closet-sized laundry area.

## Assess What Fits in the New Space

Before you decide what to do with any appliance, get exact measurements of the new home's kitchen, laundry, and utility spaces. Measure the width, depth, and height of every spot where an appliance will go, including the space above (for over-the-range microwaves) and behind (for venting and connections). Also measure doorways and hallways leading to those spots.

Many Miami condos, particularly older buildings in areas like Mid-Beach and Aventura, have compact kitchens designed for apartment-size appliances. A standard 36-inch wide refrigerator won't fit in a 33-inch opening. A full-depth range may stick out past the counter line if the new kitchen was designed for counter-depth models.

## Your Options for Appliances That Don't Fit

### Sell Them

![Stylish Commercial Washing Machines In A 1](/images/blog/2028/03/what-to-do-with-appliances-when-downsizing/stylish-commercial-washing-machines-in-a-1.webp)


Working appliances in good condition sell quickly in Miami. List them on Facebook Marketplace, OfferUp, or Craigslist with clear photos, the model number, and the age. Price them at about 30-50% of the original purchase price if they're under five years old. Stainless steel French-door refrigerators and front-load washer sets are especially popular with buyers renovating older homes throughout Hialeah and Miami Gardens.

### Donate Them

Habitat for Humanity ReStore has several locations in Miami-Dade County and accepts working appliances. They'll even schedule a free pickup for large items. The Salvation Army and local churches also accept donations. You'll get a tax-deduction receipt for the fair market value.

### Leave Them for the Buyer

If you're selling your home, leaving newer appliances can actually strengthen your sale. Buyers appreciate not having to purchase a full kitchen of appliances when they move in. Talk with your real estate agent about whether including appliances adds value to your listing. In Miami's competitive market, it can make a difference.

### Store Them Temporarily

If you're unsure about your new space or you're between homes, climate-controlled storage keeps appliances safe. This is especially relevant in South Florida, where a non-climate-controlled unit exposes your appliances to humidity levels that promote rust and mold growth. Clean and dry every appliance thoroughly before storage. Leave refrigerator and washer doors propped open to prevent musty odors.

### Recycle or Dispose

For appliances that are too old to sell or donate, Miami-Dade County offers bulk waste pickup. Schedule a pickup through the county's waste management system. Refrigerators and freezers require special handling because of refrigerant chemicals, so don't just set them on the curb. Some scrap metal recyclers will pick up old washers, dryers, and ovens for free since the metal has resale value.

## Choosing Replacement Appliances for a Smaller Space

When you're buying new appliances for a downsized home, keep these sizing guidelines in mind:

- **Refrigerator**: Counter-depth models (24-25 inches deep) fit flush with cabinets and work well in compact kitchens. Consider a top-freezer model for maximum storage in a small footprint.
- **Washer and dryer**: Stackable units or ventless combo washer-dryers are standard for Miami condos. Make sure you know whether your new unit has hookups for vented or ventless dryers.
- **Dishwasher**: 18-inch compact dishwashers are available for small kitchens and handle 2-3 place settings fewer than standard 24-inch models.
- **Range**: Apartment-size 20- or 24-inch ranges fit narrow kitchen spaces. Check whether the new place has gas or electric connections.
