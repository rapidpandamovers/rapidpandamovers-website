Planning a Miami move? Before the truck arrives, you need to know what professional movers cannot legally or safely transport. Getting caught with prohibited items on moving day means delays, extra costs, and scrambling for last-minute solutions. Here are seven categories of belongings you will need to handle yourself.

## 1. Hazardous Materials

Gasoline, propane tanks, paint thinner, aerosols, and household chemicals are fire hazards and illegal to transport on moving trucks. This includes pool chemicals common in South Florida homes, fertilizers from your garden, and cleaning solvents.

![Semi Truck Parked At A Loading Dock With 3](/images/blog/2027/06/7-possessions-movers-wont-transport/semi-truck-parked-at-a-loading-dock-with-3.webp)


**What to do instead:** Use up what you can before moving day. Dispose of the rest at Miami-Dade County Household Hazardous Waste Collection events held monthly at various locations including the North Dade Landfill and South Dade Government Center.

## 2. Explosives and Ammunition

Fireworks, ammunition, and anything explosive must be disposed of or transported personally with proper safety measures. Florida's hunting community means many Miami-area households have ammunition that needs special handling during a move.

**What to do instead:** Transport ammunition in your personal vehicle in a locked container, kept separate from firearms. Dispose of old fireworks through local law enforcement drop-off programs.

## 3. Perishable Foods

Refrigerated and frozen items will not survive most moves, especially during Miami's hot months when truck temperatures can exceed 100 degrees. Consume, donate, or dispose of perishables before moving day.

**What to do instead:** Plan a "freezer clean-out" week before your move. Donate unopened non-perishables to Feeding South Florida or local food banks. For local moves, transport a cooler with essentials yourself.

## 4. Plants (for Long-Distance Moves)

Agricultural regulations often prohibit transporting plants across state lines due to pest concerns. Florida's citrus industry is protected by strict regulations, so moving plants into or out of the state requires permits or may be prohibited entirely.

**What to do instead:** Gift plants to neighbors, sell through local plant groups on Facebook, or donate to community gardens in Coconut Grove or Wynwood. For local moves within Miami-Dade, transport plants in your own vehicle.

## 5. Pets and Living Creatures

Fish, dogs, cats, birds, and any living animals cannot travel on moving trucks. The heat inside a closed truck, even with ventilation, can be fatal, particularly during South Florida summers.

![Two Men Load Construction Materials Onto 1](/images/blog/2027/06/7-possessions-movers-wont-transport/two-men-load-construction-materials-onto-1.webp)


**What to do instead:** Drive pets in your personal vehicle with AC running. For fish tanks, bag fish separately and transport in a climate-controlled environment. Consider boarding pets for moving day to reduce stress.

## 6. Irreplaceable Documents

Passports, birth certificates, financial records, Social Security cards, wills, and legal documents should stay with you personally during the move. If a box goes missing, replacing these documents takes months.

**What to do instead:** Keep important documents in a fireproof folder or small safe that travels in your personal vehicle. Make digital copies stored in secure cloud storage before the move.

## 7. Jewelry and Cash

High-value items like jewelry, cash, coins, and small collectibles belong in your personal vehicle, not on a truck full of boxes. Moving insurance typically does not cover cash, and jewelry claims require detailed documentation.

**What to do instead:** Wear valuable jewelry or lock it in a personal safe during the move. Consider a safety deposit box for the transition period between homes.
