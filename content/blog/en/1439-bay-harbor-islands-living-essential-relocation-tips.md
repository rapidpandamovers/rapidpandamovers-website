Bay Harbor Islands is a town of about 5,800 residents spread across two small islands in Biscayne Bay, directly east of Bal Harbour and connected to the mainland by the Broad Causeway. Founded in 1947, the community has maintained a quiet, family-oriented atmosphere while sitting minutes from the beaches, shops, and restaurants of Bal Harbour, Surfside, and Miami Beach.

If you are relocating here, understanding the layout, logistics, and character of each island will help you settle in faster.

## Two Islands, Two Different Characters

Bay Harbor Islands is literally two islands, and each one feels different.

![A Stunning Aerial View Of Key West Showc 1](/images/blog/2029/10/bay-harbor-islands-living-essential-relocation-tips/a-stunning-aerial-view-of-key-west-showc-1.webp)


### East Island: Condos and Commerce

The 150-acre East Island contains the town's business district along Kane Concourse, most of the condo buildings, and a significant concentration of mid-century Miami Modern (MiMo) architecture. Bayside condo towers line the waterfront, offering bay views and direct water access. This is where most of the daily activity happens: restaurants, shops, salons, and professional offices.

Condo prices on East Island range from affordable one-bedrooms in older buildings to premium waterfront units in newer developments. HOA fees vary widely depending on building age and amenities.

### West Island: Single-Family Homes

The 103-acre West Island is almost entirely single-family residential. Streets are lined with mature tropical landscaping, and the pace is noticeably slower. Homes range from original 1950s ranch-style houses to fully renovated modern properties, many with private docks and direct bay access.

West Island attracts families looking for a house with a yard in a walkable, safe community -- a combination that's hard to find this close to the beach in Miami-Dade County.

## Schools and Education

Ruth K. Broad Bay Harbor K-8 Center serves the community and is one of the area's strongest public schools, recognized for its pre-college programming. The school's location within Bay Harbor Islands means many families walk or bike their children to campus.

Private school options nearby include Miami Country Day School and Lehrman Community Day School, both within a short drive. For high school, public school students are typically zoned for North Miami Beach Senior High.

## Shopping, Dining, and Daily Life

Kane Concourse, the main commercial strip on East Island, provides most daily necessities. You will find restaurants, cafes, a handful of retail shops, and service businesses along this two-block stretch. It's a walkable area for East Island residents and a short drive from West Island.

For serious shopping, Bal Harbour Shops is adjacent to the community, within walking distance for many residents. Surfside's Harding Avenue offers more casual dining and local shops. Between these three areas, most errands stay within a 10-minute radius.

Publix, the closest major grocery store, is a short drive west on the Broad Causeway or south in Surfside.

## Parks and Recreation

The town maintains several green spaces relative to its small size:
- **North Passive Park and South Passive Park**: Quiet green spaces on the bay side
- **92nd Street Park**: Playground and open lawn area
- **Officer Scott A. Winters Park**: The town's most popular park, with an interactive splash pad, shaded playgrounds, a pavilion, and an adjacent dog park

Tennis courts and tot lots are scattered across both islands. For beach access, Surfside Beach and the Bal Harbour beach area are both within walking or biking distance.

## Getting Around

Bay Harbor Islands is compact enough that most on-island errands can happen on foot or by bicycle. The town operates a shuttle service for residents. The Broad Causeway connects the islands to the mainland and provides quick access to I-95, the Palmetto Expressway, and points west.

Commute times from Bay Harbor Islands:
- **Downtown Miami / Brickell**: 25 to 40 minutes depending on traffic
- **Aventura / North Miami Beach**: 10 to 15 minutes
- **Miami International Airport**: 25 to 35 minutes
- **Miami Beach (South Beach)**: 15 to 25 minutes

Traffic on the Broad Causeway can be congested during rush hours and school pickup times. Residents learn to adjust schedules slightly or use alternative routes through Bal Harbour to the south.

## Moving Logistics Specific to Bay Harbor Islands

### Condo Moves on East Island

Most condo buildings on East Island require elevator reservations, loading dock scheduling, move-in deposits (typically $500 to $1,000, refundable), and a certificate of insurance from your moving company. Contact your building's management office at least two weeks before your move to get the complete list of requirements.

Older buildings may have smaller elevators and tighter hallways than newer construction. Let your moving company know the building name and address during the estimate so the crew comes prepared.

### Single-Family Moves on West Island

West Island's residential streets are relatively narrow, with cars parked on both sides. Truck access is generally manageable but may require coordinating with neighbors about temporary parking. Homes with waterfront access on the bay side sometimes have rear entries that are easier for moving large items.

### Parking and Access

Street parking is limited on both islands. Your moving crew will need to reserve curb space or use the driveway at the destination. The town does not typically require moving permits, but check with town hall if you are uncertain about any restrictions on your specific street.

## FAQ

**Is Bay Harbor Islands safe?**
Yes. The community is separated from the mainland by the Broad Causeway, and the town has its own police department. Crime rates are consistently low, and the small-town layout means residents generally know their neighbors.

![Picturesque View Of Santorini S Harbor W 3](/images/blog/2029/10/bay-harbor-islands-living-essential-relocation-tips/picturesque-view-of-santorini-s-harbor-w-3.webp)


**How walkable is Bay Harbor Islands?**
Very walkable within each island. Kane Concourse has most daily services, and Bal Harbour Shops is adjacent. Beach access is within walking distance. For anything on the mainland, you will need a car.

**What's the cost of living compared to nearby communities?**
Bay Harbor Islands is more affordable than Bal Harbour and comparable to Surfside. West Island single-family homes are priced at a premium for the area due to limited inventory and waterfront access, but East Island condos offer a wider price range.

**Are there any HOA restrictions I should know about?**
Condo buildings each have their own HOA rules regarding move-in procedures, renovations, pets, and rentals. Single-family homes on West Island do not have a traditional HOA, though the town has its own zoning and code enforcement.
