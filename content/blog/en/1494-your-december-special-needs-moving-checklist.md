Moving is stressful for anyone. When the person moving also relies on a wheelchair, uses oxygen equipment, needs grab bars in specific locations, or takes daily medication that requires refrigeration, the logistics multiply. December adds holiday scheduling pressure on top of everything else. This checklist breaks down the specific steps that keep a special needs move safe, organized, and on schedule.

## Why December Requires Extra Planning

Holiday schedules affect every part of the moving process. Medical offices close or reduce hours during the last two weeks of December, making it harder to transfer prescriptions, get medical records, or schedule equipment inspections. Utility companies may have limited customer service windows for setting up new accounts. And moving companies fill up faster as families try to settle before the new year.

![Close Up Of A Young Woman In A Wheelchai 4](/images/blog/2029/12/your-december-special-needs-moving-checklist/close-up-of-a-young-woman-in-a-wheelchai-4.webp)


Start your planning at least four weeks before your move date. For December moves, this means beginning in late November at the latest.

## The Checklist

### 4 Weeks Before: Medical and Equipment Prep

**Transfer medical care**
- Contact your current doctors and specialists to request records transfers
- Research and select new providers near your destination. Confirm they accept your insurance and are taking new patients
- If you receive in-home nursing or therapy, notify your provider at least 30 days in advance to arrange continuation of service at your new address

**Audit medical equipment**
- Make a complete list of all medical devices: wheelchairs, walkers, hospital beds, oxygen concentrators, CPAP machines, lift chairs, shower chairs, and any others
- Note the dimensions and weight of each item. Your moving company needs this to plan crew size and truck space
- Check whether any equipment is rented through insurance or a medical supply company. Coordinate return and re-setup at the new location

**Medications**
- Request a 60-day supply of all prescriptions from your pharmacy. December pharmacy hours can be unpredictable
- If any medication requires refrigeration, plan how you will maintain the cold chain during transport. A medical-grade cooler bag with ice packs works for short moves
- Carry all medication in your personal vehicle or on your person during the move, never on the truck

### 3 Weeks Before: Home Assessment

**Evaluate the new home for accessibility**
- Measure doorways, hallways, and bathroom spaces for wheelchair or walker clearance. Standard interior doors are 30 inches wide; most wheelchairs need 32 to 36 inches
- Check for step-free entry. If there are steps, determine whether a ramp exists or needs to be installed before move-in day
- Test all grab bar mounting locations. Grab bars need to be anchored into studs or blocking, not just drywall
- Confirm that the bathroom can accommodate any shower chair or transfer bench you use

**Coordinate modifications**
- If the new home needs grab bars, ramp installation, or other modifications, schedule the work to be completed before your moving day
- Florida allows same-day FPL electricity activation in most cases, but confirm your specific address. Going without power while medical equipment needs charging is not acceptable

### 2 Weeks Before: Moving Company Coordination

**Communicate specific needs to your movers**
- Share the list of medical equipment with dimensions and weights
- Specify which items are fragile, which need to stay upright, and which have batteries that need to be disconnected
- Ask about crew experience with [**special needs moving**](/special-needs-moving). Crews who have done this before will move more patiently and carefully around mobility devices
- Confirm that the moving company carries liability insurance that covers medical equipment

**Plan the essentials bag**
Pack a bag that stays with you at all times on moving day:
- All medications (labeled clearly with dosing instructions)
- Copies of medical records and insurance cards
- Phone chargers and a portable battery pack
- A change of clothes, personal care items
- Snacks and water
- Emergency contact list on paper, not just in your phone

### 1 Week Before: Final Preparations

**Confirm everything**
- Call your moving company to reconfirm the date, time, crew size, and any special instructions
- Verify that utilities are active at your new home
- Confirm that any home modifications (grab bars, ramps) are installed and tested
- Do a final inventory of medical equipment. Photograph each item from multiple angles as documentation

**Prepare the current home**
- Clear pathways for the moving crew. Remove rugs, shoes, and clutter from hallways and doorways
- Disconnect any medical equipment that needs it, following manufacturer instructions
- Label all medical equipment boxes clearly: "MEDICAL - LOAD LAST / UNLOAD FIRST"

### Moving Day

**Morning**
- Take all scheduled medications on your normal schedule
- Have your essentials bag packed and ready before the crew arrives
- Brief the crew lead on which items are medical equipment and where they go in the new home

**During the move**
- Stay accessible by phone in case the crew has questions about equipment placement
- If you are riding separately, arrive at the new home before the truck to direct equipment placement
- Medical equipment and mobility devices should be the first items off the truck and set up

**At the new home**
- Verify that all medical equipment works after setup. Test power connections, battery levels, and mechanical functions
- Check that grab bars and any installed modifications are secure
- Unpack medications and store them properly. Re-establish the cold chain for refrigerated items immediately

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How far in advance should I book a special needs move in December?**
At least three to four weeks. December availability tightens quickly, and special needs moves require crew members with specific experience.

**Will movers handle my medical equipment?**
Yes, if you work with a company experienced in special needs moving. Provide a detailed list of all equipment in advance, including weights and dimensions, so the crew arrives prepared.

**What if my new home is not yet accessible on moving day?**
This is why the 3-week home assessment step matters. If modifications will not be complete by your move date, either push the move or arrange temporary accommodations. Moving into an inaccessible home creates safety risks that are not worth the schedule pressure.

**Can I get financial help with a special needs move?**
Several nonprofit organizations, including The Salvation Army and Catholic Charities, offer moving assistance programs based on income. HUD also funds relocation assistance in certain circumstances. Contact these organizations early since December holiday closures affect their availability.
