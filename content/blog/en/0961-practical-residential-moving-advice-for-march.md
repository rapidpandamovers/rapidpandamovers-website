## Key Tips for Residential Moving in March

### Plan Ahead for Peak Times

![A Family Joyfully Unpacking Boxes In The 3](/images/blog/2028/01/practical-residential-moving-advice-for-march/a-family-joyfully-unpacking-boxes-in-the-3.webp)


March can be a busy time for moving companies. With spring cleaning activities, seasonal transitions, and Spring Break schedules at local schools, scheduling your residential moving early ensures you get your preferred date and time. Many families in Pinecrest and Palmetto Bay prefer weekday moves to avoid weekend traffic on US-1.

### Walk through your home with movers to identify special items

This single step can save hours of stress and potential problems on moving day. Our experienced crews have seen what happens when this step is skipped.

### Consider the Weather

South Florida's spring weather is predictable in some ways and surprising in others. March temperatures typically range from 70-82°F, with humidity levels climbing as we approach summer. Professional movers know how to protect your belongings from humidity, rain, and heat exposure during transport, using climate-appropriate materials and timing moves for the cooler morning hours.

### Prepare Your Belongings

Take time to inventory your items before the move. This is especially important for residential moving, as proper documentation helps ensure everything arrives safely at your new location.
