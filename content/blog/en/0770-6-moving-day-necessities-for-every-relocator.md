Moving day hits fast. One minute you're taping boxes, the next you're watching everything you own roll away in a truck. The worst time to realize your passport is buried in a box labeled "misc kitchen stuff" is when you're standing at your new condo's leasing office trying to get your keys.

Here are six items that stay with you on moving day, not on the truck.

## 1. Important Documents

Passports, IDs, birth certificates, lease agreements, closing papers, car titles, insurance cards. Keep these in a folder in your personal bag or your vehicle's glove box. Never pack them in a moving box.

![A Blue Volvo Truck Parked Outside A Ware 3](/images/blog/2027/07/6-moving-day-necessities-for-every-relocator/a-blue-volvo-truck-parked-outside-a-ware-3.webp)


If you're moving to a Miami high-rise like those in Brickell or Edgewater, you'll likely need your ID and lease agreement to get past the front desk on day one. Some buildings also require proof of insurance before letting movers into the elevator.

## 2. Medications and First Aid

Current prescriptions, over-the-counter pain relievers, allergy meds, and any medical devices you rely on. These stay within arm's reach for the next 24-48 hours.

Miami's heat and humidity can trigger headaches and dehydration, especially if you're helping with the move. Pack extra water, electrolyte drinks, and sunscreen too. If anyone in your household uses an inhaler or EpiPen, make sure everyone knows exactly where it is.

## 3. Phone Chargers and Power Banks

A dead phone on moving day means missed calls from the moving crew, the utility company trying to schedule activation, and family members asking if you need help. Pack at least two chargers (one for the car, one portable) and a fully charged power bank.

![Close Up Of A Blue Cargo Truck Parked On 4](/images/blog/2027/07/6-moving-day-necessities-for-every-relocator/close-up-of-a-blue-cargo-truck-parked-on-4.webp)


Pro tip: Keep the charger for your laptop accessible too. If you're working remotely during your move, you may need to hop on a call from a Wynwood coffee shop while waiting for the truck to arrive at your new place.

## 4. Basic Toolkit

A small toolkit saves the day more often than you'd expect. Pack a Phillips and flathead screwdriver, pliers, an adjustable wrench, a box cutter, a hammer, and a tape measure.

You'll need these for removing door hardware that blocks furniture, taking apart bed frames, and reattaching curtain rods at the new place. In older Coral Gables homes or Coconut Grove cottages, you might find doors that need hinges adjusted or cabinets that need quick fixes before you can unpack.

## 5. Cleaning Supplies

Paper towels, all-purpose cleaner, glass cleaner, a broom, and plenty of trash bags. You'll want to do a quick wipe-down of your new kitchen before putting dishes away, and you'll definitely generate trash at both locations.

For your old place, cleaning before the final walkthrough can mean the difference between getting your security deposit back or not. Many Kendall and Doral apartment complexes are strict about move-out cleaning standards.

## 6. First-Night Box

This is the box (or bag) that lets you survive night one without unpacking everything. Include:

![Close Up Of A Large Yellow Dump Truck Us 2](/images/blog/2027/07/6-moving-day-necessities-for-every-relocator/close-up-of-a-large-yellow-dump-truck-us-2.webp)


- Toiletries (toothbrush, soap, deodorant, toilet paper)
- Towels (at least one per person)
- Sheets and pillows for each bed
- A change of clothes for tomorrow
- Phone chargers (yes, again)
- Snacks and bottled water
- Pet supplies if you have animals

Label this box clearly and load it last so it comes off the truck first. Some families use a bright-colored bin or suitcase so it's impossible to miss.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Should I carry valuables myself or let movers handle them?

Jewelry, cash, important documents, family photos, and irreplaceable items should always travel with you. Professional movers are insured, but some things can't be replaced at any price.

### What if I forget something on the truck?

If the truck is still at your old location, you can usually retrieve it. Once the truck leaves, you'll need to wait until unloading at your new home. This is why the six items above stay in your car or personal bag.

### How early should I prepare my moving day bag?

Pack it the night before. On moving morning, you'll be busy directing movers and handling last-minute tasks. Having your essentials ready to grab saves stress.
