# How Many People Does It Take to Move a Hot Tub

You've seen the videos online: four guys trying to wrestle a hot tub over a fence while it slowly tips sideways. There's a reason those videos exist, and it's because people underestimate how many hands (and how much equipment) it actually takes to move a spa safely. For most Miami moves, you need more help than you think.

## Hot Tub Weights by Size

The answer to "how many people?" starts with "how heavy is it?" Here's a rough breakdown for empty hot tubs:

![A Woman Relaxes In A Steamy Outdoor Jacu 1](/images/blog/2025/09/how-many-people-does-it-take-to-move-a-hot-tub/a-woman-relaxes-in-a-steamy-outdoor-jacu-1.webp)


- **Small 2-3 person tubs**: 300-500 pounds
- **Medium 4-5 person tubs**: 500-700 pounds
- **Large 6-8 person tubs**: 700-900 pounds
- **Swim spas**: 1,000-2,500 pounds

These are dry weights. If your tub hasn't been fully drained, add 8 pounds for every gallon of water still inside. A tub with just 10 gallons of residual water in the lines and footwell is carrying an extra 80 pounds.

## The Minimum Crew Size

For a standard 4-6 person acrylic hot tub on a straightforward property with ground-level access and no major obstacles:

**Four people is the minimum.** Two on each side, using spa dollies and furniture dollies to roll the tub along a flat path. This assumes the tub weighs under 700 pounds and the entire route is paved or has smooth ground.

For larger tubs, tight access, steps, or uneven terrain, **you'll want five or six people.** The extra hands provide the lifting power needed to move around corners, tip the tub on its side to fit through gates, and safely load it onto the truck.

## When You Need Even More

Some Miami properties create situations where a small army isn't enough and you need equipment instead. If the tub sits on a second-floor deck, behind a pool cage with no removable panels, or in a fenced backyard with no gate wide enough, a crane or boom truck may be the only option. In those cases, you might have a crew of two or three on site working with the crane operator.

Waterfront homes along Biscayne Bay and in neighborhoods like Coconut Grove or Coral Gables often have this setup. The tub was craned in during the original installation, and it needs to come out the same way.

## Why "Just Getting Friends" Rarely Works

We hear this all the time: "I'll just get my buddies to help." Here's why that usually goes wrong:

![Shirtless Man Enjoying Relaxation In A H 5](/images/blog/2025/09/how-many-people-does-it-take-to-move-a-hot-tub/shirtless-man-enjoying-relaxation-in-a-h-5.webp)


- **Uneven lifting.** Without training, people lift at different speeds and angles. One person lets go or shifts their grip, and the tub drops.
- **No equipment.** Spa dollies, heavy-duty straps, and furniture slides make a huge difference. Most homeowners don't have these.
- **Injury risk.** Back injuries from improper lifting are common. A 600-pound tub sliding off a dolly can crush a foot or worse.
- **Property damage.** Scratched pool decks, dented fence panels, cracked pavers, and gouged doorframes. These repairs add up fast.

Even if you have six strong friends, they're working without the coordination and equipment that a professional [**Hot Tub Moving**](/hot-tub-moving) crew brings to every job.
