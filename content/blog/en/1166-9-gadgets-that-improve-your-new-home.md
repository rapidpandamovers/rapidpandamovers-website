Moving into a new home gives you a clean slate for smart technology. No working around previous owners' wiring decisions or outdated systems. Install these gadgets during the first few weeks while you still have the motivation and empty walls.

## 1. Smart Thermostat

The Nest Learning Thermostat and Ecobee SmartThermostat both pay for themselves within a year through energy savings. In South Florida, where AC runs constantly, even a 10% efficiency gain adds up. Installation takes about 30 minutes if you have a C-wire.

![A Google Home Mini Smart Speaker On A Wo 4](/images/blog/2028/10/9-gadgets-that-improve-your-new-home/a-google-home-mini-smart-speaker-on-a-wo-4.webp)


## 2. Video Doorbell

Ring Video Doorbell Pro 2 and Google Nest Doorbell handle package theft monitoring, letting you talk to delivery drivers from your phone. Ring works best if you already use Alexa. Nest integrates better with Google Home.

## 3. Smart Locks

The Schlage Encode Plus and August Wi-Fi Smart Lock eliminate physical keys. Create temporary codes for cleaners, dog walkers, or Airbnb guests. The codes expire automatically.

## 4. Smart Lighting

Philips Hue starter kits give you app control over bulb colors and brightness. Lutron Caseta switches replace your existing wall switches and work with almost any bulb.

![A Sleek Wireless Smart Speaker On A Wood 2](/images/blog/2028/10/9-gadgets-that-improve-your-new-home/a-sleek-wireless-smart-speaker-on-a-wood-2.webp)


## 5. Mesh WiFi System

eero Pro 6E and Google Nest WiFi Pro eliminate dead zones in homes over 2,000 square feet. Place one node per floor. Most setups take under 15 minutes through the app.

## 6. Smart Smoke Detectors

Nest Protect sends phone notifications before the ear-splitting alarm goes off. When one unit detects smoke, it tells you which room. The interconnected design means every unit in the house alerts together.

## 7. Water Leak Sensors

Govee Water Sensors cost under $15 each and run for years on a single battery. Place them near water heaters, under sinks, behind toilets, and by washing machines. A $15 sensor can prevent a $15,000 water damage claim.

## 8. Smart Plugs

Kasa Smart Plugs and Amazon Smart Plug turn any outlet into a controllable switch. Schedule lamps to turn on at sunset. Cut phantom power to electronics when you leave. Control window AC units that lack smart features.

![Close Up Of Three Smart Home Security Ca 1](/images/blog/2028/10/9-gadgets-that-improve-your-new-home/close-up-of-three-smart-home-security-ca-1.webp)


## 9. Robot Vacuum

The iRobot Roomba j7+ and Roborock S8 Pro Ultra handle daily floor maintenance. Modern models map your rooms, avoid obstacles, and empty their own dustbins. Run it while you're at work and come home to clean floors.

## Set Up Before the Boxes Are Unpacked

Install the thermostat and WiFi mesh first since other devices depend on them. Video doorbells and locks can wait until you have figured out your daily routines. Water sensors belong in place before you hook up the washing machine.
