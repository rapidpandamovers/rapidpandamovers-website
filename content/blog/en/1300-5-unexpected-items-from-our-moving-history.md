After thousands of moves across Miami-Dade County, our crews have seen it all. From Coral Gables mansions to Brickell penthouses, some items make even our most experienced movers pause and plan. Here are five of the most challenging items we have moved, and how we handled each one.

## 1. Grand Pianos

A Steinway grand piano can weigh over 1,200 pounds, and that weight is not evenly distributed. One wrong angle and you risk cracking the soundboard or snapping a leg.

![A Close Up Of A Grand Piano Keyboard In  3](/images/blog/2029/04/5-unexpected-items-from-our-moving-history/a-close-up-of-a-grand-piano-keyboard-in--3.webp)


Last year, we moved a century-old Bosendorfer from a historic home in Coconut Grove to a high-rise in Aventura. The Grove house had narrow doorways and a tricky front porch, while the Aventura building required a freight elevator reservation and protective padding for the lobby.

Our piano moving kit includes a piano board, heavy-duty straps, and specialized dollies. We remove the legs, wrap the body in quilted padding, and secure everything before the piano leaves the room. Most piano moves take three to four crew members and two to three hours of focused work.

## 2. Wine Collections

Miami collectors often have hundreds of bottles worth tens of thousands of dollars. The challenge is not just fragility but temperature control. Florida heat can ruin a wine collection in hours if bottles sit in a hot truck.

We recently relocated a 400-bottle collection from a Pinecrest wine cellar to a new home in Palmetto Bay. Each bottle was wrapped individually, placed upright in climate-controlled crates, and transported in our temperature-controlled vehicle. The entire collection arrived without a single broken bottle or compromised cork.

For wine moves, we schedule early morning pickups to avoid peak heat, use insulated containers, and minimize time between the old cellar and the new one.

## 3. Hot Tubs and Spas

That relaxing hot tub in your Kendall backyard becomes a 500 to 800 pound logistics puzzle on moving day. Even empty, these units require careful handling to avoid cracking the shell or damaging the pump system.

The process starts days before the move. We coordinate with an electrician to disconnect the unit safely, then drain all water and let internal components dry. On moving day, we use heavy-duty appliance dollies and typically four crew members to load the tub onto our flatbed.

One memorable Doral move involved a hot tub that barely fit through the backyard gate. We had to remove the gate, guide the tub through with inches to spare, then reinstall everything before leaving.

## 4. Large Aquariums

Fish keepers know their tanks are essentially giant glass boxes filled with 8 pounds per gallon of water. A 200-gallon tank weighs over 1,600 pounds when full. Moving one requires draining the tank, temporarily housing the fish, and transporting the empty tank completely level.

![Beautiful Wooden Grand Piano With Open L 1](/images/blog/2029/04/5-unexpected-items-from-our-moving-history/beautiful-wooden-grand-piano-with-open-l-1.webp)


A Wynwood collector hired us to move their 300-gallon reef tank to a new studio space in the Design District. We worked with their aquarium maintenance company to safely transfer the fish and coral to temporary tanks, drained the main tank, padded every edge, and moved it on a custom-built level platform.

The tank must stay perfectly horizontal during transport. Any tilting stresses the seams and can cause cracks that appear weeks later. We use laser levels and constant monitoring throughout the move.

## 5. Pool Tables

A regulation pool table has a three-piece slate bed weighing 450 pounds or more. That slate must be removed by specialists who know exactly how to handle it, because reassembly requires precise leveling down to fractions of an inch.

We moved a vintage Brunswick from a Miami Beach estate to a new game room in Miami Lakes. The disassembly took two hours: removing the felt, carefully extracting each slate piece, and labeling every bolt and bracket. At the new location, reassembly and leveling took another three hours to get the table playing true again.

Pool table moves are not DIY projects. We have seen plenty of tables ruined by well-meaning owners who thought they could save money. Once the slate cracks or the frame warps, the table never plays the same.
