Doral has grown from a collection of golf courses and warehouses into one of Miami-Dade County's most popular cities for families and professionals. With a population that has roughly doubled since its incorporation in 2003, the city attracts residents with its newer housing stock, strong school system, and central location between Miami International Airport and the Everglades.

If you are planning a move to Doral, this handbook covers the neighborhoods, logistics, and local details you should know before settling in.

## Doral's Neighborhoods

### Downtown Doral

![A Man Golfing Under A Clear Blue Sky At  2](/images/blog/2029/08/your-doral-moving-handbook/a-man-golfing-under-a-clear-blue-sky-at--2.webp)


The 250-acre mixed-use development at the heart of the city is Doral's most walkable area. Downtown Doral includes The Shops at Downtown Doral (over 70 retailers and restaurants), Class-A office space, and thousands of residential units ranging from luxury condos at 5350 Park to rental apartments. The area also includes the Downtown Doral Charter Elementary, which has become one of the most sought-after schools in the district.

For someone who wants to walk to dinner, shop without driving, and live in a planned urban environment, Downtown Doral is the closest thing Miami-Dade has to a traditional town center.

### CityPlace Doral

Anchored by the CityPlace shopping and entertainment complex, this area on the western side of the city offers outdoor dining, a CMX movie theater, Kings Dining & Entertainment, Miami Improv Comedy Theatre, and the Copper Blues Rock Pub. The surrounding residential developments include newer townhomes and single-family homes.

### The Golf Course Communities

Doral's original identity was built around golf, and that heritage remains. Trump National Doral (formerly the Blue Monster at Doral), the Doral Golf Resort & Spa, and several private and semi-private courses anchor residential communities where homes back up to fairways. These neighborhoods tend to be higher-end with larger lots.

### West Doral

The western portion of the city has seen rapid development with newer subdivisions featuring single-family homes and townhomes. Prices here are generally more accessible than the golf communities or Downtown Doral. West Doral has its own retail centers and is close to the Florida Turnpike for commuters heading south.

## What You Should Know Before Moving

### Schools

Doral is known for strong public schools. Downtown Doral Charter Elementary and Downtown Doral Charter Upper School are consistently high performers. The city is served by Miami-Dade County Public Schools, and several charter and private options operate in the area. School zoning should factor into your neighborhood choice if you have children.

### Commute and Transportation

Doral's location near MIA is convenient for frequent travelers, and the Palmetto Expressway (SR 826), Dolphin Expressway (SR 836), and Florida Turnpike all run through or near the city. Rush-hour traffic on these highways is heavy, especially eastbound in the morning and westbound in the evening.

There is no Metrorail stop in Doral. Public transit options are limited to bus routes, so most residents depend on cars.

### Cost of Living

Housing in Doral runs higher than the Miami-Dade average but lower than areas like Coral Gables, Key Biscayne, or Miami Beach. Expect to pay $2,200 to $3,500 per month for a two-bedroom apartment depending on the community, or $500,000 to $800,000+ for a single-family home.

### Dining and Culture

Doral has a strong Latin American influence, particularly Venezuelan and Colombian. Restaurants like Pisco Y Nazca (Peruvian) and Dragonfly Izakaya (Japanese) add variety to the dining scene. The Doral Contemporary Art Museum (DORCAM) hosts rotating exhibitions, and weekend farmers markets at Downtown Doral draw a regular crowd.

## August Moving Tips for Doral

### Heat and Timing

![A Young Golfer Focuses On His Shot On A  1](/images/blog/2029/08/your-doral-moving-handbook/a-young-golfer-focuses-on-his-shot-on-a--1.webp)


August in Doral brings the same conditions as the rest of South Florida: temperatures in the low 90s, humidity above 70 percent, and afternoon thunderstorms that arrive like clockwork between 2:00 and 5:00 PM. Schedule your move to start early in the morning, ideally by 7:00 or 8:00 AM.

### HOA Move-In Requirements

Most Doral communities, especially newer developments and condos, have HOA rules governing move-in procedures. Common requirements include:

- Advance notice (often 48 to 72 hours)
- A refundable move-in deposit ($250 to $500 is typical)
- Designated moving hours (usually weekdays between 9:00 AM and 5:00 PM)
- Elevator reservations in high-rise buildings
- Floor and wall protection during the move

Contact your HOA or property manager well before moving day to avoid surprises.

### Parking and Access

Some Doral communities have narrow interior streets and limited truck parking. Confirm that a full-size moving truck (typically 26 feet) can access your building's loading area. In Downtown Doral's higher-density blocks, loading zones may need to be reserved in advance.

## Essential First-Week Tasks

Once you have unpacked, take care of these items:

- **Update your address** with USPS, your bank, insurance, DMV, and voter registration
- **Transfer or set up utilities** through FPL (electric), Miami-Dade Water and Sewer, and your preferred internet/cable provider
- **Register vehicles** at the local DMV if you are new to Florida (you have 30 days)
- **Find a primary care doctor and dentist** in the area. Baptist Health and Nicklaus Children's Hospital both have Doral locations.
- **Explore your neighborhood** on foot or bike to learn the layout, find the closest grocery store, and meet neighbors

## FAQ

**Is Doral a good place to raise a family?**
Yes. Strong public and charter schools, numerous parks (including Doral Central Park and Downtown Doral Park), and a family-oriented culture make it one of the more popular Miami-Dade cities for families.

![Man Playing Golf On A Sunny Day At A Gol 3](/images/blog/2029/08/your-doral-moving-handbook/man-playing-golf-on-a-sunny-day-at-a-gol-3.webp)


**How far is Doral from Miami Beach?**
About 14 miles, or 25 to 45 minutes depending on traffic. The most direct route is the 836 East to I-195.

**What is the biggest downside of living in Doral?**
Traffic. The city's rapid growth has outpaced road infrastructure, and rush-hour congestion on the Palmetto and Dolphin Expressways can be significant.

**Does Doral flood?**
Parts of western Doral are in lower-lying areas, and heavy summer rains can cause temporary street flooding. Check FEMA flood zone maps for your specific address before buying or renting.
