# Moving From Storage to Your New Home

You've been paying for storage while waiting for your new place to be ready, and now it's time to get everything out and into your home. This transition is basically a second moving day, and it deserves the same level of planning as the original move. Rushing the process leads to damaged items, forgotten boxes, and a chaotic first week in your new space.

## Schedule Strategically

Don't plan storage retrieval for the same day you take possession of your new home. Give yourself at least 24-48 hours in the new place first to:

![A Collection Of Dismantled Hard Disk Dri 3](/images/blog/2028/12/moving-from-storage-to-your-new-home/a-collection-of-dismantled-hard-disk-dri-3.webp)


- **Confirm utilities are running**: AC, water, and electricity should all be verified before furniture arrives. In Miami, bringing furniture into a home without working air conditioning during summer is a fast track to sweat-soaked cushions and a miserable crew.
- **Clean the space**: Floors, countertops, and closets should be wiped down before furniture goes in.
- **Measure doorways and hallways**: Verify that large furniture pieces will fit through entries. Condo buildings in Brickell and Downtown often have narrow hallways and elevators with strict size limits.
- **Reserve the elevator**: If you're moving into a high-rise, building management typically requires advance booking for the freight elevator.

## Review Your Inventory

Pull out the inventory list you (hopefully) created when items went into storage. Cross-reference it against what's in the unit before loading the truck. This is your chance to catch any issues like missing items, water damage, or pest problems before everything is loaded and delivered.

If you didn't create an inventory list, walk through the unit and take photos of everything before the crew starts loading. This gives you documentation in case anything is damaged during transport.

## Plan Your Delivery Order

Think about which rooms need to be set up first. Most families want:

![A Well Organized Warehouse Shelving Unit 2](/images/blog/2028/12/moving-from-storage-to-your-new-home/a-well-organized-warehouse-shelving-unit-2.webp)


1. **Bedrooms**: Bed frames and mattresses first, so everyone has somewhere to sleep on night one.
2. **Kitchen**: Appliances, dishes, and cookware so you can eat and function.
3. **Bathrooms**: Toiletries, towels, and essentials.
4. **Living areas**: Couches, entertainment centers, and common spaces.

Tell the moving crew this priority order so they load the truck accordingly. Items needed last go on first (back of the truck), and items needed first go on last (front of the truck).

## Inspect Items as They Arrive

As each piece comes off the truck, do a quick check:

- Look for mold or mildew spots, especially on upholstered furniture. Even in climate-controlled storage, things can develop issues over extended periods.
- Check wood surfaces for warping, cracking, or discoloration.
- Open electronics boxes and look for condensation or corrosion.
- Test appliances before they're positioned and connected.

Report any damage immediately. If you used a professional moving and storage service, damage claims are much easier to file on delivery day than after the fact.

## Unpack With Purpose

Resist the urge to tear into every box at once. Unpack room by room, starting with the spaces you'll use most. Break down boxes as you empty them to keep walkways clear. In a Miami condo, space is tight and cardboard stacks up quickly.

![Black And White Image Of Vintage Metal F 1](/images/blog/2028/12/moving-from-storage-to-your-new-home/black-and-white-image-of-vintage-metal-f-1.webp)


Set aside items that no longer fit your new layout or lifestyle. A fresh start is a good time to donate or sell pieces that worked in your old home but don't belong in the new one.
