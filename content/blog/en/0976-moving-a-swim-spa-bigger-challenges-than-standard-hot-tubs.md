# Moving a Swim Spa: Bigger Challenges Than Standard Hot Tubs

A swim spa is a hot tub's bigger, heavier, more complicated cousin. Where a standard hot tub weighs 500 to 800 pounds empty and measures 7 feet across, a swim spa can weigh 1,000 to 2,500 pounds dry and stretch 12 to 19 feet long. Moving one out of (or into) a Miami backyard is a significantly bigger operation that requires more planning, more people, and usually more equipment.

## The Size and Weight Problem

Standard hot tubs are roughly square and can be tipped on their side to reduce their footprint for fitting through tight spaces. Swim spas are long and narrow, which limits how much you can change their orientation. A 15-foot swim spa on its side is still 15 feet long, and it won't turn corners in a narrow side yard.

Weight compounds the issue. At 1,500 to 2,500 pounds empty, a swim spa exceeds what a standard moving crew can safely lift and maneuver by hand. The weight-per-person ratio gets dangerous quickly. Even with six strong crew members, that's 250 to 400 pounds per person during lifting phases.

## Access Is the Biggest Challenge

In most Miami neighborhoods, the swim spa was delivered during construction before the fence, pool cage, or landscaping went in. Getting it out years later, with all those additions now in place, is a puzzle.

**Fences and gates**: No standard residential gate in Miami is wide enough for a 14-foot swim spa. Even with a fence section removed, you might need to take out two or three panels to create enough clearance. With concrete block walls (common in older Miami neighborhoods), removal isn't an option.

**Pool enclosures**: A swim spa inside a screened pool cage means removing a large section of the aluminum frame and screening. This typically requires a pool cage contractor who can disassemble and reassemble the affected section.

**Side yards**: The distance from the backyard to the street often runs through a narrow side yard. If the side yard is less than 8 feet wide (and many in Coral Gables, Hialeah, and Miami Springs are), the swim spa won't fit through regardless of orientation.

**Trees and landscaping**: Mature royal palms, banyan trees, and hedge lines that have grown in since the spa was installed can block the path entirely.

## When You Need Heavy Equipment

For most swim spa moves in Miami, some form of mechanical assistance is required:

![A Woman Relaxes In A Steamy Outdoor Jacu 3](/images/blog/2028/01/moving-a-swim-spa-bigger-challenges-than-standard-hot-tubs/a-woman-relaxes-in-a-steamy-outdoor-jacu-3.webp)


**Crane service**: A crane can lift the swim spa straight up and over the fence, pool cage, or any other obstruction. This is the cleanest solution for properties with limited ground access. Crane rental for a swim spa move typically costs $400 to $800, depending on the equipment size and duration.

**Forklift or Bobcat**: If there's enough ground access but the spa is too heavy for manual handling, a forklift or skid steer can move it across the yard. This requires solid ground that won't be destroyed by the equipment's weight.

**Rollers and pipe systems**: For ground-level moves across flat surfaces, heavy-duty pipe rollers can slide a swim spa along without lifting. The spa sits on parallel pipes that roll across a flat surface. This requires a clear, straight path.

## Draining a Swim Spa

A swim spa holds 1,200 to 2,800 gallons, far more than a standard hot tub. Gravity draining through a garden hose can take 4 to 6 hours. A submersible pump is almost mandatory for swim spa draining. Even with a pump, allow 45 minutes to an hour.

The internal plumbing on a swim spa is more extensive too, with more jets, more lines, and bigger pumps. Blowing out all the lines takes longer and requires more attention. In Miami's humidity, thorough drying is even more important given the larger surface area that can harbor mold.

## Pad Requirements at the New Home

A swim spa needs a much larger and stronger pad than a standard hot tub. A filled 15-foot swim spa with two people in it can weigh 18,000 to 20,000 pounds. The concrete pad should be at least 6 inches thick, reinforced with rebar, and poured over a compacted gravel base. In Miami's sandy soil, the sub-base preparation is especially important to prevent settling.
