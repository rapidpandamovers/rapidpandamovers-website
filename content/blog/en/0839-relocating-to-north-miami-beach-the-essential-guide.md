## Why Choose North Miami Beach?

North Miami Beach sits between Aventura to the north and North Miami to the south, giving residents access to upscale shopping and dining while keeping housing costs more reasonable. The city is home to over 40,000 residents and covers about 5 square miles of northeastern Miami-Dade County.

![Loc A Tranquil Scene Of Miami Beach At Sunse 1](/images/blog/2027/07/relocating-to-north-miami-beach-the-essential-guide/loc-a-tranquil-scene-of-miami-beach-at-sunse-1.webp)


The area draws families looking for affordable single-family homes, professionals who work in Aventura or Downtown Miami, and retirees attracted to the quieter pace compared to Miami Beach. The international community here includes large Russian, Haitian, and Hispanic populations, which means authentic restaurants and cultural events throughout the year.

### Location and Accessibility

North Miami Beach connects directly to I-95 via the Ives Dairy Road exit, putting Downtown Miami about 20 minutes south and Fort Lauderdale roughly 25 minutes north during non-rush hours. The city also borders US-1 (Biscayne Boulevard), which runs through the heart of the area.

Public transit options include Miami-Dade Transit bus routes along major corridors. Many residents commute to jobs in Aventura Mall, the Aventura business district, or the office parks along Biscayne Boulevard. For beach access, Haulover Beach Park sits just minutes east across the Intracoastal Waterway.

### Community and Lifestyle

Greynolds Park, a 249-acre county park with a golf course, nature trails, and picnic areas, anchors the recreational scene in North Miami Beach. The park hosts community events, outdoor yoga sessions, and family gatherings throughout the year. Oleta River State Park, Florida's largest urban park, borders the city to the east and offers kayaking, mountain biking, and mangrove trails.

The spring months bring ideal weather for outdoor activities. Temperatures average in the low 80s with lower humidity than summer, making March through May prime time for exploring the area before you settle in.

## Neighborhoods to Consider

North Miami Beach offers distinct neighborhoods, each with its own character and price point. Understanding these areas helps you find the right fit for your budget and lifestyle.

### Eastern Shores

This waterfront community features single-family homes along canal networks, many with private docks. Eastern Shores attracts boaters and families looking for more space. Expect home prices starting around $600K and reaching well over $1M for waterfront properties. The neighborhood sits close to Sunny Isles Beach and offers quick access to the beach via the 163rd Street causeway.

### Fulford and the Historic District

The area around the Fulford-by-the-Sea marker represents the city's historic core. Here you will find older single-family homes, often on larger lots, at more affordable prices than Eastern Shores. This neighborhood works well for buyers looking for renovation projects or renters seeking houses instead of apartments.

### Skylake and West North Miami Beach

West of I-95, these neighborhoods offer the most affordable housing options in the city. Apartment complexes and townhome communities line the major roads, with rent prices running $200-400 less than comparable units closer to the water. The trade-off is a longer drive to the beach, though Aventura Mall sits just a few minutes north.

### Choosing Your Perfect Spot

Consider these factors when narrowing down your search:

- **Commute time**: Test the drive to work during rush hour before signing a lease. I-95 southbound backs up significantly from 7-9am.
- **School zones**: North Miami Beach Senior High and Highland Oaks Middle School draw families to specific neighborhoods. Check school assignments before committing to an address.
- **Flood zones**: Eastern areas near the Intracoastal require flood insurance. Review FEMA maps and factor insurance costs into your housing budget.
- **HOA rules**: Many condo and townhome communities have move-in requirements including advance notice, elevator reservations, and certificates of insurance from your moving company.

## Moving to North Miami Beach in Spring

Spring ranks as one of the best seasons for a Miami-Dade move. The snowbird crowd heads north, freeing up moving company schedules and reducing competition for apartments. Temperatures stay comfortable for the physical work of moving, and you avoid the daily afternoon thunderstorms that complicate summer relocations.

### Timing Your Move

Plan your move with these local factors in mind:

- **Weekday moves**: Tuesday through Thursday offers the best availability and rates. Monday and Friday moves cost more due to weekend demand.
- **Morning start times**: Schedule movers to arrive by 8am. This beats the worst traffic and ensures your belongings are inside before any afternoon rain develops.
- **Month-end rush**: The last week of the month brings higher demand as leases turn over. Mid-month moves typically mean better availability and potentially lower rates.

## Essential Services to Locate

Getting settled in a new city means finding your go-to spots for daily necessities. Here are the key services to locate in your first weeks:

**Healthcare**: Aventura Hospital and Medical Center sits just north of the city line and handles most medical needs. Jackson North Medical Center in North Miami provides additional options. For urgent care, multiple facilities line Biscayne Boulevard.

**Schools**: Miami-Dade County Public Schools serves the area. Research school grades and programs at dadeschools.net. Popular private options include the Hebrew Academy (RASG) and several Catholic schools in neighboring communities.

**Shopping**: Aventura Mall, one of Florida's largest shopping centers, sits minutes north. For groceries, Publix has multiple locations throughout the city, with specialty markets including Presidente Supermarket for Latin groceries and several Russian markets along 163rd Street.

**Recreation**: Beyond the parks mentioned earlier, the North Miami Beach Public Library offers programs for all ages. The city operates several community centers with fitness facilities, pools, and youth programs.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does it cost to hire movers in North Miami Beach?

Local moves within Miami-Dade typically run $100-150 per hour for a two-person crew with a truck. A one-bedroom apartment move averages 3-4 hours, while a three-bedroom house may take 6-8 hours. Final costs depend on the amount of furniture, stairs or elevator access, and distance traveled.

### What is the best neighborhood in North Miami Beach for families?

Eastern Shores and the areas near North Miami Beach Senior High School attract the most families. These neighborhoods offer single-family homes with yards, good school access, and relatively quiet streets. The trade-off is higher home prices compared to western neighborhoods.

### How far is North Miami Beach from the actual beach?

Despite the name, North Miami Beach does not directly border the ocean. The closest beach access is Haulover Beach Park, about 10 minutes east via 163rd Street. Sunny Isles Beach and its public beaches sit slightly further north. Many residents also head to Oleta River State Park for waterfront activities without the ocean crowds.
