## About Virginia Gardens

Virginia Gardens is a small village in Miami-Dade County, located just south of Miami International Airport. With approximately 2,400 residents, this close-knit community offers affordable housing, convenient airport access, and a strong sense of neighborhood identity.

### Demographics and Community

- **Population**: Approximately 2,381 (2025 estimate)
- **Median Age**: 42.1 years
- **Diversity**: Hispanic (81.6%), White (15.4%)
- **Median Household Income**: $72,569
- **Poverty Rate**: 3.0% (one of the lowest in Miami-Dade)
- **Foreign-Born Residents**: 61.8% (including 43.4% naturalized citizens)

### Employment Profile

- **Working Residents**: 1,468
- **Professional/Administrative Jobs**: 80.4%
- **Self-Employed**: 14.4%
- **Private Sector**: 70.8%
- **Unemployment Rate**: 3.5%

## Neighborhoods and Character

Virginia Gardens is a predominantly residential community with single-family homes making up 57.1% of housing. The village maintains a quiet, suburban atmosphere despite its proximity to the airport and major commercial areas.

### Nearby Miami-Dade Communities

Virginia Gardens sits in a central location with easy access to several popular areas:

- **Doral:** 5-10 minutes west, home to major corporate offices and shopping at CityPlace Doral
- **Miami Springs:** Adjacent to the north, with similar small-town character and more dining options
- **Hialeah:** Just east, offering Latin markets, restaurants, and retail
- **Sweetwater:** A short drive south, near Florida International University
- **Westchester:** Minutes away, with malls and entertainment along Coral Way

### Housing Stock

- **Single-Family Homes**: 57.1%
- **Total Housing Units**: 862
- **Median Housing Costs**: $1,530/month
- **Median Rent**: $1,614/month

## Weather and Seasonal Considerations

Like all of Miami-Dade County, Virginia Gardens experiences South Florida's tropical climate:

- **Hurricane Season (June-November):** Prepare for storms with shutters and supplies. Most homes in Virginia Gardens are single-story, which can simplify hurricane prep.
- **Summer Heat (May-October):** Temperatures regularly exceed 90°F with high humidity. Plan outdoor activities for early morning or evening.
- **Dry Season (November-April):** The best weather of the year, with comfortable temperatures and low humidity. This is peak moving season.
- **Afternoon Thunderstorms:** Common from May through October, typically between 3-6pm. Schedule important outdoor activities for morning hours.

## Moving Tips for Virginia Gardens

### Best Time to Move
- **Winter months (November-March)**: Mild weather, lower humidity
- **Weekdays**: Better parking availability

### Local Considerations
- **Airport Noise**: Some areas experience aircraft noise - visit at different times
- **Street Parking**: Plan truck placement in residential areas
- **Village Services**: Contact village hall for any permit requirements
