March is a popular month for PCS moves in South Florida. With spring orders rolling in and the dry season still providing mild, comfortable weather, military families stationed at or transferring to Homestead Air Reserve Base and other South Florida installations have a strong window for relocation.

## Why March Works Well for Military Moves

March falls in Miami's dry season, meaning mild temperatures in the 70s and 80s with low humidity and very little rain. These conditions are ideal for loading trucks, transporting household goods, and settling into a new home without weather delays.

## Key Tips for Military Moving in March

### Plan Ahead for the Spring PCS Season

March kicks off the busy PCS season that runs through summer. Moving companies near military installations, especially around Homestead, fill up quickly. Schedule your move as soon as you receive orders to secure your preferred dates.

### Know Your Miami Area Options

If you're PCSing to South Florida, popular neighborhoods for military families include Kendall for its affordability, Doral for quick access to major highways, and Palmetto Bay for excellent schools. Those stationed at Homestead often choose nearby Florida City or Cutler Bay for shorter commutes.

### Keep All Documentation Organized for Reimbursement

Save every receipt, get weight tickets, and keep your orders packet together. This single step can save hours of frustration when filing for reimbursement. Our experienced crews have seen what happens when paperwork gets lost in the shuffle.

### Take Advantage of the Dry Season Weather

March is one of Miami's driest months. Unlike the wet season (May through October), you won't face daily afternoon thunderstorms or hurricane concerns. Plan outdoor loading and unloading with confidence, though it's still wise to have protective coverings ready for your belongings.

### Prepare Your Belongings

Take time to inventory your items before the move. This is especially important for military moves, as weight allowances matter for PCS relocations and proper documentation helps with any damage claims.

## PCS Move Preparation Checklist

Before your military move, make sure to:

- Coordinate with your Transportation Office for proper orders documentation
- Sort through belongings and declutter (weight allowances matter for PCS moves)
- Gather important documents including military orders, DD Form 1299, and inventory lists
- Notify DEERS, Tricare, and other military services of your address change
- Research housing options near your new duty station
- Arrange utilities at your new location
