Some items don't fit in a box. Pianos, gun safes, pool tables, hot tubs, gym equipment, wine collections -- these are the things that make standard moving companies nervous and specialty movers essential.

If you're planning a summer move in South Florida that involves one or more of these items, timing and preparation matter more than you might think. Here's how to get it right.

## Why Summer Makes Specialty Moves Harder

South Florida between June and September brings three challenges that directly affect specialty items:

![A High Angle View Of A Construction Site 3](/images/blog/2029/06/your-summer-specialty-item-moving-planning-guide/a-high-angle-view-of-a-construction-site-3.webp)


**Heat**: Temperatures regularly hit the low 90s, and surfaces in direct sunlight can reach 140 degrees or more. This matters for items with wood finishes (pianos, antique furniture), electronics, and anything with adhesives or wax components.

**Humidity**: Sustained humidity above 80% can warp wood, corrode metal, and damage electronics. A piano sitting in an un-air-conditioned truck for too long will feel the effects.

**Afternoon storms**: Nearly every summer day brings thunderstorms between 2 and 5 PM. If your pool table is halfway between the house and the truck when the sky opens up, you have a problem.

## Planning Your Specialty Item Move

### Tell Your Movers Everything During the Estimate

This is the most common mistake people make. You schedule a move, mention you have furniture and boxes, but forget to mention the 800-pound gun safe in the garage or the baby grand in the living room.

[**Specialty item moving**](/specialty-item-moving) requires specific equipment: piano boards, safe dollies, custom crating, hydraulic lift gates. If your movers don't know about these items until they show up, the move will be delayed -- or they simply won't be able to handle it that day.

During your estimate, tell us about:
- The item type, dimensions, and approximate weight
- Where it currently sits (floor level, room location, doorway widths)
- Where it needs to go (same details for the destination)
- Any stairs, tight corners, or elevator requirements

### Schedule for Early Morning

This applies to any summer move, but it's especially true for specialty items. A morning start means:

- Cooler temperatures for the crew doing heavy lifting
- The move wraps up before afternoon storms roll in
- Less heat exposure for temperature-sensitive items like pianos and wine collections
- Cooler truck interiors during loading

Aim for a 7 AM start time. By noon, most specialty items should be loaded, transported, and placed.

### Protect Against Heat and Humidity

Professional movers use climate-controlled trucks for heat-sensitive items when available. But you can also take steps on your end:

- **Pianos**: Keep the lid closed. Avoid direct sunlight during loading and unloading. The piano will need retuning after the move regardless, but minimizing temperature swings helps
- **Wine collections**: Use insulated shipping containers or coolers for transport. Wine should not sit in a hot truck
- **Electronics and arcade machines**: Power down at least 24 hours before the move. Wrap screens in moving blankets, not plastic wrap (which traps heat)
- **Pool tables**: Slate and felt are both affected by humidity. Disassemble in a climate-controlled space if possible

### Plan the Pathway

Specialty items are heavy and awkward. Before moving day:

- Measure all doorways, hallways, and stairwells at both locations
- Remove doors from hinges if needed for clearance
- Clear the path of rugs, furniture, and anything that could be tripped over
- If the item goes upstairs, confirm that the stairwell can handle the weight and turning radius
- For elevators, check the weight limit and interior dimensions

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How far in advance should I schedule a specialty item move?**
At least 2-3 weeks in summer. Specialty moves require specific equipment and sometimes additional crew members, both of which are in higher demand during peak season.

![Movers Loading Boxes Into A Truck On A S 2](/images/blog/2029/06/your-summer-specialty-item-moving-planning-guide/movers-loading-boxes-into-a-truck-on-a-s-2.webp)


**Can specialty items be moved with the rest of my household goods?**
Often yes, but it depends on the items. A piano and a standard household move can usually happen on the same day. A gun safe plus a pool table might require a separate trip due to weight and equipment needs.

**Will my piano need tuning after the move?**
Almost certainly. Most piano manufacturers recommend waiting 2-3 weeks after a move before tuning, to let the instrument acclimate to its new environment.

**Do you disassemble and reassemble pool tables?**
Yes. Pool table moves always involve full disassembly (including removing the felt and slate) and complete reassembly and leveling at the destination.
