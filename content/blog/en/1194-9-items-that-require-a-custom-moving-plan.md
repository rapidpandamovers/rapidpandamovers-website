# 9 Items That Require a Custom Moving Plan

Some belongings can't just be boxed up and tossed in the truck. They need a plan built around their specific weight, dimensions, fragility, or mechanical complexity. Here are nine items that always get their own moving strategy when our Miami crews encounter them.

## 1. Grand Pianos

A grand piano has over 12,000 individual parts, weighs 500-1,200 pounds, and has a polished finish that shows every scratch. The legs come off, the lid locks, and the body rides on a piano board. Doorways, stairs, and elevator dimensions all get measured in advance. There's no improvising with a $50,000 Steinway.

![A Person Carrying A Large Plaid Checkere 4](/images/blog/2028/11/9-items-that-require-a-custom-moving-plan/a-person-carrying-a-large-plaid-checkere-4.webp)


## 2. Wine Cellars and Collections

Temperature-controlled wine storage systems need to be decommissioned by a technician. The wine itself travels in insulated shippers during early morning hours to avoid Miami's heat. Collections over 100 bottles get inventoried and insured separately. The cooling unit, racks, and bottles each follow different packing and transport protocols.

## 3. Aquariums Over 50 Gallons

Live fish, coral, and live rock all travel separately from the glass tank. We save half the original tank water in sealed buckets to preserve the bacterial ecosystem. The tank moves empty and upright. Filtration equipment gets disassembled and packed wet to keep beneficial bacteria alive. Reassembly follows a specific sequence with a 2-hour stabilization period before reintroducing livestock.

## 4. Grandfather Clocks

The pendulum, weights, and chains come out before anything else moves. Glass panels get removed and crated. The case travels upright on a padded dolly. At the destination, precise leveling (within fractions of a degree) determines whether the clock keeps accurate time. A follow-up visit from a clock specialist is recommended within the first week.

![Facade Featuring A Baseball Scoreboard A 3](/images/blog/2028/11/9-items-that-require-a-custom-moving-plan/facade-featuring-a-baseball-scoreboard-a-3.webp)


## 5. Mounted Taxidermy Collections

Every mount has different fragile points: antler tips, glass eyes, fins, ears, and painted surfaces. We build individual padding setups for each piece. Fish mounts get cardboard fin splints. Shoulder mounts travel in oversized boxes with custom foam nests. Nothing gets stacked, and everything stays climate-controlled during transport to prevent mold on preserved hides.

## 6. Home Medical Equipment

Hospital beds, oxygen concentrators, and patient lift systems have to be operational at the new home before the patient arrives. We create a priority unloading sequence so medical equipment comes off the truck first. Battery-powered devices get their batteries disconnected. Respiratory equipment travels in sealed, moisture-proof packaging to protect against Miami humidity.

## 7. Full Home Gym Setups

A complete gym with squat rack, bench, free weights, treadmill, and rubber flooring can total 3,000+ pounds spread across dozens of pieces. Every bolt and pin gets labeled during disassembly. Electronic consoles on treadmills and bikes get wrapped like fragile electronics. We plan the destination layout in advance so heavy equipment goes in first and in the right spot.

## 8. Outdoor Kitchen and Grill Stations

Built-in grills, smokers, kegerators, and stone countertops in Miami's outdoor living spaces each need different handling. Gas lines require professional disconnection. Ceramic kamado grills weigh 160+ pounds and crack if dropped. Stone countertops travel upright on A-frame dollies. Everything needs to be reassembled and reconnected by appropriate tradespeople at the new home.

## 9. Safe Rooms and Vault Doors

Some Miami homes have reinforced safe rooms with vault-grade doors weighing 500-1,000+ pounds. These doors hang on heavy-duty hinges that require precise alignment. Removal and reinstallation needs a crew experienced with vault hardware, specialized lifting equipment, and coordination with a locksmith to ensure the locking mechanism works correctly after the move.
