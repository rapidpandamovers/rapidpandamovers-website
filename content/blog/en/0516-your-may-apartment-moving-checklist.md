## Key Tips for Apartment Moving in May

### Plan Ahead for Peak Times

May can be a busy time for moving companies. With summer activities and seasonal transitions, scheduling your apartment moving early ensures you get your preferred date and time.

### Reserve the freight elevator well in advance and confirm with building management

This single step can save hours of stress and potential problems on moving day. Our experienced crews have seen what happens when this step is skipped.

### Consider the Weather

South Florida's summer weather is predictable in some ways and surprising in others. Professional movers know how to protect your belongings from humidity, rain, and heat exposure during transport.

### Prepare Your Belongings

Take time to inventory your items before the move. This is especially important for apartment moving, as proper documentation helps ensure everything arrives safely at your new location.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does apartment moving cost in Miami?

![Upward View Of A Tall Modern Building Wi 2](/images/blog/2026/05/your-may-apartment-moving-checklist/upward-view-of-a-tall-modern-building-wi-2.webp)


Apartment moving in Miami typically costs $400-$1,200 for a one-bedroom and $800-$2,000 for a two-bedroom. High-rise buildings in Brickell or Downtown may add $200-$500 for COI requirements and elevator reservations.

### How far in advance should I reserve the freight elevator?

Book your freight elevator 2-4 weeks in advance. Popular buildings in Brickell and Downtown have limited move-in windows, and weekends fill up quickly during peak season.

### What building documents do I need for the move?

Most Miami condos require a Certificate of Insurance (COI) from your moving company, a signed move-in agreement, and sometimes a refundable deposit. Contact your building management at least two weeks before your move to get the full list.
