November is one of the best months to move a piano in South Florida. Humidity drops, temperatures settle into the upper 70s, and the summer storm season is over. But even under ideal conditions, piano moving demands preparation and the right crew. Here is what to know.

## Why November Works for Piano Moves

Pianos are sensitive to humidity and temperature swings. South Florida's summer months bring 80-90% humidity and daily afternoon thunderstorms, both of which create risk during loading, transport, and unloading. By November, humidity typically drops to 60-70%, and rain is far less frequent.

![Hand Trucks And Perforated Wooden Panel  2](/images/blog/2029/11/piano-moving-guide-for-november/hand-trucks-and-perforated-wooden-panel--2.webp)


Lower humidity also means less risk of moisture damage to the soundboard, strings, and wooden components during the time your piano sits in a truck or on a dolly outside.

### Scheduling Advantage

November falls between the busy summer moving season and the holiday rush in December. [**Piano moving**](/piano-moving) crews tend to have better availability, which means you are more likely to get your preferred date and time slot.

## How Professionals Move a Piano

Piano moving is not standard furniture moving. The equipment, techniques, and crew requirements are different.

### Equipment Used

- **Piano board (skid board)**: A padded, flat board that the piano is strapped to for transport. This distributes weight evenly and prevents tipping.
- **Piano dolly**: Heavy-duty dolly with rubber wheels designed to support the instrument's weight (300-1,200 lbs depending on type).
- **Moving blankets and stretch wrap**: Multiple layers of padding secured around the piano to prevent scratches and dings.
- **Ratchet straps**: Secure the piano to the dolly and to the truck walls to prevent shifting during transit.
- **Lifting straps**: Distribute weight across the movers' bodies for safer lifting.

### The Process

1. **Lock the keyboard lid** to prevent it from opening during the move.
2. **Wrap the piano** in moving blankets, securing with stretch wrap. Tape never touches the piano's finish directly.
3. **Secure to the piano board** using ratchet straps.
4. **Roll to the truck** on the piano dolly.
5. **Load and secure** against the truck wall with additional straps.
6. **Unload and place** at the destination, positioning against an interior wall when possible.

For grand pianos, the process includes removing the legs and lyre, wrapping them separately, and tilting the body onto the piano board.

### Crew Size

An upright piano typically requires 2-3 movers. A grand piano requires 3-4 movers minimum. If stairs are involved, add one more person per flight.

## Before the Move: What You Should Do

### Close and Lock the Lid

![Plastic Wrapped Boxes And Supplies For M 3](/images/blog/2029/11/piano-moving-guide-for-november/plastic-wrapped-boxes-and-supplies-for-m-3.webp)


The keyboard lid should be latched shut. If your piano does not have a lock, use painter's tape (never regular tape) to keep it closed.

### Clear the Path

Remove rugs, furniture, and anything else between the piano and the door at both locations. Measure doorways, hallways, and any tight turns. A standard upright piano is about 58 inches wide, and a baby grand can be over 5 feet long even with legs removed.

### Tell Your Movers About Stairs

Stairs change everything in piano moving. If either location involves stairs, let the moving company know during the quote stage. Stair carries require additional crew members and equipment, and the cost will reflect that.

## After the Move: Wait Before Tuning

Do not tune your piano immediately after a move. The instrument needs 2-4 weeks to acclimate to the humidity and temperature of its new environment. Tuning too early means the piano will go out of tune again as it adjusts.

Place the piano:
- Against an **interior wall** (exterior walls transmit heat and cold)
- Away from **windows, vents, and direct sunlight**
- On a **stable, level surface**

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How much does it cost to move a piano?**
Cost depends on the piano type, distance, and whether stairs are involved. Upright pianos are less expensive to move than grands. Contact us for a specific quote.

**Can a piano be stored temporarily?**
Yes, but it must be in a climate-controlled environment. Standard storage units with fluctuating temperatures and humidity can damage a piano within weeks.

**Will moving a piano damage it?**
Not when handled by professionals using proper equipment. The risk comes from DIY moves or using general movers without piano-specific training and tools.

**Do you move electric pianos and keyboards?**
Yes. While these are lighter and less delicate than acoustic pianos, they still benefit from professional packing and transport.
