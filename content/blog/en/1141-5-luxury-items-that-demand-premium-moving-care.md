# 5 Luxury Items That Demand Premium Moving Care

Some belongings are too valuable, too fragile, or too irreplaceable to trust to standard moving procedures. In Miami's luxury homes, these items show up in almost every move. Here are five categories that absolutely require premium handling.

## 1. Grand and Concert Pianos

A Steinway Model D concert grand weighs around 990 pounds, costs upward of $150,000, and contains more than 12,000 individual parts. Moving one requires a dedicated piano board, locking straps, a team of at least four trained movers, and a truck with air-ride suspension. The legs and lyre must be removed and wrapped separately. The lid gets padded and secured. Even the casters need protection. We've moved grand pianos from Star Island estates to Brickell penthouses, and every one demands a custom plan specific to the instrument and both locations.

## 2. Original Fine Art

A painting by a living artist from a Wynwood gallery might be worth $20,000. A piece by a blue-chip artist from an auction house could exceed $1 million. Either way, the handling requirements are strict. Oil paintings cannot be wrapped in plastic because trapped moisture causes mold. Canvases flex if boxed incorrectly. Sculptures need custom-fitted crates with internal bracing. Our team uses museum-grade packing methods including acid-free glassine, foam corner protectors, and wooden travel frames for each piece.

## 3. Wine Collections

South Florida's climate makes wine storage and transport especially tricky. A collection stored at a perfect 55 degrees in a custom cellar can be destroyed by a few hours in a truck that's reached 130 degrees in the Miami sun. White glove wine transport involves insulated containers, temperature monitoring during the move, and rapid transfer into the new cellar or storage unit. For large collections common in Coral Gables and Coconut Grove estates, we coordinate the move to minimize time outside climate control.

## 4. Antique Furniture

That 18th-century French armoire or Victorian roll-top desk survived two centuries because people handled it with care. Antique wood is dry, joints can be fragile, and original hardware is irreplaceable. Standard moving blankets aren't enough. Our white glove team uses padded slings to lift without stressing joints, wraps surfaces in acid-free tissue before any outer padding, and never uses tape directly on finished wood. We document every existing imperfection before the move so there's never a question about pre-existing versus new damage.

## 5. High-End Electronics and Home Automation Systems

Luxury homes in Miami increasingly feature whole-home automation, including integrated audio, motorized shading, security systems, and networked lighting. Disconnecting and reconnecting these systems requires technical knowledge beyond moving expertise. Our white glove service coordinates with your home automation provider, documents all system configurations, and ensures everything arrives safely. The $80,000 home theater in your Fisher Island villa gets the same careful treatment as the artwork on the walls.
