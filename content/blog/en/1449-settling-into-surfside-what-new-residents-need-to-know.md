Surfside is a small oceanfront town of about 6,000 residents wedged between Miami Beach to the south and Bal Harbour to the north. It covers roughly half a square mile, runs ten blocks from 87th Terrace to 96th Street, and is bounded by the Atlantic Ocean to the east and Indian Creek waterway to the west. Despite its size, Surfside has a distinct identity -- walkable, family-oriented, and noticeably quieter than its neighbors.

## The Harding Avenue Corridor

Harding Avenue is the commercial spine of Surfside and one of the main reasons the town feels different from Miami Beach. While Collins Avenue runs along the eastern edge with condos and beach access, Harding Avenue one block west is where daily life happens.

### Dining

Surfside has a notable concentration of kosher restaurants, reflecting the town's significant Jewish community. You will find kosher steakhouses, sushi spots, pizza shops, and bakeries along Harding Avenue and the surrounding blocks. Non-kosher options are also available, and the proximity to Bal Harbour and North Beach means dozens of additional restaurants are within a five-minute drive.

A few spots worth trying early:

- **Cafe Ragazzi**: Pizza and pasta at 9517 Harding Avenue, a Surfside fixture for over 20 years
- **Sushi Republic**: Classic sushi and Japanese dishes at 9583 Harding Avenue, a local favorite
- **The Harbour Grill**: Glatt Kosher steakhouse at 9415 Harding Avenue with dry-aged steaks and sushi
- **Flanigan's**: Casual seafood and bar food at 9516 Harding Avenue, open late

### Shopping and Errands

Harding Avenue has the essentials: a Publix grocery store, banks, a pharmacy, dry cleaners, and small retail shops. For anything beyond everyday needs, Bal Harbour Shops is a short drive north, and Aventura Mall is about 15 minutes away.

### SurfStreet Saturday

The town hosts SurfStreet Saturday events on 93rd Street between Collins and Harding Avenues, turning the block into an arts and culture block party with live performances, food vendors, a kids' activity zone, and a lounge area. It runs on the first Saturday of select months and is one of the best ways to meet neighbors quickly after moving in.

## Beach Access

Surfside Beach runs the full length of the town along Collins Avenue. Public access points are located at most east-west street ends between 88th and 96th Streets. The beach is notably less crowded than South Beach or even North Beach, with a relaxed, family-friendly atmosphere.

The town maintains lifeguard stations, beach showers, and public restrooms. Beach chairs and umbrellas are available seasonally. Unlike Miami Beach, which draws tourists from around the world, Surfside's beach is primarily used by residents and their guests, which keeps the sand and water noticeably calmer.

## Housing Stock

Surfside's housing divides into two main categories:

### Condos

The oceanfront condos along Collins Avenue range from mid-rise buildings to larger complexes. Many of these buildings have specific move-in requirements -- reserved elevator times, loading dock hours, certificate of insurance documentation, and sometimes refundable move-in deposits. Confirm these requirements with your building management well before your moving date.

The former Champlain Towers South site at 8777 Collins Avenue is now under construction as The Delmore, a 12-story luxury condo by DAMAC International designed by Zaha Hadid Architects. Expect construction activity and traffic impacts on nearby blocks through the build-out period.

### Single-Family Homes

The blocks between Harding Avenue and Indian Creek contain single-family homes on modest lots. These are older Florida-style homes, many built in the 1940s through 1960s, with a growing number being renovated or replaced with new construction. Street parking is tight, and some properties have limited driveway access, which is worth noting when planning your move.

## Schools

Surfside is served by Miami-Dade County Public Schools. Ruth K. Broad Bay Harbor K-8 Center is the zoned school for elementary and middle grades and consistently receives strong ratings. For high school, students are zoned for North Miami Beach Senior High.

Private options in the immediate area include Hebrew Academy (RASG) in Miami Beach and Scheck Hillel Community School in North Miami Beach. Bal Harbour and Bay Harbor Islands also have additional school options within a short commute.

## Getting Around

Surfside is one of the most walkable towns in Miami-Dade County, at least for daily errands. Harding Avenue is accessible on foot from most residential blocks, and the beach is never more than a few minutes' walk east.

For commuting and longer trips:

- **Collins Avenue / A1A**: The main north-south artery connecting to Miami Beach southbound and Bal Harbour and Sunny Isles Beach northbound
- **I-95**: Accessible via 96th Street or through Bal Harbour and Surfside Boulevard, about 10 minutes west
- **Downtown Miami**: 20 to 35 minutes south, depending on traffic and your route choice
- **Miami Beach (South Beach)**: 15 to 20 minutes south via Collins Avenue

Parking can be tight, especially near Harding Avenue and the beach access points. Many buildings have assigned parking, but street parking is metered and time-limited in commercial areas.

## Practical First Steps

### Utilities

Florida Power and Light handles electricity. Miami-Dade Water and Sewer covers water. Set up accounts before your move date so service is active when you arrive.

### Town Services

The Town of Surfside municipal building on Harding Avenue at 93rd Street handles resident services, building permits, and code compliance. Register for town updates and meeting notices to stay informed about development projects and community events.

### Hurricane Preparedness

Surfside sits on a barrier island, and much of the town is in a hurricane evacuation zone. Know your zone designation, stock supplies before hurricane season (June 1 through November 30), and have an evacuation plan. Condo buildings may have their own hurricane preparedness protocols and shutter requirements.

## FAQ

**What is the cost of living in Surfside compared to Miami Beach?**
Housing costs in Surfside are generally comparable to the quieter sections of Miami Beach and lower than South Beach or Mid-Beach. Condo association fees vary widely depending on the building's age and amenities. Property taxes reflect Miami-Dade County rates with the Town of Surfside millage added.

**Is Surfside good for families with children?**
Yes. The town has a family-oriented culture, good public school access, safe streets for walking, and a low-key beach environment. The kosher dining options and proximity to synagogues also make it a strong fit for observant Jewish families.

**Are there condo move-in restrictions I should know about?**
Most Surfside condos require advance scheduling for move-ins, typically with 48 to 72 hours notice. Common requirements include proof of insurance, reserved elevator and loading dock times, and sometimes a refundable deposit. Contact your building manager as soon as you have a closing or lease start date.

**How is parking in Surfside?**
Street parking near Harding Avenue and the beach is metered and limited. Most condos include assigned parking spaces. Single-family homes generally have driveways, though some older properties have limited space. On-street parking in residential areas is free but can fill up, especially on weekends and during community events.
