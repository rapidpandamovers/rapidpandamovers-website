# White Glove Moving Insurance and Valuation Options

Insurance is one of the most overlooked aspects of hiring movers, and it's the one that matters most when something goes wrong. For luxury moves in Miami where a single item can be worth more than the entire cost of the move, understanding your coverage options is not just smart planning. It's financial protection.

## The Federal Minimum: Released Value

Every licensed mover is required by federal law to offer Released Value Protection at no extra charge. This covers your belongings at $0.60 per pound per article. A 30-pound lamp worth $3,000 would only be covered for $18 under this plan. For a standard apartment move, some people accept this risk. For a white glove move involving designer furniture, original art, and electronics, Released Value is nowhere close to adequate.

## Full Replacement Value Protection

This is the standard coverage level for white glove moves. Under Full Replacement Value, the mover is liable for the replacement cost of any item that's lost or damaged. If a $10,000 dining table gets scratched, the mover either repairs it to its original condition, replaces it with an equivalent item, or compensates you for the full value. Most white glove companies include this coverage in their pricing, but the specifics vary. Ask about deductible amounts, which typically range from $250 to $1,000.

## Third-Party Moving Insurance

For high-value moves, many clients purchase additional coverage through a third-party insurance provider. Companies that specialize in transit insurance can provide policies tailored to your specific inventory. This is especially valuable for items that exceed the mover's maximum liability cap, which many companies set at a percentage of the total move value. A Coral Gables estate with $500,000 in furnishings may need supplemental coverage to fill gaps.

## Declared Value and Inventory Lists

White glove movers will ask you to declare the value of your shipment before the move. This declaration determines the maximum liability the mover assumes. Accurate documentation is critical. Our pre-move inventory process photographs and catalogs every item, creating a record that supports both the valuation declaration and any future claims. For items like antiques and artwork where replacement value isn't straightforward, we recommend having appraisals on file.

## What Coverage Typically Excludes

Even comprehensive coverage has limits. Most policies exclude damage caused by natural disasters, items packed by the homeowner (another reason to let white glove professionals handle packing), and items not listed on the inventory. Pre-existing damage documented during the walk-through is also excluded. Some policies exclude certain high-risk categories like live plants, perishable food, and hazardous materials. Review exclusions carefully before signing.

## The Claims Process

If damage occurs, the timeline and process for filing a claim matter as much as the coverage itself. Ask your mover how long you have to report damage, typically 90 days for full replacement value claims. Ask what documentation they need, and whether they use in-house repair professionals or third-party restoration specialists. For valuable antiques and art in Miami homes, working with a mover who partners with restoration experts means better outcomes than a company that just writes a check.

## How to Evaluate Coverage When Comparing Movers

When comparing white glove movers in Miami, put insurance questions at the top of your list. Ask for the coverage details in writing, not just a verbal summary. Compare deductible amounts, maximum liability caps, exclusions, and the claims timeline. A company quoting a lower move price may also be offering less coverage, which shifts the financial risk onto you.
