Professional movers legally cannot transport certain items, and others are simply too risky to put in a moving truck. Before your move, make sure these 8 items stay out of the truck for safety, legal, and practical reasons.

## 1. Flammable and Combustible Items

Gasoline, propane tanks, lighter fluid, fireworks, and ammunition are dangerous in enclosed spaces, especially in South Florida heat. Miami summers can push truck temperatures well above 120 degrees, making these items even more hazardous. Dispose of or transport these yourself in a well-ventilated vehicle.

![A Woman Organizing Cardboard Boxes In A  3](/images/blog/2028/11/8-things-to-keep-out-of-the-moving-truck/a-woman-organizing-cardboard-boxes-in-a--3.webp)


## 2. Perishable Food

Refrigerated and frozen foods won't survive a move, especially in Florida's climate. Consume, donate, or discard perishables before moving day. Canned goods and sealed dry goods are fine.

## 3. Houseplants

Most moving companies won't transport plants due to pest regulations between states and the risk of damage. Plants also need water and light that trucks can't provide. If you're moving within Miami-Dade, you can transport plants yourself, but interstate moves require checking USDA regulations. Transport valuable plants yourself in your air-conditioned car.

## 4. Important Documents and Valuables

Passports, birth certificates, jewelry, cash, and irreplaceable items should stay with you. If something is irreplaceable or essential, keep it in your personal vehicle.

![Man Packing Kitchenware Into Labeled Box 1](/images/blog/2028/11/8-things-to-keep-out-of-the-moving-truck/man-packing-kitchenware-into-labeled-box-1.webp)


## 5. Medications

Prescription medications and medical supplies should travel with you. Temperature fluctuations in a truck can affect medication efficacy, and you need immediate access.

## 6. Hazardous Chemicals

Cleaning products, paints, pesticides, pool chemicals, and automotive fluids can leak or react. Most are prohibited by moving companies. Dispose of properly or transport separately. Miami-Dade County offers hazardous waste disposal at several locations, including the Home Chemical Collection Center.

## 7. Pets

This should be obvious, but pets require climate control, water, and bathroom breaks. Never put animals in a moving truck. Transport them in your vehicle or use a pet transport service.

![Pensive Couple Taking Notes In Bedroom N 2](/images/blog/2028/11/8-things-to-keep-out-of-the-moving-truck/pensive-couple-taking-notes-in-bedroom-n-2.webp)


## 8. Anything Irreplaceable

Family photos, heirlooms, and sentimental items that can't be replaced should travel with you. Moving trucks, while safe, aren't guaranteed against every scenario.
