August is one of the busiest moving months in Miami-Dade County. Back-to-school transitions, lease turnovers, and family relocations all pile up at once. If your household includes original paintings, sculptures, or framed prints, that rush adds an extra layer of risk. Here is how to protect your art collection during a late-summer move.

## Why Art Needs Special Attention in August

High heat and humidity are the two biggest threats to artwork during a South Florida move. Canvas can warp, paper can buckle, and adhesive on frames can soften when temperatures inside a parked truck climb past 130 degrees. Afternoon thunderstorms add moisture exposure during loading and unloading.

Professional [**art moving**](/art-moving) crews plan around these conditions. They use climate-appropriate materials and schedule transport windows that minimize heat and rain exposure.

## 6 Strategies for Moving Art in Late Summer

### 1. Get Appraisals and Photograph Every Piece

![Close Up Of A Person Preparing A Package 2](/images/blog/2029/08/back-to-school-art-moving-strategies-in-miami/close-up-of-a-person-preparing-a-package-2.webp)


Before anything gets wrapped, document what you own. Take high-resolution photos of the front, back, and any existing damage. If pieces are valuable, get current appraisals updated. This documentation is essential for insurance claims if something goes wrong.

### 2. Schedule Early Morning Moves

Book your move to start at 7 or 8 a.m. The goal is to have all artwork loaded and in transit before the worst midday heat and the typical 2-4 p.m. storm window. Even a two-hour head start makes a significant difference.

### 3. Use Custom Crating for High-Value Pieces

Standard moving blankets and bubble wrap work for many household items, but original oil paintings, sculptures, and mixed-media pieces need custom wooden crates lined with acid-free materials. The crate absorbs shocks and creates a buffer against temperature swings during transport.

### 4. Control Humidity During Transport

Ask your moving crew about climate-controlled truck options. If that is not available, silica gel packets placed inside crates and wrapped bundles help absorb excess moisture. Avoid storing artwork overnight in a non-climate-controlled truck or storage unit.

### 5. Separate Glass from Frames When Possible

Glass-fronted frames are prone to cracking during moves. If the glass can be safely removed and wrapped separately, it reduces the chance of shards damaging the artwork underneath. Use painter's tape in an X pattern across glass that stays in the frame to limit shattering.

### 6. Plan Around Peak-Season Demand

August is a high-demand month for movers across Miami. Book your art moving service at least three to four weeks in advance to lock in your preferred date. Last-minute bookings during peak season often mean limited crew availability and higher rates.
