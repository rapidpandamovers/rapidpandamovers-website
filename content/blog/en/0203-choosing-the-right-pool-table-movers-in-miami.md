# Choosing the Right Pool Table Movers in Miami

Your pool table is probably one of the heaviest and most expensive pieces of furniture in your home. A quality slate table can cost $2,000 to $10,000 or more, and the wrong movers can turn that investment into a repair bill in a single afternoon. Miami has plenty of moving companies, but not all of them have the tools, training, or experience to handle a pool table properly. Here's how to find movers who actually know what they're doing.

## Look for Pool Table-Specific Experience

General movers can handle couches, dressers, and boxes. Pool tables are a different category entirely. When you're interviewing moving companies, ask these specific questions:

![A Person Arranging Billiard Balls On A B 3](/images/blog/2025/04/choosing-the-right-pool-table-movers-in-miami/a-person-arranging-billiard-balls-on-a-b-3.webp)


- **How many pool tables have you moved this year?** A company that moves pool tables regularly will have a number ready. If they hesitate or say "a few," that's a red flag.
- **Do you disassemble and reassemble the table?** The right answer is always yes. Any company that offers to move your table in one piece doesn't understand pool table construction.
- **Do you have slate jacks and pool table dollies?** These are specialized tools. General furniture dollies aren't designed for 250-pound slate pieces.
- **Do you level the table after reassembly?** Leveling is half the job. A table that's reassembled but not leveled is a table that doesn't play right.

## Check for Insurance and Licensing

In Florida, moving companies must be registered with the Florida Department of Agriculture and Consumer Services. They need an active license and proof of insurance. For pool table moves specifically, ask about:

- **Cargo insurance**: This covers damage to your table during transport. General liability isn't enough.
- **Workers' compensation**: Pool table moving is physically demanding. If an uninsured worker gets hurt in your home, you could be liable.
- **Bonding**: A bonded company provides an extra layer of financial protection.

Don't just take their word for it. Ask for certificate numbers and verify them online.

## Miami-Specific Considerations

Pool table moving in Miami comes with challenges you won't find in other cities:

- **Condo and high-rise logistics**: Buildings in Brickell, Edgewater, and Miami Beach often require freight elevator reservations, certificate of insurance (COI) naming the building, and specific move-in windows. Your movers should know this process and handle the paperwork.
- **Humidity management**: Miami's average humidity hovers around 75%, and it can spike much higher during summer months. Movers need to protect slate and felt from moisture during transport, even on a 20-minute drive.
- **Tight spaces**: Many Miami condos have narrower hallways and doorways than suburban homes. Experienced [**Pool Table Moving**](/miami-pool-table-moving) crews measure access points before moving day to avoid surprises.

## Read Reviews, But Read Them Carefully

Online reviews are helpful, but look for details. A five-star review that says "great movers!" tells you less than a four-star review that describes how the crew handled slate reassembly. Look for reviews that mention:

![A Row Of Empty Billiard Tables In A Diml 1](/images/blog/2025/04/choosing-the-right-pool-table-movers-in-miami/a-row-of-empty-billiard-tables-in-a-diml-1.webp)


- Pool table or billiard table moves specifically
- The leveling and setup process
- How the crew handled tight spaces or stairs
- Whether the table played correctly after the move

## Get Multiple Quotes, But Compare Apples to Apples

Pool table moving prices in Miami typically range from $300 to $600 for a local move, depending on the table size, number of floors, and distance. When comparing quotes, make sure each one includes:

- Full disassembly and reassembly
- Felt removal and reinstallation (or re-felting if needed)
- Precision leveling with a machinist's level
- Padding and wrapping materials
- Any building access fees or elevator reservations

A lowball quote that skips leveling or charges extra for felt removal isn't actually cheaper.
