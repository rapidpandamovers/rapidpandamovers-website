# Moving Outdoor Furniture and Heavy Patio Sets

Outdoor furniture in South Florida takes a beating from sun, salt air, and humidity year-round. By the time you're ready to move, those patio pieces have been through a lot. But they're also some of the heaviest, most awkward items in your home, and getting a cast aluminum dining set or teak sectional from a Coconut Grove backyard into a moving truck takes real planning.

## Cast Iron and Cast Aluminum Sets

Cast iron patio furniture can weigh 50-80 pounds per chair and 200+ pounds for a table. Cast aluminum is lighter but still bulky. These pieces don't fold, don't stack well, and have legs that catch on everything. We disassemble what we can, removing tabletops from pedestal bases and taking apart multi-piece seating groups. Hardware goes into labeled bags taped to each piece. Wrought iron scrollwork bends easier than you'd expect, so every piece gets blanket-wrapped with cardboard edge protection.

## Teak and Hardwood Furniture

Teak is popular in Miami for its weather resistance, but it's also extremely heavy. A teak dining table for eight can weigh over 150 pounds. Teak oil finishes dry out in the Florida sun, making surfaces prone to splitting along grain lines if they take a hard bump. We wrap teak in moving blankets and avoid stacking anything on top of flat surfaces. For storage or long-distance moves, we apply a coat of teak oil before wrapping to prevent further drying during transit.

## Outdoor Sectionals and Cushions

Modular patio sectionals from Restoration Hardware or Pottery Barn are popular in Miami outdoor living spaces. The frames need to be separated into individual sections. Cushions should be bagged in plastic, because Miami humidity means mold can start growing on damp fabric within 48 hours inside a sealed truck. We use large vacuum storage bags for cushions to reduce volume and keep moisture out. Frames get blanket-wrapped and loaded standing on their feet when possible.

## Grills and Smokers

Built-in grills stay put, but freestanding units come with you. A Weber Genesis or Traeger pellet grill weighs 100-175 pounds. We disconnect propane tanks (which ride in the cab, never the cargo area), clean out grease traps, remove grates and side tables, and wrap the unit in blankets. For kamado-style grills like Big Green Egg, the ceramic body is both heavy and fragile. A large Big Green Egg weighs about 160 pounds, and a crack in the ceramic shell means replacement, not repair. We use padded strapping and custom bracing for these.

## Fire Pits and Water Features

Gas fire pits need to be disconnected from fuel lines by a licensed professional. Stone or concrete fire pits that aren't built-in still weigh 200-600 pounds. We use furniture dollies and sometimes a two-man carry with lifting straps. Fountains and water features get drained completely, dried out for 24 hours if possible, and wrapped in waterproof padding. Pump mechanisms ship separately.

## Cleaning Before the Move

Outdoor furniture that's been sitting on a Hialeah patio collects pollen, mold, bird droppings, and salt residue. We strongly recommend pressure washing or wiping down pieces before moving day. Dirty furniture contaminates everything it touches in the truck, and wrapping grimy items in clean blankets is a waste of protective materials.
