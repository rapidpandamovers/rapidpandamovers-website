Welcome to your January guide for moving to Miami Springs! Whether you're relocating from within South Florida or making a bigger move this winter, getting to know your new community will help your move go well.

## Why Choose Miami Springs?

Miami Springs stands out as one of Miami-Dade County's most appealing locations. Founded in 1926 by aviation pioneer Glenn Curtiss, this historic community is known for its Mediterranean Revival architecture, mature oak-lined streets, and genuine small-town feel just minutes from Miami International Airport.

![Art Deco Hotel In Miami S South Beach Wi 2](/images/blog/2025/02/miami-springs-insights-tips-for-smooth-relocation/art-deco-hotel-in-miami-s-south-beach-wi-2.webp)


The area attracts families, professionals, and retirees alike, thanks to its quality of life, convenient access to major employment centers, and excellent amenities.

### Location and Accessibility

One of Miami Springs's biggest advantages is its central location. The Palmetto Expressway (SR 826) runs along the western edge, while the Airport Expressway (SR 112) provides quick access to Miami Beach. You're just 10 minutes from MIA, 20 minutes from Downtown Miami, and close to Hialeah, Doral, and Coral Gables. The Miami Springs Circle serves as the town center, with the Metrorail's Tri-Rail station nearby for commuters.

### Community and Lifestyle

Miami Springs residents enjoy a tight-knit community atmosphere. The Miami Springs Golf & Country Club has been a local landmark since the 1920s. Families gather at Curtiss Parkway Park for sports and the public pool, while the Miami Springs Community Center hosts events year-round. January brings perfect weather for the weekly farmers market and outdoor dining along the Circle.

## Neighborhoods to Consider

When planning your move to Miami Springs, consider these distinct areas. The Circle District around Westward Drive offers walkable restaurants and shops. The Country Club section near the golf course features larger lots and classic 1940s homes. The Bird Road corridor provides easy highway access. North of the canal, you'll find quieter residential streets with original Curtiss-era architecture.

### Choosing Your Perfect Spot

Consider these factors:

- **Proximity to work and schools**: Factor in your daily commute
- **Local amenities**: Parks, shopping, dining, and entertainment options
- **Property types**: Single-family homes, condos, townhouses, or apartments
- **Community vibe**: Family-oriented, young professional, or mixed demographics

## Moving to Miami Springs in January

January is an excellent time to consider your move. The winter weather in South Florida provides mild temperatures and lower humidity, ideal for the moving process.

### Timing Your Move

When planning your relocation, consider:

- **Best moving days**: Weekdays often offer better availability and rates
- **Weather considerations**: January averages 68-76°F with low humidity, perfect for moving day
- **Local events**: Check for any street closures or community events that might affect your move

## Essential Services to Locate

As a new Miami Springs resident, you'll want to find these local services:

- **Healthcare**: Palmetto General Hospital is 5 minutes away in Hialeah; Baptist Health has facilities nearby
- **Schools**: Miami Springs Elementary, Miami Springs Middle, and Hialeah-Miami Lakes Senior High serve the area; private options include Blessed Trinity Catholic School
- **Shopping**: Publix on Curtiss Parkway for groceries; Westland Mall in Hialeah and Miami International Mall in Doral for retail
- **Recreation**: Miami Springs Golf Course, the city pool, and multiple parks including Stafford Park and Veterans Park
