You're tired of the bustle in South Beach, looking for somewhere quieter but still close to Miami Beach amenities. Bay Harbor Islands offers exactly that: a small-town feel tucked between Bal Harbour and Surfside, with Kane Concourse shopping at your doorstep and the beach just minutes away. Here's what life really looks like after you make the move.

## Why Choose Bay Harbor Islands?

Bay Harbor Islands consists of two man-made islands connected by a short bridge, creating one of Miami-Dade's most walkable communities. The town spans just 0.4 square miles, which means you'll recognize your neighbors at Bagel Time or walking along the bay.

![Loc A Stunning Aerial View Of Key West Showc 1](/images/blog/2026/10/life-in-bay-harbor-islands-what-to-expect-after-moving/loc-a-stunning-aerial-view-of-key-west-showc-1.webp)


The community draws families looking for Ruth K. Broad Bay Harbor K-8 Center (an A-rated school), professionals who want a short commute to Brickell or Aventura, and retirees seeking a peaceful waterfront lifestyle without giving up urban conveniences.

### Location and Accessibility

Bay Harbor Islands sits between Bal Harbour to the north and Surfside to the south, with Indian Creek separating you from Miami Beach proper. The 96th Street causeway connects you to I-95 in under 10 minutes, making Downtown Miami accessible in 20-25 minutes during off-peak hours. Miami International Airport is about 30 minutes away.

Bal Harbour Shops, one of the country's top luxury shopping destinations, is a five-minute walk across Collins Avenue. Aventura Mall, the largest mall in Florida, is just 10 minutes north on Biscayne Boulevard.

### Community and Lifestyle

Bay Harbor Islands runs community events throughout the year, from holiday celebrations in the town center to summer concerts. The Bay Harbor Islands Community Center hosts fitness classes, youth programs, and senior activities. September brings cooler mornings perfect for waterfront walks along the bay, and the rainy season starts winding down, making outdoor activities more predictable.

## Island Layout and Housing

Bay Harbor Islands divides into East Island and West Island, each with a distinct character. East Island offers more single-family homes with private docks, perfect for boaters. West Island has more mid-rise condos and apartments, many with bay views and building amenities like pools and fitness centers.

### Housing Options

**East Island:** Mostly single-family homes, many built in the 1950s-1960s with recent renovations. Expect prices from $1.5M to $5M+ for waterfront properties. Rentals are limited but occasionally available in the $5,000-8,000/month range.

**West Island:** Mix of condos and apartments, from modest units to luxury buildings. Condo prices range from $400K to $1.5M. Rentals run $2,500-5,500/month depending on size and views.

**Kane Concourse:** The main commercial strip runs through the islands, with ground-floor retail and apartments above. Living here puts you steps from restaurants like Carpaccio or Shuckers waterfront bar.

## Moving to Bay Harbor Islands in September

September marks the tail end of hurricane season, so keep an eye on weather forecasts when scheduling your move. The upside: summer's peak heat starts to ease, and the afternoon thunderstorms become less frequent.

### Timing Your Move

**Best moving days:** Weekdays offer better availability and lower rates. Avoid the last weekend of the month when many leases turn over.

**Building rules:** If you're moving into a condo, contact the management office at least 48 hours in advance. Many Bay Harbor Islands buildings require elevator reservations, COI (Certificate of Insurance) from your moving company, and designated move-in hours (often 9am-5pm weekdays only).

**Parking:** Street parking on the islands is limited. Coordinate with your building or HOA about where the moving truck can stage.

## Services and Amenities

As a new Bay Harbor Islands resident, here's where to find what you need:

**Healthcare:** Mount Sinai Medical Center in Miami Beach is about 15 minutes south. Aventura Hospital is 10 minutes north. You'll find dentists, urgent care, and specialty offices along Biscayne Boulevard in North Miami Beach.

**Schools:** Ruth K. Broad Bay Harbor K-8 Center serves the islands and consistently earns A ratings. For high school, students attend North Miami Beach Senior High or can apply to magnet programs throughout Miami-Dade.

**Groceries:** Publix in Surfside is the closest full-service supermarket, about five minutes away. Whole Foods is at Aventura Mall. For specialty items, Epicure in Miami Beach (about 10 minutes) stocks gourmet and kosher products.

**Recreation:** The town maintains several small parks, including Broadmoor Park with tennis courts and playgrounds. The beach at Surfside is a quick bike ride or drive. For boaters, several marinas in the area offer slip rentals.
