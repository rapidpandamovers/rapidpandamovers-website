The Village of Pinecrest sits in South Miami-Dade County, roughly 15 miles south of Downtown Miami. With about 19,000 residents and a reputation for some of the best public schools in the state, Pinecrest draws families who want a quieter, greener setting while staying connected to the broader metro area.

If you are planning a November move, here is what to expect as a newcomer.

## What Makes Pinecrest Different

Pinecrest is not a neighborhood within Miami. It is an incorporated village with its own government, police force, and municipal services. That distinction matters because it means local decisions are made by a five-member village council rather than the larger Miami-Dade County bureaucracy.

![A Luxury Pickup Truck Parked In Front Of 4](/images/blog/2029/11/starting-fresh-in-pinecrest-a-newcomers-guide/a-luxury-pickup-truck-parked-in-front-of-4.webp)


The village is known for three things above all else: schools, green space, and large residential lots. Homes here sit on half-acre to full-acre properties, many shaded by mature oaks and tropical trees. The result is a canopy-covered community that feels more like a rural suburb than anything 15 miles from a major downtown.

### Location and Commute

US Route 1 (locally called Pinecrest Parkway) forms the village's eastern border and serves as the main north-south artery. From Pinecrest, you can reach Downtown Miami in about 25 to 30 minutes via US-1. The Dadeland South Metrorail station sits just north of the village boundary, providing rail access to downtown and the airport.

Pinecrest does not have its own commercial district with high-rises or major shopping centers. For daily errands, most residents drive to nearby Dadeland Mall, the shops along US-1, or South Miami's Sunset Drive. The average commute for Pinecrest residents is about 27 minutes.

### Schools

Education is the primary draw for families moving to Pinecrest. The public schools consistently rank among the top in Florida:

- **Pinecrest Elementary**: One of the highest-rated elementary schools in the state
- **Palmetto Elementary**: Another strong option in the public system
- **Palmetto Middle School**: Feeds into the area's top high school
- **Miami Palmetto Senior High**: Known for strong academics and extracurriculars

Private school options include Westminster Christian School, Gulliver Preparatory School, and Palmer Trinity School, all within a short drive.

### Pinecrest Gardens

Pinecrest Gardens is the village's cultural centerpiece. This 14-acre botanical garden and event space was formerly the Parrot Jungle tourist attraction. Today it features over 1,000 varieties of tropical plants, a splash playground for children, an amphitheater for concerts and events, and a petting zoo.

The gardens host a popular farmers market on Sundays and seasonal events throughout the year. It is one of the first places newcomers visit and a regular gathering spot for families.

## Neighborhoods and Housing

Pinecrest is primarily single-family residential. You will not find high-rise condos or large apartment complexes here. Homes range from renovated mid-century ranch houses to modern custom estates.

The area near Pinecrest Gardens and along Red Road tends to attract families looking for walkable access to the park and proximity to Pinecrest Elementary. Streets further south and west offer even larger lots and more privacy.

### What to Expect on the Market

Pinecrest is an upscale market. Median home prices exceed $1 million, and many listings in prime areas reach well above that. Property taxes reflect the higher values, but the village's low density and excellent schools help maintain strong home values.

### Choosing Your Spot

Consider these factors:

- **School assignments**: Pinecrest Elementary and Palmetto Elementary serve different zones within the village. Verify which school zone a property falls in before making an offer
- **Lot size and privacy**: Larger lots along SW 67th Avenue and south of SW 104th Street offer more seclusion
- **Flood zones**: Some areas of Pinecrest fall in FEMA flood zones. Check flood maps and factor in flood insurance costs
- **Commute route**: Residents on the eastern side of the village have quicker access to US-1, while western properties are closer to the Palmetto Expressway

## Moving to Pinecrest in November

November is one of the best months for a South Florida move. Daytime temperatures settle into the upper 70s and low 80s with low humidity. Hurricane season is winding down, and the holiday rush has not yet started, so moving companies tend to have better availability.

### Timing Your Move

- **Weekdays** offer lower rates and more scheduling flexibility
- **Morning starts** let your crew finish before any stray afternoon showers
- **Check the village calendar**: Pinecrest Gardens events can generate local traffic on Red Road. The Sunday farmers market draws a crowd as well

## Essential Services to Locate

As a new Pinecrest resident, here are the essentials to set up:

- **Healthcare**: Baptist Health South Florida campuses are nearby, and Doctors Hospital in Coral Gables is about 10 minutes north
- **Groceries**: Publix on US-1, Whole Foods at Dadeland, and several specialty markets serve the area
- **Parks**: Pinecrest Gardens (botanical garden and events), Evelyn Greer Park (sports fields and community center), Suniland Park, and Coral Pine Park
- **Dining**: Pho 79, Original Big Tomato, Trattoria Luna, and the Cheese Course are local favorites along the US-1 corridor

## FAQ

### Is Pinecrest good for families?

Pinecrest is one of the most family-oriented communities in Miami-Dade County. The village has received the "Playful City USA" designation five times. Between the top-rated schools, park system, and low-density residential setting, it consistently ranks as one of the best places to raise children in South Florida.

### How far is Pinecrest from the beach?

Key Biscayne is about 20 to 25 minutes northeast. Miami Beach is roughly 30 to 40 minutes depending on traffic via US-1 and the MacArthur or Julia Tuttle Causeway.

### Are there apartments in Pinecrest?

Very few. Pinecrest is almost entirely single-family homes. If you need apartment living, nearby South Miami and Dadeland offer more rental options within a short drive of the village.
