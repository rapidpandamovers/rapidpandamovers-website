Saying goodbye to Miami is bittersweet. After years of sunshine, Cuban coffee, and beach weekends, you are heading somewhere new. This checklist ensures nothing falls through the cracks during your [**long distance move**](/miami-long-distance-moving).

## 8 Weeks Before: Start Planning

### Get Binding Moving Estimates
Contact three moving companies for binding estimates. A binding estimate locks in your price and prevents surprise charges at delivery. For Miami to New York, expect $3,000-$6,000 for a 2-bedroom home.

### Begin Decluttering
Shipping weight costs money. Every 100 pounds you eliminate saves $50-100 on a long distance move. Start with storage areas, then tackle closets.

### Research Your Destination
If moving to a cold climate, you need winter gear. Start shopping now while Miami stores carry limited selections.

## 6 Weeks Before: Administrative Tasks

### Florida Residency Changes
- **Vehicle registration**: You have 30 days to register in most states
- **Driver's license**: Typically 30-60 days to obtain new license
- **Voter registration**: Re-register at your new address
- **Florida homestead exemption**: Notify the Property Appraiser if you own property

### Notify Important Parties
- Employer and HR department
- Banks and credit card companies
- Insurance providers (auto, health, renters/homeowners)
- Subscription services
- Doctor and dentist offices (request records)

## 4 Weeks Before: Logistics

### Book Your Moving Date
December is moderately busy. Book at least 4 weeks ahead for weekday moves. Weekend dates fill faster.

### Arrange Travel
If driving, plan your route. Miami to Atlanta is 10 hours. Miami to New York is 20 hours with overnight stops.

If flying, book tickets early. December holiday travel means higher prices and limited availability.

### Handle Your Miami Lease or Sale
- Notify landlord (typically 30-60 days required)
- Schedule final walkthrough
- Request security deposit return process

## 2 Weeks Before: Final Prep

### Winterproofing for Cold Destinations
If moving north, prepare items for temperature change:
- **Electronics**: Let them acclimate to room temperature before plugging in
- **Plants**: Most tropical houseplants will not survive cold climates without indoor placement
- **Wood furniture**: Humidity changes cause warping - condition with wood oil before shipping
- **Car**: Antifreeze, winter tires, and cold-weather fluids

### Confirm Utilities
- Schedule Miami utility disconnection for day after move
- Set up utilities at destination (schedule activation for day before arrival)
- Cancel local services (gym, lawn care, pest control)

## 1 Week Before: Pack and Organize

### Pack Essentials Box
Keep with you, not on the truck:
- 3-4 days of clothes
- Medications
- Important documents
- Phone chargers
- Toiletries
- Snacks for travel

### Florida-Specific Items to Take
- **Beach gear**: You will miss it
- **Portable AC or fans**: Useful in shoulder seasons anywhere
- **Hurricane shutters/preparation knowledge**: Sadly, not needed up north

## Moving Day

### Final Miami Walkthrough
- Check all closets, attic, garage
- Take photos for security deposit purposes
- Return keys to landlord or lock in lockbox

### Say Your Goodbyes
Stop by your favorite cafecito spot one last time. Wave to neighbors. Take a final drive down Ocean Drive or Brickell.
