Moving into a new home in Miami, Coral Gables, or anywhere in South Florida means inheriting an unknown security history. Previous owners, their friends, contractors, cleaning crews, and real estate agents may all have copies of your keys. Before you unpack that first box, take these five steps to protect your new home and family.

## 1. Change All Locks Immediately

You have no idea who has keys to your new place. Previous owners, their kids, dog walkers, housekeepers, contractors, and multiple real estate agents may all have copies floating around. Rekeying existing locks costs $15-25 per lock and takes about 15 minutes. Full lock replacement runs $75-150 per door but lets you upgrade to smart locks with keypad entry. Either option beats wondering who might walk in.

## 2. Check All Windows and Doors

Ensure every entry point locks properly. Replace or repair faulty locks right away. In Florida, impact-resistant windows and doors are common due to hurricane requirements, so check that all hurricane shutters or impact glass latches work correctly too.

## 3. Install or Update Security System

Get a security system active before your first night in the home. Options range from DIY smart devices like Ring or SimpliSafe ($100-300 for a starter kit) to professionally monitored systems through ADT or Brinks ($30-50/month). Many Miami-Dade gated communities already include security patrol, but interior alarms add protection if someone gets past the gate. Video doorbells have become standard in Florida and help identify package thieves and solicitors.

## 4. Add Exterior Lighting

Motion-sensor lights deter intruders and help you move around safely at night. Focus on entry points first: front door, back door, garage, and any side gates. Solar-powered LED fixtures work well in sunny South Florida and keep working even during power outages. Budget about $20-50 per fixture for decent quality units at Home Depot or Lowes.

## 5. Test Smoke and CO Detectors

Replace batteries in all detectors. If units are old, replace them entirely. Florida law requires working smoke alarms in all homes, and Miami-Dade County Fire Rescue recommends testing them monthly, especially during hurricane season when power outages can drain backup batteries.
