# The Cost of Piano Moving: What to Expect

Piano moving costs more than moving furniture. That surprises some people, but once you understand what goes into a piano move, the pricing makes sense. The specialized equipment, trained crews, and insurance required to safely transport a 500-pound precision instrument aren't the same as what's needed for a couch. Here's a breakdown of what drives piano moving costs in the Miami area and what you should expect to pay.

## Average Piano Moving Costs in Miami

Pricing varies based on the type of piano, the distance, and the difficulty of the move. Here are typical ranges for local moves within Miami-Dade and Broward counties:

| Piano Type | Local Move Cost |
|-----------|----------------|
| Spinet or console upright | $200 - $350 |
| Studio or full-size upright | $300 - $500 |
| Baby grand (under 6') | $400 - $600 |
| Grand piano (6' - 7') | $500 - $800 |
| Concert grand (7'+) | $700 - $1,200+ |

These ranges assume a ground-floor-to-ground-floor move with standard access. Several factors can push the cost higher.

## What Affects the Price

### Stairs

Stairs are the single biggest cost factor after piano type. Each flight of stairs adds labor time, physical risk, and sometimes additional crew members. Most piano movers charge an extra $50 to $100 per flight of stairs. A third-floor walkup in Miami Beach with narrow stairs can add $200 or more to the base price.

### Long Carry Distance

If the truck can't park close to the door, the crew has to carry or dolly the piano over a longer distance. Driveways, courtyards, and parking structures common in Miami can extend the carry. Long carry fees typically kick in after 50 to 75 feet and add $50 to $150.

### Elevator-Only Buildings

High-rise moves require freight elevator coordination, loading dock access, and building insurance documentation. These add logistical overhead. Some piano movers include high-rise fees of $50 to $200, depending on the building's requirements.

### Disassembly and Reassembly

Grand pianos require leg removal and piano board use. This is standard and included in most grand piano quotes. If any additional disassembly is needed, like removing the keyboard to fit through a narrow doorway, that adds time and cost.

### Distance

Local moves within Miami-Dade are typically priced as flat rates based on the factors above. Moves to Broward or Palm Beach counties add mileage fees. Long-distance moves to other parts of Florida or out of state are priced per mile, with typical rates of $1.50 to $3.00 per mile on top of the base handling fee.

### Specialty Situations

Crane lifts for upper-floor moves where the elevator won't work cost $500 to $2,000+ depending on the crane size and setup. Moves requiring window or door removal add time and labor.

## Why Piano Moving Costs What It Does

### Trained Crew

A piano moving crew isn't a couple of guys with a truck. They're trained specifically in piano handling techniques, disassembly procedures, and safe lifting mechanics. That training and experience is reflected in the cost.

### Specialized Equipment

Piano boards, heavy-duty dollies, stair-climbing rigs, floor runners, and multi-layer padding systems are all tools that general movers don't carry. A single piano board costs several hundred dollars, and the full equipment set represents a significant investment.

### Insurance

Carrying insurance adequate to cover instruments worth $5,000 to $100,000+ is expensive. A reputable [**Piano Moving**](/piano-moving) company maintains cargo insurance, general liability, and workers' compensation. These premiums are built into the service cost.

### Risk

Moving a piano is physically demanding and carries real risk for both the instrument and the crew. A dropped piano is a catastrophe. The pricing reflects the skill, caution, and liability management required.

## How to Get an Accurate Quote

The best way to get a reliable quote is to provide your mover with:

![Classic Wooden Upright Piano Displaying  3](/images/blog/2028/10/the-cost-of-piano-moving-what-to-expect/classic-wooden-upright-piano-displaying--3.webp)


- Piano type and approximate size
- Pickup address with floor level and any stairs
- Destination address with floor level and any stairs
- Photos of doorways, hallways, and any tight spots
- Building requirements (for high-rise moves)
- Preferred date and time

Most reputable piano movers in Miami will provide a firm quote once they have this information. Be cautious of any company that gives a vague estimate without asking about access details, because that estimate will change on moving day.

## Saving Money vs. Saving Your Piano

The temptation to save a few hundred dollars by using general movers or DIY help is strong. But piano repairs start at several hundred dollars for minor issues and climb into thousands quickly. A scratched finish, cracked soundboard, or broken leg costs far more to fix than the difference between a budget move and a professional one.
