## About Miami Gardens

Miami Gardens is the third-largest city in Miami-Dade County and one of the largest majority-Black cities in the United States. With approximately 111,000 residents, this vibrant city offers affordable housing, strong community character, and easy access to major employment centers.

## Moving Tips for Miami Gardens

### Best Time to Move

- **Fall/Winter (October-March)**: Best weather for moving, lower humidity means less sweating during load-in
- **Avoid**: NFL game days (Sundays September through January) and major Hard Rock Stadium events like the Orange Bowl or concerts. Traffic on NW 27th Avenue and NW 199th Street becomes gridlocked.
- **Pro tip**: Check the Dolphins and Hurricanes schedules before booking your move date

### Local Considerations

- **Traffic**: Plan around stadium events. Game days affect a 2-mile radius around Hard Rock Stadium, including residential streets.
- **Flood zones**: Some areas near the C-9 Canal require flood insurance. Ask about FEMA zones before signing a lease.
- **HOAs**: Rolling Oaks and several newer developments have mandatory associations with monthly fees ranging from $50-$200.
- **Hurricane prep**: Miami Gardens is inland, which means less storm surge risk than coastal areas, but you will still need hurricane shutters or impact windows.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Miami Gardens a safe place to live?

Like any large city, safety varies by neighborhood. Areas near Norwood and Rolling Oaks tend to have lower crime rates. The city has invested in community policing and neighborhood watch programs. Check the Miami-Dade County crime map for specific addresses before signing a lease.

### How much does it cost to live in Miami Gardens?

Expect to pay $1,800-$2,400/month for a 2-bedroom apartment and $350,000-$450,000 for a single-family home. This makes Miami Gardens significantly more affordable than Aventura ($3,500+ for similar apartments) or Miami Beach. Property taxes run about 2% of assessed value.

### What are the schools like?

Miami Gardens has above-average schools for Miami-Dade County. Carol City Senior High and Miami Norland Senior High are the main public high schools. Florida Memorial University offers local higher education. Many families also look at charter and magnet school options through the county.

### How is the commute from Miami Gardens?

Downtown Miami is 25-30 minutes without traffic (add 15-20 minutes during rush hour). Fort Lauderdale is 20-25 minutes north. The Tri-Rail station in Opa-locka is about 10 minutes away for commuters heading to both cities.
