Planning a move in Miami? Unfortunately, moving scams are common in South Florida, especially during peak season from April through September. Protect yourself by learning these warning signs of fraudulent movers before you sign any contracts.

## 1. No Physical Address

Legitimate companies have real offices you can visit. Here in Miami-Dade, a reputable mover should have a verifiable location you can drive to, not just a P.O. box. If they won't share their address, look elsewhere.

![A Red Flag Signals Dangerous Conditions  3](/images/blog/2028/09/7-indicators-your-mover-might-be-a-scam/a-red-flag-signals-dangerous-conditions--3.webp)


## 2. Unusually Low Estimates

If one quote is dramatically lower than others, it's likely a bait-and-switch. Moving from Brickell to Coral Gables costs real money for labor, fuel, and insurance. A quote that seems too good to be true usually is, and your final bill will be much higher than promised.

## 3. Large Cash Deposit Requests

Professional movers don't demand large cash payments upfront. This is a common scam tactic, so walk away immediately.

![Control Tower With A Clear Red Warning F 1](/images/blog/2028/09/7-indicators-your-mover-might-be-a-scam/control-tower-with-a-clear-red-warning-f-1.webp)


## 4. No Written Estimate

Everything should be in writing: the estimate, terms, insurance coverage, and all fees. Verbal agreements protect no one.

## 5. No Insurance Information Available

Licensed movers carry insurance and can prove it. If they can't or won't provide documentation, they may not be legitimate.

## 6. Unmarked Trucks

Professional companies use marked, well-maintained vehicles. Unmarked trucks suggest unregistered or fly-by-night operations.

![Two Professional Movers Carrying Cardboa 2](/images/blog/2028/09/7-indicators-your-mover-might-be-a-scam/two-professional-movers-carrying-cardboa-2.webp)


## 7. Negative or No Reviews

Check Google, Yelp, and BBB for reviews from actual Miami customers. A pattern of complaints or a complete absence of reviews signals trouble ahead. Look for specific mentions of neighborhoods like Kendall, Aventura, or Coconut Grove to verify they actually work in your area.
