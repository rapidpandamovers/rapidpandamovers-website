# Moving a Snooker Table vs Standard Pool Table

Snooker tables and standard American pool tables look similar from across the room, but when it comes to moving them, the differences are significant. Snooker tables are bigger, heavier, and more demanding in every phase of the relocation process. If you own a snooker table in South Florida (and a growing number of residents do, thanks to the area's international community), here's what makes your move different from a standard pool table move.

## Size Differences

The most obvious difference is scale:

![A Man Setting Up Billiard Balls On A Poo 3](/images/blog/2026/10/moving-a-snooker-table-vs-standard-pool-table/a-man-setting-up-billiard-balls-on-a-poo-3.webp)


- **Standard 8-foot pool table**: 44 inches x 88 inches, playing surface 44" x 88"
- **Standard 9-foot pool table**: 50 inches x 100 inches
- **Full-size snooker table**: 69.5 inches x 140 inches (nearly 12 feet long)
- **10-foot snooker table**: 57 inches x 114 inches (a smaller but still substantial option)

A full-size snooker table is roughly 40% larger than a regulation 9-foot pool table. That extra size affects everything: the space needed for disassembly, the size of doorways and hallways it must pass through, the room dimensions required at the destination, and the truck space for transport.

## Weight Comparison

Snooker tables are dramatically heavier:

- **8-foot pool table**: 750-900 pounds
- **9-foot pool table**: 900-1,100 pounds
- **Full-size snooker table**: 1,200-1,500 pounds

The extra weight comes from the larger, thicker slate bed. A full-size snooker table typically uses a five-piece slate set, compared to the three-piece set in a standard pool table. Each snooker slate section weighs 200-300 pounds. Moving five of them requires more crew members and more time.

## Slate Configuration

This is where the [**Pool Table Moving**](/pool-table-moving) process diverges most:

![A Person Arranging Billiard Balls On A B 4](/images/blog/2026/10/moving-a-snooker-table-vs-standard-pool-table/a-person-arranging-billiard-balls-on-a-b-4.webp)


**Pool tables** usually have three slate sections that meet at two seams running across the width of the table. The three-piece system is well-established, and most movers are familiar with it.

**Snooker tables** can have three, four, or five slate sections depending on the size and manufacturer. The additional seams require more beeswax filling, more precise alignment, and more time during reassembly. Because snooker is played with smaller balls on tighter pockets, even minor imperfections at the slate seams can affect play.

## Pocket and Rail Differences

Snooker pockets are smaller and more precisely angled than pool pockets. The pocket facings (the curved portions at the pocket openings) are critical to the game's difficulty, and they must go back in the exact same position after a move. Our crew marks each pocket facing and rail with detailed labels before removal.

Snooker cushion rubber is also different. It's designed for the lighter, smaller snooker balls and has a lower profile than pool table cushion rubber. Mixing up rail positions during reassembly changes the bounce characteristics, which serious players notice immediately.

## Room Requirements

A full-size snooker table needs a much larger room:

![A Row Of Empty Billiard Tables In A Diml 5](/images/blog/2026/10/moving-a-snooker-table-vs-standard-pool-table/a-row-of-empty-billiard-tables-in-a-diml-5.webp)


| Table | With 58" cues | With 52" cues |
|-------|--------------|--------------|
| 9-foot pool | 14'2" x 18'2" | 13'6" x 17'6" |
| 12-foot snooker | 16'4" x 22'2" | 15'8" x 21'6" |

Many Miami homes don't have a room large enough for a full-size snooker table. We often see them in dedicated game rooms in larger homes across Coral Gables, Pinecrest, and Key Biscayne, or in commercial club spaces. Before scheduling a move, we measure the destination room to confirm it works.

## Crew and Timeline Differences

A standard pool table move typically requires 3-4 movers and 3-4 hours total. A full-size snooker table move needs:

- 4-5 experienced movers (the extra weight demands more hands)
- 5-7 hours total (more slate pieces to handle, more seams to fill, more surface area to level)
- A larger truck or van (the frame sections and slate pieces take up significantly more space)

## Climate Concerns for Both

Miami's humidity affects both table types equally. Slate is dimensionally stable in humid conditions, but the wooden frames, rails, and felt all respond to moisture changes. For snooker tables, the added concern is that the felt (often called "baize" in snooker terminology) is a different weave than pool table felt and can react differently to humidity. Keeping the destination room air-conditioned and at consistent humidity is important for both.
