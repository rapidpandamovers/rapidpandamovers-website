Moving day in Miami can get hectic fast, whether you're relocating from Brickell to Coral Gables or heading out to Kendall. Having these five supplies within arm's reach will save you time, stress, and frustration when things get busy.

## 1. Important Documents

Passports, IDs, birth certificates, lease/closing papers - keep these on your person or in your personal vehicle, never in a moving box. In South Florida's humidity, consider a waterproof folder or ziplock bag for extra protection.

![A Classic Hong Kong Tram Traverses Moder 2](/images/blog/2028/02/5-critical-supplies-to-keep-handy-when-moving/a-classic-hong-kong-tram-traverses-moder-2.webp)


## 2. Medications and First Aid

Current prescriptions, over-the-counter essentials, and any medical devices you might need in the next 24 hours stay with you.

## 3. Phone Chargers and Power Banks

Dead phones on moving day mean missed calls from movers, utility companies, and anyone trying to help coordinate. Miami traffic can cause delays, so staying connected with your moving crew is essential.

## 4. Basic Toolkit

Screwdriver set, pliers, box cutter, and hammer are all useful for last-minute needs at both locations. You'll use these more than you expect.

![Pink Moving Trucks Parked In A Chicago A 1](/images/blog/2028/02/5-critical-supplies-to-keep-handy-when-moving/pink-moving-trucks-parked-in-a-chicago-a-1.webp)


## 5. Cleaning Supplies

Paper towels, all-purpose cleaner, and trash bags make quick clean-ups easy during the transition at both old and new locations.
