Moving day leaves your home vulnerable in ways you might not consider. Doors stay propped open for hours. Strangers walk through your space. Boxes labeled "Electronics" or "Jewelry" advertise their contents. Here is how to stay secure throughout the moving process.

## Before Moving Day

At your current home, avoid advertising that you are moving. Stacked boxes visible through windows signal that valuables are being packed. Keep curtains closed in rooms where you have been packing.

At your new home, change the locks before moving day. You have no idea how many copies of keys exist from previous owners, contractors, and real estate agents. A locksmith can rekey all exterior doors in under an hour.

## Protect Valuables During the Move

Some items should never go on the moving truck. Keep these with you personally:

- Important documents like passports and birth certificates
- Jewelry and watches
- Cash and checkbooks
- Medication
- Laptop computers with sensitive data
- External hard drives with photos or financial records

These items ride in your car, not the moving truck. Even with reputable movers, keeping irreplaceable items under your direct control eliminates risk.

## Moving Day Security

Keep exterior doors closed between loads whenever possible. In Miami neighborhoods, an open door with no one watching invites trouble. Assign someone to stay near entry points during active loading and unloading.

Avoid labeling boxes with specific contents like "Master Bedroom Jewelry" or "Office Electronics." Use a coding system instead. Number boxes and keep a separate inventory list that only you have.

## At Your Old Home

Once everything is loaded, do a final walkthrough and lock up properly. Remove any hidden spare keys from under mats, fake rocks, or other common hiding spots. If you used a lockbox during the sale, ensure the real estate agent removes it promptly.

Cancel or transfer security monitoring services to your new address. An unmonitored alarm system at your old home does nothing, and you want protection at your new place immediately.

## At Your New Home

Before bringing in furniture, walk through every room checking windows and doors. Make sure all locks work properly. In South Florida's climate, sliding glass doors are common, and many come with basic locks that need upgrading.

If the previous owners left a garage door opener, reset the code immediately. Program your own remotes and delete any stored codes you cannot account for.

## After Settling In

Within the first week, introduce yourself to immediate neighbors. A neighbor who knows you belongs there will notice if someone else tries to enter. This simple connection adds a layer of security that technology cannot replace.

Update your address with banks, credit cards, and other financial institutions promptly. Mail forwarding catches most items, but identity thieves check mailboxes at recently-sold homes looking for forwarded statements.
