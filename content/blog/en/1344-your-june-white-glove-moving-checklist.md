You have spent years collecting original artwork, hand-crafted furniture, and family heirlooms. A standard moving crew with blankets and tape is not going to cut it. White glove moving exists for exactly this situation, and if you are planning a June move in South Florida, the stakes are even higher.

Summer heat, humidity, and afternoon storms can damage delicate items that regular movers might not think twice about. Here is your complete white glove moving checklist to make sure nothing gets overlooked.

## 8 Weeks Before Your June Move

### Schedule Your In-Home Consultation

A white glove move starts with a detailed walkthrough. Your move manager will:

- Catalog every item that needs special handling
- Identify pieces requiring custom crates (built on-site to exact dimensions)
- Note access challenges at both addresses (narrow staircases, low ceilings, gated entries)
- Provide a detailed, itemized estimate with no hidden fees

### Verify Your Insurance Coverage

Standard moving insurance covers items by weight, not value. For a white glove move, confirm:

- Full replacement value coverage for high-value items
- Separate riders for artwork, antiques, and jewelry
- Documentation requirements (appraisals, photos, receipts) your insurer needs before the move

### Reserve Climate-Controlled Storage if Needed

If your move-in date does not align with your move-out, you may need short-term storage. In South Florida's summer, standard storage units reach dangerous temperatures. Climate-controlled units keep conditions at 55-85 degrees with humidity control, which matters for:

- Oil paintings and framed art
- Wine collections
- Leather furniture
- Musical instruments
- Electronics and media collections

## 6 Weeks Before

### Coordinate Building Access at Both Locations

Miami high-rises in Brickell, Key Biscayne, and Coral Gables have strict move-in policies. Handle these now:

- Submit your moving company's Certificate of Insurance (COI)
- Reserve the freight elevator for your specific date and time window
- Confirm loading dock dimensions and truck height clearances
- Get written approval for any oversized items (pianos, safes, large sculptures)

### Create Your Priority Room Setup List

Decide which rooms get set up first at your new home. For white glove service, your crew will place furniture exactly where you want it and unpack boxes room by room. Having a priority list saves hours:

1. **Master bedroom**: You will want a made bed after a long moving day
2. **Kitchen**: Essentials unpacked so you can eat and drink that first night
3. **Bathrooms**: Towels, toiletries, and basics accessible immediately
4. **Living room**: Main furniture placed for a functional space on day one

## 4 Weeks Before

### Prepare High-Value Items for Documentation

Take detailed photos of every valuable item from multiple angles. This protects you in case of damage claims and helps your move manager plan handling:

- Photograph existing scratches, chips, or wear on furniture
- Document the condition of artwork, including frame corners and glass
- Record serial numbers on electronics
- List contents of jewelry boxes and display cases

### Arrange Specialty Services

Some items need handlers beyond your moving crew:

- **Piano tuning** scheduled for 2-3 weeks after the move (pianos need to acclimate to the new space)
- **Art hanging** by a professional installer with proper hardware for your new walls
- **Chandelier installation** by a licensed electrician
- **Wine storage setup** if you are moving a collection to a new cellar or cooler

## 2 Weeks Before

### Confirm Every Detail

White glove moves have many moving parts. Verify:

- [ ] Moving date, arrival time, and estimated completion time
- [ ] Custom crates are being built and will be ready
- [ ] Both buildings are confirmed for elevator and dock access
- [ ] Insurance paperwork is complete
- [ ] Climate-controlled truck is reserved (critical for South Florida summer)
- [ ] Unpacking scope is clearly agreed upon in writing

### Prepare Your Current Home

- Clear pathways through every room for the crew
- Remove wall hangings you are taking (patch holes if needed for your deposit)
- Empty safes and lock boxes; transport those items personally
- Disconnect and prepare electronics (label cables before unplugging)

## Moving Week

### The Day Before

- Walk through your home one final time with your move manager
- Confirm the weather forecast and discuss contingency plans for rain
- Set aside personal items you will transport yourself (medications, jewelry, important documents, laptops)
- Charge all devices and download offline entertainment for the kids

### Moving Day

- Your crew arrives with protective materials already prepared
- Custom crates are brought in and items are packed on-site
- Every piece of furniture is wrapped, padded, and secured
- Climate-controlled truck is loaded with heavier items on bottom, fragile items secured on top
- At the destination, your crew places every item according to your layout plan
- Unpacking begins room by room based on your priority list

### After the Move

- [ ] Do a detailed walkthrough with your move manager, checking items against the inventory
- [ ] Test all electronics and note any issues immediately
- [ ] Check furniture placement and request adjustments (included in white glove service)
- [ ] Allow 48 hours before hanging heavy artwork so walls can settle
- [ ] File any damage claims within the window specified in your contract

## Why June Moves in South Florida Need White Glove Care

June brings specific risks that white glove service addresses:

- **Humidity damage**: Premium wrapping materials create moisture barriers that standard blankets do not
- **Heat exposure**: Climate-controlled trucks keep temperatures stable during transport, protecting wood finishes and adhesives
- **Storm delays**: A dedicated move manager monitors weather and adjusts the timeline, rather than rushing through rain
- **Peak season logistics**: Reserved elevator times and pre-arranged building access prevent the delays that plague standard moves

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does white glove moving cost compared to standard moving?

White glove service typically costs 2-3 times more than a standard move because it includes custom crating, premium materials, a dedicated move manager, and full unpacking. The exact cost depends on the number of specialty items, distance, and scope of service. Request a detailed in-home estimate for accurate pricing.

### Is white glove moving worth it for a condo move?

If you have valuable artwork, antique furniture, or a wine collection, yes. The premium materials and climate-controlled transport protect items that standard moving cannot. Many Brickell and Key Biscayne condo owners choose white glove for the building compliance handling alone.

### How long does a white glove move take?

A white glove move takes longer than standard because of custom crating and careful unpacking. A typical 3-bedroom home takes 2-3 days total: one day for packing and crating, one day for transport and placement, and a partial day for unpacking and setup.
