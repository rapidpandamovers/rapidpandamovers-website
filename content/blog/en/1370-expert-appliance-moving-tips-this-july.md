July is one of the busiest months for moves in Miami-Dade County, and summer heat adds real risk to appliance relocations. Refrigerators left in a hot truck too long, washers that leak because drain lines were not properly disconnected, dryers with loose drums from rough handling: these problems are avoidable with the right preparation.

Here are the steps that matter most when moving appliances during the hottest month of the year.

## Prep Each Appliance Before Moving Day

Every major appliance needs specific preparation. Skipping these steps leads to damage, leaks, or costly repairs at your new home.

![A Clean Organized Laundry Room Featuring 3](/images/blog/2029/07/expert-appliance-moving-tips-this-july/a-clean-organized-laundry-room-featuring-3.webp)


### Refrigerator and Freezer

- **Defrost at least 24 hours ahead.** Unplug the unit and leave the doors open. Place towels around the base to catch meltwater.
- **Empty and clean the interior.** Remove all shelves and drawers. Wrap glass shelves separately.
- **Secure the doors.** Use appliance straps or bungee cords to keep doors from swinging open during transport.
- **Keep it upright.** Tilting a fridge more than 45 degrees can cause compressor oil to flow into the cooling lines. If it must be tilted, wait 24 hours before plugging it back in.

### Washer and Dryer

- **Disconnect water supply lines** and drain any remaining water from hoses.
- **Install transit bolts** on the washer drum if you still have them. These bolts lock the drum in place and prevent internal damage during transport.
- **Clean the lint trap** on the dryer and tape the door shut.
- **Bundle hoses and cords.** Tape power cords to the back of each unit so they do not drag or catch during loading.

### Dishwasher

- **Run an empty cycle** the night before to clear residual water.
- **Disconnect the water line** and drain hose. Stuff a towel inside to absorb any leftover moisture.
- **Secure the racks.** Remove or tape down the sliding racks so they do not shift during the move.

### Stove and Oven

- **Turn off the gas supply** for gas ranges and cap the line. If you are not comfortable doing this, hire a licensed technician.
- **Remove grates and burner caps.** Wrap them separately.
- **Tape the oven door closed** and remove any loose knobs.

## July-Specific Challenges in Miami

Summer appliance moves come with extra considerations that do not apply during cooler months.

### Heat Exposure

Appliances sitting in a hot moving truck can reach internal temperatures above 130 degrees. For refrigerators and freezers, prolonged heat exposure can damage seals and gaskets. Load appliances last and unload them first at the destination.

### Afternoon Storms

Miami's daily thunderstorms typically hit between 2 and 5 PM. Water and electronics do not mix. If you are moving a refrigerator, dishwasher, or any appliance with exposed electrical components, plan your loading window around the forecast.

### Humidity and Condensation

High humidity causes condensation to form on cold appliance surfaces. Wipe down appliances after unloading and before plugging them in. Moisture inside electrical connections can cause short circuits.

### Scheduling Around the Holiday

If your move falls near the Fourth of July weekend, book early. Moving crews fill up fast around holiday weekends, and last-minute scheduling often means higher rates or limited availability.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long should a fridge sit before plugging it in after a move?**
If the fridge stayed upright during transport, you can plug it in after about 2 hours to let the compressor oil settle. If it was tilted at any point, wait a full 24 hours.

![Stylish Commercial Washing Machines In A 4](/images/blog/2029/07/expert-appliance-moving-tips-this-july/stylish-commercial-washing-machines-in-a-4.webp)


**Can I move a washer without transit bolts?**
You can, but the drum may shift and damage the suspension system. If you lost the original bolts, order replacements from the manufacturer or use towels packed tightly around the drum as a temporary fix.

**Do movers reconnect gas lines?**
Most moving companies do not reconnect gas appliances due to liability. You will need a licensed plumber or gas technician for gas stoves and dryers.
