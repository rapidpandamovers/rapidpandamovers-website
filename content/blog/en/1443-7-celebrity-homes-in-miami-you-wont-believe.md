Miami has always attracted wealth, but the past few years have rewritten what "expensive" means in South Florida real estate. The tech billionaires arrived. The hedge fund managers followed. And Indian Creek Island, once a quiet enclave, became the most exclusive address in America. Here are seven celebrity properties that show just how high the stakes have climbed.

## 1. Jeff Bezos's Indian Creek Island Compound

The Amazon founder spent over $237 million acquiring three separate properties on Indian Creek Island, the 300-acre man-made island in Biscayne Bay that locals call "Billionaire Bunker." Bezos reportedly plans to demolish at least one existing mansion and build a custom mega-estate on the combined lots. The island has its own 13-officer police force, a single bridge entrance, and boat patrol. Moving anything onto Indian Creek requires coordination with island security, a ferry schedule for heavy equipment, and building management approval that can take weeks.

## 2. Mark Zuckerberg's Record-Breaking Purchase

The Meta CEO and his wife Priscilla Chan closed on an Indian Creek mansion for $170 million, setting a new Miami-Dade County record. The nearly 28,000-square-foot compound was designed by luxury architect Ferris Rafauli and sits on almost two acres with 200 feet of Biscayne Bay frontage. The property first listed at $200 million in late 2025. Zuckerberg joins Bezos on the island, putting two of the five wealthiest people in the world within walking distance of each other.

## 3. Ken Griffin's Star Island Holdings

The Citadel founder has quietly assembled one of the largest private landholdings in Miami Beach. Griffin's Star Island purchases total over $100 million across multiple parcels. Star Island sits in Biscayne Bay connected to the MacArthur Causeway, and its 34 lots make it one of the most exclusive addresses in South Florida. Unlike Indian Creek, Star Island is gated but not fully private, with tour boats passing by daily. Moving into a Star Island property means coordinating with the guard gate, driving narrow island roads, and working within strict noise and access windows set by the homeowners association.

## 4. The Former Diddy Mansion on Star Island

Sean "Diddy" Combs's Star Island estate made headlines for reasons beyond its $35 million price tag when federal agents raided the property as part of a sex trafficking investigation. The waterfront mansion includes a pool, dock, and expansive grounds. The property's future remains uncertain, but its location on Star Island ensures that whoever ends up there will need specialized moving logistics, including marine access for oversize deliveries and coordination with both Miami Beach police and island security.

## 5. Gloria Estefan's Star Island Estate

The Queen of Latin Pop has owned her Star Island compound for decades, making it one of the longest-held celebrity properties in Miami. The waterfront estate sits on 1.34 acres with direct bay access and a private dock. Estefan's property represents a different era of Miami celebrity real estate, when artists and entertainers dominated the island before the tech and finance wave arrived. The estate's location near the island's entrance makes it one of the more accessible properties for any potential moving logistics.

## 6. Tom Brady's Indian Creek Residence

The former NFL quarterback moved to Indian Creek after purchasing a property reportedly designed with input from his ex-wife Gisele Bundchen. The home underwent extensive renovation before Brady moved in. Indian Creek's strict building codes and architectural review board mean that even renovations require approval, and construction materials often arrive by barge to avoid disrupting the single-road access. For moving purposes, Brady's crew would have faced the same challenges any Indian Creek resident faces: security clearance, ferry coordination, and limited access windows.

## 7. Larry Page's Miami-Dade Portfolio

Google co-founder Larry Page has spent over $180 million on multiple properties across Miami-Dade County. Rather than concentrating on a single island, Page has assembled a portfolio of waterfront properties. This approach reflects a growing trend among ultra-wealthy buyers: diversifying across Miami neighborhoods rather than betting everything on one address. Each property presents different moving challenges, from high-security island logistics to mainland estate access.

## What These Moves Require

Celebrity and ultra-luxury moves in Miami share common logistics challenges that standard moving companies cannot handle:

- **Discretion**: High-profile clients need movers who don't discuss the job on social media or with the press
- **Specialized equipment**: Custom art, wine collections, and high-value furniture require climate-controlled transport and white-glove handling

## How Luxury Moves Differ from Standard Relocations

The gap between a standard residential move and a celebrity-level relocation comes down to planning depth. A typical Miami house move takes a few days to coordinate. A luxury estate move on Star Island or Indian Creek can take weeks of advance work:

- Pre-move security vetting for every team member
- Custom crating for artwork and sculptures
- Climate-controlled trucks for wine collections and sensitive materials
- Coordination with architects, interior designers, and estate managers
- Multiple delivery phases spread over days or weeks

If you are relocating to a high-end Miami property, professional movers who understand these requirements make the difference between a smooth transition and a logistical headache.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How is a luxury move different from a standard move?

Luxury moves involve advance security clearance, custom crating for valuable items, climate-controlled transport, and coordination with estate managers and building review boards. Planning typically starts weeks before the actual moving day.

### Can movers access gated island communities like Indian Creek or Star Island?

Yes, but it requires advance coordination. Crew members need security vetting, vehicles need clearance, and delivery windows are often restricted by homeowner associations. Some deliveries arrive by barge rather than truck.

### How much does a luxury move in Miami cost?

Costs vary widely based on the property, access requirements, and the value of items being moved. A luxury estate move with custom crating, climate-controlled transport, and multi-day delivery can range from $10,000 to $50,000 or more.
