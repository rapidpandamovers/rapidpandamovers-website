Homestead sits at the southern tip of mainland Miami-Dade County, roughly 35 miles south of Downtown Miami. The city has grown rapidly, reaching a population of about 86,000, and it offers something most of the Miami metro does not: space, affordability, and direct access to two national parks. If you are considering a move here, this guide covers what daily life actually looks like.

## Why Homestead Stands Out

### Gateway to the Everglades and Biscayne Bay

![Close Up Of A Majestic Osprey In Flight  4](/images/blog/2029/12/relocating-to-homestead-the-essential-guide/close-up-of-a-majestic-osprey-in-flight--4.webp)


Homestead is the last major city before Everglades National Park. The Ernest F. Coe Visitor Center is roughly ten minutes from the center of town. Biscayne National Park, protecting the northernmost coral reef in the continental United States, is equally close. No other city in Miami-Dade puts you this close to two national parks without sacrificing urban services.

### Agricultural Heritage

The Redland agricultural district borders Homestead to the west and north. This area produces tropical fruits including avocados, mangos, lychees, and carambola (starfruit) on farms that have operated for generations. The iron-rich red soil, which gives the Redland its name, supports agriculture uncommon anywhere else on the U.S. mainland. U-pick farms, fruit stands, and the annual Redland Tropical Fruit Festival are part of the local culture.

### Affordability

Housing costs in Homestead run well below the Miami-Dade County average. Single-family homes with yards are attainable at price points that would buy a studio apartment in Brickell or Coral Gables. For families looking for space without leaving the county, Homestead is one of the most practical options.

## Neighborhoods to Know

### Redland

The unincorporated area west of Homestead proper is characterized by larger lots, agricultural properties, and a rural feel. Homes here sit on half-acre or larger parcels. Residents keep horses, chickens, and fruit orchards. If you want land and privacy near a growing city, this is the area.

### Downtown Homestead

The historic downtown along Krome Avenue has seen revitalization in recent years, with new restaurants, shops, and the Seminole Theatre anchoring the district. Walkability is limited but improving. Properties near downtown tend to be older homes with character.

### Leisure City and Naranja

These communities south and east of Homestead offer some of the most affordable housing in all of Miami-Dade County. Development is newer in many sections, with planned communities and townhouse developments.

## Schools

Homestead falls within the Miami-Dade County Public Schools district. Notable schools in the area include:

- **Homestead Senior High School** - One of the area's largest high schools
- **Keys Gate Charter School** - Highly rated charter option with about 844 students
- **The Charter School at Waterstone** - Another popular charter serving the community
- **Redland Middle School** - Serves the western agricultural corridor

Several private schools operate in the area as well. Families moving to Homestead from within Miami-Dade stay in the same district, simplifying transfers.

## Daily Life and Commuting

The commute to Downtown Miami takes 45-60 minutes in typical traffic via the Turnpike or US-1. The South Dade Transitway (bus rapid transit) runs from the Florida City terminal to Dadeland South Metrorail station, providing a public transit option.

Grocery shopping centers on Publix, Walmart, and Sedano's locations along US-1 and Campbell Drive. The Homestead Pavilion shopping center and newer retail along SW 137th Avenue cover most daily needs.

The Homestead-Miami Speedway brings NASCAR and other motorsport events to the area, creating periodic traffic surges and entertainment options. Bayfront Park in downtown Homestead hosts community events year-round.

## What to Know Before You Move

**The commute is real.** If you work in Downtown Miami, Brickell, or Doral, budget 45-75 minutes each way. Many Homestead residents work locally, at Homestead Air Reserve Base, or in the agricultural sector.

**Hurricane exposure.** Homestead was ground zero for Hurricane Andrew in 1992, and the city was largely rebuilt afterward. Building codes are strict, and most homes constructed after 1992 are built to high wind-resistance standards. Still, hurricane preparedness is a part of life here.

**Growth is accelerating.** New residential developments, retail centers, and road improvements are reshaping the southern part of the city. What feels semi-rural today may look very different in five years.

## Moving to Homestead

Most Homestead homes are single-family properties with driveways and ground-level access, which simplifies the moving process compared to high-rise areas. The main considerations are distance from your origin (especially for intra-county moves from North Miami-Dade), and planning around traffic on US-1 and the Turnpike.

## FAQ

**Is Homestead safe?**
Safety varies by neighborhood, as in any city of 86,000 people. Areas near the Redland and newer planned communities like Waterstone and Keys Gate tend to have lower crime rates. Research specific streets and talk to neighbors before committing.

**How far is Homestead from the beach?**
Key Biscayne and Miami Beach are about 40-50 minutes north. Homestead Bayfront Park on Biscayne Bay is just 15 minutes east and offers a marina, beach area, and views of Biscayne National Park.

**Is Homestead a good place to raise a family?**
Yes, particularly for families who value outdoor access, space, and affordability over nightlife and urban density. The national parks, Redland farms, and community parks provide activities that are hard to find elsewhere in Miami-Dade.
