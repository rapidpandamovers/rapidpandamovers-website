Moving day doesn't have to be overwhelming. After years of helping Miami families relocate across Brickell, Coral Gables, and beyond, our team has picked up some game-changing shortcuts that make packing and loading a whole lot easier. Here are the tricks that actually work.

## 1. The Suitcase Strategy

Roll clothes and pack them in suitcases with wheels. You're using luggage you'd move anyway, and wheeled bags are easier to transport than boxes.

![A Cardboard Box Labeled With Winter Clot 2](/images/blog/2024/11/7-brilliant-moving-shortcuts-that-actually-work/a-cardboard-box-labeled-with-winter-clot-2.webp)


## 2. Plate Packing Like Pros

Stand plates vertically in boxes, like records in a crate. This distributes pressure evenly and dramatically reduces breakage.

## 3. The Garbage Bag Wardrobe

Cut a hole in the bottom of a garbage bag, slip it over hanging clothes, and tie the hanger hooks together. Instant wardrobe box for free.

## 4. Hardware Organization System

When disassembling furniture, put screws and bolts in a labeled ziplock bag and tape it directly to the furniture piece. No more mystery hardware.

## 5. Strategic Loading Order

Heavy items first, then medium weight, then light. Boxes should be stacked with heaviest on bottom. This prevents crushing and maximizes space.

![Labeled Cardboard Boxes For Summer Cloth 4](/images/blog/2024/11/7-brilliant-moving-shortcuts-that-actually-work/labeled-cardboard-boxes-for-summer-cloth-4.webp)


## 6. The Stretch Wrap Technique

Wrap furniture in stretch wrap to protect surfaces and keep drawers closed. It's cheaper than moving blankets and just as effective. Bonus: in Miami's humidity, stretch wrap also protects wood finishes from moisture during the move.

## 7. Color-Coded Efficiency

Use different colored tape for each room. Movers can unload directly to the right location without reading labels. This is especially helpful when moving into Miami high-rises where elevator time is limited and speed matters.
