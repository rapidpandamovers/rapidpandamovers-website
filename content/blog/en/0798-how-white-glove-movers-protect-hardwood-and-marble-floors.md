# How White Glove Movers Protect Hardwood and Marble Floors

A single scratch across a Calacatta marble foyer or a gouge in hand-scraped walnut hardwood can cost thousands to repair. In Miami's luxury homes, where imported stone and premium wood flooring are standard features, floor protection during a move isn't optional. It's one of the most visible differences between white glove and standard moving service.

## The Problem With Standard Moving

Standard movers roll heavy furniture across floors on basic dollies with hard rubber wheels. They drag boxes along hallways. They set down heavy items without checking what's underneath. In a three-bedroom Coral Gables home with $40,000 worth of marble and hardwood, the damage from a careless move can wipe out any savings from choosing the cheaper option.

## Pre-Move Floor Assessment

White glove teams start by assessing every floor surface in both the origin and destination homes. We identify the material, whether it's marble, travertine, hardwood, engineered wood, or tile, and note any existing damage. This assessment determines which protection methods we use in each area. Marble requires different treatment than oak. A polished concrete floor in a Wynwood loft needs different protection than the imported limestone in a Key Biscayne estate.

## Protective Runners and Mats

Heavy-duty, non-adhesive floor runners go down along every path the moving crew will use. These aren't thin plastic sheets. We use thick, padded runners specifically designed to distribute weight and absorb impact. They cover hallways, entryways, staircases, and any transition areas between rooms. At the destination, runners go down before the first piece of furniture comes through the door.

## Ram Board and Hardboard Protection

For moves involving especially heavy items or frequent trips along the same path, we lay rigid protection panels on top of the padded runners. Ram Board, a heavy-duty temporary floor protection material, creates a solid surface that distributes weight across a larger area. This prevents point-load damage from dolly wheels and furniture legs on softer floor materials like pine hardwood or polished marble.

## Equipment Selection

The dollies and hand trucks we use during white glove moves feature pneumatic tires or cushioned rubber wheels that don't mark floors. Standard moving dollies have hard plastic wheels that can leave black marks and scratches. For the heaviest items, we use furniture sliders with felt bases that glide across hardwood without contact between the floor and any hard material.

## Threshold and Transition Protection

The areas where different flooring materials meet, like the transition from marble in a foyer to hardwood in a living room, are especially vulnerable. These spots often have metal or stone transition strips that catch dolly wheels and cause jolts. We place ramped protection pads over these transitions to allow smooth, continuous movement.

## Staircase Protection

Stairs take the most punishment during a move because of the combination of weight, gravity, and limited maneuverability. Our crew pads each step and installs protective covers on banisters and newel posts. For curved staircases common in Coconut Grove estates and South Beach mansions, the protection plan accounts for the geometry of the turns.

## Post-Move Inspection

After the last item is placed and the protective materials are removed, we walk through both properties to inspect every floor surface. Any issues get documented and addressed immediately. This final check gives you confidence that your floors survived the move in the same condition they started.
