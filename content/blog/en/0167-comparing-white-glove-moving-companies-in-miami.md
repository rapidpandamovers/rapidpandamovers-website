# Comparing White Glove Moving Companies in Miami

Miami has dozens of moving companies that advertise white glove service, but the quality gap between them is enormous. Some deliver the full premium experience. Others charge white glove prices while providing standard service with a few extra blankets. Here's how to evaluate and compare your options.

## Check Their Experience With Luxury Moves

Ask each company how many luxury moves they complete per month. A company that handles one or two high-value relocations a year is not in the same category as one that does ten or more monthly. Miami's luxury market, from Star Island mansions to Fisher Island estates to Brickell penthouses, presents unique challenges that only consistent experience teaches. Ask for references from clients with similar homes and belongings.

## Evaluate the Walk-Through Process

The pre-move assessment reveals a lot about a company's approach. Does a manager visit your home in person, or do they offer a quote over the phone based on square footage? A real white glove company sends someone who catalogs your belongings, measures doorways and hallways, notes items requiring custom crating, and builds a specific plan. A phone quote for a luxury move is a warning sign.

## Compare Insurance and Coverage

Request written details of each company's valuation coverage. Compare deductibles, maximum liability amounts, and what's excluded. Ask whether they offer full replacement value as standard or as an add-on. Check if they recommend third-party insurance for high-value inventories. The cheapest quote may also carry the least coverage, which turns a "good deal" into an expensive risk for homes in Coral Gables and Coconut Grove with six-figure furnishing budgets.

## Look at Their Equipment

Professional white glove movers invest heavily in specialized equipment. Air-ride suspension trucks prevent vibration damage. Custom crating materials protect artwork and fragile items. Felt-padded dollies and pneumatic-tired hand trucks protect floors. Climate-controlled vehicles handle temperature-sensitive items. Ask what equipment each company brings to the job. If their answer is "blankets and a truck," they're not providing white glove service.

## Assess Crew Training and Consistency

Find out how the company trains its crews for luxury moves. Are the movers who handle your $50,000 dining table the same people who did standard apartment moves last week? White glove companies maintain dedicated crews with specific training in handling high-value items, working in luxury buildings, and using specialized equipment. Ask whether the same crew handles your entire move or whether different teams handle different phases.

## Review Their Building Relationships

Miami's luxury high-rises have strict requirements for movers. The best white glove companies have established relationships with building management at properties across the city. They know the rules, have the insurance certificates ready, and can reserve elevators and loading docks efficiently. Ask each company about their experience with your specific building if you're moving into or out of a condo.

## Read Reviews With a Critical Eye

Online reviews tell part of the story, but look for specifics. Reviews that mention the move manager by name, describe how artwork or antiques were handled, or note the floor protection process are more useful than generic five-star ratings. Pay attention to how the company responds to negative reviews, since that response tells you how they'll handle a problem during your move.

## Get Everything in Writing

A reputable white glove company puts every detail into a written contract: the scope of service, the timeline, the insurance coverage, the crew size, and the total cost with a breakdown of charges. If a company resists putting details in writing or gives you a vague estimate, move on. In the Miami market, the companies worth hiring are the ones confident enough to document their commitments.
