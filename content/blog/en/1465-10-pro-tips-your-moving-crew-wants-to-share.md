Professional movers see dozens of moves every month. After thousands of jobs, certain patterns emerge: the things that make a move run smoothly and the mistakes that turn a 4-hour job into an 8-hour ordeal. Here are 10 tips straight from the crew.

## 1. Packing Takes Longer Than You Think

A 3-bedroom house takes 3-5 days to pack properly. Not one frantic night before the movers arrive. Starting late means half-packed boxes, loose items scattered everywhere, and a crew standing around while you finish.

![Firefighters Inspect And Prepare Equipme 1](/images/blog/2029/11/10-pro-tips-your-moving-crew-wants-to-share/firefighters-inspect-and-prepare-equipme-1.webp)


If packing yourself is not realistic, that is exactly what [**packing services**](/packing-services) exist for. A professional team can pack an entire house in a day with the right materials.

## 2. Small Boxes for Heavy Items

Books, dishes, tools, and canned goods go in small boxes. Large boxes are for light items like bedding, pillows, and lampshades. This is not a suggestion. A large box packed with books weighs 70-80 pounds, risks breaking when lifted, and puts movers at injury risk.

The rule: if it is heavy, it goes in a small box. If it is light and bulky, it goes in a large box.

## 3. Label Every Box on the Side, Not Just the Top

"Kitchen" written on top of a box is invisible when boxes are stacked. Write the room name, a brief description of contents, and "FRAGILE" if applicable on at least two sides of every box.

Better labels mean faster unloading and fewer boxes opened searching for the coffee maker on the first morning.

## 4. Tell Us About Fragile and Valuable Items

If something is irreplaceable, exceptionally fragile, or worth more than you would casually mention, tell the movers before they start. Point it out. Describe what it is. We handle everything with care, but knowing that the wooden box contains your grandmother's china versus old magazines changes how we prioritize it.

## 5. Clear the Pathways

Every obstacle between your front door and the truck adds time. Before the crew arrives:

![Four Movers Carefully Lift A Large Wrapp 4](/images/blog/2029/11/10-pro-tips-your-moving-crew-wants-to-share/four-movers-carefully-lift-a-large-wrapp-4.webp)


- Move shoes, toys, and small furniture from hallways
- Secure pets in a separate room
- Reserve the elevator if you are in a building
- Confirm parking is available for the truck

A clear path from door to truck is the single biggest factor in how fast a move goes.

## 6. Disassemble What You Can

Beds, dining tables, and modular desks move faster in pieces. If you disassemble furniture before moving day, keep all hardware in a labeled plastic bag and tape it to the furniture piece it belongs to.

We handle disassembly and reassembly as part of our [**full-service moving**](/full-service-moving), but if you want to speed things up and save on labor time, doing it yourself the night before helps.

## 7. Know Your Floor Plan Before We Arrive

"Put it somewhere for now" turns into three rearrangements later. Before the movers arrive at the new location, decide where major furniture goes. Tape paper labels to the floor or walls of each room so the crew can place items correctly the first time.

## 8. Stay Accessible, Not Underfoot

We will have questions during the move. Which boxes are priority? Where does this bookshelf go? Is this item being donated or moved? Being available by phone or in a nearby room keeps the move flowing. Standing in the doorway watching every box creates bottlenecks.

## 9. Water and Cold Drinks Go a Long Way

Moving is hard physical work, especially in Miami's heat. A cooler with water, Gatorade, or other cold drinks for the crew is always appreciated. It is not expected or required, but it is the kind of gesture movers remember.

![Two Movers In Uniform Carrying Boxes In  2](/images/blog/2029/11/10-pro-tips-your-moving-crew-wants-to-share/two-movers-in-uniform-carrying-boxes-in--2.webp)


## 10. Communication Makes Everything Better

Tell us about the narrow staircase, the broken elevator, the neighbor who parks in front of your building, or the dog that does not like strangers. Surprises on moving day slow everything down. The more we know in advance, the better we can prepare.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How early should I start packing?**
For a 2-3 bedroom home, start at least 2 weeks before moving day. Pack one room at a time, starting with rooms and items you use least.

**Should I tip the movers?**
Tipping is not required but is appreciated. A common range is $20-40 per mover for a full-day job, or $10-20 for a half-day. Cash is preferred.

**What should I NOT pack for the movers?**
Keep personal documents, medications, jewelry, cash, and irreplaceable items with you. These should never go on the truck.

**How can I make the move go faster?**
Pack everything before the crew arrives, clear pathways, have a floor plan ready, and be available to answer questions. These four things together can cut hours off a move.
