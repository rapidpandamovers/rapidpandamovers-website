# Hot Tub Weight Guide: Planning for Transport

Knowing exactly how much your hot tub weighs is the starting point for every decision about moving it: how many people you need, what equipment to bring, whether the truck can handle it, and whether the pad at your new Miami home can support it. Here's a detailed breakdown of hot tub weights by type, size, and condition.

## Dry Weights by Size

These are approximate weights for empty, drained hot tubs:

![A Couple Enjoys A Serene Afternoon In A  3](/images/blog/2026/03/hot-tub-weight-guide-planning-for-transport/a-couple-enjoys-a-serene-afternoon-in-a--3.webp)


| Tub Type | Dimensions | Dry Weight |
|----------|-----------|------------|
| Small (2-3 person) | 5'x5' to 6'x6' | 300-500 lbs |
| Medium (4-5 person) | 6'x6' to 7'x7' | 500-700 lbs |
| Large (6-8 person) | 7'x7' to 8'x8' | 700-900 lbs |
| Swim Spa | 12'x7' to 19'x8' | 1,000-2,500 lbs |

The actual weight depends on the shell material (acrylic, rotomolded plastic, or fiberglass), the frame material (wood, steel, or composite), and the number of pumps and jets. A loaded-up 8-person Jacuzzi J-485 with four pumps and a sound system weighs significantly more than a basic 8-person model from a budget brand.

## Filled Weights

A gallon of water weighs about 8.3 pounds. Hot tubs hold between 200 and 500 gallons depending on size. Add the weight of the water to the dry weight, plus the weight of 2-6 people, and you get the total operating weight:

| Tub Type | Water Volume | Filled Weight (no people) | Operating Weight (with people) |
|----------|-------------|--------------------------|-------------------------------|
| Small (2-3 person) | 150-250 gal | 1,500-2,500 lbs | 1,900-3,100 lbs |
| Medium (4-5 person) | 250-375 gal | 2,500-3,800 lbs | 3,300-4,800 lbs |
| Large (6-8 person) | 375-500 gal | 3,800-5,000 lbs | 5,000-6,600 lbs |

These filled weights matter for pad design at your new home, not for moving. You should always move a tub completely empty.

## Why Residual Water Matters

Even a "drained" hot tub holds 5 to 20 gallons of residual water in the jet lines, pump housing, heater assembly, and footwell low spots. At 8.3 pounds per gallon, that's 40 to 165 extra pounds that shifts around during lifting and transport. This shifting water makes the tub feel heavier than its actual weight because the center of gravity keeps changing.

![Adult Man Enjoying Relaxation In A Woode 1](/images/blog/2026/03/hot-tub-weight-guide-planning-for-transport/adult-man-enjoying-relaxation-in-a-woode-1.webp)


That's why thorough draining, vacuuming, and line blowout are part of proper [**Hot Tub Moving**](/hot-tub-moving) prep. Every gallon you remove makes the move safer and easier.

## How Weight Affects Your Move

### Crew Size

A 350-pound small tub can be handled by three to four people with proper equipment. An 800-pound large tub needs five to six crew members minimum. If the tub exceeds 900 pounds dry (common with swim spas and high-end large models), the move may require specialized equipment beyond standard dollies.

### Equipment Requirements

Lighter tubs (under 500 pounds) can often be carried short distances using moving straps and harnesses. Heavier tubs need spa dollies and furniture dollies for any distance beyond a few feet. Tubs over 1,000 pounds (swim spas) may require a forklift, Bobcat, or crane depending on the access situation.

### Truck Capacity

A standard moving truck can handle any hot tub from a weight perspective. The floor rating on most box trucks and flatbed trailers exceeds the weight of even the heaviest swim spa. The real concern is the loading method. A ramp rated for 1,000 pounds won't work for a 1,500-pound swim spa, so verify ramp capacity before loading day.

### Pad Requirements at the Destination

Your new hot tub pad needs to support the filled operating weight, not just the dry weight. A 4-inch reinforced concrete pad rated for 100 PSI can handle a standard hot tub. But if you're placing the tub on an elevated wood deck in a Miami home, the deck's structural capacity must be verified. This is especially important in older Miami Beach and Coral Gables homes where decks may not have been built with hot tub loads in mind.

## Finding Your Tub's Exact Weight

The fastest way is to check the owner's manual or the specification sticker on the equipment compartment door. If you can't find it, search the manufacturer's website using your model number. Most manufacturers list dry weight, filled weight, and water capacity in their spec sheets.

![Shirtless Man Smiling In An Outdoor Hot  2](/images/blog/2026/03/hot-tub-weight-guide-planning-for-transport/shirtless-man-smiling-in-an-outdoor-hot--2.webp)


If all else fails, your moving company can estimate the weight based on the tub's dimensions, material, and brand. But an exact number from the manufacturer is always better for planning purposes.
