Bay Harbor Islands continues to attract new residents from across the country during January, and it's easy to see why. Tucked between Bal Harbour and Surfside along the Intracoastal Waterway, this two-island community offers an upscale village feel just minutes from South Beach.

## Why Choose Bay Harbor Islands?

Bay Harbor Islands stands out as one of Miami-Dade County's most appealing locations. The town spans East and West Islands, connected by the 96th Street Causeway, giving residents that rare small-town atmosphere within Greater Miami. The area is known for its waterfront properties, walkable Kane Concourse shopping district, and proximity to Bal Harbour Shops.

![A Picturesque Marina With Boats Palm Tre 2](/images/blog/2025/01/bay-harbor-islands-101-what-every-new-resident-should-know/a-picturesque-marina-with-boats-palm-tre-2.webp)


The area attracts families, professionals, and retirees alike, thanks to its quality of life, convenient access to major employment centers, and excellent amenities.

### Location and Accessibility

One of Bay Harbor Islands' biggest advantages is its location between Miami Beach and Aventura. Collins Avenue runs along the eastern edge, connecting you to South Beach in 15 minutes and Aventura Mall in 10. I-95 is accessible via the 96th Street or Broad Causeway, making Downtown Miami and Brickell commutes manageable during off-peak hours.

### Community and Lifestyle

Bay Harbor Islands residents enjoy a strong sense of community. The town's parks along the Intracoastal offer kayaking and paddleboarding spots, and the annual Art Walk on Kane Concourse draws crowds from neighboring areas. Many residents walk or bike to the local restaurants and cafes rather than drive.

## Neighborhoods to Consider

When planning your move to Bay Harbor Islands, consider which island suits your lifestyle. East Island features more high-rise condos with bay views and direct waterfront access. West Island tends toward single-family homes and townhouses with a quieter residential feel. Kane Concourse, the main commercial strip, borders both islands.

### Choosing Your Perfect Spot

Consider these factors:

- **Proximity to work and schools**: Factor in your daily commute
- **Local amenities**: Parks, shopping, dining, and entertainment options
- **Property types**: Single-family homes, condos, townhouses, or apartments
- **Community vibe**: Family-oriented, young professional, or mixed demographics

## Moving to Bay Harbor Islands in January

January is an excellent time for your move. Daytime temperatures hover around 75°F with low humidity, a welcome change from Miami's summer heat. You'll also avoid the peak snowbird season traffic that builds in February and March.

### Timing Your Move

When planning your relocation, consider:

- **Best moving days**: Weekdays offer better availability since Kane Concourse sees less shopping traffic
- **Weather considerations**: January averages 5-6 rain days, so schedule around afternoon storms if needed
- **Local events**: Check the Bay Harbor Islands town calendar for Art Walk dates and holiday events that may affect parking

## Essential Services to Locate

As a new Bay Harbor Islands resident, you'll want to find:

- **Healthcare facilities**: Mount Sinai Medical Center in Miami Beach is 10 minutes away; Aventura Hospital is 15 minutes north
- **Schools**: Ruth K. Broad Bay Harbor K-8 Center serves the islands; private options include Lehrman Community Day School nearby
- **Shopping**: Publix on Kane Concourse for groceries; Bal Harbour Shops and Aventura Mall for everything else
- **Recreation**: Bay Harbor Islands Park offers waterfront walking paths; Normandy Shores Golf Club is a short drive away
