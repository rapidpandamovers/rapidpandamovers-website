Not everything in your home can go on a regular moving truck with standard padding. Some items are too heavy, too fragile, or too valuable for a general crew. Trying to save money by skipping specialty movers on these pieces often leads to damage that costs far more than professional handling would have.

Here are 10 possessions that genuinely need expert movers.

## 1. Grand Pianos

A baby grand weighs around 500 pounds. A concert grand tops 1,200 pounds. The internal strings, hammers, and soundboard are incredibly sensitive to shock and humidity. Professional piano movers use a piano board (a padded, wheeled platform), heavy-duty straps, and a team of at least three people. They know how to tip the piano on its side without stressing the legs or pedal assembly.

## 2. Wine Collections

Wine needs to stay at a consistent temperature during transport. A single hour in a hot Miami moving truck can ruin bottles that took years to age. Specialty movers use insulated, climate-controlled containers and pack each bottle in individual foam sleeves. Bottles are transported on their sides to keep corks moist and prevent oxidation.

## 3. Hot Tubs and Spas

A standard hot tub weighs 500 to 900 pounds empty. Moving one requires draining, disconnecting the electrical and plumbing lines, and securing the cover and shell. The acrylic shell cracks easily under uneven pressure. Professional movers use a flatbed truck with custom strapping to keep the tub level and stable during transit.

## 4. Large Aquariums

Fish need separate, temperature-controlled transport bags. The tank itself must be completely drained, with gravel and decorations removed and packed separately. Glass tanks over 50 gallons are extremely fragile when empty because the water pressure is no longer supporting the seams. Movers transport them upright with foam padding on every side.

## 5. Pool Tables

A pool table's slate top comes in one to three pieces, with each piece weighing up to 250 pounds. The slate must be removed from the frame, wrapped individually, and transported standing vertically. At the new location, reassembly includes precise leveling with shims. An improperly moved pool table never plays straight again.

## 6. Fine Art and Sculptures

Original paintings, framed prints, and sculptures each need custom crating built to their exact dimensions. Art movers use acid-free materials, corner protectors, and shock-absorbing foam. For high-value pieces, they add climate control to prevent paint from cracking in heat or humidity. Miami's tropical conditions make this especially important.

## 7. Full Gym Equipment

A commercial treadmill weighs 250 to 400 pounds. Weight machines, squat racks, and multi-gym stations are even heavier and have awkward shapes that make doorways and stairs a real challenge. Movers disassemble what they can and use appliance dollies with stair-climbing capabilities for the rest.

## 8. Classic and Collector Cars

Valuable vehicles cannot be driven to a new location and risk road damage or mileage depreciation. Enclosed auto transport trailers protect the paint, body, and interior from weather, road debris, and sun exposure. Movers use soft tie-downs instead of chain hooks to avoid scratching the undercarriage.

## 9. Grandfather Clocks

These tall, narrow timepieces have delicate internal mechanisms, including pendulums, weights, and chains that must be secured or removed before moving. The glass panels and wooden case are fragile, and the clock must be transported upright. Tilting a grandfather clock can permanently damage the movement mechanism.

## 10. Chandeliers and Large Light Fixtures

Crystal chandeliers are fragile, awkward to handle, and often heavier than they look. Each crystal piece should be individually wrapped and removed before the frame is taken down. The fixture itself needs a custom box with internal supports. Reassembly and rewiring at the new home require both moving expertise and electrical knowledge.
