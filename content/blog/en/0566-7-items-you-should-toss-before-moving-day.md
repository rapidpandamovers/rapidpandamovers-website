# 7 Items You Should Toss Before Moving Day

Packing for a move forces you to pick up every single thing you own, and that's the perfect moment to decide whether it's worth carrying to your new place. Here are seven categories of items that Miami movers see people haul from one home to another, only to throw them away months later. Save yourself the trouble and get rid of them now.

## 1. Worn-Out Mattresses and Pillows

If your mattress has visible sags, stains, or is more than eight years old, don't bring it with you. Mattresses are heavy, awkward to move, and take up a huge amount of truck space. A queen mattress alone can weigh 80 to 100 pounds and requires two people to maneuver through doorways and down staircases. Miami's heat and humidity also accelerate mattress wear, so if yours has been through a few South Florida summers, it's probably past its prime.

Old pillows harbor dust mites and lose their support over time. Toss them and start fresh at your new place.

## 2. Outdated Electronics

That box of tangled chargers, the DVD player you haven't used since streaming took over, the old desktop computer collecting dust in a closet. These items take up space and have almost no value. Miami-Dade County hosts electronics recycling events throughout the year, and stores like Best Buy accept old electronics for recycling at their locations in Dadeland, Dolphin Mall, and Kendall.

## 3. Expired Pantry Items and Spices

Open your kitchen cabinets and check expiration dates. Most people are shocked by how many cans, sauces, and spice jars are well past their prime. Spices lose their potency after about a year, and canned goods past their dates aren't worth the box space. Toss them, and use the weeks before your move to cook through what's still good.

## 4. Broken or Mismatched Furniture

That wobbly nightstand you've been meaning to fix for two years? The dining chairs that don't match anything? Moving is expensive, and paying to transport furniture you'd replace at your new home doesn't make financial sense. If pieces are in decent shape, donate them to Goodwill or Habitat for Humanity ReStore in Miami. If they're truly broken, a [**Junk Removal**](/junk-removal) crew can haul them away.

## 5. Unused Exercise Equipment

Treadmills, ellipticals, and weight benches are among the heaviest and most awkward items to move. If your stationary bike has been serving as a laundry rack, be honest with yourself. These items fetch decent prices on Facebook Marketplace or OfferUp in Miami, so sell them before your move. If they're in rough shape, have them removed instead of paying to move dead weight.

## 6. Old Paint, Chemicals, and Cleaning Supplies

Partially used paint cans, pool chemicals, pesticides, and solvents can't go on a moving truck. Most moving companies refuse to transport hazardous materials for liability reasons. Miami-Dade County's Home Chemical Collection Center at 8831 NW 58th Street accepts these items for free. Drop them off before moving day rather than scrambling at the last minute.

Also toss that collection of half-empty cleaning bottles under the sink. A new bottle of all-purpose cleaner costs a few dollars and is easier than wrapping and packing a dozen leaky containers.

## 7. Paper Clutter and Old Files

Tax returns older than seven years, ancient bank statements, expired warranties, and piles of junk mail that somehow accumulated into a tower. Shred sensitive documents (Office Depot and Staples locations across Miami offer shredding services) and recycle the rest. Going paperless at your new place will prevent the pile from rebuilding.

## The Payoff

Getting rid of these seven categories of items before moving day means fewer boxes to pack, less truck space needed, and a lower moving bill. It also means walking into your new home with only the things you actually want and use.
