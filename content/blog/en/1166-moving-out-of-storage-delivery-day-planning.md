# Moving Out of Storage: Delivery Day Planning

Your belongings have been sitting in storage for weeks or months, and now it's time to bring everything to your new home. Delivery day from a storage unit has its own set of logistics that differ from a standard move. The items have been stationary, stacked, and potentially accessed multiple times. Getting them to your new place efficiently takes coordination between you, the delivery crew, and your new building or neighborhood.

## Two Weeks Before Delivery

Start planning well ahead of the actual day:

![A Collection Of Dismantled Hard Disk Dri 1](/images/blog/2029/05/moving-out-of-storage-delivery-day-planning/a-collection-of-dismantled-hard-disk-dri-1.webp)


- **Confirm your new home is ready.** Utilities should be on, keys should be in hand, and any required repairs or cleaning should be finished. Loading a truck from storage only to find your new place isn't ready creates an expensive problem.
- **Book the elevator at your building.** High-rise condos in Brickell, Edgewater, and Downtown Miami typically require advance reservation for the freight elevator. Miss this step and your crew might wait hours for access.
- **Check HOA or building move-in rules.** Some properties restrict deliveries to weekdays, specific hours, or require a certificate of insurance from the moving company.
- **Visit your storage unit.** Do a walkthrough and review your inventory list. Identify anything that needs special handling on delivery day, like items that shifted during storage or boxes that show signs of moisture.

## One Week Before Delivery

- **Create a delivery priority list.** Decide which items go into your home first. Beds, kitchen essentials, and bathroom supplies should come off the truck before decorative items and seasonal goods.
- **Measure doorways, hallways, and stairwells** at your new address. If a large couch barely fit into the storage unit, it may also be tight getting into your new place. Know the dimensions before the crew arrives.
- **Arrange parking for the truck.** Miami neighborhoods have tight streets and limited parking. Doral townhome communities, Miami Beach side streets, and Downtown loading zones all require advance planning. Check whether you need a parking permit for the truck.
- **Prepare your new home.** Protect floors with drop cloths or cardboard runners along the path from the entrance to each room. Clean rooms where furniture will be placed.

## Delivery Day

- **Be at the storage unit when the crew arrives.** Walk through the unit with them, pointing out fragile items, heavy pieces, and the loading order based on your priority list. Items needed last at the new home should go on the truck first.
- **Inspect items as they're loaded.** Check for mold, moisture damage, pest evidence, or physical damage from storage. Document any issues with photos before items leave the facility.
- **Clear the unit completely.** Sweep it out and check all corners. Report any damage to the facility manager before you leave.
- **Travel to the new home ahead of the truck** if possible, so you can direct placement as items arrive.

## At the New Home

- **Direct traffic.** Tell the crew exactly where each piece goes. Moving a dresser upstairs, then realizing it belongs downstairs, wastes time and energy.
- **Assemble furniture in place.** If items were disassembled for storage, have them reassembled in the room where they'll stay. Don't build a bed frame in the living room and try to carry it down the hall.
- **Open boxes and inspect contents** before the crew leaves. If you're using a professional service with damage coverage, documenting issues on delivery day strengthens any claim.

## After Delivery

Break down boxes, organize packing materials for recycling, and update your inventory list to reflect that items are now in your home. Cancel your storage unit rental once you've confirmed everything has been retrieved and the unit is empty.
