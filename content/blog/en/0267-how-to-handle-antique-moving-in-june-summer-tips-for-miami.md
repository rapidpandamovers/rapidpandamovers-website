Moving antiques in June requires extra care. Miami's wet season is in full swing, bringing daily afternoon thunderstorms, high humidity, and temperatures in the 90s. For delicate antique furniture, artwork, and collectibles, these conditions demand careful planning and professional handling.

## Why June Requires Special Attention for Antique Moving

June marks the start of hurricane season and sits deep in Miami's wet season. Humidity regularly exceeds 75%, afternoon thunderstorms roll in almost every day between 3pm and 6pm, and heat can warp wood finishes and loosen glue joints. Professional [**Antique Moving**](/antique-moving) crews understand these risks and plan around them. Moving antiques from a Coral Gables estate or a Coconut Grove bungalow requires special attention to humidity levels that can damage wood finishes and delicate fabrics.

## Key Tips for Antique Moving in June

### Schedule Morning Moves

June afternoons bring predictable thunderstorms. Book your move for early morning, ideally starting by 7am. This gives the crew several hours of clear weather to load and transport your antiques before the storms arrive. We schedule antique moves for early morning when possible, finishing before the typical 3pm storm window.

### Get appraisals and photograph items before the move for insurance purposes

This single step can save hours of stress and potential problems on moving day. Our experienced crews have seen what happens when this step is skipped.

### Protect Against Humidity and Rain

South Florida's June humidity is the biggest threat to antiques. Professional movers use climate-controlled trucks when available, wrap items in moisture-resistant materials, and keep the truck sealed during sudden downpours. Wood furniture, oil paintings, and upholstered pieces are particularly vulnerable. We keep weather-resistant covers and padding on every truck during the wet season.

### Prepare Your Belongings

Take time to inventory your items before the move. This is especially important for antique moving, as proper documentation helps ensure everything arrives safely at your new location. Many of our clients moving from historic homes in areas like Little Havana, Design District, or South Beach have irreplaceable heirloom pieces that require detailed photographic records.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does antique moving cost in Miami?

![Two Women Wearing Masks Browse An Antiqu 3](/images/blog/2025/06/how-to-handle-antique-moving-in-june-summer-tips-for-miami/two-women-wearing-masks-browse-an-antiqu-3.webp)


Antique moving costs vary based on the number of items, their size, and required protection. Most antique moves in Miami-Dade range from $200-$800 for a few pieces, with full household collections costing more. Moves within neighborhoods like Brickell or Aventura typically cost less than cross-county moves to areas like Homestead or Miami Lakes. We provide free on-site estimates to give you an accurate quote.

### Do I need special insurance for moving antiques?

Standard moving insurance may not cover the full value of antiques. We recommend getting appraisals before the move and discussing full-value protection options. Our team can explain coverage options during your free consultation.

### How far in advance should I book antique moving in June?

Book at least 2-3 weeks ahead. Summer is a busy period for movers, and early booking ensures you get a morning time slot, which is critical for avoiding afternoon storms.

### Can antiques be moved safely in Miami's summer heat?

Yes, but precautions are necessary. We use climate-controlled trucks when available and avoid leaving items in direct sunlight. Wood furniture and oil paintings are particularly sensitive to heat and humidity, so morning moves are the best approach during June.
