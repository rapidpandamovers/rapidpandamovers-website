## Key Tips for Labor Only Moving in April

### Plan Ahead for Peak Times

April can be a busy time for moving companies. With graduation ceremonies at UM, FIU, and Miami Dade College, plus lease turnovers in Coral Gables and Coconut Grove, scheduling your labor only moving early ensures you get your preferred date and time.

### Have everything packed and the truck ready when movers arrive

This single step can save hours of stress and potential problems on moving day. Our experienced crews have seen what happens when this step is skipped.

### Consider the Weather

South Florida's April weather marks the transition from dry to wet season. While rain is still infrequent in early April, scattered showers may begin appearing later in the month. Professional movers know how to protect your belongings from humidity, rain, and heat exposure during transport, and they time loading and unloading around weather patterns.

### Prepare Your Belongings

Take time to inventory your items before the move. This is especially important for labor only moving, as proper documentation helps ensure everything arrives safely at your new location.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does labor-only moving cost in Miami?

![Two Smiling Movers Carrying Cardboard Bo 2](/images/blog/2027/04/labor-only-moving-guide-for-april/two-smiling-movers-carrying-cardboard-bo-2.webp)


Labor-only moving in Miami costs $80-$150 per hour for a two-person crew. Most jobs take 2-4 hours. You provide the truck (rental or PODS), and we provide the muscle and expertise.

### What's the difference between labor-only and hourly moving?

Labor-only means you provide the vehicle and we provide the crew. Hourly moving includes both a truck and crew. Labor-only costs less per hour since you handle your own transportation.

### Should I tip labor-only movers?

Standard tipping is $20-$40 per mover for a half-day job. A generous tip is always appreciated, especially during warm months when the work is physically demanding.
