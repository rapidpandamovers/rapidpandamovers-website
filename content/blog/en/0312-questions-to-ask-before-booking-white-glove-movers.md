# Questions to Ask Before Booking White Glove Movers

Hiring white glove movers is a bigger decision than booking a standard crew, and the price tag alone doesn't guarantee quality. Before you sign anything, the right questions will quickly separate companies that deliver premium service from those that just charge premium prices. Here's what to ask when you're comparing white glove movers in Miami.

## Do You Assign a Dedicated Move Manager?

A real white glove operation gives you a single point of contact who oversees your move from the first walk-through to the final box unpacked. This person should visit your home before moving day, build a detailed plan, and be present (or reachable) throughout. If the company tells you "whoever's available" will manage your move, that's a red flag.

## What Does Your Packing Process Look Like?

Ask for specifics. What materials do they use on wood furniture? How do they pack artwork? Do they build custom crates on-site or use prefabricated boxes? The answer tells you whether they're running a true white glove operation or just a standard move with a fancier name. For homes in Coral Gables and Pinecrest filled with antiques and designer furniture, these details matter.

## How Do You Handle Building Requirements?

If you're moving into a Brickell tower or a waterfront condo in Sunny Isles, the building will have rules about elevator reservations, loading dock hours, floor coverings, and insurance documentation. Ask the company if they've moved clients in your specific building before. Experienced white glove movers in Miami know the requirements for most luxury buildings and can handle the logistics without you making phone calls.

## What Insurance Coverage Do You Offer?

Federal law requires movers to offer basic liability at $0.60 per pound per item, which means a 20-pound lamp worth $5,000 would only be covered for $12. That's unacceptable for a white glove move. Ask about full replacement value protection, third-party insurance options, and how the claims process works. Get the details in writing before moving day.

## Can I See References From Similar Moves?

A company that regularly handles luxury relocations in Miami should have no trouble providing references from clients who moved comparable homes. Ask specifically about moves involving high-value artwork, antique furniture, or complex high-rise logistics. Vague responses or an unwillingness to share references should make you look elsewhere.

## What Happens if Something Gets Damaged?

Even the best movers face occasional mishaps. The question is how they respond. Ask about their claims process, typical resolution timeline, and whether they work with restoration specialists for high-value items. A good company will walk you through the process honestly rather than dodging the subject.

## Do Your Crews Receive Specialized Training?

White glove handling requires techniques that standard movers don't learn. Ask about crew training, how long team members have been with the company, and whether the same crew handles your entire move from start to finish. Consistency matters when people are handling your most valuable possessions.
