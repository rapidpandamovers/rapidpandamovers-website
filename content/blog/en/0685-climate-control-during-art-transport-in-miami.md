# Climate Control During Art Transport in Miami

Miami sits at the intersection of intense heat, extreme humidity, and a thriving art scene. That combination creates real problems when artwork needs to move from one location to another. A painting that spent years in a climate-controlled Brickell condo can suffer visible damage in just minutes of exposure to the conditions outside. Here's what we do to prevent that.

## How Miami's Climate Damages Art

Miami's average relative humidity hovers around 75%, climbing even higher during summer months and the wet season from June through October. When artwork moves from an air-conditioned space at 50% humidity into outdoor air at 85% humidity, condensation forms on cold surfaces almost instantly. For a glass-fronted frame, that means moisture trapped between the glass and the artwork. For an unglazed canvas, it means water absorbing directly into the paint and fabric.

![A Museum Hall Showcasing A Marble Statue 1](/images/blog/2027/01/climate-control-during-art-transport-in-miami/a-museum-hall-showcasing-a-marble-statue-1.webp)


Heat compounds the problem. The interior of a standard moving truck parked in a Miami parking lot can reach 140 degrees. At those temperatures, adhesives soften, varnish becomes tacky, wooden stretcher bars warp, and oil paint surfaces can blister. If the piece then moves back into air conditioning, rapid cooling causes contraction that leads to cracking.

## Climate-Controlled Vehicles

The most effective protection during transport is a truck with active climate control. Our art transport vehicles maintain interior temperatures between 65 and 72 degrees with humidity kept below 55%. This replicates the conditions inside a properly maintained home or gallery.

Not all "climate-controlled" trucks are equal. Some companies market insulated trucks as climate-controlled, but insulation only slows temperature change. It doesn't maintain a set range. True climate control requires an active HVAC system that runs independently of the truck's engine, keeping conditions stable even when the vehicle is parked for loading and unloading.

## Moisture Barriers and Wrapping Techniques

Even with a climate-controlled truck, artwork needs moisture protection during the transition between buildings and the vehicle. We use polyethylene sheeting as a vapor barrier around wrapped pieces. This creates a sealed environment that prevents outside air from reaching the art during the brief exposure to outdoor conditions.

![A Person Admires Art In A Modern Gallery 3](/images/blog/2027/01/climate-control-during-art-transport-in-miami/a-person-admires-art-in-a-modern-gallery-3.webp)


Silica gel packets placed inside each wrapped package or crate absorb any moisture that does get through. For larger crates, we use conditioned silica gel that's been calibrated to maintain a specific humidity range inside the enclosure.

Glassine wrapping goes on first, directly over the art surface, followed by the vapor barrier, then bubble wrap or foam for impact protection. The order matters. You want the moisture barrier as close to the piece as possible.

## Timing Your Move

When possible, we schedule art pickups and deliveries during the cooler parts of the day. Early morning moves before 9 AM mean lower temperatures and less humidity than midday transport. During summer, even a two-hour difference can mean a 15-degree swing in outdoor temperature.

We also minimize the time artwork spends outside of climate-controlled spaces. Our crews stage everything inside before opening the truck doors, then load quickly with all pieces ready to go. At the destination, the process reverses. We open the truck, move directly into the building, and close up.

## Acclimation at the Destination

Resist opening wrapped artwork immediately after delivery. If the piece traveled in a climate-controlled truck but the destination is a new home where the AC has been off during construction or staging, the indoor conditions might not be ideal yet. Let wrapped pieces sit in the new space for 24 hours to gradually adjust to the indoor environment before unwrapping.
