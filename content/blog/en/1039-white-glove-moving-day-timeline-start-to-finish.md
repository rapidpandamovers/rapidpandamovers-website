# White Glove Moving Day Timeline: Start to Finish

When you book a white glove move, the process looks very different from a standard moving day. There's a structure to every hour, managed by someone whose only job is keeping things on track. Here's what a typical white glove moving day looks like for a four-bedroom home in Miami.

## 7:00 AM - Crew Arrival and Briefing

The team arrives at your home and meets with the move manager for a pre-work briefing. Each crew member receives their assignment: who handles the master bedroom, who packs the kitchen, who manages the crating station for artwork. The move manager walks the entire team through the home, pointing out items flagged during the pre-move walk-through. Floor protection goes down in hallways, staircases, and entryways before any furniture gets touched.

![A Chambermaid Arranges Pillows In A Luxu 1](/images/blog/2028/04/white-glove-moving-day-timeline-start-to-finish/a-chambermaid-arranges-pillows-in-a-luxu-1.webp)


## 7:30 AM - Packing Begins

If packing wasn't completed the previous day, the morning starts with final packing. White glove packing is methodical. Kitchen items are wrapped individually, artwork gets layered protection, and closets are transferred to wardrobe boxes and garment bags. Drawers are emptied, contents packed, and the empty furniture gets wrapped in moving pads. In large homes across Pinecrest and Palmetto Bay, this packing phase can run two to three hours with a full crew.

## 10:00 AM - Loading Starts

Loading follows a specific order. Heavy, sturdy items go on the truck first: dressers, desks, dining tables (already disassembled). Custom-crated artwork and mirrors load into padded vertical slots. Sofas and upholstered pieces get layered with protective blankets and secured with straps. Fragile items load last to minimize weight stacking on top of them. The truck is packed with intention, not just filled.

## 12:00 PM - Mid-Day Check

The move manager does a full walk-through of the origin home. Every room, closet, cabinet, and storage area gets checked. Garage, attic, outdoor storage, pool equipment rooms. Nothing gets left behind. This checkpoint also confirms the house is in good condition, with no damage to walls, floors, or fixtures from the loading process.

![A Stylish Woman In A White Dress Holds A 3](/images/blog/2028/04/white-glove-moving-day-timeline-start-to-finish/a-stylish-woman-in-a-white-dress-holds-a-3.webp)


## 12:30 PM - Transit

For local Miami moves, transit between homes typically takes 30 to 90 minutes depending on distance and traffic. Our team avoids scheduling departures during I-95 peak hours whenever possible. For high-rise destinations in Brickell or Sunny Isles, the move manager confirms the elevator reservation and loading dock availability before the truck leaves the origin.

## 1:30 PM - Arrival and Setup at Destination

The crew lays floor protection at the new home before unloading begins. Runners cover every path from the truck to each room. If you're moving into a condo, elevator pads go up, building staff are notified, and the dock is secured. The move manager reviews the floor plan with the crew so furniture goes directly to its assigned location.

## 2:00 PM - Unloading and Placement

Furniture comes off the truck in reverse order, with fragile items first. Each piece goes directly to its designated room. The move manager references the placement plan, directing the crew to position furniture exactly where it belongs. Beds get reassembled, dining tables go back together, and shelving units are rebuilt. You can adjust placement in real time as you see how things fit in the new space.

## 4:00 PM - Unpacking

With furniture in place, the team begins unpacking boxes. Kitchen items go into designated cabinets. Books line shelves. Clothes hang in closets. Decorative items return to surfaces. For full white glove service, this continues until every box is emptied and every item has a home.

![Black Leather Driving Gloves Placed On A 4](/images/blog/2028/04/white-glove-moving-day-timeline-start-to-finish/black-leather-driving-gloves-placed-on-a-4.webp)


## 6:00 PM - Final Walk-Through and Cleanup

The move manager walks every room with you, confirming placement and noting any adjustments. All packing materials, boxes, and debris are loaded back onto the truck for disposal. Protective floor coverings are removed. The result is a home that looks lived-in, organized, and ready for your first night.
