Your move is in two days and your home looks exactly the same as it did last week. The boxes are still flat, the closets are still full, and the panic is setting in. This happens more often than anyone admits. Here are ten moves you can make right now to get back on track.

## 1. Stop Sorting and Start Boxing

When time is short, the worst thing you can do is stand in front of a closet deciding what stays and what goes. Box everything. Tape the box, label the room it came from, and move on. You can sort at the new place after the pressure is off. Perfection is the enemy of packed.

![A Cardboard Box Labeled With Winter Clot 2](/images/blog/2029/10/10-quick-tips-when-youre-running-out-of-time/a-cardboard-box-labeled-with-winter-clot-2.webp)


## 2. Use Trash Bags for Soft Items

Garbage bags are the fastest packing material you own. Slide one over a group of hanging clothes, tie the hangers together, and you have a portable wardrobe bundle that takes about 30 seconds. Bags also work for pillows, blankets, towels, stuffed animals, and any soft goods that will not break.

## 3. Pack Room by Room, Not Category by Category

Do not wander around collecting all the books from every room or gathering every electronic device. Pick one room, pack everything in it, tape the boxes, and close the door. Working room by room gives you visible progress and prevents the scattered half-packed chaos that eats hours.

## 5. Create a "Do Not Pack" Zone

Designate one corner or one room as the spot for essentials that ride with you on moving day, not on the truck. This includes:

![Crop Anonymous Guy In Casual Clothes Usi 1](/images/blog/2029/10/10-quick-tips-when-youre-running-out-of-time/crop-anonymous-guy-in-casual-clothes-usi-1.webp)


- Medications and toiletries for the next 48 hours
- Phone chargers, laptop, and important documents
- A change of clothes for everyone in the household
- Snacks, water, and basic cleaning supplies for the new place
- Pet supplies if applicable

Everything else gets packed and loaded. Having a clear "do not touch" area prevents the frantic last-minute search for car keys buried in a box.

## 6. Accept Every Offer of Help

Now is not the time to be independent. If someone offers to help, say yes and give them a specific task. Send one friend to the kitchen with boxes and tape. Put another in charge of the garage. People want to help but need direction. A clear assignment like "pack everything on these shelves into this box" is more useful than "just help wherever."

## 7. Call Multiple Moving Companies

Moving companies often have last-minute openings, especially midweek. Call several and ask about availability in the next 48 to 72 hours. Be upfront about your timeline -- companies that handle [**last-minute moving**](/last-minute-moving) are used to short-notice requests and can often mobilize a crew faster than you expect.

## 8. Photograph Everything Electronic

Before unplugging your TV, router, gaming console, or desktop setup, take photos of the cable arrangement from multiple angles. This takes 60 seconds and saves 60 minutes of frustration during reconnection. Label the cables with painter's tape if you have time, but the photos alone are enough in a pinch.

## 9. Use a Storage Unit as a Pressure Valve

If you cannot get everything moved by your deadline, a short-term storage unit buys you breathing room. Move the non-essential items into [**storage**](/storage-solutions) first, get the critical stuff into your new home, and circle back for the rest over the following week. This turns one impossible day into two manageable trips.

## 10. Prioritize Sleep the Night Before

Exhausted packing at 2 AM leads to broken dishes, forgotten boxes in back closets, and poor decisions about what to keep and toss. Set a cutoff time, get six hours of sleep, and finish the remaining packing in the morning when you can think clearly. A rested four-hour session is more productive than a delirious eight-hour marathon.

Some companies, including ours, can handle moves booked 24 to 48 hours in advance, depending on crew availability and the size of the job. Weekdays and mid-month dates have the best last-minute availability.

**Is it more expensive to book movers last minute?**
Rates are typically the same, but your date and time options are more limited. Booking even one or two days earlier gives you better scheduling flexibility.

**What if I run out of boxes?**
Laundry baskets, suitcases, dresser drawers, and large reusable bags all work as temporary packing containers. You can also use garbage bags for anything soft and unbreakable. The goal is to get items contained and transportable, not perfectly boxed.
