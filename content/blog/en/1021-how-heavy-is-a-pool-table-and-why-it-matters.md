# How Heavy Is a Pool Table and Why It Matters

When most people buy a pool table, they're thinking about the playing surface, the felt color, and where it's going to look best in the game room. Weight is rarely on their mind. That changes fast when it's time to move. A pool table is one of the heaviest single items in any home, and understanding just how heavy yours is will help you plan a safe, damage-free move.

## Pool Table Weights by Size and Type

Pool table weight varies dramatically based on size, construction, and materials. Here are typical ranges:

![A Close Up Of A Game In Progress On A Bi 3](/images/blog/2028/09/how-heavy-is-a-pool-table-and-why-it-matters/a-close-up-of-a-game-in-progress-on-a-bi-3.webp)


### Slate Tables

- **7-foot table**: 600-750 pounds
- **8-foot table**: 750-900 pounds
- **9-foot table (regulation)**: 900-1,100 pounds
- **Snooker table (12-foot)**: 1,200-1,500 pounds

The slate itself accounts for about half the total weight. A three-piece slate set for an 8-foot table weighs around 450 pounds combined (150 pounds per section). The rest comes from the hardwood frame, legs, and rail assemblies.

### MDF Tables

- **7-foot table**: 200-300 pounds
- **8-foot table**: 250-350 pounds

MDF tables are significantly lighter because the fiberboard playing surface weighs a fraction of what slate does. But "lighter" is relative. Even a 250-pound table is far too heavy for two people to safely maneuver through a doorway or down stairs.

### Coin-Operated Bar Tables

- **Standard bar table**: 350-500 pounds

These tables have slate playing surfaces plus the added weight of the coin mechanism and ball return system. They're compact (usually 7-foot) but surprisingly heavy for their size.

## Why Weight Matters for Your Move

### Crew Size

Weight directly determines how many movers you need. Our [**Pool Table Moving**](/pool-table-moving) team assigns crew members based on your table's specifications:

- **Under 300 pounds (MDF)**: 2-3 movers
- **600-900 pounds (standard slate)**: 3-4 movers
- **Over 900 pounds (large slate or snooker)**: 4-5 movers

Understaffing a pool table move is how injuries happen. Each person carrying a 200-pound slate piece up a staircase is bearing serious load. Having one extra person means nobody is at their physical limit.

### Floor Load Capacity

A 900-pound pool table concentrates its weight on 4-6 small contact points (the legs and any center supports). That's 150-225 pounds per leg, pressing into a few square inches of floor. On a concrete slab, like most Miami ground-floor homes, this isn't a concern. But on a second floor with wooden joists, or on a floating laminate floor, you need to verify the structure can handle the concentrated load.

We've seen pool tables crack floor tiles, dent hardwood, and even cause noticeable dips in poorly supported upper floors. Using furniture pads or weight-distribution plates under the legs solves most of these problems.

### Transportation

Heavier tables need larger trucks and more secure loading. A three-piece slate set at 450+ pounds requires vertical storage against the truck wall with heavy-duty straps. The truck's suspension and driving route matter too. Potholes and speed bumps that you'd never notice in a car can bounce and shift heavy slate pieces if they're not secured properly.

### Elevator and Building Limits

Miami's high-rise condos have weight limits on freight elevators, typically 2,500 to 5,000 pounds. A pool table won't exceed these limits on its own, but add the movers and their equipment, and you're closer than you'd think. We always check elevator weight limits and plan our loads accordingly.

## How to Find Your Table's Weight

If you don't know your table's weight, try these approaches:

![A Row Of Empty Billiard Tables In A Diml 2](/images/blog/2028/09/how-heavy-is-a-pool-table-and-why-it-matters/a-row-of-empty-billiard-tables-in-a-diml-2.webp)


- **Check the manufacturer's website**: Search your table's brand and model. Most manufacturers list the shipping weight.
- **Look for a label**: Many tables have a sticker or plate on the underside of the frame with model info and weight.
