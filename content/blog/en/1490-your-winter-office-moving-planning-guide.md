Winter is one of the best times to relocate a business in Miami. Many companies plan their office moves for late December or January to take advantage of holiday slowdowns, year-end lease transitions, and lighter staff schedules. If your office move is coming up, here is a step-by-step planning guide.

## Why Winter Works for Office Moves

Several factors make the winter months a smart window for commercial relocation:

![Moving Company Team With Van Parked Outs 3](/images/blog/2029/12/your-winter-office-moving-planning-guide/moving-company-team-with-van-parked-outs-3.webp)


- **Holiday downtime** -- Many businesses already have reduced operations between Christmas and New Year's, which means less disruption to daily work.
- **Off-peak moving rates** -- Commercial movers often have better availability and pricing during winter compared to the busy summer season.
- **Lease timing** -- Many commercial leases run on a calendar year, making January 1 a natural transition date.
- **Comfortable weather** -- Miami's winter temperatures in the 70s are far better for loading trucks and carrying furniture than the summer heat and afternoon storms.

## Planning Timeline

A well-organized [office move](/office-moving) starts well before moving day. Here is a general timeline:

### 8 to 12 Weeks Before

- **Finalize the new lease** and confirm your move-in date with the landlord.
- **Hire commercial movers** who specialize in office relocation. Get quotes from at least two companies.
- **Assign a move coordinator** -- One person on your team should own the logistics, from vendor communication to floor plan decisions.
- **Audit your inventory** -- Decide what furniture, equipment, and supplies are moving to the new space and what gets donated, recycled, or discarded.

### 4 to 8 Weeks Before

- **Create a floor plan** for the new office. Map out where desks, conference tables, printers, and server racks will go.
- **Notify your IT team or provider** -- They will need time to plan the network setup, phone system transfer, and equipment disconnect/reconnect.
- **Order new furniture** if needed, and confirm delivery dates align with your move schedule.
- **Notify clients and vendors** of your upcoming address change with a specific effective date.

### 1 to 4 Weeks Before

- **Back up all digital data** -- Full server backups, cloud sync checks, and individual workstation backups.
- **Label everything** -- Color-coded labels by department or area make unloading and setup much faster.
- **Photograph cable setups** behind desks and in server rooms so IT can replicate the configuration at the new location.
- **Coordinate building logistics** -- Reserve freight elevators, confirm loading dock access, and check for any building-specific move-in requirements at both locations.
- **Communicate with employees** -- Share the move date, packing instructions, and what they are responsible for (personal items, desk contents).

### Moving Day

- **Start early** -- An early morning start gives the crew a full day to load, transport, and unload.
- **Have the move coordinator on-site** at both locations to answer questions and direct furniture placement.
- **IT goes first** -- Servers, networking equipment, and phone systems should be set up and tested before employees arrive on the first workday.
- **Do a walkthrough** at the old office after everything is out to check for left-behind items and document the condition for your landlord.

## IT and Technology Checklist

Technology is usually the most sensitive part of an office move. A few steps can prevent downtime:

- **Disconnect equipment properly** -- Shut down servers following the correct procedure, label every cable, and pack monitors in padded boxes or original packaging.
- **Plan your internet transfer** -- Contact your ISP at least four weeks ahead. Some providers need to schedule a technician visit to activate service at the new address.
- **Test before the first workday** -- Set up and test internet, phones, printers, and internal systems the day before staff returns.
- **Have a backup plan** -- Keep a mobile hotspot available in case the internet connection is not ready on day one.

## Reducing Employee Disruption

- **Allow remote work during the move** -- If possible, let employees work from home for one to two days while the new office is being set up.
- **Pack personal desk items in advance** -- Give employees labeled bins or boxes a week before the move so they can pack their own belongings.
- **Provide a first-day guide** -- Share parking information, building entry procedures, nearby lunch options, and the new office layout before employees show up.

## FAQ

**How long does an office move take?**
A small office (under 10 employees) can often be moved in a single day. Larger offices may take a weekend or require a phased approach spread over several days.

**Should we move over a weekend or during the week?**
Weekends are popular because they avoid workday disruption. However, if your building restricts weekend move-ins or your team is already off for the holidays, a weekday move during a holiday week can work just as well.

**What should employees pack themselves?**
Personal items, desk decorations, and any sensitive documents. The moving crew handles furniture, equipment, and labeled office boxes.
