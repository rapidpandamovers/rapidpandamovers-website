Moving to Upper Eastside this summer? This Miami neighborhood between 50th and 77th streets along Biscayne Boulevard has a character all its own, with mid-century architecture, local boutiques, and a community that actually knows each other. Here's what you need to know to settle in.

## Why Upper Eastside Stands Out

Upper Eastside sits north of Edgewater and east of Little Haiti, with Biscayne Bay to the east and Miami Shores to the north. The neighborhood is best known for the MiMo (Miami Modern) Historic District, a 27-block stretch of Biscayne Boulevard lined with restored 1950s and 1960s hotels, boutiques, and restaurants.

![A Beautiful View Of A Drawbridge Raised  3](/images/blog/2029/06/settling-into-upper-eastside-tips-for-new-residents/a-beautiful-view-of-a-drawbridge-raised--3.webp)


Unlike some of Miami's flashier neighborhoods, Upper Eastside has a lived-in, eclectic feel. You'll find art galleries next to family-owned Cuban restaurants, vintage shops beside yoga studios, and banyan-shaded streets that feel a world away from the high-rise corridors downtown.

### Getting Around

Upper Eastside is well-positioned for commuters. Downtown and Brickell are a short drive south on Biscayne Boulevard. I-95 access at 79th Street connects you to the rest of Miami-Dade County, and the 79th Street Causeway takes you to Miami Beach in about 10 minutes.

Public transit options include Metrobus routes along Biscayne Boulevard and NE 2nd Avenue. Biking is increasingly popular too, with dedicated lanes on several streets.

### The Community Vibe

This isn't a neighborhood where people just sleep and commute. The Upper Eastside has an active civic association, regular neighborhood cleanups, and community events. Legion Park on Biscayne Bay hosts farmers markets, outdoor movies, and fitness classes. The nearby Morningside Park is another popular gathering spot for families and dog owners.

## What Makes Upper Eastside Special

### The MiMo Historic District

The stretch of Biscayne Boulevard from roughly 50th to 77th Street is the heart of the neighborhood. The restored MiMo-style buildings house some of Miami's best independent restaurants and shops, including Politan Row (a food hall in a converted motel), Blue Collar restaurant, and Jimmy's East Side Diner.

### Housing Options

Upper Eastside offers a mix of housing types:

- **Historic single-family homes**: 1920s-1940s Bungalows and Mediterranean Revival homes on tree-lined streets in Morningside and Bayside
- **Condos and apartments**: Mid-rise buildings along Biscayne Boulevard and newer developments
- **Townhouses**: A growing number of new construction projects
- **Live-work spaces**: Converted commercial buildings popular with artists and entrepreneurs

### Cost of Living

Home prices in Upper Eastside have climbed steadily, but the neighborhood still offers better value than waterfront areas like Brickell or Miami Beach. Rental prices vary widely depending on whether you're in a renovated Art Deco building on the boulevard or a single-family home in the residential streets. A local realtor who knows the sub-neighborhoods (Morningside, Bayside, Palm Grove) can help you find the right fit.

## Moving to Upper Eastside in Summer

Summer is a busy time for Miami moves, and Upper Eastside has some specific things to plan for.

### Timing Your Move

- **Parking**: Street parking on residential streets is limited, and Biscayne Boulevard has metered spots and loading zone restrictions. Your moving company should coordinate with the city for temporary parking permits if needed
- **Building access**: Condo buildings along the boulevard typically require elevator reservations and may restrict move-in hours
- **Weather**: June through September means afternoon thunderstorms almost daily. Book a morning move to beat the rain
- **Traffic**: Biscayne Boulevard gets congested during rush hours. Plan for early morning starts or mid-day moves

### What to Know Before You Arrive

Upper Eastside is a tight-knit community, so a few things will help you fit in faster:

- Introduce yourself to neighbors. People here actually talk to each other
- Join the Upper Eastside Civic Association for updates on local issues
- Visit Legion Park's Saturday morning farmers market, one of the best in Miami
- Explore the side streets off Biscayne Boulevard where many of the neighborhood's best spots hide

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Upper Eastside safe?**
Upper Eastside is generally considered safe, particularly in the Morningside and Bayside sub-neighborhoods. Like any urban area, awareness matters, but the active community presence helps keep the neighborhood secure.

![Aerial Daytime View Of Miami Florida Cap 2](/images/blog/2029/06/settling-into-upper-eastside-tips-for-new-residents/aerial-daytime-view-of-miami-florida-cap-2.webp)


**What are the best schools near Upper Eastside?**
Morningside K-8 Center is the local public school with strong community involvement. Several charter and private school options are also within a short drive, including MAST Academy on Virginia Key.

**Is Upper Eastside walkable?**
Biscayne Boulevard is very walkable with shops, restaurants, and services. The residential streets are quieter and pleasant for walking, though most errands will involve a short drive or bus ride.

**How far is Upper Eastside from the beach?**
The 79th Street Causeway connects directly to North Beach, about a 10-minute drive depending on traffic.
