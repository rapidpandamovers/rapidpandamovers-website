Miami Shores packs village charm into 2.9 square miles between North Miami and Miami proper. With 1920s Mediterranean Revival architecture, top-rated schools, and a 15-minute drive to Brickell, this community attracts families and professionals seeking small-town atmosphere with urban access. Here is how to make your Miami Shores move successful.

## Why Choose Miami Shores?

Miami Shores earned its "Village" title honestly. With just over 10,000 residents spread across 2.9 square miles, this community feels like a small town tucked between North Miami and Miami proper. The historic architecture dates back to the 1920s Mediterranean Revival era, giving the streets a character you won't find in newer developments like Doral or Aventura.

![Loc View Of Miami Skyline From Coral Gables  2](/images/blog/2027/12/miami-shores-moving-tips-local-insights-for-relocators/loc-view-of-miami-skyline-from-coral-gables--2.webp)


Families flock here for Miami Shores Elementary, one of Miami-Dade's top-rated public schools. Professionals appreciate the 15-minute drive to Brickell and the Metrorail access at nearby stations. Retirees love the walkable village center and the Bayshore Golf Course community.

### Location and Accessibility

Miami Shores sits between Biscayne Bay and I-95, making it one of the better-connected smaller communities in the county. NE 2nd Avenue runs through the village center, connecting you to local shops and restaurants. US-1 (Biscayne Boulevard) borders the east side, providing a direct route to Downtown Miami, Wynwood, and the Design District.

For commuters heading to Coral Gables, Kendall, or Miami Beach, expect 20-30 minute drives depending on traffic. The Miami-Dade Metrorail has stations in nearby North Miami (Tri-Rail) for regional connections.

### Community and Lifestyle

The Miami Shores Community Center hosts farmers markets, movie nights, and fitness classes year-round. The Bayshore Golf Course offers 18 holes along the bay, open to residents and visitors. December brings the annual holiday parade down NE 2nd Avenue, where local businesses and families decorate floats.

## Neighborhoods to Consider

Miami Shores packs distinct micro-neighborhoods into its compact footprint. Each block has its own personality, from waterfront estates to cozy bungalows.

### Choosing Your Perfect Spot

**Bayshore/Grand Concourse Area**: The most prestigious section, featuring waterfront homes on Biscayne Bay. Expect prices starting at $1.5M for bay access. This area connects to the golf course and offers some of the best sunset views in Miami-Dade.

**Village Center (NE 2nd Avenue)**: Walkable to shops, restaurants, and Barry University. More affordable than bayfront, with 1940s-era homes ranging from $500K-$800K. Perfect for young professionals who want urban convenience with a suburban feel.

**West Miami Shores**: Closer to I-95, this area offers the most affordable entry point. Homes from the 1950s-60s in the $400K-$600K range. You trade bay proximity for quicker highway access to Brickell, Coral Gables, and Pinecrest.

Consider these factors when choosing:

- **Proximity to work and schools**: Barry University is in the village; commutes to Downtown take 15-20 minutes via US-1
- **Local amenities**: The Village Center has coffee shops, restaurants, and boutiques within walking distance
- **Property types**: Mostly single-family homes; limited condo and townhouse options compared to Aventura or Miami Beach
- **Community vibe**: Family-oriented with active neighborhood associations and regular community events

## Moving to Miami Shores in December

December ranks among the best months to move anywhere in South Florida. Temperatures hover in the mid-70s, humidity drops to comfortable levels, and afternoon thunderstorms disappear until May. Your furniture won't be sitting in a steamy truck, and you won't be drenched in sweat hauling boxes.

### Timing Your Move

Plan around these Miami Shores specifics:

- **Best moving days**: Mid-week moves (Tuesday through Thursday) offer better rates and less competition for parking on residential streets
- **Village events**: Check the Miami Shores Village calendar for the holiday parade and tree lighting. NE 2nd Avenue closes for events, which can delay trucks trying to reach homes near the village center
- **Snowbird season**: December through March brings seasonal residents to South Florida. Moving companies book faster during this period, so schedule 2-3 weeks ahead

## Essential Services to Locate

As a new Miami Shores resident, here are the specific places you need to know:

- **Healthcare**: Jackson North Medical Center is 10 minutes away in North Miami. Baptist Health has an urgent care on Biscayne Boulevard for minor issues
- **Schools**: Miami Shores Elementary (K-5) and Horace Mann Middle School serve the village. Private options include Barry University's lab school
- **Grocery**: Publix on Biscayne Boulevard, Whole Foods in Aventura (15 minutes), and a weekly farmers market at the Community Center
- **Recreation**: Miami Shores Aquatic Center for swimming, Bayshore Golf Course for golf, and multiple parks including Miami Shores Dog Park on NE 99th Street

## Benefits of Living in Miami Shores

Residents who choose Miami Shores gain access to several advantages:

- **Top-rated schools**: Miami Shores Elementary ranks among Miami-Dade's best public schools
- **Historic character**: 1920s Mediterranean Revival architecture creates a unique neighborhood aesthetic
- **Walkable village center**: Shops, restaurants, and services clustered along NE 2nd Avenue
- **Strong property values**: Historic designation and limited housing stock support steady appreciation
- **Community engagement**: Active village government, regular events, and established neighborhood associations
- **Bay access**: Bayshore Golf Course and nearby marinas provide waterfront recreation
