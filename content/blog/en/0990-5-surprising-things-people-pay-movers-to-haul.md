Whether you're a long-time Miami resident or just making your July move to South Florida, there's always something new to discover. Here's our curated list to help you make the most of life in the Sunshine State this summer.

## 1. Taxidermy Collections

From mounted sailfish to full-size bears, taxidermy requires careful handling. One Coral Gables client had us move an entire trophy room, including antlers and a prized tarpon caught off Key Biscayne.

![Person In A Ghost Costume With Sunglasse 1](/images/blog/2028/02/5-surprising-things-people-pay-movers-to-haul/person-in-a-ghost-costume-with-sunglasse-1.webp)


## 2. Life-Size Statues

Garden statues, Buddha figures, and even custom mannequins. We once moved a 6-foot bronze dolphin from a Coconut Grove estate that weighed over 500 pounds. Miami's tropical gardens often feature elaborate statuary that needs expert handling.

## 3. Vintage Arcade Machines

Pinball machines and classic arcade cabinets are surprisingly heavy and incredibly fragile. The glass, electronics, and wooden cabinets all need special care.

## 4. Professional Kitchen Equipment

Commercial pizza ovens, industrial mixers, and walk-in freezer components. Miami has a thriving food scene, and when restaurant owners from Wynwood or Little Havana move home, their professional gear often comes too.

![Two People In Ghost Costumes With Sungla 3](/images/blog/2028/02/5-surprising-things-people-pay-movers-to-haul/two-people-in-ghost-costumes-with-sungla-3.webp)


## 5. Motorized Vehicles (Indoors)

Golf carts kept in garages, mobility scooters, and even a vintage motorcycle displayed in a Brickell condo living room. With so many golf communities in Miami-Dade County, indoor golf carts are more common than you'd think.
