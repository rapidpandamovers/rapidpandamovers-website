Miami attracts the rich and famous, and their homes are as impressive as you'd expect. This July, take a look at some of the most notable celebrity properties in the area, from Star Island mansions to South Beach penthouses.

## 1. Star Island Estates

Star Island sits in Biscayne Bay, connected to Miami Beach by a single bridge off MacArthur Causeway. With just 34 lots on the island, properties regularly sell for $30-50 million or more. Gloria Estefan's waterfront compound, Diddy's former estate at 39 Star Island Drive, and Rosie O'Donnell's property have all made headlines. Moving to Star Island requires passing through 24/7 security guards who verify every vehicle entering the island. Our team coordinates with island security weeks in advance to ensure smooth access.

## 2. Casa Casuarina (The Versace Mansion)

Located at 1116 Ocean Drive, this Mediterranean Revival mansion was Gianni Versace's home until his death in 1997. The fashion designer spent $33 million renovating and expanding the 1930 property, adding a 54-foot mosaic pool lined with 24-karat gold. Today it operates as The Villa Casa Casuarina, a boutique hotel. The property's 10 suites, private courtyard, and original Versace details make it one of South Beach's most photographed buildings. When celebrities or events require moves in and out, the narrow Ocean Drive location and tourist traffic present unique logistics challenges.

## 3. Fisher Island Properties

Fisher Island, accessible only by private ferry from Miami Beach Marina, has consistently ranked among America's wealthiest zip codes. The 216-acre island was once the Vanderbilt family's winter retreat. Today, approximately 700 residents enjoy a private golf course, tennis courts, marina, and beaches. Properties range from $3 million condos to $30 million estates. Every piece of furniture, appliance, and moving box must cross Biscayne Bay by ferry, requiring careful coordination with island management and ferry schedules.

## 4. Coral Gables Historic Estates

Coral Gables' tree-lined streets hide some of Miami's most impressive estates. Sylvester Stallone owned a Mediterranean Revival mansion on Leucadendra Drive. Madonna lived in a 1920s estate on North Bay Road (technically Miami Beach, but the Coral Gables area has similar properties). The city's strict architectural review board ensures new construction and renovations maintain the historic Mediterranean Revival aesthetic that defines the neighborhood. Moving antiques and artwork through narrow coral rock driveways and historic archways requires specialized equipment and experienced crews.

## 5. South Beach Penthouses

The Setai, Faena House, and One Thousand Museum attract celebrities seeking Miami Beach luxury with hotel-like amenities. Faena House's penthouse sold to hedge fund manager Ken Griffin for $60 million in 2015, setting Miami Beach records. One Thousand Museum, designed by Zaha Hadid, features a rooftop helipad and units starting at $5 million. Moving into these buildings requires freight elevator reservations, building management approval, and careful scheduling to avoid inconveniencing other residents. Our crews have moved families into all three buildings and know each property's specific requirements.

## 6. Indian Creek Island Estates

Indian Creek Island, often called "Billionaire Bunker," might be America's most exclusive address. The 300-acre island has just 40 lots, its own police force, and a private golf course. Tom Brady and Gisele Bundchen purchased a lot here for $17 million, joining residents like Jared Kushner, Ivanka Trump, Carl Icahn, and Julio Iglesias. The island has one entrance with 24/7 security that rivals diplomatic compounds. Moving to Indian Creek requires extensive background checks, vehicle registration, and pre-approval of every person entering the island.
