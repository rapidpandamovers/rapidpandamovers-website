Welcome to your March guide for moving to Opa-locka! Whether you're relocating from Hialeah, Miami Gardens, or making a bigger move from outside South Florida, getting to know your new community helps you settle in faster and feel at home sooner.

## Why Choose Opa-locka?

Opa-locka stands out as one of Miami-Dade County's most unique communities. Founded in 1926 by aviation pioneer Glenn Curtiss, the city was designed around an Arabian Nights theme, resulting in the largest collection of Moorish Revival architecture in the Western Hemisphere. City Hall alone features domes, minarets, and horseshoe arches that make it a registered historic landmark.

![A Blue Truck Transporting Livestock On A 5](/images/blog/2029/03/the-newcomers-guide-to-opa-locka-living/a-blue-truck-transporting-livestock-on-a-5.webp)


The area attracts families, professionals, and retirees alike, thanks to its quality of life, convenient access to major employment centers, and excellent amenities.

### Location and Accessibility

Opa-locka sits in northern Miami-Dade County with quick access to I-95 and the Palmetto Expressway (SR 826). The Tri-Rail station connects you to Fort Lauderdale and West Palm Beach for work commutes. Downtown Miami is about 15 miles south, while Miami Beach is roughly 20 minutes east. Miami Gardens borders to the north, and Hialeah sits just to the south, giving you easy access to shopping and dining in both areas.

### Community and Lifestyle

Opa-locka residents enjoy a strong sense of community. Sherbondy Park on Ali Baba Avenue hosts youth sports leagues and community gatherings. The annual Opa-locka Hialeah Flea Market draws bargain hunters from across Miami-Dade every weekend. Local churches and community organizations run food drives, youth programs, and cultural events throughout the year.

## Neighborhoods to Consider

When planning your move to Opa-locka, the Downtown area draws newcomers who appreciate the Moorish Revival architecture. The Magnolia North neighborhood offers quieter residential streets, while areas near the Opa-locka Executive Airport attract aviation workers and enthusiasts. If you want more urban amenities, consider neighborhoods closer to Hialeah's shopping centers or Miami Gardens' entertainment options.

## Essential Services to Locate

As a new Opa-locka resident, here are the key services you will need:

![Tow Truck Operator Loading White Gmc Pic 4](/images/blog/2029/03/the-newcomers-guide-to-opa-locka-living/tow-truck-operator-loading-white-gmc-pic-4.webp)

**Healthcare**: Jackson North Medical Center is 10 minutes away in North Miami Beach. For everyday care, Jessie Trice Community Health System operates a clinic on NW 27th Avenue. CVS and Walgreens pharmacies dot the major roads.

**Groceries**: Presidente Supermarket on NW 27th Avenue carries Caribbean and Latin American products. Sedano's in nearby Hialeah is a short drive. For bigger shopping trips, Walmart Supercenter in Miami Gardens covers most needs.

**Schools**: Nathan B. Young Elementary and Opa-locka Elementary serve the city. Middle and high school students typically attend schools in Hialeah or Miami Gardens. Several charter schools offer alternatives, including Dr. Manuel C. Barreiro Elementary.

**Recreation**: Sherbondy Park offers basketball courts, playgrounds, and picnic areas. Amelia Earhart Park in Hialeah is a 10-minute drive with disc golf, lakes, and petting zoo. The Opa-locka Executive Airport hosts aviation events and flight schools.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How far is Opa-locka from Downtown Miami?

Opa-locka is about 15 miles north of Downtown Miami. With normal traffic, expect a 20-25 minute drive via I-95 or the Palmetto Expressway. Rush hour can add 15-20 minutes to that commute.

### What makes Opa-locka architecture unique?

Opa-locka features the largest collection of Moorish Revival architecture in the Western Hemisphere. City Hall and many original buildings from the 1920s showcase domes, minarets, and arched doorways inspired by Arabian Nights stories.

### Are there good schools in Opa-locka?

Opa-locka has several public schools in the Miami-Dade County system. Nathan B. Young Elementary and Opa-locka Elementary serve younger students, while middle and high schoolers attend nearby schools in Hialeah or Miami Gardens. Several charter and private school options exist within a short drive.
