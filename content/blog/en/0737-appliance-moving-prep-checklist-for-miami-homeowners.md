# Appliance Moving Prep Checklist for Miami Homeowners

Moving in Miami comes with conditions you won't find in most other cities. The heat, the humidity, the narrow condo hallways, and the HOA rules all affect how you need to prepare your appliances. This checklist covers every step, organized by timing, so nothing falls through the cracks.

## Two Weeks Before Moving Day

- [ ] **Inventory all appliances.** List everything you're moving: fridge, washer, dryer, dishwasher, oven, microwave, wine cooler, and any specialty items.
- [ ] **Measure doorways at both locations.** Miami condos often have narrow interior doors, and older homes in Little Havana and Allapattah can have non-standard frame widths. Measure every passage between each appliance and the moving truck.
- [ ] **Check elevator and HOA requirements.** Many Brickell and Downtown Miami buildings require advance elevator reservations for moves. Some restrict appliance moves to specific hours or require insurance certificates from your moving company.
- [ ] **Order transit bolts for your washer.** If you've lost the original transit bolts, order replacements now. They take a few days to ship and you cannot safely move a washer without them.
- [ ] **Download appliance manuals.** Search your model numbers on the manufacturer's website and download PDFs. Most manuals include moving-specific instructions.
- [ ] **Decide what to move vs. replace.** Appliances over 10 years old may not be worth the cost of moving. Factor in the age, condition, and whether the new home's hookups match.

## One Week Before Moving Day

- [ ] **Use up refrigerator and freezer contents.** Plan meals around what's in the fridge and freezer. Donate unopened, non-perishable items to a local food bank.
- [ ] **Buy supplies.** You'll need appliance tape (not duct tape), moving blankets, zip-lock bags for hardware, and a bucket for draining hoses.
- [ ] **Schedule gas disconnection.** If you have a gas range or dryer, arrange for a licensed technician to disconnect the gas lines on or before moving day.
- [ ] **Photograph all connections.** Take clear photos of the water lines, gas connections, electrical outlets, and vent hookups behind each appliance. These photos save time during reconnection.
- [ ] **Clean the appliances.** Miami's humidity means mold grows fast in sealed, damp spaces. Clean the inside of your fridge, washer drum, dishwasher, and any rubber gaskets. A damp appliance locked in a moving truck becomes a mold problem within hours.

## 48 Hours Before Moving Day

- [ ] **Unplug and defrost the refrigerator.** Open the doors, place towels around the base, and let it defrost completely. Miami's warm air means more condensation during defrosting, so check the towels regularly.
- [ ] **Defrost the chest freezer.** Same process. A chest freezer can hold a surprising amount of ice buildup.
- [ ] **Run an empty dishwasher cycle.** Use hot water to flush out food residue. Leave the door cracked open afterward to dry.

## 24 Hours Before Moving Day

- [ ] **Disconnect the washer.** Turn off water valves, remove hoses, drain residual water, and install transit bolts.
- [ ] **Disconnect the dryer.** Unplug electric dryers. For gas dryers, confirm the technician has capped the gas line.
- [ ] **Clean and dry the washer drum.** Leave the door open overnight to air dry. Wipe down the rubber gasket on front-loaders.
- [ ] **Remove oven racks, burner grates, and knobs.** Wrap them individually and pack in a labeled box.
- [ ] **Remove fridge shelves and drawers.** Wrap separately and pack. Tape the power cord to the back of the fridge.
- [ ] **Secure all appliance doors.** Use appliance tape or ratchet straps. Test that each door is fully secured and won't swing open.
- [ ] **Tape cords and hoses to the back of each unit.** Loose cords catch on doorframes and trip people.

## Moving Day

- [ ] **Confirm floor protection is in place.** Masonite boards or moving blankets should cover the path from each appliance to the door.
- [ ] **Verify clearance paths are clear.** Move any furniture, rugs, or obstacles out of the way.
- [ ] **Check the truck temperature.** In summer months, a closed truck in Miami can reach 140+ degrees. Plan timing so appliances aren't sitting in extreme heat for hours.
- [ ] **Label appliance hardware bags.** Zip-lock bags with small parts should be labeled and taped to the corresponding appliance.
- [ ] **Keep appliances upright.** Refrigerators, freezers, and wine coolers should stay vertical during transport.

## At the New Home

- [ ] **Let the fridge sit upright for 2-4 hours before plugging in.** This allows the compressor oil to settle.
- [ ] **Reconnect water lines and check for leaks.** Turn on water slowly and watch every connection.
- [ ] **Have gas lines reconnected by a professional.**
- [ ] **Remove transit bolts from the washer.** Run an empty cycle to test.
- [ ] **Run test cycles on all appliances.** Confirm the dishwasher drains, the dryer heats, and the oven reaches temperature.
