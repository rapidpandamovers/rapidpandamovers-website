Graduation season brings a surge of interstate relocations as families help graduates move for new jobs and opportunities. [**Long distance moving**](/long-distance-moving) requires different planning than local moves, with federal regulations and additional logistics to consider.

## Understanding Interstate Moving Regulations

Long distance moves crossing state lines fall under federal regulation. When hiring movers for interstate relocation:

![Highway Cross Country Interstate Checklist](/images/blog/2026/05/long-distance-moving-planning-for-graduation-season/highway-cross-country-interstate-checklist.webp)


- **USDOT Number**: All interstate movers must register with the U.S. Department of Transportation and display a valid USDOT number
- **FMCSA Database**: Verify any company's license and safety record through the Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration
- **Florida Requirements**: Movers must also register with FDACS (Florida Department of Agriculture and Consumer Services)

Always verify credentials before signing a contract. Legitimate companies willingly provide their registration numbers.

## Getting Accurate Estimates

### Request In-Home Surveys

For accurate pricing, all moving companies must perform a home walkthrough before providing a quote. This allows them to assess:

- Total volume and weight of belongings
- Special handling requirements
- Access challenges at both locations
- Packing needs

### Understanding Estimate Types

- **Binding estimate**: Price is locked in regardless of actual weight (get this in writing)
- **Non-binding estimate**: Final cost based on actual weight (can increase)
- **Binding not-to-exceed**: You pay the lesser of estimated or actual cost (best protection)

May is peak moving season. Request estimates 6-8 weeks in advance to ensure availability and competitive pricing.

## Preparing for Your Interstate Move

### Declutter Before Packing

Long distance moves are charged by weight. Reduce costs by:

- Donating furniture that won't fit your new space
- Selling items that cost more to move than replace
- Disposing of items with no value
- Giving away possessions to family and friends

### Documentation

Create a detailed inventory:

- Photograph valuable items before packing
- List serial numbers for electronics
- Keep jewelry and important documents with you
- Note the condition of furniture for insurance purposes

## Climate Considerations

Miami's heat and humidity require special precautions:

- **Climate-controlled transport**: Essential for wood furniture, electronics, and sensitive items
- **Storage options**: If you can't move in immediately, use climate-controlled storage
- **Miami departures**: Schedule morning pickup to avoid afternoon heat and thunderstorms

Professional movers protect belongings with water-resistant pads and controlled loading environments.

## Timeline for Long Distance Moves

- **8 weeks before**: Get quotes, verify credentials, book movers
- **6 weeks before**: Begin decluttering and selling unwanted items
- **4 weeks before**: Start packing non-essentials
- **2 weeks before**: Confirm arrangements, notify utilities, update address
- **1 week before**: Pack remaining items, prepare essentials box
- **Moving day**: Final walkthrough, verify inventory, keep valuables with you
