Planning a move in Miami? Before packing day arrives, you need to know what your movers legally cannot transport. From hazardous materials that pose fire risks in Florida's heat to perishable foods that won't survive a trip across town, these five items require separate arrangements.

## 1. Hazardous Materials

Gasoline, propane, paint thinner, aerosols, and chemicals are fire hazards and illegal to transport on moving trucks. In South Florida's hot climate, these items become even more dangerous. Take hazardous materials to your local Miami-Dade Household Hazardous Waste Collection or contact your neighborhood's waste disposal service.

![A Collection Of Labeled Cardboard Boxes  4](/images/blog/2025/10/5-items-that-cant-go-in-the-moving-van/a-collection-of-labeled-cardboard-boxes--4.webp)


## 2. Explosives and Ammunition

Fireworks, ammunition, and anything explosive must be disposed of or transported personally with proper safety measures.

## 3. Perishable Foods

Refrigerated and frozen items won't survive most moves, especially in Miami's year-round heat. Consume what you can, then donate unopened items to Feeding South Florida or your local food bank. Clear out your fridge at least 24 hours before moving day.

![A Collection Of Large Cardboard Boxes La 1](/images/blog/2025/10/5-items-that-cant-go-in-the-moving-van/a-collection-of-large-cardboard-boxes-la-1.webp)


## 4. Plants (for Long-Distance Moves)

Agricultural regulations often prohibit transporting plants across state lines due to pest concerns. If you're moving out of Florida, the USDA restricts certain tropical plants. For local Miami moves, we can often transport potted plants in your vehicle or ours with advance notice.

## 5. Pets and Living Creatures

Fish, pets, and any living animals cannot travel on moving trucks. Arrange separate, safe transportation for them.
