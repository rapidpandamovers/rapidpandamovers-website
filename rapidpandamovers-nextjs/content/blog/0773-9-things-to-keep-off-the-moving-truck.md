Whether you're a long-time Miami resident or just making your April move to South Florida, there's always something new to discover. Here's our curated list to help you make the most of life in the Sunshine State this spring.

## 1. Hazardous Materials

Gasoline, propane, paint thinner, aerosols, and chemicals are fire hazards and illegal to transport on moving trucks.

![Prohibited Items Moving Truck Safety Guide](/images/blog/2029/04/9-things-to-keep-off-the-moving-truck/prohibited-items-moving-truck-safety-guide.webp)


## 2. Explosives and Ammunition

Fireworks, ammunition, and anything explosive must be disposed of or transported personally with proper safety measures.

## 3. Perishable Foods

Refrigerated and frozen items won't survive most moves. Consume, donate, or dispose of perishables before moving day.

## 4. Plants (for Long-Distance Moves)

Agricultural regulations often prohibit transporting plants across state lines due to pest concerns.

![Prohibited Items Moving Truck Safety Overview](/images/blog/2029/04/9-things-to-keep-off-the-moving-truck/prohibited-items-moving-truck-safety-overview.webp)


## 5. Pets and Living Creatures

Fish, pets, and any living animals cannot travel on moving trucks. Arrange separate, safe transportation for them.

## 6. Irreplaceable Documents

Passports, birth certificates, financial records, and legal documents should stay with you personally during the move.

## 7. Jewelry and Cash

High-value, easily stolen items belong in your personal vehicle, not on a truck full of boxes.

![Prohibited Items Moving Truck Safety Steps](/images/blog/2029/04/9-things-to-keep-off-the-moving-truck/prohibited-items-moving-truck-safety-steps.webp)


## 8. Medications

Keep prescriptions accessible and in your possession—never packed in boxes that might be delayed or lost.

## 9. Pressurized Containers

Fire extinguishers, scuba tanks, and aerosol cans can explode under pressure or temperature changes during transport.
