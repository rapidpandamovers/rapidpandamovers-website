Your family's safety should be a top priority when moving into a new home this spring. You don't know who had access before you, and it's better to be proactive about security than to react after something happens.

## 1. Change All Locks Immediately

Previous owners, contractors, and real estate agents may have copies of keys. Start fresh with new locks.

![Security Safety Smart Home Checklist](/images/blog/2027/04/9-essential-security-features-for-new-homes/security-safety-smart-home-checklist.webp)


## 2. Check All Windows and Doors

Ensure every entry point locks properly. Replace or repair faulty locks right away.

## 3. Install or Update Security System

Whether professional monitoring or DIY smart devices, get a security system active immediately.

## 4. Add Exterior Lighting

Motion-sensor lights deter intruders and help you navigate safely at night.

## 5. Test Smoke and CO Detectors

Replace batteries in all detectors. If units are old, replace them entirely.

## 6. Secure the Garage

Install a new garage door opener and disable any previous remotes. The garage is often an overlooked entry point.

![Security Safety Smart Home Steps](/images/blog/2027/04/9-essential-security-features-for-new-homes/security-safety-smart-home-steps.webp)


## 7. Install Deadbolts

Upgrade to high-quality deadbolts on all exterior doors. Look for Grade 1 or Grade 2 ANSI-rated locks.

## 8. Trim Landscaping Near Entry Points

Cut back bushes and trees near windows and doors. Dense vegetation provides hiding spots for intruders.

## 9. Meet Your Neighbors

Building relationships with neighbors creates a community watch network. They can alert you to suspicious activity when you're away.
