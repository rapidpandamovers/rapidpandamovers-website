The first week in a new home sets the tone for everything that follows. These December priority tasks will help you get settled safely and start your new chapter on the right foot.

## 1. Change All the Locks

You don't know who has keys from before. Change locks immediately for security and peace of mind.

![Close Up Of A Cardboard Box Filled With  4](/images/blog/2025/12/7-first-things-to-do-when-you-move-into-a-new-home/close-up-of-a-cardboard-box-filled-with--4.webp)


## 2. Test All Appliances

Run the dishwasher, check the stove, test the washer/dryer. Identify problems while warranty claims are still fresh.

## 3. Locate Emergency Shutoffs

Find and label water main, electrical panel, and gas shutoff. You'll need to know these eventually, so better to find them now.

## 4. Set Up Mail Forwarding

Visit USPS.com to ensure nothing gets lost during the transition period. This should happen before you move.

## 5. Transfer Utilities to Your Name

Electricity, gas, water, internet. Confirm all accounts are active and in your name before move-in day.

![Crop Anonymous Guy In Casual Clothes Usi 2](/images/blog/2025/12/7-first-things-to-do-when-you-move-into-a-new-home/crop-anonymous-guy-in-casual-clothes-usi-2.webp)


## 6. Schedule Critical Installations

Internet, cable, security systems. Book these early as installation windows fill up quickly.

## 7. Deep Clean Before Unpacking

If you didn't do it before moving in, clean thoroughly now while rooms are still relatively empty.

## Miami-Specific First Week Tips

If you're moving to Miami, add these to your list:

- **Check your AC system** - Florida's humidity means your air conditioning is essential. Test it immediately and schedule maintenance if needed.
- **Hurricane preparedness** - Locate your hurricane shutters or impact windows, and find out where your neighborhood evacuation shelter is located.
- **FPL account setup** - Florida Power & Light is the main electricity provider. Set up your account at fpl.com for easy bill pay and outage alerts.
- **Miami-Dade County services** - Register for trash pickup and recycling at miamidade.gov.
