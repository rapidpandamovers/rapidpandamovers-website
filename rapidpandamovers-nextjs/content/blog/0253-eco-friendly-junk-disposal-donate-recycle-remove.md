# Eco-Friendly Junk Disposal: Donate, Recycle, Remove

Throwing everything into a dumpster and calling it done is the easy route, but it's not the right one. In Miami-Dade County alone, residents generate millions of tons of waste each year, and a huge percentage of what ends up in landfills could have been donated or recycled. When you're moving or doing a home cleanout, you have a real opportunity to keep usable items out of the waste stream.

## Donate First

Before anything gets hauled away, separate the items that still have life in them. Furniture, clothing, working appliances, kitchenware, and books can all find new homes through Miami's network of donation centers.

![A Chaotic Pile Of Metal Scrap And Waste  4](/images/blog/2025/12/eco-friendly-junk-disposal-donate-recycle-remove/a-chaotic-pile-of-metal-scrap-and-waste--4.webp)


**Where to donate in Miami:**

- **Goodwill** has locations across Miami-Dade, including stores on Coral Way, Bird Road, and in Hialeah. They accept furniture, clothing, electronics, and household items during store hours.
- **The Salvation Army** on NW 27th Avenue takes clothing, furniture, and appliances. They offer free pickup for larger items when you schedule in advance.
- **Habitat for Humanity ReStore** accepts building materials, appliances, and furniture in good condition. Their proceeds fund home-building projects in Miami-Dade.
- **Miami Rescue Mission** welcomes clothing, toiletries, and household essentials.
- **Lotus House** (the largest women's shelter in the U.S., right here in Miami) accepts clothing, children's items, and household goods.

Many of these organizations will pick up directly from your home for free, which saves you the trip and keeps the donation process simple.

## Recycle What Can't Be Donated

For items that aren't in good enough shape to donate but can still be processed, Miami-Dade County's recycling programs handle more than you might think.

**Curbside recycling** accepts paper, cardboard, plastics #1-7, glass, aluminum, and steel cans. Flatten boxes to save bin space and rinse containers before tossing them in.

**Electronics recycling** is available at Miami-Dade's drop-off locations, including events hosted throughout the year at various county parks. Old computers, phones, TVs, and cables all contain recoverable metals and need to stay out of regular trash.

**Metal recycing** is especially worthwhile. Scrap yards in the Miami area, like Sims Metal Management in Medley and SA Recycling in Hialeah, accept appliances, metal furniture frames, and other ferrous and non-ferrous metals. Some will even pay you for the material.

**Mattress recycling** keeps foam, springs, and fabric out of landfills. Several facilities in South Florida break down mattresses for component recycling.
