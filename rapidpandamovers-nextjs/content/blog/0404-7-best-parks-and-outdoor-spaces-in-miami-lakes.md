Miami Lakes is a park lover's paradise. With over 100 parks within its 6.5 square miles, this family-friendly town in northwest Miami-Dade offers abundant green space for recreation and relaxation. Located between Hialeah and Miramar, Miami Lakes attracts families seeking a suburban feel with easy access to downtown Miami, Doral, and the beaches. Here are seven parks and outdoor spaces worth exploring.

## 1. Miami Lakes Optimist Park

**6075 Miami Lakes Drive**

![Discover Shanghai S Serene Park With Lus 4](/images/blog/2026/11/7-best-parks-and-outdoor-spaces-in-miami-lakes/discover-shanghai-s-serene-park-with-lus-4.webp)


This multi-purpose sports complex is the heart of Miami Lakes athletics. Features include four baseball fields, a softball field, two soccer fields, four outdoor basketball courts, four tennis courts with pickleball lines, a quarter-mile lighted walking path, batting cages, and three picnic pavilions.

## 2. Royal Oaks Park

**16400 NW 77th Avenue**

This scenic 21.6-acre park offers beautiful walking and running trails through natural landscapes. The 0.7-mile path winds through diverse terrain, making it perfect for morning jogs or leisurely afternoon strolls. Shaded benches provide rest stops along the way.

## 3. Miami Lakes K9 Cove

**NW 82nd Avenue at Palmetto Frontage Road**

Dog owners love this dedicated recreation area where pups can run, play, and socialize off-leash. Separate areas for large and small dogs ensure safe play, while agility equipment adds extra fun. Shaded seating lets humans relax while watching the canine action.

## 4. Miami Lakes Picnic Park

**6075 Miami Lakes Drive**

![Lush Greenery And Charming Architecture  2](/images/blog/2026/11/7-best-parks-and-outdoor-spaces-in-miami-lakes/lush-greenery-and-charming-architecture--2.webp)


This serene green space is perfect for family gatherings and outdoor dining. Well-maintained picnic areas, walking paths, and benches create an inviting atmosphere for weekend relaxation. The scenic setting makes it a local favorite for birthday parties and celebrations.

## 5. Amelia Earhart Park

**401 E 65th Street, Hialeah** | $7 parking | 15-minute drive

Just outside Miami Lakes, this 515-acre facility offers incredible variety. Mountain biking trails, wakeboarding at Miami Watersports Complex, a fishing pier, and the five-acre Bark Park for dogs. Bill Graham Farm Village provides educational farm experiences for kids.

## 6. Country Lake Park

**5000 NW 170th Street**

This neighborhood gem features a beautiful lake as its centerpiece. Walking paths circle the water, offering peaceful scenery and opportunities to spot local wildlife. Playground equipment and picnic areas make it family-friendly.

## 7. Garden of the Arts

**6075 Miami Lakes Drive**

![Tranquil Waterfall Cascading In A Rocky  1](/images/blog/2026/11/7-best-parks-and-outdoor-spaces-in-miami-lakes/tranquil-waterfall-cascading-in-a-rocky--1.webp)


This unique outdoor space combines nature with culture. Public art installations dot the landscaped grounds, creating an inspiring setting for walks and contemplation. It's a hidden gem within the larger park complex that many residents discover over time.

## Tips for Park Visitors

South Florida's subtropical climate means year-round outdoor access, but plan accordingly. Early mornings and late afternoons offer cooler temperatures during summer months. Many parks have shaded areas, but bring sunscreen and water. Afternoon thunderstorms are common from May through October, so check the forecast before heading out.
