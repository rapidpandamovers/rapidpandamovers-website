You have been searching Zillow for months, comparing Brickell condos to Kendall townhouses. Then you found it: a 1920s Mediterranean home with a barrel-tile roof, arched doorways, and a backyard big enough for Sunday barbecues. Welcome to Coral Gables, "The City Beautiful."

## Why Coral Gables Stands Out

Coral Gables is not just another Miami suburb. George Merrick planned it in 1925 as a master-planned community, and that attention to design shows everywhere. Strict zoning codes preserve the Mediterranean Revival architecture, and homeowners maintain lush landscaping that makes every street feel like a postcard.

![Loc Students Walking Through A Sunlit Univer 2](/images/blog/2029/03/moving-to-coral-gables-what-you-need-to-know/loc-students-walking-through-a-sunlit-univer-2.webp)


The area attracts families looking for top-rated schools like Coral Gables Senior High, professionals working in Brickell or Downtown Miami, and retirees who want walkable dining on Miracle Mile without the chaos of South Beach.

### Getting Around from Coral Gables

Your commute options beat most Miami neighborhoods. US-1 runs right through town, connecting you to Coconut Grove in 5 minutes and Downtown in 15. The Metrorail Douglas Road station puts you on the Orange Line, reaching Brickell, Government Center, and Miami International Airport without sitting in traffic. For flights, MIA is just 8 miles north.

Weekend trips are easy too. Key Biscayne and Virginia Key beaches are 20 minutes east. Homestead and the Everglades are 30 minutes south on the Turnpike.

### The Coral Gables Lifestyle

Life here revolves around outdoor spaces and community gathering spots. Venetian Pool, a spring-fed swimming spot built in a coral rock quarry, fills up with families every summer. Fairchild Tropical Botanic Garden hosts concerts and plant sales year-round. The Biltmore Hotel, a 1926 landmark, offers Sunday brunch that has become a local tradition.

Your neighbors know each other. Kids bike to school through tree-canopied streets. Parents chat at the farmers market on Saturdays. This is not a place where you wave at strangers from your car and never learn their names.

## Coral Gables Neighborhoods

Each pocket of Coral Gables has its own personality. The house price and vibe change noticeably as you move through different sections.

### Coral Gables North (Near Miracle Mile)

The downtown core centers on Miracle Mile, a pedestrian-friendly stretch of Coral Way between Douglas and LeJeune Roads. Here you find boutiques, restaurants like Ortanique and Pascal's on Ponce, and the historic Actors' Playhouse. Condos and townhouses dominate, with prices starting around $400,000 for a 2-bedroom. Walking distance to everything makes this area popular with young professionals and empty nesters.

### The Historic Neighborhoods

South of Coral Way, streets like Granada Boulevard and Alhambra Circle showcase the original 1920s estates. These are the showstopper homes with gated entrances, fountains, and guest houses. Single-family homes here range from $1.5 million to over $10 million. Families choose this area for the large lots, mature banyan trees, and proximity to the University of Miami.

### Coral Gables South (Near Sunset Drive)

The southern section along Sunset Drive offers more affordable options without sacrificing the Coral Gables schools and services. Single-family homes from the 1950s and 1960s sell in the $800,000 to $1.2 million range. You are still zoned for Coral Gables schools, but you might trade some of the historic charm for modern updates and bigger square footage.

### Riviera Section

Tucked between US-1 and Biscayne Bay, the Riviera neighborhood includes waterfront estates and the Riviera Country Club. This is old money Coral Gables, with homes dating to the original development. Expect prices above $2 million and a quieter, more exclusive atmosphere.

## What to Know Before Moving

Coral Gables has strict rules that affect your move and your daily life as a homeowner.

### HOA and City Codes

Even without an HOA, the city enforces design standards. You cannot paint your house neon green, park a boat in your driveway, or add a room without architectural review. These rules keep property values high, but they also mean renovations take longer and cost more than in unincorporated Miami-Dade.

### Moving Day Logistics

Reserve your elevator if moving into a condo on Coral Way or near Merrick Park. The city requires permits for moving trucks parked on residential streets for extended periods. Our crews know which buildings need COIs (Certificates of Insurance) 48 hours in advance and which property managers need direct coordination.

### Timing Your Coral Gables Move

March and April offer ideal moving weather before the summer humidity kicks in. Avoid moving during UM graduation weekend (early May) when traffic around campus becomes a nightmare. Check the city calendar for street closures during events like the Coral Gables Festival of the Arts.

## Schools and Services

Coral Gables families have strong public and private school options.

![Svc Red Truck With Large Covered Cargo In An 1](/images/blog/2029/03/moving-to-coral-gables-what-you-need-to-know/svc-red-truck-with-large-covered-cargo-in-an-1.webp)


**Public Schools:** Coral Gables Senior High, ranked among Florida's top 50 public high schools, serves grades 9-12. Ponce de Leon Middle School and elementary schools like Coral Gables Elementary feed into this system.

**Private Schools:** St. Philip's Episcopal, Gulliver Preparatory, and Ransom Everglades (in nearby Coconut Grove) offer private alternatives.

**Healthcare:** Doctors Hospital at 5000 University Drive provides emergency and general care. Baptist Health facilities in Kendall and Jackson Memorial in Downtown Miami handle specialized needs.

**Shopping:** Shops at Merrick Park brings Nordstrom and Neiman Marcus to the neighborhood. For groceries, Publix locations on Bird Road and Coral Way, plus specialty markets like Whole Foods on US-1, cover everyday needs.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does it cost to move to Coral Gables?

Local moves within Miami-Dade typically run $400-$1,200 depending on home size and distance. Coral Gables moves sometimes cost slightly more due to parking permits and building requirements that add time.

### Are there any move-in restrictions in Coral Gables?

Yes. Many condos require 48-hour notice and elevator reservations. The city limits moving truck parking on residential streets. Check with your building management and our team can help coordinate.

### What is the best time of year to move to Coral Gables?

Spring (March-April) offers the best weather. Avoid summer afternoon moves when thunderstorms are daily. Fall works well after hurricane season ends in November.
