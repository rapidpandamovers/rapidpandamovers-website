## Why Choose Bay Harbor Islands?

Bay Harbor Islands is one of Miami-Dade County's best-kept secrets. This small municipality consists of two islands connected by a single bridge, creating a tight-knit community feel that's hard to find elsewhere in South Florida. The town is known for its upscale waterfront condos, boutique shopping along Kane Concourse, and a walkable lifestyle that sets it apart from car-dependent Miami suburbs.

![Loc Aerial Shot Of Miami S Skyline With Wate 1](/images/blog/2030/07/welcome-to-bay-harbor-islands-a-moving-guide/loc-aerial-shot-of-miami-s-skyline-with-wate-1.webp)


The community draws families seeking Ruth K. Broad Bay Harbor K-8 Center (one of the top-rated public schools in Miami-Dade), professionals who want a quiet retreat close to work, and retirees looking for low-maintenance waterfront living. With just over 6,000 residents, you'll recognize your neighbors at the Saturday farmers market within weeks.

### Location and Accessibility

Bay Harbor Islands sits between Bal Harbour and Surfside, with Indian Creek to the west and Biscayne Bay to the east. The 96th Street Causeway connects you to Collins Avenue and the beaches in minutes. Downtown Miami is a 25-minute drive via I-95, and the Aventura Mall is 10 minutes north. Miami International Airport takes about 30 minutes depending on traffic. While the town itself is walkable, most residents keep a car for commuting and errands beyond the islands.

### Community and Lifestyle

Bay Harbor Islands residents enjoy a genuine small-town atmosphere. The town hosts regular events at Haulover Park and the waterfront promenade, from outdoor concerts to holiday celebrations. The Bay Harbor Islands Community Center offers fitness classes, after-school programs, and senior activities. Summer brings kayaking and paddleboarding on the bay, while winter means perfect weather for the weekly farmers market and outdoor dining along Kane Concourse.

## Bay Harbor Islands vs. Nearby Communities

If you're weighing Bay Harbor Islands against other Miami Beach area options, here's how it compares:

- **Vs. Bal Harbour**: Bay Harbor offers similar upscale living at lower price points. You're one bridge away from Bal Harbour Shops without the premium.
- **Vs. Surfside**: Similar small-town feel, but Bay Harbor has more waterfront properties and a quieter residential character.
- **Vs. North Miami Beach**: Bay Harbor is smaller and more walkable, with a stronger community identity and better schools.
- **Vs. Aventura**: Bay Harbor offers old-school Florida charm versus Aventura's newer high-rise development.

### Choosing Your Perfect Spot

The two islands have distinct personalities. The East Island features mostly mid-rise condos with bay views and is closer to the beach. The West Island has more single-family homes and a quieter, more residential feel. Consider these factors:

- **Proximity to work and schools**: Ruth K. Broad K-8 is on the East Island. Commuters to Downtown Miami may prefer easy I-95 access from the west side.
- **Waterfront access**: East Island condos often have direct bay access, boat docks, and water views.
- **Property types**: Condos dominate the East Island; single-family homes are more common on the West Island.
- **Budget**: Single-family homes start around $1.5M; condos range from $400K to $2M+ depending on size and views.

## Moving to Bay Harbor Islands in Summer

Summer is actually a smart time to relocate to Bay Harbor Islands. While the heat and humidity are real, you'll find better availability with moving companies and potentially lower rates than the busy fall and winter seasons when snowbirds return.

### Timing Your Move

When planning your relocation, keep these Bay Harbor-specific factors in mind:

- **Schedule morning moves**: Book your moving crew for a 7 AM start. Afternoon thunderstorms roll in almost daily from May through October, and you want furniture inside before the 3 PM downpour.
- **Check condo requirements**: Most Bay Harbor Islands condos require 48-72 hour move-in notice, elevator reservations, and a Certificate of Insurance from your moving company. Contact your building manager early.
- **Avoid holiday weekends**: July 4th week brings traffic headaches across the causeway. Book your move for mid-week if possible.
- **Consider school timing**: Moving before August gives kids time to settle before Ruth K. Broad starts the new year.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does it cost to move to Bay Harbor Islands?

Local moves within Miami-Dade typically range from $400-$1,200 depending on home size and distance. A 2-bedroom condo move from nearby Aventura or North Miami Beach usually falls in the $500-$700 range. Moves from Downtown Miami or Coral Gables run slightly higher due to distance.

### What are the condo move-in requirements?

Most Bay Harbor Islands condos require 48-72 hours advance notice, a Certificate of Insurance from your moving company, and an elevator reservation. Some buildings charge a move-in deposit (usually refundable). Contact your building manager at least a week before your move date.

### When is the best time to move to Bay Harbor Islands?

Spring (March-May) and fall (October-November) offer the best weather. Summer moves work well if you schedule for early morning. Winter brings snowbird traffic but mild temperatures. Avoid moving during major holidays and Art Basel week in December.
