Choosing the wrong moving company can turn your relocation into a nightmare. Whether you're moving from Brickell to Coral Gables or from Kendall to Aventura, scammers and unprofessional operators give the industry a bad name. Here's how to spot trouble before signing any contract.

## 1. No Physical Address

Legitimate companies have real offices you can visit. In Miami, a reputable mover should have a verifiable location - not just a P.O. box. Before hiring anyone for your Wynwood apartment move or Pinecrest home relocation, confirm their business address exists.

![A Striking Red Flag Flutters On A Pebble 3](/images/blog/2029/06/5-red-flags-when-choosing-a-moving-company/a-striking-red-flag-flutters-on-a-pebble-3.webp)


## 2. Unusually Low Estimates

If one quote is dramatically lower than others, it's likely a bait-and-switch. Miami homeowners moving from Coconut Grove or Doral often report that suspiciously cheap estimates balloon on moving day. Get at least three quotes and be wary of any that seem too good to be true.

## 3. Large Cash Deposit Requests

Professional movers don't demand large cash payments upfront. This scam is unfortunately common in South Florida. Legitimate Miami Beach and Homestead movers accept credit cards and checks, with payment due after delivery - not before.

## 4. No Written Estimate

Everything should be in writing: the estimate, terms, insurance coverage, and all fees. Verbal agreements protect no one.

![Control Tower With A Clear Red Warning F 1](/images/blog/2029/06/5-red-flags-when-choosing-a-moving-company/control-tower-with-a-clear-red-warning-f-1.webp)


## 5. No Insurance Information Available

Licensed movers carry insurance and can prove it. If they can't or won't provide documentation, they may not be legitimate.
