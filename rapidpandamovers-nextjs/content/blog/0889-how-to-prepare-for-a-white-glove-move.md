# How to Prepare for a White Glove Move

One of the biggest advantages of white glove moving is that the team handles nearly everything for you. But "nearly everything" still leaves some work on your end. A little preparation before the crew arrives makes the process faster, smoother, and ensures nothing gets missed. Here's how to get ready for a white glove move in Miami.

## Schedule the Pre-Move Walk-Through

The walk-through is where your move manager assesses the scope of the project. Make sure every space is accessible during this visit, including closets, storage rooms, the garage, any off-site storage units, and outdoor areas. If you have items in a boat house at your Key Biscayne property or a climate-controlled unit at a storage facility, mention those upfront. The more your manager sees, the more accurate the plan.

## Identify High-Value and Sentimental Items

Before the walk-through, make a list of items that require extra attention. This includes artwork with appraised values, jewelry collections, antique furniture, family heirlooms, and any piece with sentimental value that might not look expensive but matters to you. Share this list with your move manager so those items get flagged in the packing plan.

## Gather Important Documents

Personal documents like passports, birth certificates, financial records, and legal papers should travel with you, not on the moving truck. The same goes for medication, spare keys, and any small valuables like watches and jewelry that you'd rather keep on your person. Pack these in a bag you carry yourself on moving day.

## Handle Items the Movers Can't Transport

White glove movers can handle almost anything, but certain items are restricted. Hazardous materials like propane tanks, paint, pool chemicals, and gasoline need to be disposed of or transported separately. Live plants can go in your personal vehicle. Perishable food should be used up, donated, or moved in a cooler you manage yourself. Your move manager will provide a complete list of restricted items during planning.

## Prepare Your Coral Gables or Coconut Grove Home

Clear pathways through the house. Move cars out of the driveway and garage to give the truck clear access. If you have a long driveway or limited street parking, discuss logistics with your move manager in advance. Trim any overhanging branches that might interfere with the truck. For gated communities, arrange guest passes or security clearance for the moving crew.

## Communicate With Building Management

If you're leaving or entering a condo in Brickell, Aventura, or South Beach, contact your building management early. Reserve the freight elevator, confirm loading dock availability, and submit any required insurance certificates. Your white glove team can handle most of this communication, but giving them your building contact information early speeds up the process.

## Plan for Kids and Pets

Moving day is busy and potentially unsafe for young children and animals. Arrange childcare and pet care so you can focus on directing the crew and making placement decisions without distractions. If your pets will be at the new home when the crew arrives, secure them in a room that's already been set up.

## Confirm the Timeline

A few days before the move, review the detailed schedule with your move manager. Confirm arrival times, the number of crew members, the packing timeline if packing is included, and the expected completion time at the destination. Knowing the plan reduces moving-day anxiety and ensures everyone is working from the same playbook.
