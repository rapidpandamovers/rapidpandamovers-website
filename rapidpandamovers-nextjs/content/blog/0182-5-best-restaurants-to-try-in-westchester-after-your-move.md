Just moved to Westchester or planning your summer relocation? Food is one of the best ways to settle into your new community. Westchester sits along Coral Way (SW 24th Street) and Bird Road, and the neighborhood has been the heart of Miami's Cuban dining scene for over 50 years.

## 1. Versailles Restaurant

No Westchester dining list is complete without Versailles, the "World's Most Famous Cuban Restaurant." Located on Calle Ocho near the Westchester border, this landmark has served Miami since 1971. Order the lechon asado (roasted pork) and finish with cafe con leche at the famous ventanita window. The lunch counter stays busy with locals grabbing cafecito and pastelitos de guayaba.

![A Stylish Dining Setup In A Tokyo Restau 4](/images/blog/2025/06/5-best-restaurants-to-try-in-westchester-after-your-move/a-stylish-dining-setup-in-a-tokyo-restau-4.webp)


## 2. El Pub Restaurant

A local favorite since 1974, El Pub on SW 8th Street serves authentic Cuban comfort food in generous portions. The vaca frita and ropa vieja keep regulars coming back for decades. Grab a window seat and watch the Calle Ocho street life while you eat.

![Elegant Evening Dining At A Tokyo Restau 3](/images/blog/2025/06/5-best-restaurants-to-try-in-westchester-after-your-move/elegant-evening-dining-at-a-tokyo-restau-3.webp)


## 3. Pollo Tropical

Born in Westchester, this Caribbean-style chain opened its first location right here on Bird Road in 1988. Try the citrus-marinated grilled chicken with rice and beans. What started as a single Miami restaurant now has locations across Florida, but the original Westchester spirit lives on.

## 4. La Carreta

Open 24 hours on Bird Road, La Carreta is the go-to spot for late-night croquetas and Cuban sandwiches. After a long day unpacking boxes, nothing beats their medianoche at midnight. Perfect for families craving authentic Cuban fare any time of day.

![Intimate Dining Experience At A Ramen Re 2](/images/blog/2025/06/5-best-restaurants-to-try-in-westchester-after-your-move/intimate-dining-experience-at-a-ramen-re-2.webp)


## 5. Islas Canarias

Known for their legendary churros and authentic Spanish-Cuban cuisine, Islas Canarias on SW 8th Street has been a Westchester staple since 1977. The breakfast special with eggs, ham, and toast draws crowds on Saturday mornings. Don't skip dessert.
