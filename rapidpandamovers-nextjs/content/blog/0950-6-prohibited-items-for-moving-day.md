Some items simply can't travel on a moving truck due to safety, legal, or practical reasons. Before your May move, make sure you know what needs to be transported separately or disposed of properly.

## 1. Hazardous Materials

Gasoline, propane, paint thinner, aerosols, and chemicals are fire hazards and illegal to transport on moving trucks.

![A Person Holding A Cardboard Box Labeled 2](/images/blog/2030/05/6-prohibited-items-for-moving-day/a-person-holding-a-cardboard-box-labeled-2.webp)


## 2. Explosives and Ammunition

Fireworks, ammunition, and anything explosive must be disposed of or transported personally with proper safety measures.

## 3. Perishable Foods

Refrigerated and frozen items won't survive most moves. Consume, donate, or dispose of perishables before moving day.

![A Professional Mover Packs A Fragile Ite 4](/images/blog/2030/05/6-prohibited-items-for-moving-day/a-professional-mover-packs-a-fragile-ite-4.webp)


## 4. Plants (for Long-Distance Moves)

Agricultural regulations often prohibit transporting plants across state lines due to pest concerns.

## 5. Pets and Living Creatures

Fish, pets, and any living animals cannot travel on moving trucks. Arrange separate, safe transportation for them.

## 6. Irreplaceable Documents

Passports, birth certificates, financial records, and legal documents should stay with you personally during the move.

![Orange Excavator Moving Earth At A Rural 3](/images/blog/2030/05/6-prohibited-items-for-moving-day/orange-excavator-moving-earth-at-a-rural-3.webp)


## Miami-Specific Disposal Tips

Living in South Florida means you have local options for handling prohibited items. Miami-Dade County hosts household hazardous waste collection days where you can safely dispose of chemicals, paints, and aerosols. Check the Miami-Dade Solid Waste Management website for drop-off locations in Doral, Homestead, and other areas.

For ammunition and fireworks, contact the Miami-Dade Police Department for proper disposal guidance. Many gun stores in areas like Kendall and Hialeah also accept ammunition for safe disposal.

Perishable foods can go to local food banks like Feeding South Florida before your move. If you're moving from Brickell or Coral Gables, consider hosting a "clean out the fridge" dinner party for neighbors.

For plants that can't make the trip, the Fairchild Tropical Botanic Garden in Coral Gables sometimes accepts healthy specimens as donations.
