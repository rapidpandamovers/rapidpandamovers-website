# What White Glove Moving Actually Includes

The term "white glove moving" gets thrown around a lot in the moving industry, but most people have no idea what separates it from a standard move. If you're relocating a Coral Gables estate or a penthouse in Brickell, the difference matters more than you think.

## Pre-Move Planning and Walk-Through

Every white glove move begins weeks before moving day. A dedicated move manager visits your home to catalog furniture, artwork, electronics, and anything that needs special handling. They measure doorways, check elevator dimensions in high-rises, and plan the exact route each piece will take from your old home to your new one. This planning stage alone prevents most of the damage that happens during standard moves.

## Custom Packing and Crating

Standard movers wrap your belongings in moving blankets and hope for the best. White glove teams build custom wooden crates for artwork, mirrors, and sculpture. Furniture gets wrapped in acid-free tissue paper before padding goes on. Crystal, china, and glassware are individually packed with layered cushioning. Your home theater equipment goes into anti-static packaging designed to protect sensitive components from Miami's humidity.

## Floor and Property Protection

Before a single item gets moved, the crew lays down protective runners over hardwood floors, places corner guards on walls, and pads elevator interiors. In luxury condos along Biscayne Boulevard, building management often requires proof of these protections before allowing a move to proceed. White glove teams arrive with these materials as standard practice.

## Furniture Disassembly and Reassembly

Large pieces like custom bed frames, sectional sofas, and dining tables get carefully disassembled with each bolt and bracket bagged, labeled, and tracked. At the destination, everything goes back together exactly as it was. Hardware never gets lost in a random box.

## Placement and Arrangement

A standard crew drops boxes in rooms and calls it done. White glove movers place every piece of furniture exactly where you want it. They'll rearrange a living room three times if you change your mind. Artwork gets hung, shelves get loaded, and beds get made. You walk into a finished home, not a warehouse of cardboard.

## Full Unpacking and Debris Removal

Once everything is placed, the team unpacks every box, organizes items into cabinets and closets according to your preferences, and removes all packing materials. No pile of flattened boxes sitting in the garage for weeks. The crew takes everything with them when they leave.
