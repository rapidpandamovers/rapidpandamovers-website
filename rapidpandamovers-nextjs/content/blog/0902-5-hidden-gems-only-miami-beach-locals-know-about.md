Every neighborhood has secrets that only locals know. If you've recently moved to Miami Beach or are planning a move, this insider's guide will help you discover the spots that make this community special—places that don't show up in typical tourist guides.

## 1. South Pointe Park Pier

While tourists crowd the main beaches, locals head to South Pointe Park Pier at sunset. This scenic spot offers stunning views of Fisher Island and cruise ships passing through Government Cut. Pack a picnic and watch the dolphins that frequently swim nearby.

## 2. The Miami Beach Botanical Garden

Tucked away on Convention Center Drive, this free 2.6-acre urban garden is an oasis of calm. Locals come here to escape the crowds, enjoy shaded walking paths, and attend the occasional free yoga class or art exhibit.

## 3. Normandy Isle Fountain

This quiet residential area features a beautiful fountain plaza where locals gather for morning coffee. The surrounding neighborhood has charming cafes and bakeries that most visitors never discover.

## 4. North Shore Open Space Park

Far from the South Beach crowds, this 40-acre beachfront park between 79th and 87th Streets offers pristine sand, native dunes, and peaceful walking trails. It's where locals go for a genuine beach experience.

## 5. The Bass Museum Sculpture Garden

While the museum itself draws visitors, the outdoor sculpture garden is free and surprisingly peaceful. Locals bring books and laptops to enjoy the shaded seating areas surrounded by world-class art.
