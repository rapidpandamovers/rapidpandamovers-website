Planning a summer move to Bal Harbour? Whether you're relocating from Brickell, coming down from Aventura, or making a cross-country move this July, knowing what makes this exclusive village special helps you settle in faster.

## Why Choose Bal Harbour?

Bal Harbour stands out as Miami-Dade's most exclusive village, with just 3,000 residents spread across 0.3 square miles. The community centers around Bal Harbour Shops, home to Chanel, Gucci, and Prada, with oceanfront condos like the St. Regis and Oceana commanding some of the highest prices in South Florida.

![Loc Tranquil Evening View Of Modern Architec 1](/images/blog/2025/07/summer-moving-to-bal-harbour-your-complete-guide/loc-tranquil-evening-view-of-modern-architec-1.webp)


The village attracts families seeking top-rated schools in the nearby Surfside and Bay Harbor Islands area, finance professionals with easy access to Brickell, and retirees who appreciate the quieter beach atmosphere compared to South Beach.

### Location and Accessibility

Bal Harbour sits at the northern tip of Miami Beach, with Collins Avenue (A1A) running through its center. You're 20 minutes from Downtown Miami via I-95 or the Julia Tuttle Causeway, 15 minutes from Aventura Mall, and just steps from some of Miami's best beaches. Miami International Airport is about 30 minutes away, while Fort Lauderdale-Hollywood International is 25 minutes north.

### Community and Lifestyle

Bal Harbour residents enjoy Bal Harbour Beach, a quieter stretch of sand with fewer tourists than South Beach. The village hosts regular community events, and the Bal Harbour Shops outdoor mall provides a social hub year-round. Summer brings beach yoga sessions, poolside gatherings at the luxury condos, and quick escapes to Haulover Beach Park just to the north.

## Neighborhoods to Consider

Bal Harbour itself is compact, but the surrounding areas offer distinct living options. Within the village, oceanfront condos dominate, with buildings like the St. Regis Residences and Oceana Bal Harbour. Just south, Surfside offers a more residential feel with single-family homes. Bay Harbor Islands to the west provides waterfront living at slightly lower price points, while Sunny Isles Beach to the north features newer high-rise developments.

### Choosing Your Perfect Spot

Consider these factors:

- **Commute time**: Brickell is 20-25 minutes, Aventura is 10 minutes, Wynwood is about 20 minutes
- **School districts**: Ruth K. Broad Bay Harbor K-8 serves the area, with private options like Miami Country Day nearby
- **Property style**: Luxury condos dominate Bal Harbour proper, while Surfside and Bay Harbor Islands offer homes
- **Price range**: Expect $1M+ for condos in Bal Harbour, with nearby areas offering more options

## Moving to Bal Harbour in Summer

July is peak moving season in South Florida, but summer heat requires extra planning. Temperatures regularly hit 90°F with high humidity, and afternoon thunderstorms roll in almost daily between 3-6pm.

### Timing Your Move

When planning your summer relocation, consider:

- **Start early**: Schedule your move to begin at 7-8am, finishing before the afternoon heat peaks
- **Beat the storms**: Plan to have everything inside by 2pm when summer thunderstorms typically build
- **Protect your items**: Electronics and artwork need AC until they're loaded, as garages and trucks heat up fast
- **Building logistics**: Most Bal Harbour condos require 48-72 hour notice for elevator reservations

## Essential Services to Locate

As a new Bal Harbour resident, you'll want to find:

- **Healthcare**: Mount Sinai Medical Center is 15 minutes south in Miami Beach, Aventura Hospital is 10 minutes north
- **Groceries**: Publix at Bal Harbour Shops for daily needs, Whole Foods in Surfside, and Trader Joe's in Aventura
- **Schools**: Ruth K. Broad Bay Harbor K-8, Surfside Elementary, and private options like Hebrew Academy
- **Fitness**: Equinox at Bal Harbour Shops, plus beach running and yoga on the sand
