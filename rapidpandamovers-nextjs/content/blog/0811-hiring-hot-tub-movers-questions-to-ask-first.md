# Hiring Hot Tub Movers: Questions to Ask First

Choosing the wrong mover for a hot tub job can end with a cracked shell, a gouged pool deck, or a crew that shows up without the equipment to finish. Hot tubs aren't standard furniture, and not every moving company knows how to handle them. Before you hire anyone in the Miami area, ask these questions and pay attention to the answers.

## "Do You Have Experience Moving Hot Tubs Specifically?"

General movers handle couches and boxes. Hot tub moving requires different equipment, different lifting techniques, and different problem-solving skills. Ask how many hot tub moves the company has completed and whether their crew is trained specifically on spa relocation. A company that does hot tubs regularly will have spa dollies, know how to tip a tub safely on its side, and understand the weight distribution issues that come with these units.

## "What Equipment Do You Bring?"

The right answer should include heavy-duty spa dollies or appliance dollies, 4-wheel furniture dollies, moving straps, thick padded blankets, plywood sheets for path protection, and a ramp system for loading. If the company says "we just use a regular dolly and some blankets," that's a red flag. A 700-pound acrylic tub on a standard hand truck is an accident waiting to happen.

## "Are You Licensed and Insured?"

In Florida, moving companies must hold a license from the Florida Department of Agriculture and Consumer Services. Ask for the license number and verify it. More importantly, ask about insurance coverage. You want:

- **General liability insurance** that covers damage to your property (fences, patios, landscaping)
- **Cargo insurance** that covers damage to the hot tub during transport
- **Workers' compensation** that covers crew injuries on your property

Get proof of insurance in writing before the move. If someone gets hurt lifting your tub and the company has no workers' comp, you could be liable.

## "Can You Handle My Access Situation?"

Describe your property in detail. Mention the fence type and gate width, any pool enclosures, narrow side yards, steps or elevation changes, and the distance from the tub to where the truck can park. A good [**Hot Tub Moving**](/hot-tub-moving) company will ask follow-up questions, request photos, or offer a site visit. A company that says "yeah, no problem" without asking for details is guessing.

If crane service might be needed, ask whether the company handles crane coordination or if you need to arrange that separately.

## "What's Included in the Quote?"

Get clarity on exactly what the price covers. Ask specifically about:

- Wrapping and padding the tub for transport
- Fence panel removal and reinstallation (if needed)
- Loading and unloading
- Placement at the destination
- Mileage charges for longer moves
- Any potential surcharges for stairs, tight access, or heavy tubs

The best quotes come from companies that have seen your property (in person or through detailed photos) and can give you a fixed price rather than an estimate that might change on moving day.

## "What's Your Cancellation and Rescheduling Policy?"

Miami weather is unpredictable, especially during summer storm season. If a thunderstorm rolls in on your scheduled moving day, you need to know whether the company will reschedule without penalty. Ask about their policy for weather delays, last-minute cancellations, and rescheduling fees.

## "How Do You Protect the Tub and My Property?"

The answer should be specific. Moving blankets wrapped around the shell and secured with stretch wrap. Plywood sheets laid over grass or pavers to create a rolling path. Corner protectors on gates and doorframes. Straps rated for the tub's weight. If the company can't describe their protection methods in detail, they may not have any.

## "Can You Provide References or Reviews?"

Any experienced hot tub mover in Miami should have past customers willing to vouch for them. Ask for references, check Google reviews, and look at photos of completed jobs. Pay special attention to reviews that mention hot tubs, spas, or heavy items specifically.

## What Good Answers Sound Like

The company that earns your trust is the one that asks as many questions as you do. They want to know about your tub (make, model, approximate weight), your property (access, fencing, distance to truck), and your timeline. They offer specifics rather than vague reassurances. They send a written quote with itemized costs. That's the company you hire.
