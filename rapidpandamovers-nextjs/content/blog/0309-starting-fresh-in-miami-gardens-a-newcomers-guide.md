Thinking about moving to Miami Gardens? You're considering Miami-Dade County's third-largest city and Florida's largest majority African American city. This guide provides essential information to help you plan your relocation.

## About Miami Gardens

Miami Gardens is a vibrant city of approximately 111,000 residents, home to Hard Rock Stadium and a strong sense of community pride. Incorporated in 2003, it's one of Florida's newest major cities and continues to develop rapidly.

![Miami Gardens Florida Suburb Community Guide](/images/blog/2026/04/starting-fresh-in-miami-gardens-a-newcomers-guide/miami-gardens-florida-suburb-community-guide.webp)


### Demographics at a Glance

- **Population**: 111,264 (2023)
- **Median Age**: 38.1 years
- **Median Household Income**: $60,979
- **African American**: 61.7%
- **Hispanic**: 33.6%
- **Foreign-Born Residents**: 37.2%
- **Average Household Size**: 3.52 members

### Historical Significance
Miami Gardens is Florida's most populous city with a majority African American population and has the largest percentage of African Americans (66.97%) of any Florida city.

## Real Estate Market

Miami Gardens offers affordable housing options compared to coastal Miami-Dade.

### Housing Characteristics
- Single-family homes predominate
- Growing condo and townhouse developments
- More affordable than coastal areas
- Larger lot sizes than dense urban areas

### Market Features
- Median household income allows for homeownership
- Mix of established neighborhoods and new development
- Proximity to employment centers
- Value compared to neighboring communities

## Moving Tips for Miami Gardens

### Residential Considerations
- Most moves to single-family homes
- Generally easier street access than dense urban areas
- Standard residential permits usually sufficient
- Coordinate with HOAs where applicable

### Best Moving Times
- **Weekday mornings** for lighter traffic
- **Avoid game days** at Hard Rock Stadium
- **Check for major concerts/events** affecting traffic

### Climate Considerations
- Protect belongings from humidity
- Morning moves avoid afternoon heat
- Watch for summer thunderstorms
