Miami Shores packs a lot into 2.5 square miles. Tree-lined streets, waterfront properties, and one of South Florida's best-preserved collections of mid-century homes make this village a top choice for families looking to put down roots.

## Why Families Pick Miami Shores

The village sits between North Miami and Biscayne Bay, giving residents quick access to beaches, downtown, and major employers while keeping the small-town feel intact. A-rated schools, walkable streets, and neighbors who actually know each other set it apart from typical Miami suburbs.

### Getting Around

I-95 runs along the western edge of the village, putting downtown Miami 15 minutes away in light traffic. Brickell and Aventura are both easy commutes. The Tri-Rail station in nearby Miami Springs connects to Fort Lauderdale and West Palm Beach for those who work up the coast.

### The Community Feel

Block parties, holiday parades, and the monthly Movies in the Park nights at Optimist Park bring families together. The aquatic center hosts swim lessons and summer camps that keep kids busy and give parents a break.

## Four Neighborhoods Worth Your Attention

### Historic District (Grand Concourse Area)

![Svc High Angle View Of An Asda Delivery Truc 2](/images/blog/2028/06/best-neighborhoods-in-miami-shores-for-families/svc-high-angle-view-of-an-asda-delivery-truc-2.webp)


The heart of Miami Shores runs along Grand Concourse, where mature oak trees create canopy-covered streets. You will find a mix of 1930s Mediterranean Revival homes and 1950s mid-century modern architecture. The Miami Shores Country Club anchors the southern end, and many homes here back up to the golf course. Expect prices in the $800K to $1.5M range, with some larger estates going higher.

### Bayfront Section (East of Biscayne Boulevard)

The most expensive real estate in the village sits east of Biscayne Boulevard, where homes face the bay. Properties here come with private docks, water access, and sunset views. Families with boats love this section, though you will pay a premium. Prices start around $1.5M and climb from there.

### Village Center (NE 2nd Avenue Corridor)

Families who want walkability gravitate toward the blocks near NE 2nd Avenue. Restaurants, coffee shops, and the community center are all walking distance. The homes tend to be smaller and more affordable than the historic district, making this area popular with first-time buyers and young families.

### West Miami Shores (Near Biscayne Canal)

The most affordable section of the village sits west of NE 6th Avenue. Lots tend to be larger, and you will find more 1960s-era ranches than historic homes. Miami Shores Elementary and Optimist Park make this area especially attractive for families with young children.

## Schools That Draw Families Here

Miami Shores Elementary consistently ranks among the top public elementary schools in Miami-Dade County. Barry University sits within the village for college students. For middle and high school, many families choose magnet programs at Miami Country Day or private options like Archbishop Curley-Notre Dame High School nearby.

## What to Know Before Moving

Flood zones affect some eastern properties near the bay. Check FEMA maps before buying. Hurricane shutters or impact windows are standard on most homes. Property taxes run higher than surrounding areas because of the A-rated schools and village services.
