PCS season peaks from May through August when military families across the country relocate to new duty stations. If your orders are bringing you to South Florida, you're joining a community of service members stationed at Homestead Air Reserve Base, U.S. Southern Command in Doral, and Coast Guard facilities throughout Miami-Dade.

## South Florida Military Installations

Understanding where you'll be stationed helps determine the best neighborhoods:

![Svc Camouflaged Soldier In Tactical Gear Wit 2](/images/blog/2029/06/pcs-season-in-miami-military-moving-during-peak-summer/svc-camouflaged-soldier-in-tactical-gear-wit-2.webp)


**Homestead Air Reserve Base**: Located in south Miami-Dade, home to the 482nd Fighter Wing (Air Force Reserve) and the 94th Army Air and Missile Defense Command. The base reopened after Hurricane Andrew destroyed most facilities in 1992.

**U.S. Southern Command (SOUTHCOM)**: Headquartered in Doral, this unified combatant command oversees military operations in Central and South America and the Caribbean.

**Coast Guard Sector Miami**: Responsible for maritime safety, security, and environmental protection across South Florida waters.

## Best Neighborhoods for Military Families

### Near Homestead ARB

**Homestead**: The closest option with the most affordable housing in Miami-Dade. Single-family homes under $400,000 are still available. The downtown area has revitalized with restaurants and shops. Commute to base: 10-15 minutes.

**Florida City**: Just south of Homestead with similar affordability. Gateway to the Everglades and Florida Keys. Smaller town feel with growing family amenities. Commute to base: 15-20 minutes.

**Cutler Bay**: More suburban development, closer to Miami amenities while maintaining reasonable Homestead commutes. Good schools and family-oriented community. Commute to base: 25-30 minutes.

### Near SOUTHCOM in Doral

**Doral**: Walking distance to SOUTHCOM for some housing options. Excellent schools, diverse dining, and family activities at CityPlace Doral. Housing costs are higher but offset by minimal commute.

**Kendall**: Large family-friendly community with extensive housing options from condos to single-family homes. Multiple school choices and shopping centers. Commute to Doral: 20-30 minutes.

**Miami Lakes**: Planned community with parks, bike paths, and strong community events. Popular with families seeking suburban feel. Commute to Doral: 15-25 minutes.

## PCS Documentation for Reimbursement

Keep these documents organized for DLA (Dislocation Allowance) and other reimbursements:

![Svc Polish Soldiers In Uniform During A Cere 3](/images/blog/2029/06/pcs-season-in-miami-military-moving-during-peak-summer/svc-polish-soldiers-in-uniform-during-a-cere-3.webp)


- **PCS orders**: Multiple copies, both digital and paper
- **Weight tickets**: Required for PPM (Personally Procured Move) reimbursement
- **Moving company invoices**: Itemized with dates and addresses
- **Lodging receipts**: For temporary housing during transit
- **Fuel receipts**: If driving personal vehicles
- **Storage contracts**: If items go into temporary storage

Photograph everything before it goes in boxes. This documentation helps with both claims and reimbursement disputes.

## Timing Your PCS Move to Miami

Summer moves to Miami face two challenges: everyone else is also moving during PCS season, and afternoon thunderstorms arrive daily from June through September.

**Book movers early**: Start calling companies 6-8 weeks before your report date. Peak season fills up fast.

**Schedule morning loads**: Miami storms build between 2-5pm. Loading before noon reduces weather delays.

**Allow extra time**: Summer traffic is heavier with vacationers, and weather can cause unexpected delays.

## What to Expect from Miami's Climate

If you're coming from a northern duty station, prepare for:

![Svc Stylish Man In Brown Shirt And Bucket Ha 1](/images/blog/2029/06/pcs-season-in-miami-military-moving-during-peak-summer/svc-stylish-man-in-brown-shirt-and-bucket-ha-1.webp)


- **Year-round heat**: Summers are hot and humid; winters are mild
- **Hurricane season**: June through November, with peak activity August-October
- **Afternoon storms**: Brief but intense thunderstorms nearly every summer afternoon
- **High humidity**: Impacts storage decisions and what you ship
