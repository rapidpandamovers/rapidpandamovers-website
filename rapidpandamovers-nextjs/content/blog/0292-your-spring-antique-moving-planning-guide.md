Antique furniture represents generations of craftsmanship and family history. Moving these irreplaceable pieces requires specialized knowledge and careful handling that goes far beyond standard moving practices.

## Before the Move: Documentation

### Get Professional Appraisals

![Explore A Vintage Store Interior Brimmin 2](/images/blog/2026/03/your-spring-antique-moving-planning-guide/explore-a-vintage-store-interior-brimmin-2.webp)


Before packing a single item, document your antiques thoroughly:

- **Professional appraisal**: A qualified appraiser provides accurate valuations reflecting current market conditions and identifies weak spots that may need repair before moving
- **Detailed photographs**: Capture all angles, including existing wear, scratches, and distinctive features
- **Written condition notes**: Document any loose joints, cracks, or fragile areas
- **Insurance records**: Appraisal documentation supports claims if damage occurs

This documentation proves invaluable if you need to file an insurance claim.

### Inspect for Vulnerabilities

Carefully examine each piece:

- Check for loose joints or cracks that could worsen during transport
- Identify delicate carvings, veneer peeling, or aging wood requiring reinforcement
- Note glass panels, mirrors, or marble tops that need separate handling
- Look for signs of pest damage that could spread to other items

## Preparing Antiques for Transport

### Clean Before Packing

Use a dry, soft microfiber cloth to remove dust and dirt. Avoid harsh chemicals that can cause irreversible damage to aged finishes and patinas.

### Disassemble When Possible

If furniture has removable components:

- Remove legs, drawers, and glass panels
- Wrap each piece separately in protective padding
- Label all hardware and store in sealed bags
- Keep bags taped to the main piece or in a dedicated container

### Remove and Protect Hardware

Handles, knobs, and drawer pulls are most vulnerable to damage:

- Remove fixtures when possible and pack separately
- If removal isn't possible, wrap items in moving blankets
- Never use tape directly on antique surfaces

### Empty All Contents

Remove everything from drawers and cabinets. Items shifting during transport can cause dings, scratches, and structural damage from the inside.

### Proper Packing Materials

Museum conservation experts recommend three layers of protection:

- **Breathable blankets**: First layer against the furniture surface
- **Foam padding and corner protectors**: Second layer for impact absorption
- **Custom crates**: Third layer for extremely valuable or fragile pieces built to exact dimensions

Never use plastic wrap directly on wood surfaces. Trapped moisture causes warping and finish damage.

## Handling Antiques Safely

### Never Drag Furniture

Always lift from structurally sound points. Dragging strains joints and can damage both the piece and your floors. Even sturdy-looking antiques have age-related weaknesses.

### Plan Your Route

Measure all entryways, hallways, and staircases in both locations:

- Plan the route in advance to avoid tight corners
- Remove doors if necessary for clearance
- Protect walls and door frames with padding

## Climate Considerations in Miami

Miami's subtropical climate poses specific risks to antiques. Average humidity hovers around 75-80%, which can cause wood to swell, warp, or develop mold.

![Three People Browsing Vintage Wooden Fur 3](/images/blog/2026/03/your-spring-antique-moving-planning-guide/three-people-browsing-vintage-wooden-fur-3.webp)


**Spring Moving Advantages:**

- Temperatures between 75-85°F are easier on wood than summer extremes
- April and May have lower humidity than summer months
- Morning moves (before 10 AM) avoid peak afternoon heat

**Protection Strategies:**

- **Climate-controlled transport**: Prevents warping, cracking, and mold during the move
- **Gradual acclimation**: Allow pieces to adjust slowly to new environments
- **Avoid temperature extremes**: Never leave antiques in hot vehicles or direct sunlight
- **Humidity protection**: Use desiccants if pieces will be in storage
