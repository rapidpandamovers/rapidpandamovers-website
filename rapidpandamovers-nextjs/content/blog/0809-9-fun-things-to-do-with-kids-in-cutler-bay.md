Moving is tough on kids, but Cutler Bay offers plenty of ways to help them fall in love with their new neighborhood. From world-class zoos to trampoline parks, here are nine specific places that will keep your children entertained and help them make new friends.

## 1. Zoo Miami

Located on the western edge of Cutler Bay at 12400 SW 152nd St, Zoo Miami is the only tropical zoo in the continental United States. Spanning over 700 acres with 2,500 animals in more than 100 exhibits, your kids can see tigers, jaguars, koalas, crocodiles, orangutans, Asian elephants, and giraffes. The zoo operates without cages, using natural barriers instead, so animals roam in environments that mimic their native habitats. Plan for a full day and bring sunscreen.

## 2. SkyZone Cutler Bay

The world's first indoor trampoline park has a location right in Cutler Bay at 20465 S Dixie Hwy. Kids can bounce off walls, play trampoline dodgeball, and launch into foam pits. SkyZone offers toddler time for younger children and hosts birthday parties. This is your go-to on rainy afternoons or when the summer heat makes outdoor play impossible.

## 3. Quail Roost Park

Located at 11525 SW 216th St, Quail Roost Park features four different playground designs including structures that look like a pirate ship and a castle. Kids can climb walls, swing, and explore while parents relax on shaded benches. The park also has sports fields and walking paths. This is where Cutler Bay families gather on weekend mornings.

## 4. The Berry Farm

The area's biggest agritourism attraction sits just south of Cutler Bay in Homestead at 13720 SW 216th St. Families can pick their own strawberries, sunflowers, and other seasonal produce. Kids love the hayrides, obstacle course, lumber playground, and haystacks for climbing. Food vendors serve tacos, BBQ, and burgers. Check their seasonal schedule since activities vary throughout the year.

## 5. Black Point Park and Marina

At 24775 SW 87th Ave, Black Point Park offers fishing piers, boat ramps, and direct access to Biscayne Bay. This is one of only two locations where you can visit Biscayne National Park without a boat. Kids can fish from the jetty, watch boats launch, and explore the mangrove-lined shoreline. Pack a picnic and make a day of it.

## 6. Cutler Ridge Park

The main community park at 10100 SW 200th St features playgrounds, walking paths, sports fields, and a community pool. Youth sports leagues hold practices and games here, making it a great place for kids to meet future teammates and classmates. The park hosts community events throughout the year including outdoor movies and holiday celebrations.

## 7. Paint Me One

Located near Cutler Bay at 15400 SW 136th St, Paint Me One lets kids paint their own pottery. Choose from mugs, plates, figurines, and more, then paint and decorate them. The studio fires the pieces so families can pick them up later. Great for creative kids and rainy day entertainment.

## 8. Miami Roller Rink

Old-school fun at 20150 NE 26th Ave in North Miami Beach is worth the 30-minute drive from Cutler Bay. Kids can rent skates, enjoy the music, and practice their moves on the classic rink. They offer skating lessons for beginners and host birthday parties. The arcade games and snack bar keep energy up.

## 9. Biscayne National Park Visitor Center

Located at 9700 SW 328th St in Homestead, the Dante Fascell Visitor Center serves as the gateway to Biscayne National Park. Rangers offer free educational programs for kids, and the exhibits teach about the unique ecosystem where mangroves meet the ocean. Glass-bottom boat tours let kids see the underwater world without getting wet. The Junior Ranger program keeps kids engaged with activities and badges.

## Helping Kids Adjust After a Move

New activities help children process the stress of relocation. Signing up for sports leagues at Cutler Ridge Park, attending summer programs at Zoo Miami, or finding their new favorite spot at SkyZone gives kids something to look forward to and helps them build friendships in their new community.
