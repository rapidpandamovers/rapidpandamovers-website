# Luxury Furniture and White Glove Handling Techniques

A $25,000 B&B Italia sofa doesn't get moved the same way as a couch from a furniture warehouse. Luxury furniture is built differently, finished differently, and priced at a level where a single scratch or structural crack becomes a serious loss. Our white glove teams use specific handling techniques for high-end pieces, and each method exists because we've learned what goes wrong when shortcuts are taken.

## Understanding How Luxury Furniture Differs

Premium furniture uses materials and construction methods that make it both more beautiful and more vulnerable. Hand-rubbed lacquer finishes show every fingerprint and scratch. Solid wood joinery relies on precision fits that can crack under stress. Italian leather develops patina over time but also tears more easily than bonded leather. Gilded surfaces flake if they contact rough materials. Knowing what each piece is made of determines how we handle it.

## The Wrapping Process

We never apply tape directly to any finished surface. Acid-free tissue paper goes on first, protecting the finish from chemical reactions with packing materials. Next comes a layer of soft quilted moving blankets, secured with stretch wrap around the blanket, never touching the furniture itself. For pieces with ornate carved details or protruding hardware, we build foam spacers that prevent pressure points under the wrapping.

## Disassembly Protocols

Large luxury pieces often need partial disassembly to move safely. Dining tables lose their legs and extensions. Bed frames come apart at the side rails. Sectional sofas separate into their modular components. We photograph each piece from multiple angles before disassembly and bag all hardware in labeled pouches. At the destination, reassembly follows the manufacturer's torque specifications when available. For custom-built furniture common in Coral Gables and Coconut Grove homes, we consult with the maker's documentation or contact the workshop directly.

## Lifting and Carrying Techniques

White glove crews never drag furniture. Every piece gets lifted by a team using coordinated movements. For heavy items like marble-top consoles or solid wood armoires, we use shoulder harnesses and lifting straps that distribute weight evenly and keep the piece level during transport. Tilting a heavy piece even a few degrees can stress joints and shift internal components. Our crews practice these lifts before they handle client furniture.

## Loading and Securing in the Truck

Inside the truck, luxury furniture gets positioned based on weight, fragility, and surface material. Leather pieces never contact rough blankets. Polished wood stays separated from anything that could shift and make contact. Each piece gets secured with padded straps that apply even pressure without compressing cushions or bowing panels. We use divider walls inside the truck to create separate zones for different material types.

## Climate Awareness During Transport

Miami's summer heat turns a truck interior into an oven. Certain materials, particularly leather, wax finishes, and adhesive-joined veneers, are sensitive to temperature extremes. We park in shade when possible, minimize the time the truck sits loaded, and avoid transit during peak afternoon temperatures. For especially sensitive pieces, we use climate-controlled vehicles.

## Placement and Final Positioning

At the destination, furniture goes directly to its assigned location. The crew uses felt sliders to position pieces without dragging legs across the floor. Final adjustments happen slowly and deliberately. Once a piece is placed, protective wrapping comes off carefully, and the crew inspects every surface before moving to the next item. If we find any issue, it gets documented and reported to you immediately.
