Moving chaos makes for great cinema. Perfect for a January movie night after your winter move, these films capture the stress, humor, and emotion of relocation in ways that feel all too familiar.

## 1. "The Money Pit" (1986)

Tom Hanks and Shelley Long buy a mansion that falls apart around them, a comedy about the chaos that can follow a move.

![Close Up Of Popcorn And 3D Glasses On Ci 2](/images/blog/2029/01/8-movie-portrayals-of-moving-day-madness/close-up-of-popcorn-and-3d-glasses-on-ci-2.webp)


## 2. "Moving" (1988)

Richard Pryor's comedy about a cross-country relocation gone wrong captures every moving nightmare imaginable.

## 3. "The Incredibles" (2004)

A superhero family forced into witness protection and a new city shows how moves affect families, animated style.

## 4. "Up" (2009)

The ultimate statement about home attachment: a man floats his house to a new location rather than leave it behind.

## 5. "The Pursuit of Happyness" (2006)

Will Smith's struggle through homelessness shows the emotional weight of having no stable home.

![Crowded Cinema Theater With Audience Wat 1](/images/blog/2029/01/8-movie-portrayals-of-moving-day-madness/crowded-cinema-theater-with-audience-wat-1.webp)


## 6. "Fun with Dick and Jane" (2005)

Financial crisis forces a family to extreme measures, showing how job loss and moves intertwine.

## 7. "Beetlejuice" (1988)

When a family moves into a haunted house, the previous owners aren't happy about the change.

## 8. "Home Alone" (1990)

The McCallister family's chaotic departure starts one of cinema's most beloved comedies about home.
