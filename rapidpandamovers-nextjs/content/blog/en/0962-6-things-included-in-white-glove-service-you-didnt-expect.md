# 6 Things Included in White Glove Service You Didnt Expect

Most people expect white glove movers to pack carefully and handle furniture with extra care. That's the baseline. What surprises many of our Miami clients is how far the service actually extends beyond the obvious. Here are six inclusions that catch people off guard.

## 1. Hanging Your Artwork

Standard movers leave framed art leaning against walls. White glove teams hang it. We bring the hardware, the levels, the stud finders, and the patience to position each piece exactly where it looks right. For clients with gallery walls or large statement pieces above fireplaces in their Coral Gables or Coconut Grove homes, this service alone saves hours of post-move work. We follow your direction or your interior designer's specifications, drilling into the correct wall anchors and spacing pieces precisely.

![Black Leather Belt With Intricate Design 3](/images/blog/2030/06/6-things-included-in-white-glove-service-you-didnt-expect/black-leather-belt-with-intricate-design-3.webp)


## 2. Making Every Bed in the House

After a long moving day, the last thing anyone wants to do is dig through boxes looking for sheets. Our team makes every bed in the home, complete with mattress pads, fitted sheets, flat sheets, pillows, and any decorative bedding. You can walk into your master bedroom and collapse into a fully made bed. In a five-bedroom Pinecrest estate, that's five beds ready to sleep in by the time the crew leaves.

## 3. Organizing the Pantry and Kitchen Cabinets

Unpacking the kitchen is one thing. Organizing it thoughtfully is another. White glove teams don't just empty boxes into cabinets. They lay shelf liners, group items logically, and set up your kitchen so it functions from day one. Spices on a rack by the stove. Baking supplies together. Everyday dishes within reach. Wine glasses in a separate section from water glasses. It's the kind of organization most people don't get around to for months after a move.

![Brown Alligator Leather Belt With Detail 2](/images/blog/2030/06/6-things-included-in-white-glove-service-you-didnt-expect/brown-alligator-leather-belt-with-detail-2.webp)


## 4. Coordinating With Your Building's Front Desk

For condo moves in Brickell, Edgewater, and Aventura, our move manager handles the communication with building management. That includes submitting insurance certificates, reserving the freight elevator, scheduling loading dock time, and confirming all building rules before the crew arrives. You don't make a single call to the front desk. For clients moving between two condos, we handle the logistics at both buildings simultaneously.

## 5. Removing and Disposing of All Packing Materials

After unpacking, the average four-bedroom home generates enough cardboard, paper, and plastic to fill a pickup truck. White glove teams collect every piece of packing material, break down every box, and haul everything away. Your recycling bin stays empty. Your garage stays clean. The crew leaves your home looking like a move never happened, which is particularly appreciated by clients in luxury buildings where common area cleanliness standards are enforced.

## 6. Post-Move Follow-Up Adjustments

White glove service doesn't end when the crew drives away. If you realize two days later that the sofa works better on the opposite wall, or that the guest room dresser should swap with the one in the office, our team comes back to make adjustments. This follow-up period, typically available for a week after the move, lets you live in the space and make decisions based on how you actually use each room rather than guessing on moving day.
