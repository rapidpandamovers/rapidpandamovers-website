# 5 Storage Mistakes That Ruin Your Belongings

We've helped hundreds of Miami families move items in and out of storage, and we've seen what happens when storage is done carelessly. The damage isn't always dramatic. It builds slowly over weeks and months until you open your unit and find furniture you can't use, clothes you can't wear, and electronics that won't turn on. These five mistakes cause the most problems.

## 1. Skipping Climate Control

This is the single biggest mistake storage renters make in South Florida. A standard unit in July turns into an oven during the day and stays warm and humid through the night. Wood warps. Leather grows mildew. Electronics corrode from condensation. Upholstered furniture develops mold deep in the cushion padding, where you can't see it until the smell hits you. The price difference between a standard and climate-controlled unit is typically $50-$80 per month. Compare that to replacing a $2,000 leather sofa or a $500 mattress, and there's no question which option is cheaper.

## 2. Wrapping Furniture in Plastic

It seems logical: wrap the sofa in plastic to keep dust and dirt off. But in Miami's humidity, plastic creates a sealed environment that traps moisture against the surface. Within weeks, condensation forms underneath the plastic, and mold follows. This applies to plastic tarps, shrink wrap, and plastic furniture covers sold at hardware stores. Use breathable cotton covers, moving blankets, or furniture pads instead. They protect against dust and scratches while allowing air to circulate and moisture to evaporate.

## 3. Storing Items Directly on the Floor

Concrete storage floors look dry, but they transmit moisture through capillary action, especially after rain. Cardboard boxes sitting on concrete absorb that moisture from the bottom up, weakening the box and damaging whatever is inside. Fabric, paper, and wood items are particularly vulnerable. The fix is simple: place pallets, plastic shelving, or even 2x4 lumber on the floor and stack items on top. A few inches of elevation makes a significant difference in how your belongings hold up over time.

## 4. Packing Without Cleaning

Food crumbs in a kitchen appliance, a stain on a sofa cushion, dirt on a pair of boots. Small amounts of organic matter attract pests and accelerate mold growth in storage. South Florida's warm climate means insects and rodents are active year-round, and they'll find their way into storage units if there's a food source. Before anything goes into storage, clean it thoroughly. Vacuum upholstered items, wipe down appliances inside and out, and wash all clothing and linens. Let everything dry completely before packing. A damp towel sealed in a box is a mold factory waiting to happen.

## 5. Overpacking the Unit Without Airflow

Filling a storage unit wall-to-wall, floor-to-ceiling maximizes your space but minimizes air circulation. Without airflow between items, moisture has nowhere to go, and hot spots develop against walls and in corners. Leave a center aisle for walking access and maintain small gaps between stacks and the walls. This allows air to move through the unit and prevents the stagnant conditions that lead to mold, odors, and condensation damage. Yes, you might need a slightly larger unit, but the extra $30-50 per month protects everything inside.
