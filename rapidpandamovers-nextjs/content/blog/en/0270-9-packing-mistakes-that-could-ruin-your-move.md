Packing seems straightforward until something breaks. These common errors are responsible for the majority of moving damage we see. In Miami's humid climate, proper packing is even more important. Avoid these mistakes and your belongings will arrive safely.

## 1. Overpacking Large Boxes

A huge box of books is impossible to lift safely. Always use small boxes for heavy items. Your back and your movers will thank you.

![A Couple Unpacking Books In Their New Ho 2](/images/blog/2025/08/9-packing-mistakes-that-could-ruin-your-move/a-couple-unpacking-books-in-their-new-ho-2.webp)


## 2. Insufficient Cushioning

One layer of newspaper isn't enough. Fragile items need individual wrapping, padding on all sides, and zero room to shift.

## 3. Leaving Hollow Items Empty

Hollow items like vases and cups are fragile. Fill them with packing paper or soft materials for structural support.

## 4. Using Old, Weak Boxes

Old boxes lose structural integrity. In South Florida's humidity, cardboard weakens faster than you'd expect. Wet boxes are disasters waiting to happen. Use fresh, sturdy boxes from a moving supply store like U-Haul on Biscayne Boulevard or Home Depot.

## 5. Improper Dish Packing

Never stack plates horizontally. Pack them on edge, like records, with padding between each one. This prevents cracking from weight.

## 6. Not Labeling Boxes Properly

"Kitchen stuff" isn't specific enough. Label each box with contents and destination room. Mark fragile items clearly on multiple sides.

## 7. Forgetting to Disassemble Furniture

Moving assembled furniture risks damage to frames and doors. Take apart beds, tables, and shelving when possible. Keep hardware in labeled bags.

## 8. Packing Hazardous Materials

Paints, cleaners, propane tanks, and batteries can't go in moving trucks. They're dangerous and often illegal to transport. Dispose of them properly beforehand.

## 9. Waiting Until the Last Minute

Rushed packing leads to broken items and forgotten essentials. Start at least two weeks early, packing rarely-used items first. Miami traffic and summer thunderstorms can throw off your schedule, so give yourself extra buffer time.
