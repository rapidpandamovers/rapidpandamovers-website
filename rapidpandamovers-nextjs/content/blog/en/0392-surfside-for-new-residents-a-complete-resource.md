Thinking about moving to Surfside? You're considering one of Miami-Dade County's most charming beachfront communities. This guide provides essential information about Surfside to help you plan your relocation.

## About Surfside

Surfside is a small, family-friendly beach town with a population of approximately 5,700 residents. Covering just 0.50 square miles, this tight-knit community offers a village atmosphere with stunning oceanfront access. The town is notable for having one of the largest Jewish populations in the Miami metropolitan area, with approximately one-third of residents identifying as Jewish.

### Demographics at a Glance

- **Population**: Approximately 5,700 residents
- **Median Age**: 41 years
- **Median Household Income**: $93,621
- **Population Density**: 11,326 people per square mile
- **Educational Attainment**: 95.4% have high school diplomas; 59% have college degrees

## Real Estate Market

Surfside offers a mix of single-family homes, condominiums, and luxury high-rises along the oceanfront.

- **Median Home Value**: Approximately $737,000
- **Median Listing Price**: $1.3-1.4 million
- **Homeownership Rate**: 66.1%
- **Average Rent**: $1,932/month

**Important Note**: Surfside has an extreme flood risk, with 99% of properties at risk of severe flooding over the next 30 years. Factor this into your insurance planning.

## Schools and Education

Surfside is served by Miami-Dade County Public Schools, which offer strong academic programs. The area has access to 64 public elementary schools and 67 public high schools in the surrounding area. Top-rated schools nearby include International Studies Preparatory Academy (ranked #42 in Florida) and other A+ rated institutions.

## Transportation and Getting Around

### Surfside Shuttle
The town operates a free shuttle service on Saturdays from 8 AM to 1:30 PM, connecting residents to:
- Town Hall and local parks
- Surfside Publix
- Bal Harbour Shops
- Costco/Publix at Biscayne Boulevard
- Miami-Dade County bus connections

### Bus Service
Collins Avenue is served by Miami-Dade Transit routes 100, 125, and the Surfside shuttle, providing connections throughout the county.

### Parking
- Standard rate: $5/hour with 3-hour maximum
- Payment via multi-space meters or PayByPhone app
- Disabled parking: Free but subject to 3-hour limit

## Neighborhoods and Lifestyle

### Harding Avenue Corridor
The commercial heart of Surfside, featuring local shops, restaurants, and services. This walkable area is perfect for errands and casual dining.

### Oceanfront Properties
Collins Avenue features luxury condominiums with direct beach access. These properties command premium prices but offer unparalleled ocean views.

### Residential Streets
The interior neighborhoods feature charming single-family homes on tree-lined streets, perfect for families seeking a quieter atmosphere while remaining steps from the beach.

## Moving Tips for Surfside

### Building Access
Many oceanfront condos have specific moving hours and elevator reservations required. Contact your building management at least two weeks before your move.

### Parking Permits
Moving trucks may need temporary parking permits. Contact Surfside Police Department at 305-861-4862 for requirements.

### Best Moving Times
- **Weekday mornings** avoid beach traffic
- **Avoid holiday weekends** when parking is limited
- **Winter months** offer mild weather ideal for moving
