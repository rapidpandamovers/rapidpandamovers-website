# 8 Questions That Separate Good White Glove Movers From Great

Plenty of Miami movers call themselves "white glove." Fewer actually deliver on that promise. The difference between a good white glove company and a great one shows up in the details, and you can spot it by asking the right questions before you book.

## 1. What Happens if My Building Changes the Elevator Window?

Good movers adjust. Great movers already have a backup plan. A great white glove company builds buffer time into every high-rise move in Brickell or Sunny Isles because elevator schedule changes happen regularly. They maintain relationships with building managers and can often negotiate alternate windows on the spot. If the answer you get is "we'll figure it out," that's a good mover. If the answer is "we've already accounted for that," that's a great one.

## 2. Who Packs My Artwork, and What Training Do They Have?

Good movers wrap art in blankets. Great movers use museum-grade packing methods and have team members trained specifically in art handling. Ask about acid-free materials, custom crating, and whether the crew has experience with pieces from Wynwood galleries or private collections. The level of detail in the answer tells you everything about the level of care your art will receive.

## 3. How Do You Handle Damage Claims?

Good movers have a claims process. Great movers have a claims process you'll rarely need, backed by preventive measures that reduce damage to near zero. Ask about their damage rate, their average claim resolution time, and whether they work with restoration specialists for high-value items. A great company will talk about this openly and show you their track record.

## 4. Can I Speak With Your Last Three Luxury Move Clients?

Good movers have testimonials on their website. Great movers hand you phone numbers. A company that does consistent, high-quality white glove work in Coral Gables, Coconut Grove, and Key Biscayne has clients who are happy to talk about the experience. Reluctance to provide recent references is a signal that the quality isn't where it should be.

## 5. What's Your Crew Turnover Rate?

Good movers hire competent workers. Great movers retain them. A crew that has worked together for years handles your furniture differently than a team assembled that morning from a labor pool. Experienced, stable crews develop nonverbal communication during lifts, know each other's strengths, and move with a confidence that comes from repetition. Ask how long the crew assigned to your move has been with the company.

## 6. How Do You Protect Floors in a Home With Multiple Surface Types?

Good movers lay down runners. Great movers assess every floor surface and deploy specific protection for each material. Marble gets different treatment than hardwood. Polished concrete needs different protection than travertine tile. The answer to this question reveals whether the company applies a one-size-fits-all approach or customizes protection for luxury homes where flooring alone can cost more than a standard move.

## 7. What Happens After 5 PM on Moving Day?

Good movers stop when their shift ends. Great movers finish the job. A white glove move should end with your home set up, not with the crew watching the clock. Ask about overtime policies, whether the crew stays until the job is done, and what happens if the move runs longer than estimated. For a large Fisher Island estate that takes 12 hours instead of the planned 10, you want a company that stays until the last lamp is plugged in.

## 8. Do You Coordinate With Other Service Providers?

Good movers handle the move. Great movers coordinate with your interior designer, art consultant, home automation tech, and cleaning crew. A luxury move in Miami rarely involves just furniture. Great white glove companies understand that they're one part of a larger project and proactively manage their piece in relation to everyone else's schedule.
