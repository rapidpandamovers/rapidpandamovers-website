# 7 Signs You Need White Glove Moving Service

Not every move needs the premium treatment, but plenty of people realize too late that their situation called for it. A scratched antique dresser or cracked marble tabletop is an expensive lesson. Here are seven signs that your Miami move belongs in white glove hands.

## 1. You Own Furniture Worth More Than Your Car

If your living room contains a custom sectional from Roche Bobois, a handcrafted dining table from a Wynwood artisan, or a vintage Eames lounge chair, standard moving blankets are not enough. These pieces need custom wrapping, specialized padding, and movers who understand how to handle weight distribution on designer furniture. One wrong angle through a doorway can cause thousands in damage.

![Black Leather Belt With Intricate Design 3](/images/blog/2025/07/7-signs-you-need-white-glove-moving-service/black-leather-belt-with-intricate-design-3.webp)


## 2. Your Walls Display Original Artwork

Prints from HomeGoods survive a standard move just fine. Original oil paintings, limited-edition photography, and sculptural wall pieces do not. If you've collected art from galleries in the Design District or Coconut Grove, those works need acid-free wrapping, custom crating, and climate-controlled transport. White glove teams treat each piece as irreplaceable because, in most cases, it is.

## 3. You're Moving Into or Out of a High-Rise

Brickell condos, Edgewater towers, and luxury buildings along Collins Avenue come with strict moving rules. Elevator reservations, mandatory floor protection, insurance certificates, and loading dock time limits make high-rise moves logistically different from house moves. White glove companies deal with building management requirements routinely and show up prepared.

## 4. You Have a Wine Collection

Even a modest collection of 50-100 bottles deserves temperature-controlled handling, especially in South Florida. Vibration during transport can disturb sediment in aged wines, and a few hours in a hot truck can ruin an entire collection. White glove movers use insulated packaging, vibration-dampening materials, and plan transport timing to minimize heat exposure.

## 5. Your Home Has Specialty Flooring

Calacatta marble in the foyer, hand-scraped hardwood throughout the living areas, or imported tile in the kitchen all scratch easily under the weight of heavy furniture. White glove teams lay protective runners, use felt-padded dollies, and never drag anything across a floor surface. For homes in Coral Gables and Coconut Grove where flooring can cost $50 per square foot, this protection pays for itself.

## 6. You'd Rather Not Pack a Single Box

Some people enjoy the packing process. Most don't. If the thought of wrapping dishes, folding clothes into boxes, and emptying every cabinet sounds like a week you'd rather skip, white glove service handles all of it. The team packs your entire home, from the junk drawer to the crystal stemware, and unpacks everything at the destination.

## 7. You're on a Tight Timeline

Relocating for a job, closing on two properties simultaneously, or moving before a renovation deadline leaves zero room for delays. White glove teams work with dedicated move managers who build detailed timelines and keep the entire process on schedule. No guessing, no scrambling, no last-minute surprises.
