# Yard Waste Removal Before Your Move

Miami yards produce a lot of green waste. Between the tropical growth rate, hurricane season debris, and the general upkeep that South Florida landscaping demands, your property can accumulate piles of branches, palm fronds, leaves, and dead plants faster than you'd expect. If you're getting ready to move, clearing the yard is just as important as cleaning out the house, especially if you're selling or need to pass a landlord inspection.

## Why Yard Waste Matters Before a Move

A neglected yard sends the wrong message to buyers, landlords, and home inspectors. Overgrown hedges, piled-up fronds, and dead landscaping make a property look abandoned. If you're selling, curb appeal directly affects how much buyers are willing to offer. If you're renting, uncleared yard waste can cost you your security deposit.

![A Chaotic Pile Of Metal Scrap And Waste  4](/images/blog/2029/04/yard-waste-removal-before-your-move/a-chaotic-pile-of-metal-scrap-and-waste--4.webp)


For Miami homeowners, there's also the practical issue of HOA compliance. Many communities in Doral, Kendall, Weston, and Pembroke Pines have strict landscaping requirements, and leaving a mess behind can result in fines that follow you even after you've moved.

## What Counts as Yard Waste

- Palm fronds and coconuts (a constant in Miami)
- Tree branches and limbs
- Hedge trimmings and shrub clippings
- Dead plants and pulled weeds
- Grass clippings in large quantities
- Soil from garden beds
- Old mulch and landscape rock
- Root balls from removed trees or shrubs

## Miami-Dade Yard Waste Pickup Rules

Miami-Dade County provides curbside yard waste collection, but there are rules:

![A Pile Of Corroded And Wrecked Cars Stac 1](/images/blog/2029/04/yard-waste-removal-before-your-move/a-pile-of-corroded-and-wrecked-cars-stac-1.webp)


- Branches must be cut to four feet or shorter and bundled
- Yard waste should be placed in paper bags or open containers (not plastic bags)
- Piles should not exceed six cubic yards per pickup
- Collection schedules vary by neighborhood, typically once per week

If you're doing a major yard cleanout before a move, the volume often exceeds what curbside pickup can handle in a single week. Waiting for multiple weekly pickups doesn't work when you're on a moving timeline.

## Preparing Your Yard for Removal Day

To get the most efficient service:

- Pile yard waste in one accessible area, ideally near the driveway or street
- Keep branches and fronds separate from dirt and soil when possible (different disposal methods)
- Remove any items mixed in with the yard waste that aren't green waste, like garden hoses, plastic pots, and metal stakes
- Point out any hazards to the crew, such as fire ant mounds, wasp nests, or unstable tree limbs

## Timing Your Yard Cleanout

Schedule your yard cleanup for one to two weeks before your move or listing photos. This gives the lawn time to recover from any major work, and it lets you do a final touch-up mow and edge before the big day. If you've removed trees or large shrubs, the bare spots may need fresh sod or mulch to look presentable.
