# DIY vs Professional Pool Table Moving: Real Costs

The quote from a professional pool table mover came in, and you're thinking: "How hard can it be? I'll rent a truck and get some friends to help." We hear this a lot. And we also get calls the next day from homeowners who cracked their slate, stripped their rail bolts, or tore their felt trying to save a few hundred dollars. Here's an honest breakdown of what DIY pool table moving actually costs compared to hiring pros.

## The True Cost of DIY

Most people only think about the truck rental when they plan a DIY move. The real expenses add up fast:

![A Person Arranging Billiard Balls On A B 1](/images/blog/2026/06/diy-vs-professional-pool-table-moving-real-costs/a-person-arranging-billiard-balls-on-a-b-1.webp)


### Equipment Costs

- **Truck rental**: $50-$100 for a local move, plus gas and mileage
- **Furniture dollies**: $15-$30 to rent (and standard dollies aren't designed for 250-pound slate pieces)
- **Moving blankets**: $20-$40 to buy or rent enough to wrap all the components
- **Socket wrench set**: $20-$50 if you don't already own one (you'll need specific sizes for rail bolts)
- **Staple puller**: $10-$15 for felt removal
- **Machinist's level**: $40-$80 for a quality 4-foot level (a bubble level from the hardware store won't cut it)
- **Beeswax and scraper**: $15-$25 for seam filling

**Equipment subtotal: $170-$340**

### The Pizza-and-Beer Crew

You'll need at least three strong friends, ideally four. Each slate piece weighs 150 to 250 pounds, and the frame is another 150+. Most people offer food and drinks as payment, but even that adds $50-$100. And here's the thing: your friends aren't insured. If someone throws out their back carrying slate down your stairs, that's on you.

### The Hidden Costs

This is where DIY gets expensive. Without experience, mistakes are almost guaranteed:

- **Cracked slate**: Replacement cost is $250-$600 per piece, and three-piece sets often need to be matched. A single hairline crack from dropping a slate piece 2 inches can ruin it.
- **Torn felt**: Re-felting costs $300-$800 depending on the cloth quality. In Miami's humidity, old felt is especially fragile and tears easily during amateur removal.
- **Stripped rail bolts**: Over-tightened or cross-threaded bolts during reassembly cost $50-$150 to repair and often require a professional to extract.
- **Damaged frame**: Dragging or tipping the frame can crack joints. Frame repairs run $200-$500.
- **Improper leveling**: A table that isn't leveled correctly doesn't play correctly. Hiring someone to come level it after the fact costs $100-$200.

**One mistake can easily exceed the cost of professional moving.**

## The Cost of Professional Moving

Professional [**Pool Table Moving**](/pool-table-moving) in Miami typically runs:

- **Standard local move** (same floor, under 20 miles): $300-$450
- **Move involving stairs or elevator**: $400-$550
- **Move with re-felting**: $500-$800
- **Long-distance within Florida**: $600-$1,000+

That price includes full disassembly, wrapping and padding, transport, reassembly, precision leveling, and beeswax seam filling. It also includes insurance coverage if anything goes wrong.

## Time Investment

DIY time is consistently underestimated. For someone who has never disassembled a pool table:

![Close Up Of A Billiard Table With Chalk  3](/images/blog/2026/06/diy-vs-professional-pool-table-moving-real-costs/close-up-of-a-billiard-table-with-chalk--3.webp)


- **Disassembly**: 2-4 hours (a pro does this in 45-60 minutes)
- **Loading and transport**: 1-2 hours
- **Unloading and reassembly**: 3-5 hours (a pro does this in 90 minutes to 2 hours)
- **Leveling**: 1-2 hours if you know what you're doing, much longer if you don't

You're looking at a full day of hard physical labor, and that's assuming everything goes smoothly. A professional crew typically completes a local move in 3-4 hours total.

## When DIY Might Make Sense

We'll be honest: there are situations where DIY is reasonable. If you have a lightweight MDF table (under 300 pounds), you're moving it within the same house, and you have experience with basic furniture assembly, you can probably handle it. But for any slate table, any move involving stairs, or any move onto a truck, the risk-to-reward ratio strongly favors hiring professionals.
