# White Glove Movers and Smart Home Reconnection

Modern luxury homes in Miami run on technology. From Crestron-controlled lighting to Lutron motorized shades, Sonos whole-home audio, and Ring or Verkada security cameras, the average high-end home has dozens of connected devices that all need to be disconnected, moved, and set back up. Standard movers unplug things and hope someone else figures out the rest. White glove service includes a plan for every device in your smart home.

## Why Smart Homes Complicate Moves

A standard home has furniture, appliances, and boxes. A smart home adds a network layer on top of all that. Devices talk to a hub. The hub talks to the internet. Sensors, cameras, locks, and thermostats all depend on specific placement, Wi-Fi signal strength, and configuration settings. Moving these devices isn't just a matter of packing and unpacking. It's a technology project layered into a moving project. Our white glove teams treat it accordingly.

## Pre-Move Technology Documentation

Before disconnecting anything, we document your entire smart home setup. This includes photographing the location of every device, recording network names and configurations, noting which devices connect to which hub, and cataloging any custom automation routines. For homes in Brickell and Coral Gables running Savant or Control4 systems, we coordinate directly with the installer to save system configurations that would take hours to recreate from scratch.

## Disconnecting and Packing Connected Devices

Each device gets its own packing protocol. Security cameras are removed from mounts and packed with their mounting hardware in labeled bags. Smart thermostats come off the wall with their wiring documented. Motorized shade motors get disconnected from window treatments. Mesh Wi-Fi nodes are labeled by location so they can be placed in equivalent spots at the new home. Smart speakers, hubs, and bridges are packed in anti-static bags to protect their electronics from Miami's humidity.

## Network Infrastructure

The network is the backbone of every smart home. Our team identifies your router, switches, access points, and any hardwired connections. For homes with structured wiring, including Ethernet drops in every room and centralized patch panels, we document the entire layout. At the destination, we work with your network installer to get the infrastructure running before reconnecting devices, since nothing else works without the network.

## Reinstallation at the New Home

Device placement at the destination follows the documentation created before the move. Security cameras return to equivalent vantage points. Motion sensors cover the same zones. Smart locks go on the same doors. Thermostats reconnect to HVAC systems. For devices that need professional installation, like in-wall keypads or recessed speakers, we coordinate the installer's visit as part of the move timeline.

## Testing and Verification

After reinstallation, we test every device. Lights respond to commands. Cameras show live feeds. Door locks engage and disengage remotely. Shades raise and lower. Music plays through the correct zones. For complex Crestron or Savant systems in Key Biscayne and Star Island estates, we schedule a follow-up visit from the home automation integrator to verify full system functionality and recalibrate any touchscreens or remote interfaces.

## Voice Assistants and Routines

Alexa, Google Home, and Apple HomeKit routines often break during a move because room assignments and device locations change. Our team updates device names and room assignments in each app so your "Good Morning" routine still dims the bedroom lights and starts the coffee maker, just in a different house. This takes patience and familiarity with each platform.
