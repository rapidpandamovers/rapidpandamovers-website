Surfside spans just one square mile but packs in beaches, fine dining, and a walkable downtown with retro charm. Here are eight ways to spend your weekends after moving to this coastal gem between Miami Beach and Bal Harbour.

## 1. Spend a Morning at Surfside Beach

Surfside Beach runs along the entire eastern edge of town, with public access points along Collins Avenue. The sand is clean, the water is clear, and the crowds are smaller than South Beach. Arrive before 10 AM on weekends to snag a good spot. Metered parking is available along Harding Avenue and side streets, or walk from anywhere in town.

![A Vibrant Beach Scene In Clearwater Fl F 3](/images/blog/2028/03/8-weekend-activities-to-enjoy-in-surfside/a-vibrant-beach-scene-in-clearwater-fl-f-3.webp)


**Pro tip:** The beach at 93rd Street has lifeguards and is close to restaurants for a lunch break.

## 2. Dine at the Surf Club Restaurant

This Michelin-starred restaurant inside the Four Seasons Surf Club serves classic American cuisine in an elegant 1930s setting designed by architect Russell Pancoast. The Sunday brunch is legendary. Reservations are required, often weeks in advance for weekend dinners. Located at 9011 Collins Avenue.

## 3. Swim at the Surfside Community Center

The Surfside Community Center at 9301 Collins Avenue has a swimming pool with a waterslide, perfect for families. The facility also offers fitness classes and a cafe. Weekend pool hours run from 9 AM to 5 PM. Residents get discounted rates, so bring proof of address.

![Aerial View Of Beach Construction With M 2](/images/blog/2028/03/8-weekend-activities-to-enjoy-in-surfside/aerial-view-of-beach-construction-with-m-2.webp)


## 4. Shop Bal Harbour Shops

Walk or bike north to Bal Harbour Shops, one of the most exclusive outdoor malls in the country. Stores include Chanel, Gucci, Prada, and Neiman Marcus. Even if high-end shopping is not your thing, the architecture and gardens make for a pleasant stroll. Located at 9700 Collins Avenue, just a 10-minute walk from Surfside.

## 5. Grab a Classic Deli Meal at Josh's Deli

Josh's Deli at 9517 Harding Avenue puts a modern spin on Jewish deli classics. Try the pastrami sandwich, matzo ball soup, or the house-cured lox. The casual atmosphere is perfect for a weekend lunch. Surfside has a large Jewish community, and Josh's is a neighborhood favorite.

![Charming Beach Homes On Belleair Beach F 4](/images/blog/2028/03/8-weekend-activities-to-enjoy-in-surfside/charming-beach-homes-on-belleair-beach-f-4.webp)


## 6. Kayak at Oleta River State Park

Oleta River State Park sits about 15 minutes south in North Miami and offers kayak and paddleboard rentals. Paddle through mangrove trails, spot manatees, or bike the mountain biking trails. The park opens at 8 AM and is less crowded early on Saturday mornings. Entry fee is $6 per vehicle.

## 7. Walk the Downtown Strip Along Harding Avenue

Harding Avenue is the main commercial street running through Surfside. Walk from 93rd to 96th Street to find cafes, kosher restaurants, boutiques, and salons. Saturday mornings bring a relaxed vibe as residents grab coffee and run errands. Stop at Lenny's Deli for breakfast or The Carrot for fresh juice.

![Scenic Aerial Photo Of St Augustine Beac 5](/images/blog/2028/03/8-weekend-activities-to-enjoy-in-surfside/scenic-aerial-photo-of-st-augustine-beac-5.webp)


## 8. Enjoy Champagne at The Champagne Bar

The Champagne Bar at the Four Seasons Surf Club boasts the largest champagne selection in Miami. The oceanfront setting makes it perfect for sunset drinks. No reservation needed for the bar, but arrive by 6 PM on weekends to get a seat. Located at 9011 Collins Avenue.
