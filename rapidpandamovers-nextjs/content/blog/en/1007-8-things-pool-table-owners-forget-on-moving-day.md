# 8 Things Pool Table Owners Forget on Moving Day

Moving day is chaotic. You're coordinating the movers, packing last-minute boxes, and trying to get out of the old place on time. Pool table owners have an extra layer of planning, and in the rush, certain details get overlooked. After years of [**Pool Table Moving**](/pool-table-moving) across Miami, we've compiled the things that pool table owners forget most often. Save yourself the headache and check this list before your moving crew arrives.

## 1. Measuring the Exit and Entry Paths

You know the table fits in your current room because it's already there. But did it come in through the front door, or was it assembled inside during construction? And at the new home, have you confirmed that the hallway, doorway, or stairway is wide enough for the slate pieces? We've arrived at homes where the only exit required removing a sliding glass door from its track. Measure every doorway, hallway turn, and stairway along both the exit route and the entry route before moving day.

## 2. Booking the Freight Elevator

If you live in a Miami condo, the freight elevator isn't optional for a pool table move. Standard passenger elevators are too small for slate pieces and too weight-restricted for the combined load of movers plus components. Many buildings in Brickell, Edgewater, and Miami Beach require a minimum of one week's notice to reserve the freight elevator, plus a certificate of insurance (COI). Some buildings only allow moves on certain days. Book early or risk rescheduling your entire move.

## 3. Deciding About the Felt Before Moving Day

Should you keep your existing felt or install new cloth? This decision needs to happen before the movers show up, not while they're standing in your game room with staple pullers in hand. If you want new felt, your moving company needs to order the right color, material, and size in advance. Deciding on the spot means either reusing old felt you don't want or delaying reassembly while new cloth is sourced.

## 4. Clearing the Area Around the Table

On moving day, your game room should have nothing around the pool table except floor. Move the cue rack, bar stools, side tables, dart board, and anything else before the crew arrives. The disassembly process requires space to lay out components, set up slate jacks, and wrap pieces in blankets. A cluttered room adds 30-45 minutes to the job and increases the risk of someone tripping while carrying a 200-pound slate piece.

## 5. Taking Photos of the Setup

Before disassembly begins, take photos of your table from multiple angles. Photograph the pockets, the rail positions, the leg style, and the overall layout. These photos are a reference for reassembly, especially if you want the table oriented the same way in the new room. They're also useful documentation in case of any damage claims.

## 6. Collecting All the Accessories

Pool table accessories scatter across the game room over time. Before moving day, gather everything:

![Close Up Of Billiard Balls And Cue Stick 3](/images/blog/2028/08/8-things-pool-table-owners-forget-on-moving-day/close-up-of-billiard-balls-and-cue-stick-3.webp)


- All cue sticks (check behind doors, in closets, and under furniture)
- The bridge stick and mechanical bridge head
- Ball set (count them, you should have 16 for standard pool)
- Chalk cubes
- Table brush
- Table cover
- Triangle rack and/or diamond rack
- Scoreboard or overhead counter
- Cue rack mounting hardware
- Billiard light fixture (if you're taking it)

Put everything in one box or bag so nothing gets left behind or mixed in with general household items.

## 7. Preparing the New Room

The game room at your new home needs to be ready before the pool table arrives. That means:

- The room should be empty and clean
- Climate control should already be running (especially important in Miami, where a closed-up room without AC can reach 90+ degrees with extreme humidity)
- Any planned flooring, painting, or wall work should be finished
- The billiard light electrical box should be installed (if applicable)
- Furniture pads or floor protectors should be positioned where the legs will go

Showing up with a disassembled pool table to a room that's still being painted means storing the components somewhere for days, which isn't ideal for the felt or the wood frame.

## 8. Scheduling a Post-Move Level Check

Your pool table will be leveled during reassembly, but certain floor types settle after the table is in place. Carpet compresses under the leg weight over 2-3 weeks. Hardwood floors can dent slightly. Even tile grout can compress. Plan to have the table re-checked for level 2-3 weeks after the move. Many pool table moving companies, including ours, offer follow-up leveling at a reduced rate when booked alongside the initial move.
