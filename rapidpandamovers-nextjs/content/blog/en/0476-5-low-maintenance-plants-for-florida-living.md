South Florida's spring climate is perfect for houseplants, but only if you choose the right ones. These varieties thrive in Miami's May humidity and light conditions, bringing life to your new home without demanding expert care.

## 1. Pothos (Epipremnum aureum)

Nearly indestructible. Thrives in low light, tolerates irregular watering, and purifies air. Perfect for Miami beginners. You'll see pothos trailing from shelves in Wynwood lofts and Doral townhomes alike.

![Dense Foliage Of Monstera Leaves In A Tr 3](/images/blog/2027/05/5-low-maintenance-plants-for-florida-living/dense-foliage-of-monstera-leaves-in-a-tr-3.webp)


## 2. Bird of Paradise (Strelitzia)

Makes a dramatic statement and loves Miami's bright light and warmth. Can grow impressively large indoors.

## 3. Snake Plant (Sansevieria)

Tolerates neglect, low light, and inconsistent watering. Ideal for air-conditioned environments like Brickell high-rises where AC runs constantly against the Miami heat.

![Elegant Yellow Freesia Flowers In A Blue 1](/images/blog/2027/05/5-low-maintenance-plants-for-florida-living/elegant-yellow-freesia-flowers-in-a-blue-1.webp)


## 4. Monstera Deliciosa

The iconic split-leaf philodendron thrives in Miami's humidity. Grows impressive leaves with proper care.

## 5. Bromeliad

Native to tropical climates, bromeliads love humidity and add colorful blooms to any space. You can find beautiful specimens at local nurseries in Kendall and Homestead, where growers take advantage of South Florida's ideal conditions.

![Vibrant Yellow Cymbidium Orchids In An O 4](/images/blog/2027/05/5-low-maintenance-plants-for-florida-living/vibrant-yellow-cymbidium-orchids-in-an-o-4.webp)


## Moving with Plants in Miami

Whether you're relocating from Brickell to Coral Gables or moving into a new Coconut Grove condo, transporting plants takes special care. The good news: these five varieties handle the stress of moving better than most. Just avoid leaving them in a hot moving truck for extended periods during Miami's warm months.
