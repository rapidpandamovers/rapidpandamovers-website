Whether you're a long-time Miami resident or just making your move to South Florida, there's always something new to explore. Here's our list of historic buildings that tell the story of Miami's growth from a small town to a major metropolis.

## 1. Vizcaya Museum and Gardens

This 1916 Italian Renaissance-style estate in Coconut Grove has moved countless artifacts and artworks over its century.

![Bright Day On Ocean Drive Featuring The  3](/images/blog/2025/11/8-storied-structures-in-the-miami-area/bright-day-on-ocean-drive-featuring-the--3.webp)


## 2. The Freedom Tower

Known as "Miami's Ellis Island," this 1925 building processed Cuban refugees and stands as a symbol of migration.

## 3. The Biltmore Hotel

This 1926 Coral Gables landmark has served as a hospital, a university, and a luxury hotel. Each transition required massive moves of furniture, equipment, and historical artifacts.

## 4. Art Deco Historic District

South Beach's iconic buildings from the 1930s and 40s have housed countless residents through decades of Miami history.

## 5. Coral Gables City Hall

Built in 1928, this Mediterranean Revival building has witnessed the growth of one of Miami's most elegant communities.

![Captivating View Of Miami S Iconic Skyli 2](/images/blog/2025/11/8-storied-structures-in-the-miami-area/captivating-view-of-miami-s-iconic-skyli-2.webp)


## 6. The Deering Estate

Located at Cutler Bay, this 1920s estate was the winter home of Charles Deering. The property includes the Stone House and Richmond Cottage, both overlooking Biscayne Bay. The estate's transition from private residence to public park preserved its historic character.

## 7. Ancient Spanish Monastery

This 12th-century Spanish monastery was disassembled stone by stone and shipped to North Miami Beach in 1925. William Randolph Hearst purchased the building in Spain, and the massive relocation project took years to complete.

## 8. Plymouth Congregational Church

Built in 1917 in Coconut Grove, this coral rock church features a hand-carved wooden door from a 17th-century Spanish monastery. The building has hosted community gatherings and weddings for over a century.
