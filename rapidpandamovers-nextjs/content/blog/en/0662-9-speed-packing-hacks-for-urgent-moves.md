Your move is in three days and you haven't started packing. The boxes are still flat, the tape is somewhere in the garage, and panic is setting in. Take a breath. Our Miami crews have helped hundreds of families pack entire homes in under 48 hours. Here are nine speed techniques that actually work when time is short.

## 1. The Suitcase Strategy

Roll clothes and pack them in suitcases with wheels. You already own luggage that needs to move anyway, and wheeled bags roll faster than you can carry boxes. This works especially well for Miami residents moving to high-rises in Brickell or Aventura, where elevator time is limited and every trip counts.

## 2. Stand Plates Vertically

Pack plates vertically in boxes, like records in a crate. This distributes pressure evenly and cuts breakage dramatically. Wrap each plate in packing paper first, or use foam plates between them if you have extras from your last barbecue.

## 3. Garbage Bag Wardrobes

Cut a hole in the bottom of a garbage bag, slip it over hanging clothes, and tie the hanger hooks together. Instant wardrobe cover for free. This protects clothes from Miami's humidity during the move and keeps everything dry if you hit afternoon thunderstorms.

## 4. Tape Hardware to Furniture

When disassembling beds, tables, or shelves, put screws and bolts in a labeled ziplock bag and tape it directly to the furniture piece. No more mystery hardware when you're reassembling at midnight in your new Coconut Grove apartment.

## 5. Load by Weight

Heavy items go in the truck first, then medium weight, then light. Stack boxes with heaviest on bottom. This prevents crushing and maximizes space. Even for a short move from Kendall to Coral Gables, proper loading means faster unloading.

## 6. Stretch Wrap Everything

Wrap furniture in stretch wrap to protect surfaces and keep drawers closed. A $15 roll from Home Depot covers more furniture than $100 worth of moving blankets. Wrap dresser drawers shut instead of emptying them to save an hour of packing time.

## 7. Color-Code by Room

Assign each room a tape color: blue for bedroom, green for kitchen, red for living room. Stick colored tape on each box. Movers unload directly to the right room without stopping to read labels. At your new place, this simple system can save 30 minutes of confusion.

## 8. Towels as Padding

Use towels, blankets, and linens to wrap fragile items. Beach towels work perfectly for picture frames. Those extra blankets you keep for when the AC gets too cold? Excellent padding for electronics. You're moving these items anyway.

## 9. Pack an Overnight Bag Last

Load your overnight bag last so it comes off the truck first. Include toiletries, phone chargers, medications, a change of clothes, and important documents. You'll thank yourself at 10pm when you're too tired to hunt through boxes.

## What to Expect from Speed Packing

When you use these techniques, you can realistically pack:

- **Studio apartment**: 2-3 hours with one person
- **1-bedroom**: 4-5 hours with one person, 2-3 hours with two
- **2-bedroom**: 6-8 hours with two people
- **3-bedroom house**: 1-2 days with 2-3 people working consistently

These estimates assume you're already decluttered. If you're also sorting and donating, add 50% more time.
