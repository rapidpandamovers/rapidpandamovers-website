# 8 Specialty Items and How Experts Move Them

Not everything in a home fits neatly into a box. Some items need specialized equipment, trained crews, and a plan built around their specific size, weight, or fragility. Here are eight items our Miami teams handle regularly, and how we get each one from Point A to Point B without damage.

## 1. Grand Pianos

A baby grand weighs 500-600 pounds; a concert grand tops 1,200. We lock the lid, wrap the body in padded blankets, and use a piano board with heavy-duty straps to keep it stable. The legs come off and travel separately. Every doorway and turn gets measured in advance. In high-rise condos along Biscayne Boulevard, freight elevator reservations are mandatory.

## 2. Gun Safes

Residential safes range from 400 to 2,000 pounds. We use safe dollies with stair-climbing attachments and 4-point ratchet straps. The path from room to truck gets protected with plywood sheets. In older Miami homes with narrow hallways, we sometimes remove door trim to gain the extra inch needed for clearance.

## 3. Marble Dining Tables

Marble cracks from vibration and uneven support, not just impact. We separate the slab from the base, wrap it in foam board and moving blankets, and transport it upright on an A-frame dolly. Suction cup lifters rated for damp surfaces handle the reassembly, which matters in Miami's humidity.

## 4. Hot Tubs

An empty hot tub weighs 600-900 pounds. Plumbing and electrical get disconnected by licensed professionals, the shell drains completely, and we use a crane or rigging setup to clear fences and landscaping. Getting one out of a tight Kendall backyard through a 36-inch gate sometimes means the gate comes off its hinges temporarily.

## 5. Antique Grandfather Clocks

The pendulum, weights, and chains come out first. Glass side panels get removed and wrapped separately. The case travels upright, strapped to a padded dolly. At the new location, the clock gets leveled to within a fraction of a degree so the pendulum swings accurately.

## 6. Large Aquariums

Fish ride in insulated coolers with battery-powered aerators. The tank travels empty and upright. Live rock stays wet in sealed buckets of tank water. We save at least half the original water to preserve the bacterial ecosystem. Reassembly and livestock reintroduction follow a strict 2-hour minimum stabilization window.

## 7. Wine Collections

Heat is the enemy. We use insulated shipping boxes with cell dividers and schedule transport for early morning to avoid peak Florida temperatures. For high-value collections, a climate-controlled van keeps bottles at cellar temperature throughout the trip. Wine fridges stay upright and need 24 hours before plugging in at the new home.

## 8. Home Gym Equipment

Squat racks get disassembled with every bolt labeled and bagged. Treadmill consoles are wrapped like fragile electronics. Rubber flooring rolls up and travels upright. Free weights go into reinforced small boxes, never exceeding 60 pounds per container. A full home gym can total 3,000+ pounds across dozens of individual pieces.
