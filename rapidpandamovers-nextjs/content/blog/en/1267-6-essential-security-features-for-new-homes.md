The first 48 hours in your new home set the tone for your family's security. Moving trucks come and go, boxes stack up in open garages, and strangers see exactly what valuables you own. This is when homes are most vulnerable. Here are the six security priorities to tackle before you unpack your first box.

## 1. Rekey All Exterior Doors Before Moving Day

Schedule a locksmith for the day before you move in, not after. Previous owners may have distributed keys to neighbors, dog walkers, cleaners, and contractors. In Miami's turnover-heavy real estate market, a single home might have had dozens of key holders over the years.

![Assorted Firearms Arranged On A Wooden T 3](/images/blog/2029/12/6-essential-security-features-for-new-homes/assorted-firearms-arranged-on-a-wooden-t-3.webp)


Rekeying costs $15-25 per lock and takes about 30 minutes for a typical home. Smart locks with rotating codes offer extra flexibility for Miami's gig economy - you can create temporary access for Instacart deliveries or cleaning services without sharing physical keys.

## 2. Inspect Every Window Latch and Sliding Door

Walk through each room with a checklist. Test every window lock, especially on ground-floor and second-floor rooms accessible from roofs or trees. Miami's older homes in Little Havana and Coconut Grove often have jalousie windows with worn latches that provide minimal security.

Sliding glass doors present the biggest risk. Without secondary locks, they can be lifted off their tracks in seconds. Install a security bar at floor level and pin locks at the top of the frame. Budget $20-50 for supplies at any Home Depot in Miami-Dade.

## 3. Activate Monitoring Before the Move

Your security system should be active the day boxes start arriving. Moving trucks advertise your new belongings to everyone watching, and an empty home with stacked valuables presents an easy target.

![Close Up View Of Warning Label On A Fire 1](/images/blog/2029/12/6-essential-security-features-for-new-homes/close-up-view-of-warning-label-on-a-fire-1.webp)


For Miami condos in Brickell or Aventura towers, building security offers baseline protection but door sensors and cameras add crucial coverage for your unit. DIY systems like Ring or SimpliSafe can be installed in hours and provide smartphone alerts when someone approaches your door.

## 4. Install Motion-Activated Exterior Lighting

Darkness provides cover for break-ins. Motion-sensor lights around entry points create instant visibility and startle anyone approaching. Miami's early winter sunsets mean you may arrive home after dark from October through February.

Focus on the front door, garage side entrance, backyard, and any gates. Solar-powered options work exceptionally well in South Florida and require no wiring. Mount lights high enough that intruders cannot easily unscrew bulbs.

## 5. Replace Smoke Detector Batteries and Test Carbon Monoxide Alarms

Fire safety is security too. Replace all batteries on move-in day regardless of their apparent condition. Florida code requires smoke detectors in every bedroom and on each floor. Units older than 10 years should be replaced entirely.

![Detailed Close Up Of A Gun Barrel Restin 2](/images/blog/2029/12/6-essential-security-features-for-new-homes/detailed-close-up-of-a-gun-barrel-restin-2.webp)


Carbon monoxide detectors are critical if your Miami home has gas appliances or an attached garage. Test all alarms monthly - Miami's humidity can affect sensor performance over time.

## 6. Reset Garage Door Opener Codes

The garage represents the most common entry point for residential break-ins. Previous owners may have distributed opener remotes to family members, neighbors, or service providers. Resetting the code takes five minutes and eliminates this vulnerability completely.

Hold the "learn" button on your opener for 10-15 seconds to clear all programmed remotes. Then reprogram only your own devices. Consider upgrading to a smart garage opener that sends alerts when the door opens unexpectedly.
