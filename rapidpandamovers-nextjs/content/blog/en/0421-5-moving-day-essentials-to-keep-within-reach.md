Moving day in Miami comes with its own set of challenges. Between the heat, the traffic on I-95, and coordinating everything from Brickell to Kendall, having the right supplies within reach can save your day. Here are the five essentials every Miami mover should keep close by.

## 1. Important Documents

Passports, IDs, birth certificates, lease or closing papers. Keep these on your person or in your personal vehicle, never in a moving box.

![Crop Anonymous Guy In Casual Clothes Usi 1](/images/blog/2026/03/5-moving-day-essentials-to-keep-within-reach/crop-anonymous-guy-in-casual-clothes-usi-1.webp)


## 2. Medications and First Aid

Current prescriptions, over-the-counter essentials, and any medical devices you might need in the next 24 hours stay with you.

## 3. Phone Chargers and Power Banks

Dead phones on moving day mean missed calls from movers, FPL, and anyone trying to help coordinate. Keep a portable charger handy, especially if you're stuck in traffic near the Palmetto or waiting for building access in Downtown Miami.

![Crop Unrecognizable Man In Casual Clothe 2](/images/blog/2026/03/5-moving-day-essentials-to-keep-within-reach/crop-unrecognizable-man-in-casual-clothe-2.webp)


## 4. Basic Toolkit

Screwdriver set, pliers, box cutter, hammer. These handle last-minute needs at both locations, from reassembling IKEA furniture to adjusting hurricane shutters. You'll use these more than you expect.

## 5. Cleaning Supplies

Paper towels, all-purpose cleaner, trash bags. These help with quick clean-ups during the transition at both old and new locations. In Miami's humid climate, a quick wipe-down before the AC kicks in makes a big difference.
