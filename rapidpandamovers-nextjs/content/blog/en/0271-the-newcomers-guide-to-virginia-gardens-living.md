Virginia Gardens continues to attract new residents from across the country during August, and it's easy to see why. This small village of just under 2,500 residents sits right next to Miami International Airport, offering affordable housing with quick access to everything Miami-Dade has to offer.

## Why Choose Virginia Gardens?

Virginia Gardens stands out as one of Miami-Dade County's most affordable and convenient communities. The village covers just half a square mile, creating a tight-knit neighborhood where residents know their neighbors. Tree-lined streets and well-maintained single-family homes define the landscape here.

![Capture Of A Modern Building In Miami Sh 4](/images/blog/2025/08/the-newcomers-guide-to-virginia-gardens-living/capture-of-a-modern-building-in-miami-sh-4.webp)


The area attracts families, airport employees, and retirees looking for a quiet residential feel without the Miami price tag. Homes here sell for significantly less than neighboring Coral Gables or Miami Springs, making it popular with first-time buyers.

### Location and Accessibility

Virginia Gardens sits directly west of Miami International Airport along NW 36th Street (the main airport entrance road). The Palmetto Expressway (SR 826) runs along the western border, putting Downtown Miami just 15 minutes away and Miami Beach about 25 minutes east.

Public transit options include several Metrobus routes along 36th Street. For frequent flyers, living minutes from MIA terminals is a major perk. The Metrorail Orange Line stop at MIA connects you to the entire county rail system.

### Community and Lifestyle

Virginia Gardens residents enjoy a strong sense of community. The Virginia Gardens Park on NW 38th Terrace offers a playground, basketball courts, and green space for weekend barbecues. The village hosts annual events like holiday celebrations and National Night Out gatherings.

For shopping and dining, residents head to nearby Miami Springs on Canal Street or the Westland Mall area. Publix and Sedano's supermarkets sit within a 5-minute drive. The Doral restaurant scene on 87th Avenue is also just minutes away.

## Neighborhoods to Consider

Virginia Gardens is small enough that the entire village feels like one neighborhood. Most homes cluster along NW 37th and 38th Streets. Properties closer to NW 57th Avenue tend to be quieter, while those near 36th Street have easier highway access.

### Choosing Your Perfect Spot

Consider these factors when house hunting:

- **Proximity to work**: MIA employees love the 5-minute commute. Doral offices are 10 minutes west.
- **School zoning**: Virginia Gardens Elementary is the local public school. Miami Springs Senior High serves older students.
- **Property types**: Most homes are single-family ranch-style from the 1950s and 1960s.
- **Flood zones**: Check FEMA maps. Some areas require flood insurance.

## Moving to Virginia Gardens in August

August brings peak summer heat to South Florida. Temperatures hit the low 90s daily, and afternoon thunderstorms roll in almost every day around 3 PM. Plan your move for early morning when possible.

### Timing Your Move

When planning your relocation, consider:

- **Best moving days**: Weekdays offer better availability and rates. Avoid the first and last days of the month.
- **Weather strategy**: Start by 7 AM to beat the afternoon storms. Keep tarps ready for sudden downpours.
- **Hurricane season**: August falls in peak hurricane season. Monitor forecasts and have a backup date if a storm threatens.

## Essential Services to Locate

As a new Virginia Gardens resident, you'll want to find these nearby essentials:

- **Schools**: Virginia Gardens Elementary (K-5), Hialeah-Miami Lakes Senior High, and several private options in Miami Springs.
- **Shopping**: Westland Mall for retail, Publix and Sedano's for groceries, and Target in Doral.
