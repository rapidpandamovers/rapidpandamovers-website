# When to Hire Piano Movers vs General Movers

You're planning a move across Miami, and your piano needs to come along. The moving company handling your furniture says they can take the piano too. Should you let them? Or should you hire a separate team that specializes in pianos? The answer depends on several factors, and getting it wrong can cost you thousands in damage.

## What General Movers Do Differently

Most general moving companies are great at what they do: packing boxes, wrapping furniture, loading trucks efficiently. Their crews are trained to handle sofas, dressers, appliances, and dining tables. They have dollies, straps, and blankets designed for standard household items.

![Artistic Close Up Of A Hand Playing Pian 3](/images/blog/2026/12/when-to-hire-piano-movers-vs-general-movers/artistic-close-up-of-a-hand-playing-pian-3.webp)


But pianos aren't standard household items. A general mover's crew may have moved a few pianos over the years, but they probably don't have piano-specific equipment like piano boards, leg bolt tools, or stair-climbing rigs designed for 800-pound loads. They may not know how to properly remove and reattach grand piano legs. They may not understand that laying an upright piano on its back can damage the action mechanism.

We've been called to repair situations where general movers dropped a grand piano during leg removal, cracked an upright's case going down stairs, or scratched a high-gloss finish because they used regular moving blankets without a cotton underlayer.

## When General Movers Might Be Fine

For a basic digital piano or a keyboard on a stand, a general moving crew can handle the job. These instruments weigh under 100 pounds, don't have precision internal mechanisms affected by vibration, and can be treated like sensitive electronics with proper padding.

![Black And White Piano Keys With A Sheet  5](/images/blog/2026/12/when-to-hire-piano-movers-vs-general-movers/black-and-white-piano-keys-with-a-sheet--5.webp)


A small spinet piano (the shortest type of upright, typically under 40 inches tall and 300 pounds) being moved across a single floor with wide doors and no stairs is also a lower-risk scenario that experienced general movers may handle successfully.

## When You Absolutely Need Piano Movers

### Grand Pianos of Any Size

Every grand piano, from a 4'11" petite grand to a 9' concert grand, requires leg removal, a piano board, and a crew that knows the disassembly and reassembly process. This is non-negotiable. General movers almost never carry piano boards.

### Moves Involving Stairs

Any piano move that involves stairs, whether up, down, straight, or spiral, needs a crew with specific staircase experience and equipment. The weight, the awkward shape, and the consequences of a slip make this too risky for an untrained crew.

### High-Rise Buildings

Moving a piano into a Brickell or Downtown Miami high-rise means freight elevators, loading dock scheduling, building insurance requirements, and potentially tight corridors on the destination floor. Piano moving crews handle these logistics regularly and know the building management requirements.

### Valuable or Antique Pianos

If your piano is worth more than $10,000 or has sentimental value that can't be replaced, specialist movers with proper insurance are the only reasonable choice. General moving insurance typically covers items by weight (around $0.60 per pound), which would value your $30,000 Steinway at about $360.

### Long-Distance Moves

Moving a piano from Miami to another state adds hours of road vibration, temperature changes, and multiple loading and unloading points. Piano movers use climate-appropriate scheduling, proper truck securing, and acclimation protocols.

## The Cost Difference

Yes, dedicated [**Piano Moving**](/piano-moving) service costs more than adding a piano to a general move. But the price gap is smaller than most people expect. A local piano move in the Miami area typically runs between $200 and $600 depending on the piano type, access difficulty, and distance. Adding a piano to a general moving quote might save you $100 to $200, but the savings disappear instantly if something goes wrong.
