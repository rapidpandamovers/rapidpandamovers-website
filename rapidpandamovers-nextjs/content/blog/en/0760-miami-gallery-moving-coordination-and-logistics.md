# Miami Gallery Moving: Coordination and Logistics

Miami's gallery scene runs at a pace most cities can't match. Between Art Basel in December, Untitled Art Fair, NADA, and a rotating calendar of shows across Wynwood, the Design District, and Little Haiti, galleries here are constantly installing, deinstalling, and relocating artwork. When an entire gallery needs to move to a new space, the coordination involved goes far beyond what a standard moving company can handle.

## Pre-Move Planning for Galleries

A gallery move starts weeks or months before the first piece comes off the wall. The planning phase involves:

![A Detailed Marble Sculpture In An Art Ga 4](/images/blog/2029/03/miami-gallery-moving-coordination-and-logistics/a-detailed-marble-sculpture-in-an-art-ga-4.webp)


**Exhibition scheduling**: Work backward from your next opening. Most gallery moves need at minimum two weeks for packing, transport, and reinstallation. If you have active exhibitions, coordinate with artists and lenders about removal timing and insurance handoffs.

**Inventory reconciliation**: Confirm exactly what's in the space. Galleries often have consigned work, pieces on loan, items in back storage, and work currently on display. Each category may have different handling requirements, ownership, and insurance arrangements.

**Building coordination**: Both the origin and destination buildings need to be prepped. Reserve freight elevators, confirm loading dock access, check hallway dimensions, and verify that the new space has adequate power and climate control running before artwork arrives.

## The Role of the Registrar

For galleries with a registrar or collections manager, this person becomes the quarterback of the move. They maintain the master inventory, track condition reports, coordinate insurance coverage, and serve as the point of contact between the gallery, the moving team, and any artists or lenders.

If your gallery doesn't have a registrar, designate someone on your team to fill that role for the duration of the move. Having a single person who knows where every piece is at every stage prevents confusion and loss.

## Packing a Gallery Space

Gallery packing follows a specific order. Start with the storage room and back-of-house pieces that aren't part of a current show. These can be packed and loaded days before the main move without disrupting operations.

![Discover Classic Art In An Elegantly Des 2](/images/blog/2029/03/miami-gallery-moving-coordination-and-logistics/discover-classic-art-in-an-elegantly-des-2.webp)


Current exhibition work comes down last, usually the day before or morning of the move. Each piece gets a condition report, wrapping in glassine and acid-free materials, and placement into a mirror box or custom crate depending on value and fragility.

Flat files, plan drawers, and print storage racks contain some of the most vulnerable items in the gallery. Works on paper need acid-free interleaving and rigid support boards. Pack these items carefully and label the outside of each container with a contents list.

## Transport Logistics in Miami

Miami traffic and building access create unique challenges. Schedule truck arrivals during off-peak hours when possible, especially for Wynwood galleries where street parking is limited and foot traffic is heavy on weekends. Loading dock reservations at both locations prevent delays.

Our trucks maintain climate control throughout the drive, which matters during Miami's long summer when exterior temperatures routinely exceed 90 degrees. For moves across town, the trip is short, but even twenty minutes in an un-cooled truck can introduce damaging heat and humidity.

For gallery moves involving multiple truckloads, stagger the schedule so the first load is fully unloaded before the second arrives. This prevents artwork from sitting on a loading dock or in a hallway.

## Installation at the New Space

Unpack artwork into a clean, climate-controlled space. Let pieces acclimate for 24 hours before hanging or placing them. Check each piece against its condition report and flag any concerns immediately.

![Woman Stands In A Modern London Art Gall 1](/images/blog/2029/03/miami-gallery-moving-coordination-and-logistics/woman-stands-in-a-modern-london-art-gall-1.webp)


Work with your installation team to plan wall placement before unwrapping. Moving a heavy framed piece from one wall to another after hanging wastes time and increases handling risk.
