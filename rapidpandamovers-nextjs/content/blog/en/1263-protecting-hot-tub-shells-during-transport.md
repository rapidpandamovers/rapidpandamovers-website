# Protecting Hot Tub Shells During Transport

An acrylic hot tub shell is surprisingly fragile for something that looks so sturdy. The glossy surface scratches easily, the corners chip on impact, and the shell can crack if stressed in the wrong direction during lifting or transport. Given that a replacement shell costs $1,000 to $3,000 or more, protecting it during a move is worth every extra minute of prep.

## Common Types of Transport Damage

Before you can prevent damage, it helps to understand how it happens:

![A Woman Enjoys A Relaxing Time In An Out 1](/images/blog/2029/12/protecting-hot-tub-shells-during-transport/a-woman-enjoys-a-relaxing-time-in-an-out-1.webp)


- **Surface scratches**: Caused by the tub sliding against rough surfaces, unpadded truck beds, or debris on the ground during loading.
- **Corner chips and cracks**: Acrylic corners are vulnerable to impact. Bumping a gate post, door frame, or the truck's side wall can chip the shell.
- **Stress cracks**: These happen when the tub flexes too much during lifting or sits unevenly on the truck. If one corner is supported and the rest hangs free, the shell can develop hairline cracks along stress points.
- **Cosmetic scuffs**: Straps or ropes tightened directly against the acrylic leave marks and can dull the finish.

In Miami's heat, acrylic becomes slightly more flexible, which can be a double-edged sword. The material is less likely to crack from impact but more prone to warping if stored or transported in a way that puts sustained pressure on one area.

## Wrapping the Shell

The first line of defense is heavy-duty moving blankets. These thick, padded blankets wrap around the entire exterior of the tub, covering the acrylic surface, cabinet panels, and corners.

Here's the wrapping process our [**Hot Tub Moving**](/hot-tub-moving) team follows:

1. **Lay blankets over the acrylic surface first.** Cover the entire interior of the shell with blankets draped across the seats, footwell, and rim. This protects the inside when the tub is tipped on its side.
2. **Wrap the exterior.** Drape blankets over the outside of the shell and cabinet, overlapping them to avoid gaps.
3. **Secure with stretch wrap.** Use industrial stretch wrap (not tape) to hold the blankets in place. Wrap the stretch film around the entire tub, going over and under to keep everything snug. Never apply tape directly to the acrylic surface.
4. **Add corner protection.** Foam corner guards or extra folded blankets on the four corners of the tub provide additional impact protection at the most vulnerable points.

## Securing the Tub on the Truck

How the tub sits on the truck matters as much as the wrapping. The tub should be:

![Shirtless Man Enjoying Relaxation In A H 3](/images/blog/2029/12/protecting-hot-tub-shells-during-transport/shirtless-man-enjoying-relaxation-in-a-h-3.webp)


- **Positioned on its side** on the truck bed, resting on the cabinet frame (the structural part), not on the acrylic shell.
- **Supported evenly** so the weight is distributed across the frame. No corner should be hanging off the edge of the truck bed.
- **Strapped with ratchet straps** through the cabinet frame or around the entire tub-and-blanket bundle. Straps should be tight enough to prevent shifting but not so tight that they compress the shell.
- **Padded where straps contact the tub.** Place blankets or foam between every strap and the tub's surface to prevent strap marks and pressure points.

On Miami roads, potholes and construction zones (which are everywhere) cause jolts and vibrations. A poorly secured tub will shift, slide, and bang against the truck bed with every bump.

## Protecting the Cover

The spa cover should always be removed and transported separately. Covers have rigid foam inserts that crack easily if bent, and the vinyl exterior scuffs quickly. Wrap the cover in a blanket or a dedicated cover bag and lay it flat in the truck, away from heavy items.

## Protecting the Cabinet

The exterior cabinet (usually made of synthetic wood, plastic panels, or actual wood) takes a beating from bumps and scrapes during the move. Cabinet panels can crack, dent, or come loose if hit during transport. The blanket wrap protects most of the cabinet, but pay extra attention to the bottom edges and corners that are most likely to contact the ground, dolly, or truck bed.

## After Arrival

Once the tub is placed at its new location, remove all wrapping carefully. Inspect the entire shell for any new scratches, chips, or cracks before the movers leave. Document any damage with photos immediately. If you find an issue, having it recorded while the moving crew is still on site makes the claims process much smoother.
