# How to Prepare Your Piano for Moving Day

Moving day goes smoother when the piano is ready before the crew arrives. There's a real difference between a piano that's been properly prepped and one that hasn't, both in terms of how long the move takes and how safe it is for the instrument. Our [**Piano Moving**](/piano-moving) teams in Miami handle the heavy lifting, but there are several things you can do ahead of time to protect your investment and speed up the process.

## One Week Before the Move

### Get the Piano's Current Condition on Record

![An Elderly Man Playing Piano In A Dark J 1](/images/blog/2028/03/how-to-prepare-your-piano-for-moving-day/an-elderly-man-playing-piano-in-a-dark-j-1.webp)


Take photos of your piano from every angle: top, sides, keyboard, legs, pedals, and any existing scratches or marks. These photos serve as proof of condition in case an insurance claim ever comes up. Store them on your phone and email a copy to yourself so the timestamp is verified.

### Clear the Room

Remove everything from the top of the piano. Sheet music, picture frames, vases, metronomes, whatever lives on your piano needs to be packed separately in boxes. Items left on the piano can fall off during wrapping and damage the instrument or themselves.

Clear a wide path from the piano to the front door (or whichever exit the movers will use). Move side tables, chairs, area rugs, and anything along the route. In many Miami homes, that means clearing the hallway, pulling up a runner rug, and moving the entry table.

### Measure Doorways and Tight Spots

Even though our crew does a pre-move assessment, it helps to measure doorways, hallways, and any turns between the piano and the exit. Share these measurements when you schedule the move. If a doorway is under 34 inches wide, we'll plan to remove the door from its hinges.

## Two to Three Days Before

### Close and Secure the Lid

Close the piano's main lid (the top panel on a grand, the top on an upright) and the keyboard fallboard. If either has a lock, lock it. If not, use painter's tape to hold them closed. Do not use regular packing tape or duct tape, which leave adhesive residue that damages the finish.

On a grand piano, lower the prop stick and make sure the lid sits flat. Some owners leave the lid propped open for weeks at a time, and the prop stick can get stuck.

### Disconnect Any Electronics

If your piano has a PianoDisc system, Silent Piano module, or any other electronic add-on, check the manufacturer's instructions for transport. Some systems have internal components that should be locked or secured. Unplug any power cables and coil them loosely with a twist tie.

### Handle the Piano Bench

Piano benches with storage compartments should be emptied. Pack the sheet music and accessories inside separately. The bench travels as its own item, wrapped in a moving blanket. If your bench has adjustable height, lower it to the most compact setting.

## The Morning Of

### Climate Prep for Miami Moves

![Artistic Close Up Of A Hand Playing Pian 2](/images/blog/2028/03/how-to-prepare-your-piano-for-moving-day/artistic-close-up-of-a-hand-playing-pian-2.webp)


If your move is scheduled during summer, close windows and run the AC the night before. You want the piano's interior wood to be as stable as possible before it goes outside. A dry, cool piano handles the brief outdoor exposure better than one that's already been sitting in a warm room.

### Protect Your Floors

Lay down floor runners or heavy cardboard from the piano to the exit door. Miami homes feature a lot of tile, marble, and polished concrete, and piano dollies and boards can scratch these surfaces. We bring our own floor protection, but having the path prepped saves time.

### Keep Pets and Kids Clear

A piano in motion is not safe to be around. The crew needs room to work and can't be watching for a curious dog or a small child underfoot. Keep pets in a closed room and kids with another adult during the move.

### Be Available for Decisions

Stay nearby on moving day. The crew may need to ask about placement at the destination, preferred exit routes, or how to handle an unexpected obstacle. Having you available for quick decisions keeps the move on schedule.

## What NOT to Do

- **Don't try to move the piano yourself before the movers arrive.** Even sliding it a few inches on a hardwood floor can scratch the floor and misalign the casters.
- **Don't wrap the piano yourself.** Our crew has specific wrapping techniques and materials. Well-intentioned wrapping with the wrong materials can cause more harm than good.
- **Don't remove grand piano legs yourself.** This requires specific tools and knowledge of the bolt pattern, and getting it wrong risks dropping the piano body.

## After the Movers Leave

Once the piano is in its new spot, don't play it right away. Give it two to three weeks to acclimate to the new environment before calling your tuner. In Miami, where each home has its own AC setup and humidity profile, this settling time matters.
