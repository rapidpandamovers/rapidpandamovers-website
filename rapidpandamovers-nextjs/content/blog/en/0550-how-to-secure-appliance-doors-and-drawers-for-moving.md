# How to Secure Appliance Doors and Drawers for Moving

An unsecured refrigerator door that swings open during transport can slam into a wall, bend its own hinges, and crack the door seal. A washer lid that flips up catches on doorframes and stairway ceilings. Oven drawers that slide out shift the center of gravity and make a heavy appliance dangerously unstable. We see the aftermath of these problems regularly during moves across Miami, and they're all preventable with about 15 minutes of prep per appliance.

## Why Unsecured Doors and Drawers Are a Problem

When a moving truck accelerates, brakes, or turns, everything inside shifts. A refrigerator door that's magnetically sealed might hold during normal use, but it won't hold when the truck hits a pothole on I-95 or takes a sharp turn off Biscayne Boulevard. The magnetic seal is designed for gentle, everyday use, not for the forces of transport.

Beyond the doors themselves, an open door changes the appliance's footprint. It sticks out, catches on walls and other items, and makes the appliance harder to maneuver through tight spaces. In Miami condos where the hallway from your unit to the freight elevator is barely wide enough for a fridge, a swinging door turns a manageable move into a scratched wall and a dented appliance.

## The Right Way to Secure Each Appliance

### Refrigerator Doors

For French-door refrigerators, wrap a ratchet strap around the entire unit horizontally, positioned at handle height. Tighten until both doors are held firmly against the frame. For single-door models, one strap is usually enough. If you're using tape instead of straps, use appliance-grade tape or painter's tape. Avoid duct tape on stainless steel, brushed metal, or painted finishes. The adhesive leaves residue that's hard to remove and can discolor the surface in Miami's heat.

If your fridge has a bottom freezer drawer, strap it separately or tape it closed at both ends. These drawers are heavy and slide out easily when the unit is tilted back on a dolly.

### Washer and Dryer Doors

Top-loading washer lids should be taped down with two strips of appliance tape forming an X pattern. Front-loading washer doors need a strap around the body of the machine or tape over the door handle to the chassis. Don't tape over the glass window on a front-loader since the adhesive is harder to clean off glass.

Dryer doors, both front-loading and top-loading, get the same treatment. For stackable washer-dryer combos, secure both doors independently. If the dryer sits on top, its door can swing open and hit the ceiling or the person carrying the unit.

### Oven and Range

Remove knobs before worrying about the door. Knobs catch on moving blankets and snap off. Tape the oven door shut with two strips of tape. If your oven has a storage drawer at the bottom, tape it closed or remove it entirely. A heavy oven drawer sliding open while someone is carrying the range on a dolly creates a dangerous shift in weight.

For double ovens, secure both doors. Upper oven doors on wall-mounted units sometimes need additional support since the hinges can loosen when the unit is detached from the wall.

### Dishwasher

The dishwasher door is the easiest to secure. One strip of tape from the front of the door to the top panel keeps it from dropping open. Inside, tape the upper and lower racks in their tracks so they don't roll forward. The silverware basket should be removed and packed separately.

### Small Appliance Doors and Lids

Microwave doors, air fryer baskets, stand mixer bowls, and slow cooker lids all need attention. Tape microwave doors shut. Remove air fryer baskets and pack them separately. Secure stand mixer bowls with tape or bubble wrap. Slow cooker lids should be wrapped and placed inside the pot for transport.

## Materials You'll Need

- **Appliance-grade tape**: Designed to hold firmly but peel off cleanly. Available at hardware stores and moving supply shops.
- **Painter's tape**: A decent alternative for delicate finishes. Not as strong, so use more strips.
- **Ratchet straps**: Best for heavy doors like refrigerator French doors and bottom freezer drawers. Get 1-inch straps for appliances.
- **Bungee cords**: A quick option for washer and dryer doors, but not as reliable as ratchet straps since they can slip.
- **Stretch wrap**: Wrapping the entire appliance in stretch wrap secures doors and also protects the exterior finish. Works well for short moves around Miami where appliances are on the truck for less than an hour.

## Common Mistakes

- **Using duct tape on finished surfaces.** The adhesive bakes onto surfaces in the heat of a moving truck. Stick with appliance tape.
- **Only taping the center of a door.** Doors flex. Tape them at the top and bottom edges too.
- **Leaving removable parts inside.** Fridge drawers, oven racks, and dishwasher baskets should be removed and packed, not just taped in place.
- **Forgetting the ice maker arm.** That thin wire arm inside the freezer snaps off easily. Tape it in the up (off) position.
