Moving into a new home in Miami is exciting, but those first few days are critical for setting yourself up for success. Whether you're settling into a Brickell high-rise, a Coral Gables family home, or a cozy Coconut Grove bungalow, these five tasks should be at the top of your to-do list.

## 1. Change All the Locks

You don't know who has keys from before. Previous owners, their family members, contractors, or old neighbors might still have copies. Change all exterior locks immediately. In Miami's urban neighborhoods like Wynwood and Edgewater, smart locks with keypad entry are popular for added convenience and security.

## 2. Test All Appliances

Run the dishwasher, check the stove, test the washer/dryer. In South Florida's humidity, also check your AC is cooling properly and inspect for any musty smells that could indicate mold. Identify problems while warranty claims are still fresh. Most home warranties have a 30-60 day window for reporting existing issues.

## 3. Locate Emergency Shutoffs

Find and label your water main, electrical panel, and gas shutoff (if applicable). In Miami, also locate your hurricane shutters or impact window documentation. Homes in flood-prone areas like Pinecrest or parts of Kendall should have a clear understanding of where to shut off power before a major storm. You'll need to know these locations eventually, so better to find them now than during an emergency.

## 4. Set Up Mail Forwarding

Visit USPS.com to ensure nothing gets lost during the transition period. This should happen before you move. If you're in a gated community in Doral or Aventura, also register with your HOA and provide gate codes to expected visitors and delivery services. Many Miami Beach condos require separate mail registration with the building management.

## 5. Transfer Utilities to Your Name

Contact FPL for electricity, Miami-Dade Water and Sewer for water service, and your preferred internet provider. Most Miami neighborhoods are served by AT&T, Xfinity, or local fiber options. Confirm all accounts are active and in your name before move-in day. Nothing is worse than arriving to a hot house with no AC in the Florida heat.

## Benefits of Completing These Tasks First

Knocking out these five priorities in your first 48 hours pays off immediately:

- **Peace of mind**: Knowing your home is secure and functional
- **Problem detection**: Catching issues while warranties and seller disclosures are fresh
- **Smooth unpacking**: No interruptions from utility surprises or mail confusion
- **Emergency preparedness**: Ready for Florida's unpredictable weather from day one

## What to Expect Your First Week

The first week in your new Miami home sets the tone for your entire experience. With these five tasks handled, you can focus on unpacking room by room, exploring your new neighborhood, and meeting your neighbors. Most families find that completing critical tasks early leaves weekends free for discovering local parks, restaurants, and community events.
