Every moving company has stories. After years of helping Miami families relocate, we've accumulated some memorable requests. Here are eight that made us pause and then figure out how to make it happen.

## 1. A Life-Sized Dinosaur Statue

A Coral Gables collector had a 12-foot T-Rex replica in his backyard. The fiberglass creature weighed over 800 pounds and wouldn't fit through any gate. We ended up using a crane to lift it over the fence. Now it guards a property in Pinecrest.

![Close Up Of A Humorous Ufo Parking Sign  4](/images/blog/2031/07/8-strangest-moving-requests-weve-received/close-up-of-a-humorous-ufo-parking-sign--4.webp)


## 2. An Entire Backyard Tiki Bar

Not just the bar, but the bamboo roof, the carved wooden posts, the neon signs, and even the sand pit around it. A Kendall homeowner wanted the whole tropical setup moved to his new place in Key Biscayne. Disassembly took a full day.

## 3. A Vintage Neon Sign Collection

Seventy-three fragile neon signs, each requiring custom padding and careful handling. The collector from Wynwood insisted we photograph each one before and after. Every sign survived the move to his new warehouse in Hialeah.

## 4. A Complete Home Recording Studio

Soundproofing panels, a mixing console the size of a dining table, vintage microphones, and racks of equipment. A Miami Beach musician needed everything moved without damaging the acoustic treatment. The reassembly took longer than the move itself.

![Creepy Portrait Of A Young Man Biting In 3](/images/blog/2031/07/8-strangest-moving-requests-weve-received/creepy-portrait-of-a-young-man-biting-in-3.webp)


## 5. A Taxidermy Zoo

One Coconut Grove estate had bears, lions, a giraffe head, and about forty other taxidermy pieces. Each required careful wrapping to protect ears, antlers, and fur. The new owner in Palmetto Bay now has the most interesting conversation starters in the county.

## 6. A Working Jukebox Collection

Fifteen vintage jukeboxes from the 1950s and 60s, each filled with original vinyl records. Moving them required keeping them upright while protecting the delicate mechanisms inside. An Aventura collector now hosts the best parties in his neighborhood.

## 7. A Championship-Caliber Air Hockey Table

This wasn't a standard home game table. The tournament-regulation table weighed over 400 pounds and required perfectly level transport to keep the air system intact. It now lives in a Doral game room.

![Person In A Ghost Costume With Sunglasse 1](/images/blog/2031/07/8-strangest-moving-requests-weve-received/person-in-a-ghost-costume-with-sunglasse-1.webp)


## 8. A Fortune Teller Booth

A complete carnival fortune teller machine, complete with the animatronic figure inside. The antique Zoltar-style booth came from a Brickell penthouse and moved to a restaurant in South Beach. Yes, it still works.

Strange items don't scare us. If you've got something unusual that needs moving, chances are we've handled something similar. Our crews know how to protect the weird, the valuable, and the irreplaceable.
