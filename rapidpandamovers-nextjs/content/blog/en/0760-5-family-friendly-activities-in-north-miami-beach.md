Your family just moved to North Miami Beach. The boxes are unpacked (mostly), and now the kids are asking the question every parent dreads: "What are we going to do today?"

Good news. North Miami Beach sits at the sweet spot between touristy Miami Beach and the quieter suburbs. You get beaches without the South Beach crowds, parks with actual parking, and museums that your kids will actually want to visit twice.

Here are five family activities that locals return to again and again.

## 1. Haulover Beach Park

**Address:** 10800 Collins Ave, Miami Beach, FL 33154

![A Family Enjoys A Fun Day At A Sandy Bea 1](/images/blog/2027/07/5-family-friendly-activities-in-north-miami-beach/a-family-enjoys-a-fun-day-at-a-sandy-bea-1.webp)


Haulover Beach Park stretches for nearly a mile along the Atlantic, and the family-friendly section (north end, near the kite field) has lifeguards on duty year-round. The 7,500-square-foot Bill Graham Skate Park keeps older kids busy for hours with its pump track and bowl. Pack a cooler and grab a picnic table under the sea grape trees.

**Pro tip:** Arrive before 10am on weekends. Parking fills up by noon, and the $7 parking fee is per vehicle, not per person, making this one of the best beach values in Miami-Dade.

## 2. Oleta River State Park

**Address:** 3400 NE 163rd St, North Miami Beach, FL 33160

![A Family Having Fun Splashing Water Near 5](/images/blog/2027/07/5-family-friendly-activities-in-north-miami-beach/a-family-having-fun-splashing-water-near-5.webp)


At 1,043 acres, Oleta River State Park is Florida's largest urban park, and it feels worlds away from the city. The park's bay-side beach has calm, shallow water perfect for kids who are still getting comfortable with waves. Rent kayaks or paddleboards from the concession stand and explore the mangrove trails together.

The park also offers rustic cabins if your family wants to try camping without going too far from home. Book early because these fill up months ahead, especially during spring break and holiday weekends.

**Best for:** Families with kids who love nature, biking, or water sports. The mountain bike trails range from beginner to advanced.

## 3. Phillip and Patricia Frost Museum of Science

**Address:** 1101 Biscayne Blvd, Miami, FL 33132

The Frost Museum sits about 20 minutes south in downtown Miami's Museum Park, and it's worth the drive. The 500,000-gallon Gulf Stream Aquarium lets you watch sharks and sea turtles from above, below, and through a walk-through tunnel. The planetarium runs shows throughout the day (the space visualizations beat anything you'll find on YouTube).

**Money-saving tip:** Miami-Dade residents get discounted admission. Bring a utility bill and ID. Annual memberships pay for themselves after two visits if you have multiple kids.

## 4. Miami Children's Museum

**Address:** 980 MacArthur Causeway, Miami, FL 33132

![A Mother And Daughter Enjoy Quality Time 4](/images/blog/2027/07/5-family-friendly-activities-in-north-miami-beach/a-mother-and-daughter-enjoy-quality-time-4.webp)


This museum was built for hands-on play. Kids can captain a cruise ship, run a supermarket, build with blocks bigger than they are, and record music in a real studio. The "Me, Myself, and I" exhibit helps younger children learn about their bodies, while the Bank of America exhibit teaches older kids about money and careers.

The museum hosts Baby STEAM sessions for ages 1-3 on weekday mornings, combining sensory play with early learning concepts. Check their calendar for special family fun days, which often include free activities and performances.

**Getting there:** The museum is on Watson Island between Miami and Miami Beach. Take I-195 and look for the colorful building on the north side of the causeway.

## 5. Surfside Beach

**Address:** Collins Ave at 93rd St, Surfside, FL

![A Young Boy Plays On The Beach With A To 2](/images/blog/2027/07/5-family-friendly-activities-in-north-miami-beach/a-young-boy-plays-on-the-beach-with-a-to-2.webp)


Surfside sits just south of North Miami Beach and offers a quieter beach experience than its neighbors. The town maintains a family-friendly atmosphere with clean sand, attentive lifeguards, and actual public restrooms (a rarity in South Florida beach towns).

The gentle waves make this spot ideal for kids learning to boogie board or swim. Walk north on the beach path and you'll hit the shops and restaurants along Harding Avenue, where Carrot Express and Cafe Ragazzi offer kid-friendly menus.

**Weather note:** Summer afternoons bring predictable thunderstorms between 3-5pm. Plan your beach time for the morning and head to an indoor activity after lunch.
