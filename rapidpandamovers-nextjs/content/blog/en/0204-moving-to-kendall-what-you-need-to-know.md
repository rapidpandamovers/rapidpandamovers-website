Kendall continues to attract new residents from across the country during August, and it is easy to see why. Located in southwestern Miami-Dade County, Kendall combines suburban comfort with easy access to everything Miami has to offer.

## Why Choose Kendall?

Kendall is one of Miami-Dade County's most popular neighborhoods for families and professionals. The area offers more affordable housing than Coral Gables or Coconut Grove while still providing excellent schools, shopping, and dining.

![A Cheerful Family Takes A Selfie While S 4](/images/blog/2025/08/moving-to-kendall-what-you-need-to-know/a-cheerful-family-takes-a-selfie-while-s-4.webp)


The area attracts families, professionals, and retirees alike. With Dadeland Mall nearby, Baptist Hospital of Miami within minutes, and Florida International University just up the road, residents have everything they need close to home.

### Location and Accessibility

Kendall sits right off the Palmetto Expressway (SR 826) and US-1, making commutes to Downtown Miami, Brickell, or Miami Beach straightforward. The Dadeland Metrorail stations connect you to the rest of Miami-Dade without fighting traffic. Miami International Airport is about 20 minutes north depending on traffic.

### Community and Lifestyle

Kendall residents enjoy a strong sense of community. Dadeland Mall, The Falls shopping center, and the Kendall Village Center offer shopping and dining. Parks like A.D. Barnes Park and Tropical Park provide green space for families. The weekly farmers market at Pinecrest Gardens draws crowds year-round.

## Neighborhoods to Consider

When planning your move to Kendall, The Crossings area is popular with newcomers for its family-friendly streets and good schools. Each neighborhood has its own feel. Some offer walkable dining and shopping, while others provide quiet, tree-lined streets.

### Top Kendall Neighborhoods

Here are some areas to explore:

- **The Crossings**: Established community with good schools like G. Holmes Braddock High
- **Kendall Lakes**: More affordable single-family homes near Tamiami Trail
- **Three Lakes**: Newer development with larger lots and gated communities
- **Sunset**: Close to Dadeland Mall with easy Metrorail access
- **West Kendall**: Growing area near the Turnpike with newer construction

## Moving to Kendall in August

August is a busy month for moves as families settle in before school starts. The summer weather in South Florida means hot and humid conditions with daily afternoon thunderstorms.

![A Joyful Family Sitting Together With Mo 1](/images/blog/2025/08/moving-to-kendall-what-you-need-to-know/a-joyful-family-sitting-together-with-mo-1.webp)


### Timing Your Move

When planning your relocation, consider:

- **Best moving days**: Weekdays typically offer better availability and rates
- **Weather**: Schedule your move for morning hours before the afternoon storms roll in
- **School calendar**: Miami-Dade schools start in mid-August, so book movers early
- **Traffic**: Avoid moving during rush hour on US-1 or the Palmetto, which gets backed up around 5 PM

## Essential Services to Locate

As a new Kendall resident, you will want to find:

- **Healthcare**: Baptist Hospital of Miami, Kendall Regional Medical Center, and South Miami Hospital are nearby
- **Schools**: Sunset Elementary, Kenwood K-8, and G. Holmes Braddock Senior High are well-regarded public options
- **Grocery**: Publix locations throughout Kendall, plus Whole Foods at Dadeland and Sedanos for Latin groceries
- **Recreation**: A.D. Barnes Park has tennis courts and a pool, while Tropical Park hosts sports leagues and events
