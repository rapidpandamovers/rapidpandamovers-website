Welcome to your September guide for moving to Golden Beach! Whether you're relocating from Aventura down the road or making a bigger move from Coral Gables this fall, getting to know your new community will help you settle in quickly.

## Why Choose Golden Beach?

Golden Beach stands out as one of Miami-Dade County's most exclusive addresses. This tiny municipality of just 0.5 square miles is home to about 900 residents who enjoy oceanfront estates, gated privacy, and a police force that maintains 24-hour patrol.

![A Beautifully Arranged Dining Table With 4](/images/blog/2026/05/your-golden-beach-moving-handbook/a-beautifully-arranged-dining-table-with-4.webp)


The area attracts families, professionals, and retirees alike, thanks to its quality of life, convenient access to major employment centers, and excellent amenities.

### Location and Accessibility

Golden Beach sits between Sunny Isles Beach to the south and Hallandale Beach to the north, with A1A (Collins Avenue) running through town. You're about 20 minutes from Aventura Mall and its dining scene, 30 minutes from Brickell's financial district, and Fort Lauderdale-Hollywood International Airport is just 15 minutes away. Bal Harbour Shops is a quick 10-minute drive south for high-end retail.

### Community and Lifestyle

Golden Beach residents enjoy an intimate small-town atmosphere within a major metro area. The Golden Beach Recreation Center hosts holiday parties, summer camps, and fitness programs. Unlike busier areas like Miami Beach or Sunny Isles, there are no commercial businesses here - it's purely residential, which many families find appealing.

## Neighborhoods to Consider

Golden Beach is essentially one neighborhood - a single street of oceanfront and Intracoastal properties. Most homes sit on Ocean Boulevard, with estates ranging from $5 million to over $20 million. If you're considering nearby alternatives, Sunny Isles Beach offers more condo options, while Aventura provides master-planned community living.

### Choosing Your Perfect Spot

Consider these factors:

- **Proximity to work**: Downtown Brickell is 30 minutes; Doral business parks are about 35 minutes
- **Schools**: Golden Beach has no public schools - kids attend Norman S. Edelcup/Sunny Isles Beach K-8 or private schools in Aventura
- **Property types**: Single-family estates only - no condos or apartments in Golden Beach proper
- **Privacy level**: This is one of Miami-Dade's most private communities with strict access controls

## Moving to Golden Beach in September

September marks the tail end of hurricane season, so keep an eye on weather forecasts. The fall weather in South Florida brings slightly lower temperatures and humidity levels compared to July and August, making physical moving tasks more comfortable.

![A Person Relaxes On A Beach Under A Stra 1](/images/blog/2026/05/your-golden-beach-moving-handbook/a-person-relaxes-on-a-beach-under-a-stra-1.webp)


### Timing Your Move

When planning your relocation, consider:

- **Best moving days**: Mid-week moves (Tuesday-Thursday) often have better availability
- **Weather considerations**: September averages 88°F with afternoon thunderstorms - morning moves work best
- **Town requirements**: Golden Beach requires advance notice for moving trucks and may have time restrictions on large vehicle access
- **Gate access**: Coordinate with town security ahead of time for smooth entry

## Essential Services to Locate

As a new Golden Beach resident, you'll want to find:

- **Healthcare**: Memorial Regional Hospital in Hollywood (15 min), Aventura Hospital (10 min), Mount Sinai in Miami Beach (25 min)
- **Schools**: Norman S. Edelcup K-8 in Sunny Isles, private options include David Posnack JCC and American Heritage in Plantation
- **Shopping**: Publix at Diplomat Mall, Aventura Mall (20 min), Bal Harbour Shops (10 min), Whole Foods in Hallandale
- **Dining**: Head to Sunny Isles for casual spots or Aventura for upscale dining at Bourbon Steak or Makoto
