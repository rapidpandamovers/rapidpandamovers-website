Just moved into your new Miami home? The first week is all about getting settled and making sure everything works. Here are six priorities that will help you feel at home faster and avoid headaches down the road.

## 1. Change All the Locks

You don't know who has keys from the previous owners or tenants. In busy neighborhoods like Brickell, Coconut Grove, or Little Havana where buildings often have multiple units, this is especially important. Change locks right away for security and peace of mind. Many local locksmiths offer same-day service throughout Miami-Dade County.

![Two Movers Carrying Boxes In A Sunlit Ro 3](/images/blog/2031/01/6-first-week-priorities-in-your-new-home/two-movers-carrying-boxes-in-a-sunlit-ro-3.webp)


## 2. Test All Appliances

Run the dishwasher, check the stove, test the washer/dryer, and make sure the AC is working properly. In South Florida's heat and humidity, a functioning air conditioner isn't optional. Identify problems while warranty claims are still fresh and before the summer months when HVAC repair schedules fill up.

## 3. Locate Emergency Shutoffs

Find and label your water main, electrical panel, and gas shutoff (if applicable). During hurricane season, you'll need to know where these are. If you're in a flood-prone area like parts of Miami Beach or Sweetwater, knowing your water shutoff location can save you from serious damage.

## 4. Set Up Mail Forwarding

Visit USPS.com to make sure nothing gets lost during the transition period. This should happen before you move. If you're moving within Miami, your mail should redirect within a few days.

![Young Woman Moving Boxes Out Of An Apart 1](/images/blog/2031/01/6-first-week-priorities-in-your-new-home/young-woman-moving-boxes-out-of-an-apart-1.webp)


## 5. Transfer Utilities to Your Name

FPL (Florida Power & Light) for electricity, Miami-Dade Water and Sewer, and your internet provider - confirm all accounts are active and in your name before move-in day. FPL often has wait times for new service, so schedule this at least a week ahead.

## 6. Schedule Critical Installations

Internet, cable, and security systems need to be booked early. AT&T, Xfinity, and local providers often have week-long wait times in popular Miami neighborhoods. If you're in a high-rise in Downtown or Edgewater, check with building management about approved vendors first.
