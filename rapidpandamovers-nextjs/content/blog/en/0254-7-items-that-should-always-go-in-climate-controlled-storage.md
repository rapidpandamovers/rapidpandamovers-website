# 7 Items That Should Always Go in Climate-Controlled Storage

Standard storage units in South Florida are just metal boxes that bake in the sun. Inside temperatures can swing from 60 degrees on a January night to 120 degrees on an August afternoon, with humidity rarely dropping below 65%. That environment destroys certain items faster than most people expect. Here are seven categories that always belong in climate-controlled storage.

## 1. Wood Furniture

Solid wood responds to humidity and temperature changes by expanding and contracting. In Miami's climate, this cycle happens constantly. Over weeks and months, dining tables develop cracks along the grain. Drawer joints loosen. Veneer lifts and bubbles as the adhesive underneath softens in the heat. Antique pieces are especially vulnerable because older finishes and glues weren't designed for tropical conditions. Climate control keeps conditions stable enough that wood maintains its structure.

## 2. Leather Goods

Leather sofas, chairs, jackets, handbags, and shoes absorb moisture from humid air. Within weeks in a standard Miami storage unit, leather surfaces develop mildew spots that are difficult to remove without professional treatment. Extended exposure causes the leather to dry out, crack, and lose its suppleness permanently. A climate-controlled unit with humidity below 55% prevents both mold growth and drying damage.

## 3. Electronics and Appliances

Computers, televisions, gaming consoles, audio equipment, and small kitchen appliances contain circuit boards and wiring that corrode when exposed to moisture. Condensation forms on internal components during temperature swings, and that moisture causes short circuits and oxidation. Storing electronics in a standard unit during a Miami summer is essentially guaranteeing they won't work when you retrieve them.

## 4. Photographs and Documents

Paper absorbs humidity rapidly. Photo prints stick together, ink bleeds on documents, and mold grows on paper surfaces within weeks of humid storage. Family photo albums, birth certificates, financial records, and any irreplaceable paper items need temperature and humidity control. Even in climate-controlled conditions, store these in sealed, acid-free containers with silica gel packets for extra protection.

## 5. Musical Instruments

Pianos, guitars, violins, brass instruments, and drums are built from materials that react strongly to environmental changes. Piano soundboards crack in dry heat. Guitar necks warp in humidity. Brass develops tarnish and corrosion. Wood instruments lose their tuning stability when stored in fluctuating conditions. Professional musicians in Miami know this, which is why they invest in climate-controlled storage whenever instruments need to be stored between moves or during renovations.

## 6. Artwork and Collectibles

Canvas paintings stretch and sag in humidity. Framed prints develop foxing (brown spots) on the paper. Sculptures made from organic materials can warp or deteriorate. Wine collections stored without temperature control lose their character within months. Sports memorabilia, comic books, vinyl record collections, and stamps all require stable conditions to maintain their value and condition.

## 7. Mattresses and Upholstered Furniture

Mattresses and padded furniture act like sponges in humid environments. Moisture gets absorbed into foam, batting, and padding where you can't see it, creating conditions for mold growth deep inside the item. By the time you notice a musty smell, the damage is extensive. In Miami, where mold grows aggressively in warm, damp conditions, climate-controlled storage is the only responsible choice for any upholstered piece you plan to use again.
