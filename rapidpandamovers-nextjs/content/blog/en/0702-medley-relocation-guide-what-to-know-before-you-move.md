Medley is unlike any other town in Miami-Dade County. With a residential population of fewer than 1,000 people spread across roughly 9 square miles, it functions primarily as an industrial and commercial hub rather than a traditional residential community. Before you move here, you need to understand what makes this location work.

## Understanding Medley's Unique Character

Medley exists as Florida's largest industrial municipality by land area. More than 2,500 businesses operate here, employing over 60,000 workers who commute in daily. The town generates significant tax revenue from commercial properties, which means no residential property taxes for the handful of residents who live here.

![Svc A Delivery Truck Driving Along A Coastal 1](/images/blog/2028/10/medley-relocation-guide-what-to-know-before-you-move/svc-a-delivery-truck-driving-along-a-coastal-1.webp)


The residential areas cluster mainly in the southeastern section of town, near NW 74th Street. Housing options are limited, primarily consisting of older single-family homes and a few small apartment complexes.

## Location and Commute Times

Medley's location offers excellent highway access:

- **Palmetto Expressway (SR 826)**: Runs directly through Medley
- **Florida's Turnpike**: Access points within 5 minutes
- **I-75**: 10 minutes north via the Turnpike
- **Miami International Airport**: 15 minutes south

Commute times to major employment centers:
- Downtown Miami: 25-30 minutes (no rush hour), 45-60 minutes (peak)
- Doral: 10 minutes
- Hialeah: 10 minutes
- Miami Lakes: 8 minutes
- Coral Gables: 25 minutes

## Housing Realities

If you're considering Medley for its low taxes, know that housing inventory is extremely limited. Most people who work in Medley live in neighboring communities:

**Doral** (5 minutes east): Modern master-planned community with excellent schools, shopping at CityPlace Doral and Miami International Mall, and rental apartments averaging $2,200-3,500 per month.

**Hialeah** (10 minutes south): More affordable housing options, strong Cuban-American community, access to Westland Mall and numerous local restaurants.

**Miami Lakes** (8 minutes north): Upscale planned community with tree-lined streets, Main Street shopping district, and good schools including Miami Lakes K-8 Center.

## What You'll Find in Medley

**Shopping and Dining**: Limited options within Medley proper. Most residents shop at the nearby Dolphin Mall (5 minutes) or CityPlace Doral. For groceries, the closest Publix locations are in Doral and Hialeah.

**Healthcare**: Palmetto General Hospital in Hialeah (10 minutes) and Kendall Regional Medical Center (20 minutes) serve the area.

**Schools**: Medley has no public schools. Students attend schools in the Miami-Dade County Public Schools system, typically zoned for Hialeah or Doral schools depending on their address.

**Parks and Recreation**: Medley Park on NW 106th Street offers a playground, basketball courts, and picnic areas. For more recreational options, head to Amelia Earhart Park in Hialeah (10 minutes) or Miami Lakes Optimist Park (15 minutes).

## What to Expect When Moving to Medley

**First month:** The industrial atmosphere may surprise you. Truck traffic is constant during business hours, and commercial activity defines the area. Most residents adjust quickly once they establish routines with nearby communities for shopping and dining.

![Svc Red Ford F 150 Truck Driving On A Bridge 2](/images/blog/2028/10/medley-relocation-guide-what-to-know-before-you-move/svc-red-ford-f-150-truck-driving-on-a-bridge-2.webp)


**Commute patterns:** You'll develop familiarity with Palmetto Expressway exits and learn which times avoid congestion. The lack of local traffic lights in residential areas makes in-and-out driving efficient.

**Community life:** Social connections happen through Doral, Hialeah, and Miami Lakes rather than within Medley itself. Churches, gyms, and community groups in neighboring towns become your local network.

## Benefits of Medley Living

- **No residential property taxes** from the town itself
- **Excellent highway access** for commuters working throughout Miami-Dade
- **Short commutes** for workers at Medley-based businesses
- **Proximity to Dolphin Mall and CityPlace Doral** for retail and dining
- **Quieter residential pockets** away from commercial zones
- **Lower overall cost of living** compared to nearby master-planned communities

## Who Should Move to Medley?

This location makes sense for:

- Business owners who want to live near their Medley warehouse or distribution center
- Workers seeking extremely short commutes to Medley-based employers
- Buyers who specifically want to avoid residential property taxes
- Investors looking for commercial or industrial property

If you're seeking a traditional residential neighborhood with schools, parks, and walkable amenities, consider Doral, Miami Lakes, or Hialeah instead.
