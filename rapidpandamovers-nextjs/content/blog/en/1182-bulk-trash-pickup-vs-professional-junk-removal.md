# Bulk Trash Pickup vs Professional Junk Removal

Miami-Dade County offers a free bulk trash pickup service for residents, which sounds great until you need something gone on a specific timeline. If you're preparing for a move, a home sale, or just want your property cleared, understanding the differences between county bulk pickup and professional junk removal helps you choose the right option for your situation.

## How Miami-Dade Bulk Trash Pickup Works

Miami-Dade County provides bulk waste collection for unincorporated areas, while individual cities (Miami, Hialeah, Coral Gables, Doral, etc.) run their own schedules. Here's the general framework:

![Interior Of An Abandoned Building Clutte 3](/images/blog/2029/06/bulk-trash-pickup-vs-professional-junk-removal/interior-of-an-abandoned-building-clutte-3.webp)


- **Scheduling:** You typically need to call 311 or submit a request online to schedule a pickup. Some neighborhoods have set bulk pickup days (often once a week or biweekly), while others require individual appointment scheduling.
- **Placement:** Items must be placed at the curb by 6 AM on your scheduled day. They can't block sidewalks, fire hydrants, or storm drains.
- **Size and weight limits:** Items should be manageable for the collection crew. Extremely heavy or oversized items may be refused.
- **What's accepted:** Furniture, mattresses, appliances (with Freon removed), and general household items. Yard waste has separate collection rules.
- **What's not accepted:** Hazardous waste, construction debris, tires, auto parts, and electronics in some jurisdictions.

The service is free, which is its biggest advantage. The catch is the timing, restrictions, and the fact that you're responsible for getting everything to the curb.

## The Limitations of Bulk Pickup

**Timing is the biggest issue.** If your bulk pickup day is Thursday and you need items gone by Tuesday for a home showing or a moving truck arrival, you're out of luck. Some areas only offer bulk pickup twice a month, which means a multi-week wait.

**Volume limits apply.** Most jurisdictions limit the amount of material you can put out per pickup. If you're clearing a whole house or garage, it might take three or four pickup cycles to get through everything.

**You do all the labor.** Getting a couch, a refrigerator, or a stack of old furniture from inside your house to the curb is hard physical work, especially in Miami's heat. There's no crew coming to your door. If it's not at the curb, it doesn't get picked up.

**Curb appeal suffers.** Piles of junk sitting at your curb for days aren't a great look, particularly if you're selling your home. HOAs in communities across Kendall, Doral, and Pembroke Pines may fine you for having items at the curb outside of approved windows.

**Items can be picked through.** Once items are at the curb, scavengers may rummage through them, leaving a mess of scattered debris. In some Miami neighborhoods, this is a regular occurrence.

## When to Use Each Service

**Use bulk trash pickup when:**
- You have a few items and aren't in a rush
- Items are already near the curb or easy to move there
- Your pickup schedule aligns with your timeline
- Budget is your primary concern

**Use professional junk removal when:**
- You need items gone by a specific date
- Items are inside the home, upstairs, or in hard-to-reach areas
- You have a large volume that exceeds bulk pickup limits
- You want donation and recycling handled for you
- You're selling your home and curb appearance matters
