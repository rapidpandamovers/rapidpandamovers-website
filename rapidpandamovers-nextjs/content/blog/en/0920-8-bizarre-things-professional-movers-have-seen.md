Professional movers see inside hundreds of homes every year, and sometimes what's behind those doors is truly unexpected. Here are some of the most bizarre things our crews and colleagues in the industry have encountered on the job.

## 1. The Room Full of Mannequins

One crew opened a spare bedroom door to find over 50 vintage mannequins, all dressed in period clothing and positioned as if having a party. The client collected them for decades, and each one needed careful individual wrapping.

![A Person With A Pumpkin Head Stands In A 1](/images/blog/2030/03/8-bizarre-things-professional-movers-have-seen/a-person-with-a-pumpkin-head-stands-in-a-1.webp)


## 2. A Homemade Submarine

In a Homestead garage (of course it was Florida), movers discovered a functional two-person submarine. The owner built it from scratch and insisted it came with them to the new house. It took a crane and a flatbed to make it happen.

## 3. The Indoor Treehouse

A Coral Gables client had a full-sized treehouse built inside their living room, complete with a real tree trunk running through the ceiling. Moving day involved carefully dismantling it and replanting the tree in their new Pinecrest backyard.

## 4. Collections Gone Wild

We've seen 3,000+ snow globes, an entire room of taxidermied animals, 500 ventriloquist dummies, and one person's collection of airplane sick bags from every airline they'd ever flown.

![Person In A Ghost Costume With Sunglasse 2](/images/blog/2030/03/8-bizarre-things-professional-movers-have-seen/person-in-a-ghost-costume-with-sunglasse-2.webp)


## 5. Hidden Rooms

Secret rooms behind bookcases in Brickell penthouses, panic rooms in Miami Beach mansions, and one Wynwood house with a hidden basement that the new owners didn't know existed. Movers found it while measuring for furniture placement.

## 6. Exotic Animals

From pet alligators in Kendall to rooms full of rare birds in Coconut Grove to someone's personal "insectarium" with thousands of live bugs in a Doral warehouse conversion, South Florida homes have surprised us more than once.

## 7. Time Capsule Houses

Some homes haven't been touched since the 1970s: shag carpet, avocado appliances, and all original furniture still in plastic wrap. It's like stepping into a museum.

![Two People In Ghost Costumes Sitting On  4](/images/blog/2030/03/8-bizarre-things-professional-movers-have-seen/two-people-in-ghost-costumes-sitting-on--4.webp)


## 8. The Walk-In Safe

One Aventura client had a bank vault door installed in their high-rise condo leading to a room-sized safe. What was inside? Vintage comic books worth millions.
