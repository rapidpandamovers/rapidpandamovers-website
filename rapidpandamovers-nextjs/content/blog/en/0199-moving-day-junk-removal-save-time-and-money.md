# Moving Day Junk Removal: Save Time and Money

You've packed the boxes, the truck is booked, and moving day is tomorrow. But there's still a pile of stuff in the garage that isn't going with you, a broken recliner in the living room, and a stack of old mattresses that somehow multiplied. Trying to deal with junk on the same day you're moving is stressful, but with the right plan, it doesn't have to wreck your timeline or your budget.

## The Real Cost of Moving Things You Don't Need

Every item on the truck costs you money. Most Miami moving companies charge by the hour, and the more stuff that needs loading, transporting, and unloading, the longer the job takes. A truck full of things headed straight for the trash at your new place might cost you an extra $200 to $400 in labor and fuel.

![A Pile Of Corroded And Wrecked Cars Stac 2](/images/blog/2025/07/moving-day-junk-removal-save-time-and-money/a-pile-of-corroded-and-wrecked-cars-stac-2.webp)


Then there's the truck space itself. If your junk pushes you from a 20-foot truck to a 26-foot truck, that's another $50 to $100 for the day. It adds up fast, and you end up paying to move things you're going to throw away anyway.

## Schedule Junk Removal Before the Movers Arrive

The smartest play is to have your [**Junk Removal**](/junk-removal) crew come the day before or the morning of your move, a few hours ahead of your movers. This way, by the time the moving truck pulls up, every item left in the house is something that's actually going to your new place.

We've seen plenty of Miami customers save themselves a full hour on their move just by clearing out the junk first. That's a direct savings on hourly moving rates, plus a smoother experience for everyone.

## What to Remove Before Moving Day

Walk through each room and tag anything that won't be making the trip. Common items people leave behind include:

![Aerial Shot Showcasing Diverse Scrap Met 4](/images/blog/2025/07/moving-day-junk-removal-save-time-and-money/aerial-shot-showcasing-diverse-scrap-met-4.webp)


- Old mattresses and box springs (especially if you bought new ones for the new place)
- Broken or worn-out furniture that isn't worth reupholstering
- Outdated electronics like bulky TVs, old desktop computers, and printers
- Exercise equipment that's become a clothes rack
- Boxes from the last move that you never unpacked
- Patio furniture that's rusted or sun-damaged from years of Miami weather
