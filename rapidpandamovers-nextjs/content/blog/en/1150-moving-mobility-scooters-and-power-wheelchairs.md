# Moving Mobility Scooters and Power Wheelchairs

For anyone who depends on a mobility scooter or power wheelchair, having that equipment ready and working at the new home is a top priority. It's not optional gear. It's how they get around. South Florida has a large population of mobility device users, and we handle these moves with the understanding that the device needs to arrive intact and be operational the moment the owner walks through the door.

## Types of Mobility Equipment We Move

Power wheelchairs range from compact folding models at 50 pounds to heavy-duty bariatric chairs at 300+ pounds. Mobility scooters come in 3-wheel and 4-wheel configurations, weighing anywhere from 80 to 250 pounds depending on the class. We also move stair lifts, portable ramps, and patient lift systems. Each has different handling requirements.

## Battery Disconnection and Handling

All power mobility devices run on rechargeable batteries, either sealed lead-acid (SLA) or lithium-ion. SLA batteries can leak acid if tipped or damaged. Lithium-ion batteries pose a fire risk if punctured. We disconnect batteries before transport, tape over the terminals to prevent short circuits, and pack them in a padded, upright container away from metal objects. For devices with non-removable battery packs, we power the device completely off and secure it in the upright riding position.

## Disassembly for Transport

Many scooters break down into 3-5 pieces for car transport, but for a household move, we often keep them assembled and load them onto the truck via the lift gate. The seat swivels off, the battery comes out, and the tiller (steering column) folds or locks. For power wheelchairs, we remove the joystick controller if it's detachable and wrap it separately. Footrests and armrests that detach get removed to prevent bending during loading.

## Securing in the Truck

A 200-pound power wheelchair rolling free in a moving truck is a battering ram. We strap mobility devices to the truck wall using ratchet straps through the frame, not around plastic body panels. Wheels get chocked with wooden blocks. The device rides in its normal upright position. We never stack boxes or furniture on top of a wheelchair or scooter because the plastic shrouds and electronic components can't handle the weight.

## Protecting Electronics and Controls

Modern power wheelchairs have sophisticated electronic controllers, Bluetooth connectivity, and programmable driving profiles. The joystick module on a high-end chair can cost $1,500+ to replace. We wrap controllers in bubble cushion and secure them against the chair frame. Display screens get cardboard protection. Charging cables and spare parts travel in a labeled bag attached to the chair.

## Stair Lifts and Home Modifications

If your current home has a stair lift, we coordinate with the manufacturer or a certified installer for removal. Stair lift rails are custom-cut to each staircase, so the same rail may not fit at the new home. We transport the chair unit and rail, and the installer handles modification and reinstallation. Grab bars, threshold ramps, and other accessibility modifications at the old home can sometimes be removed and reinstalled, depending on the mounting method and wall construction at the new place.

## Setting Up at the New Home

Mobility equipment gets unloaded and set up before anything else. We reassemble the device, reconnect the battery, and test basic functions. The user should have access to their chair or scooter the moment they arrive. We also make sure pathways through the new home are clear enough for the device to pass. Standard interior doorways are 32 inches wide, and many power chairs need the full clearance.
