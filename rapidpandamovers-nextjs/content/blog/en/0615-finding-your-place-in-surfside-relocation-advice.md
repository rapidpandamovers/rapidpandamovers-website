Surfside is a one-square-mile beach town tucked between Miami Beach and Bal Harbour. While tourists flock to South Beach, Surfside offers a quieter alternative with the same Atlantic Ocean access. Here is what you need to know before making your move.

## Why Choose Surfside?

Surfside attracts people who want beach living without the chaos of busier Miami neighborhoods. The town has about 6,000 residents, many families, and a significant Jewish community with kosher restaurants and synagogues. Collins Avenue runs through with high-rise condos while Harding Avenue provides a walkable downtown strip.

![Man Working On A Laptop By The Rocky Bea 4](/images/blog/2028/03/finding-your-place-in-surfside-relocation-advice/man-working-on-a-laptop-by-the-rocky-bea-4.webp)


The town has its own police department, community center with pool, and active local government that keeps the streets clean and the beaches maintained.

### Location and Accessibility

Surfside sits between Bal Harbour to the north and North Beach (Miami Beach) to the south. Collins Avenue and A1A run through town. Aventura Mall is about 10 minutes north. Downtown Miami and Brickell are 25-30 minutes south via I-95 or Collins Avenue. Miami International Airport is about 35 minutes away.

There is no Metrorail station in Surfside. Most residents drive or use rideshare. Parking is metered along Harding Avenue and beach access points. Street parking in residential areas requires permits.

### Community and Lifestyle

Surfside has a small-town feel unusual for coastal South Florida. Residents recognize each other at the beach, the community center, and local restaurants. The Surfside Community Center at 9301 Collins Avenue has a swimming pool with waterslide, fitness classes, and community events. The Surfside Beach Club provides beach chairs and umbrellas for residents.

## Neighborhoods to Consider

When planning your move to Surfside, the Harding Avenue area is particularly popular with newcomers for its local shops and restaurants. The oceanfront condos along Collins Avenue attract those seeking beach access and ocean views. The western side of town offers quieter, tree-lined residential streets with single-family homes. If Surfside feels too small, consider nearby Bal Harbour for luxury living, or North Miami Beach and Sunny Isles for more housing variety at different price points.

### Choosing Your Perfect Spot

Consider these factors:

- **Proximity to work and schools**: Factor in your daily commute
- **Local amenities**: Parks, shopping, dining, and entertainment options
- **Property types**: Single-family homes, condos, townhouses, or apartments
- **Community vibe**: Family-oriented, young professional, or mixed demographics

## Essential Services in Surfside

As a new Surfside resident, here are key services to know:

- **Healthcare**: Mount Sinai Medical Center is the closest major hospital, about 10 minutes south in Miami Beach. Aventura Hospital is about 15 minutes north.
- **Schools**: Surfside is zoned for Ruth K. Broad Bay Harbor K-8 Center and Miami Beach Senior High School. Private options include nearby Hebrew Academy (RASG) and Lehrman Community Day School.
- **Groceries**: Publix at 9400 Harding Avenue is the main grocery store in town. Whole Foods and Trader Joe's are available in Aventura.
- **Beach Access**: Public beach access points run along Collins Avenue from 88th to 96th Streets.
