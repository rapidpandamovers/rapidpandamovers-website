You have the keys to your new Miami home. Before the excitement of unpacking takes over, handle these critical tasks first. They're easier to complete now and will save you headaches later.

## 1. Change All the Locks

You don't know who has keys from before. Previous owners, contractors, cleaning crews, or even neighbors might have copies floating around. In Miami neighborhoods like Brickell condos or Coral Gables homes, turnover means plenty of people may have had access. Call a locksmith or visit Home Depot on Coral Way to rekey or replace all exterior locks on day one.

## 2. Test All Appliances

Run the dishwasher through a full cycle, check every stove burner, and test the washer and dryer. In South Florida, also run the AC for at least an hour to confirm it cools properly. Miami heat doesn't forgive a broken AC. Document any issues immediately while warranty claims or seller negotiations are still possible.

## 3. Locate Emergency Shutoffs

Find and label your water main, electrical panel, and gas shutoff (if applicable). In Miami, knowing where your water shutoff is becomes critical during hurricane season or if a pipe bursts. The water main is typically near the street or in a utility closet. Your electrical panel is often in the garage or a hallway closet. Take photos and share locations with everyone in your household.

## 4. Set Up Mail Forwarding

Visit USPS.com to forward mail from your old address. This should happen before you move, but if you forgot, do it now. Mail forwarding takes a few days to activate. While you're at it, update your address with your bank, employer, and the Florida DMV within 30 days of moving.

## 5. Transfer Utilities to Your Name

Contact FPL (Florida Power & Light) for electricity. If your home has gas, contact TECO Peoples Gas. Set up water through Miami-Dade Water and Sewer. For internet, Xfinity and AT&T are the main providers in most Miami neighborhoods. Confirm all accounts are active and in your name before move-in day to avoid sitting in the dark.

## 6. Schedule Critical Installations

Internet, cable, and security systems book out fast, especially during peak moving season. In busy Miami areas like Doral or Aventura, you might wait a week or more for installation windows. Book these appointments the day you get your keys. If you're in a condo, check with building management about installation requirements.

## 7. Deep Clean Before Unpacking

Even if the previous owners cleaned, do it again yourself or hire a service. In Miami's humid climate, pay special attention to areas prone to mold: under sinks, inside cabinets, around windows, and in the garage. Clean while rooms are still empty because once furniture is in place, you won't move it again for years.

## 8. Check Smoke and Carbon Monoxide Detectors

Test every detector and replace batteries with fresh ones. Don't assume they work just because they're installed. Florida code requires smoke detectors in every bedroom and on each floor. If your home has an attached garage or gas appliances, carbon monoxide detectors are also required. Pick up extras at the Lowes on Bird Road if needed.

## 9. Meet Your Immediate Neighbors

Knock on doors and introduce yourself within the first few days. In Miami neighborhoods from Coconut Grove to Kendall, neighbors can tell you which trash day is really pickup day, warn you about parking restrictions, and keep an eye on your place during hurricane evacuations. A quick hello builds goodwill that pays off later.

## 10. Document the Home's Condition

Walk through every room with your phone recording video. Photograph existing damage: scratched floors, wall marks, appliance dents, stained grout. If you're renting, email these to your landlord immediately to protect your deposit. If you bought, this documentation helps track warranty items and serves as a baseline for insurance claims.
