You've found a rental in North Miami Beach, but you're not sure which neighborhood fits your lifestyle. Eastern Shores has waterfront homes, Ojus offers affordable apartments near Aventura Mall, and the area around Oleta River State Park attracts outdoor enthusiasts. Here's what our moving crews have learned from hundreds of North Miami Beach relocations.

## Why North Miami Beach Stands Out

North Miami Beach sits between Aventura and North Miami, giving residents access to upscale shopping at Aventura Mall while paying lower rent than neighboring Sunny Isles Beach. The city has a strong international community, with Russian, Haitian, and Latin American influences shaping local restaurants and shops along West Dixie Highway and NE 163rd Street.

![Svc A Delivery Truck Driving Along A Coastal 1](/images/blog/2027/07/north-miami-beach-moving-guide-neighborhoods-and-tips/svc-a-delivery-truck-driving-along-a-coastal-1.webp)


Families move here for the mix of waterfront living without Miami Beach prices. Young professionals appreciate the 25-minute commute to Brickell via I-95, while retirees enjoy the quieter pace and proximity to Oleta River State Park, the largest urban park in Florida at 1,043 acres.

### Getting Around

North Miami Beach connects to major corridors that make commuting straightforward. I-95 runs along the western edge, putting Downtown Miami 30 minutes south during off-peak hours. The Biscayne Boulevard corridor (US-1) provides a surface-street alternative, lined with restaurants and shops.

For public transit, the 3 and 93 Metrobus routes run along Biscayne Boulevard, connecting to the Metrorail at Northside Station. Many residents bike along the East Coast Greenway trail that passes through Oleta River State Park.

### Local Character

The community centers around a few gathering spots. Greynolds Park hosts weekend picnics and fishing along the Oleta River. The Intracoastal Waterway attracts kayakers and paddleboarders, with rental shops near Haulover Park. Friday nights bring families to the restaurants along NE 163rd Street, where Cuban bakeries sit next to Russian delis.

## North Miami Beach Neighborhoods

Each pocket of North Miami Beach has distinct advantages depending on your priorities.

### Eastern Shores

This waterfront community features single-family homes with private docks along the Intracoastal. Homes here range from $700K to $2M, attracting boating families who want water access without condo fees. The neighborhood has its own HOA with security patrols. Eastern Shores Elementary is walkable, and Oleta River State Park sits at the eastern boundary.

### Ojus

West of I-95, Ojus offers the most affordable housing in the area. Apartment complexes along NE 183rd Street rent for $1,400-$1,800 for two bedrooms. The neighborhood is minutes from Aventura Mall and the Promenade Shops. Families here often send kids to Ojus Elementary, which has dual-language programs.

### Skylake

Between Eastern Shores and Ojus, Skylake has mid-century homes on larger lots. Prices hover around $400K-$600K, making it popular with first-time buyers. The area has mature oak trees, quieter streets, and quick access to both I-95 and Biscayne Boulevard.

### Uleta

South of 163rd Street, Uleta borders North Miami and has more of a mixed-use feel. Apartment towers share blocks with strip malls and auto shops. Rent runs lower here, and the 9 bus connects to Aventura.

## July Moving Tips for North Miami Beach

Summer moves in South Florida require specific planning. July averages 90°F with humidity above 75%, and afternoon thunderstorms roll in around 3pm almost daily.

### Schedule Morning Loads

Book your move to start at 7am or 8am. Our crews aim to have trucks loaded before noon when temperatures peak. Afternoon storms bring lightning, and movers cannot safely carry furniture during electrical storms. A morning start also beats rush hour traffic on I-95.

### Condo Move-In Requirements

Many North Miami Beach condos require 48-72 hour notice before move-in. Buildings along the Intracoastal often need elevator reservations, certificates of insurance from your moving company, and refundable deposits ($300-$500 is typical). Contact your building manager at least one week ahead.

### Protect Your Belongings from Humidity

Cardboard boxes left in a garage overnight will soften in Miami humidity. Keep packing materials inside with AC running until load day. Electronics, photos, and books are particularly sensitive. We recommend climate-controlled storage if your new place won't have AC running when you arrive.

## Services and Amenities

### Healthcare

Aventura Hospital sits five minutes north on Biscayne Boulevard, with a Level II trauma center. Mount Sinai Medical Center is 20 minutes south in Miami Beach. Several urgent care clinics operate along 163rd Street, including a CVS MinuteClinic and CareSpot.

### Schools

North Miami Beach Senior High serves the area for grades 9-12. Elementary options include Eastern Shores Elementary (rated B+), Ojus Elementary with Spanish dual-language, and several private schools including Talmudic University. Many families opt for magnet programs at nearby Aventura Waterways K-8.

### Shopping and Dining

Aventura Mall is the largest shopping center in Florida, with 300+ stores including Nordstrom, Bloomingdale's, and Apple. For groceries, Publix has locations on 163rd Street and in Ojus, while Whole Foods is at Aventura Commons. International markets include the Russian Deli on West Dixie Highway and several Caribbean grocery stores.

### Recreation

Oleta River State Park offers mountain biking trails, kayak rentals, and cabin camping. Haulover Beach Park is popular for swimming and has a clothing-optional section. Greynolds Park provides fishing, picnic areas, and a golf course. The Intracoastal Waterway gives boat owners direct access to Biscayne Bay.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does it cost to move in North Miami Beach?

Local moves within Miami-Dade start at $99/hour for a two-person crew plus truck. A typical one-bedroom apartment takes 2-3 hours, while a three-bedroom home runs 5-7 hours. Condo moves may add time for elevator waits and long hallway carries.

### What documents do I need for a condo move?

Most North Miami Beach condos require a certificate of insurance (COI) from your moving company naming the condo association as additionally insured. You'll also need to reserve the freight elevator and may need to pay a refundable deposit. Contact your building manager at least one week before your move.

### What's the best time of year to move to North Miami Beach?

October through April offers cooler weather and less rain. Summer moves are busier but work fine with morning scheduling. Avoid moving during hurricane season peak (August-October) if possible, and always have a backup date in case of storm delays.
