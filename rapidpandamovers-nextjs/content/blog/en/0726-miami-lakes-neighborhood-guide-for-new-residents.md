Miami Lakes continues to attract new residents from across the country during May, and it's easy to see why. As a premier Miami-Dade location, Miami Lakes offers a unique blend of community, convenience, and lifestyle options.

## Why Choose Miami Lakes?

Miami Lakes stands out as one of Miami-Dade County's most appealing locations. Developed by the Graham family in the 1960s, this master-planned community spans about 6.5 square miles and features over 60 man-made lakes, 11 miles of walking trails, and the popular Main Street shopping district with its small-town feel.

![Colorful Scene Capturing Miami Beach S H 2](/images/blog/2027/05/miami-lakes-neighborhood-guide-for-new-residents/colorful-scene-capturing-miami-beach-s-h-2.webp)


The area attracts families, professionals, and retirees alike, thanks to its quality of life, convenient access to major employment centers, and excellent amenities.

### Location and Accessibility

One of Miami Lakes's biggest advantages is its strategic location within Miami-Dade County. Residents enjoy easy access to I-75, the Palmetto Expressway (SR 826), and the Florida Turnpike. Downtown Miami is about 20 miles south, while Fort Lauderdale is roughly 25 miles north. Miami International Airport is a 20-minute drive. Nearby communities include Hialeah to the south, Miramar to the north, and Doral just 10 minutes to the southwest.

### Community and Lifestyle

Miami Lakes residents enjoy a strong sense of community. Royal Oaks Park offers playgrounds, sports fields, and picnic areas. The Miami Lakes Optimist Park hosts youth sports leagues year-round. The town hosts regular events including the Miami Lakes Art in the Park festival, Fourth of July celebrations at Main Street, and weekly farmers markets. The Mary Collins Community Center provides classes and programs for all ages.

## Neighborhoods to Consider

When planning your move to Miami Lakes, several distinct neighborhoods deserve consideration. Main Street offers walkable access to restaurants, shops, and entertainment. Royal Oaks features larger single-family homes with established trees. Miami Lakes East provides easy access to I-75 and newer construction. The Lakes section surrounds several of the community's signature waterways, offering waterfront living. Loch Lomond and Loch Ness neighborhoods attract families with their quiet streets and proximity to schools.

### Choosing Your Perfect Spot

Consider these factors:

- **Proximity to work and schools**: Factor in your daily commute
- **Local amenities**: Parks, shopping, dining, and entertainment options
- **Property types**: Single-family homes, condos, townhouses, or apartments
- **Community vibe**: Family-oriented, young professional, or mixed demographics

## Moving to Miami Lakes in May

May is a good time for your move, though you should plan around the weather. Temperatures typically reach the mid to upper 80s, and afternoon thunderstorms become more common as wet season approaches. Schedule your move for morning hours to avoid the typical 3-5 PM storm window. May also falls before peak hurricane season (June through November), so you avoid the busiest storm months.

### Timing Your Move

When planning your relocation, consider:

- **Best moving days**: Weekdays often offer better availability and rates
- **Weather considerations**: Schedule morning moves before afternoon thunderstorms roll in
- **Local events**: Check for any street closures or community events that might affect your move

## Essential Services to Locate

As a new Miami Lakes resident, you'll want to find:

- **Healthcare facilities**: Palmetto General Hospital is nearby in Hialeah. Baptist Health and Memorial Regional are within 15 minutes.
- **Schools**: Miami Lakes K-8 Center, Barbara Goleman Senior High, and several charter schools including Doral Academy serve the area.
- **Shopping**: Main Street offers local boutiques and restaurants. Miami Lakes Town Center has major retailers. Shops at Pembroke Gardens and Dolphin Mall are short drives away.
- **Recreation**: Royal Oaks Park, Optimist Park, and the 11-mile trail system provide outdoor activities. The Don Shula Hotel & Golf Club offers a championship course.

## Benefits of Choosing Miami Lakes

Living in Miami Lakes offers advantages that attract families and professionals alike:

- **Planned community design**: Unlike sprawling suburbs, Miami Lakes was thoughtfully designed with connected neighborhoods, walking trails, and community gathering spaces
- **Strong schools**: Miami-Dade County schools in the area consistently perform above district averages
- **Business-friendly**: The town attracts corporate headquarters and offers local employment opportunities
- **Central location**: Easy access to both Miami and Fort Lauderdale employment centers without living in either urban core
