PCS orders just dropped and you're relocating to South Florida this April. Spring marks the busiest military moving season, with families scrambling for trucks and movers booking out weeks in advance. Here's what our crews have learned from hundreds of military relocations to Miami-Dade County.

## Why Spring Military Moves Require Extra Planning

April through June is peak PCS season. Every branch cycles personnel simultaneously, which means moving companies across Miami get slammed. Add Easter weekend, spring break, and afternoon thunderstorms to the mix, and you've got a logistical puzzle that requires advance planning.

## Key Tips for Military Moving in April

### Book Movers Six Weeks Out

April availability disappears fast. If your report date is firm, lock in moving dates the moment you have orders in hand. Military families near Kendall, Pinecrest, and Palmetto Bay often compete for the same moving windows.

### Keep Documentation in One Folder

Your DD Form 1299, orders, weight tickets, and receipts all need to stay together. Missing a single document can delay reimbursement by weeks. Our crews have seen families lose hundreds in out-of-pocket costs because paperwork got packed in a random box.

### Plan Around Miami Weather

South Florida's spring pattern is predictable: clear mornings, thunderstorms by 3pm. Schedule loading and unloading for early hours. If you're moving to a condo in Brickell or Aventura, reserve the freight elevator for morning slots before afternoon storms hit.

### Inventory Everything with Photos

Military moves involve weight calculations and potential claims. Document electronics, furniture condition, and high-value items before anything gets wrapped. This matters especially for families relocating from overseas posts where items have traveled thousands of miles already.
