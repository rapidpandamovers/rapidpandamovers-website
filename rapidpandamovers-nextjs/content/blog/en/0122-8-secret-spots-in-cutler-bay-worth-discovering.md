You just moved to Cutler Bay and you are ready to explore beyond the usual spots. While Southland Mall and Black Point Marina get all the attention, this South Miami-Dade community has quieter corners that residents keep to themselves. Here are eight places worth finding.

## 1. Black Point Ocean Grill

**Location: 24775 SW 87th Avenue, at Black Point Marina**

![A Man Labels A Cardboard Box Kitchen Dur 1](/images/blog/2025/01/8-secret-spots-in-cutler-bay-worth-discovering/a-man-labels-a-cardboard-box-kitchen-dur-1.webp)


Tucked inside Black Point Marina, this waterfront restaurant stays under the radar despite its prime location. Order the fish tacos or the catch of the day while watching boats head out to Biscayne Bay. Weekday lunches are especially quiet, and sunset views rival anything in Coconut Grove without the crowds. Most visitors rush past to get to the boat ramps, never noticing the grill.

## 2. The Lakes by the Bay Walking Trail

**Location: Access from Lakes by the Bay Park, SW 200th Street**

This paved path circles through one of Cutler Bay's most scenic neighborhoods. The route passes waterfront homes, shaded areas with mature trees, and lake views that make you forget you are in Miami-Dade. Early mornings bring herons and egrets, and the flat terrain works for strollers and bikes. Park at Lakes by the Bay Park and head south along the water.

## 3. Frankie's Pizza

**Location: 9118 Coral Way (SW 24th Street), near Cutler Bay border**

Technically in unincorporated Miami-Dade just north of Cutler Bay, this family-owned spot has served thin-crust New York-style pizza since the 1950s. The interior looks like a time capsule, red vinyl booths and all. Locals order the plain cheese pie and garlic knots. Cash only. This is not a chain trying to look vintage; Frankie's is the real thing.

## 4. Cutler Ridge Park Disc Golf Course

**Location: 10100 SW 200th Street (Cutler Ridge Park)**

![Cardboard Box Filled With Clothes Hanger 2](/images/blog/2025/01/8-secret-spots-in-cutler-bay-worth-discovering/cardboard-box-filled-with-clothes-hanger-2.webp)


One of the few free disc golf courses in Miami-Dade, this 18-hole layout winds through pine rockland and open fields. The course challenges intermediate players while staying accessible for beginners. Weekday mornings are nearly empty. Bring your own discs; there is no rental shop, but that keeps the casual crowds away.

## 5. The Deering Estate at Cutler

**Location: 16701 SW 72nd Avenue**

Yes, the Deering Estate is technically a tourist attraction, but most Cutler Bay residents have never explored its full 450 acres. Skip the main house tour that visitors favor and head straight to the nature trails. The coastal hardwood hammock trail leads to the bay, and the canoe launch offers kayak access to Biscayne Bay. Monthly full moon kayak tours are worth the reservation.

## 6. Sergio's Cuban Restaurant (Cutler Bay Location)

**Location: 18851 S Dixie Highway (US-1)**

The Kendall and Coral Gables Sergio's locations get the crowds, but the Cutler Bay outpost on US-1 stays quieter. The menu is the same Cuban comfort food: vaca frita, ropa vieja, and cortaditos that fuel your afternoon. The croquetas preparadas sandwich is a local secret, and the pastelitos de guayaba are baked fresh daily.

## 7. Whispering Pines Park

**Location: 19701 SW 89th Court**

![Young Adult Male Smiling While Handling  3](/images/blog/2025/01/8-secret-spots-in-cutler-bay-worth-discovering/young-adult-male-smiling-while-handling--3.webp)


This small neighborhood park hides behind residential streets and rarely appears on maps. A short nature trail winds through pine rockland habitat, and the playground stays empty compared to larger parks. The picnic pavilions work for small gatherings. Locals bring their dogs here in the early morning before the heat arrives.

## 8. Bill Sadowski Park and Nature Center

**Location: 17555 SW 79th Avenue (Palmetto Bay, bordering Cutler Bay)**

Just across the Cutler Bay border in Palmetto Bay, this 30-acre park feels like a different world. The nature center hosts weekend astronomy programs, and the trails through native hammock offer shade on hot days. The park stays quiet because it lacks the amenities that draw bigger crowds. Bring binoculars; birdwatchers spot warblers and hawks along the paths.
