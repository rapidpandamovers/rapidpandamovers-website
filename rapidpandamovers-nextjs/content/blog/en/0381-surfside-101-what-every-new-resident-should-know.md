You are tired of crowded beaches where you cannot find a parking spot or a quiet stretch of sand. You want that small-town feel where the barista knows your order and neighbors wave hello during morning walks. Surfside delivers exactly that, just 20 minutes north of Miami Beach, where 0.46 square miles of beachfront living feels like a well-kept secret.

## Why Choose Surfside?

Surfside sits between Bal Harbour to the north and Miami Beach to the south, giving residents the best of both worlds. You get the upscale shopping of Bal Harbour Shops within walking distance, plus the nightlife and restaurants of South Beach just 15 minutes away, all without the crowds, traffic, or tourist noise.

![Loc Aerial Photo Showcasing A Suburban Neigh 1](/images/blog/2026/10/surfside-101-what-every-new-resident-should-know/loc-aerial-photo-showcasing-a-suburban-neigh-1.webp)


The town's 6,000 residents enjoy a beach that rarely feels crowded, even on weekends. Surfside Beach has lifeguards year-round, clean restrooms, and actual parking, something you cannot say about most Miami-Dade beaches.

### Location and Accessibility

Collins Avenue runs straight through town, connecting you to A1A for beach cruising north to Fort Lauderdale or south to Key Biscayne. I-95 sits about 10 minutes west via 96th Street, making Downtown Miami commutes around 25 minutes without rush hour traffic.

The Miami Beach Trolley runs a free route along Collins Avenue, connecting Surfside to Bal Harbour and Miami Beach. For longer trips, the Brightline station in Aventura Mall is just 15 minutes north, offering quick access to Fort Lauderdale and West Palm Beach.

### Community and Lifestyle

Surfside feels like a small town because it operates like one. The community center hosts movie nights on the beach during cooler months, and the annual Surfside Spectacular brings neighbors together every spring. Local restaurants like Cafe Ragazzi on Harding Avenue become gathering spots where regulars know each other by name.

The town maintains strict building height limits, keeping the skyline low and the beach views unobstructed. No building can exceed 12 stories, which preserves that village atmosphere Miami Beach lost decades ago.

## Neighborhoods to Consider

Surfside packs a lot of personality into less than half a square mile. Each block has its own feel, and choosing the right location makes a difference in your daily routine.

### Harding Avenue District

This is the heart of Surfside. Harding Avenue runs parallel to Collins, one block west, and serves as the town's main street. You will find Cafe Ragazzi, 26 Sushi, Josh's Deli, and local shops within a few blocks. Living near Harding means walking to breakfast, grabbing groceries at Publix, and never needing your car on weekends.

### Oceanfront Living

The high-rise condos along Collins Avenue offer direct beach access and ocean views. Buildings like the Surf Club Four Seasons, Fendi Chateau, and the Surf Club Hotel attract residents who want resort-style amenities. These buildings typically require elevator reservations for move-ins, so plan 48 hours ahead and coordinate with building management.

### West Surfside

The western blocks near Abbott Avenue offer single-family homes with actual yards, rare in this part of Miami-Dade. Families with kids tend to settle here for the extra space and quieter streets. You are still just a 5-minute walk to the beach but with room for a backyard grill and maybe a pool.

### Choosing Your Perfect Spot

Consider these factors when picking your block:

- **Beach access priority**: Oceanfront condos mean sand between your toes in minutes
- **Walkability needs**: Harding Avenue area keeps everything within a 10-minute walk
- **Space requirements**: West Surfside offers yards and garages for families
- **Budget range**: Single-family homes average $1.5M while condos range from $500K to $5M+

## Moving to Surfside in October

October brings ideal moving conditions to South Florida. Hurricane season starts winding down, temperatures drop from summer highs into the low 80s, and humidity becomes tolerable. You can load boxes without sweating through your shirt.

### Timing Your Move

Plan your Surfside move with these local factors in mind:

- **Best moving days**: Tuesday through Thursday offer the best availability and rates, especially mid-month
- **Weather windows**: Morning moves before 2pm avoid the afternoon storms common through October
- **High Holy Days**: Surfside has a large Orthodox Jewish community, so moving companies book up around Rosh Hashanah and Yom Kippur
- **Building logistics**: Most Surfside condos require 48-72 hour notice for elevator reservations and loading dock access

### Condo Move-In Requirements

If you are moving into a Collins Avenue high-rise, expect these requirements:

- Certificate of Insurance (COI) naming the building as additional insured
- Elevator reservation during approved moving hours (often 9am-5pm weekdays only)
- Security deposit ranging from $500-$2,000
- Pre-move inspection of hallways and elevators

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does moving to Surfside cost?

Local moves to Surfside typically run $400-$800 for a one-bedroom and $800-$1,500 for a three-bedroom, depending on building requirements and access complexity. High-rise condos with strict elevator schedules may add $100-$200 for the extra coordination time.

### What makes Surfside different from Miami Beach?

Surfside maintains a 12-story building height limit, creating a village feel rather than a concrete canyon. The beach is less crowded, parking is easier to find, and the community is tighter-knit. You trade nightlife and tourist attractions for quiet mornings and neighbors who know your name.

### Is Surfside safe?

Surfside consistently ranks among the safest municipalities in Miami-Dade County. The small size means police response times are quick, and the tight-knit community keeps an eye on things. Most crime consists of car break-ins, easily prevented by not leaving valuables visible.
