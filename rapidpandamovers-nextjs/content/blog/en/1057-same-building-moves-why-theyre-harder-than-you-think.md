You're moving from apartment 8A to apartment 15B in the same building. No truck needed. Just carry things to the elevator, ride up seven floors, and you're done. Simple, right?

Not quite. Same-building moves present challenges that catch people off guard.

## The Elevator Problem

In Miami high-rises, the freight elevator is the bottleneck. You share it with other residents, building maintenance, deliveries, and anyone else moving that day.

![Close Up Of An Aged Urban Apartment Buil 2](/images/blog/2028/11/same-building-moves-why-theyre-harder-than-you-think/close-up-of-an-aged-urban-apartment-buil-2.webp)


Typical building policies:
- Freight elevator reservations limited to 4-hour blocks
- Only one household can book per day (or per half-day)
- Deposits of $200-500 required
- Move windows restricted to specific hours (often 9 AM - 5 PM weekdays only)

If you're carrying furniture between floors, you need the freight elevator. Residential elevators aren't large enough for couches, mattresses, or dining tables. Breaking furniture down and reassembling on the other end adds hours to what seemed like a quick move.

## The "Just a Few Trips" Fallacy

People underestimate how much they own. A one-bedroom apartment contains 40-60 boxes worth of items on average, plus furniture. Each elevator trip takes 5-10 minutes including loading, travel, and unloading.

Math: 50 items x 8 minutes per round trip = 400 minutes = 6.5 hours

That's just for moving items to the elevator, riding, and carrying them to your new unit. Add packing, unpacking, and the inevitable issues with items that don't fit, and you're looking at a full weekend project for a DIY move.

Professional movers with dollies, proper technique, and experience complete same-building moves in 2-4 hours.

## What Buildings Actually Require

Most Miami condo and apartment buildings require:

**Advance notice**: Building management needs 7-14 days notice for elevator reservations. Some buildings only allow moves on specific days.

**Certificate of Insurance**: Even for same-building moves, many buildings require COI from your movers. This protects the building if something damages common areas during your move.

**Floor and wall protection**: Someone has to pad the elevator walls and protect hallway floors. Some buildings provide this; others expect your movers to handle it.

**Security deposit**: Refundable if no damage occurs to common areas.

**Supervision**: Some luxury buildings require a building engineer or property manager present during moves.

## The Hidden Costs of DIY

When you try to save money by moving yourself within the building, you often lose:

![Exterior View Of A Modern Apartment Buil 3](/images/blog/2028/11/same-building-moves-why-theyre-harder-than-you-think/exterior-view-of-a-modern-apartment-buil-3.webp)


**Time**: Weekend hours spent hauling boxes instead of setting up your new space

**Energy**: Physical exhaustion from repeated lifting and carrying

**Social capital**: How many friends can you ask to help move furniture through an elevator?

**Damage risk**: Without proper equipment, furniture gets scratched, walls get scuffed, and you're on the hook for repairs

**Deposit**: If you damage common areas without insurance, it comes from your pocket

## When Same-Building Moves Make Sense

The economics work in your favor when:
- You're upgrading to a larger unit without leaving a building you love
- Rent increases in your current unit exceed the cost of moving within the building
- Your lease allows an internal transfer without breaking your agreement
- You want a different view, floor, or layout
