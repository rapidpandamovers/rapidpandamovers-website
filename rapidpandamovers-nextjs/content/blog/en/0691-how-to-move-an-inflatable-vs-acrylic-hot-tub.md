# How to Move an Inflatable vs Acrylic Hot Tub

Not all hot tubs are built the same, and the difference between moving an inflatable spa and a permanent acrylic tub is enormous. One you can handle yourself with some patience. The other is a full-scale moving operation. If you own either type in the Miami area, here's what each move actually involves.

## Moving an Inflatable Hot Tub

Inflatable spas (like the Intex PureSpa or Coleman SaluSpa models that are popular for Miami patio setups) are the easy ones. They weigh 50 to 100 pounds empty and pack down to the size of a large duffel bag.

![Adult Man Enjoying Relaxation In A Woode 3](/images/blog/2028/09/how-to-move-an-inflatable-vs-acrylic-hot-tub/adult-man-enjoying-relaxation-in-a-woode-3.webp)


### Steps to Move an Inflatable

1. **Power down and unplug.** Inflatable tubs run on 120V and plug into a standard GFCI outlet. Just unplug it.
2. **Drain the water.** Connect a garden hose to the drain port or use a small submersible pump. A typical inflatable holds 150 to 250 gallons, so this takes 30 to 60 minutes with gravity drainage.
3. **Deflate the tub.** Most inflatables have a built-in deflation valve. Open it and let the air escape. You can speed this up by using the pump unit's deflate function if it has one.
4. **Dry it out.** Wipe down the interior and let it air dry. In Miami's humidity, give it extra time or use towels to speed things up. Any moisture left inside will turn to mold once the tub is folded and packed.
5. **Fold and pack.** Once dry, fold the tub loosely and pack it in its original box or a large storage bag. Pack the pump unit, hoses, and filter separately.

The whole process takes about 2 hours, and you don't need professional help unless the inflatable is part of a larger household move.

## Moving an Acrylic Hot Tub

This is a completely different animal. Acrylic hot tubs (brands like Jacuzzi, Hot Spring, Bullfrog, and Sundance that are common in Miami backyards) are permanent installations weighing 400 to 900 pounds empty. They're hardwired to 240V electrical circuits, often plumbed into the home's water system, and frequently boxed into spaces that make removal a challenge.

![Shirtless Man Enjoying Relaxation In A H 1](/images/blog/2028/09/how-to-move-an-inflatable-vs-acrylic-hot-tub/shirtless-man-enjoying-relaxation-in-a-h-1.webp)


### Preparation (1-2 Days Before)

- **Hire a licensed electrician** to disconnect the 240V circuit. In Miami-Dade County, this work should be done by a professional.
- **Drain the tub completely** using a submersible pump or gravity drain. A standard acrylic tub holds 300 to 500 gallons.
- **Blow out the internal plumbing** with compressed air to remove trapped water from jet lines and the pump housing.
- **Remove accessories**: cover, steps, headrests, filter cartridges, and any attached features.
- **Clean and dry the shell.** Acrylic scratches easily, so avoid abrasive cleaners. Let it dry thoroughly.

### The Move Itself

This is where professional [**Hot Tub Moving**](/hot-tub-moving) comes in. A crew of 4 to 6 movers will:

- Wrap the shell in moving blankets for protection
- Tip the tub onto its side (carefully, with multiple people controlling the angle)
- Slide it onto spa dollies or furniture dollies
- Move it through the yard, past fences and gates, and along the access path to the truck
- Load it onto a flatbed using ramps and straps

At the destination, the process reverses. The tub gets placed on a level pad, the electrician reconnects the wiring, and you refill and rebalance the water chemistry.

## Key Differences at a Glance

| Factor | Inflatable | Acrylic |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| Empty weight | 50-100 lbs | 400-900 lbs |
| Electrical | 120V plug-in | 240V hardwired |
| Drain time | 30-60 min | 1-2 hours |
| People needed | 1-2 | 4-6 |
| Professional movers needed | No | Yes |
| Typical cost | Free (DIY) | $300-$600+ |

## A Note on Rotomolded Tubs

There's a middle category worth mentioning: rotomolded (plastic) hot tubs like some DreamMaker or AquaRest models. These weigh 200 to 400 pounds empty and usually run on 120V. They're heavier than inflatables but lighter than acrylic. Two to three strong people with a furniture dolly can usually handle the move, though professional help is still recommended for larger rotomolded models.
