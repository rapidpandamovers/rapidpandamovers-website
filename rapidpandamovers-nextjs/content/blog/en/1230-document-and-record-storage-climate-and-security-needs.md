# Document and Record Storage: Climate and Security Needs

Paper is surprisingly fragile. It absorbs moisture, yellows in heat, attracts insects, and deteriorates faster than most people expect. In Miami's tropical climate, documents stored without proper conditions can become unreadable within a single summer. Whether you're storing personal records during a move or business files during an office relocation, getting the environment right matters more than the lock on the door.

## Why Miami's Climate Threatens Paper Records

Paper's biggest enemy is moisture. When humidity exceeds 60%, paper fibers absorb water from the air, causing pages to swell, stick together, and develop mold. Miami's average relative humidity sits around 75% year-round, with spikes above 90% during summer rainstorms.

![A Well Organized Warehouse Aisle With St 1](/images/blog/2029/10/document-and-record-storage-climate-and-security-needs/a-well-organized-warehouse-aisle-with-st-1.webp)


Heat accelerates the breakdown. Documents stored in a non-climate-controlled unit in Hialeah or Homestead can experience internal temperatures above 100 degrees during summer months. That combination of heat and moisture creates conditions where:

- **Mold grows on paper surfaces** within weeks, sometimes days.
- **Ink bleeds and fades**, making printed text and handwritten notes illegible.
- **Adhesives on labels and bindings** soften and fail.
- **Photos develop sticky surfaces** and fuse together permanently.
- **Insects and silverfish** thrive in warm, humid environments and feed on paper products.

## Climate Requirements for Document Storage

The ideal storage environment for paper documents maintains:

- **Temperature**: 65-70 degrees Fahrenheit, consistent without major fluctuations.
- **Humidity**: 30-40% relative humidity. This is significantly lower than what most standard climate-controlled storage units offer, so ask specifically about dehumidification capabilities.
- **Air quality**: Filtered air circulation that prevents dust and contaminant buildup.

For most personal document storage needs during a move, a standard climate-controlled unit (55-80 degrees, under 55% humidity) provides adequate protection for weeks or months. For longer-term or archival-quality storage, look for facilities with dedicated document storage programs.

## Security Requirements

Documents carry personal and business information that needs protection beyond a padlock:

- **Individual unit alarms**: Your unit should trigger an alert if opened outside of your access window.
- **Video surveillance**: Cameras covering hallways, entrances, and loading areas with footage retained for at least 30 days.
- **Access logging**: The facility should track who enters and exits, with timestamps.
- **Fire suppression**: Sprinkler systems or dry chemical suppression designed to protect paper from both fire and water damage.

For business records subject to regulatory requirements (medical files under HIPAA, financial records under SOX compliance, legal files with attorney-client privilege), the storage facility should be able to document its security protocols in writing.

## How to Pack Documents for Storage

Proper packing extends the life of stored documents:

![Warehouse Worker Operating A Pallet Jack 2](/images/blog/2029/10/document-and-record-storage-climate-and-security-needs/warehouse-worker-operating-a-pallet-jack-2.webp)


- **Use acid-free boxes and folders** for anything with long-term value. Standard cardboard contains acids that break down paper over time.
- **Stand file folders upright** in boxes rather than stacking flat. This prevents compression damage and makes retrieval easier.
- **Avoid plastic bags or sealed containers** that can trap moisture. Paper needs to breathe.
- **Place silica gel packets** inside document boxes to absorb excess humidity.
- **Wrap photos individually** in acid-free tissue paper and store them flat in rigid containers.

## Organizing for Retrieval

Label document boxes with clear categories: tax records by year, medical records by family member, legal documents by type. Maintain a digital index of what's in each box so you can find specific files without opening every container.

If you're storing records you'll need to access periodically, keep those boxes near the front of the unit and clearly marked.
