# How to Prepare Your Appliances for a Move

Your appliances are probably the heaviest and most expensive items in your home. A full-size refrigerator can weigh over 300 pounds, and a loaded washer tips the scale at nearly 200. When you're moving across Miami or heading to a new home in Coral Gables, getting these big-ticket items ready takes more than just unplugging them.

## Why Appliance Prep Matters

Skipping preparation is how people end up with a broken compressor or water damage across their new hardwood floors. Refrigerators that aren't defrosted leak during transport. Washers with unsecured drums bang around in the truck and crack their housings. Dryers with lint-packed vents become fire hazards at the new address. Every appliance has specific steps you need to handle before moving day, and cutting corners costs more than the time you saved.

## Preparing Each Major Appliance

### Refrigerator and Freezer

Start 24 to 48 hours before your move. Unplug the unit and leave the doors open to defrost completely. Place towels around the base to catch water runoff. In Miami's humidity, this step takes longer than you'd expect because moisture from the air keeps condensing inside the unit. Clean the interior with baking soda and water, then dry everything thoroughly. Remove shelves and drawers, wrap them in moving blankets or bubble wrap, and pack them separately. Tape the power cord to the back of the fridge so it doesn't catch on doorways.

### Washer

Turn off the water supply valves behind the machine. Disconnect the hot and cold water hoses and drain any remaining water into a bucket. If you have a front-loader, check for water trapped in the door gasket. The most overlooked step: installing transit bolts. These metal bolts lock the drum in place during transport. Most washers come with them originally, and you can order replacements from the manufacturer. Without transit bolts, the drum swings freely and can damage internal components during the drive from Kendall to your new place in Doral.

### Dryer

Unplug electric dryers and disconnect the vent hose. For gas dryers, shut off the gas valve and disconnect the supply line. We strongly recommend having a professional handle gas disconnections. Clean out the lint trap and the vent duct while you have access. Tape the power cord and vent hose to the back of the unit.

### Dishwasher

Run an empty cycle the night before to flush out food particles. After the cycle finishes, disconnect the water supply and drain hose under the sink. Leave the door slightly open overnight to let the interior air dry. Secure the racks inside with tape so they don't slide around.

### Oven and Range

Remove racks, burner grates, and knobs. Wrap these separately since they scratch and chip easily. Clean the interior so old grease doesn't bake onto surfaces in a hot moving truck (a real concern during Miami summers when truck interiors can hit 140 degrees). For gas ranges, have a licensed technician disconnect the gas line.

## General Tips for All Appliances

- **Document your connections.** Take photos of how water lines, gas lines, and electrical connections look before you disconnect anything. This saves time and guesswork during reconnection.
- **Save your manuals.** If you don't have paper copies, download PDFs from the manufacturer's website. Many have specific moving instructions.
- **Use original packaging when possible.** If you kept the boxes and foam inserts, use them. They were designed to protect your exact model.
- **Clean everything.** Mold grows fast in Miami's climate. A damp appliance sealed up in a moving truck becomes a mold factory in hours.
