# Donating Furniture Before a Move: Where and How

Moving a couch costs money. Moving a couch you don't want at your new place costs money and makes no sense. If you're planning a move in the Miami area and have furniture that won't be making the trip, donating it is the best option. Your unwanted pieces go to someone who needs them, you lighten your moving load, and you get a tax-deductible receipt. Here's where and how to donate furniture in Miami-Dade.

## Where to Donate Furniture in Miami

**Habitat for Humanity ReStore** is the top destination for furniture donations in Miami-Dade. They accept couches, tables, chairs, dressers, desks, bed frames, shelving, and more. Items need to be in usable condition (no major stains, tears, or structural damage). ReStore offers free pickup for larger items when you schedule through their website or by phone. Proceeds fund Habitat's home-building projects in the community.

**The Salvation Army** accepts furniture at their Miami locations and offers free pickup for qualifying donations. Schedule online or call their donation hotline. They take living room, bedroom, and dining room furniture, as well as mattresses in good condition.

**Goodwill** accepts smaller furniture pieces at their donation centers on Coral Way, Bird Road, and throughout Miami-Dade. Larger items may need to be dropped off, as pickup availability varies by location.

**Miami Rescue Mission** accepts furniture donations to support their residential programs. They're particularly interested in bed frames, mattresses, dressers, and basic household furniture.

**Lotus House** (the largest women's shelter in the U.S., located in Miami) welcomes bedroom furniture, children's furniture, and household essentials for residents transitioning to permanent housing.

**Furniture Bank of South Florida** collects and distributes furniture to families in need throughout Miami-Dade and Broward counties. They accept most household furniture in decent condition.

## What Condition Does Furniture Need to Be In?

Donation centers have standards. Your furniture should be:

- Structurally sound (no broken legs, cracked frames, or missing parts)
- Free of major stains, rips, or pet damage
- Clean and free of pests (bed bugs are an automatic rejection everywhere)
- Functional (drawers open, doors close, mechanisms work)

If a piece is borderline, call the organization and describe it before scheduling a pickup. They'd rather tell you upfront than send a truck for something they can't accept.

## How to Schedule a Pickup

Most organizations need three to seven days' notice for furniture pickup in Miami-Dade. During peak moving season (May through September), wait times can stretch longer. Here's the general process:

1. Contact the organization by phone or through their website
2. Describe the items (type, quantity, condition, and size)
3. Provide your address and preferred pickup window
4. Place items in an accessible location (ground floor, near the front door or garage) on pickup day
5. Get your donation receipt from the driver

Make sure someone is home during the pickup window. Drivers typically give a broad time range rather than a specific appointment.

## Pickup Day Tips

Move furniture to the first floor or garage before the pickup crew arrives. Most donation pickup drivers will not go upstairs, through narrow hallways, or into basements. If you live in a Miami high-rise, bring items down to the loading dock or lobby.

Remove personal items from drawers and shelves. Check between cushions. We've heard plenty of stories about cash, jewelry, and important documents being accidentally donated inside furniture.

## Tax Deduction Benefits

Furniture donations to qualified nonprofits are tax-deductible if you itemize. The IRS allows you to deduct the fair market value of donated items. For furniture, this typically means the price the item would sell for at a thrift store, not what you paid for it new.

Keep your donation receipts organized. Note the items donated, their condition, and an estimated value for each. For donations worth more than $500, you'll need to file IRS Form 8283 with your tax return.
