Weekends in Miami Beach offer endless possibilities, especially in March when the weather is perfect and spring break energy fills the air. Whether you prefer relaxing brunches, outdoor adventures, or cultural experiences, here are six specific things to do this weekend.

## 1. Explore the Lincoln Road Farmers Market

**611 Lincoln Road (between Washington Ave and Meridian Ave)** | Sundays 9 AM - 6:30 PM

![A Fashionable Man Poses Confidently In M 1](/images/blog/2026/03/6-things-to-do-in-miami-beach-this-weekend/a-fashionable-man-poses-confidently-in-m-1.webp)


This year-round Sunday tradition features fresh fruits, vegetables, cut flowers, baked bread, local honey, jams, and spices. The 10-block pedestrian promenade also offers world-class shopping and people-watching. Arrive early for the best selection and cooler temperatures.

## 2. Take an Art Deco Walking Tour

**Miami Design Preservation League** | Multiple tours daily

South Beach's Art Deco Historic District contains over 800 buildings within one square mile. The official MDPL two-hour tour explores landmark hotels like The Tides (1936) and the National Hotel (1939), sharing stories of mob life, Hollywood glamour, and the architectural revolution that defined Miami Beach. Self-guided audio tours are available in four languages.

## 3. Catch Sunrise at Lummus Park Beach

**Ocean Drive from 5th to 15th Street** | Free

![Art Deco Architecture On Lincoln Road In 5](/images/blog/2026/03/6-things-to-do-in-miami-beach-this-weekend/art-deco-architecture-on-lincoln-road-in-5.webp)


Miami Beach's most iconic beach runs along palm-studded Lummus Park facing the gorgeous Art Deco hotels of Ocean Drive. Arrive at sunrise for uncrowded sand and perfect photo opportunities. The restored sand dunes and coastal vegetation make this the quintessential South Beach experience.

## 4. Stroll the Spanish Village of Espanola Way

**Espanola Way between Washington and Drexel Avenues**

This tree-lined pedestrian street, modeled after Mediterranean villages of Spain and France, offers a quieter alternative to the Ocean Drive crowds. Browse local boutiques, dine at sidewalk cafes, and discover why this 1920s architectural gem remains a favorite among locals and artists.

## 5. Visit the Leonardo da Vinci Exhibition

**Frost Science Museum** | Through April 2026

![Shirtless Man With Sunglasses And Tumble 2](/images/blog/2026/03/6-things-to-do-in-miami-beach-this-weekend/shirtless-man-with-sunglasses-and-tumble-2.webp)


"Leonardo da Vinci – 500 Years of Genius" explores the Renaissance master's multidisciplinary legacy with interactive displays, rare artifacts, and life-size replicas of his revolutionary machines. Perfect for a midday escape from the beach heat.

## 6. Kayak Through Oleta River State Park

**3400 NE 163rd Street, North Miami Beach** | $6 per vehicle

Just 15 minutes from Miami Beach, Florida's largest urban park offers kayaking through mangrove tunnels, paddleboarding on calm estuaries, and 15 miles of biking trails. Rent equipment at the Oleta River Outdoor Center and spot manatees, dolphins, and exotic birds.
