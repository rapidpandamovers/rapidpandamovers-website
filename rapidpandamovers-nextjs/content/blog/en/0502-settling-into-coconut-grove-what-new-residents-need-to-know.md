Thinking about moving to Coconut Grove? You're considering Miami's oldest continuously inhabited neighborhood, known for its bohemian spirit, lush tropical canopy, and artistic community. This guide provides essential information to help you plan your relocation.

## About Coconut Grove

Coconut Grove, affectionately called "The Grove," is Miami's oldest neighborhood with a history dating back to the 1800s. This eclectic community of approximately 20,000 residents maintains its artistic, bohemian character despite ongoing development.

![A Tranquil Tropical Scene Featuring A Ru 3](/images/blog/2026/07/settling-into-coconut-grove-what-new-residents-need-to-know/a-tranquil-tropical-scene-featuring-a-ru-3.webp)


### Demographics at a Glance

- **Population**: Approximately 19,000-20,000 residents
- **Median Age**: 40 years
- **Character**: Artistic, bohemian, affluent
- **Housing Costs**: Median $2,156/month
- **Average Home Value**: $628,600

### The Grove's Character
- Tree-lined streets with tropical canopy
- Historic buildings and modern developments
- Artistic community and galleries
- Waterfront lifestyle
- Walkable village center

## Real Estate Market

Coconut Grove offers diverse housing from cottages to waterfront estates.

### Price Ranges
- **Entry-level condos**: Starting around $400,000
- **Single-family homes**: $800,000-$2 million
- **Waterfront properties**: $2-10 million+
- **Historic cottages**: Varies widely

### Property Types
- Historic bungalows and cottages
- Modern high-rise condos
- Waterfront estates
- Townhouse communities

### Gentrification Impact
While preserving its character, the Grove has seen significant development, with some longtime artist communities replaced by upscale retail and residential projects.

## Moving Tips for Coconut Grove

### Street Considerations
- Narrow historic streets
- Large banyan trees limit access
- Limited parking in village center
- Permit requirements for some areas

### Historic Property Rules
- Preservation guidelines may apply
- Tree protection ordinances
- Facade considerations
- HOA restrictions in some areas

### Best Moving Times
- **Weekday mornings** before traffic
- **Avoid festival weekends**
- **Art festival week** is extremely congested
- **School drop-off times** affect traffic

### Loading Challenges
- Street parking limited
- Some streets too narrow for large trucks
- Coordinate with neighbors
- Consider smaller truck/shuttle approach
