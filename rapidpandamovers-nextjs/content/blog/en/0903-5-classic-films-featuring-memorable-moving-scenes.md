Moving is such a universal experience that Hollywood has explored it countless times. Perfect for a February movie night after your winter move, these films capture the stress, humor, and emotion of relocation in ways that feel all too familiar.

## 1. "The Money Pit" (1986)

Tom Hanks and Shelley Long buy a mansion that falls apart around them. This comedy perfectly captures the chaos that can follow a move.

![Aerial Shot Of A Crowded Urban Parking L 2](/images/blog/2028/02/5-classic-films-featuring-memorable-moving-scenes/aerial-shot-of-a-crowded-urban-parking-l-2.webp)


## 2. "Moving" (1988)

Richard Pryor's comedy about a cross-country relocation gone wrong captures every moving nightmare imaginable.

## 3. "The Incredibles" (2004)

A superhero family forced into witness protection and a new city shows how moves affect families, animated style.

## 4. "Up" (2009)

The ultimate statement about home attachment. A man floats his house to a new location rather than leave it behind.

![Three Men Loading Wooden Boxes On A Truc 3](/images/blog/2028/02/5-classic-films-featuring-memorable-moving-scenes/three-men-loading-wooden-boxes-on-a-truc-3.webp)


## 5. "The Pursuit of Happyness" (2006)

Will Smith's struggle through homelessness shows the emotional weight of having no stable home.
