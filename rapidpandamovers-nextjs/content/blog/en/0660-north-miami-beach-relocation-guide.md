North Miami Beach delivers waterfront living at prices that won't empty your bank account. Wedged between Aventura's luxury towers and North Miami's urban core, NMB offers a diverse, international community with quick access to Oleta River State Park and the beaches of Sunny Isles.

## Why Choose North Miami Beach?

Affordability drives most buyers here. Compared to neighboring Aventura, where condos start well above market average, North Miami Beach offers similar proximity to the water at significantly lower price points. The tradeoff: older housing stock and less walkable commercial areas. But for value-conscious buyers who prioritize location, NMB delivers.

![Capture The Serene And Dramatic Sunset O 3](/images/blog/2028/07/north-miami-beach-relocation-guide/capture-the-serene-and-dramatic-sunset-o-3.webp)


The international community here includes significant Russian, Haitian, and Latin American populations. This diversity shows in the restaurants, markets, and cultural events throughout the city.

### Location and Accessibility

I-95 runs along the western edge of the city. From the highway, downtown Miami is 25 minutes south. Fort Lauderdale is 25 minutes north. Miami Beach is 15 minutes via the 163rd Street causeway.

Biscayne Boulevard (US-1) runs north-south through the city center, providing the main commercial corridor. The beaches of Sunny Isles are a 10-minute drive east. Oleta River State Park, one of the largest urban parks in Florida, sits on the city's southern border.

### Community and Lifestyle

Oleta River State Park anchors the recreational options. Kayaking, paddleboarding, mountain biking trails, and beach access draw residents year-round. The Intracoastal Mall on 163rd Street handles shopping needs. The diverse restaurant scene ranges from Russian delis to Caribbean jerk joints to Brazilian steakhouses.

## Neighborhoods to Consider

**Eastern Shores**: The premium address in NMB. Waterfront homes with private docks line the Intracoastal. Prices here approach Aventura levels, but you get single-family homes instead of condos.

**Keystone Point**: Bay-front community with boating access. Mid-century homes with larger lots. Popular with families who want water access without Eastern Shores prices.

**Skylake**: Affordable single-family homes on the western side of the city. Near Oleta River State Park. Good value for first-time buyers.

**163rd Street corridor**: Condo and apartment options near the Intracoastal Mall. Most walkable area in NMB with shopping and restaurants accessible on foot.

### Choosing Your Perfect Spot

Consider these factors:

- **Proximity to work and schools**: Factor in your daily commute
- **Local amenities**: Parks, shopping, dining, and entertainment options
- **Property types**: Single-family homes, condos, townhouses, or apartments
- **Community vibe**: Family-oriented, young professional, or mixed demographics

## Moving to North Miami Beach

Most NMB moves are straightforward. Single-family homes have driveway access. Condo buildings along the 163rd Street corridor require elevator coordination, but nothing as complicated as Brickell or Aventura towers.

![Colorful Scene Capturing Miami Beach S H 1](/images/blog/2028/07/north-miami-beach-relocation-guide/colorful-scene-capturing-miami-beach-s-h-1.webp)


### Timing Your Move

- **Traffic**: Avoid Biscayne Boulevard during rush hours (8-9 AM, 5-7 PM)
- **Weather**: Summer storms hit 3-5 PM; start early
- **Parking**: Street parking is generally available in residential areas

## Essential Services Near You

**Healthcare**: Aventura Hospital is 10 minutes north. Jackson North Medical Center is 10 minutes south. Urgent care options line Biscayne Boulevard.

**Schools**: North Miami Beach Senior High, Ojus Elementary, Highland Oaks Elementary. Private options include Talmudic Academy and several charter schools.

**Shopping**: Intracoastal Mall, Aventura Mall (10 minutes), Publix, Whole Foods, and international markets throughout.

**Recreation**: Oleta River State Park, Greynolds Park, and easy beach access to Sunny Isles and Haulover.
