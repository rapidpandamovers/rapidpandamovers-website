Living near South Miami means easy access to some of Florida's best beaches, perfect for fall outings. Whether you're looking for family-friendly shores, water sports, or quiet relaxation, here are the coastal destinations worth your time.

## 1. Matheson Hammock Park Beach

**Address:** 9610 Old Cutler Rd, Miami, FL 33156

![A Man Walks Along The Sandy Shore Of Sou 1](/images/blog/2027/11/5-best-beaches-near-south-miami-for-a-day-trip/a-man-walks-along-the-sandy-shore-of-sou-1.webp)


Just 10 minutes from South Miami via Old Cutler Road, this 630-acre park features a man-made atoll pool naturally flushed by Biscayne Bay tides. The calm, shallow waters make it safe for swimmers of all ages. Rent kayaks, paddleboards, or canoes at the marina. Pro tip: arrive before 10 AM on weekends to snag parking, especially during cooler fall months when locals flock here.

## 2. Crandon Park Beach

**Address:** 6747 Crandon Blvd, Key Biscayne, FL 33149

A locals' favorite across the Rickenbacker Causeway, about 20 minutes from South Miami. Features umbrella rentals, a vintage carousel, skatepark, and beachside restaurants like the Oasis for Cuban coffee and sandwiches. The calm, shallow waters stretch out for yards, making it ideal for families with young children. Parking is $8 per vehicle.

## 3. Bill Baggs Cape Florida State Park

**Address:** 1200 Crandon Blvd, Key Biscayne, FL 33149

Home to the historic Cape Florida Lighthouse, built in 1825 and the oldest standing structure in Miami-Dade County. Climb the 109 steps for panoramic views of Biscayne Bay and the Miami skyline. The wide, sandy beaches here consistently rank among the nation's best. Kayak rentals and paved cycling trails make it easy to explore. State park entry is $8 per vehicle.

## 4. Virginia Key Beach Park

**Address:** 4020 Virginia Beach Dr, Miami, FL 33149

![A Scenic View Of Ocean Beach San Francis 3](/images/blog/2027/11/5-best-beaches-near-south-miami-for-a-day-trip/a-scenic-view-of-ocean-beach-san-francis-3.webp)


A historic beach with a rich civil rights history. During segregation, this was the only Miami beach open to Black residents. Today, it offers calm waters, a vintage carousel, and mountain bike trails winding through coastal hammock. Much quieter than South Beach, it's perfect for families who want space to spread out. The beach hosts occasional outdoor movie nights during cooler months.

## 5. Hobie Island Beach Park

**Address:** Rickenbacker Causeway, Key Biscayne, FL

Miami's go-to spot for water sports, located along the Rickenbacker Causeway with shallow, protected waters ideal for windsurfing, paddleboarding, and kayaking. One of the few dog-friendly beaches in Miami-Dade, so bring your pup. Free parking along the causeway, though spots fill up fast on weekends. The views of downtown Miami from here are hard to beat.
