Most people know not to pack grandma's china with the cleaning supplies, but moving trucks have stricter rules than you might expect. Professional moving companies are legally prohibited from transporting certain items, and breaking these rules can void your insurance, damage other belongings, or create serious safety hazards.

Here are the nine categories that should never go on the moving truck, whether you're relocating from Brickell to Coral Gables or heading out of state entirely.

## 1. Hazardous Materials

Gasoline, propane tanks, paint thinner, pool chemicals, and aerosol cans pose serious fire risks inside an enclosed truck. Florida law and DOT regulations prohibit movers from transporting these materials. Our crews in Kendall and Doral regularly ask customers to set aside garage chemicals before loading day.

![Close Up Of A Large Yellow Dump Truck Us 4](/images/blog/2029/04/9-things-to-keep-off-the-moving-truck/close-up-of-a-large-yellow-dump-truck-us-4.webp)


**What to do instead:** Use up what you can, properly dispose of the rest at Miami-Dade hazardous waste facilities, and buy new supplies at your destination.

## 2. Explosives and Ammunition

Fireworks, ammunition, flares, and anything explosive are strictly prohibited. This includes hunting supplies and items left over from Fourth of July celebrations. Even spent shell casings in your garage drawer can cause problems during an inspection.

**What to do instead:** Transport firearms and ammunition personally in your vehicle following Florida's transport laws, or contact a licensed dealer for temporary storage.

## 3. Perishable Foods

That freezer full of Publix steaks will not survive a move. Even local Miami relocations can take several hours with loading and unloading time. Refrigerated items will spoil, frozen foods will thaw, and the mess becomes your problem.

**What to do instead:** Host a barbecue for friends helping you pack, donate unopened items to local food banks in Wynwood or Little Havana, or time your grocery shopping so you run out naturally before moving day.

## 4. Plants (for Long-Distance Moves)

Moving from Miami Beach to another state? Florida's agricultural inspection stations may confiscate your plants. Regulations exist to prevent pests and diseases from spreading across state lines. Even local moves can stress plants, especially tropical varieties that do not tolerate temperature changes.

![Red Trailer Truck At A Warehouse Loading 1](/images/blog/2029/04/9-things-to-keep-off-the-moving-truck/red-trailer-truck-at-a-warehouse-loading-1.webp)


**What to do instead:** Gift plants to Coconut Grove neighbors, take cuttings you can regrow, or transport them yourself in your air-conditioned car.

## 5. Pets and Living Creatures

This should go without saying, but your dog, cat, fish, or iguana cannot ride in the back of a moving truck. Beyond being illegal and inhumane, the enclosed space lacks climate control. Miami summer temperatures can make truck interiors reach 140 degrees.

**What to do instead:** Keep pets with you, use a pet transport service, or arrange for a friend to watch them on moving day. For fish tanks, drain them and transport the fish separately in bags or containers.

## 6. Irreplaceable Documents

Passports, birth certificates, Social Security cards, property deeds, wills, and financial records belong with you personally. These items are impossible to replace quickly and could be needed at any moment during your move.

**What to do instead:** Pack a dedicated folder or fireproof box that stays in your personal vehicle. Include recent tax returns, medical records, and insurance policies.

## 7. Jewelry and Cash

High-value items like jewelry, coin collections, and cash are not covered by standard moving insurance. Even with the most trustworthy crew, boxes get shuffled, and small items can disappear into the chaos of moving day.

**What to do instead:** Wear valuable jewelry or carry it in a bag you keep with you. Consider a safe deposit box for items you do not need immediate access to.

## 8. Medications

Prescription medications should never be packed in boxes that might get lost, delayed, or stuck in a hot truck. Florida's heat can degrade medication effectiveness, and being without essential prescriptions creates real health risks.

![Two Men Load Construction Materials Onto 3](/images/blog/2029/04/9-things-to-keep-off-the-moving-truck/two-men-load-construction-materials-onto-3.webp)


**What to do instead:** Keep all medications in your personal bag with at least a week's supply accessible. Bring copies of prescriptions in case you need refills during the transition.

## 9. Pressurized Containers

Fire extinguishers, scuba tanks, propane tanks for your grill, and compressed air cans can explode under pressure changes or temperature fluctuations. A truck sitting in the Aventura sun becomes a pressure cooker.

**What to do instead:** Have tanks professionally emptied before moving, transport fire extinguishers in your vehicle, and dispose of aerosols that cannot be transported.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What happens if prohibited items are found on the truck?

The moving company may refuse to transport your shipment, or you could void your insurance coverage. In some cases, there are legal penalties for transporting hazardous materials improperly.

### Can I transport these items in my own vehicle?

Most of these items can travel in your personal car, except hazardous materials which may require special disposal. Always check state regulations for firearms and ammunition transport.

### Will movers check my boxes for prohibited items?

Professional movers will ask about hazardous materials and may refuse obviously problematic items. However, they typically do not inspect sealed boxes, which is why it is your responsibility to pack correctly.
