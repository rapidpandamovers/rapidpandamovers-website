Homestead sits at the southern edge of Miami-Dade County, about 35 miles from Downtown Miami. The city of roughly 80,000 residents offers something unusual for South Florida: affordable housing with actual yard space. If you're considering a move here, here's what you need to know.

## Housing Costs and What You'll Find

Homestead's median home price runs significantly lower than Miami's average. Single-family homes with 3-4 bedrooms on quarter-acre lots are common, a rarity in more urban parts of the county.

![Loc Peaceful Riverside View With Palm Trees  2](/images/blog/2028/10/making-homestead-your-new-home-a-practical-guide/loc-peaceful-riverside-view-with-palm-trees--2.webp)


Rental prices also trend lower. A 2-bedroom apartment averages $1,800-2,200 per month compared to $2,500+ in areas like Kendall or Doral. This affordability attracts young families, first-time homebuyers, and people who work remotely but want more space.

The tradeoff is distance. Homestead requires longer commutes to central Miami, though the extension of US-1 and the Turnpike provides relatively direct routes.

## Neighborhoods Worth Exploring

### Downtown Homestead

The historic core along Krome Avenue has undergone revitalization in recent years. You'll find locally-owned restaurants, shops, and the weekly Homestead Championship Rodeo grounds nearby. Housing here includes older single-family homes and some townhouse developments.

### Redland

The Redland agricultural district north and west of downtown features larger properties, often with fruit trees, nurseries, or horse facilities. If you want an acre or more, this is where to look. Schools in the Redland area include Redland Elementary and South Dade Middle.

### Leisure City

This unincorporated area just north of Homestead proper offers some of the most affordable housing in South Florida. Proximity to Homestead-Miami Speedway means noise during race events, but prices reflect this.

### Silver Palm

A master-planned community with newer construction, Silver Palm offers HOA-managed neighborhoods with community amenities. This area appeals to families seeking turnkey homes without the work of maintaining older properties.

## The Commute Reality

Let's be direct about commuting from Homestead:

- **To Kendall**: 25-35 minutes without traffic, 45-60 during rush hour
- **To Downtown Miami**: 45-55 minutes without traffic, 75-90 during peak
- **To Miami International Airport**: 40-50 minutes
- **To Key Largo**: 30 minutes south on US-1

The Turnpike Extension and US-1 are your main routes. Rush hour traffic heading north on the Turnpike backs up significantly at the Florida City toll plaza. Many Homestead residents adjust work schedules to avoid the 7-9 AM and 4-7 PM windows.

## Schools in Homestead

Miami-Dade County Public Schools serves Homestead. Notable schools include:

- **Homestead Senior High School**: Comprehensive high school with magnet programs
- **South Dade Senior High School**: Located in the Homestead area with automotive and agricultural programs
- **Charter schools**: Several charter options including Renaissance Charter School

Private school options are more limited than in northern Miami-Dade, but Our Lady of the Holy Rosary Catholic School provides a local alternative.

## What's Nearby

Homestead's location offers unique access:

- **Everglades National Park**: 15 minutes to the park entrance
- **Biscayne National Park**: 20 minutes to the Dante Fascell Visitor Center
- **Homestead-Miami Speedway**: NASCAR and IndyCar races draw large crowds several times yearly
- **Schnebly Redland's Winery**: Local winery with tropical fruit wines
- **Robert Is Here Fruit Stand**: Legendary produce stand and smoothie spot

For shopping, the Homestead Pavilion provides big-box retail, while the Florida Keys Outlet Marketplace in Florida City offers discount shopping.

## What to Expect After Moving to Homestead

**First week:** Establish your shopping routes. Learn which Publix or Walmart becomes your regular stop, find a gas station near your commute path, and locate the nearest urgent care. The distance from central Miami becomes tangible when you need something specific.

**First month:** The pace of life feels different from northern Miami-Dade. Neighbors wave from their yards. Traffic moves slower on local roads. You'll adjust to driving slightly longer distances for most errands and appointments.

**First year:** Most newcomers develop a hybrid routine, handling daily needs in Homestead while making occasional trips to Kendall or Miami for specialty shopping, dining, or entertainment. Weekend trips to the Everglades and Keys become natural.

## Benefits of Living in Homestead

- **Affordable housing** with actual yard space uncommon in Miami-Dade
- **Lower cost of living** overall compared to central Miami
- **Quick access to Everglades and Biscayne National Parks** for outdoor activities
- **Agricultural character** with fruit stands, nurseries, and open spaces
- **Strong community feel** in established neighborhoods
- **Growing downtown** with local restaurants and shops
- **Distance from urban congestion** while remaining in Miami-Dade County
