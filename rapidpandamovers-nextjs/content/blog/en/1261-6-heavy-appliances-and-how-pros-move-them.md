# 6 Heavy Appliances and How Pros Move Them

Heavy appliances are the items most likely to cause injuries, property damage, and broken equipment during a move. Trying to muscle a 350-pound refrigerator down a flight of stairs or wrestle a cast-iron range through a narrow doorway without the right technique ends badly. Our crews move these appliances daily across Miami-Dade, and each one requires specific equipment and handling. Here's how the pros do it.

## 1. French-Door Refrigerator (250-400 lbs)

The French-door fridge is the most popular style in Miami homes, and also the widest. With doors and handles, these units can measure 38-40 inches across, which is wider than many interior doorways in older Miami homes.

![A Clean Organized Laundry Room Featuring 4](/images/blog/2029/12/6-heavy-appliances-and-how-pros-move-them/a-clean-organized-laundry-room-featuring-4.webp)


**How we move it:** After defrosting and prepping (removing shelves, disconnecting water, securing doors), we tilt the fridge back onto an appliance dolly rated for 800+ pounds. Two ratchet straps lock it to the dolly, one at the top third and one at the midsection. The person at the bottom controls speed and direction while the person at the top stabilizes. For tight doorways, we remove the fridge doors at the hinges, which takes about five minutes and recovers 3-4 inches of width. Floor protection goes down before the dolly moves an inch.

## 2. Front-Load Washer (150-250 lbs)

Front-loading washers are dense. The concrete counterweights inside that keep the drum stable during spin cycles add 50-80 pounds to the unit. Without transit bolts installed, the drum swings freely and can crack the internal housing or damage the shock absorbers during transport.

**How we move it:** Transit bolts go in first. These metal bolts lock the drum to the frame so it can't move. We disconnect water hoses, drain residual water from the pump filter, and tape hoses to the back. The washer goes onto the appliance dolly the same way as a fridge, strapped at two points. In many Miami condos where laundry hookups are in a closet, getting the washer out requires removing the closet doors and sometimes the bi-fold track. We reassemble everything at the new location after installation.

## 3. Gas Range (130-250 lbs)

Gas ranges combine heavy cast iron grates, a solid steel body, and a gas connection that requires professional handling. The weight varies significantly. A basic 30-inch gas range weighs around 130 pounds. A professional-style 48-inch range from a brand like Viking or Wolf can exceed 400 pounds.

![A Cozy Laundry Closet With A Dryer And V 1](/images/blog/2029/12/6-heavy-appliances-and-how-pros-move-them/a-cozy-laundry-closet-with-a-dryer-and-v-1.webp)


**How we move it:** After a licensed technician disconnects the gas line and caps the pipe, we remove all racks, grates, drip pans, and knobs. These get packed separately. The range goes on the dolly in the upright position. For pro-style ranges that are too heavy for a standard dolly, we use a four-wheel furniture dolly with a low platform. Two or three crew members lift and position the range onto the platform, then strap it down for the roll to the truck. In neighborhoods like Coconut Grove and Pinecrest where gourmet kitchens are common, we move these heavy ranges regularly.

## 4. Chest Freezer (100-300 lbs)

Chest freezers are awkward. They're wide, low to the ground, and there's no good grip point on the smooth exterior. The lid wants to swing open. And the compressor is sensitive to tilting.

**How we move it:** We strap the lid closed with ratchet straps running over the top and under the bottom. The freezer goes onto a flat furniture dolly rather than a standard appliance dolly since the wide, flat shape sits more stably on a platform. We strap it down and keep it as level as possible during the entire move. Through doorways, we lift it over thresholds rather than rolling over them to avoid tipping. Many Miami families keep their chest freezer in the garage, which means we're rolling across the driveway and loading up a ramp into the truck.

## 5. Stacked Washer-Dryer (300-400 lbs combined)

Stacked units are common in Miami condos and apartments where floor space is limited. Moving them presents a unique challenge: do you separate the stack and move each unit individually, or move them as one unit?

**How we move it:** In most cases, we separate the stack. The stacking kit is removed, and each unit moves independently on its own dolly. Moving a stacked unit as one piece (6+ feet tall, 350+ pounds, with a high center of gravity) is risky and rarely worth it. The exception is a single-unit combo washer-dryer that can't be separated. Those we strap securely to an appliance dolly and move carefully, with extra attention to balance. Getting a stacked pair through the hallways of a Brickell high-rise takes patience and careful cornering.

## 6. Built-In Double Wall Oven (200-300 lbs)

Double wall ovens are installed at chest height inside a tall cabinet, which makes them physically difficult to extract. You're lifting 200+ pounds from an elevated position while working in the confined space of a kitchen.

![Hands Washing A Fresh Red Apple Under Ru 2](/images/blog/2029/12/6-heavy-appliances-and-how-pros-move-them/hands-washing-a-fresh-red-apple-under-ru-2.webp)


**How we move it:** After turning off the breaker and disconnecting the wiring, we remove the mounting screws through the oven's side flanges. The oven slides out on its built-in rails, but it gets heavy fast once it clears the cabinet support. Two crew members receive the weight while a third guides it from inside the cabinet opening. We lower it onto a padded furniture dolly and strap it for transport. At the new home, the reverse process requires the same coordination. Homes in Coral Gables and Key Biscayne frequently have these high-end built-in setups, and we handle them with the same care as the cabinetry surrounding them.
