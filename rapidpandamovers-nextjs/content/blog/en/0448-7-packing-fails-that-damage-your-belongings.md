Whether you're moving from a Brickell high-rise to a Coral Gables family home or relocating to a Coconut Grove bungalow, packing mistakes can turn an exciting move into a costly headache. Here are the seven most common packing fails we see in Miami-Dade and how to avoid them.

## 1. Overpacking Large Boxes

A huge box of books is impossible to lift safely. Use small boxes for heavy items, always. Your back and your movers will thank you.

![A Collection Of Large Cardboard Boxes La 4](/images/blog/2026/04/7-packing-fails-that-damage-your-belongings/a-collection-of-large-cardboard-boxes-la-4.webp)


## 2. Insufficient Cushioning

One layer of newspaper isn't enough. Fragile items need individual wrapping, padding on all sides, and zero room to shift.

## 3. Leaving Hollow Items Empty

Hollow items like vases and cups are fragile. Fill them with packing paper or soft materials for structural support.

![A Man Labels Cardboard Boxes For Moving  3](/images/blog/2026/04/7-packing-fails-that-damage-your-belongings/a-man-labels-cardboard-boxes-for-moving--3.webp)


## 4. Using Old, Weak Boxes

Old boxes lose structural integrity. In Miami's humidity, cardboard absorbs moisture quickly, making weak boxes even weaker. Wet boxes are disasters waiting to happen. Use fresh, sturdy boxes from a moving supply store.

## 5. Improper Dish Packing

Never stack plates horizontally. Pack them on edge, like records, with padding between each one. This prevents cracking from weight.

## 6. Forgetting to Seal Liquids

Shampoo, cleaning products, and cooking oils leak during moves. In South Florida's summer heat, pressure changes in a hot moving truck can force lids open. Tape lids shut and place in sealed plastic bags to prevent disasters.

![Hands Carefully Packing Glassware Into A 5](/images/blog/2026/04/7-packing-fails-that-damage-your-belongings/hands-carefully-packing-glassware-into-a-5.webp)


## 7. Not Reinforcing Box Bottoms

Heavy items need tape across the bottom seam and edges. Many boxes fail when lifted because the bottom wasn't secured properly.
