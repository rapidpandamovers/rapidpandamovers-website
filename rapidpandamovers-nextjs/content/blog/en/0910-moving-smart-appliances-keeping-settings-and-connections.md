# Moving Smart Appliances: Keeping Settings and Connections

Modern kitchens and laundry rooms are full of Wi-Fi-connected appliances. Smart refrigerators with touchscreens, washers you control from your phone, ovens that sync with recipe apps, and dryers that send notifications when the cycle is done. Moving these appliances means more than just disconnecting water lines and power cords. You also need to preserve the digital settings, transfer the smart home connections, and reconfigure network access at the new address. Here's how to handle the tech side of your appliance move.

## Why Smart Appliance Settings Matter

Over time, you customize your smart appliances. The refrigerator learns your preferred temperature zones. The washer stores your custom wash cycles. The oven saves favorite recipes and cooking presets. The dryer remembers your preferred dryness settings. Losing all of that means reprogramming everything from scratch, which is time-consuming and annoying.

![A Cozy Laundry Closet With A Dryer And V 3](/images/blog/2028/02/moving-smart-appliances-keeping-settings-and-connections/a-cozy-laundry-closet-with-a-dryer-and-v-3.webp)


Beyond convenience, some smart appliances have diagnostic data stored internally. Samsung SmartThings, LG ThinQ, and GE SmartHQ apps track usage history and alert you to maintenance needs. Losing that data means losing your service history.

## Before the Move: Document Everything

### Screenshot Your Settings

Open each appliance's companion app and take screenshots of:
- Custom cycles and presets
- Temperature settings for each zone
- Notification preferences
- Connected accounts and integrations
- Scheduled operations (like a washer set to run at off-peak hours)
- Firmware version numbers

### Note Your Smart Home Integrations

If your appliances connect to a smart home hub (Amazon Alexa, Google Home, Apple HomeKit, or Samsung SmartThings), list which appliances are linked to which platform. You'll need to re-add them at the new home.

### Check Cloud Storage

Most smart appliance brands store your settings in the cloud, linked to your user account. This means your preferences should transfer automatically when you log back into the app from the new location. Confirm this by checking the manufacturer's support page for your model. If settings are cloud-synced, the transition is simpler. If they're stored locally on the appliance, you'll need to re-enter them manually.

## Disconnecting Smart Appliances

### Wi-Fi Disconnection

![Hands Washing A Fresh Red Apple Under Ru 4](/images/blog/2028/02/moving-smart-appliances-keeping-settings-and-connections/hands-washing-a-fresh-red-apple-under-ru-4.webp)


Before unplugging any smart appliance, open its app and disconnect it from your home Wi-Fi network. On most apps, this is under Settings > Network or Device > Remove. Some brands (like Samsung) recommend removing the device from the SmartThings app entirely and re-adding it at the new home. Others (like LG) keep the device registered to your account and just need a new Wi-Fi connection.

If you skip this step and just unplug the appliance, it stays paired to your old network. At the new home, it may refuse to connect to the new Wi-Fi until you factory reset the network module, which erases locally stored settings.

### USB and Bluetooth Connections

Some smart fridges use Bluetooth for initial setup or USB ports for firmware updates. Note which connections your appliances use. If there's a USB drive plugged into the fridge (some models use them for photo displays or music), remove it and pack it separately.

### Smart Home Hub Removal

In your Alexa, Google Home, or HomeKit app, remove each appliance from its current room assignment. Don't delete your account or reset the hub, just remove the individual device listings. You'll re-add them at the new home once everything is connected to the new Wi-Fi.

## During the Move

Smart appliances move the same way as their non-smart counterparts physically. The electronics are built into the control panels and designed to handle normal transportation. The main concern is protecting the touchscreen displays on smart refrigerators. These screens scratch easily and cost $300-$800 to replace. Cover the screen with a microfiber cloth taped gently in place, then a layer of cardboard taped over that. Don't use bubble wrap directly on touchscreens since the bubbles can press buttons or leave marks.

## Reconnecting at the New Home

### Set Up Your Wi-Fi First

![Man Pouring Liquid Detergent Into A Stac 2](/images/blog/2028/02/moving-smart-appliances-keeping-settings-and-connections/man-pouring-liquid-detergent-into-a-stac-2.webp)


Before reconnecting any smart appliance, make sure your internet service and Wi-Fi router are up and running at the new home. In Miami, Xfinity and AT&T are the major ISPs, and installation can take a few days if it's a new connection. Plan to have internet ready on move-in day or the day after.

### Reconnect Each Appliance to Wi-Fi

Plug in the appliance and power it on. Open the companion app (SmartThings, ThinQ, SmartHQ, etc.) and follow the app's prompts to connect the appliance to your new Wi-Fi network. Most apps walk you through this step by step.

Make sure you're on the 2.4GHz band, not 5GHz. Most smart appliances only support 2.4GHz Wi-Fi. If your router combines both bands under one network name, you may need to temporarily split them during setup or stand close to the router during pairing.

### Restore Settings

If your settings were cloud-synced, they should appear automatically once the appliance reconnects and syncs with the server. If not, refer to your screenshots and re-enter your preferences manually.

### Re-Add to Smart Home

Open your Alexa, Google Home, or HomeKit app and add each appliance to the appropriate room in the new home. Set up any automations or routines that reference the appliances (like "Alexa, set the fridge to party mode").
