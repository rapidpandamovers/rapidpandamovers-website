Key Biscayne sits on a barrier island just off the Miami coast, connected to the mainland by the Rickenbacker Causeway. The 1.2-mile stretch of highway separates this quiet village from the bustle of Brickell and downtown, creating a retreat that feels worlds away from typical Miami living.

## What Makes Key Biscayne Different

About 14,000 people live on this 2-mile-long island. Crandon Park and Bill Baggs Cape Florida State Park take up nearly half the land, leaving the residential areas compact and walkable. Beach access is everywhere. The village center has everything you need daily, so many residents rarely cross the causeway except for work.

### The Commute Reality

The Rickenbacker Causeway toll runs about $2.25 each way with SunPass. Morning rush hour into Brickell takes 15-20 minutes. Afternoon return can stretch longer, especially during tourist season. Many residents with downtown jobs consider the toll and commute time worth the trade-off for island living.

### Schools and Families

Key Biscayne K-8 Center serves the island's elementary and middle school students. The public school consistently ranks among the top in Miami-Dade County. For high school, students typically attend Coral Gables Senior High or choose private options on the mainland.

## Housing Options on the Island

### The Village Center

The blocks around Crandon Boulevard offer walkability that is rare in South Florida. Restaurants, shops, and the Winn-Dixie grocery store are all within walking or biking distance. Condo buildings dominate this area, with a mix of older and recently renovated properties.

### Oceanfront Properties

The eastern side of the island faces the Atlantic. Single-family homes here command premium prices for direct beach access. Hurricane considerations matter more on this side. Flood insurance and impact windows are standard.

### Bay Side and Harbor Drive

The western side overlooks Biscayne Bay with views of the Miami skyline. Properties here tend toward larger lots with private docks. The sunsets are spectacular.

## Moving to Key Biscayne

The causeway adds a layer of logistics to Key Biscayne moves. Moving trucks pay the same toll as cars, but the bridge has weight limits that affect large trucks. Building management in condo communities controls elevator access strictly.
