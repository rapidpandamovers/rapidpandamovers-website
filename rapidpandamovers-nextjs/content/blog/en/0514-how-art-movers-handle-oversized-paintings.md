# How Art Movers Handle Oversized Paintings

A painting that spans eight feet across a living room wall makes a statement. Getting it out the door, into a truck, and safely to a new home makes a headache. Oversized paintings are some of the trickiest pieces we move in Miami, whether they're contemporary canvases from a Wynwood gallery or commissioned murals hanging in Design District penthouses. Standard packing methods don't apply, and the logistics require planning that starts well before moving day.

## What Counts as Oversized?

In the art moving world, any painting larger than about 48 by 72 inches qualifies as oversized. At that size, standard mirror boxes and picture boxes won't fit. The piece is too large for one person to handle safely, and doorways, elevators, and staircases become obstacles rather than passageways. Many of the oversized pieces we move in Miami measure six to ten feet in at least one dimension, and some exceed that.

## Measuring the Route, Not Just the Painting

Before moving day, our team surveys the path from where the painting hangs to where the truck will park. We measure every doorway, hallway turn, elevator opening, and stairwell landing. A 96-inch painting can't make a 90-degree turn in a 36-inch hallway, so we need to know that in advance and plan an alternate route.

![An Elegant Art Gallery Room Featuring Cl 5](/images/blog/2027/08/how-art-movers-handle-oversized-paintings/an-elegant-art-gallery-room-featuring-cl-5.webp)


In many Miami high-rises, especially newer towers in Brickell and Edgewater, freight elevators are the only option. We coordinate with building management to reserve elevator time and confirm the interior dimensions. Some older buildings in Miami Beach have elevators that simply can't accommodate large artwork, and the piece has to travel via stairwell with extra crew members.

## Custom Crating for Large Paintings

Oversized paintings almost always need custom wooden crates. We build these on-site, measuring the piece and constructing a plywood shell lined with closed-cell foam. The painting sits inside the crate on foam blocks that keep it suspended, with no contact between the painted surface and the crate walls.

For extremely large or heavy pieces, we add internal bracing to prevent the crate from flexing during transport. Cross-members inside the crate distribute the load evenly so no single point bears too much weight.

## The Two-Person (or More) Carry

Oversized paintings are never a one-person job. Depending on the size and weight, we assign two to four crew members for the carry. Each person wears clean cotton gloves to prevent skin oils from transferring to the frame or canvas edges. We communicate constantly during the carry, calling out turns, steps, and clearances.

![Art Gallery With Classical Statues And P 2](/images/blog/2027/08/how-art-movers-handle-oversized-paintings/art-gallery-with-classical-statues-and-p-2.webp)


For pieces too large or heavy to hand-carry, we use specialized flat carts with pneumatic tires that absorb vibration. The painting rides upright on the cart, strapped in place, and gets wheeled to the truck along a path cleared of any obstacles.

## Loading and Securing in the Truck

Oversized paintings travel upright in the truck, never flat. Flat transport puts the full weight of the frame and glass on the center of the canvas, which causes sagging, stretching, and cracking. Our trucks have padded vertical racks and ratchet straps that hold large pieces firmly without applying pressure to the face of the painting.

We position oversized pieces against the front wall of the truck so they don't shift during braking. Smaller items get loaded around them, with padding between everything.
