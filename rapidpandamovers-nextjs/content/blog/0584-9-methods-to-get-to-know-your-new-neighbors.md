Strong community connections make a house feel like home. But building relationships in a new neighborhood takes intentional effort, especially after a January move. Here's how to become part of your new community.

## 1. Wave and Say Hello

Simple acknowledgment creates familiarity. Make eye contact, smile, and greet neighbors you see.

![Neighbors Community Friendship Checklist](/images/blog/2028/01/9-methods-to-get-to-know-your-new-neighbors/neighbors-community-friendship-checklist.webp)


## 2. Introduce Yourself Door-to-Door

Within the first week, knock on immediate neighbors' doors and introduce yourself briefly.

## 3. Join Local Social Media Groups

Nextdoor, Facebook community groups, and neighborhood apps connect you with local information and people.

## 4. Attend Community Events

Farmers markets, block parties, school events—show up where your community gathers.

![Neighbors Community Friendship Guide](/images/blog/2028/01/9-methods-to-get-to-know-your-new-neighbors/neighbors-community-friendship-guide.webp)


## 5. Support Local Businesses

Frequenting neighborhood shops and restaurants makes you a familiar face and supports your community.

## 6. Offer Help

Volunteer to water plants during vacations, offer to collect packages—small gestures build relationships.

## 7. Walk Pets at Consistent Times

Dog owners meet each other naturally. Regular walks at the same time create opportunities for repeated interactions.

![Neighbors Community Friendship Overview](/images/blog/2028/01/9-methods-to-get-to-know-your-new-neighbors/neighbors-community-friendship-overview.webp)


## 8. Host a Simple Gathering

Once settled, invite immediate neighbors for coffee or a casual get-together. Low-pressure invitations are often welcomed.

## 9. Join Neighborhood Organizations

HOA boards, neighborhood watch, school PTAs, or community gardens connect you with engaged neighbors who care about the community.
