Packing seems straightforward until something breaks. These common summer errors are responsible for the majority of moving damage we see. Avoid them and your June belongings will arrive safely.

## 1. Overpacking Large Boxes

A huge box of books is impossible to lift safely. Use small boxes for heavy items—always. Your back and your movers will thank you.

![Packing Mistakes Boxes Fragile Steps](/images/blog/2030/06/7-box-packing-mistakes-that-cost-you-money/packing-mistakes-boxes-fragile-steps.webp)


## 2. Insufficient Cushioning

One layer of newspaper isn't enough. Fragile items need individual wrapping, padding on all sides, and zero room to shift.

## 3. Leaving Hollow Items Empty

Hollow items like vases and cups are fragile. Fill them with packing paper or soft materials for structural support.

## 4. Using Old, Weak Boxes

Old boxes lose structural integrity. Wet boxes are disasters waiting to happen. Use fresh, sturdy boxes from a moving supply store.

## 5. Improper Dish Packing

Never stack plates horizontally. Pack them on edge, like records, with padding between each one. This prevents cracking from weight.

![Packing Mistakes Boxes Fragile Tips](/images/blog/2030/06/7-box-packing-mistakes-that-cost-you-money/packing-mistakes-boxes-fragile-tips.webp)


## 6. Not Labeling Boxes Clearly

Generic labels waste time. Write the room destination and a brief contents list on multiple sides of each box for easy identification.

## 7. Packing Liquids Without Sealing

Shampoo, cleaning supplies, and other liquids need their lids taped and then placed in sealed plastic bags. Temperature changes cause leaks.
