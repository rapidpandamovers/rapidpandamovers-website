Welcome to your guide for moving to Opa-locka! This historic city offers unique Moorish architecture, affordable housing, and a rich cultural heritage in northern Miami-Dade County.

## About Opa-locka

Founded in 1926 by aviation pioneer Glenn Curtiss, Opa-locka is a historic city known for its unique Moorish Revival architecture inspired by One Thousand and One Nights (Arabian Nights). With approximately 16,000 residents, this city offers affordable housing and convenient access to major employment centers.

![Beautiful Andalusian Style Archway With  1](/images/blog/2026/07/opa-locka-for-new-residents-a-complete-resource/beautiful-andalusian-style-archway-with--1.webp)


### Demographics and Community

- **Population**: Approximately 15,978 (2025 estimate)
- **Median Age**: 35.2 years
- **Diversity**: Hispanic (51.7%), Black/African American (43.1%), White (3.2%)
- **Median Household Income**: $31,680
- **Foreign-Born Residents**: 39.3%

### Cultural Heritage
Opa-locka has significant Caribbean and Latin American influences, with notable populations from the Bahamas, Haiti, Jamaica, Dominican Republic, and Cuba.

## Historic Architecture

### Moorish Revival Style
Opa-locka features the largest collection of Moorish Revival architecture in the Western Hemisphere:

- **City Hall**: Iconic domed building
- **Historic District**: Multiple buildings from the 1920s
- **Street Names**: Named after characters from Arabian Nights
- **Designed By**: Bernhardt E. Muller, architect

This unique architectural heritage makes Opa-locka a historically significant community unlike anywhere else in Florida.

## Location and Accessibility

### Transportation

Opa-locka benefits from excellent transportation access:

- **Opa-locka Airport**: General aviation airport within the city
- **Opa-locka Metrorail Station**: Direct rail access to Downtown Miami
- **NW 27th Avenue**: Major north-south corridor
- **Palmetto Expressway**: Nearby access
- **Interstate 95**: Easy access east

### Commute Times
- **Downtown Miami**: 20-25 minutes (by Metrorail faster)
- **Miami Beach**: 30-35 minutes
- **Fort Lauderdale**: 25-30 minutes
- **Miami International Airport**: 15-20 minutes
