Miami has long attracted the rich and famous, and their homes reflect the city's unique blend of tropical beauty and over-the-top luxury. From gated island communities to penthouse towers overlooking Biscayne Bay, celebrity real estate in South Florida makes headlines year after year.

## 1. Star Island Estates

Star Island sits in Biscayne Bay between Miami Beach and downtown Miami, accessible only by a guarded bridge. The 86-acre island has just 34 lots, making it one of the most exclusive addresses in the country. Madonna owned a waterfront estate here for over a decade. Gloria Estefan and her husband Emilio still call the island home, while former resident Diddy sold his mansion for $35 million in 2021.

The tight-knit community and single access point mean moving here requires advance coordination with island security. Trucks must be approved days ahead, and all deliveries face strict time windows.

![Luxury waterfront mansion on Star Island with private dock and palm trees](/images/blog/2024/12/5-celebrity-homes-in-miami-you-wont-believe/celebrity-mansion-miami-star-island.jpg)

## 2. Casa Casuarina (The Versace Mansion)

When Gianni Versace purchased this 1930 Mediterranean Revival mansion on Ocean Drive in 1992, he transformed it into a 23,000-square-foot palace complete with a 54-foot mosaic pool lined with 24-karat gold. After his death in 1997, the property changed hands several times before becoming a boutique hotel.

The mansion's ornate interiors, hand-painted ceilings, and museum-quality details make it one of the most photographed buildings in Miami Beach. Even as a hotel, it retains the Versace aesthetic that made it famous.

## 3. Fisher Island Private Estates

Fisher Island ranks as the wealthiest ZIP code in America, with a median income exceeding $2 million. The island is accessible only by private ferry, helicopter, or yacht. Oprah Winfrey, Boris Becker, and Julia Roberts have all owned property here.

![Luxury waterfront estate in Miami with palm trees and pristine landscaping](/images/blog/2024/12/5-celebrity-homes-in-miami-you-wont-believe/luxury-waterfront-estate-miami.jpg)

Moving to Fisher Island presents unique logistics challenges. All furniture, vehicles, and belongings must cross by ferry. Scheduling requires coordination with the island's management weeks in advance. Movers need clearance for every truck and crew member.

## 4. Coral Gables Historic Mansions

Coral Gables has attracted celebrities who prefer Mediterranean Revival architecture and tree-lined streets over waterfront flash. Sylvester Stallone owned a sprawling estate here for years. The neighborhood's strict zoning preserves its 1920s character while accommodating modern luxury features.

These historic homes often have narrow driveways, mature landscaping, and delicate architectural details that require careful handling during moves. Antique furniture and artwork need extra protection given the estates' museum-quality interiors.

## 5. Miami Beach High-Rise Penthouses

The tower condos along Collins Avenue and in Sunny Isles Beach have drawn celebrities seeking privacy with panoramic views. David and Victoria Beckham own a penthouse at One Thousand Museum, the Zaha Hadid-designed tower downtown. Lionel Messi purchased a unit in the same building.

![Modern luxury penthouse in Miami Beach with floor-to-ceiling windows and ocean views](/images/blog/2024/12/5-celebrity-homes-in-miami-you-wont-believe/miami-beach-penthouse-luxury.jpg)

High-rise moves require reserved elevator time, loading dock scheduling, and often overnight hours to avoid disrupting other residents. Buildings like One Thousand Museum have strict protocols that professional movers must follow.
