Before your movers arrive, there are certain items they simply cannot put on their truck. Federal regulations, insurance policies, and basic safety rules prohibit transporting specific materials. Knowing this list ahead of time saves you from last-minute scrambling on moving day.

Here are eight categories of items you will need to transport yourself or dispose of before your move.

## 1. Hazardous Materials

Gasoline, propane tanks, paint thinner, pool chemicals, and aerosol cans are fire hazards and illegal to transport on moving trucks. This includes partially full containers that Miami homeowners often forget about in their garages.

![A Cheerful Man Carries A Large Couch Acr 1](/images/blog/2026/09/8-items-banned-from-professional-moving-trucks/a-cheerful-man-carries-a-large-couch-acr-1.webp)


**What to do:** Use up what you can, give extras to neighbors, or bring them to Miami-Dade County's Household Hazardous Waste drop-off locations in Doral or South Dade.

## 2. Explosives and Ammunition

Fireworks, ammunition, flares, and anything explosive must be disposed of or transported in your personal vehicle with proper safety measures. Movers face federal fines for transporting these materials.

**What to do:** Transport ammunition yourself in your car's trunk, away from heat. For old fireworks or flares, contact your local fire department for safe disposal guidance.

## 3. Perishable Foods

Refrigerated and frozen items will not survive most moves, especially during Miami's hot and humid summers. Temperatures inside moving trucks can exceed 120 degrees Fahrenheit, spoiling food within hours.

**What to do:** Plan meals around your freezer contents in the weeks before moving. Donate unopened canned goods to Feeding South Florida or local food banks in Wynwood, Kendall, or Aventura.

## 4. Plants (for Long-Distance Moves)

Agricultural regulations prohibit transporting plants across state lines due to pest and disease concerns. Florida has strict inspection requirements to protect its $8 billion agriculture industry from invasive species.

**What to do:** Gift plants to friends, sell them on Facebook Marketplace, or take cuttings. For local moves within Miami-Dade, most movers can transport plants if the trip is under a few hours.

## 5. Pets and Living Creatures

Fish, dogs, cats, birds, and any living animals cannot travel on moving trucks. The dark, unventilated cargo space is dangerous for pets, and movers are not trained in animal care.

**What to do:** Transport pets in your personal vehicle with air conditioning. For fish, drain the tank, bag fish separately, and keep them cool. Consider boarding pets in Coral Gables or Coconut Grove on moving day if you need to focus on the move.

## 6. Irreplaceable Documents

Passports, birth certificates, Social Security cards, financial records, wills, and property deeds should stay with you personally during the move. If a moving truck gets delayed or damaged, you do not want these items inaccessible.

![Two Movers Load A Van With Furniture Dur 3](/images/blog/2026/09/8-items-banned-from-professional-moving-trucks/two-movers-load-a-van-with-furniture-dur-3.webp)


**What to do:** Pack these in a fireproof folder or small safe that travels in your car. Keep digital copies backed up online as an extra precaution.

## 7. Jewelry and Cash

High-value items like jewelry, cash, collectible coins, and family heirlooms belong in your personal vehicle. Moving insurance typically excludes these items, and they are easy targets if boxes go missing.

**What to do:** Wear valuable jewelry or pack it in your purse or carry-on bag. Consider a safe deposit box at your bank in Brickell or Downtown Miami for especially valuable items during the transition.

## 8. Medications

Keep prescriptions accessible and in your possession, not packed in boxes that might be delayed or lost. Temperature-sensitive medications can be ruined by heat exposure in the truck.

**What to do:** Pack medications in a cooler bag if temperature-sensitive. Keep a week's supply in your travel bag. Transfer prescriptions to a pharmacy near your new address before the move if possible.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can movers transport cleaning supplies?

Most standard cleaning products are fine. However, bleach, ammonia, and other harsh chemicals should be disposed of or transported separately, as they can leak and damage other items.

### What about car batteries or lawn equipment with fuel?

Drain all fuel from lawn mowers, weed trimmers, and similar equipment before moving day. Car batteries should be disconnected and transported in your personal vehicle.

### Do these rules apply to local moves within Miami?

Yes, the same restrictions apply whether you are moving from Kendall to Coral Gables or from Miami to New York. Federal DOT regulations govern all commercial moving trucks.
