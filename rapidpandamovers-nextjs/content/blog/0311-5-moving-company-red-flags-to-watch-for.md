Before you sign anything with a moving company, you need to know how to verify their legitimacy. Florida requires specific registrations, and interstate movers have federal requirements. Here are five warning signs and exactly how to verify each one.

## 1. No Verifiable Florida Registration

Every moving company operating in Florida must register with FDACS (Florida Department of Agriculture and Consumer Services). This is non-negotiable for local moves.

![Close Up Of A Hand Signing A Formal Docu 1](/images/blog/2026/04/5-moving-company-red-flags-to-watch-for/close-up-of-a-hand-signing-a-formal-docu-1.webp)


**How to verify:**
- Visit the FDACS Moving Company Search at [csapp.fdacs.gov/moversearch](https://csapp.fdacs.gov/moversearch)
- Search by company name or registration number
- Confirm active status and no outstanding complaints
- Check that the registration covers household goods moving

If a company cannot provide their FDACS registration number or is not found in the database, do not hire them for a local move in Miami-Dade, Broward, or Palm Beach counties.

## 2. Missing USDOT Number for Interstate Moves

Any company moving your belongings across state lines must have a USDOT number from the Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration. This is federal law.

**How to verify:**
- Ask for their USDOT number directly
- Search the FMCSA database at [safer.fmcsa.dot.gov](https://safer.fmcsa.dot.gov/CompanySnapshot.aspx)
- Confirm the operating authority is active (not "Pending" or "Revoked")
- Check their safety rating and complaint history

Moving from Miami to Atlanta, New York, or anywhere out of state? The company must be federally registered. No exceptions.

## 3. Estimate Without Home Walkthrough

Professional movers need to see your belongings before providing an accurate quote. If a company gives you a binding estimate over the phone without visiting your home, expect the price to increase on moving day.

**What to expect from legitimate companies:**
- In-home or virtual video survey of your belongings
- Itemized inventory list
- Written estimate specifying binding vs. non-binding terms
- Clear explanation of what is and is not included

In Miami, moving a 3-bedroom home in Coral Gables costs differently than a studio in Brickell. Any company that quotes the same price without seeing your place is guessing or planning to charge more later.

## 4. No Proof of Insurance

Every licensed mover must carry cargo insurance and liability coverage. If they cannot show proof of insurance, your belongings have no protection if something goes wrong.

![Hands Signing Lease Documents On A Cardb 2](/images/blog/2026/04/5-moving-company-red-flags-to-watch-for/hands-signing-lease-documents-on-a-cardb-2.webp)


**What to request:**
- Certificate of Insurance (COI) showing current coverage
- Minimum liability of $0.60 per pound per item (federal minimum for interstate)
- Full replacement value protection option
- Proof of workers' compensation (protects you from liability if a mover is injured)

Many Miami condo buildings, including those in Aventura, Sunny Isles Beach, and Brickell, require a COI before allowing movers into the building. Legitimate movers provide these routinely.

## 5. Demands Large Cash Deposit

No reputable moving company requires a large cash deposit upfront. This is the most common tactic in moving scams. Once they have your cash and your belongings loaded on the truck, you have no leverage.

**Standard payment practices:**
- Small deposit (10-20%) to hold your date, often by credit card
- Balance due upon delivery and inspection
- Credit card, check, or certified funds accepted
- Never pay everything in cash before the move completes

If a mover demands 50% or more in cash before loading, walk away. Miami's competitive market unfortunately attracts rogue operators who disappear with deposits or hold belongings hostage for inflated fees.

## How to File a Complaint

If you have been scammed by a moving company in Florida:

- **Local moves**: File with FDACS at 1-800-HELP-FLA or [FloridaConsumerHelp.com](https://www.floridaconsumerhelp.com)
- **Interstate moves**: File with FMCSA at [nccdb.fmcsa.dot.gov](https://nccdb.fmcsa.dot.gov)
- **All moves**: Report to BBB and leave honest reviews to warn others
