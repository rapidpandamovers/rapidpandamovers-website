Miami Springs keeps drawing new residents from across the country, and it's easy to see why. This historic city near Miami International Airport offers something rare in Miami-Dade: a genuine small-town feel with mature oak trees, mid-century architecture, and a tight-knit community.

## Why Choose Miami Springs?

Miami Springs stands out as one of Miami-Dade County's most distinctive communities. Founded in 1926 by aviation pioneer Glenn Curtiss, the city retains its original Pueblo Revival architecture and strict zoning that preserves its residential character.

![A Miami Monorail Travels Past Modern Hig 2](/images/blog/2027/07/your-miami-springs-moving-handbook/a-miami-monorail-travels-past-modern-hig-2.webp)


Unlike the high-rise development you'll find in Brickell or Aventura, Miami Springs limits building heights and maintains large lot sizes. The result is a walkable neighborhood with established trees providing welcome shade during hot summer afternoons.

### Location and Accessibility

Miami Springs sits just north of Miami International Airport, making it popular with airline employees and frequent travelers. The Palmetto Expressway (SR 826) runs along the western edge, providing quick access to Doral's business district and the Dolphin Expressway to Downtown Miami.

Commute times from Miami Springs:
- **Downtown Miami**: 20-25 minutes via SR 112
- **Coral Gables**: 15-20 minutes via US-1
- **Doral**: 10 minutes via Palmetto Expressway
- **Miami Beach**: 30-35 minutes via the Airport Expressway

The Metrorail doesn't reach Miami Springs directly, but the Tri-Rail station in Hialeah is a short drive away for commuters heading north to Fort Lauderdale or West Palm Beach.

### Community and Lifestyle

Miami Springs residents take pride in their community. The Miami Springs Golf and Country Club anchors the eastern side of town, while Curtis Park and Stafford Park host youth sports leagues and community events year-round.

The Circle, Miami Springs' downtown area along Westward Drive, features local restaurants, a farmers market on Sundays, and the city's historic municipal pool. Unlike the tourist-heavy scenes in Wynwood or South Beach, the Circle caters to residents looking for casual dining and neighborhood shops.

## Neighborhoods to Consider

Miami Springs covers about three square miles, but distinct areas offer different experiences.

### The Country Club Section
The eastern portion near the golf course features larger lots and some of the city's most expensive homes. Tree canopy coverage here is extensive, and many homes date to the 1950s and 1960s with original terrazzo floors.

### West Miami Springs
Closer to the Palmetto Expressway, homes tend to be more affordable with smaller lots. This area works well for buyers prioritizing quick highway access over lot size.

### Downtown/Circle Area
Homes within walking distance of the Circle command a premium for their walkability. These blocks attract young families who want to stroll to restaurants and the farmers market.

### Choosing Your Perfect Spot

Consider these factors:

- **Airport noise**: Eastern Miami Springs sits under flight paths. Tour properties during busy flight times
- **Flood zones**: Some areas require flood insurance. Check FEMA maps before buying
- **School assignments**: Miami Springs Elementary and Middle School serve the city. High schoolers attend Hialeah-Miami Lakes Senior High
- **HOA rules**: Some sections have deed restrictions limiting exterior modifications

## Moving to Miami Springs in Summer

Summer moves in South Florida require planning around the weather. Afternoon thunderstorms roll in almost daily between June and September, usually between 2 PM and 6 PM.

![Aerial Daytime View Of Miami Florida Cap 3](/images/blog/2027/07/your-miami-springs-moving-handbook/aerial-daytime-view-of-miami-florida-cap-3.webp)


### Timing Your Move

When planning your relocation, consider:

- **Morning starts**: Schedule your move to begin at 7 or 8 AM to beat the afternoon storms
- **Hurricane season**: June through November brings tropical weather. Monitor forecasts and have a backup date
- **School calendar**: Miami-Dade schools typically start in mid-August. Move early in summer to get settled

### Weather Prep for Moving Day

South Florida summer heat is no joke. Our crews stay hydrated and take breaks, but you should prepare too:
- Keep cold water and sports drinks available
- Run the AC at your new place before the furniture arrives
- Protect electronics from humidity during transport

## Essential Services to Locate

As a new Miami Springs resident, you'll want to find:

- **Healthcare**: Palmetto General Hospital in Hialeah is the closest ER. Baptist Health has facilities throughout the area
- **Schools**: Miami Springs Elementary is well-regarded. Private options include nearby Our Lady of the Lakes in Miami Lakes
- **Shopping**: Town & Country Mall on NW 36th Street handles most errands. For more variety, Dolphin Mall and Miami International Mall are 10 minutes away
- **Groceries**: Publix on Curtiss Parkway serves the community. Trader Joe's fans head to Pinecrest or Aventura
