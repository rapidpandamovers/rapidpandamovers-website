After handling thousands of Miami moves, our crews have seen what works and what causes headaches. These seven insider tips come straight from the professionals who load trucks every day in Brickell high-rises, Coral Gables estates, and everywhere in between.

## 1. Packing Takes Longer Than You Think

A 3-bedroom house takes 3-5 days to pack properly. Not one frantic night. Start early and pace yourself. In Miami's humidity, you will also want to run the AC while packing. Sweating through box after box leads to rushed decisions and forgotten items. Our Kendall and Doral crews consistently see last-minute packers run into trouble with breakables.

## 2. Small Boxes for Heavy Items

Books, dishes, and tools go in small boxes. Large boxes are for light items like bedding and clothes. This is not optional, it is physics. A large box filled with books becomes impossible to lift safely and puts strain on movers' backs. We see this mistake constantly, especially in moves from older Coconut Grove homes with extensive book collections.

## 3. Labeling Saves Everyone Time

"Kitchen" is not enough. Write "Kitchen - Coffee Station - Fragile" on the box AND the side so stacked boxes are identifiable. When we arrive at your new Aventura condo or Wynwood loft, good labels mean your coffee maker gets unpacked first instead of buried under twelve boxes marked "miscellaneous."

## 4. Fragile Items Need Communication

If something is exceptionally fragile or valuable, tell movers before they touch it. We are careful, but we are not psychic. That antique mirror from your grandmother? Point it out. The custom art piece from a Wynwood gallery? Let us know. A quick heads-up helps us give special items the attention they deserve.

## 5. Access Matters More Than You Think

Clear pathways, reserved elevators, and available parking make moves faster. Every obstacle adds time and stress. If you are moving from a Brickell high-rise, reserve the service elevator in advance. Moving to a Coral Gables home with a long driveway? Make sure we can get the truck close to the door.

## 6. Disassembled Furniture Helps

Beds, tables, and desks move faster when disassembled. Keep hardware in labeled bags taped to the furniture. Those IKEA bed frames and large sectionals from your Miami Beach apartment will fit through doorways much easier in pieces. Plus, reassembly goes smoothly when all the screws and bolts stay together.

## 7. Have the New Layout Ready

Know where furniture goes before we arrive at the new place. Uncertainty means multiple rearrangements. Walk through your new home beforehand and decide where the couch goes, which bedroom gets which bed, and where the dining table fits. Our crews work efficiently when they can place items once and move on.
