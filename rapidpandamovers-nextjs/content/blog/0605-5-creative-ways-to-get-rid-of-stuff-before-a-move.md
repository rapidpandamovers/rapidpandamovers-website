# 5 Creative Ways to Get Rid of Stuff Before a Move

Donating to Goodwill and selling on Facebook Marketplace are the obvious go-to methods for getting rid of stuff before a move. But when you're facing a tight timeline or dealing with items that don't fit neatly into those categories, you need some less conventional approaches. Here are five ways Miami residents are clearing their homes before moving day.

## 1. Host a Moving Sale Party

Forget the traditional yard sale with folding tables and price stickers. Turn your pre-move purge into a social event. Invite friends, neighbors, and coworkers over for drinks and let them shop your house. Everything is priced to go, and the social setting means people are more likely to take things they might skip at a regular garage sale.

![A Chaotic Pile Of Metal Scrap And Waste  2](/images/blog/2028/03/5-creative-ways-to-get-rid-of-stuff-before-a-move/a-chaotic-pile-of-metal-scrap-and-waste--2.webp)


In Miami neighborhoods like Wynwood, the Design District, and Coconut Grove, these "everything must go" parties have become popular among younger renters and homeowners. Share it on your Instagram story or neighborhood WhatsApp group, and you'll be surprised how fast things move when people are having a good time.

## 2. Use Buy Nothing Groups

Miami-Dade has active Buy Nothing groups on Facebook for nearly every neighborhood, from Doral to South Miami to Miami Beach. These hyperlocal gifting communities let you post items for free, and neighbors pick them up from your porch. It's faster than selling because there's no price negotiation, no shipping, and no flaky buyers.

Post your items with clear photos and a brief description. Things like kids' toys, kitchen items, cleaning supplies, gardening tools, and books typically get claimed within hours. For larger items like furniture, the response is even faster since people love free pieces they can pick up down the street.

## 3. Arrange a Donation Pickup Marathon

Instead of making multiple trips to various donation centers, schedule pickups from several organizations in the same week. Salvation Army, Habitat for Humanity ReStore, and Vietnam Veterans of America all offer free pickup in the Miami area. Assign different categories to different organizations: furniture to ReStore, clothing and household items to Salvation Army, and general goods to Vietnam Veterans.

![Aerial Shot Showcasing Diverse Scrap Met 4](/images/blog/2028/03/5-creative-ways-to-get-rid-of-stuff-before-a-move/aerial-shot-showcasing-diverse-scrap-met-4.webp)


Having three pickups scheduled across a single week means you can sort once and have the items removed in stages. This approach also maximizes your tax-deductible donation receipts.

## 4. Repurpose and Upcycle Through Local Makers

Miami has a thriving community of artists, crafters, and upcyclers who want your old stuff. Scrap wood, metal pieces, old picture frames, vintage hardware, fabric remnants, and broken furniture all have value to someone building or creating.

Post materials on Craigslist under the "free" section or connect with local maker spaces. The Moonlighter Makerspace in Little Haiti and other community workshops often accept donated materials. What looks like junk to you might be exactly the raw material someone needs for their next project.

## Combining Methods Gets Results

The most efficient approach uses all five methods in sequence. Start with the social sale two to three weeks out, list on Buy Nothing groups that same week, schedule donation pickups, offer leftovers to local makers, then book your junk removal for whatever remains. By moving day, you're packing only what you truly want at your new place.
