Moving with kids this spring? Biscayne Park is a fantastic choice for families. From April adventures to everyday activities, here's your guide to keeping the whole family entertained and connected to your new community.

## 1. Get the Library Card First Week

Your Biscayne Park library card is free and unlocks story times, summer reading programs, movie screenings, and craft sessions. This should be one of your first stops after moving.

![Family Kids Activities Guide](/images/blog/2029/04/5-family-friendly-activities-in-biscayne-park/family-kids-activities-guide.webp)


## 2. Search Miami-Dade Parks for 'Splash Pad'

Florida heat demands water activities. The county parks department lists all Biscayne Park area splash pads and spray parks—free and perfect for young kids.

## 3. Join the Nextdoor Parents Group

Search Nextdoor for 'Biscayne Park parents' or 'kids.' Local parents share the real scoop on which playgrounds are best, which activities are worth it, and where to find deals.

![Family Kids Activities Planning](/images/blog/2029/04/5-family-friendly-activities-in-biscayne-park/family-kids-activities-planning.webp)


## 4. Time Outdoor Activities Around Heat

In Biscayne Park, plan outdoor family activities for before 10am or after 4pm. Midday Florida sun is brutal on kids (and parents). Indoor activities or pool time fill the hot hours.

## 5. Sign Up for Museum Memberships Early

Miami Children's Museum, Zoo Miami, and science centers near Biscayne Park offer annual memberships. If you'll visit more than twice, they pay for themselves and often include guest passes.
