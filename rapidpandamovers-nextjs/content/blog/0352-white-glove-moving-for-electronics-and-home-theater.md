# White Glove Moving for Electronics and Home Theater

A high-end home theater system can easily cost $50,000 to $200,000, and that's before the custom installation work. When you're relocating a Coral Gables estate or a Brickell penthouse with a dedicated media room, moving the electronics requires the same precision as installing them in the first place.

## Why Electronics Need Special Handling

Home theater components are sensitive to vibration, static electricity, temperature swings, and moisture. A 4K laser projector contains optical elements that can shift out of alignment from rough handling. Amplifiers and receivers have delicate internal wiring. Speakers with custom crossover networks can suffer from solder joint failures if they take hard impacts. Miami's humidity adds another risk layer, since moisture can corrode circuit boards and connectors during transport.

## The Documentation Step

Before disconnecting anything, our white glove team photographs every connection point in your system. We label each cable at both ends, map the signal flow from source to display, and record any custom calibration settings. For systems programmed by a Crestron or Savant integrator, we coordinate with your installer to save configuration files and note any settings that need recalibration after the move.

## Packing Home Theater Components

Each component gets its own packing protocol. Projectors go into custom foam-lined cases. Speakers are wrapped in anti-static material and then padded with closed-cell foam. Amplifiers travel in shock-absorbing crates designed to prevent internal component movement. Flat-panel displays get double-boxed with corner protectors and foam rails. We never stack components or lay screens face-down.

## Handling In-Wall and In-Ceiling Equipment

Many luxury homes in Miami Beach and Key Biscayne feature in-wall speakers, recessed subwoofers, motorized projection screens, and acoustic panels. Removing these requires tools, patience, and knowledge of how they were mounted. Our team carefully extracts in-wall equipment, patches or covers the openings at the origin, and works with your installer at the destination to ensure proper reinstallation.

## Cable Management and Reconnection

Professional home theater installations route cables through walls, conduit, and custom raceways. We salvage usable cable runs, bring replacement cable stock when existing runs can't be reused, and reinstall everything to the same standard as the original setup. Loose cables stuffed behind a receiver are not how we leave a system.

## Calibration Coordination

Moving a projector even a few inches changes the throw distance and alignment. Surround sound systems depend on speaker placement relative to the seating position. After placing all components in the new space, we work with your AV integrator to schedule recalibration. For simpler systems, our team handles basic setup and testing in-house.

## Protecting Supporting Furniture

Media consoles, equipment racks, acoustic furniture, and custom cabinetry that house electronics need their own handling plan. We remove components from furniture before moving either piece, protecting both the electronics and the cabinetry from damage during transport.
