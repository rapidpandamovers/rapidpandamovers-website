# Pool Table Disassembly: What Professional Movers Do

If you've ever wondered what actually happens when a professional crew shows up to disassemble your pool table, this post walks through the process from start to finish. There's a reason this isn't a one-person job, and there's a reason the sequence matters. Every step protects a different part of the table, and skipping or rushing any of them risks damage that's expensive to fix.

## Before a Single Bolt Comes Out

Professional [**Pool Table Moving**](/pool-table-moving) starts before the wrench comes out. Our crew begins with an inspection of the table and the space around it:

![A Person Arranging Billiard Balls On A B 3](/images/blog/2027/11/pool-table-disassembly-what-professional-movers-do/a-person-arranging-billiard-balls-on-a-b-3.webp)


- **Table assessment**: We note the brand, model, and construction type. A Brunswick Gold Crown breaks down differently than a Valley bar-style table. We check for any pre-existing damage and document it with photos.
- **Felt condition**: Is the cloth worth saving? If it's in good shape and the owner wants to reuse it, we'll remove it carefully. If it's worn or dated, we'll plan for re-felting during reassembly.
- **Hardware inventory**: Pool tables use dozens of bolts, screws, nuts, and staples. We bring labeled bags and a Sharpie. Every piece of hardware gets organized by location (left rail, right rail, head rail, foot rail, legs, etc.).
- **Path check**: We confirm the route from the table to the truck. Doorways, hallways, stairs, and elevator access all get measured if we haven't already done a pre-move assessment.

## Step 1: Pocket and Rail Removal

The first components to come off are the pockets and rails. Most tables have six pockets held by screws or bolts underneath the rail assembly. The rails themselves are attached to the slate with bolts that run through the rail and into threaded inserts in the slate.

We loosen rail bolts in a specific pattern, working from the center outward on each rail. This prevents the rail from binding or cracking due to uneven pressure. Each rail is numbered or marked so it goes back in the same position during reassembly. Rail cushions are specific to their location, and swapping them changes how balls bounce off the bumpers.

## Step 2: Felt Removal

With the rails off, the felt is exposed. If we're saving it, removal is slow and deliberate:

![A Vibrant Shot Of A Billiards Table With 5](/images/blog/2027/11/pool-table-disassembly-what-professional-movers-do/a-vibrant-shot-of-a-billiards-table-with-5.webp)


- A flat-head staple remover lifts each staple individually. Pliers can pinch and tear the cloth.
- The felt is peeled back gently, starting from one end and working toward the other.
- Once free, the cloth is rolled (never folded) around a cardboard tube.
- The rolled felt is wrapped in a clean cotton sheet for transport. We avoid plastic in Miami's heat because trapped moisture leads to mildew fast.

If the felt is being replaced, removal is quicker since we don't need to preserve it.

## Step 3: Slate Removal

This is the most physically demanding and technically sensitive part of the job. The slate on a standard three-piece table consists of three sections, each weighing 150 to 250 pounds. They're held to the frame with screws and sealed together with beeswax.

Our crew:

- Scrapes the beeswax from the seams using a putty knife
- Removes the screws holding each slate section to the frame
- Uses slate jacks to lift each piece straight up and off the frame
- Wraps each slate piece in thick moving blankets immediately
- Secures the wrapped slate with straps and positions it for transport

Slate is strong under compression but brittle under impact. It can't be dropped, bumped, or flexed. Even setting it down too hard on a concrete garage floor can cause a crack. Our guys treat each piece like it costs $500 to replace, because it does.

## Step 4: Frame Breakdown

With the slate removed, the frame and legs come apart. Most pool table frames bolt together with heavy-duty carriage bolts or lag screws. We:

![Close Up Of Billiard Balls And Cue Stick 1](/images/blog/2027/11/pool-table-disassembly-what-professional-movers-do/close-up-of-billiard-balls-and-cue-stick-1.webp)


- Remove the legs, labeling each one (front left, front right, etc.) since they may not be interchangeable
- Detach cross-supports and side rails from the frame
- Wrap all wooden components in moving blankets to prevent scratches and dings
- Bag all hardware and tape it to the frame for safekeeping

## Step 5: Loading

Loading order matters. Slate pieces go into the truck first, standing vertically and strapped to the wall. The frame, rails, and legs fill in around them, padded so nothing shifts. The felt tube rides in the cab or in a protected spot where it won't get crushed.

For Miami condo moves, we often stage everything in the loading dock area before bringing the truck, since freight elevator access is usually time-limited.
