Moving into a new home is the perfect opportunity to implement smart technology. Starting fresh this summer means you can set things up right from the beginning, without working around existing systems.

## 1. Smart Thermostat

Nest, Ecobee, or similar devices learn your schedule and optimize energy use. They pay for themselves in utility savings.

![Smart Home Technology Gadgets Planning](/images/blog/2028/06/9-gadgets-that-improve-your-new-home/smart-home-technology-gadgets-planning.webp)


## 2. Video Doorbell

Ring, Nest, or other video doorbells let you see visitors and delivery people from anywhere via smartphone.

## 3. Smart Locks

Keyless entry, remote locking, and temporary codes for visitors eliminate key management hassles.

## 4. Smart Lighting

Automated lighting controlled by voice, app, or schedule adds convenience and security.

## 5. Mesh WiFi System

Large homes benefit from mesh networks that eliminate dead zones and provide consistent coverage everywhere.

## 6. Smart Smoke Detectors

Nest Protect and similar devices send phone alerts and communicate with each other during emergencies.

![Smart Home Technology Gadgets Steps](/images/blog/2028/06/9-gadgets-that-improve-your-new-home/smart-home-technology-gadgets-steps.webp)


## 7. Water Leak Sensors

Small sensors near water heaters, under sinks, and by washing machines alert you to leaks before disasters.

## 8. Smart Plugs

Turn any outlet into a smart outlet. Control lamps, fans, and small appliances remotely or set schedules for energy savings.

## 9. Robot Vacuum

Automated floor cleaning saves time and keeps your new home consistently clean without daily effort. Modern models map your home and avoid obstacles.
