Moving a safe during February? You're making a smart choice. The cooler winter months in Miami-Dade County offer ideal conditions for moving heavy items like gun safes, fireproof safes, and security vaults.

## Understanding Safe Moving Requirements

Safes are among the most challenging items to move. A small to medium gun safe weighs between 200 and 600 pounds, while larger models can weigh 600 to 2,000 pounds. Some home safes with composite or pour-in insulation weigh up to 3,100 pounds. This weight requires specialized equipment and trained professionals.

![Safe Security Heavy Checklist](/images/blog/2026/02/winter-moving-safe-moving-best-practices/safe-security-heavy-checklist.webp)


Before your move, you must know the exact weight of your safe. This single step determines the equipment needed and the size of the crew required. Two people can move safes under 250 pounds. Safes between 250 and 400 pounds require at least three helpers with proper equipment.

## Essential Equipment for Safe Moving

Professional [**safe moving**](/safe-moving) requires specialized tools:

- **Heavy-duty appliance dolly**: Must be rated for 500+ pounds with metal straps
- **Stair-climbing dolly**: Essential for navigating stairs with robust wheels designed for heavy loads
- **Hydraulic lifts**: Allow smoother transitions across different surfaces
- **Moving blankets and padded covers**: Protect the safe from scratches and protect your floors and walls
- **Moving sliders or plywood sheets**: Distribute weight and prevent floor damage

Never use a regular lightweight dolly. The consequences of equipment failure with a 500+ pound safe can be catastrophic.

## Floor Protection Guidelines

Different flooring types require specific protection:

- **Hardwood**: Never drag or slide directly. Use moving sliders or plywood to distribute weight
- **Tile**: Avoid letting corners or legs drop directly on tile, as tiles can crack under heavy pressure
- **Carpet**: Provides more grip but safes can still tip. Use plywood sheets for heavier safes

## Florida Moving Company Requirements

When hiring safe movers in Florida, verify they meet state requirements. As of July 2024, Florida law (Senate Bill 304) requires:

- **FDACS Registration**: All movers must display their IM registration number on vehicles, contracts, and advertisements
- **Insurance Coverage**: Minimum $10,000 cargo liability insurance per shipment
- **Payment Options**: Must accept at least two of three forms: cash, cashier's check, or credit card
- **Written Estimates**: Required before conducting any move

Verify any moving company's license at the FDACS Business License Lookup tool before hiring.

## Miami-Dade Building Considerations

If you live in a condo or high-rise, contact your building management before your move. Most Miami-Dade buildings require:

- Elevator reservation for moving heavy items
- Certificate of Insurance (COI) from your moving company
- Specific loading dock or service elevator scheduling
- Weight verification for elevator capacity

The Miami-Dade County Office of Elevator Safety regulates all elevators in the county (except Miami and Miami Beach). Buildings have strict weight limits, and your safe may require special arrangements.

## Preparing Your Safe for the Move

Follow these steps before moving day:

![Safe Security Heavy Tips](/images/blog/2026/02/winter-moving-safe-moving-best-practices/safe-security-heavy-tips.webp)


1. **Empty the safe completely**: Remove all contents, including firearms, documents, and valuables
2. **Remove shelves**: Take out all removable interior components
3. **Secure the door**: Close and lock it to prevent opening during transport
4. **Measure precisely**: Record height, width, depth, and weight for the moving team
5. **Transport firearms separately**: Most moving companies cannot legally transport firearms

## Placement at Your New Location

Once your safe arrives, consider placement carefully:

- **Location**: A closet, basement, or reinforced room offers privacy and security
- **Visibility**: Keep away from windows and obvious entry points
- **Leveling**: Adjust supports to ensure the safe is level, protecting internal lock mechanisms
- **Anchoring**: Use heavy-duty anchor bolts into concrete or reinforced surfaces. Even a 1,000-pound safe can be tipped or dragged if unsecured
