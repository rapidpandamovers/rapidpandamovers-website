# DIY Art Packing vs Professional Art Movers

You're standing in your living room staring at a wall of artwork that needs to get across town. The question hits everyone: should you pack it yourself or hire professionals? The answer depends on what you're moving, how much of it there is, and how much risk you're willing to accept. Here's an honest breakdown from a team that does this every day in Miami.

## When DIY Makes Sense

If you have a small number of framed prints or posters with standard glass, and they're not particularly valuable, DIY packing can work. You'll need mirror boxes from a moving supply store, painter's tape, bubble wrap, foam corner protectors, and packing paper. Budget about 15 to 20 minutes per piece for proper wrapping.

![A Museum Hall Showcasing A Marble Statue 4](/images/blog/2029/07/diy-art-packing-vs-professional-art-movers/a-museum-hall-showcasing-a-marble-statue-4.webp)


Prints with replaceable frames, wall art from retail stores, and sentimental pieces with low dollar value are all reasonable DIY candidates. The key is honest assessment. If breaking the piece would upset you but not financially hurt you, DIY is worth trying.

## Where DIY Breaks Down

The trouble starts when any of these apply:

**The piece is valuable.** An original painting worth $2,000 or more deserves professional handling. The cost of a professional packing job on a single piece runs far less than the cost of restoration if something goes wrong.

**The piece is large.** Anything over four feet in either dimension becomes difficult for one or two untrained people to handle safely. Large canvases flex when carried incorrectly, and oversized frames crack at the corners when they catch a doorframe.

**The medium is delicate.** Oil paintings, watercolors, pastels, and works on paper all need specific materials and techniques. Bubble wrap pressed directly against an oil painting leaves permanent impressions. Newspaper ink transfers onto mats. Plastic wrap traps moisture against surfaces.

**You have a lot of pieces.** Packing three pieces is manageable. Packing thirty is a full day of careful work with specialty materials that most people don't have on hand.

**Miami's climate is a factor.** Moving art in July means dealing with 90-degree heat and 80% humidity the moment you step outside. Professional teams use climate-controlled trucks and moisture barriers. DIY moves usually involve a regular van or truck and an open tailgate.

## Cost Comparison

DIY packing materials for a single framed piece run about $15 to $25, including a mirror box, tape, and bubble wrap. Multiply that by your collection size and add your time at whatever you value an hour of your Saturday.

![An Elegant Art Gallery Room Featuring Cl 3](/images/blog/2029/07/diy-art-packing-vs-professional-art-movers/an-elegant-art-gallery-room-featuring-cl-3.webp)


Professional art packing for a standard framed piece typically costs $30 to $75, which includes materials, labor, and proper technique. Custom crating for valuable or oversized pieces runs $150 to $500 per piece. That price also buys insurance coverage, climate-controlled transport, and the peace of mind that trained handlers are doing the work.

For collections worth $10,000 or more, the professional option usually costs 2% to 5% of the collection's total value. That's a reasonable insurance premium for items that can't be replaced at any price.

## The Middle Ground

Some clients pack their less valuable pieces themselves and hire us for the high-value and oversized items. This hybrid approach saves money on the everyday prints while ensuring the irreplaceable pieces get museum-grade treatment. We see this often with Miami collectors who have both retail art and original works in the same home.

Beyond better materials and techniques, professional art movers bring experience with problem-solving. We know how to get a six-foot painting through a 30-inch doorway.
