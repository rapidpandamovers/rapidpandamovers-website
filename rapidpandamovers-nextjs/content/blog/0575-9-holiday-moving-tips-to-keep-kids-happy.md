Moving during December adds a layer of complication that other times of year don't have. Kids already associate this season with routine, traditions, and anticipation. Disrupting that with a move requires extra care and planning.

## 1. Keep Holiday Traditions Intact

Pack decorations last and unpack them first at the new home. If you always have a tree, set one up even if boxes surround it. If cookie baking is your tradition, make sure the kitchen is functional for that. Traditions anchor kids during upheaval.

![A Family Enjoying A Festive Christmas Se 4](/images/blog/2027/12/9-holiday-moving-tips-to-keep-kids-happy/a-family-enjoying-a-festive-christmas-se-4.webp)


## 2. Time the Move Around School Breaks

December offers natural windows. Moving during winter break means kids don't miss school and have time to adjust before classes resume. Many Miami families time their moves for the week between Christmas and New Year's for exactly this reason.

## 3. Create a Holiday Countdown Calendar

Make the move part of the excitement by creating a countdown that incorporates both moving milestones and holiday events. Day 15: Pack the playroom. Day 10: Say goodbye to our old neighbors. Day 5: Move day. Day 1: Celebrate in our new home.

![A Family With Young Children Celebrates  2](/images/blog/2027/12/9-holiday-moving-tips-to-keep-kids-happy/a-family-with-young-children-celebrates--2.webp)


## 4. Let Kids Design Their New Space

Before the move, visit the new home (or share photos if that's not possible) and let kids plan their room. Where will the bed go? What color should we paint? What posters go where? Giving them ownership reduces anxiety about the unknown.

## 5. Schedule Holiday Activities in the New Neighborhood

Research what's happening near your new Miami home. Zoo Miami hosts ZooLights throughout December. Coconut Grove's Peacock Park has holiday events. The Gables hosts Santa visits. Book something for the first week so kids have an event to anticipate.

## 6. Pack a Holiday Essentials Box

Beyond the usual essentials box, create one specifically for the season: favorite ornaments, stockings, wrapping paper for any remaining gifts, holiday pajamas, and a few decorations to make the new place feel festive immediately.

![A Happy Family Sharing A Christmas Dinne 1](/images/blog/2027/12/9-holiday-moving-tips-to-keep-kids-happy/a-happy-family-sharing-a-christmas-dinne-1.webp)


## 7. Maintain Gift-Giving Plans

Kids worry about Santa finding them at the new address. Younger children might need reassurance. Older kids still care about gift plans. Don't let the move chaos derail what matters to them about the season.

## 8. Host a Small Celebration After Move-In

Once you're settled enough, host a simple gathering. Hot cocoa, pizza, and a movie night. Invite a few new neighbors if the timing works. It marks the transition and creates a positive first memory in the new home.

![Father Dressed As Santa Claus With Happy 3](/images/blog/2027/12/9-holiday-moving-tips-to-keep-kids-happy/father-dressed-as-santa-claus-with-happy-3.webp)


## 9. Acknowledge What They're Leaving Behind

December moves often mean leaving behind neighborhood traditions: the house with the best lights, the annual block party, friends they won't see daily. Let kids feel that loss. Take photos. Write notes to old neighbors. Closure matters.
