Welcome to your March guide for moving to Medley! Whether you're relocating from Doral, Hialeah, or making a bigger move from outside South Florida this spring, getting to know your new community will help you settle in quickly.

## Why Choose Medley?

Medley stands out as one of Miami-Dade County's most appealing locations. The community offers an industrial and commercial atmosphere and is particularly known for business parks and logistics.

![A Ups Truck Parked In An Urban Alleyway  1](/images/blog/2030/03/medley-living-practical-relocation-tips/a-ups-truck-parked-in-an-urban-alleyway--1.webp)


The area attracts families, professionals, and retirees alike, thanks to its quality of life, convenient access to major employment centers, and excellent amenities.

### Location and Accessibility

Medley sits right off the Palmetto Expressway (SR 826) and is minutes from the Dolphin Expressway (SR 836), giving you quick access to most of Miami-Dade County. Commuters heading to Downtown Miami, Brickell, or Miami International Airport will find the location convenient. The neighboring city of Doral is just to the south, offering shopping at Miami International Mall and CityPlace Doral.

### Community and Lifestyle

Medley residents enjoy a strong sense of community. Local parks, recreation facilities, and community events bring neighbors together throughout the year. The spring season is particularly vibrant, with community events and outdoor activities bringing residents together.

## Neighborhoods to Consider

Medley is unique in Miami-Dade County as it's primarily an industrial and commercial town with a small residential population. Most residents live in the western portion near NW 74th Street. If you're looking for more residential options, consider neighboring Hialeah Gardens or Doral, which offer easy access to Medley's job centers while providing more housing variety.

### Choosing Your Perfect Spot

Consider these factors:

- **Proximity to work**: Many relocate here for jobs in logistics, warehousing, and distribution
- **Commute times**: Test your route to Downtown Miami or Coral Gables during rush hour
- **Nearby amenities**: Shopping and dining in Doral, Hialeah, or Miami Lakes
- **Housing stock**: Limited residential options in Medley proper; consider adjacent communities

## Moving to Medley in March

March is an excellent time to consider your move. The spring weather in South Florida provides warming temperatures with occasional afternoon showers for the moving process.

### Timing Your Move

When planning your relocation, consider:

- **Best moving days**: Weekdays typically offer better availability and rates
- **Weather considerations**: March in Miami means temperatures in the low 80s with occasional afternoon showers
- **Traffic patterns**: Avoid rush hour on the Palmetto Expressway; truck traffic picks up early in this industrial area

## Key Services to Locate

As a new Medley resident, you'll want to find:

![Exterior Of A Brown Industrial Building  3](/images/blog/2030/03/medley-living-practical-relocation-tips/exterior-of-a-brown-industrial-building--3.webp)


- **Healthcare facilities**: Palmetto General Hospital in Hialeah is the closest major hospital
- **Schools**: Medley has limited schools; most families use Miami-Dade County schools in Hialeah or Doral
- **Shopping**: Westland Mall in Hialeah, Miami International Mall, and shops along NW 87th Avenue
- **Recreation**: Amelia Earhart Park in Hialeah offers trails, fishing, and a petting zoo
