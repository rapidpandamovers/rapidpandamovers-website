Planning a move in Miami? Before loading up the truck, you need to know what professional movers cannot legally transport. From hazardous materials to perishable foods, these restrictions exist to protect you, your belongings, and the moving crew. Here's what you need to handle separately on moving day.

## 1. Hazardous Materials

Gasoline, propane, paint thinner, aerosols, and chemicals are fire hazards and illegal to transport on moving trucks.

![A Mover Carefully Handles Boxes In A Bri 1](/images/blog/2025/09/10-things-movers-arent-allowed-to-transport/a-mover-carefully-handles-boxes-in-a-bri-1.webp)


## 2. Explosives and Ammunition

Fireworks, ammunition, and anything explosive must be disposed of or transported personally with proper safety measures.

## 3. Perishable Foods

Refrigerated and frozen items won't survive most moves. Consume, donate, or dispose of perishables before moving day.

## 4. Plants (for Long-Distance Moves)

Agricultural regulations often prohibit transporting plants across state lines due to pest concerns.

## 5. Pets and Living Creatures

Fish, pets, and any living animals cannot travel on moving trucks. Arrange separate, safe transportation for them.

![Confident Young Lady In Casual Wear Carr 2](/images/blog/2025/09/10-things-movers-arent-allowed-to-transport/confident-young-lady-in-casual-wear-carr-2.webp)


## 6. Irreplaceable Documents

Passports, birth certificates, financial records, and legal documents should stay with you personally during the move.

## 7. Jewelry and Cash

High-value, easily stolen items belong in your personal vehicle, not on a truck full of boxes.

## 8. Medications

Keep prescriptions accessible and in your possession. Never pack them in boxes that might be delayed or lost.

## 9. Pressurized Containers

Fire extinguishers, scuba tanks, and aerosol cans can explode under pressure or temperature changes during transport.

![Smiling Woman Holding A Box Marked Fragi 3](/images/blog/2025/09/10-things-movers-arent-allowed-to-transport/smiling-woman-holding-a-box-marked-fragi-3.webp)


## 10. Anything Irreplaceable

Family photos, heirlooms, and items of extreme sentimental value should travel with you in your personal vehicle.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Why can't movers transport hazardous materials?**
Federal regulations prohibit commercial carriers from transporting flammable, explosive, or corrosive materials. This protects the moving crew, your belongings, and the public.

**What should I do with items movers can't transport?**
Dispose of hazardous materials at Miami-Dade County drop-off sites. Transport valuables, medications, and documents in your personal vehicle. Donate or consume perishables before moving day.

**Can I pack prohibited items if I don't tell the movers?**
Never hide prohibited items in boxes. If discovered, the moving crew must stop work. You could also be liable for any damage or injuries that result.
