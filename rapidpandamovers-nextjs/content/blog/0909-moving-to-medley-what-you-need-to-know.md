Medley continues to attract new residents from across the country during February, and it's easy to see why. Located in the heart of Miami-Dade County, Medley offers affordable living options combined with quick access to surrounding neighborhoods like Doral, Hialeah, and Miami Lakes.

## Why Choose Medley?

Medley is primarily an industrial and commercial hub, home to over 2,500 businesses but fewer than 1,000 residents. This makes it unique in Miami-Dade County - if you work in logistics, warehousing, or light manufacturing, living here means practically no commute.

![Exterior Of A Brown Industrial Building  1](/images/blog/2030/02/moving-to-medley-what-you-need-to-know/exterior-of-a-brown-industrial-building--1.webp)


The town sits along the Palmetto Expressway (SR 826) and Okeechobee Road (US 27), making it easy to reach Doral's shopping and dining in about 10 minutes, or Downtown Miami in roughly 25 minutes during off-peak hours.

### Location and Accessibility

Medley sits at the crossroads of the Palmetto Expressway, Florida Turnpike, and Okeechobee Road. This puts you within a 15-minute drive of Hialeah, Miami Lakes, and Doral. Miami International Airport is just 8 miles south, which is helpful if your work involves frequent travel or shipping.

### Community and Lifestyle

With such a small residential population, Medley feels more like a work campus than a traditional neighborhood. Most residents here are business owners or employees who want to live close to their workplace. For shopping, dining, and entertainment, you'll head to nearby Doral Town Center or Miami Lakes' Main Street.

### Living in Medley

Day-to-day life in Medley revolves around convenience and work-life balance. The lack of traffic lights along most commercial streets means quick errands, and the 24-hour nature of many businesses means services like gas stations and convenience stores stay open late. The trade-off is limited nightlife and dining options within town limits, though Doral and Hialeah are both under 15 minutes away for restaurants, entertainment, and groceries.

## Housing Options in Medley

Medley's residential options are limited compared to neighboring communities. You'll find some single-family homes and mobile home parks, with most housing concentrated along NW 74th Street and near the town hall.

![Exterior View Of A Modern Building Entra 3](/images/blog/2030/02/moving-to-medley-what-you-need-to-know/exterior-view-of-a-modern-building-entra-3.webp)


### Nearby Alternatives

If Medley proper doesn't have what you need, consider these nearby areas:

- **Doral**: 10 minutes east, with newer developments, restaurants, and family amenities
- **Miami Lakes**: 15 minutes north, known for its planned community feel and good schools
- **Hialeah**: 10 minutes southeast, offering more affordable housing and Cuban culture
- **Palm Springs North**: Just across Okeechobee Road, with suburban-style neighborhoods

## Moving to Medley in February

February is a good time to relocate in South Florida. Temperatures hover around 75 degrees, humidity is lower, and you avoid the summer rain that can delay outdoor loading.

### Timing Your Move

When planning your relocation, keep in mind:

- **Weekday moves**: Typically offer better availability and lower rates than weekends
- **Truck access**: Medley's industrial streets handle large trucks well, but check for any loading dock requirements at your destination
- **Rush hour**: Avoid moving during 7-9 AM and 4-6 PM when Palmetto Expressway traffic backs up

## Essential Services Near Medley

Since Medley is primarily industrial, you'll find most services in neighboring towns:

![Monochrome Image Of Jacksonville Lift Br 5](/images/blog/2030/02/moving-to-medley-what-you-need-to-know/monochrome-image-of-jacksonville-lift-br-5.webp)


- **Healthcare**: Palmetto General Hospital in Hialeah (5 miles), or Baptist Health facilities in Doral
- **Schools**: Students attend Hialeah-Miami Lakes Senior High or Miami Lakes schools through Miami-Dade County Public Schools
- **Groceries**: Walmart Supercenter on NW 79th Avenue, Publix and Sedano's in Doral
- **Recreation**: Amelia Earhart Park is 5 minutes east in Hialeah, with trails, lakes, and sports facilities
