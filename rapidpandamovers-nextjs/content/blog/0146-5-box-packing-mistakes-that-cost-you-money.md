Packing seems straightforward until something breaks. These common errors are responsible for the majority of moving damage we see in Miami. Avoid them and your belongings will arrive safely at your new South Florida home.

## 1. Overpacking Large Boxes

A huge box of books is impossible to lift safely. Use small boxes for heavy items. Always. Your back and your movers will thank you, especially when hauling boxes up to a Brickell high-rise.

![Close Up Of Hands Packing A Box Labeled  1](/images/blog/2025/03/5-box-packing-mistakes-that-cost-you-money/close-up-of-hands-packing-a-box-labeled--1.webp)


## 2. Insufficient Cushioning

One layer of newspaper isn't enough. Fragile items need individual wrapping, padding on all sides, and zero room to shift. This is especially true for moves during Miami's hot summers when boxes can sit in warm moving trucks.

## 3. Leaving Hollow Items Empty

Hollow items like vases and cups are fragile. Fill them with packing paper or soft materials for structural support.

![Crop Unrecognizable Man In Casual Clothe 4](/images/blog/2025/03/5-box-packing-mistakes-that-cost-you-money/crop-unrecognizable-man-in-casual-clothe-4.webp)


## 4. Using Old, Weak Boxes

Old boxes lose structural integrity. In Miami's humid climate, cardboard absorbs moisture fast. That garage box from your last move? It's probably already compromised. Use fresh, sturdy boxes from a moving supply store.

## 5. Improper Dish Packing

Never stack plates horizontally. Pack them on edge, like records, with padding between each one. This prevents cracking from weight.
