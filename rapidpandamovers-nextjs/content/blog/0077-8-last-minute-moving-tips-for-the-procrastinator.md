Life happens, and sometimes you end up with less time to prepare than you'd like. If your move in Miami is approaching fast and you're behind, don't panic. Whether you're relocating from Brickell to Coral Gables or making a quick move to Wynwood, these strategies will help you pull it together quickly.

## 1. Focus on Must-Haves Only

Separate absolute necessities from nice-to-haves. Move essentials first; everything else can follow or be donated.

![Capture The Unstoppable Force Of Nature  1](/images/blog/2024/10/8-last-minute-moving-tips-for-the-procrastinator/capture-the-unstoppable-force-of-nature--1.webp)


## 2. Use Trash Bags for Clothes

Garbage bags over hanging clothes are fast and effective. No folding required. Just tie the hangers together.

## 3. Skip the Sorting

When time is critical, box everything and sort at the new place. Speed trumps organization in emergency situations.

## 4. Hire Professional Packers

Yes, it costs more, but professional packers can handle in hours what takes you days. In Miami's heat and humidity, especially during summer months, having pros handle the heavy lifting while you stay cool is worth every penny.

## 5. Accept Every Offer of Help

Now is not the time to be independent. Accept every offer of assistance from friends and family.

![Minimalist Clock With A Black Friday Sal 2](/images/blog/2024/10/8-last-minute-moving-tips-for-the-procrastinator/minimalist-clock-with-a-black-friday-sal-2.webp)


## 6. Call Multiple Moving Companies

Moving companies often have last-minute availability. Call several until you find one with an open slot. In Miami, avoid scheduling during peak traffic hours on I-95 or the Palmetto Expressway if possible.

## 7. Consider a Storage Unit

If you can't get everything moved by deadline, a short-term storage unit buys you flexibility. Climate-controlled units are a must in South Florida to protect furniture and electronics from humidity damage.

## 8. Prioritize Sleep

Exhausted packing leads to broken items and forgotten essentials. Rest is actually productive.
