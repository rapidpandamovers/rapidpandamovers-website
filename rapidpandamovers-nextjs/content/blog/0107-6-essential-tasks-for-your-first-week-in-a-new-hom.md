The first week in a new home sets the tone for everything that follows. These December priority tasks will help you get settled safely and start your new chapter on the right foot.

## 1. Change All the Locks

You don't know who has keys from before. Previous owners, contractors, or real estate agents might still have copies. Change locks immediately for security and peace of mind. In Miami neighborhoods like Coral Gables or Coconut Grove, many locksmiths offer same-day service.

![A Man In A Yellow Shirt Checks Off A Lis 1](/images/blog/2024/12/6-essential-tasks-for-your-first-week-in-a-new-home/a-man-in-a-yellow-shirt-checks-off-a-lis-1.webp)


## 2. Test All Appliances

Run the dishwasher, check the stove, test the washer/dryer. Identify problems while warranty claims are still fresh.

## 3. Locate Emergency Shutoffs

Find and label water main, electrical panel, and gas shutoff. You'll need to know these eventually, so it's better to find them now. In South Florida, knowing your water shutoff is especially important during hurricane season when pipes can burst from pressure changes.

![Cheerful Young Man And Woman Smiling Whi 4](/images/blog/2024/12/6-essential-tasks-for-your-first-week-in-a-new-home/cheerful-young-man-and-woman-smiling-whi-4.webp)


## 4. Set Up Mail Forwarding

Visit USPS.com to ensure nothing gets lost during the transition period. This should happen before you move.

## 5. Transfer Utilities to Your Name

Electricity, gas, water, internet: confirm all accounts are active and in your name before move-in day. For Miami-Dade residents, FPL handles electricity while Miami-Dade Water and Sewer covers water service. Set these up at least a week in advance to avoid delays.

![Pensive Couple Taking Notes In Bedroom N 3](/images/blog/2024/12/6-essential-tasks-for-your-first-week-in-a-new-home/pensive-couple-taking-notes-in-bedroom-n-3.webp)


## 6. Schedule Critical Installations

Internet, cable, security systems: book these early as installation windows fill up quickly.
