You rented the truck, recruited friends with pizza promises, and then you see it: that 800-pound grand piano in the living room. Some items are simply too heavy, too fragile, or too specialized for DIY moving. Here are five items that Miami homeowners should always leave to the professionals.

## 1. Grand Pianos

A grand piano weighs between 500 and 1,200 pounds, with delicate internal mechanisms that shift out of tune from the slightest jolt. Moving one requires a piano board, specialized straps, and a team trained specifically in piano transport. In Miami's older homes, especially in Coral Gables and Coconut Grove, narrow doorways and terrazzo floors add extra challenges. One wrong move can crack the soundboard, snap a leg, or gouge your hardwood floors.

## 2. Wine Collections

South Florida's heat and humidity are enemies of wine storage. During a move, temperature fluctuations can ruin a collection in hours. Professional movers use climate-controlled vehicles and custom wine crates that keep bottles horizontal and protected. For collectors in Brickell's luxury condos or Pinecrest estates, proper handling means the difference between preserved vintages and expensive vinegar.

## 3. Hot Tubs and Spas

Hot tubs weigh 400-900 pounds empty and require electrical disconnection, complete draining, and often a crane or specialized equipment for removal. Many Miami backyards, especially in Kendall and Doral, have tight access points that make extraction tricky. Professionals know how to disconnect plumbing safely and can handle the logistics of getting a bulky spa through gates, around pools, and onto a truck.

## 4. Large Aquariums

Fish need separate transport in bags with their tank water, and they cannot survive long without proper oxygenation. The tank itself must be completely drained and moved perfectly level to prevent stress cracks in the glass. For Miami aquarium enthusiasts, the humid climate actually helps during transport, but timing matters. Our crews have moved 200-gallon tanks from homes in Aventura and Key Biscayne, and the process requires careful coordination.

## 5. Pool Tables

A regulation pool table has a slate top weighing 450-800 pounds that must be removed by trained technicians. The slate often comes in three pieces, and reassembly requires precise leveling with a machinist's level. Move a pool table wrong, and it will never play true again. Many Miami game rooms, especially in homes throughout Miami Lakes and Palmetto Bay, feature high-quality tables that deserve proper care.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does it cost to move a grand piano in Miami?

Piano moving in Miami typically ranges from $300-$800 depending on the piano size, distance, and obstacles like stairs or tight corners. Concert grands and antique pianos cost more due to their weight and value.

### Can movers transport my fish with the aquarium?

Fish should always be transported separately from the tank. We recommend bagging fish with their tank water and using a battery-powered air pump for moves longer than an hour.

### Do I need to drain my hot tub before moving day?

Yes, hot tubs must be completely drained at least 24 hours before the move. This allows the plumbing to dry and prevents water damage during transport.
