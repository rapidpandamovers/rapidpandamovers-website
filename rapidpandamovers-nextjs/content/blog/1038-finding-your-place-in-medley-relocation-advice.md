## Why Choose Medley?

Medley stands out as Miami-Dade County's industrial powerhouse. Unlike residential-focused communities, Medley is purpose-built for business, featuring over 2,000 businesses in just 9 square miles. The town is strategically positioned at the crossroads of Florida's Turnpike, the Palmetto Expressway, and SR 836, making it an ideal logistics and distribution hub.

The area primarily attracts businesses in manufacturing, warehousing, distribution, and logistics. With its business-friendly policies and abundant commercial real estate, Medley has earned its reputation as South Florida's commerce capital.

### Location and Accessibility

One of Medley's biggest advantages is its unmatched transportation infrastructure. The town sits at the intersection of three major highways, providing quick access to:

- **Miami International Airport** - Just 10 minutes away
- **Port of Miami** - Under 30 minutes for cargo shipments
- **Downtown Miami** - 15 minutes via SR 836
- **Fort Lauderdale** - 30 minutes via Florida's Turnpike

This connectivity makes Medley ideal for businesses requiring rapid distribution capabilities throughout South Florida and beyond.

### Business Environment

Medley operates differently than most municipalities. With virtually no residential population (under 900 residents), the town focuses exclusively on supporting commercial enterprises. This means:

- Streamlined permitting processes
- Business-oriented local government
- No residential noise complaints or zoning conflicts
- 24/7 operational flexibility for most businesses

### Northwest 74th Street Corridor

This primary commercial artery features:

- Large warehouse spaces (50,000+ sq ft)
- Modern distribution centers
- Direct highway access
- Major retailers and distributors

### Milam Dairy Road Area

Popular with food processing and cold storage operations due to:

- Proximity to food distribution networks
- Established cold chain infrastructure
- Lower lease rates than 74th Street

### Okeechobee Road Corridor

The eastern gateway to Medley offers:

- Excellent visibility
- Mixed commercial/retail opportunities
- Easy access to Hialeah and Miami Lakes

### Choosing Your Perfect Location

Consider these factors when selecting your Medley address:

- **Ceiling height requirements**: Warehouse spaces range from 20 to 40+ feet
- **Loading dock specifications**: Cross-dock facilities vs. single-sided loading
- **Power requirements**: Heavy manufacturing may need industrial power hookups
- **Truck access**: Consider turning radii for 18-wheelers

## What to Expect When Moving to Medley

### The Permitting Process

Moving a business to Medley involves several steps:

1. **Business Tax Receipt Application** - Required for all businesses
2. **Certificate of Use** - Verifies your business type matches zoning
3. **Fire Inspection** - Mandatory for all commercial spaces
4. **Building Permits** - For any tenant improvements or modifications

The Town of Medley business services department is notably efficient, often processing permits faster than neighboring municipalities.

### Utility Setup

Coordinate with these providers for your new location:

- **Florida Power & Light** - Electric service
- **Miami-Dade Water & Sewer** - Water and sewer
- **Florida City Gas** - Natural gas
- **Various providers** - Telecommunications and internet

### Is Medley only for large businesses?

No. While Medley hosts many large operations, there are numerous smaller warehouse bays and flex spaces suitable for small and medium businesses. Spaces start at around 1,000 square feet.

Technically yes, but options are extremely limited. Most Medley workers commute from nearby communities like Hialeah, Doral, or Miami Lakes.

Medley has numerous restaurants serving the business community, from quick-service to sit-down options. For retail shopping, nearby Hialeah and Doral offer extensive options.
