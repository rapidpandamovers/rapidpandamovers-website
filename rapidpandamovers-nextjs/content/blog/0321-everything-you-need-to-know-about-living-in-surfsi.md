Surfside continues to attract new residents from across the country, and it's easy to see why. This small beachfront town of just over 5,000 residents sits between Bal Harbour to the north and Miami Beach to the south, offering a rare combination of quiet coastal living with easy access to Miami's urban amenities.

## Why Choose Surfside?

Surfside stands out as one of Miami-Dade County's most appealing locations for families and professionals seeking a slower pace without sacrificing convenience. The town spans just half a square mile, creating a walkable community where neighbors actually know each other. Unlike the tourist crowds in nearby South Beach or the high-rise density of Sunny Isles, Surfside maintains a genuine small-town feel with local shops along Harding Avenue and uncrowded beaches.

![Charming Beach Homes On Belleair Beach F 4](/images/blog/2026/05/everything-you-need-to-know-about-living-in-surfside/charming-beach-homes-on-belleair-beach-f-4.webp)


The area attracts families drawn to Ruth K. Broad Bay Harbor K-8 Center, young professionals who work in Brickell or Downtown Miami, and retirees who appreciate the kosher restaurants and close-knit Jewish community. The town's commitment to keeping buildings under 12 stories preserves ocean views and maintains the relaxed atmosphere that drew people here in the first place.

### Location and Accessibility

Surfside sits along Collins Avenue (A1A), putting you 20 minutes from Downtown Miami and 15 minutes from Aventura Mall. The 93rd Street beach entrance offers free parking for residents, and the town's flat terrain makes biking practical for daily errands. For commuters, the Bal Harbour Shops area provides easy access to I-95 via 96th Street, while the upcoming Brightline extension will add another transit option for reaching Fort Lauderdale and West Palm Beach.

### Community and Lifestyle

Surfside residents gather at the Surfside Community Center on Collins Avenue for everything from yoga classes to art shows. The town hosts monthly First Fridays on Harding Avenue, where local businesses stay open late and food trucks line the street. The beach itself remains the main attraction, with lifeguards on duty and a playground at 93rd Street that fills up on weekend mornings.

## Neighborhoods to Consider

Surfside is small enough that location matters less than in larger cities, but different areas do have distinct characters. The blocks east of Collins Avenue, closest to the beach, command premium prices but put you steps from the sand. Properties along Harding Avenue offer walkability to shops and restaurants, while the western blocks near Bay Drive provide quieter streets and bay views.

### Choosing Your Perfect Spot

Consider these factors when picking your specific location:

- **Proximity to work and schools**: Collins Avenue condos work well for commuters using A1A, while families often prefer the residential streets west of Harding
- **Local amenities**: The commercial stretch along Harding Avenue (between 93rd and 96th) puts restaurants, banks, and shops within walking distance
- **Property types**: East of Collins is almost entirely condos, while west of Harding offers single-family homes and townhouses
- **Community vibe**: The area near the Surfside Community Center attracts families with kids, while Bay Drive appeals to those seeking waterfront quiet

## Moving to Surfside: Timing Tips

May brings warming temperatures and the start of South Florida's afternoon thunderstorm season. Morning moves work best this time of year, letting you finish before the typical 3pm storms roll in. The rental market tends to open up as snowbirds head north, creating more options for newcomers.

### Planning Your Relocation

When scheduling your move, keep these local factors in mind:

- **Best moving days**: Weekday moves avoid weekend beach traffic on Collins Avenue and offer better availability from moving companies
- **Weather considerations**: Schedule loading and unloading for morning hours before afternoon storms develop
- **Building requirements**: Most Surfside condos require COI (Certificate of Insurance) from your moving company and 48-hour advance notice for elevator reservations

## Essential Services to Locate

As a new Surfside resident, you'll want to establish these essentials:

- **Healthcare**: Mount Sinai Medical Center is 10 minutes south in Miami Beach, while Aventura Hospital sits 15 minutes north
- **Schools**: Ruth K. Broad Bay Harbor K-8 serves the area, with private options including Hebrew Academy and Lehrman Community Day School nearby
- **Shopping**: Publix on 96th Street handles groceries, while Bal Harbour Shops (literally next door) and Aventura Mall cover everything else
- **Recreation**: The town pool at the Community Center, 93rd Street beach access, and North Shore Open Space Park in neighboring Miami Beach
