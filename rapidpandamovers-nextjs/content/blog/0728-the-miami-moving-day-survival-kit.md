Moving in Miami hits different. Even in December, temperatures reach the 80s by midday. Combine that with humidity that can climb over 70% and you need a survival kit tailored to South Florida conditions.

## Hydration Station

### Water and More Water
Stock a cooler with at least 2 gallons of water per person. Freeze some bottles the night before so they stay cold longer. Keep the cooler in an air-conditioned vehicle or shade.

![Loc View Of Miami Skyline From Coral Gables  1](/images/blog/2028/12/the-miami-moving-day-survival-kit/loc-view-of-miami-skyline-from-coral-gables--1.webp)


### Electrolyte Drinks
Plain water is not enough during physical work in Miami heat. Gatorade, Liquid IV packets, or coconut water replace the salt you sweat out. Skip the energy drinks because caffeine accelerates dehydration.

## Sun and Heat Protection

### Sunscreen and Hat
Even when it is overcast, Miami's UV index stays high. Apply SPF 30+ before heading outside and reapply every 2 hours. A wide-brim hat protects your face and neck.

### Cooling Towels
These microfiber towels stay cool when wet. Drape one around your neck during outdoor loading and unloading. Worth every penny in Miami summers, still useful in December.

### Portable Fans
Battery-operated fans or handheld misters provide quick relief. Small USB-powered desk fans work great if you have a power bank.

## Miami-Specific Must-Haves

### Bug Spray
Florida mosquitoes do not take moving day off. DEET-based repellent keeps them away while you prop doors open for loading.

### Humidity Fighters
Pack silica gel packets in boxes with electronics, books, and anything paper. Miami's humidity causes damage quickly, especially to items sitting in a hot truck.

### Rain Gear
December afternoon storms are rare but not impossible. Keep tarps handy to cover furniture during quick showers. Plastic sheeting protects mattresses and upholstered items.

## Comfort and Safety Essentials

### First Aid Kit
Band-aids, antiseptic, pain relievers, and anti-chafing cream. Moving means bumps, scrapes, and sore muscles. Heat can also cause headaches, so pack ibuprofen or acetaminophen.

### Snacks That Won't Melt
Granola bars, nuts, dried fruit, and crackers. Skip chocolate bars and anything that turns to mush in the heat. Keep snacks in the cooler alongside your drinks.

### Change of Clothes
Pack a fresh shirt and shorts. You will sweat through whatever you are wearing by noon. A clean outfit for the drive to your new place makes a difference.

## Electronics Protection

### Important Documents and Keys
Keep in an air-conditioned car, not in the moving truck. Heat damages passports, birth certificates, and sensitive paper documents.

### Phone and Power Bank
Fully charged power bank for your phone. Coordinate with movers, confirm utility activation, and handle last-minute emergencies.

## The First-Night Box

Pack separately and keep accessible:
- Toiletries
- Towels
- Bedding
- Phone chargers
- One outfit for tomorrow
- Any medications

Label this box clearly so movers bring it in first.
