Moving doesn't have to break the bank, even in October. With smart planning and the right strategies, you can significantly reduce your fall moving costs without sacrificing quality or safety.

## 1. Declutter Before Getting Quotes

Fewer items equals lower costs. Aggressively declutter before movers assess your load—every box saved is money saved.

![Budget Saving Money Affordable Essentials](/images/blog/2026/10/9-smart-ways-to-save-on-moving-expenses/budget-saving-money-affordable-essentials.webp)


## 2. Time Your Move Strategically

Mid-week, mid-month moves cost less than weekend or end-of-month moves. Avoid peak summer months when possible.

## 3. Get Multiple Quotes

Compare at least three estimates from reputable companies. Make sure they're all based on the same inventory for accurate comparison.

## 4. Do Your Own Packing

Professional packing adds cost. If you have time, pack everything yourself with proper materials from a discount store.

## 5. Source Free Boxes

Check with local stores, Facebook Marketplace, and Nextdoor for free moving boxes. Many people give them away after their moves.

## 6. Sell Before You Move

Host a garage sale or list items online. Fewer belongings mean lower moving costs and extra cash.

![Budget Saving Money Affordable Guide](/images/blog/2026/10/9-smart-ways-to-save-on-moving-expenses/budget-saving-money-affordable-guide.webp)


## 7. Use What You Have

Pack with linens, towels, and clothes instead of buying packing paper. Suitcases make great heavy-item containers.

## 8. Ask About Discounts

Many movers offer military, senior, or AAA discounts. Ask upfront—savings add up quickly.

## 9. Book Early

Last-minute bookings cost more. Schedule your move at least 4-6 weeks ahead for better rates.
