You have the keys to your new home. Before the excitement of unpacking takes over, handle these critical tasks first. They're easier to complete now and will save you headaches later.

## 1. Change All the Locks

You don't know who has keys from before. Change locks immediately for security and peace of mind.

![New Home Checklist Settling In Guide](/images/blog/2027/11/10-critical-steps-after-getting-your-new-keys/new-home-checklist-settling-in-guide.webp)


## 2. Test All Appliances

Run the dishwasher, check the stove, test the washer/dryer. Identify problems while warranty claims are still fresh.

## 3. Locate Emergency Shutoffs

Find and label water main, electrical panel, and gas shutoff. You'll need to know these eventually—better to find them now.

## 4. Set Up Mail Forwarding

Visit USPS.com to ensure nothing gets lost during the transition period. This should happen before you move.

## 5. Transfer Utilities to Your Name

Electricity, gas, water, internet—confirm all accounts are active and in your name before move-in day.

## 6. Schedule Critical Installations

Internet, cable, security systems—book these early as installation windows fill up quickly.

![New Home Checklist Settling In Tips](/images/blog/2027/11/10-critical-steps-after-getting-your-new-keys/new-home-checklist-settling-in-tips.webp)


## 7. Deep Clean Before Unpacking

If you didn't do it before moving in, clean thoroughly now while rooms are still relatively empty.

## 8. Check Smoke and Carbon Monoxide Detectors

Test all detectors and replace batteries. These safety devices are too important to assume they work.

## 9. Meet Your Immediate Neighbors

A brief introduction now establishes a positive relationship. Neighbors can be invaluable for local tips and emergency help.

## 10. Document the Home's Condition

Take photos and videos of every room, especially any existing damage. This protects you when you eventually move out.
