Welcome to your comprehensive guide for moving to Virginia Gardens! This small, tight-knit village offers affordable living near Miami International Airport with a strong Hispanic community feel.

## About Virginia Gardens

Virginia Gardens is a small village in Miami-Dade County, located just south of Miami International Airport. With approximately 2,400 residents, this close-knit community offers affordable housing, convenient airport access, and a strong sense of neighborhood identity.

![Virginia Gardens Miami Residential Planning](/images/blog/2027/10/finding-your-place-in-virginia-gardens-relocation-advice/virginia-gardens-miami-residential-planning.webp)


### Demographics and Community

- **Population**: Approximately 2,381 (2025 estimate)
- **Median Age**: 42.1 years
- **Diversity**: Hispanic (81.6%), White (15.4%)
- **Median Household Income**: $72,569
- **Poverty Rate**: 3.0% (one of the lowest in Miami-Dade)
- **Foreign-Born Residents**: 61.8% (including 43.4% naturalized citizens)

### Employment Profile

- **Working Residents**: 1,468
- **Professional/Administrative Jobs**: 80.4%
- **Self-Employed**: 14.4%
- **Private Sector**: 70.8%
- **Unemployment Rate**: 3.5%

## Neighborhoods and Character

Virginia Gardens is a predominantly residential community with single-family homes making up 57.1% of housing. The village maintains a quiet, suburban atmosphere despite its proximity to the airport and major commercial areas.

### Housing Stock

- **Single-Family Homes**: 57.1%
- **Total Housing Units**: 862
- **Median Housing Costs**: $1,530/month
- **Median Rent**: $1,614/month

## Schools and Education

Virginia Gardens students attend Miami-Dade County Public Schools:

### Assigned Schools
- **Miami Springs High School**: Serves Virginia Gardens students
- Various elementary and middle school options in the area

The village's proximity to airport-area employers makes it attractive for working families seeking affordable housing with good school access.

## Location and Accessibility

### Transportation Advantages

Virginia Gardens offers unmatched airport proximity:

- **Miami International Airport**: 5 minutes
- **Palmetto Expressway (SR 826)**: Direct access
- **Dolphin Expressway (SR 836)**: Easy access to Downtown Miami
- **NW 36th Street**: Main commercial corridor

### Commute Times
- **Downtown Miami**: 15-20 minutes
- **Miami Beach**: 25-30 minutes
- **Doral**: 5-10 minutes
- **Coral Gables**: 15-20 minutes

## Major Employers Nearby

The airport area provides abundant employment opportunities:

- **Miami International Airport**: Thousands of jobs in aviation, retail, and services
- **Airport-Area Hotels**: Hospitality employment
- **Freight and Logistics Companies**: Warehousing and distribution
- **Doral Business District**: Corporate opportunities nearby

## Cost of Living

Virginia Gardens offers one of the more affordable options in Miami-Dade County:

- **Housing Costs**: Significantly lower than Miami Beach, Coral Gables, and Aventura
- **Property Taxes**: Village taxes plus Miami-Dade County
- **Utilities**: Standard South Florida rates
- **Insurance**: Flood insurance may be required in some areas

## Community Character

### What Makes Virginia Gardens Special

- **Small-Town Feel**: With only ~2,400 residents, neighbors know each other
- **Stable Community**: Low poverty rate and steady employment
- **Cultural Identity**: Strong Hispanic heritage and community events
- **Airport Convenience**: Perfect for frequent travelers or airport workers
- **Affordability**: More accessible housing than surrounding areas

## Moving Tips for Virginia Gardens

### Best Time to Move
- **Winter months (November-March)**: Mild weather, lower humidity
- **Weekdays**: Better parking availability

### Local Considerations
- **Airport Noise**: Some areas experience aircraft noise - visit at different times
- **Street Parking**: Plan truck placement in residential areas
- **Village Services**: Contact village hall for any permit requirements
