# How White Glove Service Handles Closet and Wardrobe Packing

A walk-in closet in a Coral Gables estate or a Brickell penthouse can hold a wardrobe worth tens of thousands of dollars. Designer suits, couture dresses, luxury handbags, and fine jewelry all need handling that goes beyond tossing clothes in a garbage bag and hoping for the best. Here's how our white glove team packs and moves even the most extensive wardrobes.

## The Pre-Pack Assessment

Before touching a single garment, the team inventories the closet. We categorize items by type: hanging garments, folded items, shoes, handbags, accessories, and specialty pieces like furs or leather goods. We note items that need individual protection, such as beaded gowns that can't be folded or structured blazers that need shoulder support. For custom closet systems common in Miami luxury homes, we also plan around built-in features like pull-out drawers, rotating shoe racks, and jewelry trays.

## Hanging Garments

Suits, dresses, coats, and other hanging items transfer directly from the closet rod into wardrobe boxes. These tall, sturdy boxes include a built-in metal bar at the top. Garments travel on their own hangers, maintaining their shape without folding. For delicate fabrics like silk, cashmere, and vintage pieces, we add garment bags before placing them in the wardrobe box. Each box holds about two feet of closet rod space, so a large master closet may require 10 to 15 wardrobe boxes.

## Folded Clothing and Knitwear

Sweaters, t-shirts, and other folded items get packed in clean, lined boxes with tissue paper layered between garments. We never compress knitwear or stack heavy items on top of delicate fabrics. Cashmere and wool pieces get cedar inserts to protect them during transit, especially during Miami's humid summer months when moisture can trigger mildew on natural fibers.

## Shoes and Handbags

High-end shoes get stuffed with acid-free tissue paper to maintain their shape, then individually wrapped and placed in compartmentalized boxes. We keep pairs together and avoid stacking heavy shoes on top of lighter ones. Designer handbags receive similar treatment: stuffed with tissue to hold their structure, wrapped in soft cloth or protective bags, and packed without compression. For handbag collections that include structured pieces from brands like Hermes or Chanel, each bag gets its own padded slot.

## Jewelry and Accessories

Fine jewelry travels with the homeowner, not on the truck. We advise clients to personally transport watches, rings, necklaces, and any pieces with significant value. Costume jewelry and fashion accessories like belts, scarves, and sunglasses are packed in lined trays or small boxes with soft dividers. Tie collections get rolled and placed in sectioned containers.

## Specialty Items: Furs and Leather

Fur coats and leather jackets need breathable storage during the move. We pack these in garment bags made from natural fabric, never plastic, which can trap moisture and cause damage. These garments get their own section in wardrobe boxes with extra spacing to avoid crushing the pile on furs or creasing leather.

## Unpacking at the Destination

At the new home, the wardrobe gets the same attention on the receiving end. Hanging garments go directly from wardrobe boxes to closet rods. Folded items are placed in drawers according to your organization system. Shoes return to racks or shelves. The goal is to replicate your original closet layout so everything feels familiar from day one. For clients moving into a new custom closet system, we work with your closet designer's layout plan.
