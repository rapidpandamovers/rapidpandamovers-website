Just moved to Westchester or planning your relocation? Food is one of the best ways to explore your new neighborhood. Beyond the famous Cuban spots, Westchester hides diverse culinary treasures in strip malls along Bird Road and Coral Way.

## 1. Arbetter's Hot Dogs

Serving Westchester since 1959, Arbetter's is a no-frills hot dog stand that locals swear by. Cash only, paper plates, and the best chili dogs in Miami. Find them on Bird Road in their original location.

![Close Up Of Gourmet Indian Cuisine And C 1](/images/blog/2026/08/8-best-restaurants-to-try-in-westchester-after-your-move/close-up-of-gourmet-indian-cuisine-and-c-1.webp)


## 2. Frankie's Pizza

Open since 1955, Frankie's serves super crispy square slices that have earned a cult following. The thin-crust Roman-style pizza comes out of the oven golden and crunchy. Perfect for a quick, affordable lunch after unpacking boxes.

## 3. Kon Chau

People drive across South Florida for this dim sum spot. Many locals call it the best Chinese restaurant in Miami. Order the har gow (shrimp dumplings), siu mai, and roast duck. Weekend mornings get packed early.

## 4. Pueblito Viejo

This Colombian restaurant brings vibrant energy with its colorful interior and authentic food. Try the bandeja paisa for a hearty meal or the arepas con queso as a starter. Great for families who want something beyond Cuban cuisine.

## 5. La Sin Rival Bakery

More than just a bakery, La Sin Rival serves excellent croquetas and Cuban pastries. Staff have been known to break into song and dance. Grab pastelitos de guayaba and cafe con leche at the counter.

## 6. Canton Palace

All-day dim sum in the heart of Westchester. Beyond the carts, the menu covers classic Cantonese dishes. The crispy roast pork and wonton soup stand out. Family-style portions work well for groups.

## 7. Graziano's Argentine Steakhouse

A Bird Road classic for all things meat. The parrillada (mixed grill) feeds two generously. Argentinian wines pair perfectly with their chorizo and morcilla. Save room for dulce de leche dessert.

![Sophisticated Dining Setup With Gourmet  3](/images/blog/2026/08/8-best-restaurants-to-try-in-westchester-after-your-move/sophisticated-dining-setup-with-gourmet--3.webp)


## 8. El Toro Loco Steakhouse

Started as a food truck in 2014, now a full-service steakhouse with devoted fans. The ribeye and churrasco attract meat lovers from across Miami-Dade. Portions run large and prices stay reasonable for the quality.
