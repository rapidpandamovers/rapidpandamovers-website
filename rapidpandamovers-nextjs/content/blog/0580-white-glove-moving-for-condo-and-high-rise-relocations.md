# White Glove Moving for Condo and High-Rise Relocations

Moving into or out of a luxury high-rise in Miami is a completely different operation than moving between houses. Between building rules, elevator logistics, loading dock schedules, and the sheer challenge of getting a king-size bed frame into a 35th-floor unit, condo moves demand planning and coordination that standard movers rarely provide.

## Building Rules and Compliance

Every luxury building in Brickell, Edgewater, Sunny Isles, and South Beach has its own set of moving regulations. Some allow moves only on weekdays between 9 AM and 5 PM. Others require 48 hours' notice. Many demand a certificate of insurance naming the building and its management company as additional insureds, with minimum coverage amounts that vary by property. Our white glove team maintains a database of requirements for buildings across Miami-Dade County and handles the paperwork well before moving day.

## Elevator Reservations and Padding

In a building with three elevators, you might get access to one for a four-hour window. That elevator needs to be padded from floor to ceiling before anything gets loaded. Blanket pads go on the walls, a protective mat covers the floor, and corner guards shield the door frame. Some buildings supply their own elevator pads, while others require the mover to bring them. Our team confirms these details with building management during the planning phase.

## Loading Dock Coordination

Luxury high-rises have designated loading areas, and most only accommodate one move at a time. If another resident scheduled their move on the same day, you might get bumped to an afternoon slot. White glove movers handle dock reservations early and build buffer time into the schedule for exactly these situations. We also confirm truck height clearances, since many underground loading docks in Brickell have low ceilings that won't fit a full-size moving truck.

## Working Through Tight Spaces

Condo layouts present challenges that houses don't. Narrow hallways, tight corners, and doorways that are inches too small for certain furniture pieces require creative problem-solving. Our crews measure every doorway and hallway in advance. Furniture gets disassembled to its smallest safe components when needed. For oversized items that won't fit through the unit's front door, we coordinate hoisting through balconies in buildings that allow it.

## Protecting Common Areas

The path from the loading dock to your unit passes through lobbies, corridors, and elevators that belong to the building. Damage to these areas becomes your liability. White glove teams lay protective runners from the dock to the elevator and from the elevator to your front door. We protect door frames, corner walls, and any surfaces our equipment might contact along the route.

## Floor Protection Inside the Unit

Many Miami condos feature porcelain tile, marble, or engineered hardwood throughout. Protective floor coverings go down inside your unit before the first piece of furniture crosses the threshold. We use non-adhesive runners that won't leave residue on polished surfaces and rigid protection boards for areas where heavy items will be staged.

## Move-Out Inspections

When leaving a condo, many buildings require a pre-move inspection and a post-move walkthrough. Any damage to common areas or the unit itself discovered after the move can result in charges against your deposit. Our white glove team documents the condition of all areas before and after the move, giving you and the building clear evidence of the property's state.
