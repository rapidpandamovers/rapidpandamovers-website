Welcome to your guide for moving to Sunny Isles Beach! This oceanfront city offers luxury high-rise living, beautiful beaches, and a vibrant international community on a barrier island between the Atlantic Ocean and Intracoastal Waterway.

## About Sunny Isles Beach

Sunny Isles Beach is a coastal resort city on a barrier island in northeastern Miami-Dade County. With approximately 22,100 residents, this densely populated city is known for its luxury condominiums, international residents, and stunning oceanfront location.

- **Population**: Approximately 22,125 (2023)
- **Median Age**: 48 years
- **Diversity**: White (82.9%), Hispanic (48.2%), Black (2.2%), Asian (2%)

## Moving Tips for Sunny Isles Beach

### High-Rise Considerations
- **Elevator Reservations**: Required for most buildings
- **Move-In Hours**: Strict schedules (often weekdays only)
- **Loading Dock Access**: Coordinate with building management
- **Insurance Requirements**: Buildings may require mover's insurance
- **Deposit**: Many buildings require move-in deposits

### Best Time to Move
- **Fall (September-November)**: After summer season
- **Weekdays**: Better building access availability
- **Avoid**: Peak tourist season (December-April)

### What to Know
- Most buildings are managed by HOAs with strict rules
- Hurricane preparedness is essential - impact windows standard
- Flood insurance required
- Parking is limited - confirm arrangements

### Weather Considerations
Sunny Isles Beach experiences South Florida's tropical climate:
- **Summer (June-September)**: Hot and humid with daily afternoon thunderstorms, highs in the 90s
- **Hurricane Season (June-November)**: Have a plan and know your evacuation zone
- **Winter (December-March)**: Pleasant temperatures in the 70s-80s, peak tourist season
- **Salt Air**: Protect electronics and metal items from corrosion during moves
