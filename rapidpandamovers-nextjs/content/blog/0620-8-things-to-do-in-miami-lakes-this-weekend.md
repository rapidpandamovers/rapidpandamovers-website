Miami Lakes has 99 parks, a walkable Main Street, and more weekend activities than most South Florida suburbs. Here are eight ways to spend your time after moving to this family-friendly community.

## 1. Explore Amelia Earhart Park

**Address:** 401 E 65th St, Hialeah, FL 33013 (right on the Miami Lakes border)

![Empty Streets With Palm Trees And Modern 2](/images/blog/2028/04/8-things-to-do-in-miami-lakes-this-weekend/empty-streets-with-palm-trees-and-modern-2.webp)


This 500-acre park is the crown jewel of the area. It has a skate park, mountain biking trails, a petting farm with pony rides, and a 90-acre lake. Miami Watersports Complex operates a cable wakeboard park here with wakeboarding, waterskiing, kneeboarding, and paddleboarding rentals. Open daily from sunrise to sunset. Entry fee is $7 per vehicle on weekends.

## 2. Cool Off at McDonald Water Park

**Address:** 14175 NW 59th Ave, Miami Lakes, FL 33014

A mile south of downtown Miami Lakes, this public water park has a 1,000-foot lazy river that winds through palm trees and a tunnel with a waterfall. The park has slides, a wave pool, and picnic areas. Open weekends and holidays during spring, daily during summer. Admission is around $8 for residents.

## 3. Walk Main Street Miami Lakes

Main Street is the heart of the community, featuring brick sidewalks, old-fashioned lamps, and striped awnings. Walk from one end to the other and find restaurants, boutiques, the Miami Lakes Hotel, and CMX Theaters. Saturday mornings are particularly pleasant for a coffee and stroll.

## 4. Catch a Show at Main Street Players

**Address:** 6766 Main St, Miami Lakes, FL 33014

This community theater has been staging productions since 1973. Shows range from comedies to musicals to dramas, with performances Thursday through Sunday. Tickets are affordable, usually $25-35. Check their schedule at mainstreetplayers.com for current productions.

## 5. Play at Optimist Park

**Address:** 6075 Miami Lakeway N, Miami Lakes, FL 33014

![Neon Lit Exterior Of Shorty S Bar B Q Re 3](/images/blog/2028/04/8-things-to-do-in-miami-lakes-this-weekend/neon-lit-exterior-of-shorty-s-bar-b-q-re-3.webp)


This park north of downtown has baseball and softball fields, soccer fields, basketball courts, and tennis courts. Weekend mornings bring youth sports leagues, but facilities are open for public use when not reserved. Bring your own equipment for a pickup game.

## 6. Relax at Veterans Park

**Address:** 6030 Miami Lakes Dr, Miami Lakes, FL 33014

Veterans Park offers a quieter weekend option with picnic pavilions, a playground, a fitness trail with modern equipment, and a half-court for basketball. The Mary Collins Community Center hosts weekend yoga and tai chi classes. Check miamilakes-fl.gov for class schedules.

## 7. Dine Along Main Street

Main Street has restaurants for every taste:

- **Shula's 2 Steak and Sports**: Sports bar atmosphere with steaks and American classics
- **La Rosa Negra**: Peruvian cuisine with ceviche and grilled meats
- **Viva Mexico**: Mexican favorites in a casual setting
- **Anthony's Coal Fired Pizza**: Wood-fired pizzas and Italian dishes

Weekend brunch options include Mimi's Cafe and First Watch. Reservations recommended for Saturday dinner.

## 8. Watch a Movie at CMX Theaters

**Address:** 6801 Main St, Miami Lakes, FL 33014

This modern cinema on Main Street has reclining seats, in-theater dining, and a full bar. It is a step up from standard movie theaters, making it perfect for date nights or family outings. Check showtimes and reserve seats online.
