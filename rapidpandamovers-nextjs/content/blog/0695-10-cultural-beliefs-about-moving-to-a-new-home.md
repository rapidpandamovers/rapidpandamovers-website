Whether you're a long-time Miami resident or just making your October move to South Florida, there's always something new to discover. Miami's diverse communities, from Little Havana to Little Haiti, from the Jewish neighborhoods of Aventura to the Brazilian enclaves in Brickell, bring rich traditions to the moving experience. Here's our curated list of cultural beliefs about moving to a new home.

## 1. Don't Move on a Rainy Day

Many cultures consider rain on moving day to be bad luck, as water symbolizes washing away good fortune. Others view it as cleansing.

![A Beautiful Tree Adorned With Traditiona 3](/images/blog/2028/10/10-cultural-beliefs-about-moving-to-a-new-home/a-beautiful-tree-adorned-with-traditiona-3.webp)


## 2. Carry Salt Into Your New Home First

Salt has protected against evil spirits in folklore worldwide. Many people sprinkle salt in corners or carry a container across the threshold.

## 3. Never Move on a Friday

In Western traditions, Friday moves are considered unlucky, possibly from superstitions about Friday the 13th.

## 4. Bring a New Broom

Old brooms are said to sweep away good luck and bring old problems. A new broom represents a fresh start.

## 5. Don't Look Back at Your Old Home

Some believe looking back as you leave brings bad luck or means you'll return to your old troubles.

![A Vibrant Display Of Traditional Fortune 1](/images/blog/2028/10/10-cultural-beliefs-about-moving-to-a-new-home/a-vibrant-display-of-traditional-fortune-1.webp)


## 6. Boil Milk and Rice First

In Indian tradition, boiling milk until it overflows symbolizes abundance. Cooking rice first brings prosperity.

## 7. Enter with Bread and Salt

Slavic and Jewish traditions involve entering with bread (so you never know hunger) and salt (so life always has flavor).

## 8. Burn Sage or Incense

Many cultures burn sage, incense, or herbs to cleanse negative energy from a new space before settling in.

## 9. Move on an Auspicious Day

In Chinese tradition, consulting the lunar calendar for a lucky moving date ensures good fortune in your new home.

![Close Up Of Numerous Blue Evil Eye Charm 2](/images/blog/2028/10/10-cultural-beliefs-about-moving-to-a-new-home/close-up-of-numerous-blue-evil-eye-charm-2.webp)


## 10. Let a Cat Enter First

In Russian folklore, letting a cat enter your new home first absorbs any negative energy and brings good luck.

## Miami's Melting Pot of Moving Traditions

Living in Miami means you might encounter many of these traditions among your neighbors. In Coral Gables and Kendall, Cuban families often bring a glass of water to absorb negative energy. Haitian communities in Little Haiti may use Florida Water cologne for spiritual cleansing. Jewish families moving to Aventura or Sunny Isles often hang a mezuzah on the doorframe before fully moving in. No matter which traditions you follow, Miami welcomes them all.
