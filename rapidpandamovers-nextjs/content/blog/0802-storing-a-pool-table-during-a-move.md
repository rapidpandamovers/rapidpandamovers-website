# Storing a Pool Table During a Move

Sometimes your move-out date and move-in date don't line up. Maybe you're closing on the new house two weeks after leaving the old one. Maybe renovations at the new place are running behind. Whatever the reason, your pool table might need to spend time in storage. Storing a fully assembled pool table is out of the question, and storing a disassembled one the wrong way can cause damage that only shows up after reassembly.

## Why Pool Tables Can't Stay Assembled in Storage

You can't just wheel a pool table into a storage unit and leave it. The table is designed to sit on a level, stable surface. In storage:

![A Person Arranging Billiard Balls On A B 2](/images/blog/2029/06/storing-a-pool-table-during-a-move/a-person-arranging-billiard-balls-on-a-b-2.webp)


- The legs can sink unevenly into flooring, warping the frame over time
- Temperature and humidity swings put stress on the joints and glue
- Dust, insects, and moisture attack the felt and wood
- The weight concentrated on a few contact points can cause structural stress if the unit floor isn't perfectly flat

Full disassembly is required before storage, just as it would be for a move. Our [**Pool Table Moving**](/pool-table-moving) crew handles the breakdown and prepares each component for storage conditions.

## Choosing the Right Storage Facility

For a pool table, climate-controlled storage is not optional in Miami. Here's why:

### Temperature

Non-climate-controlled units in South Florida can exceed 100 degrees in the summer. Heat causes wood to expand, glue to soften, and MDF to break down. Extreme heat also warps cue sticks if you're storing accessories alongside the table.

### Humidity

Miami's average humidity runs 73-77%, and it spikes above 85% during summer storms. In a non-controlled unit, that moisture attacks every part of your table:

- **Slate**: While slate itself is dimensionally stable, moisture can seep into the beeswax residue on the surface, and prolonged dampness causes mineral deposits.
- **Felt**: Moisture is felt's worst enemy. Damp cloth develops mildew quickly, and mildew stains are permanent. Even if the felt doesn't look moldy, it can develop a musty smell that never comes out.
- **Wood frame and rails**: Wood absorbs and releases moisture constantly. In a humid, uncontrolled environment, the frame joints can swell, warp, and eventually crack.

Climate-controlled units maintain temperatures between 60 and 80 degrees and humidity below 55%, which keeps all materials stable.

## How We Prepare a Table for Storage

After disassembly, each component gets specific treatment for storage:

![Classic Billiards Table Setup With Cue B 4](/images/blog/2029/06/storing-a-pool-table-during-a-move/classic-billiards-table-setup-with-cue-b-4.webp)


### Slate Pieces

- Wrapped in heavy moving blankets
- Stood upright and strapped to a wall or A-frame storage rack
- Never laid flat and never stacked (weight and vibration can cause cracks)
- Placed on wooden blocks or pallets to keep them off the concrete floor

### Felt

- If reusable, rolled around a cardboard tube (never folded)
- Wrapped in a breathable cotton bag
- Stored in a dry area away from the floor
- If the felt is worn, we recommend replacing it during reassembly rather than storing compromised cloth

### Frame and Legs

- All joints are loosened but components stay together where possible to prevent losing hardware
- Wrapped in moving blankets
- Stored flat or leaning against a wall
- Hardware bags are taped directly to the frame pieces they belong to

### Rails and Pockets

- Rails are wrapped individually in moving blankets
- Rubber cushions are sensitive to heat, so climate control is especially important for these
- Pockets are bagged and labeled

## How Long Can a Pool Table Stay in Storage?

With proper climate-controlled storage, a disassembled pool table can remain in storage for months or even years without damage. We've reassembled tables that spent over a year in storage and they played perfectly.

Without climate control, damage can start within weeks during a Miami summer. We've seen mildew on felt after just 10 days in a non-controlled unit during August.
