Cutler Bay sits inland, but seven great beaches are within easy driving distance. Here are the best options for a day trip, ranked by proximity and what makes each one worth the drive.

## 1. Homestead Bayfront Park

**Distance:** 15 minutes south via US-1

![Charming Beach Homes On Belleair Beach F 1](/images/blog/2028/04/7-best-beaches-near-cutler-bay-for-a-day-trip/charming-beach-homes-on-belleair-beach-f-1.webp)


**Address:** 9698 SW 328th St, Homestead, FL 33033

This park on Biscayne Bay is the closest beach option from Cutler Bay. The waters are calm, making it ideal for families with young kids. The park has a marina, boat ramp, and rental facilities. Entry fee is $7 per vehicle on weekends, $5 on weekdays. The beach area is small but never overcrowded.

## 2. Matheson Hammock Park

**Distance:** 20 minutes north via US-1

**Address:** 9610 Old Cutler Road, Coral Gables, FL 33156

This unique park features an atoll pool, a man-made lagoon that fills with water from Biscayne Bay at high tide. The calm, shallow water is perfect for small children. The park has picnic pavilions, a boat ramp, and Red Fish Grill, a waterfront restaurant. Entry fee is $7 per vehicle. Arrive early on weekends because parking fills up by mid-morning.

## 3. Crandon Park Beach (Key Biscayne)

**Distance:** 30 minutes via Rickenbacker Causeway

![Scenic View Of Cape Florida Lighthouse S 4](/images/blog/2028/04/7-best-beaches-near-cutler-bay-for-a-day-trip/scenic-view-of-cape-florida-lighthouse-s-4.webp)


**Address:** 6747 Crandon Blvd, Key Biscayne, FL 33149

Consistently rated among the top beaches in the United States, Crandon Park offers two miles of shoreline, calm waters protected by a sandbar, and full amenities. Lifeguards are on duty, and the beach has restrooms, outdoor showers, and concession stands. Entry fee is $8 per vehicle. The Rickenbacker Causeway toll is $2.25 each way.

## 4. Bill Baggs Cape Florida State Park

**Distance:** 35 minutes via Rickenbacker Causeway

**Address:** 1200 Crandon Blvd, Key Biscayne, FL 33149

This state park at the southern tip of Key Biscayne has a historic lighthouse built in 1825, bike rentals, and a wide beach with soft sand. The Lighthouse Cafe and Boater's Grill serve food on-site. Entry fee is $8 per vehicle plus the causeway toll. Lighthouse tours are available for a small additional fee.

## 5. Virginia Key Beach

**Distance:** 25 minutes via Rickenbacker Causeway

**Address:** 4020 Virginia Beach Dr, Key Biscayne, FL 33149

This historic beach was the only beach open to Black residents during segregation and has been restored as a public park. Today it offers quiet stretches of sand, kayak and paddleboard rentals, and trails for biking. Entry fee is $8 per vehicle. The beach is less crowded than Key Biscayne options, making it good for those who want more space.

## 6. Haulover Beach Park

**Distance:** 40 minutes north via US-1 or I-95

![Vibrant Hotel Facade With Palm Trees In  2](/images/blog/2028/04/7-best-beaches-near-cutler-bay-for-a-day-trip/vibrant-hotel-facade-with-palm-trees-in--2.webp)


**Address:** 10800 Collins Ave, Bal Harbour, FL 33154

This popular beach between Aventura and Bal Harbour has a fishing pier, food concessions, and chair rentals. The northern section is clothing-optional, so pick your area accordingly. Parking is $7 per vehicle. The beach has strong waves at times, making it better for adults and strong swimmers.

## 7. John Pennekamp Coral Reef State Park (Key Largo)

**Distance:** 50 minutes south via US-1

**Address:** 102601 Overseas Highway, Key Largo, FL 33037

The first undersea park in the United States, John Pennekamp offers snorkeling, scuba diving, and glass-bottom boat tours. The park has a small beach, but the main draw is the coral reef and underwater scenery. Entry fee is $8 per vehicle plus $4.50 per person. Snorkel trips cost around $30 and leave multiple times daily.
