# The White Glove Unpacking Experience Explained

Most people dread the unpacking phase of a move more than the move itself. Boxes sit unopened for weeks, kitchens stay disorganized for days, and that feeling of being "settled in" takes far longer than expected. White glove unpacking eliminates all of that. Here's what the process actually looks like when professionals handle it.

## Unpacking Starts With a Plan

Before opening a single box, the move manager walks through your new home with you. Room by room, you discuss where things should go. Which cabinets hold the everyday dishes versus the entertaining pieces? Where do the linens go in the master closet? Should books be organized by subject or size? This conversation shapes everything that follows, so the team places items according to your preferences rather than making guesses.

## Kitchen Unpacking

The kitchen is usually the most time-consuming room to unpack. White glove teams start here because it's where daily life begins. Every dish, glass, pot, and utensil gets unwrapped and placed in the cabinet or drawer you designate. Shelf liners go down first. Stemware gets polished before placement. Pantry items are organized by category. For the large gourmet kitchens common in Coral Gables and Pinecrest estates, this process alone can take a full team several hours.

## Closet and Wardrobe Setup

Clothes arrive on wardrobe bars or in garment boxes. The team hangs everything in your closets, organizing by type and color if you prefer. Folded items go into drawers. Shoes are placed on racks or shelves. Accessories like belts, ties, and jewelry get arranged in designated storage. For walk-in closets with custom built-ins, the team works around the existing organization system.

## Living Spaces and Bedrooms

Furniture is already placed during the initial setup, so unpacking here focuses on the details. Books go onto shelves. Decorative objects return to tabletops and mantels. Bedding gets placed on beds and made up completely, including pillows and throws. Picture frames land on nightstands and dressers. Lamps get connected and positioned. When the team finishes a room, it should feel like you've lived there for a month.

## Artwork Hanging

If you've opted for the full unpacking experience, our team hangs artwork according to your direction. We use proper mounting hardware appropriate for the wall type and the weight of each piece. For clients working with an interior designer, we follow their placement specifications exactly.

## Debris Removal

This is one of the most underappreciated parts of white glove unpacking. Once everything is placed, the team breaks down every box, bundles all packing paper and bubble wrap, and removes it from your home. No trips to the recycling center. No flattened cardboard stacked in the garage. Your home is clean and box-free the moment the crew leaves.

## The Timeline

For a standard three-to-four-bedroom home in Miami, full unpacking typically takes one full day with a crew of four to six people. Larger estates along Biscayne Bay or in Coconut Grove may require two days. The move manager keeps the timeline on track and checks in with you throughout the process.
