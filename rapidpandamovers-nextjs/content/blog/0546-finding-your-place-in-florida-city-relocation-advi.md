## Why Choose Florida City?

Florida City sits at the southern tip of mainland Florida, just 30 miles from Downtown Miami but worlds apart in lifestyle. This is the last town before the Florida Keys, and the gateway to Everglades National Park and Biscayne National Park.

![Loc Bird S Eye View Of A Residential Area In 1](/images/blog/2027/10/finding-your-place-in-florida-city-relocation-advice/loc-bird-s-eye-view-of-a-residential-area-in-1.webp)


The town of roughly 13,000 residents draws people who want space, affordability, and access to nature without giving up Miami-area jobs. Average home prices run $150,000 to $300,000 less than Coral Gables or Kendall, and you get an actual yard instead of a condo balcony.

### Location and Accessibility

Florida City sits at the intersection of US-1 and the Florida Turnpike. The Turnpike runs you straight to Homestead (5 minutes), Cutler Bay (20 minutes), or Downtown Miami (45 minutes without traffic). US-1 takes you to the Keys, with Key Largo about 25 miles south.

The community operates the Florida City Trolley connecting to the Homestead Transit Center, where you can catch Miami-Dade Transit buses or the South Dade Transitway to the Dadeland South Metrorail station.

### Community and Lifestyle

Florida City has a small-town feel that surprises newcomers from urban Miami. The Farmer's Market at the Florida City State Farmer's Market on Palm Drive runs year-round, selling fresh produce from nearby farms. Robert Is Here fruit stand, just outside town, has been a local institution since 1959.

## Neighborhoods to Consider

Florida City is compact, but distinct areas offer different experiences.

### Downtown Florida City

The commercial core along Palm Drive and US-1 puts you walking distance from restaurants, shops, and the weekly farmer's market. Housing here is primarily affordable apartments and small homes, popular with young families and workers at nearby businesses.

### Cameron Estates

This residential subdivision on the west side of town offers newer single-family homes built in the 2000s. Expect 3-4 bedroom homes with garages and yards, popular with families who want space and commute to jobs in Homestead or Cutler Bay.

### Redland Area (Unincorporated)

Just north of Florida City, the Redland offers larger lots (half-acre to multi-acre properties) in an agricultural setting. You'll find older homes with character, small farms, and properties zoned for animals. Popular with people escaping the suburbs who want genuine rural Florida living.

### Choosing Your Perfect Spot

Consider these factors:

- **Commute:** Cameron Estates offers quick Turnpike access; Downtown puts you near local jobs
- **Schools:** Florida City Elementary feeds into Homestead schools; check current ratings
- **Property size:** Downtown for compact lots, Redland for acreage
- **Lifestyle:** Downtown for walkability, outlying areas for privacy and space

## Moving to Florida City: Timing Tips

Fall brings ideal moving weather to South Florida. October through April offers lower humidity and fewer afternoon thunderstorms. Summer moves work fine but schedule morning starts to beat the 3pm storms.

### Timing Your Move

When planning your relocation, consider:

- **Best moving days:** Weekdays offer better mover availability and lower rates. Monday and Tuesday are often cheapest.
- **Weather windows:** October through April is dry season. May through September brings daily afternoon storms, so schedule morning moves.
- **Local events:** The Redland Riot country festival (January) and Everglades Day (February) bring traffic to the area. Plan around these if possible.
- **Hurricane season:** June through November carries hurricane risk. Moving insurance is worth the investment during these months.

## Essential Services to Locate

As a new Florida City resident, you'll want to find these local resources:

- **Healthcare:** Homestead Hospital (10 minutes north) provides emergency and specialty care. Baptist Health Urgent Care in Homestead handles non-emergencies.
- **Schools:** Florida City Elementary serves the town. Middle and high schoolers attend Homestead schools. Keys Gate Charter School offers K-12 options.
- **Shopping:** Walmart Supercenter sits at the US-1 and Palm Drive intersection. Publix is 5 minutes north in Homestead. The Outlet Marketplace at Florida City has discount retail.
- **Recreation:** Everglades National Park is 10 minutes west. Biscayne National Park is 15 minutes east. Florida City Community Park offers sports fields, a pool, and walking trails.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does it cost to move to Florida City?

A typical 2-bedroom move from central Miami to Florida City runs $400-700 for a local move. Distance from your origin, amount of furniture, and whether you need packing services affect the final price. [Get a free quote](/quote) for your specific move.

### Is Florida City safe?

Florida City has seen significant improvements in recent years. Like any community, some areas are better than others. Cameron Estates and the Redland are particularly quiet. Talk to locals and drive through neighborhoods at different times of day before choosing.

### What's the commute to Miami like?

From Florida City to Downtown Miami is about 45 minutes without traffic, 60-75 minutes during rush hour. Many residents work closer by in Homestead, Cutler Bay, or the Kendall area, cutting commute times significantly.

### Does Florida City flood?

Florida City sits slightly elevated compared to some South Florida areas and doesn't typically experience tidal flooding. Standard flood insurance through FEMA is available and recommended for most properties.
