Whether you're moving within Brickell, relocating from Coral Gables to Coconut Grove, or making the leap from out of state to South Florida, choosing the right moving company is critical. Miami's competitive moving market includes many reputable companies, but unfortunately, some bad actors prey on unsuspecting customers. Here are five warning signs to watch for before you hire.

## 1. No Physical Address

Legitimate companies have real offices you can visit. In Miami-Dade County, you can verify a moving company's physical location and check their registration with the Florida Department of Agriculture and Consumer Services. A P.O. box only or no verifiable address is a major red flag that should send you elsewhere.

![A Moving Truck Parked Outside A Modern A 4](/images/blog/2025/07/5-ways-to-spot-a-bad-moving-company/a-moving-truck-parked-outside-a-modern-a-4.webp)


## 2. Unusually Low Estimates

If one quote is dramatically lower than others, it's likely a bait-and-switch. Your final bill will be much higher than quoted.

## 3. Large Cash Deposit Requests

Professional movers don't demand large cash payments upfront. This is a common scam tactic, so walk away immediately.

## 4. No Written Estimate

Everything should be in writing: the estimate, terms, insurance coverage, and all fees. Verbal agreements protect no one.

![Tow Truck Operator Loading White Gmc Pic 2](/images/blog/2025/07/5-ways-to-spot-a-bad-moving-company/tow-truck-operator-loading-white-gmc-pic-2.webp)


## 5. No Insurance Information Available

Licensed movers carry insurance and can prove it. If they can't or won't provide documentation, they may not be legitimate.
