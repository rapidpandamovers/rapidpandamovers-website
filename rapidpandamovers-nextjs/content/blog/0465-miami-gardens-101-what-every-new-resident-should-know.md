## About Miami Gardens

Miami Gardens is the third-largest city in Miami-Dade County and one of the largest majority-Black cities in the United States. With approximately 111,000 residents, this vibrant city offers affordable housing, strong community character, and easy access to major employment centers.

## Neighborhoods

Miami Gardens features diverse residential areas with distinct personalities:

![Svc A U Haul Moving Truck Parked On Gravel W 2](/images/blog/2027/04/miami-gardens-101-what-every-new-resident-should-know/svc-a-u-haul-moving-truck-parked-on-gravel-w-2.webp)


### Lake Lucerne
The northwestern section near the lake offers a quieter, family-oriented atmosphere with well-maintained single-family homes. Streets here tend to have more mature trees and larger lots.

### Norwood
Located in the central part of the city, Norwood features a mix of housing styles from mid-century ranch homes to newer constructions. Easy access to NW 27th Avenue makes commuting straightforward.

### Scott Lake
This eastern neighborhood sits closer to I-95, making it ideal for commuters heading to Fort Lauderdale or downtown Miami. You'll find a mix of single-family homes and townhouses here.

### Carol City
One of the original neighborhoods, Carol City has deep roots in the community. Expect older, established homes with character and active neighborhood associations.

## Moving Tips for Miami Gardens

### Best Time to Move
- **Fall/Winter (October-March)**: Cooler temperatures and lower humidity make moving more comfortable
- **Early Morning**: Schedule your move to start by 8am before afternoon heat kicks in
- **Avoid**: NFL game days and major events at Hard Rock Stadium, when traffic backs up for miles

### Local Considerations
- **Traffic**: Plan around stadium events. Check the Dolphins and Hurricanes schedules before booking your move date
- **Parking**: Most neighborhoods have driveways, but confirm with your new home
- **HOA**: Some communities have move-in restrictions, elevator reservations, or specific hours for moving trucks
- **Weather**: Summer moves (May-October) mean afternoon thunderstorms. Morning loads are your friend

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Miami Gardens a good place to live?

Miami Gardens offers affordable housing compared to coastal Miami-Dade cities, with median home prices well below Coral Gables or Miami Beach. The city has strong community ties, good access to employment centers, and entertainment options like Hard Rock Stadium. Families appreciate the suburban feel while staying connected to greater Miami.

### How much does it cost to live in Miami Gardens?

With a median household income around $61,000, Miami Gardens provides middle-class living in Miami-Dade County. Housing costs are significantly lower than Aventura, Kendall, or Doral, making it attractive for first-time homebuyers and families looking for more space.

### How far is Miami Gardens from the beach?

Miami Gardens sits about 15-20 miles from Miami Beach, depending on your neighborhood. Expect a 25-35 minute drive to the coast via I-95 or the Turnpike, longer during rush hour or when events are happening at Hard Rock Stadium.

### What are the best schools in Miami Gardens?

Miami-Dade County Public Schools serves the area with several well-regarded options. Florida Memorial University, a historically Black university, is located right in Miami Gardens for higher education. Miami-Dade College campuses are also easily accessible for community college courses.
