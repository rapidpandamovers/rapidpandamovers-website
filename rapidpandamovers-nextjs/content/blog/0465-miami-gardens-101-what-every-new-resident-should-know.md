Welcome to your comprehensive guide for moving to Miami Gardens! As the third-largest city in Miami-Dade County, Miami Gardens offers suburban living, diverse communities, and major sports and entertainment venues.

## About Miami Gardens

Miami Gardens is the third-largest city in Miami-Dade County and one of the largest majority-Black cities in the United States. With approximately 111,000 residents, this vibrant city offers affordable housing, strong community character, and easy access to major employment centers.

![Miami Gardens Florida Suburb Community Tips](/images/blog/2027/04/miami-gardens-101-what-every-new-resident-should-know/miami-gardens-florida-suburb-community-tips.webp)


### Demographics and Community

- **Population**: Approximately 110,979 (2025 estimate)
- **Diversity**: Black/African American (61.7%), Hispanic (33.6%), White (2.5%)
- **Foreign-Born Residents**: 37.2%
- **Median Household Income**: $60,979
- **Average Household Income**: $76,120

### Education Levels
- High school diploma or higher: 85.1%
- Bachelor's degree or higher: 17.5%

## Housing Market

### Housing Stock
- **Total Housing Units**: 36,280
- **Single-Family Homes**: 69%
- **Attached Units (Duplex/Townhouse)**: 5.8%
- **Mobile Homes**: 1%

### Household Characteristics
- **Total Households**: 34,515
- **Average Household Size**: 3 members
- **Family Households**: 73.7%

## Major Employers and Economy

- **Employed Residents**: 50,215

- **Unemployment Rate**: 8.2%
