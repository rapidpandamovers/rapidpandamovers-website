Your student just got accepted to FIU, UM, or MDC. Now comes the hard part: getting their entire life from your Kendall home into a 12x12 dorm room before the semester starts. November adds another wrinkle with Thanksgiving break, limited university hours, and everyone else trying to move at the same time.

## The November Student Moving Challenge

**The Problem:** Move-in weekends at Miami universities turn into parking lot chaos. Elevators back up for hours, RAs enforce strict time windows, and your carefully planned Saturday turns into a 10-hour ordeal. Miss your window and you're rescheduling around classes that have already started.

![A Diverse Group Of Friends Enjoying Leis 1](/images/blog/2027/11/november-student-moving-advice-for-families/a-diverse-group-of-friends-enjoying-leis-1.webp)


**Why This Matters:** Students who arrive stressed and disorganized start the semester behind. Unpacked boxes become clutter, missing items become emergency purchases, and that futon you couldn't fit becomes six months of sleeping on a bare mattress.

**The Fix:** Plan your November student move around university schedules, not around your own calendar. Here's how Miami families get it done right.

## Miami Universities: Know Your Move-In Rules

### Florida International University (FIU)

FIU's Biscayne Bay and Modesto Maidique campuses have different rules. MMC dorms fill up fast during fall move-in, and November transfers need to check with Housing for specific windows. Parking at Lakeview Hall and Panther Hall fills quickly, so arrive before 8am.

### University of Miami (UM)

UM's Coral Gables campus has strict vehicle access during move-in. Stanford and Hecht residential colleges require advance scheduling, and the university limits how long vehicles can remain parked. UM's November housing transitions typically happen during Thanksgiving week when most students leave.

### Miami Dade College (MDC)

MDC's Kendall, Wolfson, and Hialeah campuses don't have traditional dorms, but many MDC students move into nearby apartments. The Brickell and Downtown areas see heavy student apartment turnover, especially around term changes.

## Key Tips for Student Moving in November

### Book Movers Two Weeks Early

November brings competing demands: Thanksgiving prep, Black Friday shopping, and end-of-semester transitions. Moving companies in Doral, Hialeah, and Kendall book quickly during this period. Call at least two weeks ahead to lock in your preferred time slot.

### Coordinate with University Housing

Contact the housing office before scheduling anything. Ask about:
- Elevator reservation requirements
- Loading dock access and time limits
- Parking permit needs for moving vehicles
- Whether November arrivals need different check-in procedures

Our crews move students into UM, FIU, and Barry University regularly. We know which dorms have freight elevators and which ones require carrying everything up stairs.

### Consider the Weather

November in Miami averages 78 degrees with occasional rain. This is actually ideal moving weather compared to the summer months. Still, afternoon showers can pop up. Schedule morning moves to avoid the 2-4pm rain window that hits South Florida most days.

### Prepare Your Belongings

Student moves are different from household moves. You're packing for a small space with limited storage. Before loading the truck:

- Measure the dorm room dimensions (most Miami dorm rooms are 10x12 to 12x14)
- Check if the bed can be lofted for under-bed storage
- Confirm what the university provides (desk, dresser, bed frame)
- Pack vertical storage solutions like over-door organizers
