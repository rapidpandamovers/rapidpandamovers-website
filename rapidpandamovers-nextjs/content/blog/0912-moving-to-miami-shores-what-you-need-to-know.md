## Why Choose Miami Shores?

Miami Shores sits between Miami and North Miami, offering something increasingly rare in South Florida: a quiet, tree-canopied village feel just 20 minutes from downtown. The historic homes along NE 96th Street and Biscayne Boulevard showcase the architectural character that makes this community special.

![Loc View Of Miami Skyline From Coral Gables  1](/images/blog/2030/02/moving-to-miami-shores-what-you-need-to-know/loc-view-of-miami-skyline-from-coral-gables--1.webp)


Families move here for Miami Shores Elementary (rated A by the state) and stay for the slower pace. Professionals appreciate the easy commute to Aventura, the Design District, or Wynwood. Retirees love the walkable village center along NE 2nd Avenue.

### Location and Accessibility

Miami Shores connects to I-95 via NE 103rd Street, putting Downtown Miami 15 minutes south and Fort Lauderdale 30 minutes north. The Brightline station in Aventura is a 10-minute drive, and Miami International Airport sits 20 minutes west. For beach days, Surfside and Bal Harbour are just over the causeway.

### Community and Lifestyle

The Miami Shores Community Center hosts everything from youth basketball leagues to senior fitness classes. Bayshore Park offers waterfront access along Biscayne Bay, while the Miami Shores Country Club provides golf within walking distance of many homes. February brings the annual Art Festival along the village center, and the weekly farmers market on Saturday mornings is where you'll meet half the neighborhood.

## Neighborhoods to Consider

Miami Shores packs distinct micro-neighborhoods into its compact footprint. Each area has its own personality, and knowing the differences helps you find the right fit.

### The Village Center (NE 2nd Avenue)

The walkable heart of Miami Shores runs along NE 2nd Avenue from 94th to 98th Street. You'll find independent coffee shops, the Grand cinema, local restaurants, and boutiques. Homes here are smaller bungalows from the 1930s-40s, often under 1,500 square feet but full of original details. Expect to pay $500K-700K.

### Bayshore/Waterfront

East of Biscayne Boulevard, larger homes back up to Biscayne Bay. These properties range from $1M to $3M+ depending on water access. The Bayshore neighborhood offers the most privacy and the best views, but you'll drive to shops and restaurants.

### Grand Concourse Area

The streets around the Miami Shores Country Club feature some of the largest historic homes in the village. These Mediterranean Revival and Art Deco properties date from the 1920s-30s and command premium prices ($800K-1.5M). Golf course proximity adds value.

### North Miami Shores

The area north of 103rd Street offers more affordable options, with smaller homes from the 1950s-60s. Prices start around $400K, making this the entry point for first-time buyers who want the Miami Shores address and schools.

## Moving to Miami Shores in February

February offers ideal moving conditions: highs around 77 degrees, minimal rain, and lower humidity than summer months. You won't be sweating through box after box in 95-degree heat, and your movers will thank you.

### Timing Your Move

Plan around these Miami Shores specifics:

- **Parking permits**: The village requires permits for moving trucks on residential streets. Apply through Village Hall on NE 2nd Avenue at least 48 hours ahead.
- **Best moving days**: Tuesday through Thursday typically have the best availability. Monday follows a weekend of moves, and Friday starts a new rush.
- **February events**: The Miami Shores Art Festival (usually mid-February) closes portions of NE 2nd Avenue. Check the village calendar before booking.
- **School zone timing**: If you're moving near Miami Shores Elementary or Country Club, avoid the 7:30-8:30 AM and 2:30-3:30 PM windows when traffic backs up.

As a new Miami Shores resident, here's where to find what you need:

- **Healthcare**: Aventura Hospital and Mount Sinai are both 15 minutes away. For urgent care, Baptist Health on Biscayne Boulevard serves the immediate area.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What's the cost of living in Miami Shores?

Expect to pay $2,200-2,800/month for a 2-bedroom rental, or $450K-800K to buy a single-family home. Property taxes run about 2% annually. The village has no additional municipal taxes beyond Miami-Dade County rates.

### Is Miami Shores safe?

Miami Shores consistently ranks among the safest communities in Miami-Dade County. The village has its own police department with regular patrols, and the small-town atmosphere means neighbors watch out for each other.

### How are the schools?

Miami Shores Elementary holds an A rating from the Florida Department of Education. Many families also choose St. Rose of Lima for private K-8 education within the village.
