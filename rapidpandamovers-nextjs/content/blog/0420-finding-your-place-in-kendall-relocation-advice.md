Thinking about moving to Kendall? You're considering one of Miami-Dade's most established and family-friendly suburban communities. This guide provides essential information to help you plan your relocation.

## About Kendall

Kendall is a large unincorporated census-designated place in Miami-Dade County with approximately 78,500 residents. Known for its family-friendly atmosphere, Kendall offers suburban living with urban amenities, making it a popular choice for families and professionals alike.

![Loc A Stunning View Of Miami S Illuminated S 1](/images/blog/2026/12/finding-your-place-in-kendall-relocation-advice/loc-a-stunning-view-of-miami-s-illuminated-s-1.webp)


### Demographics at a Glance

- **Population**: 78,527 (2023)
- **Median Age**: 41.9 years
- **Median Household Income**: $83,664
- **Hispanic**: 65.5%
- **White (Non-Hispanic)**: 23.8%
- **Foreign-Born Residents**: 44.1%
- **Cost of Living Index**: 117.5 (above national average)

### Suburban Character
Kendall offers an urban-suburban mix with a family-friendly atmosphere. Most residents own their homes, and the area features numerous restaurants, coffee shops, and parks.

## Real Estate Market

Kendall offers more affordable housing than coastal areas while maintaining quality neighborhoods.

### Current Market
- **Median Home Value**: $672,600
- **Median Per Capita Income**: $49,885
- **Median Gross Rent**: $1,826

### Housing Types
- Single-family homes with yards

### Neighborhoods Within Kendall
Kendall borders many distinct communities:
- Pinecrest (east)
- Palmetto Bay (southeast)

- Sunset (north)

## Location and Accessibility

### Geographic Position
Kendall is located 14 miles southwest of Downtown Miami, providing suburban living with urban access.

### Major Roads
- US-1 (South Dixie Highway)
- Kendall Drive (SW 88th Street)
- SW 117th Avenue
- SW 137th Avenue

### Metrorail Access
Dadeland North and Dadeland South Metrorail stations provide direct access to:
- Downtown Miami
- Brickell
- University of Miami
- Miami International Airport

## Moving Tips for Kendall

### Residential Move Considerations
- Most moves to single-family homes or townhouses
- HOA requirements vary by community
- Gated community access coordination needed
- Standard residential streets accommodate moving trucks

### Best Moving Times
- **Weekday mornings** after school drop-off
- **Avoid rush hour** on US-1 and Kendall Drive
- **Consider mall traffic** near Dadeland

### HOA Coordination
Many Kendall communities have HOAs:
- Review moving guidelines
- Notify management in advance
- Understand damage deposit requirements
- Check for approved moving hours
