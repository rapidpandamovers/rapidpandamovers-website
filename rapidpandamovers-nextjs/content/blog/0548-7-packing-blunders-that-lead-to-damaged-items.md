Packing seems straightforward until you open a box at your new home and find shattered dishes or crushed belongings. These common mistakes are easily avoided with the right approach.

## 1. Overpacking Large Boxes

A huge box of books is impossible to lift safely. Use small boxes for heavy items, always. Your back and your movers will thank you.

![A Person Sealing A Cardboard Box With A  2](/images/blog/2027/10/7-packing-blunders-that-lead-to-damaged-items/a-person-sealing-a-cardboard-box-with-a--2.webp)


## 2. Insufficient Cushioning

One layer of newspaper isn't enough. Fragile items need individual wrapping, padding on all sides, and zero room to shift.

## 3. Leaving Hollow Items Empty

Hollow items like vases and cups are fragile. Fill them with packing paper or soft materials for structural support.

## 4. Using Old, Weak Boxes

Old boxes lose structural integrity. Wet boxes are disasters waiting to happen. Use fresh, sturdy boxes from a moving supply store.

## 5. Improper Dish Packing

Never stack plates horizontally. Pack them on edge, like records, with padding between each one. This prevents cracking from weight.

## 6. Forgetting to Label Fragile Items

If movers don't know a box contains delicate items, they'll handle it like everything else. Clearly mark "FRAGILE" on multiple sides.

![Close Up Of A Cardboard Box Filled With  1](/images/blog/2027/10/7-packing-blunders-that-lead-to-damaged-items/close-up-of-a-cardboard-box-filled-with--1.webp)


## 7. Packing Liquids Without Sealing

Shampoo bottles, cleaning supplies, and other liquids can leak during transport. Tape lids, place in plastic bags, and pack upright.

## Extra Tips for Miami Movers

South Florida's humidity adds another layer of challenge. Moisture can weaken cardboard boxes, so store your packed boxes in air-conditioned spaces whenever possible. If you're moving from a Brickell high-rise or a Coral Gables estate, avoid leaving boxes in the loading dock or garage for too long on hot, humid days.

Heat also affects certain items. Electronics, candles, and anything with adhesives can suffer in a hot moving truck parked in the Wynwood sun. Plan your move timing to avoid peak afternoon heat, especially during Miami's summer months.
