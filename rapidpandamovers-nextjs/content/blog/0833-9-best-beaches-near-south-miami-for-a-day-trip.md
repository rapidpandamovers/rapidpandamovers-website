Living in South Miami puts you within easy reach of some of Florida's most beautiful beaches. From calm lagoons perfect for families to pristine state parks with historic lighthouses, here are nine beaches worth exploring on your next day trip.

## 1. Matheson Hammock Park Beach

**Address:** 9610 Old Cutler Rd, Coral Gables, FL 33156
**Distance from South Miami:** 4 miles (10 minutes)

This hidden gem features a man-made atoll pool that fills with fresh seawater at high tide, creating calm, shallow waters ideal for young children. The surrounding mangroves provide a unique backdrop you won't find at typical beaches.

**Best time to visit:** Weekday mornings for minimal crowds. **Parking:** $7 per vehicle on weekdays, $8 on weekends. Lots fill by 10am on weekends, so arrive early.

## 2. Crandon Park Beach

**Address:** 6747 Crandon Blvd, Key Biscayne, FL 33149
**Distance from South Miami:** 12 miles (25 minutes)

Consistently ranked among America's top beaches, Crandon Park offers two miles of pristine shoreline with calm, shallow waters. The sandbar extends far out, making it perfect for wading. Amenities include cabana rentals, a nature center, and excellent picnic facilities.

**Best time to visit:** Early morning or late afternoon to avoid peak crowds. **Parking:** $8 per vehicle. The Rickenbacker Causeway toll is $2.25.

## 3. Bill Baggs Cape Florida State Park

**Address:** 1200 S Crandon Blvd, Key Biscayne, FL 33149
**Distance from South Miami:** 14 miles (30 minutes)

Home to the historic Cape Florida Lighthouse (built in 1825), this state park beach offers a more natural, less developed experience. The lighthouse tours provide stunning views of Biscayne Bay and the Atlantic.

**Best time to visit:** Sunrise for photographers, sunset for unforgettable views. **Parking:** $8 per vehicle for up to 8 people. Lighthouse tours run Thursday through Monday.

## 4. Virginia Key Beach

**Address:** 4020 Virginia Beach Dr, Miami, FL 33149
**Distance from South Miami:** 10 miles (20 minutes)

This historically significant beach offers a quieter alternative to nearby Miami Beach. The waters are calm, and the park includes mountain bike trails, kayak rentals, and a vintage carousel. It's particularly popular with families seeking a low-key atmosphere.

**Best time to visit:** Weekends for the full park experience with food vendors. **Parking:** $8 per vehicle. Beach admission is free.

## 5. South Pointe Park Beach

**Address:** 1 Washington Ave, Miami Beach, FL 33139
**Distance from South Miami:** 11 miles (25 minutes)

Located at the southern tip of Miami Beach, this beach offers front-row seats to cruise ships passing through Government Cut. The adjacent park features walking paths, a playground, and stunning views of Fisher Island and the downtown skyline.

**Best time to visit:** Late afternoon to watch cruise ship departures. **Parking:** Metered street parking and nearby garages ($2-4/hour). The park itself is free.

## 6. Lummus Park Beach

**Address:** Ocean Dr & 10th St, Miami Beach, FL 33139
**Distance from South Miami:** 12 miles (30 minutes)

The iconic Miami Beach experience, Lummus Park stretches along Ocean Drive with its famous Art Deco backdrop. Wide sandy beaches, volleyball courts, and the bustling boardwalk make this the quintessential South Florida beach scene.

**Best time to visit:** Early morning for exercise, evening for people-watching. **Parking:** Street meters and public garages throughout South Beach ($2-5/hour). Consider taking a rideshare to avoid parking hassles.

## 7. Haulover Beach Park

**Address:** 10800 Collins Ave, Miami Beach, FL 33154
**Distance from South Miami:** 18 miles (35 minutes)

Known for excellent fishing and kite surfing, Haulover offers a mile of beach with distinct sections including a clothing-optional area at the north end. The inlet provides access to both bay and ocean fishing.

**Best time to visit:** Midweek for fewer crowds. **Parking:** $7 per vehicle. The park has ample lots that rarely fill up.

## 8. Oleta River State Park

**Address:** 3400 NE 163rd St, North Miami Beach, FL 33160
**Distance from South Miami:** 20 miles (35 minutes)

Florida's largest urban park offers a unique beach experience focused on water sports. While not a traditional swimming beach, it's perfect for kayaking, paddleboarding, and mountain biking through mangrove trails. Kayak and paddleboard rentals are available on-site.

**Best time to visit:** Morning for calmer waters and cooler paddling. **Parking:** $6 per vehicle. Rental equipment available first-come, first-served.

## 9. Homestead Bayfront Park

**Address:** 9698 SW 328th St, Homestead, FL 33033
**Distance from South Miami:** 18 miles (25 minutes)

This southern gem offers a man-made beach on Biscayne Bay with calm waters and stunning views of the bay. It's less crowded than northern beaches and serves as a gateway to Biscayne National Park.

**Best time to visit:** Anytime—it rarely gets crowded. **Parking:** $7 per vehicle. The marina adjacent to the park offers boat tours to Biscayne National Park.
