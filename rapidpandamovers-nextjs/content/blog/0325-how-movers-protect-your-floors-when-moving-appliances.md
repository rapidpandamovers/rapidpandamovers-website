# How Movers Protect Your Floors When Moving Appliances

A 300-pound refrigerator rolling across your kitchen floor can leave a scratch that costs $500 to repair. A washer dragged across hardwood can gouge it down to the subfloor. We've walked into homes across Miami-Dade where previous movers left permanent damage because they didn't bother with floor protection. Here's how professional movers prevent that from happening.

## Why Appliances Destroy Floors

The problem comes down to weight, surface area, and friction. A full-size refrigerator concentrates over 300 pounds onto four small wheels or leveling feet, each about an inch across. That creates enormous pressure per square inch on your flooring. Slide that appliance even a foot without protection and you've got a visible mark.

![A Close Up Of An Industrial Washing Mach 2](/images/blog/2026/05/how-movers-protect-your-floors-when-moving-appliances/a-close-up-of-an-industrial-washing-mach-2.webp)


Miami homes are especially vulnerable. Porcelain tile is popular throughout Doral, Kendall, and Hialeah, and while it's durable for foot traffic, it chips under concentrated loads. Polished marble in luxury condos along Brickell scratches easily. Engineered hardwood in newer homes dents from heavy point loads. Even vinyl plank flooring, which people assume is indestructible, tears when a heavy appliance catches an edge.

## Floor Protection Methods Pros Use

### Masonite Boards

Masonite is the gold standard for appliance moving floor protection. These thin, rigid hardboard sheets create a smooth, flat surface that distributes weight evenly. We lay them from the appliance's current position all the way to the truck, creating a protected highway. The boards handle the weight without flexing, and the smooth surface lets an appliance dolly roll without catching on grout lines or floor transitions.

### Moving Blankets and Furniture Pads

For shorter distances or as an additional layer of protection, thick moving blankets laid flat on the floor prevent scratches from anything that might slip or shift. We use them under Masonite boards on especially delicate floors, like the marble entryways in many Miami Beach homes. The blanket absorbs impact while the Masonite spreads the load.

### Ram Board and Floor Runners

Ram board is a heavy-duty temporary floor protection made from recycled fibers. It comes in rolls and can be taped down to cover long stretches of hallway or living area. We use it when the path from the appliance to the door is long or involves multiple rooms. It stays put, resists punctures, and peels up without leaving residue.

### Plastic Floor Protectors (Shoe Covers for Appliances)

For appliances with feet instead of wheels, we use plastic glide pads that slip under each foot. These let us slide the appliance smoothly across tile or hardwood without the metal or rubber feet making direct contact. It's a simple solution that works well for short-distance repositioning.

## Special Situations in Miami Homes

### Condo Hallways and Elevators

![A Cozy Laundry Closet With A Dryer And V 1](/images/blog/2026/05/how-movers-protect-your-floors-when-moving-appliances/a-cozy-laundry-closet-with-a-dryer-and-v-1.webp)


High-rise buildings in Brickell, Edgewater, and Sunny Isles often require movers to use specific elevators and protect common-area flooring. We lay floor protection through hallways, into the elevator, and out to the loading dock. Many building managers inspect for damage after a move, and any marks can result in charges against your security deposit.

### Outdoor Walkways

In many Miami homes, you have to cross a patio, carport, or walkway between the house and the truck. Pavers crack under heavy loads, and concrete can chip. We bridge these areas with plywood or Masonite to distribute weight.

### Stairs

Moving a refrigerator or washer down stairs means all that weight is concentrated on the edge of each step. Without protection, the stair nosing chips and cracks. We pad each step with moving blankets secured in place, and we use stair-climbing dollies that grip without digging into the surface.

Floor repair costs in Miami-Dade range from manageable to expensive:

- **Hardwood scratch repair**: $200-$500
- **Tile replacement** (single cracked tile): $100-$300 plus matching
- **Marble polishing** (scratch removal): $300-$800
- **Vinyl plank replacement**: $150-$400

## What You Can Do Before Movers Arrive

- Clear the path from each appliance to the front door
- Move rugs and runners out of the way (they bunch up under dollies)
- Point out any areas where flooring is already loose or damaged so the crew can take extra care
- Let us know about floor transitions like thresholds between rooms
