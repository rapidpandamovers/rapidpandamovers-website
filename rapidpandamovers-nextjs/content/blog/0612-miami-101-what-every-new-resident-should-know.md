Miami attracts thousands of new residents each year from across the country. The city offers beaches, diverse culture, no state income tax, and year-round warm weather. Here is what you need to know before making your move.

## Why People Move to Miami

Miami is not just a vacation destination. The metro area has over 6 million residents and a growing economy. Major industries include finance, healthcare, tourism, real estate, and international trade. The Port of Miami handles more cruise passengers than any other port in the world.

![Captivating View Of Miami S Iconic Skyli 4](/images/blog/2028/03/miami-101-what-every-new-resident-should-know/captivating-view-of-miami-s-iconic-skyli-4.webp)


Florida has no state income tax, which draws high earners from New York, California, and other high-tax states. Miami also serves as a gateway to Latin America, with many companies headquartering regional operations here.

### The Weather Reality

Yes, Miami is warm. But humidity runs high from May through October, and afternoon thunderstorms are almost daily during summer. Hurricane season runs June 1 through November 30. You will need flood insurance, hurricane shutters or impact windows, and a plan for evacuation routes.

Winter is the trade-off. While friends up north shovel snow, you will enjoy 70-degree days and low humidity from December through April.

## Neighborhoods to Consider

Miami has dozens of distinct neighborhoods. Here are the most popular for new residents:

- **Brickell**: The financial district with high-rise condos, upscale dining, and walkable streets. Average rent for a one-bedroom runs about $2,800. Ideal for young professionals.
- **Coral Gables**: Tree-lined streets, Mediterranean architecture, and top-rated schools like Coral Gables High. Median home prices exceed $1 million. Great for families.
- **Coconut Grove**: Bohemian vibe with waterfront parks like Peacock Park, outdoor cafes, and a tight-knit community feel. CocoWalk provides shopping and dining.
- **Kendall**: Suburban feel with more affordable housing, shopping at Dadeland Mall, and easy Turnpike access. Good schools and family-friendly.
- **Wynwood**: Arts district famous for the Wynwood Walls murals, galleries, breweries like J. Wakefield, and a creative atmosphere. Rents have climbed as the area gentrified.
- **Doral**: Family-friendly with golf courses like Trump National Doral and proximity to Miami International Airport. Large Venezuelan and Colombian communities.
- **Aventura**: Upscale shopping at Aventura Mall (one of the largest in the US), waterfront living, and access to both Miami and Fort Lauderdale.

### Choosing Your Neighborhood

Consider these factors:

- **Commute**: I-95, the Palmetto Expressway (826), and Metrorail connect most areas. Rush hour on I-95 means 20-minute trips become 90-minute ordeals.
- **Budget**: Brickell and Coral Gables run higher than Kendall or Doral
- **Lifestyle**: Beach access matters to some, nightlife to others, school districts to families
- **Property type**: High-rises dominate Brickell and Aventura, while Coral Gables and Kendall offer single-family homes

## Things That Surprise New Residents

**The traffic is serious.** I-95 and US-1 (Dixie Highway) clog during rush hour. Many locals use the Turnpike despite the tolls because it moves faster. Get a SunPass transponder before your move.

**Spanish helps.** Over 70% of Miami-Dade residents speak Spanish at home. You can live here without it, but speaking some Spanish opens doors and builds connections.

**Everything runs late.** "Miami time" is real. Dinner reservations at 9 PM are normal. Events that start at 8 PM might get going by 9. Adjust your expectations.

**Air conditioning costs add up.** Your electric bill will run $200-400 monthly for most homes. FPL (Florida Power & Light) is the main provider.

**Water tastes different.** Miami tap water comes from the Biscayne Aquifer. It is safe but has a distinct mineral taste. Many residents use filters or buy bottled water.

## Services to Set Up Before You Arrive

- **Electric**: Florida Power & Light (FPL) - set up service 2-3 days before move
- **Internet**: Atlantic Broadband, AT&T, or Xfinity depending on area
- **Driver's license**: Must get Florida license within 30 days of establishing residency
- **Vehicle registration**: Also 30-day requirement, handled at any Tax Collector office
- **Voter registration**: Register at any DMV visit or online
