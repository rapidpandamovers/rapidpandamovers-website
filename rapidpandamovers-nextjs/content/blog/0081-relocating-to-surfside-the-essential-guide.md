Welcome to your October guide for moving to Surfside! Whether you're relocating from within South Florida or making a bigger move this fall, understanding your new community helps ensure a smooth move.

## Why Choose Surfside?

Surfside stands out as one of Miami-Dade County's most appealing locations. The community offers a quiet beach town atmosphere and is particularly known for family beaches and small-town atmosphere.

![Empty Streets With Palm Trees And Modern 1](/images/blog/2024/10/relocating-to-surfside-the-essential-guide/empty-streets-with-palm-trees-and-modern-1.webp)


The area attracts families, professionals, and retirees alike, thanks to its quality of life, convenient access to major employment centers, and excellent amenities.

### Location and Accessibility

Surfside sits between Bal Harbour to the north and Miami Beach to the south, giving residents the best of both worlds. Collins Avenue runs through town, connecting you to the shops at Bal Harbour and the nightlife of South Beach. Downtown Miami is about 20 minutes away via the Julia Tuttle Causeway (I-195), making it practical for professionals who work in Brickell or Downtown. The town also has its own free trolley service that runs along Collins Avenue.

### Community and Lifestyle

Surfside maintains a tight-knit community feel that's rare in South Florida. The Surfside Community Center on Collins Avenue hosts fitness classes, youth programs, and community events. Residents gather at the weekly farmers market, walk their dogs along the beach, and know their neighbors by name. The town's small footprint (just 0.46 square miles) means you can walk to restaurants on Harding Avenue, grab coffee at local cafes, and still be home in minutes.

## Neighborhoods to Consider

When planning your move to Surfside, consider these distinct areas:

- **Harding Avenue Corridor**: The commercial heart of Surfside with kosher restaurants, bakeries, and local shops. Great for walkability.
- **Collins Avenue Oceanfront**: Condo towers with direct beach access, ocean views, and resort-style amenities. Popular with retirees and seasonal residents.
- **West Surfside**: The quieter residential streets between Abbott Avenue and Indian Creek. Single-family homes with more space and privacy.
- **96th Street Area**: Close to Bal Harbour Shops and Surfside's northern boundary. Mix of condos and townhomes.

### Choosing Your Perfect Spot

Consider these factors:

- **Proximity to work and schools**: Factor in your daily commute
- **Local amenities**: Parks, shopping, dining, and entertainment options
- **Property types**: Single-family homes, condos, townhouses, or apartments
- **Community vibe**: Family-oriented, young professional, or mixed demographics

## Moving to Surfside in October

October is an excellent time to consider your move. The fall weather in South Florida provides gradually cooling temperatures and decreasing humidity for the moving process.

### Timing Your Move

October is one of the best months to move in South Florida:

- **Weather**: Temperatures drop to the low 80s, humidity decreases, and the daily afternoon thunderstorms become less frequent
- **Condo requirements**: Most Surfside condos require 48-72 hour notice for move-ins, elevator reservations, and a Certificate of Insurance from your moving company
- **Best moving days**: Weekdays offer better availability and rates. Avoid Jewish holidays (many Surfside residents observe) and any community events on Collins Avenue
- **Beach access**: Surfside beach parking is free for residents with a permit. Apply at Town Hall once you're settled

## Key Services to Locate

As a new Surfside resident, you'll want to locate:

![People Enjoying A Sunny Day At A Crowded 2](/images/blog/2024/10/relocating-to-surfside-the-essential-guide/people-enjoying-a-sunny-day-at-a-crowded-2.webp)


- **Healthcare**: Mount Sinai Medical Center is just south in Miami Beach, about 10 minutes away. Several medical offices and urgent care clinics are on Collins Avenue.
- **Schools**: Ruth K. Broad Bay Harbor K-8 Center serves Surfside students. Private options include Hebrew Academy (RASG) and several schools in nearby Bal Harbour.
- **Groceries**: Publix is on 95th Street, and kosher markets including Kosher Kingdom are on Harding Avenue.
- **Recreation**: Surfside Beach, the Community Center pool, and nearby Bal Harbour Park. Tennis courts available at the North Shore Open Space Park.
