Miami's year-round humidity creates unique challenges for moving antiques. Even during winter "dry season," relative humidity rarely drops below 60%. Here is what happens to antiques during a move and how [**professional antique movers**](/antique-moving) prevent damage.

## How Humidity Damages Antiques

### Wood Expansion and Contraction
Wood absorbs moisture from the air. In Miami's humidity, antique wood furniture expands. Move that same piece into an air-conditioned truck, and it contracts. These rapid changes cause:
- Cracking along grain lines
- Joint separation
- Veneer lifting and bubbling
- Drawer and door warping that affects function

Antique pieces from dry climates are especially vulnerable. A mahogany desk shipped from New England reacts dramatically to Miami's moisture levels.

### Leather Deterioration
Antique leather chairs, desk tops, and book bindings absorb humidity differently than wood. High moisture levels cause:
- Mold and mildew growth in crevices
- Softening that leads to permanent stretching
- Separation from wood substrates
- Finish deterioration

Leather needs gradual humidity transitions, not the sudden changes that occur when moving from a climate-controlled home to a truck bed in the Miami sun.

### Textile and Upholstery Damage
Antique textiles, from silk damask to horsehair upholstery, suffer in humidity:
- Mold growth in batting and padding
- Color bleeding in non-colorfast fabrics
- Fiber weakening from moisture absorption
- Musty odors that are nearly impossible to remove

### Metal Corrosion
Bronze, brass, and iron hardware on antiques corrode faster in salt-laden Miami air:
- Surface oxidation accelerates
- Pitting begins on unprotected metals
- Lock mechanisms seize
- Hinges stiffen or freeze

## Professional Protection Methods

### Climate-Controlled Transport
Professional antique movers use trucks with temperature and humidity control. These vehicles maintain conditions similar to your home, preventing the rapid environmental changes that cause damage.

### Protective Wrapping Layers
We use a specific layering system:
1. **Acid-free tissue** next to surfaces to prevent moisture contact
2. **Cotton padding** for shock absorption
3. **Tyvek or similar breathable wrap** to block humidity while allowing air circulation
4. **Moving blankets** for exterior protection

Plastic wrap is avoided for antiques because it traps moisture against surfaces.

### Acclimation Protocols
Before unwrapping at your new location, antiques need time to adjust. We recommend:
- Leave wrapped for 24-48 hours in the new space
- Gradually unwrap over several hours
- Keep pieces away from AC vents during acclimation
- Monitor for any signs of stress during the first week

## Preparing Your Antiques for a Miami Move

### Pre-Move Assessment
Before moving day:
- **Photograph everything** from multiple angles
- **Document existing damage** in writing
- **Get appraisals updated** for insurance purposes
- **Note loose components** like removable glass, drawers, or hardware

### Condition the Wood
Apply a quality paste wax to wooden antiques one week before moving. This creates a moisture barrier during transport. Avoid oil-based products because they can attract dust and leave residue.

### Remove Hardware When Possible
Brass pulls, handles, and escutcheons should be removed, wrapped individually, and packed in labeled bags. This prevents scratching and makes pieces easier to wrap securely.

## Common Miami Move Scenarios

### Coral Gables Estate to Aventura Condo
Moving antiques from a historic Coral Gables home (often with higher indoor humidity) to a modern, tightly sealed Aventura high-rise requires extra acclimation time. The humidity differential can be 20% or more.

### Out-of-State to Miami
Antiques arriving from dry climates like Arizona or Colorado need weeks to acclimate. We recommend running a humidifier near the pieces for the first month to ease the transition.

### Miami to Out-of-State
The reverse challenge. Pieces accustomed to high humidity moving to drier climates may shrink and crack. Dehumidifier use at the destination should be gradual.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Should I buy extra insurance for antiques?
Yes. Standard moving insurance covers replacement value, not antique value. We help coordinate additional coverage through specialty insurers.

### How long does acclimation take?
Plan for 24-48 hours minimum. Pieces from drastically different climates may need a week or more.

### Can you move antiques during summer?
Yes, but we strongly recommend climate-controlled transport from June through September when humidity exceeds 80%.
