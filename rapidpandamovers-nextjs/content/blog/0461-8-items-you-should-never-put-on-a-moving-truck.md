Some items simply cannot travel on a moving truck - for safety, legal, or practical reasons. Before your April move, make sure you know what needs to be transported separately or disposed of properly.

## Why Some Items Are Prohibited

Moving companies operate under strict federal regulations enforced by the Department of Transportation (DOT) and Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA). Violating these rules can result in fines up to $75,000 per violation. More importantly, prohibited items create genuine safety hazards for movers, other drivers, and your belongings.

![Prohibited Items Moving Truck Safety Planning](/images/blog/2027/04/8-items-you-should-never-put-on-a-moving-truck/prohibited-items-moving-truck-safety-planning.webp)


## 1. Propane Tanks

**Even "empty" propane tanks are prohibited.**

Why: Propane tanks retain flammable gas residue even when the gauge reads empty. In a hot moving truck (which can reach 120°F+ in summer), pressure buildup creates explosion risk.

**What to Do:**
- Use up fuel before moving
- Return tanks to exchange locations (many hardware stores accept them)
- Contact your propane supplier about pickup
- Never dispose of in regular trash

This includes: Grill tanks, camping stove canisters, torch tanks, and any pressurized propane container.

## 2. Gasoline and Fuel

**All petroleum products are prohibited.**

This includes:
- Gasoline
- Diesel fuel
- Kerosene
- Lighter fluid
- Camping fuel
- Motor oil (some movers will transport sealed containers)

**What to Do:**
- Run lawn equipment until tanks are empty (at least 24 hours before move)
- Use up fuel in vehicles you're driving yourself
- Contact local hazardous waste facilities for disposal

**Lawn Equipment Note:** Lawn mowers, generators, and power equipment CAN be moved IF gasoline is completely drained and the engine is run until it dies.

## 3. Household Chemicals

**Cleaning products and chemicals pose multiple hazards.**

Prohibited items include:
- Bleach and ammonia cleaners
- Drain cleaners
- Oven cleaners
- Pesticides and herbicides
- Pool chemicals
- Fertilizers
- Paint and paint thinner
- Solvents

**Why:** Chemical spills damage belongings, containers can rupture under heat and pressure, and mixing of leaked chemicals can create toxic reactions.

**What to Do:**
- Use up products before moving
- Give unopened items to neighbors
- Contact Miami-Dade's hazardous waste program for disposal
- Some paint stores accept paint returns

## 4. Aerosol Cans

**Pressurized containers are dangerous in hot trucks.**

This includes:
- Hair spray
- Spray paint
- Bug spray
- Air fresheners
- Cooking sprays
- WD-40 and lubricants

**Why:** Aerosols can explode when temperatures exceed their safety limits. Moving truck cargo areas regularly exceed 120°F in Florida summers.

**What to Do:**
- Transport in your air-conditioned vehicle
- Use up before moving
- Dispose of empty cans in regular recycling

## 5. Firearms and Ammunition

**Transport these yourself - don't give them to movers.**

Moving companies generally decline firearms due to:
- Complex state-by-state regulations
- Liability concerns
- Federal transportation laws

**Legal Transport Requirements:**
- Research gun laws in both origin and destination states
- Transport firearms unloaded and in locked cases
- Keep separate from ammunition
- Carry required permits and documentation
- Consider a licensed firearms shipping service for long distances

**Ammunition:** Particularly dangerous in hot trucks. Always transport yourself or ship through proper channels.

## 6. Perishable Foods

**Anything that can spoil is prohibited for good reason.**

Prohibited items:
- Frozen foods
- Refrigerated items
- Fresh produce
- Open food packages
- Anything requiring temperature control

**Why:**
- Spoiled food attracts pests
- Creates odors that permeate other items
- Health hazards from bacteria growth
- Can leak and damage belongings

**For Local Moves:**
- Transport perishables in a cooler in your car
- Move them last and unpack first

**For Long-Distance Moves:**
- Use up or donate perishables
- Non-perishables in sealed containers may be acceptable

## 7. Plants

**Most moving companies decline houseplants.**

![Prohibited Items Moving Truck Safety Steps](/images/blog/2027/04/8-items-you-should-never-put-on-a-moving-truck/prohibited-items-moving-truck-safety-steps.webp)


Reasons:
- Plants may not survive extended time in dark trucks
- Temperature extremes cause damage
- Some states restrict plant transport (pest prevention)
- Plants can harbor insects that infest other items

**What to Do:**
- Transport small plants in your vehicle
- Give large plants to friends or neighbors
- Take cuttings to start new plants
- Check destination state regulations for restrictions

**Florida Note:** Some plants are regulated when leaving the state. Check USDA requirements for long-distance moves.

## 8. Valuables and Irreplaceables

**While not prohibited, these shouldn't go on the truck.**

Keep with you:
- Jewelry
- Cash and financial documents
- Important legal documents (deeds, passports, birth certificates)
- Irreplaceable family photos
- Prescription medications
- Car titles and registration
- Computer backup drives
- Keys to new home

**Why:**
- These items cannot be replaced if lost
- Moving company liability is limited
- Immediate access may be needed

## Bonus: Items to Handle Carefully

### Scuba Tanks and Compressed Gas
Any pressurized tank is prohibited. Have tanks professionally emptied before moving.

### Nail Polish and Remover
Highly flammable and can damage other items if leaked.

### Lithium Batteries (Large)
E-bike batteries, large power tool batteries, and similar items may be restricted. Check with your mover.

### Wine and Alcohol
While not prohibited, movers may decline due to breakage and leakage risk. Professional wine movers exist for valuable collections.

## What Happens If Movers Find Prohibited Items?

1. They will remove the item from your shipment
2. You may be asked to dispose of it immediately
3. Your move may be delayed
4. In serious cases, your contract could be voided
5. No refund for items not transported

## Disposal Resources in Miami-Dade

**Hazardous Waste:**
- Miami-Dade's Home Chemical Collection Centers
- Scheduled collection events throughout the year
- Check miamidade.gov for locations and schedules

**Electronics:**
- Many electronics stores accept old devices
- Miami-Dade offers e-waste recycling

**Paint:**
- Some paint stores accept returns
- Latex paint can be dried and disposed as regular trash
- Oil-based paints require hazardous waste disposal
