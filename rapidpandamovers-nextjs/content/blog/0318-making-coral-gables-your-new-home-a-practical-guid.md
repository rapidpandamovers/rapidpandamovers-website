Thinking about moving to Coral Gables? Known as "The City Beautiful," Coral Gables is one of Miami-Dade's most prestigious communities, featuring Mediterranean architecture, tree-lined streets, and a commitment to aesthetic excellence that dates back to its founding in the 1920s.

## About Coral Gables

Coral Gables is a planned community of approximately 50,400 residents, developed by George Merrick in the 1920s as a Mediterranean-inspired paradise. The city maintains strict architectural standards that preserve its unique character and historic charm.

![Lush University Campus In Coral Gables F 3](/images/blog/2026/05/making-coral-gables-your-new-home-a-practical-guide/lush-university-campus-in-coral-gables-f-3.webp)


### Demographics at a Glance

- **Population**: 50,379 (2024)
- **Median Age**: 39 years
- **Median Household Income**: $130,803
- **Land Area**: 12.9 square miles
- **Hispanic Population**: 56.4%
- **Foreign-Born Residents**: 38.5%

### The Mediterranean Vision
George Merrick's vision for Coral Gables included:
- Mandatory Mediterranean Revival architecture
- Lush landscaping and scenic plazas
- Named entrances and gateways
- International "villages" showcasing diverse architectural styles

## Architecture and Historic Preservation

### Mediterranean Mandate
The Coral Gables Zoning Code requires "Coral Gables Mediterranean Architecture" that reflects George Merrick's original vision. The city offers incentive bonuses for compliant construction.

![Modern University Campus Building By A T 4](/images/blog/2026/05/making-coral-gables-your-new-home-a-practical-guide/modern-university-campus-building-by-a-t-4.webp)


### Historic Landmarks
- **Biltmore Hotel**: National Historic Landmark, 1926
- **Venetian Pool**: Historic public swimming pool in a coral rock quarry
- **Coral Gables Congregational Church**: Merrick's memorial church
- **The Villages**: Seven themed architectural neighborhoods

### Architectural Review
All new construction and significant renovations require Board of Architects approval to maintain the city's aesthetic standards.

## Moving Tips for Coral Gables

### Historic District Considerations
- Tree protection ordinances apply
- Parking restrictions near historic properties
- Permits may be needed for large moving trucks

![View Of A Modern University Campus In Co 5](/images/blog/2026/05/making-coral-gables-your-new-home-a-practical-guide/view-of-a-modern-university-campus-in-co-5.webp)


### HOA and Covenant Requirements
Many Coral Gables neighborhoods have:
- Architectural review committees
- Moving notification requirements
- Specific permitted hours for moving

### Best Moving Times
- **Early morning** before Miracle Mile traffic
- **Avoid UM game days** and graduation weekends
- **Weekdays** when business traffic is lighter

### Street Considerations
Many streets are narrow with limited parking. Plan for:
- Temporary parking permits
- No-parking zone coordination
- Protection for street trees and landscaping
