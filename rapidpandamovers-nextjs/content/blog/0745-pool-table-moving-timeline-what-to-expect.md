# Pool Table Moving Timeline: What to Expect

Knowing how long a pool table move takes helps you plan the rest of your moving day. Whether you're coordinating with a full-house moving crew or just relocating the table on its own, you don't want to be guessing about timing. Here's a realistic breakdown of what happens and how long each phase takes, based on thousands of moves we've completed across Miami.

## Before Moving Day: Planning and Prep (1-2 Weeks Out)

The work starts well before our truck arrives:

![A Person Arranging Billiard Balls On A B 1](/images/blog/2029/01/pool-table-moving-timeline-what-to-expect/a-person-arranging-billiard-balls-on-a-b-1.webp)


- **Initial quote and scheduling** (15-30 minutes): You tell us the table brand, size, location, and destination. We provide a quote and lock in a date.
- **Pre-move assessment** (30-60 minutes, if needed): For complicated moves involving stairs, tight hallways, or condo buildings, we visit your home to measure access points and coordinate with building management. This isn't always necessary for straightforward ground-floor-to-ground-floor local moves.
- **Building coordination** (varies): For Miami condo moves, we handle the COI (certificate of insurance) and freight elevator reservation. Some buildings in Brickell and Downtown require 1-2 weeks of advance notice, so don't wait until the last minute.

## Moving Day: Arrival and Setup (15-20 Minutes)

Our [**Pool Table Moving**](/pool-table-moving) crew arrives at the scheduled time with all equipment loaded: slate jacks, dollies, moving blankets, hardware bags, tools, and leveling instruments. We do a quick walkthrough to confirm the plan, lay down floor protection along the carry path, and set up our workspace.

## Disassembly (45-75 Minutes)

The teardown follows a precise sequence:

- **Pockets and rails** (15-20 minutes): Six pockets come off, then the four or six rail sections are unbolted, labeled, and wrapped.
- **Felt removal** (10-20 minutes): If saving the felt, this step takes longer because every staple must be pulled individually without tearing the cloth. If the felt is being replaced, removal is faster.
- **Slate removal** (15-25 minutes): Three slate sections are lifted off the frame using slate jacks, wrapped in heavy moving blankets, and strapped.
- **Frame and legs** (10-15 minutes): The frame is disassembled and the legs are removed, labeled, and padded.

The total time for disassembly depends on the table's construction. A simple home table takes about 45 minutes. A high-end three-piece slate table with ornate woodwork might take 75 minutes.

## Loading and Transport (30-90 Minutes)

Loading takes 15-20 minutes for a standard table. Slate pieces go in first, standing upright and strapped to the truck wall. The frame, rails, and legs get padded and secured around them.

Transit time depends on the distance. Most local Miami moves are 20-45 minutes of drive time. Long-distance moves within Florida add hours, obviously. We account for traffic patterns too. Moving through Downtown or the I-95 corridor during rush hour can add 30-45 minutes to what should be a short trip.

## Unloading and Reassembly (90-150 Minutes)

Reassembly is the longest phase because it includes precision leveling:

![A Row Of Empty Billiard Tables In A Diml 2](/images/blog/2029/01/pool-table-moving-timeline-what-to-expect/a-row-of-empty-billiard-tables-in-a-diml-2.webp)


- **Unloading and carrying in** (15-25 minutes): Components are brought to the room in the correct order: frame first, then slate, then rails and accessories.
- **Frame assembly and leveling** (15-20 minutes): The frame is reassembled, placed in position, and leveled on the new floor.
- **Slate installation and alignment** (30-40 minutes): Each slate section is positioned, aligned with its neighbors, and leveled individually. Seams are filled with beeswax and scraped smooth.
- **Felt installation** (20-30 minutes): The cloth is stretched, stapled, and trimmed. Re-felting with new cloth takes closer to 30-45 minutes.
- **Rail and pocket reinstallation** (15-20 minutes): Rails are bolted back in their original positions, pockets are attached.
- **Final leveling and testing** (15-20 minutes): We run a precision level across the entire surface, then roll balls from multiple angles to confirm the table plays true.

## Total Timeline Summary

| Move Type | Total Time |
|-----------|-----------|
| Same room or same floor | 2-3 hours |
| Local move (under 20 miles, no stairs) | 3-4 hours |
| Local move with stairs or elevator | 4-5 hours |
| Local move with re-felting | 4-5 hours |
| Long-distance (within Florida) | 5-8+ hours |

## Tips for a Smooth Timeline

- **Clear the path**: Remove any furniture, rugs, or obstacles between the table and the nearest exit. This saves 15-20 minutes of maneuvering.
- **Have the new room ready**: Don't schedule your table delivery for the same hour the painters are finishing. The room should be clear and accessible.
- **Decide on felt before moving day**: If you might want new felt, choose your color and material in advance so we can bring it along.
