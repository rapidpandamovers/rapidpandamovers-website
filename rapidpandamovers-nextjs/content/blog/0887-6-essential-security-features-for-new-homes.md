Your family's safety should be a top priority when moving into a new home this winter. You don't know who had access before you, and it's better to be proactive about security than to react after something happens.

## 1. Change All Locks Immediately

Previous owners, contractors, and real estate agents may have copies of keys. Start fresh with new locks.

![Security Safety Smart Home Checklist](/images/blog/2029/12/6-essential-security-features-for-new-homes/security-safety-smart-home-checklist.webp)


## 2. Check All Windows and Doors

Ensure every entry point locks properly. Replace or repair faulty locks right away.

![Security Safety Smart Home Essentials](/images/blog/2029/12/6-essential-security-features-for-new-homes/security-safety-smart-home-essentials.webp)


## 3. Install or Update Security System

Whether professional monitoring or DIY smart devices, get a security system active immediately.

## 4. Add Exterior Lighting

Motion-sensor lights deter intruders and help you navigate safely at night.

![Security Safety Smart Home Planning](/images/blog/2029/12/6-essential-security-features-for-new-homes/security-safety-smart-home-planning.webp)


## 5. Test Smoke and CO Detectors

Replace batteries in all detectors. If units are old, replace them entirely.

## 6. Secure the Garage

Garage doors are a common entry point for intruders. Ensure the door locks properly and consider a smart garage door opener.

![Security Safety Smart Home Tips](/images/blog/2029/12/6-essential-security-features-for-new-homes/security-safety-smart-home-tips.webp)
