Moving costs are often calculated by weight and volume. The less you move, the less you pay—and the faster your move goes. Here are five proven methods to lighten your load before moving day.

## 1. The Four-Box Method

Go room by room with four labeled boxes: Keep, Donate, Sell, and Trash. Every item must go into a box. No "maybe" pile allowed. This method forces quick decisions and prevents endless deliberation.

![Declutter Organize Minimalism Essentials](/images/blog/2030/02/5-methods-to-lighten-your-load-before-moving/declutter-organize-minimalism-essentials.webp)


## 2. The One-Year Rule

If you haven't used something in the past year, you probably won't use it in your new home. Be honest with yourself about holiday decorations you skip, exercise equipment gathering dust, and clothes that don't fit.

## 3. Digitize What You Can

Scan old photos, documents, and paperwork. Rip CDs to digital files. Take photos of sentimental items you don't need to keep physically. You'll preserve the memories without the boxes.

![Declutter Organize Minimalism Guide](/images/blog/2030/02/5-methods-to-lighten-your-load-before-moving/declutter-organize-minimalism-guide.webp)


## 4. Host a Moving Sale

Turn unwanted items into moving money. A weekend garage sale or online marketplace listings can generate hundreds of dollars while clearing out clutter. Price to sell—getting rid of items is the goal.

## 5. Donate Strategically

Many charities will pick up donations for free. Schedule a pickup a week before your move to handle large items like furniture and appliances. Keep donation receipts for tax deductions.
