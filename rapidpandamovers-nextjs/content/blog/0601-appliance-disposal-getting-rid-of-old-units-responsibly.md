# Appliance Disposal: Getting Rid of Old Units Responsibly

That old refrigerator in the kitchen has been humming louder every year, and the washer in the laundry room leaves clothes wetter than it should. When you're moving to a new Miami home, outdated appliances are heavy anchors you don't want to drag with you. But appliances can't just go out with the regular trash. They contain refrigerants, oils, and metals that require proper handling.

## Why You Can't Just Leave Them on the Curb

Large appliances are among the most regulated items when it comes to disposal. Refrigerators, freezers, and air conditioners contain refrigerants like Freon (R-22) or newer R-410A. Federal law under the Clean Air Act requires these to be recovered by a certified technician before the unit is scrapped. Dumping an appliance with refrigerant on the curb or at an unauthorized site can result in fines.

Miami-Dade County's bulk trash pickup will take some appliances, but there are restrictions and scheduling requirements. You need to call 311 or schedule online, and pickup may not happen for a week or more. If you're on a moving timeline, that delay can be a problem.

## Options for Appliance Disposal in Miami

**Retailer take-back programs:** When you buy a new appliance from Home Depot, Lowe's, or Best Buy and pay for delivery, most will haul away your old unit for a small fee (typically $15 to $50). This is the easiest option if you're replacing the appliance before you move.

**Scrap metal recycling:** Appliances are loaded with recyclable steel, copper, and aluminum. Scrap yards in the Miami area, including facilities in Medley and Hialeah, accept old appliances. Some will pick up for free or even pay you a few dollars per unit, depending on current metal prices. The catch is that refrigerant units need to be properly evacuated first.

**Donation:** If your appliance still works, organizations like Habitat for Humanity ReStore accept functioning refrigerators, washers, dryers, and stoves. Salvation Army also takes working appliances. Getting a tax-deductible donation receipt is a nice bonus.

We disconnect the unit (for non-gas appliances), carry it out of your home, load it onto the truck, and ensure it's disposed of or recycled properly. This is especially useful when you have multiple appliances to remove at once or when the units are in hard-to-reach spots like second floors or tight laundry closets.

## What About Gas Appliances?

If your stove, dryer, or water heater runs on gas, you'll need a licensed plumber or the gas company to disconnect it before any removal company can take it. FPL and other local utilities can handle disconnection, but schedule this a few days in advance of your junk removal appointment. Never attempt to disconnect a gas line yourself.

## Timing Your Appliance Removal

If you're moving, coordinate appliance removal around your move date. The best approach is to have old appliances removed the same day new ones are delivered, or to schedule removal a day or two before the movers arrive. This keeps the kitchen and laundry room clear for packing and moving.
