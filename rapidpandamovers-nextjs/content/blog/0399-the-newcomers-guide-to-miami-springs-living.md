Thinking about moving to Miami Springs this November? You're considering one of Miami-Dade County's most desirable communities. This fall guide will help you understand what makes Miami Springs special and how to plan your relocation.

## Why Choose Miami Springs?

Miami Springs stands out as one of Miami-Dade County's most appealing locations. The community offers a historic and charming atmosphere and is particularly known for tree-lined streets and small-town feel.

![Loc Captivating View Of Miami S Iconic Skyli 1](/images/blog/2026/11/the-newcomers-guide-to-miami-springs-living/loc-captivating-view-of-miami-s-iconic-skyli-1.webp)


The area attracts families, professionals, and retirees alike, thanks to its quality of life, convenient access to major employment centers, and excellent amenities.

### Location and Accessibility

One of Miami Springs's biggest advantages is its proximity to Miami International Airport, just minutes away. Residents enjoy easy access to the Palmetto Expressway (SR 826) and Airport Expressway (SR 112), making commutes to Downtown Miami, Coral Gables, and Doral straightforward. The city sits between Hialeah to the north and Doral to the west, giving you quick access to major shopping centers like Miami International Mall and Doral's restaurant scene.

### Community and Lifestyle

Miami Springs residents enjoy a strong sense of community that's rare in South Florida. The city maintains its own police department, recreational programs, and public events. Residents gather at spots like the Miami Springs Golf and Country Club, the community pool complex, and Curtiss Parkway for evening walks. The fall season is particularly vibrant, with Veterans Day activities, the annual Miami Springs Art Festival, and holiday events at the Circle.

## Neighborhoods to Consider

When planning your move to Miami Springs, the Downtown area along Westward Drive is particularly popular with newcomers. Each neighborhood has its own character. Some offer walkable dining and shopping near the Circle, while others provide quiet, tree-lined streets with classic 1920s architecture.

### Choosing Your Perfect Spot

The areas near Curtiss Parkway and South Royal Poinciana Boulevard tend to have larger lots with mature oak trees. Homes closer to the Circle offer easier walking access to local restaurants like El Novillo and the Miami Springs Pharmacy, a local landmark since 1956. Properties near the golf course command premium prices but offer beautiful views.

Consider these factors:

- **Proximity to work**: Minutes from MIA, 20 minutes to Brickell, 15 minutes to Doral
- **School district**: Miami Springs Senior High and Miami Springs Elementary have strong community ties
- **Property types**: Mostly single-family homes with Mission and Mediterranean Revival styles
- **Lot sizes**: Generally larger than nearby Hialeah, with mature landscaping

## Essential Services to Locate

As a new Miami Springs resident, you'll want to find:

- **Healthcare**: Palmetto General Hospital in Hialeah is the closest major hospital, about 10 minutes away. Baptist Health and Jackson Memorial are 15-20 minutes south
- **Schools**: Miami Springs Elementary, Miami Springs Middle, and Miami Springs Senior High serve the community. Private options include nearby Monsignor Edward Pace High School
- **Shopping**: Publix on Curtiss Parkway handles daily needs. Miami International Mall and Dolphin Mall are 10 minutes away for larger shopping trips
- **Recreation**: Miami Springs Golf Course, the community pool, and multiple parks including Eldridge Park and Stafford Park
