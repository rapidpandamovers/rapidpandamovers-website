# Moving Workshop and Power Tools Safely

Garage workshops and home woodshops contain some of the heaviest, sharpest, and most awkward items in any household. A table saw, drill press, and workbench together can easily exceed 1,000 pounds, and every one of those tools has exposed cutting surfaces that can damage other belongings or injure movers if not properly secured. We move complete workshops across Miami-Dade regularly, and the process starts with a careful inventory.

## Table Saws and Miter Saws

A contractor table saw weighs 250-350 pounds. Cabinet saws run 400-600 pounds. We lower the blade fully, unplug the machine, and lock the blade guard in place. The rip fence and miter gauge come off and ship separately to prevent bending. For miter saws, we lock the head in the down position using the pin lock, remove the blade, and wrap the base in moving blankets. Every exposed blade gets a blade guard or cardboard cover taped in place.

## Drill Presses and Band Saws

Floor-standing drill presses weigh 150-300 pounds and are top-heavy due to the motor and head assembly. We lower the quill, tighten the table clamp, and remove the drill bit and chuck key. Band saws need the blade tension released and the blade removed entirely. A coiled band saw blade is a spring-loaded razor, so it gets wrapped in cardboard, coiled, and taped before going into a labeled box.

## Workbenches and Tool Chests

A solid hardwood workbench with a woodworking vise can weigh 300 pounds or more. If the bench disassembles, we take it apart and label each section. Tool chests and rolling cabinets need every drawer locked or taped shut. A Snap-on or Husky chest with full drawers can hit 500 pounds, and an open drawer during a hand truck ride will throw the weight distribution off dangerously. We strap drawers shut with ratchet straps around the entire cabinet.

## Air Compressors and Dust Collectors

Air compressors need to be fully drained of air pressure and water before transport. The drain valve opens at the bottom of the tank. Neglecting this step means a pressurized tank rolling around the truck, which is a safety hazard. Dust collectors with large collection bags get emptied and the bags removed. Flexible ducting gets coiled and bagged separately.

## Hand Tools and Small Power Tools

Hand tools go into the toolboxes and chests they live in. Loose tools in open bins become projectiles in a moving truck. We organize power tools by type: cordless drills, sanders, routers, and jigsaws each get wrapped in their cases or in individual moving blankets. Battery packs come out of cordless tools and ride in a separate container to prevent accidental activation during transport.

## Hardware and Fasteners

Those bins of screws, bolts, nails, and specialty hardware are easy to overlook and a nightmare to sort through if they dump. We tape container lids shut and place organizer cases flat in boxes. Label makers are your friend here. If hardware is loose in jars or coffee cans, we transfer everything to zip-lock bags labeled with the contents.

## Setting Up the New Workshop

At the destination, we place heavy equipment first since you won't want to move a 500-pound table saw around other items. We position machines with enough clearance for material feed and outfeed. In Florida garages where heat and humidity are constant, we recommend rubber mats under machines to prevent rust from moisture wicking up through the concrete slab.
